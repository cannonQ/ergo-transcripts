# Rosen Bridge: Binance Smart Chain Integration Goes Live

> **Published:** 2025-02-27 | **Author:** Ergo Platform | **Tag:** Ecosystem, Announcements, DeFi | **Type:** news

**Related concepts:** Addresses, Box, Layer scaling, Native tokens, Rosen Bridge, Transaction

---

It is undeniable that the Ergo Platform continues to outpace most of the blockchain industry in developer activity. Since its inception, Ergo’s Rosen Bridge has successfully bridged assets between Ergo, Cardano, Bitcoin, and Ethereum via its secure system of decentralized guards and watchers. As of today, Rosen Bridge now supports the bridging of assets to Binance Smart Chain (BSC).

The addition of Binance Smart Chain opens the door for the immediate flow of Ergo and Cardano assets into BSC and its large market of DEXs. In other words, it is now possible to list an asset such as rsERG on BSC DEXs. In fact, compatible assets from all of the blockchains that are bridged on Rosen Bridge can be used cross-chain to form new liquidity pools on the respective blockchains.

It will come as no surprise that much of the Ergo community has felt that the industry’s major centralized exchanges have been reticent in their willingness to list $ERG on their platforms. Many have cited that Ergo is a unique Layer 1 blockchain, and as such, it would require significant developer resources to integrate it into a CEX. The Ergo developer team has repeatedly offered to assist with such integrations for free, but they have yet to be taken up on that offer.

Rosen Bridge was built to allow for greater interoperability between blockchains and the free flow of money for all bridged assets. With Rosen Bridge, there is no need for CEXs as Ergo can exist on any of the bridged chains and any liquidity pairing can be created for the immediate trading of rsERG on these blockchains. Ergo is no longer at the mercy of having to try and negotiate listing fees or ask for a CEX to consider listing the $ERG coin. Anyone who uses crypto can now easily access any of the tokens on these blockchains without the need for an intermediary. Subsequently, anyone who has not had access to $ERG and its native assets because of CEXs can now acquire these tokens and interact with the incredible products that are being designed on Ergo.

Ergo has always been about decentralization and limiting control by singular/centralized entities. It is about empowering people to take control of their money and having fair access to DeFi services, such as decentralized lending and borrowing protocols. Rosen Bridge has just rewritten the script on bridge security, and it has significantly reshaped the way people can interact with all of the bridged blockchains. The question is, who will take advantage of this opportunity and help jump start a true decentralized money revolution?

If you are someone who has a significant $ERG holding and would like to explore earning fees for providing liquidity, then Rosen Bridge may be of particular interest to you. Many Ergonauts are familiar with the Rosen Bridge user experience and interface, but if you are new to this product, we will provide a short guide on how to use the bridge.

## Interacting with Rosen Bridge

1. Visit the [Rosen Bridge Website](https://app.rosen.tech/)

![unnamed (4).png](https://storage.googleapis.com/ergo-cms-media/unnamed_4_ea9ed64174/unnamed_4_ea9ed64174.png)

2. You will then need to select your **Source** and **Target** blockchains. To select your Source blockchain (the chain you wish to move assets off of), just click on the drop menu and choose from Ergo, Cardano, Bitcoin, Ethereum, or Binance Smart Chain. Similarly, you can select your Target blockchain (the chain you want to move your assets to) via the drop menu. 

3. Once you have selected the Source and Target blockchains, you will need to connect your wallet.

![unnamed (5).png](https://storage.googleapis.com/ergo-cms-media/unnamed_5_efe6e7604e/unnamed_5_efe6e7604e.png)

_Please note that you will need to have created wallets on both the Source and Target blockchains before you attempt your transaction._

In the example above, we are using Cardano as our Source blockchain, and after connecting our wallet to the Rosen Bridge, the dApp will be able to read which assets are available for bridging.

4. In order to move the desired assets to Binance Smart Chain, you will need to have your BSC wallet address ready for input in the **Target Address** box on the website. 

5. Select the **Token**, or asset, that you wish to move to BSC in the corresponding field on the dApp’s website.

6. Choose the number of tokens you want to send. 

Once you have completed these steps, you can bridge the desired assets to your Binance Smart Chain wallet. It is important to note that the transaction is not immediate. Rosen Bridge is specifically designed for utmost security. It is an intentional design choice to have bridge transactions carefully vetted and verified via the system of guards and watchers. Instant bridge transactions would be nice, but as history has shown, weak bridges have been prone to attacks and lost funds. Rosen Bridge is built to be secure and it significantly reduces attack vectors through its decentralized design - ensuring that funds are safe and arrive at their target destination. 

If you would like to learn more about Rosen Bridge and its revolutionary design, please visit the [Rosen Bridge website](https://rosen.tech/). The team continues to build infrastructure for more blockchains while simultaneously adding support for an increasing number of native tokens from the respective blockchains. The success of this bridge is well documented, and with time, it is clear that it has the potential to become the main hub for cross-chain interoperability throughout the industry.

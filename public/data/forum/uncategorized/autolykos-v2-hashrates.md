my oc setting is diff than what i think a lot of people do because i’m focusing on using the least amount of power. i’ve noticed there are more errors with mining the more power you use. this setup is stable and very low power.

what i do is find the lowest voltage that each card “prefers” and lock it from there.  
to do that grab the voltage 710 or 700 and bring it up slightly above the max clock frequency that occurs after dropping your power limit and core clock to the very bottom.  
(don’t do anything until DAG is generated, otherwise you’ll get more errors)  
core clock fluctuates, so watch what max it goes to.  
bring the voltage slightly above that max.  
in msi you’ll see that it doesn’t go lower than 731 for my card in particular. each one of my cards has a different voltage that it won’t go lower than.  
i lock it from that point slightly above max clock that occurs. doing this drops the power usage drastically and increases hashrate.  
none of my cards are LHR, so that might also be a reason lol  
another factor that is huge for this setup is temperature.  
you need to make sure that your cards stay as cool as possible.  
i use 2 honeywell fans on each side of my rig to push the hot air in one direction. fan speeds for all cards are set to 100% at all times.  
high hashrate is meaningless if your shares are full of errors.

### Post #493 — @MaverickBg (2022-04-07)

Hey, I am sorry for the late reply.

I did modify the bios with Polaris Bios Editor, but only the memory timings.

You dont need really high Memory on those cards on Ergo.

In the advanced config I had this: amdmemtweak –REF 20  
I also noticed that putting “Memory Controller Voltage” to 850mV lowers each card by 5w at the wall extra on top of everything else. Tried with 800mV but after a day one of the cards crashed so I put it back to 850mV. Maybe something like 820 or 830 might work but I decided to not experiment.

With nanominer I was getting Rejected shares (around 99.85% but still) and with lolminer also. With trm I was having the best performance with lowers consumption.

Also in a test about if higher fan rpm consumes more electricity I noticed that if the cards are cooler they consume less energy. This is because conductivity is worse when they are hotter so actually putting the fans a bit higher will also lower your consumption and when you stack all those stuff together it makes a big difference.

This is all I gathered in my journey with the RX580 4GB Nitro+.

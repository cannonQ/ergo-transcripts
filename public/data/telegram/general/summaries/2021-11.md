# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General
**Month:** 2021-11

---

## Executive Summary

November 2021 was a landmark month for the Ergo ecosystem, defined by three major themes: the launch of significant infrastructure (Spectrum DEX going live on mainnet, Ergo Auctions v2, GetBlok decentralized mining pool), the formalization of Ergo Foundation governance and business development (legal incorporation, Dan Friedman appointment as strategic advisor, Kairon Labs market maker engagement), and deep technical discussion around scaling, privacy, and cross-chain interoperability. Community engagement was high throughout, with weekly AMAs launched, developer education initiatives announced, and active debate on the emission schedule soft fork proposal.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Spectrum DEX Launch
Spectrum DEX (then called ErgoDex) launched on Ergo mainnet with AMM pools live for ERG/SigmaUSD and ERG/SigmaRSV pairs, representing the first DEX on any eUTXO-based blockchain. The launch was initially complicated by a Yoroi dApp connector version (0.7.501) breaking compatibility, which was resolved and approved through the Chrome store within days. Order book DEX functionality was confirmed for Q2 2022, with concentrated liquidity targeted for Q1 2022. The eUTXO architecture's constraint of one transaction per block per wallet was noted as a known challenge that the ErgoDex team had already addressed before it became a widely discussed problem. Integration with Cardano was pushed from Q4 2021 to mid-January 2022 due to transaction assembly and JavaScript SDK complexity.

### Ergo Foundation Governance & Business Development
The Ergo Foundation was formally incorporated as a legal entity in November, with a new logo selected following community vote. Dan Friedman — formerly IOHK business developer who helped launch the Cardano ICO in Japan — was appointed as strategic advisor to the Ergo Foundation board, announced via Bloomberg on 2021-11-10. Separately, Kairon Labs (previously used by COTI and Ethereum Classic) was signed as market maker to improve ERG liquidity. Dan outlined plans for professional marketing campaigns, Tier 1 exchange listing efforts (including Binance, under NDA), and establishing ERG presence on other DEXes including PancakeSwap and Uniswap within weeks to months. A Good Whale Fund for dApp development grants was announced with open applications.

### Emission Schedule Soft Fork
A detailed soft fork proposal circulated throughout the month: reduce block rewards by 21 ERG incrementally until reaching a 24 ERG floor, then redirect remaining rewards to a re-emission contract at 3 ERG/block for a long-tail payout. This aims to reduce inflation pressure and extend miner revenue beyond 2027 without a hard fork. Implementation requires miner voting; block 700,000 (approximately 101 days away at time of writing) was identified as the relevant milestone, with a 30-day voting window followed by 30-day activation. The community discussion noted that smart pools under development may eventually return direct miner voting beyond current pool-level signaling.

### Cross-Chain Interoperability: Bitcoin, Taproot, and anetaBTC
Bitcoin's Taproot upgrade (BIP340 Schnorr signatures and simple Sigma protocols) was identified as opening a significant interoperability path with Ergo. The Ergo team confirmed that Schnorr adaptor signatures are the key primitive needed for non-custodial atomic swaps and sidechaining between Bitcoin and Ergo; documentation exists but developers with high-level cryptography skills are needed. The anetaBTC project announced its goal to bring trustless wrapped BTC to both Ergo and Cardano using these mechanisms — contrasted explicitly with WBTC's custodial model (controlled by BitGo). The Rosen Bridge cross-chain bridge to Ethereum, BSC, and Solana was targeted before Cardano due to PAB maturity issues; Wormhole integration was expected Q4 2021 with multiple bridges hoped for Q1 2022.

### Palmyra, Chainlink, and Oracle Pools
Dan Friedman's Palmyra ecosystem outlined its use of Chainlink proof-of-reserve functions through a proxy in Winter Protocol to enable decentralized trade finance via on-chain warehouse receipts. Physical commodities deposited in bonded warehouses generate on-chain receipts using IoT sensors (weight, motion, camera) paired with Chainlink oracles for continuous proof of existence. Oracle Pool v1 (ERG/USD, ERG/ETH, run by Ergo Foundation and community members) and Oracle Pool v2 (running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards) were both operational. Plans were discussed to use capital from a future token sale to build out native Ergo Oracle Pools, reducing dependence on external oracle networks and making Ergo oracles more secure and feature-complete.

### NIPoPoWs, Bulletproofs, and Scaling
kushti discussed active work on NIPoPoWs as the current scaling focus, with UTXO set snapshots planned for the next update and groundwork for SPV clients being laid in Sigma Rust. Bulletproof verification in ErgoScript was explored as a path to compact Sigma protocols for range proofs and generic zero-knowledge statements without trusted setup — positioned as a trustless alternative to SNARKs. Practical applications discussed include verifiable credentials, privacy-preserving DeFi, and hybrid contracts where sensitive data is verified without being disclosed on-chain. On L2 scaling, the team signalled preference for L1 optimizations first, with multiple L2 paths (Lightning-style, Hydra, plasma rollups, merge-mined sidechains) left open rather than committing prematurely to a single winner.

### Security: DDoS Mitigation and Node Spam Protection
An active DoS attack on nodes running public APIs prompted kushti to advise all operators to close port 9053 or move the API to an alternate port. Version 4.0.44 added memory limits and spam protection. Storage rent base fee (0.14 ERG per UTXO) and mempool minimum propagation fee filters were identified as the primary mechanisms for node operators to mitigate spam attacks. A fee estimation API was noted as existing in the node but untested and unused by applications — identified as a gap worth addressing. The discussion also clarified that mempool spam transactions eventually flush naturally as fee markets tighten and miners ignore sub-minimum transactions.

### Wallet Ecosystem
The iOS wallet (Android port by community member) entered private beta with a Christmas release target. Ledger integration was in the Yoroi frontend integration stage following Tesseract backend completion (May–September 2021), described as "a mile off" from production. Multiple wallets were in active development: Nautilus, Minotaur, safew, Satergo, and the Yoroi dApp Connector (built by Ergo Foundation, used by SundaeSwap on Cardano). ErgoPay was identified as a planned solution to reduce structural dependence on the Yoroi dApp connector. Yoroi issues (slow sync, freezes with many transactions, no mobile support) were recurring community pain points throughout the month.

### GetBlok Decentralized Mining Pool
GetBlok — Ergo's first smart contract-based decentralized mining pool — launched live in November, developed from ErgoHack. The pool offers transparent on-chain payment distribution, subpooling for groups to combine hashpower for solo mining efforts, and a globally distributed low-latency architecture. A raffle was run to fund initial operations. The launch was positioned as a demonstration of Ergo's programmable mining infrastructure and as a step toward returning direct miner voting on protocol parameters.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Siavash, 2021-11-03): How many transactions can Ergo process per second?
  **A** (@Glasgow｜WON'T DM): Ergo has smart contracts and can bundle thousands of transactions into one; simple TPS comparisons don't apply. More detailed analysis expected with v5. See the [scaling solutions blog post](https://ergoplatform.org/en/blog/2021-10-29-ergo-ecosystem-scaling-solutions/).

- **Q** (@A, 2021-11-03): If Ergo were used at half Ethereum's level, would it have scaling issues as-is?
  **A** (@kushti): With a concrete set of contracts (Rosen, Oracle Pools, Spectrum, lending) showing similar design patterns, Ergo has a real transaction profile that makes L1 and L2 optimizations tractable. Unlike chains that implement sharding or parallel chains on empty networks, Ergo can now optimize against actual usage patterns. Layer 2 solutions are being considered but are not urgent.

- **Q** (community, AMA, 2021-11-19): Can you explain what bulletproofs are and their relevance to Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a form of zero-knowledge technique allowing specific statements — including range proofs — to be proven in zero-knowledge without a trusted setup. They are a specific form of compact Sigma protocol. The goal is to verify bulletproof proofs directly in ErgoScript, which would enable a variety of zero-knowledge protocols to be used alongside Ergo contracts — including Groth one-out-of-many proofs (used in early Zcoin). Practical applications include verifiable credentials and hybrid contracts where sensitive data is verified without being revealed on-chain.

- **Q** (@Paulo, 2021-11-24): Is there a full node light wallet using NIPoPoWs in development?
  **A** (@Glasgow｜WON'T DM): Yes, NIPoPoWs are the current scaling focus. UTXO set snapshots are planned for the next update, and groundwork for SPV clients is being laid in Sigma Rust.

- **Q** (@Noah, 2021-11-25): What is the storage rent fee for not moving ERG for four years?
  **A** (@Glasgow｜WON'T DM): 0.14 ERG per UTXO — adjustable by miner vote.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-11-26): How does Ergo handle DDoS/spam attacks on the mempool?
  **A** (@kushti): Node operators can close port 9053 or move the API. Version 4.0.44 added memory limits and spam protection. Mempool minimum propagation fee rates can be raised so spammers cannot even reach the mempool. Spam transactions that are not picked up eventually flush from the mempool after enough blocks. There is also a fee estimation API in the node, but it is currently untested and unused by applications — worth someone testing.

- **Q** (@Ilya, 2021-11-21): Why are some addresses in Ergo Explorer very long?
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin): These are Pay-to-Script (P2S) addresses. A P2S address is a serialized script, so its length is not fixed — it depends on the complexity of the script.

- **Q** (@Odiseus, 2021-11-25): What incentives exist for running node infrastructure beyond ideology?
  **A** (@Gazza｜WON'T DM): Running a full node is required to operate DEX backend bots and serve as an Oracle Pool provider — both carry financial incentives. This should organically sustain a healthy node count.

- **Q** (@TMR.ΣRG, 2021-11-28): How is anetaBTC different from WBTC on Ethereum? What does Taproot enable?
  **A** (@Frosty / @TMR.ΣRG): WBTC is custodial (controlled by BitGo). anetaBTC uses Bitcoin's Taproot (BIP340 Schnorr signatures and Sigma protocols) to enable non-custodial atomic swaps — same cryptographic primitives as Ergo's Sigma protocols. This allows trustless pegging and peer-to-peer anonymous swaps without a central custodian.

- **Q** (@Tommy, 2021-11-20): When can we expect a way to hold ERG and earn rewards similar to COTI treasury staking?
  **A** (@Glasgow｜WON'T DM): Ergo is proof of work, not proof of stake. The closest equivalent is a profit-sharing solution in development. Current yield options include Spectrum DEX AMM liquidity provision, SigmaUSD/SigmaRSV, CoinEx AMM pools, Gate.io AMM pools, and KuCoin Grid-Bots — each carrying impermanent loss risk.

- **Q** (@theta decay, 2021-11-02): How is ErgoMixer different from Samourai's Whirlpool and Wasabi's CoinJoin?
  **A** (@Glasgow｜WON'T DM): ErgoMixer is the world's first non-interactive, non-custodial token mixer. It works with any native or wrapped token on Ergo, not just the base currency. See the [ErgoMixer documentation](https://ergonaut.space/en/ErgoMixer).

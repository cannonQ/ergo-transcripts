# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Month:** December 2019
**Chat:** Ergo General

---

## Executive Summary

December 2019 was a foundational month for Ergo's community and infrastructure, marked by external validation from notable figures, early wallet tooling development, and conceptual groundwork for DEX design. Activity was low-to-moderate but focused, with key milestones including the first Ergo Foundation grant, the launch of the Coinbarn browser extension wallet, and a high-profile public endorsement from Charles Hoskinson.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### External Recognition & Community Growth
The month's most significant community event was Charles Hoskinson's unsolicited public endorsement on December 5, describing Ergo as "one of the most interesting projects in the history of the crypto-currency space" and specifically praising AVL trees, non-outsourceable puzzles, and Sigma protocols. This was accompanied by a GAINS AMA on December 12 with a 1,000 ERG prize pool, which served as a structured community onboarding event. Italian-language community members also joined during this period, reflecting early international growth.

### Wallet Development (Coinbarn & Browser Extension)
Significant progress was made on the Coinbarn wallet across the month. A JavaScript library rewrite was completed to enable browser-based wallet functionality. The browser extension was submitted to the Chrome store, where it entered manual review due to cryptocurrency plugin scrutiny. A developer build for manual Chrome installation was published as an interim solution. By December 28, a Coinbarn desktop version was also released. Mobile wallet development was noted as contingent on community demand. Security guidance throughout emphasised self-custody of native ERG over web wallet alternatives.

### Mining Infrastructure & Node Operations
Community members asked extensively about GPU mining requirements (GTX 1060/1070), power consumption, and node hardware feasibility. Key data points established: ~40,000 GTX 1070 equivalents active on the network; full node sync requiring ~1.1GB RAM; light node sync taking ~25 minutes. Raspberry Pi was confirmed viable for light node operation. A critical security warning was issued: precompiled miner binaries should not be trusted, as the miner operates with private keys and self-compilation from source is always recommended. A node version (3.2.0) with reduced memory footprint was flagged as forthcoming.

### DEX Vision & Atomic Swaps
Discussion across weeks coalesced around Ergo's native support for atomic exchanges as a foundation for decentralised trading. It was noted that standard atomic swaps are simpler in Ergo than in older blockchain generations due to core design, with order matching intended to be off-chain while settlement occurs on-chain. This was framed as the basis for building an innovative DEX, contrasting with the frustrations users were experiencing with centralised venues (particularly Tidex, which required 100+ confirmations and caused multi-hour deposit delays). The Komodo HyperDEX was discussed as a comparative reference, with block confirmation time identified as the primary latency bottleneck rather than order matching.

### Supply & Circulating Data Discrepancies
A recurring issue through the month was incorrect supply data on CoinMarketCap and CoinGecko. CoinMarketCap was displaying circulating supply as total supply due to the treasury contract's gradual unlock mechanism. The actual circulating supply at the time was 8,327,325 ERG per Ergo Explorer. Branding was also clarified: "Ergo" for the platform, "ERG" for the currency unit. Dev-locked coin unlock timelines were requested but not fully documented in chat.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@scalahub, 2019-12-19): Are precompiled miner binaries officially supported?
  **A** (@Dmitry): "It is always better to compile by yourself from sources as far as miner operates with your private keys."

- **Q** (@ANGELpool, 2019-12-17): What are the hardware requirements for running a node? Is Raspberry Pi viable?
  **A** (@Unknown): Full node requires ~1GB RAM with ~25% CPU during sync (~74 minutes); light node requires ~1GB RAM with ~25 minute sync. Raspberry Pi is feasible for light node operation. Node version 3.2.0 was flagged as forthcoming with reduced memory footprint.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2019-12-27): How do atomic swaps work in Ergo compared to older blockchains?
  **A** (@Unknown): "Ergo has atomic exchange built in the core for assets. A standard atomic swap is also more simple in Ergo than in older generations of blockchains, additionally the design with no bloat. Matching should be off-chain; different protocols could be built on top."

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2019-12-27): What is slowing HyperDEX performance — confirmations or order matching?
  **A** (@Charles Gonzalez): "Order matching is instant if there are orders available. The slow part is the block confirmations."

- **Q** (@KJSDNGlksdnnds, 2019-12-16): Is it safe to hold ERG on Waves?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Risk exists that the ERG-Waves gateway could be hacked, abandoned, or that the Waves blockchain could fail, rendering wrapped ERG worthless. A hardware wallet mitigates account compromise risk. Holding native ERG is safer.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2019-12-19): Can we say Ergo has the most secure smart contracts?
  **A** (@Unknown): Bitcoin clones offer simplicity; Ergo offers richer functionality including privacy via Sigma protocols. By end of 2020, Ergo has a strong chance to be "real winner in this category" given its solid model, tooling support, and formal verification capability.

- **Q** (@Pepeger, 2019-12-02): Testnet node stuck in "active synchronization" with only headers loaded; unable to perform wallet operations.
  **A** (@Unknown): Issue resolved by correctly setting the block storage directory. "Active synchronization" status means UI wallet operations are unavailable until sync completes and full blocks are downloaded.

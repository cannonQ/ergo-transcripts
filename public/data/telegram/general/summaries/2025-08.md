# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: August 2025

---

## Executive Summary

August 2025 was defined by three converging themes: significant protocol infrastructure progress (sub-blocks moving toward mainnet, Lithos Protocol testnet preparation, and the P2P InfraDAO going live), a reshaping of the ecosystem's economic and governance layer (Better Money Labs, Autolykos DAO, and the Spectrum Network wind-down), and an intensifying philosophical clarification of Ergo's identity as a decentralized, state-resistant, peer-to-peer money platform. Ergo protocol 6.0.1 shipped, sub-blocks devnet relaunched, and the community began serious work on DeFi flywheel design, DEX contract upgrades, and decentralized hosting infrastructure.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Sub-blocks: From Audit to Devnet

Sub-blocks were the single most technically significant thread throughout August. kushti reported the security audit and optimization pass completed early in the month, with a public testnet supporting miners targeted for after the 6.0.1 release. Protocol 6.0.1 shipped on 2025-08-09. The sub-blocks devnet was subsequently relaunched on 2025-08-22 with an updated seed node (`http://213.239.193.208:9058/info`). Early October was confirmed as the mainnet target for 6.0 with sub-blocks enabled. Technical discussion covered timestamp-based contracts and block-height-dependent dApps that will need reconsideration under faster block times, with kushti noting security considerations were baked into Ergo's design ethos from day one. Community interest in building perpetuals and options platforms on sub-blocks was noted, with DuckPools v2 developers citing an October/November target for their next dApp.

### Lithos Protocol Development

Armeanio provided consistent updates throughout the month: difficulty contract completion, off-chain fraud proof contract work, stratum code optimization for TRM users and NISPs, share storage improvements, and documentation/variable cleanup. Testnet preparation was described as underway by mid-August. These updates represent steady progress toward a production-ready decentralized mining pool infrastructure layer.

### Better Money Labs and Ergo Treasury Vision

kushti announced Better Money Labs — a for-profit entity co-founded with Bruno and VCs from major Korean funds — aimed at building an Ergo Treasury and supplying ERG to large institutional players. The whitepaper was published at `https://github.com/BetterMoneyLabs/whitepaper`. A companion Ergo Forum post framed the vision around peer-to-peer currencies and digital free banking, with kushti explicitly welcoming hundreds or thousands of other treasury operators to build on the same infrastructure. This was paired with a "Call for Action" post inviting builders to join the peer-to-peer economy initiative.

### Spectrum Network Wind-down and ErgoDEX Future

Spectrum Network (cross-chain bridge project) announced closure and return of funds to SPF holders. The ErgoDex frontend and all pools remain fully operational on-chain — accessible via ErgoDex.io, `dex.mewfinance.com`, and CrystalPool — with source code remaining open-source. Spectrum Labs fees were redirected to the InfraDAO revenue stream. Pgr456 entered discussions with the Spectrum team about a DAO takeover and redesigned pool contracts that would enforce DAO-level fees regardless of UI choice, ensuring sustainable funding for offchain execution bots and infrastructure. Meanwhile, Patato independently released ErgoDEX Local — a standalone, zero-platform-fee fork of Spectrum DEX running entirely locally — initially for Linux.

### Autolykos DAO Seed Round

Aco Šmrkas launched the Autolykos DAO manifesto and seed round on MewFinance in week 3, offering `$LYKOS` tokens to founding DAO members. The seed round reportedly closed in approximately 5 hours versus the 14 days originally anticipated. The DAO is positioned as a decentralized, community-driven mining governance and venture framework. A companion proposal for a Collateralized Equity Token (CET) model with profit-sharing was also floated by LIFΣ OF IF₳.

### P2P InfraDAO and Decentralized Hosting

The P2P InfraDAO made tangible progress throughout August. qx() reported the first community member successfully setting up a node, explorer, and nginx/certs, with GEO/proximity routing load balancers going live (USA/East and Australia endpoints). GitHub setup instructions were iteratively improved based on community feedback. Celaut (formerly Xelato) introduced single-command installation for node participation, with Josemi noting the auto-install requires sudo access and providing manual mode instructions. A published article from Ergo NEWS covered the InfraDAO's role in decentralizing node hosting and ecosystem maintenance.

### DeFi Architecture: LST, Storage Rent, and Fee Models

A substantive multi-week technical conversation covered three interrelated DeFi design problems. First: whether a Liquid Staking Token (LST) protocol is viable on Ergo's proof-of-work chain — consensus was yes, if high-fee protocols (perpetuals, yield tokenization) redirect fees into staking reward pools to create a DeFi flywheel. Second: whether self-refreshing smart contracts can circumvent storage rent — kushti clarified that refresh transactions still require mining fees, with larger boxes incurring higher storage rent; rational miner pricing will eventually close the arbitrage. Third: DAO-enforced contract-level fees (rather than UI-level fees) were proposed as the correct architecture for sustainable DEX and dApp funding, ensuring bots and infrastructure remain funded regardless of whether users self-host.

### DEX Pool Contract Redesign

Pgr456, Pulsarz, and HQΣr engaged in detailed technical discussion on Spectrum DEX pool contract architecture. Key issues: the current contract design expects the first output to be the successor pool box, which prevents multi-swap atomic transactions (e.g., ERG → SigmaUSD → SigmaRSV → ERG in a single TX). Proposed fixes include custom output index support and NFT-based pool successor lookup. Separate pool types were also discussed — standard AMM pools with DAO + LP provider fees, and royalty pools with additional project fees, inspired by Splash on Cardano. kushti noted that custom output index support is likely the required primitive. Consensus: the migration would require a major contract update, not an incremental change.

### Ergo's Identity: State Resistance and P2P Money

A persistent philosophical thread ran through all four weeks. kushti articulated that "crypto" as a category is effectively over (2012–2024), with only two viable paths: p2p money or corporate co-option. Bank chains (Stripe, Tether, Circle building L1s) represent the latter; Ergo is positioned as the former. Armeanio framed the regulatory environment starkly: "state resistance or bust." Discussion of TradeOgre's seizure and Xeggex's closure reinforced the urgency — non-KYC CEX infrastructure is being eliminated, making on-chain DEX and p2p tooling existentially important. The Ergo Book project was proposed with the working title *"Ergo Book: a handbook on separating money from state"*, with Russian translation support offered.

### Community Events and Meetups

A Colorado Springs meetup was announced for October 2026 at a local non-profit venue, with Chris Ray offering to prepare Korean BBQ, Shabu Shabu, and sushi for the Saturday dinner. A headcount request was set for the first week of October. kushti proposed replacing the weekly "Ergo AMA" format with community calls, noting the AMA framing is misleading. The weekly AMA was cancelled twice during August due to logistics.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Pulsarz, 2025-08-25): Can we support arbitrage in a single transaction (e.g., ERG → SigmaUSD → SigmaRSV → ERG)? Currently requires three separate TXs. The pool contract expects the first output to be the successor pool box.
  **A** (@HQΣr): Multiple swaps in one TX are doable. (@Pgr456): Could change the contract to search for the successor by pool NFT, allowing only one pool to be used once per TX. (@kushti, 2025-08-26): Custom output index support is likely the required primitive.

- **Q** (@DiscordBridge/yea.ok 🍉, 2025-08-18): Can an LST protocol work on Ergo (PoW)? Can perpetuals or other protocols generate sufficient yield to power staking rewards?
  **A** (@HQΣr): Feasible if high-fee protocols (perpetuals, yield tokenization) redirect fees to a staking reward pool. MewFinance already offers delegation and rewards distribution; can build lock-based APR on top. Full flywheel: fees → LST yield → continued dApp composability.

- **Q** (@DiscordBridge/Jim, 2025-08-22): What prevents perpetual contracts from disrupting storage rent's intended effects?
  **A** (@kushti): Contracts can self-refresh (e.g., every 3 years) but refresh transactions require mining fees. Currently mining fees are lower than storage rent, but rational miners will price accordingly over time. Larger contract boxes also incur higher storage rent if not executed.

- **Q** (@DiscordBridge/arkadia, 2025-08-21): Isn't a contract-level fee just a choice — won't most users avoid self-hosting anyway and just pay UI fees?
  **A** (@HQΣr, @Pgr456): True, most users won't self-host. But the correct solution is DAO-enforced contract-level fees (e.g., 0.3% on pools) applying to all interactions — UI, bot, or direct — ensuring infrastructure and offchain bot funding remains sustainable regardless of frontend choice.

- **Q** (@Ioannis, 2025-08-13): Realistically, how many days until sub-blocks on mainnet?
  **A** (@Ruben): October approximately. (@Richi, 2025-08-17, confirmed): Early October.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2025-08-12): How much would it cost to attack Ergo?
  **A** (@cannon_q): Around 9,500–12,000 3090-class GPUs to reach 50% of hashrate; fluctuates with network conditions.

- **Q** (@HQΣr, 2025-08-14): What prevents a 51% attack on a merge-mined chain?
  **A** (@kushti): Nothing prevents a 51% takeover in a permissionless environment — that's a property of permissionless PoW by design. All claimed "fixes" replace permissionless with permissioned. Merge-mined sidechains increase the security budget (cost of ownership per unit time) without changing that fundamental property.

- **Q** (@DiscordBridge/yea.ok 🍉, 2025-08-13): Would a perpetuals/strike-finance-style dApp work well on sub-blocks?
  **A** (@kushti): Happy to help with sub-blocks API on testnet when ready. (@DiscordBridge/kb): Perpetuals/options is the next dApp target; working on DuckPools v2 first (ETA October/November).

- **Q** (@Ruben, 2025-08-07): Can locked ERG (e.g., 10-year lock) be put to work without risking funds? Any representative staking or DAO-controlled usage?
  **A** (@Luca D'Angelo): HODLerg and phoenixfi.app offer representative staking for locked ERG.

- **Q** (@Giufa, 2025-08-07): What does Spectrum Network closing mean for liquidity pools?
  **A** (@HQΣr): No change — all LP is on-chain. Use MewFinance or other frontends. (@kushti): ErgoDex.io and CrystalPool also available.

- **Q** (@gravemind, 2025-08-29): Is a Raspberry Pi 5 a good option for running a full Ergo node?
  **A** (@TheStophe): A 3B+ can handle it with a ~$80 500GB external SSD. Recommended implementation: https://github.com/jkrek17/Ergo

- **Q** (@DiscoverErgo, 2025-08-30): Best DEX on Cardano for buying rsERG by liquidity?

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Sub-blocks mainnet coordination**: Who leads parameter testing on the sub-blocks devnet? What are the success criteria before the early October mainnet upgrade, and which dApps need to update timestamp/block-height dependencies?

- **ErgoDex DAO transition**: Pgr456's discussions with the Spectrum team on DAO takeover and new pool contracts are ongoing. When will this formally launch? What is the governance model and fee structure for the successor contracts?

- **DeFi flywheel prerequisites**: The LST/perpetuals/yield-tokenization flywheel is theoretically sound but requires perpetuals and options platforms to be built first. Who is building them, and on what timeline?

- **Multi-swap atomic transactions**: Custom output index support has been identified as the required primitive for multi-hop swaps in a single TX. Is this an ErgoScript change, a contract-level convention, or both? What is the implementation path?

- **Monero bridge and double merge-mined sidechain**: Community interest in an XMR bridge and a double merge-mined GPU sidechain for Monero 51% attack mitigation was raised but received no concrete timeline or commitment.

- **Ergo Book**: Working title confirmed, contributors identified but overcommitted. Chapter assignments and deadlines not finalized; Russian translation support offered but coordination pending.

- **EIP-37 (difficulty adjustment) feedback**: JL flagged the DAA as problematic post-ETH Merge but declined to engage technically. No concrete proposal or forum post provided; the underlying concern remains unaddressed.

- **Marketing coordination**: Multiple initiatives exist (Autolykos DAO, Sigmanauts, PoCoP, ErgoCapital fund, mining pool outreach) but lack unified metrics, budget allocation, or coordination mechanism.

- **Ledger + Nautilus integration**: USB device selection failure reported by multiple users; directed to Discord but no confirmed fix status.

- **iPhone/iOS wallet**: Nautilus mobile remains Android-only; no App Store submission timeline confirmed.

- **Rosen watcher profitability**: Community asked whether operating Rosen watchers is currently profitable; no definitive answer given.

- **AMA format replacement**: kushti proposed community calls instead of the AMA format, but no new structure, cadence, or host arrangement has been confirmed.

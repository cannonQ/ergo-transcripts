# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Month:** April 2020
**Chat:** Ergo General

---

## Executive Summary

April 2020 was a foundational community-building month for Ergo, dominated by exchange listing activity, early DeFi and tooling development, and growing grassroots community organisation. The month saw meaningful technical progress — including mixer development, DEX contract work, and the first stablecoin experiment — alongside a series of exchange listings and the launch of the first mining pools, signalling the project's transition from launch-phase to early ecosystem growth.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Exchange Listings & Market Activity
Ergo expanded its exchange presence significantly throughout April. Early in the month, Citex opened deposits and added an ERG/USDT trading pair. By month's end, Ergo was listed across eight platforms: ProBit, HotBit, Tidex, Citex, Waves.exchange, GJ.com, ViteX, and P2PB2B. A larger exchange integration was described as "in progress" but remained unconfirmed. Community members repeatedly raised questions about tier-1 listings (Binance, Coinbase, Kraken, Bittrex); responses were limited to "soon, stay tuned." A CoinMarketCap market cap discrepancy was identified — CMC incorrectly deducted treasury funds, contrary to standard PoW coin treatment. EFYT holders on Tidex were urgently advised to withdraw ahead of delisting.

### Mining Ecosystem Development
Mining pool infrastructure began taking shape in late April. The jasondavies pool and ergopool.io were noted as being in development, with HiveOS support confirmed for AMD miners and the Ergo Foundation offering collateral to early testers. Autolykos was confirmed as ASIC- and pool-resistant PoW with a 2-minute block time and 75 ERG block reward. Community members asked about per-GPU earnings (1080 Ti, 2080 Ti) and profitability at various price points, with guidance directed to the official mining blog post.

### DEX Contracts & DeFi Tooling
Substantial DEX progress was made during W17. Denyz drafted DEX contracts with partial filling capability, modelled scenarios in the Ergo Playground, exposed parameters and templates in ergo-contracts, and began formal verification. Deadit deployed a testnet explorer with order book and token pages. Ilia began DEX matcher development. A non-interactive mixer proof-of-concept using ErgoScript was also in development, with Explorer API integration enabling mixing in light wallets and CLI scripts. Token mixing and stablecoin mixing were identified as high-value but sensitive use cases. Version 3.2.3 was confirmed as ~90% ready, incorporating mixer UI improvements and other protocol enhancements.

### Stablecoin Experiments & Oracle Integration
The first one-way convertible stablecoin experiment was published on the Ergo Forum, using a rate oracle holding contract. This was described by kushti as the beginning of DeFi exploration on Ergo, though he flagged uncertainty about whether Ergo was yet ready to serve as a backbone for new economic models. Oracle pool integration was under active exploration. Approximately 130 tokens had already been issued on-chain by users, mostly as experiments.

### Privacy & Cryptographic Capabilities
Discussions across multiple weeks highlighted Ergo's privacy-preserving architecture. The mixer web app (by Anon92048) was published and working. Distributed threshold signatures (distinct from multisig) were noted as being in development. ErgoScript's Sigma protocols, zero-knowledge proofs, and non-interactive DeFi capabilities were explained to newcomers as core differentiators. Cross-blockchain atomic swaps were also teased as an upcoming external product announcement.

### Community Organisation
Regional community groups were formally listed: Indonesia, India/Bangladesh, China, Russia, Persian, and a mining-specific channel. The Ergo Foundation team clarified that community managers were not needed in the near term and that AMA proposals should be routed through Chie for marketing filtering. A security warning was issued: users should not run any binaries received via Telegram. Ergo was nominated for the Atomars voting competition. kushti noted that the Dev Fund was mostly unlocked and that it was an appropriate time to allocate resources toward strong development and community building.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Rich, 2020-04-26): Does Ergo operate without gas?
  **A** (@kushti): Not quite. Each script has a complexity estimation with a complexity limit per block, similar to Ethereum. Paying for computation is not part of the protocol (unlike storage rent, which is enforced). Currently miners do not charge for script validation complexity, but may do so in future.

- **Q** (@Rich, 2020-04-26): What is Ergo's block time, and will it be reduced?
  **A** (@filippounits): Block time is 2 minutes. There is currently no plan to reduce it.

- **Q** (@Rich, 2020-04-26): What dApps can be built given a 2-minute block time?
  **A** (@Unknown, 2020-04-26): Every coin in Ergo is protected by a program in ErgoScript based on non-interactive zero-knowledge proofs, enabling real non-custodial and non-interactive DeFi. Ergo also supports custom tokens, powerful contracts in the extended UTXO model, composable Sigma protocols, storage rent for long-term survival, and efficient SPV security via NIPoPoWs — making it a fully programmable crypto computer.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-04-28): Will cross-blockchain atomic swaps be available? Do both parties need to be online?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes, an announcement is coming from an external product. Both parties likely do not need to be online simultaneously.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-04-28): Can users run light/full nodes and earn fees via privacy/decentralisation services without mining?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo's design allows this. A proof-of-concept needs to be delivered to demonstrate it in practice.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-04-28): What is Ergo's mining algorithm and daily emission?
  **A** (@bez): Autolykos (ASIC- and pool-resistant PoW); 2-minute block time; 75 ERG block reward.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-W16): What is Ergo's max supply?
  **A** (@filippounits): 97,739,924.5 ERG total; 15,623,475 ERG mined at the time of the message (visible at https://explorer.ergoplatform.com/).

- **Q** (@Phantom, 2020-W16): Did Ergo conduct an ICO? How is the project funded?
  **A** (@Unknown / @filippounits): No VC funding and no ICO. See: https://ergoplatform.org/en/blog/2020_02_12_welcome_to_smart_money/

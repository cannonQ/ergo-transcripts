# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Developer Knowledge Base Summary
**Month:** February 2021

---

## Executive Summary

February 2021 was defined by two landmark events: the successful completion of the Autolykos v2 hard fork (memory-hard, GPU-friendly, ASIC-resistant PoW) and the launch of the SigmaUSD/SigmaRSV stablecoin system implementing the AgeUSD protocol. Both events generated substantial technical activity, community education challenges, and revealed real limitations — particularly eUTXO throughput constraints under high demand — that will shape development priorities going forward.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Autolykos v2 Hard Fork (Block 417792, 2021-02-02)

The network successfully transitioned to Autolykos v2 at block 417792. The new PoW algorithm is memory-hard, GPU-friendly, and ASIC-resistant. Initial post-fork difficulty was high, causing block times of 10–15 minutes versus the 2-minute target, but difficulty adjusted over subsequent epochs. Mining software improved rapidly: MHS_Sam, SRBMiner, and Nanominer all shipped releases within days showing 2–4x hashrate improvements for NVIDIA cards. Network hashrate dropped from ~27 TH/s to ~300–400 GH/s immediately post-fork before stabilizing around 1 TH/s by mid-month.

Exchange integration failures were a significant post-fork problem. CoinEx, Hotbit, and Probit all experienced transaction assembly issues — most critically, failing to check mempool state before broadcasting, or using flawed parallel withdrawal mechanisms generating invalid transactions. kushti worked directly with exchanges and prepared an updated integration guide. An updated node config parameter (`ergo.chain.voting.version2ActivationDifficultyHex`) was briefly required before being superseded by node v4.0.3.1.

### SigmaUSD / SigmaRSV Launch (AgeUSD Protocol, 2021-02-25)

The SigmaUSD stablecoin and SigmaRSV reserve coin went live on mainnet on 2021-02-25 via sigmausd.io, implementing the AgeUSD protocol co-developed with EMURGO. Over 500,000 ERG was locked in the reserve within hours, with ~$2.34M submitted on launch day per the official announcement. The launch immediately exposed eUTXO concurrency limitations — high demand caused frequent transaction failures as the single-UTXO contract became a bottleneck. Robert Kornacki explained that asynchronous eUTXO protocols are under research but not yet deployable.

Core mechanics clarified through extensive community discussion: SigmaRSV represents proportional ownership of the ERG reserve (not a direct price multiplier); SigmaUSD is pegged to $1 backed by ERG collateral. The reserve ratio must remain between 400%–800% under normal operation — below 400%, SigmaRSV redemption locks (preventing forced liquidation); above 800%, new SigmaRSV minting halts. A bootstrap period (uncapped SigmaRSV minting) runs until block ~450,000 (~March 12), during which the 800% ceiling is not enforced, affecting SigmaRSV/ERG pricing dynamics. A 1% protocol fee on all minting/redemption accrues to the reserve; a 0.25% UI fee applies additionally.

A critical UX hazard was identified and broadcast: Yoroi's "send all ERG" function also sends all native tokens (SigmaRSV/SigmaUSD) in the same wallet. Sending to an exchange that does not support Ergo native tokens results in permanent token loss. This was flagged to Yoroi developers and announced officially via the Ergo Twitter account.

### Stablecoin Naming (SigmaUSD / SigmaRSV)

The month opened with an extended community debate over naming. Candidates included SigmaUSD, CipherUSD, kUSD, AIR/EARTH, and USDe/eUSD. By week two, consensus coalesced around **SigmaUSD (ΣUSD)** for the stablecoin and **SigmaRSV (ΣRSV)** for the reserve coin, aligned with Ergo's Sigma protocols branding. Keyboard accessibility of the Σ symbol and SEO implications were raised as concerns but not treated as blockers.

### Exchange Listings

Multiple exchange listings shipped across February: TradeOgre (2021-02-08), BitMart (2021-02-24, ERG/BTC and ERG/USDT), BiKi (~2021-02-25), and a fourth listing announced for 2021-02-15. A larger Chinese exchange was teased for end of month. Active exchanges at month end: Gate.io, CoinEx, Hotbit, Tidex, Probit, TradeOgre, BitMart, BiKi. Binance was discussed as a long-term target; the team noted exchange listing fees (reportedly 5% of token supply) as a barrier and advocated for organic growth first.

### Node Releases and Infrastructure

Multiple node releases shipped across the month (v4.0.5 through v4.0.7), addressing post-fork performance optimizations, API key hash requirements, and exchange wallet configurations. kushti also announced work on a 10x faster ErgoTree interpreter for complex scripts, targeted for the v5.0 soft-fork. A fee estimation API was confirmed present in v4.0.3 but noted as untested and unused by applications — flagged as a community task. The ergonaut.space community wiki launched (glasgowm), auto-synced to GitHub, and rapidly became the primary reference for SigmaUSD mechanics.

### Ledger Hardware Wallet and Yoroi Mobile

Both remained undelivered at month's end. The Ledger integration — assigned to a community developer via a grant nearly a year prior — had not been delivered; the foundation announced engagement of professional developers. Yoroi mobile ERG support was on the Q1 2021 roadmap but no specific date was confirmed. Safari/iOS compatibility for sigmausd.io was confirmed broken due to missing WASM support in Apple's engine; Firefox on Android was confirmed working.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@cymatic, 2021-02-28): How is the reserve ratio calculated? My formula `(s_SigRSV * p_SigRSV) / (s_SigUSD * p_SigUSD) * 100` is always off by exactly 100 from the displayed value.
  **A** (@Chris): The correct formula is `(s_SigRSV * p_SigRSV + s_SigUSD * p_SigUSD) / (s_SigUSD * p_SigUSD) * 100`. The ERG used to mint SigmaUSD is itself part of the reserve and must be included in the numerator. Source: https://github.com/Emurgo/age-usd/blob/main/ageusd-headless/src/equations.rs

- **Q** (@David F, 2021-02-08): If I deposit 10,000 ERG as reserve and the price goes up 1000%, do I get 100,000 ERG back?
  **A** (community): No. SigmaRSV represents proportional ownership of the reserve (book value per share), not a price multiplier. Reserve providers can only redeem when reserves cover all SigmaUSD obligations multiplied by the minimum reserve ratio (400%). The actual return depends on reserve ratio at entry, fee accumulation, and ERG price at redemption.

- **Q** (@Klaus, 2021-02-25): Can I receive fewer ERG back than I deposited when redeeming SigmaRSV, ignoring fees?
  **A** (@glasgowm): Yes. SigmaRSV and SigmaUSD are distinct instruments. If ERG price has dropped since you minted SigmaRSV, you will receive fewer ERG on redemption. There are no forced liquidations in either case.

- **Q** (multiple users, 2021-02-28): Why did SigmaRSV value not increase when ERG price rose ~15%?
  **A** (@glasgowm): During the uncapped bootstrap period (until block ~450,000), the 800% reserve ratio ceiling is not enforced, so SigmaRSV is subject to dilution from new minting. The SigmaRSV/ERG exchange rate moves differently during bootstrap versus post-cap operation. Additional ERG in the reserve comes only from protocol fees, not from price appreciation.

- **Q** (@Damon AndTheSea, 2021-02-26): What happens if the reserve stays below 400% for an extended period — are funds permanently locked?
  **A** (@Armeanio): No. A below-400% lock creates the least-diluted SigmaRSV entry point, incentivizing new capital to enter and restore the ratio. The lock is always potentially recoverable — it is the protocol's alternative to forced liquidation, not equivalent to it.

- **Q** (@cymatic, 2021-02-27): What does the "$1.2 billion contract volume" figure on the explorer actually represent?
  **A** (@glasgowm): A UTXO accounting artifact. Each transaction appended to an existing UTXO inflates the "Total Received" figure — a $2M + $1 transaction results in $4M+ total received. Use the "Final Balance" field on the contract address to determine actual reserve size.

- **Q** (multiple users): Will Ergo support flash loans?
  **A** (@kushti): No flash loans found in Ergo; likely not possible due to the eUTXO model's fundamental differences from account-based chains.

- **Q** (@Lowell, 2021-02-17): What happens to spammers' transactions in the mempool if they are never picked up?
  **A** (@kushti): Transactions time out and are flushed from the mempool after a couple of hours if never included in a block. Node operators can also raise the minimum propagation fee rate via mempool filters to prevent low-fee spam from entering their mempool in the first place. A fee estimation API exists in node v4.0.3 but is not yet used by applications.

- **Q** (@mensrea, 2021-02-05): Will Ergo support flash loans?
  **A** (@kushti): No flash loans found in Ergo; likely not possible due to the eUTXO model.

- **Q** (multiple, 2021-02-15): What is the purpose of PoW versus PoS for Ergo?
  **A** (community): NIPoPoWs from genesis enable true light clients without trusting third-party services — something Cardano cannot provide natively. PoW also enables merge-mined sidechains with trustless pegging. Charles Hoskinson's discussion of aggregating computation capability under PoW: https://youtu.be/Y27Q3wL_Hko?t=207

- **Q** (@yernar, 2021-02-17): Getting "API key hash must be set" error when running ergo.jar.
  **A** (@kushti): Set the API key hash in your config using a Blake2b256 hash (tool: https://toolkitbay.com/tkb/tool/BLAKE2b_256). A default value will be set in v4.0.8.

- **Q** (@A_A_, 2021-02-13): If dApp users transact in SigmaUSD, do they still need ERG for transaction fees?
  **A** (community): Yes. All transaction fees on Ergo are paid in ERG regardless of which tokens are being transacted.

- **Q** (@ApophisRO, 2021-02-07): What percentage is premined or allocated to developers?
  **A** (@Orhan): Zero premine. The treasury receives ~10% of emission for the first two years (until approximately July 2021), then drops to ~4%. Cryptographic proof of no premine exists.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **eUTXO throughput / SigmaUSD concurrency:** The fundamental bottleneck causing minting failures at launch remains unresolved. Asynchronous eUTXO protocols are under research with no implementation timeline.

- **SigmaRSV post-bootstrap behavior:** The bootstrap period (no 800% cap) ends at block ~450,000 (~March 12). How SigmaRSV pricing and reserve ratio dynamics will change post-cap is not yet empirically understood by the community.

- **Cardano AgeUSD port — token relationship:** Whether Cardano's Plutus-based AgeUSD implementation creates entirely separate tokens or has any interoperability with Ergo SigmaUSD/SigmaRSV is unconfirmed.

- **Ledger hardware wallet support:** Grant recipient has not delivered after ~12 months; professional developers now engaged but no timeline given.

- **Yoroi mobile ERG support:** On Q1 2021 roadmap; no specific date confirmed.

- **Safari/iOS compatibility for sigmausd.io:** Broken due to missing WASM support; no fix timeline.

- **Merge-mined sidechain constructions:** kushti mentioned upcoming publication of sidechain designs with trustless pegging (improved vs. BIP-301) rewarding mainchain miners for delivering sidechain data. Details and timeline TBD.

- **ErgoScript bulletproof verification:** kushti working on implementing bulletproof verification in ErgoScript to enable zero-knowledge proofs for hybrid contracts. Technical feasibility and timeline still being explored.

- **Fee estimation API adoption:** Present in node v4.0.3 but untested and unused by wallets or dApps; flagged as a community task with no owner assigned.

- **Exchange listing strategy:** Debate unresolved between paying large fees for top-tier listings (Binance reportedly requires 5% of token supply) versus waiting for organic growth to attract free listings.

- **SigmaUSD utility without ErgoDEX:** Real stablecoin utility for miners and DeFi participants depends on a functioning DEX for ERG↔SigmaUSD liquidity without going through centralized exchanges. Spectrum DEX targeted for partial 2021 delivery.

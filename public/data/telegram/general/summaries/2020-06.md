# Ergo General — Monthly Developer Knowledge Base Summary
**Month:** June 2020

---

## Executive Summary

June 2020 was a pivotal month for the Ergo ecosystem, marked by the announcement of the Emurgo partnership, which directly expanded the 2020 roadmap to include a stablecoin and Yoroi wallet integration. Alongside this, significant protocol and tooling milestones were reached — including the release of ErgoMixer v1.0.0, the publication of the Oracle Pools specification, and the first successful mainnet test of distributed (threshold) signatures. A hard fork with four targeted improvements was proposed and discussed with the community.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Emurgo Partnership & Roadmap Expansion
Announced on June 9, the Emurgo partnership had immediate and lasting effects on the roadmap. Yoroi wallet (100K+ Cardano users) will add Ergo support along with a Metamask-like external dApp signing protocol — a significant UX milestone for dApp developers. Emurgo Research published the first output, a design patterns document for extended UTXO systems, and the Oracle Pools specification followed in week 26. The stablecoin was added to the 2020 roadmap as a direct result of the partnership; previously it had not been planned for that year. kushti stated that the full updated 2020 roadmap plus a 2021–23 sketch would be published after the Ergoversary.

### Oracle Pools Specification
The Oracle Pools design was jointly released by Emurgo and the Ergo Foundation in late June as a novel approach to decentralised oracles on the extended UTXO model. Oracle Pools use Ergo's native currency for stake, implement epoch-based scheduling, stake-slashing disincentives for misbehaving oracles, and hierarchical aggregation of oracle data. This was presented as addressing core limitations of systems like Chainlink by being natively on-chain and trustless. The spec was published on the Ergo forum and GitHub, and a detailed comparison with Chainlink's model was included. An Oracle Pool v1 instance was already being run by Ergo Foundation members, with Oracle Pool v2 planned to run for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards.

### ErgoMixer v1.0.0 Release
An anonymous developer released ErgoMixer v1.0.0 during week 25, featuring a browser-based UI (replacing the prior CLI), multiple ring support, auto-splitting, and token-based fees. A v1.0.1 update followed mid-week. Docker images were available for Linux; Windows and macOS binaries remained outstanding. Token mixing support was flagged as coming soon. Cypra wallet (AMA held during week 24) separately announced plans to integrate ErgoMix for Ergo alongside CoinJoin for Bitcoin, with ErgoMix mixing targeted for October–November 2020 after its basic MVP.

### Distributed (Threshold) Signatures
kushti reported successful mainnet testing of threshold signatures (EIP-11) during week 24. Unlike Bitcoin multisigs, these preserve zero-knowledge properties — blockchain observers cannot determine which parties signed. The first 2-of-3 protected funds were spent via API on mainnet. Documentation for wallet developers was noted as forthcoming. This was described as a foundational primitive enabling more advanced privacy and governance applications.

### Hard Fork Proposal
A hard fork was proposed during the week 26 community chat with four components: (1) improved memory-hardness of Autolykos PoW, (2) reduction of storage rent period from 4 years to 3 years, (3) switching from AIT to JIT costing in contract execution, and (4) undisclosed security fixes. End-user impact was described as minimal, with no token swap required. The timeline was dependent on miner voting; no firm date was set. kushti emphasised Ergo's community-driven governance model throughout the discussion.

### Extended UTXO Model as Competitive Advantage
Multiple discussions reinforced Ergo's position as the most mature extended UTXO smart contract platform. Cardano's Goguen (post-Shelley) will implement a similar model, but Ergo's community has 2+ years of practical experience from testnets since 2018. DeFi contract examples published on the forum included a tethered stablecoin, yAssets protocol (decentralised bonds), LETS, targeted microloans, and zero-interest loans. The eUTXO model was highlighted as enabling novel hybrid contracts, privacy protocols, and deterministic execution — properties not available on account-based chains.

### Network Growth & Exchange Activity
Hash rate progressed from approximately 6.52 TH/s at the start of the month to 7.79 TH/s (claimed ATH) by week 25, reflecting mining ecosystem growth. Ergo entered CoinGecko's top 300. Ergo won the Sistemkoin voting round, with ERG integration starting. The CoinsBit airdrop was delayed due to fraud filtering (~30% of addresses removed); payouts remained pending through month-end. Concerns about exchange data quality on Delta and Messari were flagged to the team.

### Block Speed & Throughput Discussion
A recurring thread across weeks 24–26 discussed potential speed improvements for Ergo. Paths considered included sub-blocks (microblocks), sidechains, and layer 2 solutions. kushti's view was that optimisation should be deferred until a concrete set of applications with known transaction profiles exists — optimising an empty chain is counterproductive. The discussion also surfaced a nuance: dApps tied to block height rather than timestamp would be affected by any block time reduction. A dedicated testnet for parameter tweaking was noted as available; kushti invited interested community members to lead experiments.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-06-02): Is Scala knowledge a prerequisite for learning ErgoScript?
  **A** (@scalahub / @kushti): Not mandatory. Two to three hours of basic Scala overview is helpful, but ErgoScript has its own syntax shared with many modern languages and can be learned independently.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-06-06): How can Ergo enable cross-chain token movement and mixing?
  **A** (@Unknown): Ergo has NIPoPoWs and can implement TxChain (https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/580), allowing blockchains to interact and trustlessly move tokens via wrapped tokens. ErgoMix requires multiple rounds so pre-mixing or alternative solutions are needed.

- **Q** (@Rich, 2020-06-09): Why such aggressive ERG distribution at launch rather than a constant 100M emission?
  **A** (@kushti): The emission schedule may be aggressive for current adoption levels, but security peaks around year 4 when applications create transaction load. Miners are stabilised via storage rent combined with block rewards — a stronger long-term model than Bitcoin's pure halving, which leaves miners with no fee income at year 8.

- **Q** (@Kookster, 2020-06-09): Does the Yoroi integration mean Ledger support?
  **A** (@kushti): Ledger support is coming separately. The Yoroi implication is larger — it delivers an external dApp signing protocol (the Metamask paradigm for Ergo) to 100K+ existing users.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-06-15): Will token mixing be integrated as standard ErgoMix in Cypra?
  **A** (@NC): Yes, CoinJoin for BTC and ErgoMix with token mixing for Ergo are both planned.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-06-15): What is an indexer in Cypra's architecture?
  **A** (@NC): A public node that scans BTC and Ergo blockchains, constructs BIP-158 filters required for wallet operation, and acts as a distributed bulletin board for atomic swaps (preventing DoS). Token mining incentivises indexer operation; operators earn transaction fees and token emission.

- **Q** (@scalahub, 2020-06-17): What distinguishes Ergo from other cryptocurrencies?
  **A** (@scalahub): Key features include the eUTXO model (not account-based), predicate scripts on full transactions and block headers, advanced ErgoScript, storage rent, custom tokens, data inputs, deterministic execution, pool-resistant Autolykos mining, Sigma protocols, and lightweight client support suitable for devices like a Raspberry Pi.

- **Q** (@b j, 2020-06-26): How is Ergo different from other chains? What does it solve?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo is a classic PoW cryptocurrency with unique features: powerful financial contracts in a strict Bitcoin-like model, Generalized Schnorr Proofs for cryptographic protocol applications (ErgoMixer as the first example), stateless clients, efficient SPV clients, and storage rent.

- **Q** (@Dreamweaver, 2020-06-26): Is Oracle Pools still at concept/research stage or is there a product ready?
  **A** (@scalahub): Ergo mainnet has been live for over a year. Oracle Pools are already under development, not merely conceptual.

- **Q** (@Franco, 2020-06-26): How many days to mine a block solo at ~40 MH/s?
  **A** (@kushti): Approximately 260 days at current difficulty. Better to wait for a mining pool.

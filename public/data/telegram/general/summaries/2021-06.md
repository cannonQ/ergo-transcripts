# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Developer Knowledge Base Summary
**Month:** 2021-06
**Chat:** Ergo General

---

## Executive Summary

June 2021 was dominated by three interconnected themes: the preparation and execution of ErgoHack (Ergo's first community hackathon), the imminent beta launch of Spectrum DEX (then called ErgoDex), and sustained technical debate around Oracle Pool security, scaling architecture, and Ergo's long-term PoW commitment. The month culminated in pre-Ergoversary momentum, with exchange listings bottlenecked on third-party exchange timelines and the ErgoDex UI as the final gate before public beta.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### ErgoHack — First Community Hackathon
ErgoHack launched June 11–12, 2021, as Ergo's first community hackathon, with core devs serving as mentors in Discord. Submissions included Raffle, Notarize, and stealth address tooling; one team (Shrimp) withdrew due to scope exceeding the 48-hour window. Results were announced June 20. The event was treated as experimental — a proof of concept for community-led development — and was considered successful enough to warrant future iterations. kushti confirmed the roadmap would be updated after the hackathon.

### Spectrum DEX (ErgoDex) Beta Launch Preparation
The Spectrum DEX backend was completed and heavily tested throughout June, with the UI remaining the primary bottleneck. Ilya Oskin confirmed the initial release would include a simple AMM with ERG/SigmaUSD and ERG/SigmaRSV pools, requiring Yoroi Nightly for early access due to ongoing dApp Connector work with Emurgo. The launch was consistently described as "late June/early July," contingent on Yoroi dApp Connector stability. Cardano native token trading was explicitly scoped for early October 2021, pending Plutus smart contract support. No governance token was included in the initial release; the question of whether one would ever be added remained open. Wrapped assets (wrappedETH, wrappedBTC) were confirmed as native Ergo tokens — no Ethereum-side gas fees for transfers within Ergo.

### Oracle Pool Architecture & Security
Significant debate arose around Oracle Pool v1 and v2 security models. Community members raised Sybil attack concerns — specifically whether a single adversarial actor could control >50% of pool oracles to corrupt data. Glasgow clarified that pools use hierarchical structures and game-theoretic disincentives via collateral and slashing, with trust scores based on oracle reputation as the primary defense. No cryptographic guarantee for off-chain data accuracy is possible by design; the security model relies on the economic cost of corrupting a supermajority. Oracle Pool v1 is run by Ergo Foundation members and others; Oracle Pool v2 was running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards. Palmyra (then ZenGate) announced use of Chainlink's proof-of-reserve functions for on-chain warehouse receipts, coupled with IoT sensors (cameras, weight/motion sensors), with an explicit plan to also build out native Ergo oracles using raised capital.

### Scaling Architecture & Block Time Discussion
kushti and others discussed the multi-layer scaling roadmap (L0/L1/L2) in detail. The key insight offered was that Ergo now has a concrete application set — SigmaUSD, Oracle Pools, Spectrum DEX, Trade House, lending contracts — which makes it feasible to reason about on-chain optimizations, layer 2 solutions, and faster confirmation protocols in a grounded way, unlike chains that speculate on sharding from day one with empty blocks. Sub-blocks and sidechains were both mentioned as candidates for exploration. Faster block times were flagged as non-trivial: many contracts and dApps depend on block height rather than timestamp, and increasing block speed without careful protocol review risks cascading security issues. A testnet for tweaking parameters (difficulty, block time) was noted as available; kushti invited interested community members to lead experimentation.

### Autolykos, Pool Resistance & Mining Decentralization
Autolykos v1 was originally designed with non-outsourceability to prevent mining pools, but a WTSC 2020 paper ("Bypassing Non-Outsourceable Proof-of-Work Schemes Using Collateralized Smart Contracts") demonstrated that smart contracts could bypass this. The feature was disabled to prevent only large actors from exploiting the loophole while smaller miners could not. The focus shifted to memory hardness to resist ASICs. Nanopool's share of hashrate (historically >50%, sometimes 60%) was a recurring community concern throughout the month. Decentralized smart pools were identified as the long-term solution but remained in the planning stage. kushti firmly rejected any move to PoS, citing CPU mining's susceptibility to botnets and Chinese datacenter concentration as reasons GPU PoW remains the right model.

### SigmaUSD Stability Mechanics
Several discussions explained how SigmaUSD maintains its peg under price stress. With a reserve ratio (RR) at ~337% during this period, the system has substantial backing; below 100% would mean insufficient collateral. Storage rent and the SigmaRSV mechanism incentivize RSV minting when ERG price drops, protecting the peg. SigmaUSD was confirmed as always redeemable at protocol level, meaning it is unlikely to trade below redemption cost on Spectrum DEX except in edge cases where the contract is temporarily locked.

### Storage Rent & Long-term Miner Incentives
Storage rent was explained as approximately 0.13 ERG per UTXO every 4 years — a low fee designed for UTXO set survivability and long-term miner revenue after block emission ends (~8 years). Implementation was noted as beginning before full emission ends to allow impact assessment. The model was contrasted with Bitcoin's unresolved long-tail security budget problem.

### Cross-Chain Interoperability (Gravity, SUSY, Rosen Bridge)
The Gravity protocol enables Ergo to port to connected blockchains (ETH, BSC, Waves, Fantom), transferring both ERG tokens and Oracle Pool data. SUSY (Gravity's atomic swap platform) supports ERG/ETH/Waves bridges and could bring cross-chain liquidity to Spectrum DEX. NIPoPoWs were cited as the foundational primitive enabling trustless sidechains and light-client verification across chains without downloading full block headers.

### Ergo–Cardano Relationship
The Ergo–Cardano complementarity was a recurring topic. kushti's prior role as an IOHK researcher and Emurgo's commercial partnership with Ergo underpinned the ErgoDex cross-chain roadmap. Charles Hoskinson described kushti as "the smartest person in blockchain" on the Lex Fridman podcast (~1:45:02), calling Ergo "the spiritual successor to Bitcoin." Community infographics posted on r/cardano gained 700+ upvotes before some were removed by moderators.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@J, 2021-06-18): Why did Ergo allow mining pools if Autolykos was originally designed to be pool-resistant?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Autolykos v1 had built-in non-outsourceability, but the WTSC 2020 paper "Bypassing Non-Outsourceable Proof-of-Work Schemes Using Collateralized Smart Contracts" showed smart contracts could bypass the mechanism. The feature was disabled to prevent only large players from exploiting the loophole. Decentralized smart pools are planned as the long-term solution.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-06-18): Is it accurate to say "Ergo can guarantee data accuracy" with its oracle solution?
  **A** (@Peppers CK / @Glasgow): No cryptographic guarantee is possible for off-chain data. Security relies on game-theoretic disincentives — collateral, slashing, trust scores, and hierarchical pool structures — plus the economic cost of bribing a supermajority of oracles.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-06-18): Can a single adversarial actor control >95% of oracles in a pool for a Sybil attack?
  **A** (@Glasgow / @Peppers CK): Theoretically possible in a naive pool design; mitigated by Oracle Pool v1/v2 hierarchical structures, reputation-based trust scores, and collateral-backed slashing. Flagged for further developer review.

- **Q** (@Kryptik, 2021-06-10): Will Ergo move toward CPU mining to reduce GPU e-waste?
  **A** (@kushti): No current plan. CPU mining invites botnets and Chinese datacenter dominance. Autolykos is GPU-optimized; ASICs are inevitable but acceptable. No policy change committed.

- **Q** (@Gazza, 2021-06-13): Will ERG/SigmaUSD trading on Spectrum DEX avoid SigmaUSD minting/redemption fees?
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin): No direct fee avoidance; LPs may maintain price spreads to compensate for protocol fees.

- **Q** (@Gazza, 2021-06-13): Will the initial AMM allow single-asset liquidity provision?
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin): No — pairing is required; single-asset provision is economically irrational (funds would be immediately arbitraged away).

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-06-21): How does Ergo's light client work — does it use state roots, witnesses, or stateless techniques?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Ergo uses NIPoPoWs for light clients (verifying blockchain events without downloading headers). UTXO set snapshots for quick bootstrapping are on the roadmap.

- **Q** (@Snowman Jason, 2021-06-25): What is the storage rent rate — is 0.13 ERG per block too high?
  **A** (@Glasgow): 0.13 ERG per 4 years per UTXO, not per block. Minimal impact on active users; designed for long-term UTXO set cleanup and miner revenue post-emission.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-06-18): How will Ergo maintain censorship resistance with only 3 active mining pools?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Noted as an important concern. Sigma protocols enable trustless LETS and other mechanisms for economic participation without CEX dependency; decentralized smart pools are the intended mining-layer solution.

- **Q** (@Kallo Shaman, 2021-06-19): Can DEXes work independently if oracles currently use CEX prices?
  **A** (@Glasgow / @A): DEXes would use DEX prices once sufficient volume exists. Current reliance on CEX prices is a bootstrapping choice — liquidity providers self-correct through arbitrage as on-chain markets deepen.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-06-25): What is Ergo's long-term miner incentive model after block emission ends?
  **A** (Community / @Glasgow): Two mechanisms: storage rent (demurrage ~0.13 ERG/UTXO/4yr) and transaction fees. Storage rent begins before full emission ends (~year 3 implementation, ~8 year emission horizon) to allow impact assessment. Contrasted favourably with Bitcoin's unresolved security budget problem.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-06-12): Why does Ergo's difficulty respond so quickly to hashrate spikes if recalculated at set intervals?
  **A** (Community): Epoch = 1024 blocks (~34 hours). Difficulty is based on recent epoch timing; if past epochs were mined faster than the 2-minute target, difficulty continues rising even if hashrate stabilises.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-06-21): What are the considerations around faster block times for Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Non-trivial. Many contracts and dApps use block height rather than timestamp; increasing block speed cascades into emission contract timing, security assumptions, and parameter dependencies. Requires careful protocol review. A testnet exists for community-led experimentation — interested contributors invited to DM.

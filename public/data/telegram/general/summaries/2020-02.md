# Ergo General — Monthly Summary: February 2020

---

## Executive Summary

February 2020 was characterised by Ergo's early infrastructure buildout: the testnet launched, the first mining pool entered testing, and the community focused heavily on exchange access, node setup, and GPU mining performance. Alongside these operational concerns, substantive technical discussions emerged around storage rent mechanics, Sigma protocol-based voting, eUTXO advantages, and Ergo's economic model — laying intellectual groundwork for the DeFi vision that would define later development.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### DeFi Vision & Positioning
Debate ran throughout February on how Ergo should position itself in the emerging DeFi landscape. Comparisons to THORChain, Kava, Cardano, and Ethereum PoS framed Ergo's distinctiveness: truly peer-to-peer, non-interactive smart contracts enabled by eUTXO and Sigma protocols were identified as the core differentiator. The argument that PoW enables non-custodial DeFi in ways PoS cannot was articulated repeatedly, with ERG's role as collateral in DeFi contracts cited as a demand driver. Community members acknowledged, however, that Ergo had not yet settled on a clear public narrative — "is it really mixing? do we do ICOs too and ride DeFi?" — and that marketing lagged significantly behind technical quality.

### Storage Rent Mechanics
The storage rent model received detailed attention in W07. The core mechanic was clarified: any box untouched for the storage period becomes spendable by anyone, provided the spender follows "spend to self" rules that preserve register contents, with the storage fee taken as incentive. This was distinguished from simple size-based fee markets and positioned as superior to 0-value output handling on other chains — creating a deflationary pressure and state-cleanup mechanism absent from Bitcoin or Ethereum. The mechanism was also referenced in W09 as a long-term answer to emission sustainability concerns.

### Mining: Pool Launch, GPU Performance & Decentralisation
The Ergopool.io mining pool entered testnet testing during W07, featuring a decentralised fund distribution model where pool operators do not control hash power directly — miners distribute earnings themselves. Ergo's miner distribution (~97% "other" pools) was highlighted as exceptionally decentralised compared to Bitcoin. GPU mining performance was discussed in W09, with a GTX 1080 Ti reporting 37.8411 MH/s on open-source miners; the closed-source miner at erg.ezpool.io was reported to achieve approximately 4× better hashrate, prompting concern from some community members that closed-source miner dominance undermined Ergo's original decentralisation goals.

### Exchange Access & Listings
HotBit withdrawal delays affected multiple users through mid-February but were resolved by February 19. Ergo was available on Waves Exchange, ViteX, Tidex, and HotBit with varying confirmation requirements (32–100 confirmations). Community interest in Binance, Bittrex, and Atomic Wallet integration was noted but no active pursuit confirmed. ViteX showed a newly-listed status but wide spreads due to low volume. A Vite Exchange AMA was announced. The Coinomat-operated Ergo gateway on Waves Exchange was clarified as now maintained by the Waves.Exchange team.

### Node Setup & Configuration
W09 saw community troubleshooting of full node deployment (version 3.2.1), including wallet access failures, config file setup, REST API binding, and systemd permission issues. The GitHub wiki was the primary reference. These discussions reflected a growing but technically varied user base beginning to self-host nodes.

### Economic Model & Emission
Debate in W09 examined whether Ergo's ~50 ERG/day emission schedule was economically sustainable absent large-scale adoption. kushti's position was that Ergo's new economic model, combined with storage rent as a deflationary mechanism, handled long-term incentives without requiring adoption to be a design constraint. Sceptics drew parallels to unresolved questions about Bitcoin's long-term security budget. No consensus was reached.

### Community Growth & Marketing
The @ErgoSocials channel launched in W09 as a community-driven news distribution mechanism for Twitter and Reddit. The main group crossed 1,000 members; a Bangladesh/India community group (@ergoplatform_bangla_hindi) already exceeded 1,100 users. Signature campaigns on Bitcointalk, ad banners, and retweet-with-giveaway strategies were discussed. The community broadly agreed that technical quality exceeded marketing investment, but no funded campaign was confirmed.

### Privacy-Preserving Voting with Sigma Protocols
A technical proposal in W06 outlined community voting using Sigma protocols with El-Gamal encryption: voting outcomes would be encrypted on-chain with minimal load, preserving voter privacy while enabling community-directed fund movement. This was flagged as a candidate for the 2021 roadmap.

### Scorex Framework & Ergo's Technical Lineage
W09 included a detailed historical discussion of Scorex, the modular blockchain framework kushti created during his time as a core NXT developer. Waves adopted an early Scorex prototype in 2016 but later diverged — rejecting light client support (feature conflicts) and Sigma protocols (regulatory concerns). Ergo subsequently adopted and extended Scorex. NIPoPoWs were mentioned as part of Ergo's protocol innovations built atop this lineage.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Jason Davies, 2020-02-W07): How does storage rent actually get collected?
  **A** (@Jason Davies): Once a box has not been spent for the storage period, anyone can spend it according to specific "spend to self" rules that preserve all register contents, taking the storage fee as incentive. This creates cleanup incentives while preserving data integrity.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-02-W07): If the fee market is based on transaction size, what is the inherent problem with 0-value outputs?
  **A** (@Jason Davies): In most blockchains, 0-value outputs still incur transaction fees, preventing spam. Ergo's storage rent model excludes 0-value outputs entirely, making it superior to size-only fee markets.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-02-W06): How does privacy-preserving community voting work in Ergo?
  **A** (@Unknown): Using Sigma protocols with El-Gamal encryption: the voting outcome is a value encrypted with El-Gamal, producing minimal on-chain load with privacy-preserving voting and the ability to move funds via community voting at potentially large scale.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-02-W07): Why is PoW preferable to PoS for DeFi on Ergo?
  **A** (@Jason Davies): PoW has well-tested security parameters. ERG can serve as collateral in DeFi contracts, creating demand pressure. Non-custodial, non-interactive DeFi is achievable on PoW in ways that are difficult on PoS systems.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-02-W07): What does eUTXO provide for payment networks?
  **A** (@Unknown): eUTXO enables simple and efficient payment solutions such as FastSwap/FairSwap protocols, which are more complex to implement on account-based models.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-02-W09): Did Waves reject Scorex? If so, why?
  **A** (@Unknown): Waves used an early Scorex prototype (from the NXT consensus era). Later, Waves rebuilt completely for its own needs and diverged from Scorex due to conflicts over light client support and rejection of Sigma protocols on regulatory grounds.

- **Q** (@NC, 2020-02-W09): Node 3.2.1 starts from command line but fails under systemd with "Failed to load key from wallet. Wallet is locked."
  **A** (@NC, resolved): The issue was a file permissions problem with the systemd service context, not a configuration error. Running the node under the correct user context resolved it.

- **Q** (@Vadim V, 2020-02-W09): GTX 1080 Ti getting 37.8411 MH/s — is this normal?
  **A** (@Unknown): Open-source miners significantly underperform; the closed-source miner at erg.ezpool.io achieves approximately 4× better hashrate.

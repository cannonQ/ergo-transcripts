# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: January 2024

---

## Executive Summary

January 2024 was a landmark month for the Ergo ecosystem, dominated by three converging themes: the live launch and expansion of Rosen Bridge cross-chain infrastructure, major protocol roadmap announcements (sub-blocks, merged-mined sidechains, Sigma v6.0), and a five-day ErgoSummit2024 virtual conference showcasing ecosystem depth across DeFi, zero-knowledge proofs, and scalability. Community energy was high despite persistent frustrations around CEX access and marketing gaps, with grassroots initiatives (Kraken listing fundraiser, Binance Feed articles, Twitter polls) filling the void.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Rosen Bridge: Live Operations & Expansion Roadmap
The ERG↔Cardano bridge launched operationally at app.rosen.tech early in the month and remained a central topic throughout January. Community discussion focused on liquidity depth (rsERG/ADA pool ~287k ADA TVL, rsRSN/ADA ~56k ADA TVL), permit/watcher bottlenecks (800 ERG + 30k RSN hardcoded per permit), and slippage trade-offs vs. direct DEX swaps. rsHOSKY was added as a wrapped token. The Ethereum bridge was confirmed in active development with a 4–6 month estimate if fully funded; Bitcoin bridge sequencing remained unclear. A manual TX refund of permits for a high-fee bridge incident (missed signature) was processed to GARD's DAO, with watchers' permits restored. SigmaFi rsADA lending (up to 200k rsADA at 10–20% APY) launched on DuckPools, with a duckDAO vote pending on accepting rsADA as ERG pool collateral.

### Protocol Roadmap: Sub-blocks, Sidechains & Sigma v6.0
kushti published a detailed 2024 core development roadmap covering sub-blocks (targeting ~1-second confirmation times at 128th mainchain difficulty), p2p optimisations, signature re-validation skip, RocksDB migration, and modularisation for sidechains. Sigma v6.0 implementation and JIT costing for Sigma Rust were highlighted as priority items. A dedicated ErgoSummit2024 presentation on merged-mined sidechains explained trustless pegging mechanics (superior to Bitcoin's BIP 301), on-chain emission contract incentives for mainchain miners delivering sidechain data, and Ergo-specific sidechain constructions planned for publication to the forum. Community debate continued on whether sub-blocks or layer 2 solutions represent the better throughput path, with kushti noting that a concrete set of live dApps (Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, Trade House, lending contracts) now provides the benchmarks needed to optimise meaningfully—something earlier chains lacked.

### ErgoSummit2024
A five-day virtual summit (January 15–19) featured presentations on bulletproofs and zero-knowledge protocols (kushti), merged-mined sidechains (kushti), order book DEXes and Trade House mechanics (qx()), Palmyra ecosystem and Chainlink Oracle integration (Dan Friedman), and the Gluon Gold/SigmaGold stablecoin design. All sessions were recorded on the Ergo YouTube channel. The summit doubled as the Ergoversary celebration marking Ergo's fifth year.

### kushti's Transition from Ergo Foundation
Alexander Chepurnoy announced his departure from Ergo Foundation management to focus full-time on protocol development, teaching, and research (ChainCash, contractual money theory, sidechain constructions). He confirmed continued core developer role with no protocol continuity concerns. Community sentiment was strongly positive; the announcement was framed as a healthy separation enabling deeper technical focus.

### Bulletproofs & Zero-Knowledge Protocols
kushti presented bulletproofs as trustless alternatives to SNARKs (no trusted setup required), usable for range proofs and generic statements. The key insight for Ergo: if bulletproof verification can be done in ErgoScript, then a wide variety of zero-knowledge protocols—including Groth one-out-of-many (used in an initial version of Zcoin) and verifiable credentials—become composable with Ergo contracts, enabling sensitive data to be proven without disclosure on-chain. Sigma protocols were identified as a specific related form.

### DeFi Ecosystem: SigmaUSD, DuckPools & Spectrum DEX
SigmaUSD reserve ratios and SigmaRSV mechanics were extensively discussed: SigRSV holders benefit from SigmaUSD minting demand and ERG price appreciation; redemption removes proportional ERG from treasury. DuckPools saw strong adoption (70k+ SigmaUSD locked, $5k additional inflow post-Binance article), with ~26% APY at observed rates. Spectrum Finance SPF farming pool was confirmed ending in February, transitioning to ERG/SigmaRSV farming. Order book DEX mechanics (Trade House, Auction House) were contrasted with AMM pools: full slippage transparency, no impermanent loss, partial order fills, peer-to-peer matching with no single upgrade key dependency.

### Palmyra & Chainlink Oracle Partnership
Dan Friedman detailed Palmyra's use of Chainlink proof-of-reserve functions via proxy for on-chain warehouse receipt issuance in decentralised trade financing. IoT devices (weight sensors, cameras) coupled with Chainlink oracles provide continuous proof-of-existence for commodities in bonded warehouses. Dan clarified this is not zero-sum with Ergo Oracle Pools—the plan includes building out native Oracle Pool v1 and v2 infrastructure using capital from the Palmyra token sale, with Dexy Gold running on Oracle Pool v2 with GORT rewards already operational. The broader point: Palmyra is Ergo-native but not Ergo-exclusive, and ecosystem-level tribalism about tool choices was cautioned against.

### CEX Access & Grassroots Marketing
ERG's absence from Binance, Kraken, and Coinbase remained a recurring frustration. A community-led Kraken listing fundraiser was launched (Telegram: t.me/ErgoKrakenFundraiser) targeting ~$500k via 1% community donations, though exact Kraken requirements remained exploratory via Cardano Foundation intermediation. Binance Feed articles on DuckPools and Ergo topics were published for 25–50 USDT, generating measurable on-chain inflow. Sigmanauts tested Twitter ads and explored market maker partnerships. A Twitter L1 bracket poll (Ergo vs. Kadena finals) mobilised ~1.5k community votes; Ergo was declared winner, with bot accusations on both sides treated as secondary to the awareness value.

### Node Operations & Tooling
Ergo node v5.0.17 introduced a reindex bug (fixed in v5.0.18 without requiring re-reindex). Docker Desktop on Mac caused database corruption (SST file loss); native binary recommended. Deleting `history/extra` folder was the resolution for extra index issues without full rescan. Watcher UI v2.2.1 released. A community-redesigned Ergo Explorer launched at ergexplorer.com (built by Aco/Satergo). ErgoNames subnaming system announced: recursive AVL tree registry enabling unlimited subname nesting (e.g., sigma.noah.crux.erg) with wallet sign-off at each level.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Vinicius, 2024-01-08): Node running v5.0.18 with `extraIndex=true` throwing "Ask timed out" and "Readers are not initialized" errors despite multiple resyncs. Running inside Docker Desktop on Mac.
  **A** (@Glasgow / @Pulsarz): v5.0.17 introduced a reindex bug; v5.0.18 fixed it without requiring a full re-reindex. Docker Desktop on Mac causes SST file loss during sync—run the node outside Docker as a native binary. To resolve extra index issues without full rescan, delete the `history/extra` folder (back up `history` first).

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-01-05): Current throughput is ~12k TXs/day—how much headroom exists at full blocks?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Approximately 1M TXs/day at full blocks; block size and other parameters are adjustable as needed.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-01-05): Sub-blocks use 128th difficulty for 1-second blocks. Why 128th rather than 120th?
  **A** (Community): 128th is a conservative estimate based on sub-block propagation speed. 1-second confirmation is feasible for dApps needing fast finality (e.g., BlitzTCG) where low-value assets reduce reorg attack incentive.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2024-01-01): Is there a DEX design that eliminates impermanent loss entirely?
  **A** (@Rj): IL only occurs when asset prices diverge. Any exchange requires balanced value transfer—no trustless solution exists without external mechanisms such as oracle-based rebalancing or over-collateralisation.

- **Q** (Community, 2024-01-17): How do you buy 100k ERG using ADA via Rosen Bridge?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Two routes: (1) swap ADA→rsERG on a Cardano DEX, then redeem rsERG→ERG via Rosen; (2) convert ADA→rsADA via Rosen, then swap rsADA→ERG on Spectrum DEX. Best route depends on current DEX liquidity depth.

- **Q** (@Me, 2024-01-18): How do stealth addresses work—are funds mixed before arrival?
  **A** (@Glasgow / @kushti / @qx()): Stealth addresses generate a one-time address unlinkable to the recipient's public key. Funds are not mixed—each payment simply arrives at a fresh address. Critically: do not transfer stealth-received funds to any previously-used address, or the unlinkability is broken.

- **Q** (@Rj, 2024-01-18): Is a sidechain the same as a sub-block?
  **A** (@kushti): No. Sub-blocks and sidechains are distinct scaling approaches. The sidechains video does not address sub-blocks.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-01-15): Can normal ERG holders stake RSN for watcher rewards?
  **A** (Community): Lending RSN on DuckPools is currently the closest option, though yields are small. Rosen tokenomics include staking rewards expected to be implemented in future.

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2024-01-14): How to generate Ergo seed phrases and addresses fully offline?
  **A** (@Koutelier): Use https://ergopaperwallet.org — the repository can be forked and run locally without internet access.

- **Q** (Community, 2024-01-17): What is the slippage comparison between Rosen Bridge, Spectrum Simple Swap, and a CEX for a 5k ERG swap?
  **A** (@qx()): Spectrum Simple Swap showed ~4% slippage on a 5k ERG test. Rosen Bridge removes LP-depth slippage dependency but charges ~0.5% bridge fee. For large-value asset movement without slippage, Rosen is preferable; for direct token swaps, Simple Swap or a CEX may be preferable depending on spread.

- **Q** (@Phillip Scheindlinger, 2024-01-27): How many years before UTXO consolidation is required due to storage rent?
  **A** (@Rj): Approximately every 4 years, depending on transaction frequency. The TokenJay app in Ergo Mobile Wallet shows the exact timeline for a given UTXO set.

- **Q** (Community, ErgoSummit2024): What are bulletproofs and how do they relate to Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a trustless zero-knowledge technique (no trusted setup, unlike SNARKs) originally used in Monero for range proofs but generalisable to arbitrary statements. If bulletproof verification can be implemented in ErgoScript, it opens composability with Ergo contracts for privacy-preserving applications—proving sensitive data meets conditions without revealing it on-chain. Groth one-out-of-many proofs (used in early Zcoin) are a related construction already usable in this framework.

- **Q** (Community, ErgoSummit2024): Why is now the right time to optimise Ergo's throughput?
  **A** (@kushti): Other blockchains have pursued sharding and layer 2 solutions from day one on empty chains, making it impossible to optimise for real workloads. Ergo now has a concrete set of live contracts—Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, Trade House, lending contracts—with similar design patterns, making genuine optimisation tractable for the first time.

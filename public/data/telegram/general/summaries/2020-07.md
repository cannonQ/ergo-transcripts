# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: 2020-07

---

## Executive Summary

July 2020 was a pivotal month for Ergo's ecosystem development, marked by the launch of Oracle Pools research and specification, a significant exchange listing on CoinEx, and rapid progress across the full DeFi stack including DEX contracts, the ErgoMix mixer, and stablecoin groundwork. Technical development accelerated on multiple fronts simultaneously — version 3.3.0, the 4.0 hard fork, and Yoroi/Ledger wallet integrations — while the Ergo Foundation began restructuring and the community grappled with marketing coordination, treasury sustainability, and exchange listing strategy.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Oracle Pools & Emurgo Partnership
The Oracle Pools initiative was the single most significant development theme of July. The EMURGO research team (Robert Kornacki) published a new UTXO-based oracle model combining lessons from existing projects, culminating in a formal Oracle Pools smart contract specification following EIP-6 standard, covering epoch mechanics, state machines, and governance-slash-pool design. Two versions were under development: Oracle Pool v1 (run by EF members and others) and Oracle Pool v2 (targeting Dexy Gold with GORT rewards). kushti confirmed that Oracle Pools must launch before any stablecoin design can be published, and that capital from the token sale may be invested to build out native Ergo Oracle infrastructure and tooling. The community noted that, unlike Chainlink, Oracle Pools do not require a separate token — ERG is used for collateral and slash stakes.

### Version 3.3.0 and 4.0 Hard Fork
Version 3.3.0 — featuring a massive wallet rework and external application (scan) support — was completed and tested through the month, with release slated for early August. Simultaneously, the 4.0 hard fork reached near-final state, incorporating more memory-hard Autolykos PoW, security fixes, and a contractual layer soft-fork improvement path. A significant discovery was made late in the month: Ergo's Merkle tree construction is suitable for compact blocks (analogous to Bitcoin's BIP for L0 scalability), offering an alternative to the current block propagation design. ProgPoW remained the community favourite for algorithm direction, pending an EIP from Jason and subsequent miner voting.

### Exchange Listings and Market Infrastructure
The month culminated in the CoinEx listing (July 28), which attracted 254K+ ERG deposits within hours and included a 35,000 ERG promotional trading event. Earlier in the month, discussion focused on a strategy of targeting approximately one tier-2 exchange listing per month (CoinEx, MXC, BiBox considered; CoinEx won community vote) while pursuing tier-1 exchanges (Binance, Huobi, OKEx) through non-fee-based routes requiring community visibility and voting. Citex was flagged as unreliable (wallet closed, fake volumes, unresponsive staff) and was temporarily removed from the official exchange listing page along with P2PB2B.

### Wallet Ecosystem and Ledger Support
The wallet landscape expanded through July. Ledger hardware wallet support was confirmed as funded by the Ergo Foundation with a 2020 delivery target. Yoroi integration was confirmed via the EMURGO partnership, with Daedalus to follow. Multiple independent wallet projects were underway including Cypra.io and a wallet featuring inbuilt node and NIPoPoWs-based bootstrapping. A rounding bug in the ergo-js JavaScript library was identified in Magnum Wallet, with a workaround documented. BIP-44 support was discussed for backward compatibility across wallet standards.

### Ergo Foundation Restructuring and Treasury Sustainability
The Ergo Foundation proposed restructuring with active contributors as members and irregular contributors receiving monthly rewards. Candidates nominated for the first wave included @bobertk (EMURGO representative), @filippounits, and @mhs_sam. The development treasury was estimated to last approximately 18 months at a monthly operational cost of $10–20K USD, with post-fund sustainability options discussed including crowdfunding, Moloch-style DAOs, community investment cooperatives, consulting, and protocol-funded research (e.g., the mixer already earning ERG). A Smart Paper educational initiative (interactive multimedia content modelled on nipopows.com) was also approved.

### Autolykos, Mining, and PoW Memory-Hardness
Mining centralization concerns around Autolykos were a recurring topic, particularly following the emergence of HBM FPGAs (Osprey Mining ECU50) as competitive with GPUs. The community debated whether to implement memory-hardness fixes and how to prevent one large actor from dominating hashrate. A consensus formed that private pools cannot be forbidden algorithmically, and that the upcoming hard fork would signal the memory-hardness improvement. Miner behaviour analysis showed approximately 20K ERG daily withdrawals from two major miners, with some accumulation ahead of exchange listings.

### DEX Contracts and DeFi Stack
DEX contracts were completed and published in the ergoplatform/ergo-contracts repository by mid-month, with matcher testing to follow. ErgoMix was confirmed deployed on mainnet (Docker image available), developed by anonymous contributors. The stablecoin design — incorporating a storage rent/demurrage model with depreciating value and rewards to creators rather than charges — was endorsed by kushti. The privacy-preserving DeFi thesis crystallised: Ergo's niche is anonymous DeFi via Sigma protocols, enabling privacy-preserving voting, auctions, mixing, and on-chain credentials.

### Explorer Infrastructure and DDoS Attack
The Explorer experienced a significant infrastructure crisis in Week 30: a UTXO data loss bug required traffic switching to a replica node, followed shortly after by a DDoS attack primarily from Russian IPs. Responses included API blocking and rate-limiting improvements. kushti characterised the attack as "amateurish" but noted it was preparation for potentially harder attacks.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Ollie, 2020-07-01): For a $15K+ buy order, should I use limit orders to avoid slippage on Tidex?
  **A** (@Kookster): Do not market buy. A 150K ERG order was filled on Tidex earlier in the week; suggest 2300–2500 sat range. Limit orders will fill within 24 hours.

- **Q** (@SRV, 2020-07-04): Is TPS a bottleneck for Ergo smart contracts? How many contracts can execute per block?
  **A** (@kushti): TPS already raised +80% by miners during year one. Offchain protocols can resolve contractual agreements faster. Average block time: 2 minutes.

- **Q** (2020-07-04): Do we have a disassembler equivalent for ErgoTree?
  **A** (@kushti): No convenient one exists yet. Current method: parse + pretty-print (recently completed), then inspect manually.

- **Q** (@Steph, 2020-07-14): How is it possible to know if a coin is lost?
  **A**: Storage rent takes care of lost coins naturally — they are returned to circulation over time.

- **Q** (@cyw0rx, 2020-07-14): Can CoinsBit exchange be trusted for buying ERG?
  **A** (@kushti): Citex currently has issues — Ergo wallet is closed, staff unreachable, volumes are fake. Recommended exchanges are ProBit, HotBit, Tidex, Waves.Exchange, CoinsBit, with most liquidity at ProBit.

- **Q** (@Steph, 2020-07-16): How many TPS can Ergo do?
  **A** (@kushti): TPS depends on transaction weight. Mixer and DEX transactions are heavy; simple transfers are light. Can send to 100 addresses in one transaction. TPS up 100% during first year since mainnet launch; miners vote to increase computational limits per block.

- **Q** (2020-07-16): How can Ergo do something Cardano won't be able to for crowdfunding, oracles, DEXes, stablecoins, auctions?
  **A**: Ergo has Sigma protocols enabling privacy-preserving smart contracts (e.g., ErgoMix already deployed on mainnet); NIPoPoWs allow cross-chain interactivity. Cardano and Ergo will be complementary.

- **Q** (@kushti, 2020-07-16): What is the Magnum Wallet bug affecting certain amounts?
  **A** (@kushti): Magnum has a rounding bug in its JavaScript library with amounts like "1.15" (same issue was in the node panel, now fixed). Workaround: change the amount slightly. Also, sending before a previous transaction confirms may fail because ergo-js does not support spending outputs of unconfirmed transactions.

- **Q** (2020-07-20): Does an additional ERC-20-style token complicate Oracle Pools?
  **A**: ERG is used for collateral and slash stake. A Chainlink-style token adds cost and complexity with zero additional value.

- **Q** (@cypheryield, 2020-07-20): How does collateral lockup yield work in PoW (vs. PoS like Waves Neutrino)?
  **A**: Collateral providers bet on asset appreciation; gains are absorbed by the collateral position and losses are buffered; stablecoin holders assume a fixed collateral ratio.

- **Q** (2020-07-20): Are atomic swaps possible on Ergo?
  **A**: Atomic exchange is built into the core for tokens; a full DEX is very possible and can be made privacy-preserving.

- **Q** (2020-07-20): Why the DDoS attack on the Explorer?
  **A** (@kushti): Amateurish — sending API requests through proxies; likely preparation for harder attacks.

- **Q** (@Velvia, 2020-07-23): Getting an error when creating a wallet in Windows.
  **A** (@kushti): Use Oracle JDK >= 9 or OpenJDK to avoid strong crypto licence issues.

- **Q** (@Velvia, 2020-07-23): Can I restore an Ergo wallet mnemonic seed in Yoroi?
  **A**: Initial release uses BIP-44 external chain only for backward compatibility; no import/export standard yet, but Yoroi can add support if a standard is developed.

- **Q** (@Steph, 2020-07-17): What happens after 2.5 years when treasury funds run out?
  **A** (@kushti): The fund will likely still exist due to extra funds already present. Crowdfunding is on the 2020 roadmap. Standard OSS funding models apply: consulting, sponsorships, contract-based pools. The mixer is already earning ERG as an example of self-sustaining protocol infrastructure.

- **Q** (2020-07-28): Will ERG be compatible with Daedalus or Yoroi first?
  **A** (@Kookster): Yoroi integration is proceeding first since EMURGO is leading the integration work.

- **Q** (@Marco, 2020-07-08): Node returning `previousFullHeaderId: null`, `fullHeight: null`, `headersHeight: null`.
  **A**: Node hasn't started syncing; check the config file. Synchronisation is complete when `fullHeight` and `headersHeight` match at `http://127.0.0.1:9053/swagger#/info/getNodeInfo`. Change log level to INFO with Java flag `-Dlogback.stdout.level=INFO` to reduce log file size.

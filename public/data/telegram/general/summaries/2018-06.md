# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General
**Month:** 2018-06

---

## Executive Summary

June 2018 was dominated by foundational tokenomics discussions — clarifying EFYT supply, the ERG emission curve, and the EFYT→ERG swap mechanics — alongside preparation for and execution of a community Q&A session on June 14th. The month also saw substantive technical discussion of Ergo's core architectural differentiators: storage rent as a post-emission security model, lightweight full-node validation via UTXO transformation proofs, the dual Σ-Cash/Σ-Data chain design, and the shift away from Equihash toward a proof-of-data-possession PoW algorithm before Testnet2.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EFYT Supply, Burn, and ERG Swap Mechanics
Community members raised persistent questions about token supply figures, which were complicated by inaccurate data on CoinMarketCap. The team clarified that 3,029,000 EFYT were burned (burn tx: `BLruST2C4jAhkzTMZZwe8YASovVGPtqR5pqfrVzbiGHt`) to align the remaining supply with Ergo's emission curve, leaving a max of ~1.97M EFYT in circulation. The confirmed swap ratio is 1 EFYT = 1 ERG + 1 ERGOdata, occurring during the first year of mainnet. Total ERG supply is 100M over 8 years, plus a separate 100M ERGOdata — not a shared pool. Founders receive 10% of first-year mined coins (~1.971M ERG) for foundation development; circulating supply at mainnet launch will be 0, identical to Bitcoin's genesis.

### Emission Curve, Early Inflation, and Economic Model
Several community members expressed concern about steep early-stage inflation and insufficient incentives for EFYT holders beyond the swap. The team acknowledged the concern and confirmed that an economic model paper with demand-scenario simulations was in development, targeting publication around September 2018 alongside the Yellow Paper specification. kushti confirmed the 1:1 EFYT→ERG ratio is fixed and non-negotiable. The long-term post-emission security model relies on two miner income streams: **storage rent** (per the FC'18 paper) and optional fees for script execution cost estimation — intentionally analogous to Ethereum's transition away from pure block rewards.

### PoW Algorithm and ASIC Resistance
Ergo was running Equihash during TestNet1, but the team confirmed a switch to a new PoW algorithm based on proof-of-data-possession research (arxiv.org/pdf/1603.07926.pdf) before Testnet2, with pooling/outsourceability resistance as the primary design goal. ASIC resistance remains a stated objective but was candidly described as an ongoing arms race without formal security guarantees. The ZenCash Equihash private-block-injection attack (38 blocks) was discussed as a known threat vector; the team stated awareness and commitment to staying ahead of similar attacks.

### Dual Chain Architecture (Σ-Cash & Σ-Data)
A recurring source of confusion was resolved: Ergo is **not** pursuing a PoW+PoS hybrid. Both the Σ-Cash and Σ-Data chains use PoW. They share the same underlying technology, support sidechains, and use different parameters suited to their respective use cases — Σ-Cash for monetary transactions, Σ-Data for data-intensive applications. Σ-Cash can be released conditionally on events in Σ-Data, enabling cross-chain programmability. This is distinct from kushti's separate TwinsCoin research.

### Light Client Architecture and Node Incentives
The team described two full-node operating modes: a Bitcoin-like mode storing the UTXO set (required for miners) and a lightweight mode that verifies proofs of UTXO transformations (per eprint.iacr.org/2016/994), enabling users to "be their own bank" on consumer hardware without mining. Only miners receive direct protocol-level incentives; light-node operators benefit through sovereignty rather than token rewards. Bitcoin-NG was explicitly rejected as adding protocol complexity without sufficient long-term benefit. Second, third, and fourth-layer protocols and sidechains are supported via ErgoScript flexibility but not mandated at the base layer.

### Governance Model
A three-layer governance structure was outlined: (1) miner voting on protocol parameters (block size, computational cost limits, etc.) per 256-block epoch; (2) soft-fork compatibility mechanisms to avoid hard forks; (3) Foundation-organized polls across developer, miner, stakeholder, business, and user representative groups. Tens of parameters are intended to be exposed to miner adjustment over time.

### Testnet Participation and Node Operations
TestNet1 (~60% of final environment) was running across the month with connectivity instability reported around June 12th. Community members successfully set up custom-configuration test nodes using `application.conf` and a well-known peers list. The current testnet version lacked wallet functionality, preventing community members from generating transactions independently. TestNet2 (95–100% of final environment) was described as the final phase before mainnet. Mining capability was confirmed as coming later in 2018. Bug reports from testnet participation may earn rewards; testnet coins do not convert to mainnet ERG.

### NIPoPoWs and Academic Foundation
The team consistently emphasized Ergo's scientific rigor. NIPoPoWs (nipopows.com) was highlighted as foundational unpublished research underpinning the protocol. The Yellow Paper specification was targeted for September 2018. Scorex — the framework underlying Ergo — was clarified as a project created and maintained by the Ergo team, not merely adopted from elsewhere.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-06-07): What is the circulating supply at mainnet launch?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Circulating supply will be 0 at mainnet launch, identical to Bitcoin. All coins are mined according to the published emission curve. Founders receive 10% of first-year mined coins (~1.971M ERG) for foundation development.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-06-07): Will EFYT holders receive a swap to mainnet coins?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Yes — 1 EFYT = 1 ERG + 1 ERGOdata. The swap occurs during the first year of mainnet operation.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-06-07): Will coins mined on testnet convert to real ERG?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): No. However, significant bug discoveries may be rewarded.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-06-14): What incentives exist for miners after all ERG is emitted?
  **A** (@Dmitry, translating kushti): Miners transition to two income sources: (1) **storage rent** collected from dormant UTXOs (per the FC'18 storage rent paper), and (2) optional fees for script execution cost estimation. These are the intended long-term replacements for block rewards.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-06-14): What are the incentives for running a full node without mining?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Only miners receive direct protocol-level incentives. The lightweight full-node mode — which verifies proofs of UTXO transformations rather than storing the full UTXO set — enables users to "be their own bank" on consumer hardware. The incentive is sovereignty, not token rewards.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-06-14): Is Ergo planning hyper-inflationary emission? How is this consistent with a sound money goal?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Hyper-inflation only occurs with constant demand. Economic simulations modelling demand scenarios are underway; the full economic model paper is in development.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-06-14): Are you aware of the ZenCash Equihash vulnerability? Will Ergo be ASIC-resistant?
  **A** (@Dmitry): The team is aware. Before Testnet2, Ergo will switch from Equihash to a PoW algorithm based on proof-of-data-possession research, with pooling/outsourceability resistance as the primary goal. ASIC resistance remains a stated objective but is an ongoing arms race without formal security guarantees.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-06-14): Why was Bitcoin-NG rejected? Do you support second-layer protocols?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Bitcoin-NG adds protocol complexity without solving sufficient long-term problems. Second-layer solutions are not silver bullets. ErgoScript's flexibility allows the community to experiment with second, third, and fourth-layer protocols and sidechains without mandating them at the base layer.

- **Q** (@Waylay, 2018-06-22): Is Ergo pursuing twin PoW+PoS chains?
  **A** (@Unknown): No. TwinsCoin is separate research co-authored by kushti. Both Σ-Cash and Σ-Data will use PoW. They share the same underlying technology, support sidechains, and differ in parameters suited to their respective use cases.

- **Q** (@Kirill, 2018-06-28): How do I launch a test node with non-default parameters?
  **A** (@ponte): Reference `application.conf` in `/src/main/resources/application.conf` in the repository, and consult the Node Configuration File wiki on GitHub.

- **Q** (@ponte, 2018-06-28): Unable to connect to well-known peers — what is the issue?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ / @ponte, resolved): Ensure clean installation with all dependencies updated; provide logs if the error persists. Issue was subsequently resolved by using an updated well-known peers list.

- **Economic Model Publication**: Full paper with demand-scenario simulations targeting September 2018; details of long-term viability of storage rent + execution fees as miner income remain unconfirmed.

- **ASIC Resistance Guarantees**: No formally secure ASIC-resistant algorithm confirmed; acknowledged as an ongoing arms race.

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Month:** March 2024

---

## Executive Summary

March 2024 was dominated by three converging themes: protocol-level security and upgrade discussions (SigmaUSD minting limits in response to the BearWhale attack, Gluon Gold testnet launch, Rosen Bridge v2 migration), ecosystem infrastructure expansion (Sigmanauts Mining Pool launch, Nautilus/Minotaur wallet releases, MEXC listing progress), and broader strategic debates around marketing, community fragmentation, and the role of decentralized AI. The month saw significant developer momentum with multiple simultaneous launches and testnet deployments, while the community grappled with governance questions around update keys, oracle design, and how to position Ergo against competing ecosystems.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### SigmaUSD BearWhale Attack & Protocol Response

A coordinated attack on SigmaUSD was identified and analysed across W10–W12. The BearWhale's strategy involved minting large SigmaUSD quantities when the reserve ratio was high, dumping ERG on CEXes to suppress price, exploiting the lagging oracle to redeem at profit. Community wallet tracking identified approximately 1.7M ERG spread across multiple addresses. kushti proposed a 0.5% of reserves per oracle epoch minting cap as a mitigation measure, with community debate about whether 0.25% would be preferable. The update key holder situation added complexity: DarkSide was asked to send keys to kushti "asap." Separately, Armeanio proposed either capping mints or saving changes for a larger upgrade, and expressed willingness to eventually burn the update key. No final consensus on the optimal cap percentage was reached by month end.

### Gluon Gold (Dexy Gold) Testnet Launch

Gluon Gold moved from internal testing to public testnet during W12, with the live interface at gluon.gold requiring a Nautilus Testnet wallet. Bruno confirmed the protocol will be **fully immutable from day 0 with no update keys**, a deliberate contrast to SigmaUSD's governance situation. GORT buyback contracts were already operational for fee distribution. The two-oracle approach (Shu model using a moving average or Kalman filter) was under consideration but not finalised. Bruno demonstrated the system at Backdrop Build v3. Discussion also covered the broader landscape of Djed-variant implementations: SigmaUSD (Ergo), Djed on Cardano (COTI), Milkomeda Osiris, and Zephyr — with SigmaUSD noted as the most successful deployment. Zephyr's dual-oracle approach (latest price + moving average) was contrasted with Ergo's min/max model.

### Rosen Bridge v2 Migration

The Rosen Bridge underwent a significant v2 upgrade during W11. Guards blocked new event triggers during migration; users experienced 5–6 hour processing delays. Watchers were required to follow an unlock→update→lock procedure, with explicit warnings not to perform watcher actions mid-migration. A post-migration issue surfaced involving a slashed permit from an accidental high-fee transaction — a manual TX was executed to GARD's DAO to refund affected users, though a missed signature delayed resolution. Community discussion also explored Rosen Bridge's potential as a cross-chain data layer (not just transaction events), with Joe Armeanio cited as building toward this broader vision. The bridge's trustless, decentralised design was defended against comparisons to Wormhole's more centralised architecture.

### Sigmanauts Mining Pool Launch

The Sigmanauts Mining Pool (pool.ergo-sig-mining.net) launched in W11 with PPLN payouts, a 1% fee directed to the treasury, storage rent collection in development, and DAO governance via Paideia planned. A Docker-based self-hosting option for the UI was made available. The launch represented a concrete step toward community-owned mining infrastructure, complementing the broader Sigmanauts governance work.

### Wallet Ecosystem Updates

Two significant wallet releases shipped in W11: Minotaur v2 Release Candidate (multisig, token minting/burning, SigmaUSD, ErgoPay, dynamic dApp setup) and progress on Nautilus multi-platform support (Mac/OSX/Win/Android/iOS). Community discussion clarified that Ledger native integration remains in dev mode only. A dust-attack victim with 7,000+ unspent boxes prompted detailed community guidance on UTXO consolidation — empirically, 2,000 boxes per transaction at 0.00005 ERG per box was found optimal. Paper Wallet was confirmed to generate 24-word seeds compatible with Minotaur.

### MEXC Exchange Listing

Integration with MEXC was funded by the Ergo Foundation before the community raffle completed, with hummingbot testing underway for market-making support. The standard integration timeline is 2–3 months (Alephium precedent); internal estimates pointed to late March or early April, though "2 weeks" became a running community joke. No official listing date was announced by month end. The community also discussed the broader strategy of market making, with Sigmanauts vetting MM candidates and debate about professional MM involvement versus organic liquidity.

### Block Speed, Sub-blocks & Sidechains

A recurring thread across all four weeks concerned network performance improvements. Discussion covered sub-blocks, faster block times, and merge-mined sidechains. kushti noted that block height-based smart contracts would be affected by any block time change and that worst-case security scenarios were factored into Ergo's original design. A testnet (6.0) was identified as available for community experimentation on block time parameters, and kushti invited interested community members to lead a testnet working group. The trustless pegging model for sidechains was compared favourably to BIP 301, with mainchain miners rewarded for delivering sidechain data.

### Trade House & Order Book DEX Architecture

qx() provided extended advocacy for Trade House's order book model over AMM-based DEXes. Key advantages highlighted: full depth and liquidity transparency, complete slippage control, no impermanent loss, partial order fills, peer-to-peer matching, and no single central contract (each order lives on-chain independently). The model was contrasted with Serum/FTX's history as a cautionary tale about upgrade keys in order book DEXes. Sharing liquidity between Trade House orders and Spectrum pools was discussed as a future possibility.

### ChainCash Development

kushti announced ChainCash publicly via a video release in W12 and established a public Telegram group (t.me/chaincashtalks) in W13. The project aims to solve money supply inelasticity by enabling note issuance based on collateral, trust, and reputation. Server-side development was ongoing with Rust developers being invited to contribute. Community members explored use cases including time banks (tracking hours rather than money) and AI-assisted note management.

### Marketing, Community Fragmentation & AI Integration

W13 surfaced ongoing tensions between organic growth and aggressive marketing strategies, and whether siloed project channels fragment the main community. The introduction of airdrop mechanics (referencing BNB, Solana, and GATE precedents) was proposed as an engagement driver. AI integration on Ergo generated discussion around automation for SigmaFi bond selection, ChainCash note management, and trade bots — with consensus that "crypto + AI" lacks a clear killer use case but that Ergo's infrastructure is well-suited to support it. kushti's forum post on Artificial Economic Intelligence was referenced as a foundational framing document.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2024-03-12): What are bulletproofs and how could they be used in Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge technique for proving specific statements (e.g. range proofs) without a trusted setup, unlike SNARKs. Verification is possible in ErgoScript. Related proofs like Groth one-out-of-many could support verifiable credentials and hybrid contracts combining zero-knowledge proofs with on-chain execution — opening a whole new design space for privacy-preserving contracts that avoid data disclosure while still being verified on-chain.

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2024-03-13): Why isn't SigmaRSV a reliable HODL if SigmaUSD adoption increases and ERG appreciates?
  **A** (@TMR.ΣRG, @CW): SigmaRSV is PvP — the reserve can be drained if SigmaUSD holders exit at profit. The BearWhale exploited this by minting maximum SigmaUSD when the reserve ratio was high, dumping ERG on CEXes to suppress price, then exploiting the lagging oracle to redeem at profit.

- **Q** (@Hasnain, 2024-03-08): Why can't exchanges use sub-blocks for confirmation counts once implemented?
  **A** (@CW): They could, but the confirmation requirement would scale to ~1,200 sub-blocks, which defeats the purpose of the improvement for exchange integration.

- **Q** (@Son Nguyen, 2024-03-08): Is there a way to lock 1,000–2,000 ERG for 10 years?
  **A** (@qx()): Create a temporary wallet, send ERG to it, create a loan offer of 1 COMET with ERG as collateral at 0% for 10 years, fulfil from a permanent wallet with 1 COMET, then discard the temporary wallet keys. Liquidate after 10 years. Timing fulfilment during off-peak hours (3am–6am EST) reduces opportunistic interference. HodlBox is an alternative but appears to unlock on a fixed date (~10/15/24) rather than a custom duration.

- **Q** (@gg, 2024-03-10): If I mint rsERG with BTC, am I the only one who can recover it?
  **A** (@qx()): You don't mint rsERG with BTC. You lock BTC in a multisig wallet on the Bitcoin side and receive rsBTC on Ergo side — equal amounts always locked to rsBTC in circulation. rsBTC is a wrapped bridged asset; there is no minting relationship. Arbitrage mechanisms automatically balance prices between chains.

- **Q** (@cannon_q, 2024-03-20): How should transaction throughput claims be evaluated (e.g. "450 transactions for $1")?
  **A** (@cannon_q): A 1:1 transaction costs ~0.001 ERG (~$0.0022). However, ErgoScript allows batching: a single transaction with 10,000 outputs costs the same 0.001 ERG, making cost per output ~$0.00022. Documented example: 10,000 airdrop recipients for 0.0011 ERG total.

- **Q** (@Affable Villain / @Ioannis, 2024-03-21): How do you consolidate a wallet with 7,000+ unspent boxes?
  **A** (@Aco): Empirically optimal consolidation: 2,000 boxes per transaction at 0.00005 ERG per box. Satergo handles this well.

- **Q** (@Fkn A Jared, 2024-03-20): Could Ergo implement RGB or RGB++ compatibility similar to Nervos?
  **A** (@kushti): Implementation seems doable per the light paper, but the necessity is questioned — Ergo's eUTXO model already provides much of the same functionality natively. Some concerns about RGB++ design exist.

- **Q** (@Alien, 2024-03-24): Can a wallet be slashed if someone rugs the community?
  **A** (@qx()): No. Ergo's decentralisation prevents protocol-level slashing or censorship. Community can attempt social/legal routes (mixer monitoring, CEX blocking if police are involved), but funds moved through the mixer and DEX contracts are fully irrecoverable by design.

- **Q** (@Anonymous, 2024-03-04): Is the Ergo blockchain quantum-proof?
  **A** (@kushti / @Glasgow): The PoW component (Autolykos) is quantum-resistant. Sigma protocols and Schnorr signatures are not. See docs.ergoplatform.com/faq/#quantum.

- **Q** (@Z J, 2024-03-20): Is there a simple onboarding path for new users unfamiliar with crypto?
  **A** (@Glasgow): ergonaut.space/en/Guides/yield provides a step-by-step progression from wallet setup to DeFi participation. The AI chatbot at ergoplatform.org is trained on Ergo docs. Developer crash courses at docs.ergoplatform.com/dev/get-started/.

- **Q** (community, 2024-03-13): How do mempool spam attacks work on Ergo, and what protections exist?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo has mempool filters allowing node operators to raise minimum propagation fee rates, preventing spam from even entering the mempool. Fee estimation API exists in the node but is not yet widely used by applications. From node version 4.0.44, limits prevent downloading too many transactions from a single peer. Fee market dynamics mean spammers' low-fee transactions eventually flush out after 10–40 blocks as standard-fee transactions take priority. The 2020 mixer spam event resolved this way — fees increased slightly for a few weeks, then normalised.

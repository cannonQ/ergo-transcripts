# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General | **Month:** 2022-09

---

## Executive Summary

September 2022 was defined by two major external shocks and Ergo's rapid technical response to them: the Ethereum Merge on September 15th caused a dramatic hashrate influx that exposed limitations in Ergo's difficulty adjustment algorithm, prompting the community to draft, vote on, and begin activating EIP-37 within a single month. Alongside this, Ergo achieved its most significant exchange listing to date (Huobi), launched the Rosen Bridge soft launch process, and made measurable progress on Ledger hardware wallet support and the DexyGold stablecoin.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Ethereum Merge & Hashrate Volatility
The Ethereum Merge (September 15) sent shockwaves through Ergo's network. Hashrate spiked from a baseline of ~25 TH/s to peaks of 170–230 TH/s before collapsing back to ~80–120 TH/s. Ergo's difficulty adjustment algorithm — which recalculates every 1024 blocks using a least-squares method averaged over 8 epochs — was not designed for such extreme volatility. Block times stretched from under 1 minute during the spike to 5–10+ minutes afterward, disrupting dApp interactions, SigmaUSD oracle updates, and miner profitability across the board. Defenders of the existing design (including kushti) noted it protects against time-drifting and coin-hopping attacks, and that faster per-block adjustment (as used by Ethereum) is gameable via timestamp manipulation. Nevertheless, the severity of the disruption made clear that a protocol response was warranted.

### EIP-37: Difficulty Adjustment Hard Fork
Kushti drafted EIP-37 within days of the merge's aftermath, proposing to reduce epoch length from 1024 blocks to 128 blocks and cap difficulty changes at 50% per epoch. Simulations demonstrated meaningfully lower deviation from target block time during hashrate swings. Community discussion was extensive, covering security trade-offs, governance representation (solo miners vs. large pools), and what else should be bundled into the fork. The hard fork required a 90% miner supermajority (232/256 blocks). Node versions 4.0.100, 4.0.101, and 4.0.103 were released in rapid succession to support voting; GetBlok.io notably implemented a proof-of-vote system allowing individual miners — not just pool operators — to signal preference via GBGT governance tokens. By late September, major pools (2miners, Woolypooly, Nanopool) had upgraded and voting was underway. The Ergo Foundation also allocated a temporary 36 ERG per block bonus to miners to incentivize continued network support during the difficult adjustment period.

### Huobi Exchange Listing
Ergo was listed on Huobi (ranked #5–7 by trading volume on CoinGecko) on September 8, with ERG/USDT and ERG/BTC pairs. This was broadly regarded as a meaningful Tier 1 listing. Initial deposit confirmation requirements were set at 80 blocks — later reduced to 25 following community and Armeanio's direct engagement with Huobi. Trading volumes reached ~$300k within hours of launch. Huobi offered promotional staking at 50% APY (up to 200 ERG, 30 days). Armeanio also opened discussions with Huobi about future listings of native Ergo tokens such as ErgoPad. CoinMarketCap separately corrected Ergo's long-standing circulating supply error during this period, improving ranking metrics, though the retroactive chart update created a misleading apparent spike.

### Rosen Bridge Development
The Rosen Bridge progressed from internal testing to soft launch by late September. Distributed signature generation was successfully tested on Cardano testnet early in the month, with guard service deadlock and P2P network bugs identified and resolved. By the final week, the soft launch had formally begun on Ergo and Cardano mainnets using test tokens (to be burnt before full launch). A small set of watchers and guards — initially controlled by the team — were operational, with plans to onboard external entities. The bridge does not require any Cardano-side smart contracts, only multisig wallets, making it relatively chain-agnostic.

### DexyGold Stablecoin
Kushti announced DexyGold — an algorithmic gold-pegged stablecoin — near the end of September, opening a dedicated Telegram group for design and deployment discussion. It will utilize Ergo's Oracle Pools and is positioned as a commodity-backed complement to SigmaUSD. A logo design contest (25 ERG prize) was launched concurrently. Oracle Pool v2 was noted as already running for DexyGold with GORT rewards.

### Ledger Hardware Wallet Progress
The Ergo Ledger app continued through Ledger's review process throughout the month. An early sideload guide for Nano S Plus users was published, and a GitHub PR was merged addressing initial review feedback. Ledger subsequently requested recipient address display — a non-trivial change within memory constraints — which the team worked to accommodate. The review also presented an opportunity to potentially remove the maximum token-per-transaction limit. No confirmed release date was established, but meaningful progress was made across the month.

### Mining Ecosystem & Profitability
GPU mining became broadly unprofitable post-merge due to overcapacity and the European energy crisis. Community discussion focused on miner retention strategies: GetBlok.io's subpool system (earning additional tokens like NETA, COMET, and ErgoPad alongside ERG), the SigmaUSD minting mechanism as a profit-hedging tool, and potential future loyalty token mechanisms. Pool centralization was a recurring concern — 2miners briefly exceeded 50% of network hashrate (including solo pool), prompting community pushback and calls to redistribute to GetBlok, Herominers, Woolypooly, and others. The block reward reduction from 48 to 45 ERG occurred during this period.

### #DiscoverErgo Campaign & Ergoversary
The #DiscoverErgo social media campaign executed on September 15 with approximately $4,500 in prizes (50 winners: 25 by engagement, 25 randomly selected). The campaign aimed to educate incoming Ethereum miners about the Ergo ecosystem. The Ergo 3rd anniversary (Ergoversary) was also observed at the end of September, with the team noting 5,811 developer commits over the preceding year. ErgoHack V was announced in partnership with Flux, Alephium, and the Djed Alliance, with a Mining and Minting theme and the largest prize pool to date.

### dApp Ecosystem Development
Several dApp milestones occurred across the month. DuckPools lending protocol launched in beta with a 0.5 ERG deposit cap. Trade House (order book DEX by C8) was discussed as a significant complement to AMM-based Spectrum DEX, offering partial fills, transparent order depth, no impermanent loss for single-sided provision, and full user control over execution conditions. Spectrum DEX progress toward Cardano testnet relaunch was noted. Night Owl Casino targeted a mid-October launch, expected to drive SigmaUSD utility. Paideia DAO demos were anticipated shortly.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@koukarin, 2022-08-30): How does the eUTXO model handle sending tokens when there is insufficient ERG for fees in the output box?
  **A** (@Klaus): A minimum ERG amount per box is enforced by the protocol; wallets manage this automatically to prevent creation of empty or unspendable boxes.

- **Q** (@zhang zee, 2022-09-01): Why does DuckPools require two blocks to show deposits?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Off-chain bots execute contracts in the second block. This can be optimised to one block if dApps account for mempool transactions directly.

- **Q** (@zhang zee, 2022-09-01): Why not reduce block time to 60 seconds and halve rewards to maintain the emission schedule?
  **A** (community): That is what layer 2 solutions are for. Changing block time is a hard fork decision with complex implications for timestamp-dependent contracts.

- **Q** (@Han, 2022-09-08): Do we have any plans or projects to build a zero-knowledge layer on Ergo?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Ergo has Sigma protocols built into the core — hence the logo. Layer 2 work is just getting started. Bulletproof verification in ErgoScript is being explored, which would enable SNARK-equivalent capabilities without a trusted setup, opening possibilities for verifiable credentials, privacy features, and hybrid contracts.

- **Q** (@Manolo, 2022-09-10): Can you sideload Ledger support for Ergo onto a Ledger Nano X?
  **A** (community): Sideloading currently only works on the Nano S Plus. Official release is pending Ledger team approval.

- **Q** (@N, 2022-09-04): SigmaUSD redemption failed after 45 minutes with a "minimal ERG" error.
  **A** (@Glasgow): Send 0.01 ERG to the proxy wallet address first, then retry the refund. The proxy address requires ERG to cover the transaction fee.

- **Q** (community, 2022-09-17): Why does Ergo use an 8-epoch average for difficulty adjustment rather than faster per-block adjustment?
  **A** (@kushti): Adjusting difficulty per block is insecure because timestamps are highly gameable at that granularity. An epoch must be long enough to minimise the effect of ~20-minute timestamp manipulation windows. The conservative design also preserves miner governance integrity — Ethereum-style fast adjustment was likely gamed by F2Pool to gain disproportionate protocol voting influence.

- **Q** (community, 2022-09-20): Can the difficulty adjustment algorithm be changed via soft fork?
  **A** (@kushti): No. A DAA change requires a hard fork.

- **Q** (@Jonii, 2022-09-23): Can 10% of block rewards go to node operators as an incentive?
  **A** (@Aberg): This creates an attack vector — an actor could trivially spin up 1,000 nodes from the same database with minimal resources to capture a disproportionate share of rewards.

- **Q** (@JotaJs, 2022-09-28): Does a Raspberry Pi miner's vote carry the same weight as a miner with an RTX GPU?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Voting weight is proportional to blocks mined, so higher hashpower equals greater voting influence.

- **Q** (@Oleg, 2022-09-29): After EIP-37 activates, does difficulty recalculate using the last 8 epochs of 128 blocks, or does it start fresh?
  **A** (@nostalgia): It still uses an 8-epoch average, but each epoch is now 128 blocks instead of 1024, making the adjustment 8× more responsive. The historical calculation is performed immediately upon activation using the new formula.

- **Q** (@user, 2022-09-29): Once the hard fork activates, will it take 128 more blocks to reset difficulty, or does it reset immediately?
  **A** (community): The new difficulty is calculated immediately upon fork activation using the updated formula applied to recent block history.

- **Q** (@Jesper, 2022-09-29): What was the reason for the 50% difficulty change cap in EIP-37?
  **A** (community): The cap prevents extreme difficulty swings. With 128-block epochs, it takes approximately 7.5 hours to catch up to a hashrate surge, compared to ~24 hours under the old DAA, while still smoothing the difficulty drop when hashrate exits.

- **Q** (@several, 2022-09-17): How does storage rent work and when will it be collected?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Storage rent only applies to UTXOs with no transactions for 4 years. If you receive ERG across multiple boxes and spend only one, the unspent boxes retain their original rent timers. To reset all timers, consolidate your full balance into a single box by sending it to yourself. The active collection mechanism is not yet live.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **EIP-37 activation timeline**: Voting was underway at month's end but confirmation that 90% threshold (232/256 blocks) would be achieved — and the precise activation block — remained open. The fastest possible activation was estimated at ~22 days from voting start.

- **What to bundle with the hard fork**: Community debated whether EIP-37 should be "lean" (DAA change only) or include NIPoPoW fixes, unsigned 256-bit integer support, and new Merkle tree types for ErgoScript. No consensus reached.

- **Ledger official release**: Multiple review cycles completed, latest revision (recipient address display) in progress. No confirmed release date.

- **Binance listing**: Armeanio indicated ongoing discussions but under NDA. Volume and liquidity remain cited barriers. A second Tier 1 listing in Q4 was hinted at but unconfirmed.

- **Rosen Bridge full mainnet launch**: Soft launch with test tokens began; timeline for full production launch (real assets, external watchers/guards) not announced.

- **Node 5.0 (EIP-27) activation**: Voting progressing, activation expected late November, but precise block height not confirmed.

- **Mining profitability and retention**: No durable mechanism beyond GetBlok subpools and the temporary EF bonus established to retain miners through prolonged low-profitability conditions. Long-term fee market and storage rent sustainability modelling remains incomplete.

- **Oracle pool reliability during hashrate volatility**: Oracle price updates are coupled to block time; no proposal yet for decoupling this for layer 2 or sidechain applications requiring higher-frequency price data.

- **Pool centralisation**: 2miners briefly exceeded 50% hashrate; no protocol-level disincentive mechanism proposed. Relies entirely on miner education and voluntary redistribution.

- **#DiscoverErgo recurrence**: Successful one-time campaign; question of whether to make it a monthly recurring event left open with no formal decision on funding structure.

- **DexyGold design and launch timeline**: Group opened, logo contest launched, but technical specification and deployment schedule not yet established.

- **Sidechains with merge-mining**: kushti confirmed construction to be published "soon," but no document released by month's end.

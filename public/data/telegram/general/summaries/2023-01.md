# Ergo General Chat — 2023-01 Monthly Summary

## Executive Summary

January 2023 was a landmark month for Ergo's infrastructure and ecosystem maturity, dominated by three converging themes: hardware wallet integration (Ledger launch in developer mode, Yoroi deprecation driving wallet migration), DeFi protocol launches and upgrades (Spectrum Finance yield farming and SPF IDO, SigmaUSD reserve dynamics, SigmaFi lending beta, DuckPools IDO), and organizational development (Sigmanauts transparency reforms, ErgoHack VI announcement, Lithos Protocol launch). Underlying all activity was a consistent thread of protocol-level discussion around storage rent mechanics, emission reduction, block speed optimization, and Rosen Bridge progress toward mainnet.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Ledger Hardware Wallet Integration
The Ergo Ledger app launched in developer mode during the first week of January, representing a major milestone for institutional and security-conscious holders. The app passed all functional tests with the Ledger team conducting final production validation. A key limitation — maximum 20 distinct tokens per single transaction on Nano S due to memory constraints — was confirmed as a hardware-level constraint applying uniformly across Ledger models (binary must be identical). The public release via Ledger Manager remained pending throughout the month. Community guides were published by Koutelier and ErgoCube to assist early adopters. Users were advised to generate new seed phrases on the device rather than importing existing ones, preserving the hardware security model.

### Yoroi Wallet Deprecation & Migration
EMURGO announced the sunset of Ergo support in Yoroi, with new wallet creation disabled immediately and full deprecation scheduled for February 28, 2023. This drove substantial community activity around wallet migration guidance. Recommended alternatives included Nautilus (Chrome/Brave extension), SafeW, Minotaur (desktop multisig-capable), Satergo (standalone desktop), and Terminus (iOS). The migration path is straightforward — restoring the same seed phrase in a new wallet — with no on-chain action required. Armeanio explored the possibility of taking over the Yoroi backend post-sunset to extend the migration window, noting that legacy users can appear years after wallet launches. kushti confirmed a disk overflow issue on Yoroi's server was resolved. The wallet transition prompted a broader community chat on January 10 at 17:00 UTC.

### Spectrum Finance: Yield Farming & SPF IDO
Spectrum Finance shipped its yield farming feature in the second week of January — a fully decentralized, smart contract-based reward distribution mechanism distinct from ErgoPad-style staking. Projects can configure emission amount and duration; GuapSwap integration enables pre-wallet reward token swapping. The Ergo mainnet deployment preceded a planned Cardano version. The SPF community IDO round launched January 26, with 37% sold within the first 24 hours. SPF utility encompasses off-chain executor fees, Spectrum Networks validator rewards, and protocol fee reduction. qx() prepared to provide initial liquidity post-IDO.

### SigmaUSD Reserve Ratio & Stability
The SigmaUSD reserve ratio dropped below 400% (to ~389%) during the month following large minting events (400k+ SigmaUSD minted over three days), restricting new SigmaUSD minting. Community workarounds included price appreciation, SigmaRSV minting, and Spectrum DEX trading. Users reported transaction failures, stuck refunds, and oracle price range rejections throughout January; TokenJay was recommended over sigmausd.io as a more reliable interface. The proxy address refund mechanism at sigmausd.io/#/refund was verified as functional. kushti advised withdrawing SigmaRSV positions when reserve ratio exceeds 400%, characterizing the protocol as a zero-sum game between SigmaUSD and SigmaRSV holders.

### SigmaFi Lending Protocol
A private beta of SigmaFi demonstrated collateralized lending using Cardano stake keys as collateral — 77 LOVE collateral for a 1 ERG loan, with liquidation requiring lender signature and loan deadline stored in the smart contract R7 register as a BigInt. Public testnet availability was pending at month's end. The protocol addresses a key use case: accessing liquidity without liquidating staking positions, with staking yield potentially exceeding the borrow rate for leveraged returns. FleetSDK can decode bond box parameters via `SParse<bigint>(bondBox.additionalRegisters.R7)`.

### DuckPools IDO
DuckPools, a pool-based lending platform for ERG and native assets, announced its IDO launch on ErgoPad. The staker round whitelist went live during the final week of January. The platform targets both collateralized and uncollateralized lending use cases that were simultaneously being discussed in the community chat series on decentralized lending protocols.

### Rosen Bridge Development
Multi-week debugging of watcher bugs was completed, with public testnet deployment described as imminent throughout the month. A soft testnet was made available at soft.rosen.tech with explicit warnings about unfinished status and funds risk. Additions included logging APIs, PostgreSQL support, Koios updates, P2P optimizations, and cold storage fixes. Minotaur wallet dApp connector testing was underway. No official mainnet launch date was confirmed by month's end. The Rosen Bridge v3.1 watcher release contained a bug requiring a hold; v3.1.1 was subsequently released alongside Watcher UI v2.2.1 (`docker compose pull` → `docker compose down` → `docker compose up -d` for upgrade). A manual TX was executed to refund users whose permits were slashed in an accidental high-fee transaction.

### Lithos Protocol Launch
Lithos Protocol was presented as a decentralized successor to the recently shuttered GetBlok mining pool, featuring on-chain share processing via NIPoPoWs, smart contract-based payments, and miner-configurable risk-to-reward ratios. A Twitter Space was held January 19 with detailed technical breakdown. GetBlok's shutdown after approximately one month was attributed to UI/UX issues and hashrate isolation (subpool architecture preventing aggregation), particularly disadvantaging smaller miners.

### ErgoHack VI – Cypherpunk Finance
ErgoHack VI was announced with registration open January 19 through February 21 and the hackathon running February 8–22. The prize pool comprised 18k SigmaUSD and 300k ErgoPad tokens. Registration was available at ergohack.io.

### Sigmanauts Transparency & Governance Reforms
Extensive discussion throughout the month focused on the Sigmanauts program's accessibility, voting processes, membership visibility, and potential conflicts of interest with Ergo Foundation members. Vote 23 passed, mandating that meeting screen shares, notes, and transcripts be made public starting January 22 — with a privacy warning at the start of each Zoom session allowing name changes. The first public meeting notes were posted by qx() on January 22, covering accomplishments including the SigmaVote app, distributed Twitter access, Twitter Spaces hosting capability, mission statement drafts, and recording/publishing workflows. Community chats migrated to Reddit format for the decentralized lending protocols discussion series, enabling asynchronous participation across time zones. Treasury legal structure discussions (DAO, WY DAO LLC, anon structures) remained unresolved with legal guidance from ErgoPad and Spectrum pending.

### Presearch Marketing Grant
The Ergo Foundation secured $50k in Presearch advertising credits (250k PRE tokens) alongside a homepage takeover valued at approximately $45k and social partnerships. An Ergo logo and keyword bidding strategy was planned for late January, with the homepage placement recommended for late February or March 2023.

### Block Reward Emission Reduction
The block reward reduced from 45 ERG to 42 ERG per block on January 9, 2023 (block ~914400) — confirmed by kushti as the first block at the new rate. This continued Ergo's linear emission schedule: from 75 ERG at genesis in 2020, progressing toward ~3 ERG/block by 2027. Discussion of storage rent as a mechanism that increases effective emission beyond 2025 projections was noted, with kushti emphasizing demurrage's role in sustaining miner incentives.

### Storage Rent & NFT Safety
Community debate on whether NFTs in low-ERG UTXOs face loss risk under storage rent mechanics. Key clarification: miners claim approximately 0.14 ERG per collection cycle; tokens themselves are unaffected (with 1 ERG in a UTXO, it takes ~32 years for miners to deplete it). EIP-68 was proposed to burn singleton tokens rather than allow miners to claim them, with community divided on retroactive solutions versus UX-level auto-consolidation. Wallet auto-consolidation via chained transactions and UI alerts was proposed as the more practical near-term solution.

### Node Version 6.0 & Block Speed Optimization
Community discussion on potential block speed improvements confirmed that any changes to block time affect applications relying on block height rather than timestamps. A testnet 6.0 environment with configurable parameters was announced for community-driven scientific observation. kushti noted "drafts" on microblocks and sidechains to be revived post-Ergoversary, and emphasized that concrete use-case-driven design should precede optimization given Ergo's established set of production contracts (Oracle Pools, Rosen, Spectrum DEX, Trade House, lending contracts). Community members interested in leading the testnet effort were invited to DM kushti for access to the testing Telegram group.

### Merged Mining Expansion
KAS, ALPH, and RXD were confirmed as already supported for merge mining on Ergo pools, visible via miningpoolstats.stream/ergo. Community interest in further merge mining opportunities and technical feasibility remained active.

### Monetary Policy & CBDC Discussion
Extended community discussion on the weaponization of programmable money and central bank digital currencies, with Nigeria's cash withdrawal restrictions cited as a cautionary real-world example. Discussion reinforced Ergo's founding mandate for economic freedom and the importance of non-custodial, censorship-resistant infrastructure.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@OxCapitulation, 2023-01-03): What can be done with optional indexes for Explorer-like queries — will dApps always depend on the Explorer?
  **A** (@kushti): Many dApps can avoid Explorer dependency by relying on a group of nodes with additional indexes. Not all apps can switch (a node with optional indexes becomes heavier), but this represents good progress for lightweight dApp infrastructure.

- **Q** (@Pulsarz, 2023-01-03): Has there been analysis on transaction throughput changes between old and new costing models? The mempool still appears filled despite improvements.
  **A** (@kushti): 4.x and 5.x nodes are holding invalid transactions; 5.x nodes are downloading from 4.x. Pool mining configurations are filtering complex transactions post-merge. The Ergo Explorer is sampling one of several 2miners nodes running different versions. Coordination between pool operators is needed.

- **Q** (@qx(), 2023-01-03): How do grid bots work and can normal users run one? Will Spectrum support them?
  **A** (@Gazza): A grid bot UI would be beneficial for users. Spectrum discussions are ongoing — possible integration using the same liquidity pools. Yield farming is currently the development priority.

- **Q** (@TMR.ΣRG, 2023-01-16): When people say "Monero can be built on top of Ergo," what does that mean — a sidechain, or bridging ERG for privacy functions?
  **A** (@qx()): The dApp itself can use ring signatures for ERG to support privacy. No sidechain is needed.

- **Q** (@eπ, 2023-01-16): How will Cardano staking rewards be handled for wrapped ADA transacted via Rosen Bridge?
  **A** (@qx()): Currently an interesting idea without public implementation details. Tracking would require off-chain oversight; Rosen's current design does not distinguish returning from new users — it just processes the swap.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-01-18): Who is the counterparty when exercising a SigmaO option token? If I buy a $10 call and ERG goes to $20, who sells ERG at $10?
  **A** (@Unknown): Similar to writing covered calls — option tokens trade on a DEX, so the counterparty is whoever holds the other side of the position.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-01-18): How do I calculate the instantaneous leverage of SigmaRSV? If ERG increases by Y%, how much does SigmaRSV NAV increase?
  **A** (@Unknown): At 4:1 collateralization with a 10% ERG price increase: collateral rises from $4 to $4.4, equity from $3 to $3.4, yielding approximately 1.33x leverage (13.33% SigmaRSV gain).

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2023-01-09): Can an existing software wallet seed phrase be linked to a Ledger device?
  **A** (@Flying Pig): Inadvisable. The entire point of a Ledger is that the seed phrase never touches an internet-connected device. A new seed should be generated on the device itself.

- **Q** (@Foyaz Sheikh, 2023-01-06): How many ERG can a Ledger hold? Does the 20-token limit apply permanently?
  **A** (@Flying Pig): No limit on token count held in the wallet. The 20-token limit applies per transaction (Nano S memory constraint). The Ledger policy requires identical binaries across models, so the limit applies uniformly. No ETA for removal.

- **Q** (@HelixEvo, 2023-01-10): Why would someone rent ERG via DuckPools instead of simply buying it?
  **A** (@TMR.ΣRG): Leverage and avoiding taxable events — borrowing stablecoins, spending, and repaying over time. SigmaFi extends this further: using stake keys as collateral allows accessing liquidity without liquidating staking positions.

- **Q** (@HelixEvo, 2023-01-27): What is the maximum number of derived addresses an Ergo wallet can have?
  **A** (@Aberg): 2^31–1 (2,147,483,647) — the signed 32-bit integer limit.

- **Q** (@gg, 2023-01-28): Can NFTs unmoved for four years be drained by storage rent?
  **A** (@Koutelier): If the UTXO holds sufficient ERG (≈0.14 ERG), NFTs are safe for centuries. Miners claim approximately 0.14 ERG per collection cycle; tokens are unaffected. With 1 ERG in a UTXO, it takes approximately 32 years for miners to deplete it.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-01-10): What is the fee structure of SigmaUSD, and when should SigmaRSV positions be exited?
  **A** (@kushti): The 2.25% fee routes 0.25% to the frontend and the remainder to the reserve, benefiting SigmaRSV holders. kushti advised withdrawing SigmaRSV when the reserve ratio exceeds 400%, characterizing the protocol dynamics as a zero-sum game.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-01-18): Can ErgoMixer be legally targeted the way Tornado Cash was?
  **A** (community): ErgoMixer is self-run and non-custodial — there is no central service, removing the arrest vectors present in the Tornado Cash case. The hop feature mitigates CEX transaction filtering. Wallet identity exposure remains a risk if a CEX deposit is directly traceable.

- **Q** (@T F, 2023-01-06): How do you run an oracle on Ergo? What is the maintenance and payment model?
  **A** (@Glasgow): The oracle-core tool is required; some developer skills are needed for custom oracle types. Operators are paid in oracle tokens. Maintenance is comparable to other continuously running services. Price feeds are usable by any chain.

- **Q** (community, 2023-01-19): How does Lithos Protocol differ from GetBlok, and why did GetBlok fail?
  **A** (Lithos team, Twitter Space 2023-01-19): GetBlok failed due to UI/UX issues and hashrate isolation — the subpool architecture prevented aggregation, disadvantaging smaller miners. Lithos uses on-chain share processing via NIPoPoWs with smart contract-based payments and miner-configurable risk-to-reward ratios.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Ledger public release timeline**: When does the Ergo Ledger app move from developer mode to Ledger Manager official release? Testing by the Ledger team was ongoing at month's end with no confirmed date.

- **Rosen Bridge mainnet launch**: Development continued throughout January with no official mainnet launch date confirmed. The plasma layer and Minotaur dApp connector testing remained in progress.

- **Spectrum Networks architecture**: The $SPF token's utility referenced "Spectrum Networks validator rewards," but the technical definition of Spectrum Networks (sidechain, layer 2, or other) was not clarified publicly during the month.

- **Node 6.0 block speed parameters**: Community interest in faster blocks exists; no consensus on specific parameters (block time, difficulty adjustment) reached. A testnet environment is available but leadership for the proposal and observation-gathering process has not been formally assigned.

- **EIP-68 and storage rent UX**: The proposal to burn singleton tokens rather than allow miner claims remained unresolved. Community was split between EIP-68, retroactive solutions, and wallet-level auto-consolidation as the preferred path.

- **Sigmanauts treasury legal structure**: Multiple structures discussed (DAO, WY DAO LLC, anonymous multisig) with legal guidance from ErgoPad and Spectrum pending. Minotaur multisig testing with burner tokens was planned but not completed.

- **Yoroi backend continuity**: Armeanio's exploration of taking over the Yoroi backend post-sunset to extend the migration window had no confirmed status at month's end.

- **Native Ergo oracle expansion**: Dan Friedman expressed intent to allocate Palmyra fundraising capital toward building out native Ergo Oracle Pools, but only two Oracle Pool v1 operators were active. Timeline and scope remained undefined.

- **Koinly Ergo integration**: Status changed to "in progress" during the month; expected completion date not confirmed.

- **NIPoPoW bootstrapping implementation**: kushti indicated a February target for NIPoPoW bootstrapping relevant to sidechain pegging and trustless relay; no code published by month's end.

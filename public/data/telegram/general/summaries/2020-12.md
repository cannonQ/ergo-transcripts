# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: December 2020

---

## Executive Summary

December 2020 was a pivotal month for Ergo, dominated by three converging themes: the finalisation of the Autolykos v2 hard fork (scheduled 27 January 2021), the imminent launch of the AgeUSD stablecoin (SigmaUSD), and a rapid expansion of ecosystem tooling by both named and anonymous developers. The month closed with the ErgoSummit announcement (23 January 2021), Charles Hoskinson's public endorsement of Ergo and kushti, and the launch of Sigmaverse.io — collectively marking Ergo's transition from research prototype to functioning DeFi platform.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Autolykos v2 Hard Fork (27 January 2021)
The single most discussed technical topic of the month. The hard fork at block #414,720 disables pool resistance (enabling conventional mining pools), reduces minimum GPU RAM requirements from 4 GB to 2 GB, and introduces progressive memory hardening: the N-value (table size) grows to 2^26 by block 614,400, then increases 5% every 51,200 blocks until block 9,216,000, after which it is fixed. The algorithm remains Wagner-based (similar to Equihash) but retains ASIC resistance. The rationale for removing pool resistance — after months of internal debate — was to attract small miners who cannot mine independently, particularly ETH miners anticipating the ETH 2.0 transition. Code was released for audit in the final week of December. [@kushti, msg#68016, 2020-12-04; msg#71139, 2020-12-23; msg#73128-73129, 2021-01-03]

### AgeUSD / SigmaUSD Stablecoin
A collateralised algorithmic stablecoin co-developed with EMURGO and IOHK, to be deployed in ErgoScript on Ergo and Plutus on Cardano, was confirmed for announcement at ErgoSummit. The design is algorithmic (not 1:1 backed), though the precise collateralisation ratio and liquidation mechanics were deliberately withheld until the summit. Oracle Pool v1 was confirmed as underfunded and to be replaced with an improved version before the stablecoin goes live. Charles Hoskinson confirmed he holds ERG and described kushti as "one of the smartest people he has ever met," significantly boosting community confidence. Launch expected approximately one week after the January hard fork. [@kushti, msg#70989, 2020-12-22; msg#72545-72547, 2020-12-29; @Richi, msg#72817, 2020-12-31]

### Ecosystem Tooling & Anonymous Developer Contributions
December saw a burst of ecosystem development. The Ergo Headless dApp Framework (Rust, by Robert Kornacki/EMURGO) shipped with tutorials; the Ergo Bootstrap tool enabled one-click node and explorer deployment; CoinBarn wallet extension v0.1.0 launched for token issuance; Sigmaverse.io aggregated all Ergo dApps, tokens, NFTs, and Oracle Pools into a single portal; and the Ergo Auction House gained image/GIF preview support within 24 hours of a user request. kushti credited anonymous developers with approximately 50% of core ecosystem applications (Ergo Utils, ErgoMixer, Ergo Auction House) and called for five more anonymous contributors. A programmable mixer API was also announced, enabling bots to issue SigmaUSD and immediately mix outputs. [@kushti, msg#68811, 2020-12-11; msg#71646-71650, 2020-12-24; @Dmitry Usov, msg#72064, 2020-12-27]

### Scalability Roadmap & Speed Improvements
kushti outlined a 2021 scalability plan: stateless clients (enabling Raspberry Pi full nodes), sub-block confirmation protocols (Bitcoin-NG or Flux-like) via soft fork, and sidechain consideration. The block extension section already supports BitcoinNG-style microblocks and Aspen-like service chains. A community testnet group was proposed for parameter experimentation (block time, difficulty). A significant caveat was raised: many dApps use block height rather than timestamps, so faster blocks could break expiry logic in existing contracts. No consensus path was chosen; research phase set for January–February 2021. [@kushti, msg#67645, 2020-12-03; msg#70070, 2020-12-17; msg#70076, 2020-12-17]

### Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge & Chainlink
Oracle Pool v1 (EF members plus external participants) and Oracle Pool v2 (running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards) were both operational but underfunded. Palmyra (formerly ZenGate) announced a Chainlink partnership for proof-of-reserve functionality on on-chain warehouse receipts supporting decentralised trade financing. Armeanio clarified this is not zero-sum: Chainlink was chosen for network size and tooling, while native Ergo oracles remain the long-term goal, with Palmyra's token raise earmarked to fund Oracle Pool infrastructure build-out. Multiple oracle solutions (API3, Chainlink, Wolfram Blockchain Labs) are planned in parallel. [@Armeanio, msg#67641-67649, 2020-12-03; @kushti, msg#72545, 2020-12-29]

### NFTs, Auctions & Token Issuance
The Ergo Auction House (ergoauctions.org) went live with growing activity, supporting singleton tokens as NFTs (quantity = 1), with artwork URLs stored in register R9 and auto-uploaded via ergoutils.org. Commemorative NFTs were announced for ErgoSummit attendees. Token issuance via CoinBarn and ErgoUtils became accessible without technical knowledge. A standard for music/audio NFTs was flagged as needed but not yet defined. Approximately 250 tokens had been issued by early December, mostly for testing. [@kushti, msg#71657-71709, 2020-12-24; @V, msg#72968, 2021-01-01]

### NIPoPoWs, Bulletproofs & Cross-Chain Communication
Ergo Cast Episode 5 featured Dionysis Zindros on NIPoPoWs — Ergo's most distinctive PoW feature enabling trustless light-client sync and cross-chain communication without full chain history. kushti also confirmed that Bulletproofs verification is possible in ErgoScript, enabling trustless zero-knowledge proofs (range proofs, verifiable credentials, Groth one-out-of-many) without a trusted setup — opening hybrid on-chain/off-chain privacy patterns where sensitive data is proven but not disclosed. [@kushti, msg#67405-67407, 2020-12-02; msg#68016, 2020-12-04]

### Mining Economics & Community Sentiment
The decision to abandon pool resistance was contentious but ultimately framed as inclusive: small miners, Cardano SPOs, and ETH miners migrating post-ETH 2.0 are the target audience. Current mining requires 4 GB RAM (dropping to 2 GB post-fork). GPU mining profitability was assessed as poor for most hardware until after the hard fork. The EFYT token (Ergo Waves token) swap deadline was confirmed as 1 July 2021 at 1:1 ratio on Waves DEX. Emission mechanics were clarified: 75 ERG/block static for the first two years (until July 2021), then declining by 3 ERG per quarter, with full supply (~97.4M ERG) reached around 2027.

### Privacy, Regulation & DeFi Strategy
France's mandatory KYC announcement (2020-12-09) prompted discussion of Ergo's position as the boundary approaches between compliant and truly private crypto. kushti noted Ergo's mixer is fully functional with public nodes and requires no node operation. Armeanio cautioned against DeFi yield farming as "solution looking for a problem" and advocated for real utility. Smart contract formal verification was confirmed as already implemented for some contracts (see ergo-contracts GitHub repository).

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-12-04): What is the difference between Autolykos v1 and v2?
  **A** (@kushti): Pool resistance switched off; block candidate changes during mining now permitted (enabling fast confirmation protocols); some ASIC optimisation avenues closed. Progressive memory hardening introduced with N-value growing to 2^26 by block 614,400.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-12-04): Why is pool resistance being disabled?
  **A** (@kushti): To attract small miners who cannot mine independently; pools with collateral barriers remain possible. Research published at https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/044.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-12-04): Are Bulletproofs usable in Ergo smart contracts?
  **A** (@kushti): Verification in ErgoScript is possible. This enables trustless zero-knowledge proofs without trusted setup — range proofs, Groth one-out-of-many, verifiable credentials. Sensitive data can be proven in-contract without disclosure, opening hybrid privacy patterns.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-12-04): What vector commitment scheme does Ergo use?
  **A** (@kushti): The Edrax VC scheme (kushti co-author). Hash-function-based only — no non-standard cryptography required. Enables stateless fullnodes. Presentation: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PHY7JnLrK5o

- **Q** (@Scott Z, 2020-12-09): If I send 2.5 ERG from a 5 ERG UTXO, where does the change go? My node restarted and I cannot see the 2.499 ERG change.
  **A** (@kushti): Change UTXO returns to your sending address. Issue is likely wallet derivation path tracking after restart; verify the change endpoint is being monitored.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-12-11): What prevents double-spend in Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo is a classical proof-of-work blockchain; PoW consensus prevents double-spend.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-12-12): Can Ergo smart contracts be formally verified?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes — some contracts already are. See https://github.com/ergoplatform/ergo-contracts

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-12-17): How scalable is Ergo currently and what is planned?
  **A** (@kushti): Currently: stateless clients enabling Raspberry Pi full nodes. Planned 2021: sub-block confirmation protocols (Bitcoin-NG or Flux-like) via soft fork; sidechain consideration. Research phase January–February 2021.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-12-17): Would a sub-block protocol resemble Waves microblocks?
  **A** (@kushti): Bitcoin-NG or Flux-like protocol. Several academic papers exist; best fit for Ergo and possible sidechain combination still being evaluated.

- **Q** (@matiwinnetou, 2020-12-22): Will the SigmaUSD stablecoin be implemented in Plutus or ErgoScript?
  **A** (@kushti): Cardano implementation in Plutus; Ergo implementation in ErgoScript.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-12-22): How does order book creation work on Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Order created in one transaction; matching requires a second transaction. Order encodes the ERG ↔ token swap conditions. AMM DEXes are also possible and efficient on Ergo; both models are viable.

- **Q** (@matiwinnetou, 2020-12-28): ADA/USD oracle on Ergo shows $0.162 but market is $0.178+. Why the discrepancy?
  **A** (@kushti): The pool is underfunded. It will be replaced with a better solution before the stablecoin launches.

- **Q** (@CryptoTeller, 2021-01-03): Is pool resistance no longer the strategy?
  **A** (@kushti): Correct — after many months of discussion, conventional pool infrastructure is now the standard approach. It invites small-time miners who cannot mine independently.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-12-27): When will all ERG be mined?
  **A** (@Damon AndTheSea / @Richi): Block interval is 2 minutes; 75 ERG/block for the first two years (static until July 2021), then declining by 3 ERG per quarter. Full supply (~97.4M ERG) reached approximately 2027. Inflation rate at end-2020 was ~57%; BTC by comparison is ~1.76%.

- **Q** (implied, 2020-12-23): Will dApps relying on block height break if block time is reduced?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes — many contracts use block height rather than timestamps, so block time changes affect expiry conditions. This is a significant complexity that must be resolved before any speed increase is implemented.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **SigmaUSD design details**: Collateralisation ratio, liquidation mechanics, and multi-chain deployment strategy deliberately withheld until ErgoSummit (23 January 2021). Community split on whether design is 1:1 backed or purely algorithmic.

- **Scalability path decision**: Sub-blocks (Bitcoin-NG/Flux), sidechains, layer 2, or faster block time — no consensus chosen. Block time reduction carries known risk to block-height-dependent contracts. Research group needed; kushti invited interested community members to DM for testnet Telegram access.

- **Oracle Pool infrastructure**: Native Ergo Oracle Pools confirmed underfunded and requiring replacement before stablecoin launch. New design and funding timeline (tied to Palmyra token raise) not yet specified.

- **Ledger integration**: Grant approved and half-paid since May 2020; no completion date provided. @jasondavies tagged repeatedly with no public response.

- **Major exchange listing**: Strong community demand for Binance, FTX, or Coinbase listing. Gate.io listed after contacting Ergo first. kushti confirmed new round of outreach; no timeline. Binance noted to require significant leverage conditions.

- **Music/audio NFT standard**: kushti acknowledged need for a song NFT standard in ErgoUtils and Auction House but flagged it as pending anon_real's availability.

- **Mining centralisation post-hard fork**: Removal of pool resistance raises long-term centralisation risk. kushti stated this would need assessment "in 3–4 years"; no mitigation mechanism specified.

- **Regulatory response**: France's mandatory KYC law and anticipated US stablecoin regulation create open questions about Ergo's compliance posture without compromising privacy architecture.

- **Spanish and emerging-market communities**: Spanish Telegram group inactive despite moderation; Nigeria/Africa DeFi opportunity noted by community but no formal outreach plan confirmed.

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Month:** February 2018
**Chat:** Ergo General

---

## Executive Summary

February 2018 was a foundational month for the Ergo community, dominated by the first structured founder Q&A (February 6th), which clarified core tokenomics, the EFYT→ERG conversion mechanism, and the dual-chain architecture. The month also established the testnet roadmap through to mainnet, confirmed the Equihash-based GPU mining algorithm, and outlined Ergo's privacy philosophy — lightweight anonymity via Sigma protocols rather than SNARKs or ZCash-style shielded pools.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EFYT → ERG Token Swap & Tokenomics
The conversion mechanics were clarified during the February 6th Q&A. EFYT holders will exchange tokens 1:1 via a user-initiated gateway on the Waves DEX — the user sends EFYT to a provided address, and the backend handles the swap automatically. ERG starts at zero supply at mainnet launch, with 10% of first-year mined coins reserved for the Ergo Foundation to buy back EFYT. The dual-chain architecture adds further nuance: each EFYT converts to 1 ErgoMoney + 1 ErgoData, reflecting two parallel chains with identical technology but different parameters. Community members raised concerns that early EFYT purchasers receive no special premium beyond the 1:1 rate, and the emission curve was deferred for future publication.

### Testnet Roadmap & Releases
Version 0.2.1 (TestNet0) was released during W06. The published roadmap stages: **TestNet0** (v0.2.1, current) → **TestNet1** (Sigma transaction language, April 2018) → **TestNet2** (rollerchain, new economy model, parameter tuning) → **MainNet** (conditional on successful parameter tuning). Mainnet timing remained unspecified beyond "Q2 2018 clarity." The node can be run natively on Windows as a standard Java program (`java -jar ergo-assembly-0.2.1.jar`), without Docker.

### Mining Algorithm
Ergo's proof of work algorithm was confirmed as Equihash-based and GPU-minable. Standard GPU rigs are sufficient for initial participation. Questions about mobile mining on mainnet were raised but not resolved in this period.

### Privacy & Anonymity Philosophy
Ergo's approach to privacy was articulated clearly: wallet-level ring signatures with a reasonable default ring size, explicitly rejecting SNARKs (due to new cryptographic assumptions and efficiency concerns) and ZCash-style shielded transaction pools. Anonymous transaction features were confirmed as planned. The tradeoff acknowledged is that ZCash offers a larger anonymity set, but Ergo prioritises practical, lightweight privacy using simpler cryptographic building blocks aligned with Sigma protocols.

### Smart Contracts & ErgoScript
ErgoScript smart contracts were confirmed as a core feature. The specification and early implementation are available in the [sigmastate-interpreter repository](https://github.com/ScorexFoundation/sigmastate-interpreter). Full Sigma transaction language support is targeted for TestNet1 in April 2018.

### Legal Structure & Project Governance
The project operates under a Russian Ltd company for development, with a non-profit foundation being established in Great Britain. An airdrop of 100K tokens was planned for IOHK employees, with a separate 100K public airdrop and formal grant programs announced for no earlier than Summer 2018.

### Community Building & Branding
The community identified the need for a website refresh and rebranding ahead of mainnet. Core team members were active in public events: Dmitry presented live at the London Genesis event (W07), and kushti was confirmed to speak on zero-knowledge proof protocols at Binary District (March 2nd). The first structured Q&A was recorded and published.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Andrey Kochkin, 2018-02-06): When are smart contracts planned, and where is the specification available?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Current code at the [sigmastate-interpreter repo](https://github.com/ScorexFoundation/sigmastate-interpreter); TestNet1 roadmap targets full Sigma transaction language in April 2018.

- **Q** (@MMAforMoney, 2018-02-06): How does Ergo's privacy compare to Zerocoin/ZCash? What are the pros and cons?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Ergo avoids SNARKs due to new cryptographic assumptions and inefficiency. Simpler building blocks (Sigma protocols) are used for practical, lightweight wallet-level anonymity. ZCash offers a superior anonymity set via shielded pools, but Ergo prioritises practicality. Ring signatures with a reasonable default ring size are used at wallet level.

- **Q** (@Guang Guang, 2018-02-06): If 10% of mined ERG goes to the foundation, does that mean continuous dilution?
  **A** (@Dmitry): At launch, 0 ERG exist. EFYT conversion happens during year one; 10% of issued tokens go to the foundation, who use them to exchange back for EFYT — it is not an ongoing mining tax beyond year one.

- **Q** (@Fn Ln, 2018-02-06): Can Ergo be compiled and run natively on Windows without Docker?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Yes, as a standard Java program. Download the JAR from the release and execute: `java -jar ergo-assembly-0.2.1.jar`

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-02-06): Will the EFYT↔ERG swap be manual or automatic?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): The user initiates the transfer by sending EFYT to the provided gateway address; the backend automation handles the swap. A step-by-step guide will be provided at launch.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-02-25): Can Bank of China's blockchain scaling patent be applied to or threaten Ergo?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Blockchains cannot realistically be patented — even minor forks produce a legally distinct protocol. Banks and centralised entities will not implement truly decentralised systems, as doing so contradicts their existing power structures.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Emission curve**: Dmitry committed to publishing a concrete emission schedule "in a few weeks" (2018-02-06) — not yet delivered by month end.
- **EFYT→ERG conversion trigger**: At what block height or time threshold does the conversion window open and close? Explicit parameters not stated.
- **Exchange listings post-mainnet**: Which exchanges beyond Waves DEX will list ERG at mainnet launch?
- **Mass adoption / non-technical user onboarding**: No concrete strategy articulated for merchants or non-technical ("granny") users.
- **Mobile mining viability**: Hardware requirements for mining, and whether mobile devices can participate at mainnet launch, remain unanswered.
- **Grant program mechanics**: Application process, selection criteria, and vesting schedule for the Summer 2018 grant program not yet detailed.
- **Scorex framework usage**: Whether Ergo uses the Scorex framework was asked but not answered in this period.
- **ICO vs. exchange-only distribution**: Whether Ergo will pursue any ICO route beyond Waves DEX availability and airdrops remains unclear.
- **Dual-chain parameters**: Specific differences in parameters between ErgoMoney and ErgoData chains not yet published.

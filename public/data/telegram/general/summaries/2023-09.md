# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: September 2023

---

## Executive Summary

September 2023 was a pivotal month for Ergo's DeFi infrastructure, marked by the progression of Dexy Gold toward mainnet launch, the activation of Oracle Pools v2, and a major European exchange listing on Bitpanda. The ecosystem saw parallel advances across multiple dApp layers—SigmaFi collateralized lending, Paideia DAO governance, the Sigmanauts mining pool, and Rosen Bridge fundraising—alongside substantive community debates on block speed optimization and the Autolykos difficulty curve.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Dexy Gold & Oracle Pools v2

Oracle Pools v2 shipped a stable release early in the month and went live on mainnet by late September running the XAU/ERG price feed for Dexy Gold. Kushti performed final checks on the buyback contract before launch, with the GORT (Gold Oracle Reward Token) distribution model creating a positive feedback loop: Dexy protocol fees buy back GORT from open-market LPs, rewarding oracle operators. GORT call options (10 ERG cost, 50 ERG strike, October 14 expiry) launched on SigmaOptions to bootstrap liquidity. The full Dexy Gold mainnet launch sequence was confirmed as: (1) GORT LP launch, (2) buyback contract deployment and testing, (3) testnet UI, (4) mainnet. Dexy Gold was positioned throughout the month as an alternative to SigmaUSD, which remained locked due to reserve ratio constraints and was unable to mint new supply.

### Bitpanda Listing & Exchange Landscape

ERG went live on Bitpanda on September 14 in trade-only mode, with full deposit/withdrawal capability expected Q4 2023/Q1 2024. Bitpanda operates as a brokerage sourcing liquidity from Gate.io, providing a KYC-compliant EU on-ramp. The CoinEx hack (~$27.8M across ETH, TRON, and Polygon) on September 12 kept Ergo's hot wallet intact, with the team moving funds to cold storage; the broader community used the incident to reinforce self-custody messaging. ERG also listed on NonKyc.io with ERG/BTC, ERG/USDT, and ERG/XMR pairs by late September. ERG-WXG was delisted from WX Network with a redemption window until October 10, following the Waves ecosystem collapse discussed as a cautionary tale about centralized team control over L1, DeFi, and gateways simultaneously.

### Block Speed & Scaling Debate

A sustained multi-week discussion examined Ergo's block time (currently 2 minutes) and potential optimizations. Kushti outlined a weak blocks / sub-blocks approach inspired by Bitcoin NG microblocks, targeting approximately 20-second confirmation intervals. Key technical nuances emerged: dApps relying on block height rather than timestamps would be unaffected differently, emission contracts and their tail-off schedules depend on block intervals, and exchange confirmation requirements may not decrease proportionally with faster blocks—meaning real-world finality for CEX users may remain constant. The community also debated whether on-chain optimizations, layer 2 solutions, or sidechains were most appropriate given Ergo's concrete, identifiable application set (Rosen Bridge, Oracle Pools, Spectrum DEX, Trade House, lending contracts). No protocol change was proposed or committed; kushti noted a testnet channel exists for anyone passionate enough to lead parameter testing.

### Autolykos Difficulty Adjustment & Mining

Community members flagged pool-hopping behavior in Autolykos mining: large entities enter at low difficulty and exit during high-difficulty spikes, disadvantaging consistent miners. No protocol change was proposed; the consensus was that this is standard PoW behavior, miners have tools to address it but lack coordinated incentive, and core developers are prioritizing node improvements, Dexy Gold, SigmaFi, and indexed node work. Separately, the Sigmanauts DAO mining pool launched testing with a 5–15 GH/s target hashrate, a 1% treasury fee, and storage rent mining planned for implementation within weeks.

### Rosen Bridge & Palmyra

Rosen Bridge secured 850,000 ADA from Cardano Catalyst Fund 10 (alongside Spectrum DEX and Palmyra/ZenGate), described as accelerating rather than gating core delivery. Kushti announced a Rosen-centric infrastructure hosting workshop to be recorded publicly. Palmyra's use of Chainlink proof-of-reserve via proxy functions in Winter Protocol for on-chain warehouse receipts was detailed: IoT devices (cameras, weight sensors, motion sensors) provide continuous proof-of-existence for commodity reserves, with plans to integrate native Ergo oracles using token sale capital to build out that infrastructure.

### SigmaFi Collateralized Lending

SigmaFi saw notable adoption momentum driven by HodlERG3 (from Phoenix Finance's resurrected hodlcoin game) being used as collateral. The first hodlERG3-backed loans appeared, with users leveraging the game's non-decreasing price mechanics to borrow ERG or SigmaUSD against hodlcoin positions at rates ranging from 10–38% APR. The lending dashboard exhibited a multi-hour display lag despite on-chain confirmation, with SigmaFi reading directly from chain rather than an indexer; clearing browser cache resolved most display issues.

### Paideia DAO

Paideia closed beta shipped fixes for a stake snapshot contract bug, reevaluation failure on quorum miss, and partial unstake prevention (to block "profit sniping"). An automatic DAO treasury transfer post-vote was demonstrated on Ergo; Cardano deployment was underway. Paideia's Catalyst Fund 10 proposal did not pass, noted as surprising given Cardano's governance emphasis.

### ErgoHack VII

ErgoHack VII was announced for October 13–15, 2023 with an 18,000 SigmaUSD prize pool. A community raffle launched to fund additional bounties including TrustWallet WalletCore support, a Blockly-based ErgoScript visual editor, Language Server Protocol integration, and Metamask Snaps. The raffle reached 24.3% of target within one day of launch.

### Storage Rent

Storage rent went live in July 2023; September discussions clarified mechanics: any UTXO dormant for 4+ years incurs a 0.14 ERG charge, with miners able to claim those boxes as revenue. The Nautilus wallet's "wallet optimization" feature consolidates UTXOs, resetting the demurrage clock. Mining pools had not yet prioritized implementing storage rent claiming despite activation.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Ahmad, 2023-09-04): "I need a contract address for ERG to add it to Trust Wallet."
  **A** (@Glasgow): "No contract address — Ergo is a layer 1." ERG is a native asset on its own blockchain, not an EVM token.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-09-04): Chrome update broke app.spectrum.fi, hodlcoin.co.in, and sigmafi.app rendering.
  **A** (@qx()): Clear app data "from all time" in Chrome settings. Cache clear resolved rendering issues for all three sites.

- **Q** (@DrTwentyOne, 2023-09-04): Can users avoid storage rent by transacting, or does the 4-year period still trigger?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Moving assets to a new box resets the demurrage clock. The 4-year dormancy period itself is hard-coded and would require a hard fork to modify; a change was proposed in year one but did not gain support.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-09-04): SigmaFi loan confirmed on-chain but not showing in lender dashboard after 2+ hours.
  **A** (@qx()): On-chain transaction confirmed via explorer. SigmaFi reads directly on-chain without a pre-cached indexer, so display delays can occur. Disconnect/reconnect wallet, try incognito mode, or clear page cache. Issue resolved after several hours; root cause remains unclear.

- **Q** (2023-09-07): P2SH vs P2S — what is P2SH and when is it preferable?
  **A** (@Glasgow): P2SH stores only the first 192 bits of the Blake2b256 hash of the script on-chain; the full script is submitted off-chain at spend time. P2S stores the full script on-chain. P2SH is cheaper but riskier — if the script is lost, funds are permanently inaccessible and dApps become harder for others to interact with. P2SH is relevant for ErgoTree contracts exceeding 4KB and will likely see broader adoption only under heavy on-chain load.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-09-14): Can a node wallet mnemonic be restored in Nautilus?
  **A** (@qx()): Node wallets append a mnemonic password to the seed phrase; Nautilus cannot restore these. Use Satergo (which supports mnemonic passwords) or create a new Nautilus wallet and transfer funds from the node wallet.

- **Q** (@Edgar, 2023-09-14): Will developers reduce block time?
  **A** (@Pulsarz / @kushti): A weak blocks / sub-blocks approach is being explored, similar to Bitcoin NG microblocks targeting ~20-second intervals. Fee distribution between weak and full blocks is TBD. Note: dApps tied to block height rather than timestamps will be affected differently; emission contracts depend on block intervals; and exchanges may require proportionally more confirmations, meaning real finality for users may not improve as expected.

- **Q** (@rubens, 2023-09-09): Is there ongoing debate about the Autolykos difficulty curve? Large entities enter at low difficulty and exit at high spikes, disadvantaging consistent miners.
  **A** (@qx()): This is standard PoW chain-hopping behavior. Miners have the tools to propose changes (e.g., slower difficulty adjustment curves) but lack coordinated incentive to implement them. Core developers are focused on other priorities. Pool operators could propose and lead a solution via a hard fork if motivated.

- **Q** (@XtraKt, 2023-09-19): Should small miners sell ERG to the SigmaUSD contract to support the peg, or does it have the same effect as selling on a CEX?
  **A** (community): SigmaUSD minting is currently locked due to low reserve ratio — not possible. Selling into Dexy Gold when operational provides more ecosystem benefit than CEX sales.

- **Q** (@Richi, 2023-09-19): Can multiple oracle price feeds share a single reward token for better liquidity, rather than each having a unique GORT-type token?
  **A** (community): Each oracle has its own reward token due to fixed epoch-based distribution mechanics. Different protocol fee accruals (Dexy vs. SigmaUSD v2) reward operators differently; separate tokens prevent cross-subsidy complications.

- **Q** (@qx, 2023-09-26): What is the maximum box size, and what is the minimum ERG per box?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Max box size is 4,096 bytes; max transaction size is 96 KB. At 360 nanoERG/byte, the minimum per box is ~1,474.56 nanoERG (~0.00000147456 ERG). In practice, Lilium sends 0.001 ERG and Spectrum sends 0.0002 ERG minimum per box.

- **Q** (@United4life, 2023-09-26): How do you prevent storage rent / demurrage charges?
  **A** (@qx()): Use the Nautilus "wallet optimization" feature (four-box menu icon, far right). This sends all assets to yourself in a new box, resetting the demurrage clock.

- **Q** (@blake starky, 2023-09-26): How do you make an Ergo node publicly visible?
  **A** (@qx()): Open port 9030 (P2P) and 9053 (mobile wallet). Add `scorex.network.nodeName = "YOUR_NAME"` to the node config and restart. The node should appear on ergonodes.net/list within approximately one day.

- **Q** (@Luivatra, 2023-09-30): How can Ergo oracles be used cross-chain on Cardano?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Oracle data stored as inputs on Ergo is publicly readable by any contract, but a subscription model is not possible. Cross-chain use requires a Rosen Bridge-like setup: multi-sig storage of oracle data on Cardano, with oracle pool operations running on Ergo for low fees. Fairly incentivizing oracle operators in a cross-chain context remains an unresolved structural challenge.

- **Q** (2023-09-29): Can contracts be attached to tokens on Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): No — only the inverse is possible. Tokens can be locked in a box protected by a contract, but contracts cannot be attached to tokens themselves.

- **Q** (2023-09-29): How much does Ergo blockchain data compress with NIPoPoWs and UTXO snapshots?
  **A** (community): NIPoPoWs combined with a UTXO snapshot enables approximately 100 MB for full security verification of historical chain state. This compares favorably to Solana's ~100TB+ yearly ledger growth — Ergo's entire ledger fits on a few dollars of consumer storage.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Dexy Gold mainnet timeline**: All prerequisite steps (GORT LP, buyback contract, testnet UI) confirmed; no specific launch date given. "Very soon" stated multiple times without commitment.
- **Weak blocks / sub-blocks implementation**: Approach agreed in principle; fee distribution model, orphan rate tolerance, wallet UX updates, and naming convention all unresolved. A testnet group exists but needs a motivated leader.
- **SigmaUSD reserve recovery**: Reserve ratio too low to mint; no concrete plan to restore utility. Dexy Gold positioned as alternative but does not solve existing SigmaUSD liquidity shortfall on Spectrum.

- **Autolykos difficulty curve**: Pool-hopping disadvantaging consistent miners acknowledged; no miner-led proposal or hard fork process initiated.
- **Cross-chain oracle incentives for Cardano**: How to fairly compensate oracle operators for Cardano integrations without a subscription model remains structurally unsolved.
- **Metamask Snaps integration**: ErgoHack VII raffle bounty offered; no delivery timeline established.
- **Satergo Ledger support**: Hardware communication non-functional despite programmatic work; blocker unidentified.
- **Rosen Bridge launch readiness**: Infrastructure workshop scheduled; production launch date TBD.

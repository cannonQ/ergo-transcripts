# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Month:** 2019-01
**Chat:** Ergo General

---

## Executive Summary

January 2019 was dominated by anticipation of Ergo's imminent mainnet launch, with the community focused on understanding the EFYT token mechanics, early mining setup, and Ergo's unique cryptographic capabilities. Core developer Dmitry (kushti) was actively engaging the community through bounties, technical Q&A, and documentation pointers, laying the groundwork for developer onboarding ahead of launch.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Mainnet Launch Anticipation
The community was in a holding pattern awaiting mainnet, with estimates ranging from "end of January" to "within 4 weeks" as of early February. The voting mechanism was announced as live during this period. The Ergo Data Chain was formally deferred to post-mainnet design work, with kushti stating the team had not yet begun thinking about it.

### EFYT Token Mechanics
Significant community discussion clarified the role of EFYT (Ergo First Year Token), issued on the Waves blockchain. It was used to fund development (salaries, office rent) via OTC sales and the Waves DEX. The swap to native ERG would occur after mainnet launch. The total ERG supply was confirmed at 97,739,925 coins via the published emission curve. Community members seeking pre-mainnet exposure were directed to the Waves DEX as the only available option.

### ErgoScript Developer Engagement
kushti offered a 100 EFYT (~$200) bounty for original ErgoScript examples, pointing developers to the sigmastate-interpreter test suite and Sigma protocol whitepaper as learning resources. This was an early attempt to bootstrap developer education ahead of mainnet, with kushti acknowledging significant friction was expected given the novelty of the language.

### Ergo's Cryptographic Differentiators
Community members explored Ergo's unique technical capabilities relative to Ethereum and other chains: NIPoPoWs supported from genesis, scalable sidechains, Sigma protocols, ring signatures, cheap mixing, and 2-of-3 multisig without signer disclosure. Autolykos (the PoW algorithm) naming and design rationale were also discussed, with references to the mythological Autolycus origin.

### Early Mining & Node Setup
A developer asked about configuring solo mining on the Ergo testnet. The response directed them to kushti's node setup blog post and the `application.conf` mining parameter. GPU miners were noted as not yet available at this stage.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Gozzy, 2019-01-11): Where can EFYT be purchased, and was it exclusive to airdrop applicants?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): EFYT was partly airdropped and partly sold on the Waves DEX. Two coins (ERGOmoney and ERGOdata) will be minted at mainnet launch. Waves DEX is the current purchase venue.

- **Q** (@Mu 🚀, 2019-01-11): Is there a whitepaper available?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Final version still being completed; draft available in the GitHub yellow papers folder.

- **Q** (@Gozzy, 2019-01-11): Are there learning materials on Ergo smart contracts?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Sigma protocol whitepaper available at `sigmastate-interpreter/docs/wpaper/`; examples in the test suite; the Sigma protocol paper provides extensive contract examples in LaTeX format.

- **Q** (@Евгений, 2019-02-06): How do I set up solo mining on the Ergo testnet?
  **A** (@Unknown): Set up a full Ergo node per kushti's blog post, then enable mining by setting the mining parameter to `true` in `application.conf`. GPU miners are not yet available.

- **Q** (@Gozzy, 2019-02-06): Is there thorough documentation for the Scorex framework?
  **A** (@Dmitry): See the Scorex Tutorial PDF and presentation videos on GitHub/YouTube. A toy blockchain implementation for students is also available as supplementary material.

- **Q** (@Nikolai, 2019-02-07): Does Ergo allow creation of colored coins (tokens)?
  **A** (@Unknown): Yes. Ergo supports tokens, NIPoPoWs from genesis, and scalable sidechains from launch.

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: 2021-03

---

## Executive Summary

March 2021 was dominated by the SigmaUSD v1 oracle front-running exploit, its aftermath, and the extended effort to deploy a hardened v2 protocol. Alongside this, the month saw Ergo's first significant exchange listing on BiKi, growing Ergo–Cardano integration signals from Charles Hoskinson and IOHK, and meaningful progress on both the Spectrum DEX specification and the Oracle Pool governance framework.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### SigmaUSD v1 Exploit, Freeze, and v2 Relaunch

A whale exploited the hourly oracle update latency in the v1 AgeUSD protocol by front-running price posts — buying SigmaUSD at stale prices before updates, then exiting profitably, draining value from SigRSV holders. The Ergo Foundation froze the oracle pool at $3.16/ERG on 2021-03-04 to prevent further damage. The UI's 0.25% fee was simultaneously removed by the anonymous developer (anon-real/DarkSideOfBrrrrr), reducing total redemption cost to 1%. Community discussion was extensive: early SigRSV holders who minted at 5,000–7,000% reserve ratios experienced ~7% losses on redemption. Philosophical debate emerged about zero-governance immutable protocols versus upgradeable governed systems, with no community consensus reached.

SigmaUSD v2 development proceeded throughout the month, with oracle refresh intervals reduced from 60 minutes to 15 minutes (later confirmed at 12 minutes), fees raised from 1% to 2% (final deployed total 3.25%), and a 0.5% per-posting smoothing cap added — requiring a whale to hold 2.5+ hours and absorb ~4.5% round-trip fees to profit. The new oracle pool was confirmed running at 12-minute intervals by end of March, with the v2 contract deployed by DarkSide and the UI expected the following week. v1 was moved to old.sigmausd.io.

### BiKi Exchange Listing

ERG was listed on BiKi with deposit opening around 2021-03-10, trading delayed multiple days before going live on 2021-03-18 at 18:00 GMT+8. Day-one volume was reported as solid. The listing was accompanied by a promotional airdrop distributing 1,000 ERG to early participants, which caused significant Telegram spam but brought roughly 300 new members to the community. CoinEx experienced extended maintenance affecting ERG deposits and withdrawals throughout late March. Community debate continued about exchange listing strategy, with consensus forming around organic growth via technology delivery over expensive tier-1 listings — Binance fees of ~$250k plus token supply requirements were cited as reasons to prioritize building the Ergo DEX instead.

### Spectrum DEX (ErgoDEX) Development

EIP-0014 specification advanced significantly during the month, with GitHub PR #27 merged showing combined AMM and order book designs. The eUTXO model's unique capability to support both AMM liquidity pools and order book contracts on the same chain — sharing liquidity between exchange types — was highlighted as a differentiator unavailable on other blockchains. Working groups were formed for both approaches. ErgoSwap was confirmed targeting H1 2021 delivery, described as dependent on SigmaUSD v2 deployment. Community discussion covered the significance of order book DEXes for transparency, slippage control, and partial order fills.

### Ergo–Cardano Integration and Charles Hoskinson Signal

Charles Hoskinson publicly highlighted Ergo in an IOHK presentation and confirmed he holds only three cryptocurrencies: BTC, ADA, and ERG — calling Ergo "the most underrated project in crypto." kushti appeared on the Cardano 360 livestream discussing the eUTXO model and the Ergo–Cardano relationship. The Emurgo partnership was confirmed, with the Yoroi dApp connector (a MetaMask-like browser extension for Ergo) expected within a week of early March, initially for Ergo with Cardano interoperability planned when Plutus launched. Community framing emphasized Ergo as a complementary DeFi layer to Cardano rather than a competitor.

### Oracle Pool Governance and Decentralization

The oracle freeze prompted extended community discussion about Oracle Pool centralization — the pools are run by "a dozen non-anonymous people with governance tokens" who can freeze price feeds. kushti posted a governance proposal on the Ergoforum covering oracle pool and SigmaUSD bank governance. The forum thread established a structure for community input. Decentralization of admin keys was confirmed as planned but without a timeline. Debate continued about financial stake-based versus contribution-based governance token distribution, with no consensus reached.

### Mining: Autolykos v2, Hashrate Growth, and Pool Centralization

Autolykos v2's pool-resistance (Non-Outsourceable PoW) was removed in a February hardfork to enable fair GPU pooled mining, with memory-hardness retained as the primary ASIC defense. The network saw rapid hashrate growth throughout March. NBMiner added Ergo support for NVIDIA GPUs on approximately 2021-03-19. GTX 1060 3GB and RX 570 cards were confirmed profitable for at least 2–3 years given the DAG growth schedule (5% every 3 months starting at block ~600k). Nanopool exceeded 51% of network hashrate by late March, raising centralization concerns; kushti clarified that Ergo's self-amendable protocol treats supermajority miner consensus as governance rather than attack.

### NFT Auction House

ErgoAuctions received UI improvements and added audio NFT support. Community interest grew in creating NFT collections comparable to Cardano projects like SpaceBudz. Recurring bugs were reported with the minting process (requiring dual sends of ERG and token separately) and with preview image display (requiring a specific checkbox selection). anon_real acknowledged the reports but root causes were not fully resolved during the month.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@cymatic, 2021-03-05): What are bulletproofs?
  **A** (@kushti): A zero-knowledge technique for proving specific statements (e.g., range proofs as used in Monero) without a trusted setup — a trustless alternative to SNARKs. Working to enable bulletproof verification in ErgoScript, which would allow a variety of zero-knowledge protocols (bulletproofs, Groth one-out-of-many, etc.) alongside Ergo contracts for hybrid execution with data privacy.

- **Q** (@cymatic, 2021-03-10): What were the v2 SigRSV protection mechanisms?
  **A** (@cymatic): Fees raised from 1% to 2% per transaction; oracles post every 12–15 minutes (vs. 60 minutes); 0.5% smoothing cap per posting. A whale must incur ~4.5% in round-trip fees and wait 2.5+ hours to profit — making the v1 exploit uneconomical.

- **Q** (@Pizza, 2021-03-11): How is it possible that pool-resistant Ergo can be mined on pools?
  **A** (@btt / @Glasgow): Pool-resistance (Non-Outsourceable PoW) was removed in the Autolykos v2 hardfork to enable fair pooled GPU mining. Memory-hardness is now the primary defense against ASICs. Reference: "Bypassing Non-Outsourceable Proof-of-Work Schemes Using Collateralized Smart Contracts" (Chepurnoy, FC 2020).

- **Q** (@Gazza, 2021-03-16): What are the differences between Ergo's Oracle Pools and Chainlink's oracle architecture?
  **A** (@cymatic): Linked to Robert Kornacki's comparison post on the Ergoforum showing architectural differences — Oracle Pools use an on-chain pool-based model with ERG-denominated incentives and governance tokens, distinct from Chainlink's network model.

- **Q** (@c8, 2021-03-16): Can a single Oracle Pool produce multiple price pairs instead of deploying separate pools?
  **A** (@c8): Creating a EUR stablecoin is technically straightforward: deploy a new oracle pool for the ERG-EUR pair and redeploy the AgeUSD contract pointing to the new oracle. Effectively a contract copy with a different price feed.

- **Q** (@Brian Kent, 2021-03-19): How do you deploy simple ErgoScript smart contracts?
  **A** (@cymatic): Contracts are not deployed externally. They are compiled into strings and deployed *within* UTXO boxes in transactions — the contract script is included directly in the transaction box.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-03-19): How did the Ergo team freeze the SigmaUSD oracle pool? Who controls Oracle Pools?
  **A** (@Community): The Ergo team retains admin keys. The pools are not yet decentralized, but decentralization is planned for the future.

- **Q** (@Ilya Sofronov, 2021-03-19): How long will a GTX 1060 3GB remain profitable for Ergo mining given DAG growth?
  **A** (@kushti): A couple of years minimum. The DAG grows 5% every 3 months starting at block ~600k.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-03-22): Can Ergo be easily 51% attacked and what would the consequences be?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Ergo's self-amendable protocol means a miner supermajority is treated as governance, not an attack. Parameters like block size and computational cost can be adjusted through on-chain miner voting to prevent rented hashpower attacks. The honest mining assumption holds: if 51% want to change the chain, that is voting.

- **Q** (@Olli Tiainen, 2021-W09): What is Ergo's vision?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Ergo enables new financial interaction models via flexible Sigma protocols accessible to developers. It provides superior support for real-world financial agreements through multi-stage contracts, ErgoScript, formal verification, easy oracle creation, and complex signature schemes. The use case is "contractual money."

- **Q** (@JCFishing, 2021-W09): Does Ergo have an oracle solution that can compete with Chainlink?
  **A** (@Glasgow): "They're much better than Chainlink's." Oracle Pools provide a fundamentally new oracle model. See: https://www.ergoforum.org/t/oracle-pools-a-new-oracle-model/263

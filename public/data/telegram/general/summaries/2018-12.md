# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: 2018-12

## Executive Summary

December 2018 was a pre-launch period focused on TestNet progression, mining specifications, and community onboarding ahead of the anticipated MainNet launch. The dominant themes were establishing a realistic MainNet timeline, clarifying the storage rent/demurrage mechanism, and expanding the development team. Activity was relatively low but substantive, with core developers Dmitry and kushti directly engaging community questions about technical architecture and project roadmap.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### MainNet Launch Timeline
Initial community expectations of a January 2019 MainNet launch were corrected by Dmitry to a more realistic March (optimistic) to May (less optimistic) 2019 estimate, contingent on completing TestNet versions 1.9 and 2.0 without major bugs and passing a security audit. TestNet v1.8.0 and v1.8.1 shipped during the month, with v1.9 announced as imminent at month's end.

### Mining Specifications
The base proof-of-work algorithm was confirmed as Equihash, GPU-friendly with a minimum 2GB GPU memory requirement. CPU mining was available on TestNet but described as highly inefficient. GPU miner implementation was scheduled to begin following the TestNet 1.8 release. No power consumption benchmarks were yet available and were deferred until GPU miner code was published.

### Storage Rent / Demurrage Mechanism
Community members raised concerns about long-term holders losing coins. The mechanism was clarified: UTXOs inactive for four years can have their coins recirculated to prevent deflation analogous to Bitcoin's fixed-supply deflationary pressure. Moving coins to a new wallet resets the dormancy timer. The four-year period was chosen as a balance between user-friendliness and system health relative to the eight-year emission schedule.

### EFYT → ERG Swap and Token Supply
Total ERG supply was confirmed at 100 million. The EFYT-to-ERG swap was explained as dependent on ERG mining going live, with estimates of Q2 2019 at the earliest and late 2019–early 2020 as more realistic. Specific swap mechanics, price parity, and distribution windows remained unspecified at month's end.

### Developer Team and Repository Structure
The development team was clarified as distributed across multiple GitHub repositories rather than concentrated in a single repo, explaining why GitHub contributor counts appeared low. Two additional developers were confirmed as being hired for formal code verification work. The repository ownership structure was publicly disclosed for the first time.

### Exchange Listing Strategy
The listing approach was clarified as a two-stage process: secure principle agreement with a major exchange before MainNet, with the actual listing occurring after MainNet launch. No specific exchange names were disclosed.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-12-06): What are the mining requirements for Ergo?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Base algorithm is Equihash, GPU-friendly. Minimum 2GB GPU memory required. Full details to be published alongside GPU miner code release.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-12-06): Any power consumption tests done yet?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Not yet; GPU miner implementation will follow immediately after the TestNet 1.8 release, at which point benchmarks will be available.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-12-06): Team size concerns — only 2 active developers visible on GitHub?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Ergo development is spread across multiple repositories with distributed contributors: kushti and oskin1 on the main `ergoplatform/ergo` repo; ergomorphic and greenhat on `sigmastate-interpreter`; VictorMikheev on `scorex-util`. Contributor count per repo understates total team size.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-12-06): Will Ergo have a web3 or MetaMask equivalent?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Work is planned post-TestNet 2 but is not required for MainNet launch. The goal is to deliver it as soon as possible, on a timeline independent from MainNet deployment.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-12-30): How does storage rent (demurrage) affect long-term holders — if coins are inactive for four years, do they disappear?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Dormant coins are recirculated after four years, preventing the deflationary pressure seen in Bitcoin where lost coins permanently reduce circulating supply. Simply moving coins to a new wallet resets the dormancy timer. The four-year period was chosen as a practical balance: enough time for any active user to perform at least one transaction, while maintaining system health relative to the eight-year emission schedule.

- **Q** (@Mu 🚀, 2018-12-30): What is the total ERG supply, and when can the EFYT→ERG swap occur?
  **A** (@Unknown): Total supply is 100 million ERG. The swap requires ERG mining to be live, estimated no earlier than Q2 2019, with late 2019–early 2020 being more realistic. Swap mechanics and price parity have not yet been specified.

- **Q** (@Gozzy, 2019-01-06): I have a physics background and am interested in learning Scorex and Scala — where should I start?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Run the TestNet and connect with DMeshkov for collaboration. Scala is identified as high long-term value for blockchain core development. No specific reading materials were recommended in the discussion.

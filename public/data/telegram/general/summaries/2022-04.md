# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: April 2022

---

## Executive Summary

April 2022 was dominated by three converging themes: EIP-27 emission reduction moving from testnet toward a confirmed activation height, a broad multi-week discussion on Ergo's scalability roadmap (sub-blocks, weak blocks, L2 frameworks, and sidechain designs), and wallet ecosystem turbulence as Yoroi failures accelerated community migration to Nautilus, SafeW, Satergo, and Minotaur. Ledger hardware wallet integration made visible progress but remained unfinished throughout the month, while major ecosystem content — including the ErgoPulse roundtable featuring Charles Hoskinson — generated significant community engagement.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EIP-27: Emission Reduction & Activation

EIP-27 progressed from early testnet testing to a confirmed activation height over the course of April. The first indicative vote was completed in W14, with the activation procedure refined to require 90% miner support within any epoch before triggering. A DevNet round identified and resolved issues, and by W16 the activation height was set at approximately block 777,217 (~June 21st). The block reward reduction is 12 ERG per block upon activation, with the next scheduled 3 ERG reduction following at block ~784,800. EIP-27 tokens were confirmed live by W17, extending the mining emission schedule. Pools and miners were advised to prepare accounting changes. No fixed hard deadline was announced, and formal voting mechanism details were not fully elaborated in chat.

### Wallet Ecosystem: Migration Away from Yoroi

Yoroi experienced widespread sync and hanging failures throughout the month, particularly for wallets with large transaction histories (e.g., mining wallets). The community response was consistent across all four weeks: seed phrases are portable across all Ergo wallets, and users were directed to restore in Nautilus, SafeW, Satergo, or Minotaur. SafeW became available on the Chrome Web Store during W16. A UX issue specific to Nautilus was identified in W17 — privacy mode enabled by default creates derived addresses that the Android wallet cannot detect, causing apparent balance discrepancies. @MrStahlfelge confirmed this should be disabled by default but the fix was not shipped during the month.

### Ledger Hardware Wallet Integration

Ledger support was a recurring topic across all four weeks. The core delay was attributed to developer relocation due to the Russia-Ukraine conflict. By W15, the Tesseract team had identified and resolved a signing issue. In W16, the developer confirmed the bug fix was complete and the app had moved to private testing and app store approval. By W17, wallet developers confirmed they had all required materials and were in active testing. As of month's end the integration remained unreleased, with "v. close" the strongest commitment offered.

### Scalability: Sub-blocks, Weak Blocks, and L2

A substantive multi-participant scalability discussion took place primarily in W17 (2022-04-28 roundtable with kushti, scalahub, Cheese, Ilya Oskin) but threads ran across the whole month. Topics included:
- **Sub-blocks**: Documented in scaling docs; improve transaction inclusion speed but do not provide finality equivalent to PoS chains.
- **Weak blocks**: Described by kushti as share-like constructs (difficulty/N) currently discarded; could be rewarded via smart contracts and are relevant to merge-mined sidechain designs.
- **Transaction propagation**: Node 4.0.16 merged an optimised sync protocol; a planned rework from push to pull mode would allow nodes to filter high-cost transactions and reduce bottlenecks.
- **JIT costing (5.0)**: Will enable miners to propose increased TPS; mempool transaction sorting identified as currently unfair to computationally heavy transactions, with a fix planned around 5.0 activation.
- **Sidechain security**: NIPoPoWs discussed as challenging for Bitcoin bridging; SPV approaches and federated models explored by scalahub for Ergo-BTC bridge.
- **ErgoDex scaling**: Hybrid sidechain/cross-chain approach with Roll-DPos consensus teased; detailed article promised for May.

kushti noted that unlike chains that deploy sharding or L2 from day one on empty chains, Ergo now has a concrete set of deployed contracts (Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, AMM pools, Trade House, lending contracts, SigmaUSD) that provide a realistic benchmark for optimisation.

### Rosen Bridge & Cross-Chain Development

Rosen Bridge was mentioned across several weeks as under active development, with the Master Developer working on implementation and Cardano expected to be the first deployment. No confirmed timeline was provided beyond community speculation of ~3 months. scalahub noted that NIPoPoWs are not straightforward for Bitcoin bridging, and federated relay approaches were being evaluated for the Ergo-BTC bridge.

### ErgoPulse & Major Community Content

The month opened with the ErgoPulse video release (W14), a pre-recorded roundtable in Dubai featuring Charles Hoskinson, kushti, DanielTetsuyama, and Armeanio. This was cited as a major ecosystem milestone. A Reddit AMA replaced a planned YouTube AMA in W15 due to a 2FA access issue. A weekly AMA series continued throughout the month (April 8, 22, 29), covering EIP-27, scalability, exchange listings, and ecosystem updates.

### Exchange Listings

Two tier-1 CEX integrations were confirmed as in progress throughout W15–W16, with node, wallet, matching engine, and testing stages all required per integration. Specific exchange names were not confirmed; Binance, Huobi, Coinbase, and FTX were mentioned speculatively by community members. Armeanio cautioned against Binance listing as premature during market downturn. No official announcements were made during the month.

### CoinMarketCap Supply Discrepancy

CoinMarketCap continued to report an incorrect circulating supply of ~32M ERG throughout the month, versus the correct ~54M ERG available from the Ergo API and reported by CoinGecko. The issue was flagged repeatedly with no resolution or correction timeline from CMC.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Pongko, 2022-04-05): Why does Ergo difficulty decrease when nethash increases?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Linked to Autolykos difficulty adjustment mechanism — uses average of last 5 epochs (1024 blocks each). See https://docs.ergoplatform.com/dev/protocol/autolykos/#difficulty-adjustment

- **Q** (@Fabulaowski, 2022-04-06): Does receiving mining rewards reset the storage rent 4-year clock?
  **A** (@Glasgow): No. Storage rent applies per UTXO only if unmoved for 4 years. Receiving funds does not reset the timer on an existing box.

- **Q** (@Koutelier, 2022-04-12): How to acquire all addresses holding a specific token?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Use Ergo Explorer API: `api.ergoplatform.com/api/v1/boxes/unspent/byTokenId/<tokenid>?offset=0&limit=100`. Loop with increasing offset to retrieve all holders (~10k addresses possible).

- **Q** (@Ole Isler, 2022-04-19): Can I guarantee access to SigmaUSD funds in my Yoroi wallet if Yoroi fails?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Funds are not stored in Yoroi — they exist on the Ergo blockchain. Restore your seed phrase in any Ergo wallet (Nautilus, SafeW, Satergo) to access them. The seed phrase is the key, not the wallet software.

- **Q** (@Kallo Shaman, 2022-04-20): If I use multiple addresses in one wallet, can others detect they're linked?
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge): Only if you share your extended public key. Without sharing it, there is no way for others to know addresses belong to the same wallet.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-04-28): Can ErgoPay enable multi-wallet transaction signatures where all parties must sign before execution?
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge): ErgoPay protocol supports it and the transaction structure is compatible. Limitation: no current UI or AppKit implementation for multi-key signing. For true multisig escrow, either use multiple transactions or embed the logic in a smart contract.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-04-28): How to build an escrow service where two parties atomically swap assets?
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge): Place the token in a box secured by a smart contract defining: (1) an outbox with the counterparty's p2pk address plus agreed ERG amount, (2) a block height timeout check, (3) a second outbox with token to counterparty's address. The buyer executes if conditions are met; the seller can reclaim after timeout. The contract controls execution, not wallet signatures. See Auction House and AgeUSD contracts for real-world examples.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-04-28): Are weak blocks analogous to mining pool shares? Who receives rewards?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes, a weak block is effectively a share. Currently they are discarded. They could be rewarded via smart contracts — rewards sourced from the block producer or the protocol itself. This is directly relevant to merge-mined sidechain designs where weak block producers need incentivisation.

- **Q** (@Cheese, 2022-04-28): Do NIPoPoWs enable trustless two-way pegs like ZK-rollups on Ethereum?
  **A** (@scalahub): NIPoPoWs are challenging for Bitcoin bridging because Bitcoin does not natively support them. Researching SPV approaches for an Ergo-BTC bridge; currently leaning toward federated solutions for security.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-04-28): Do sub-blocks provide finality equivalent to PoS chains' few-second finality?
  **A** (@kushti): Sub-blocks speed transaction inclusion but do not provide PoS-equivalent finality. PoS fast finality claims assume fast global propagation, which is unrealistic for blocks supporting heavy smart contracts. Few-second PoW finality would require the fastest global block propagation, which conflicts with meaningful block time design.

- **Q** (@Fabulaowski, 2022-04-28): How are seed phrases generated offline, and can two paper wallets generate the same address?
  **A** (@Kallo Shaman, @Glasgow): Addresses exist mathematically across a 256-bit entropy space (~10^77 possibilities). The probability of collision is astronomically low — characterised as less probable than quantum tunnelling through a desk. The seed phrase is a representation of a point in that space, not a wallet-specific construct.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-04-13): What is the impact of faster block times on dApps that rely on block height rather than timestamps?
  **A** (@kushti): dApps relying on block height (not timestamp) are affected by block speed changes — emission contracts, tail emission schedules, and timeout logic would all be impacted. Timestamp-agnostic designs mitigate some risk but not all. Security considerations around worst-case scenarios informed the original block time choice.

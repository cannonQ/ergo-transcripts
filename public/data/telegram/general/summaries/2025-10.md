# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: October 2025

---

## Executive Summary

October 2025 was a landmark month for Ergo, defined by three converging themes: major protocol milestones (6.0 soft fork activation, block reward reduction to 9 ERG), a surge of new financial infrastructure (USE stablecoin preparation, Mew lending audit, Oracle Pool v2 deployment), and deep community debate around PoW economics inspired by Kadena's bankruptcy. Developer productivity was notably high, with kushti systematically building a contract library and multiple ecosystem projects reaching testable states.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### 6.0 Soft Fork Activation
The 6.0 protocol soft fork activated successfully on mainnet on 2025-10-06 with full consensus between 5.0 and 6.0 nodes. The activation enables 6.0-only transactions to propagate while 5.0 nodes can continue running indefinitely. kushti noted that sub-blocks implementation — a frequently discussed follow-on upgrade — still depends on broader community consensus and miner voting rather than any pre-agreed schedule. A community testnet (DevNet) running on CPUs was made available for parameter tweaking experiments, with kushti inviting interested community members to join via the ErgoDevelopers Telegram group. The activation also enabled the vibe-coded trustless Bitcoin client and hashrate oracle work that followed immediately after.

### Block Reward Emission & PoW Economics
The scheduled block reward reduction to 9 ERG per block occurred on 2025-10-05. This triggered extended community discussion about PoW emission economics, framed by Kadena's collapse. Kadena's premine distribution model — where mining parity with presale holders would not be reached until 2033 — was analyzed as a fundamental failure of open money design. Armeanio introduced the "parity metric" (when miner block rewards cumulatively equal presale/premine allocations) as the most important measure of fairness in a PoW network, with Ergo's rapid parity contrasted favorably. The discussion broadened into infrastructure resilience: merged mining was proposed as a long-term security subsidy for post-emission Ergo, and storage rent was highlighted (~16.64M ERG collected from ~201K boxes) as a unique anti-spam and secondary emission mechanism with no comparable implementation on other PoW chains.

### Contract Library & Developer Infrastructure
kushti undertook a systematic vibe-coding effort throughout October, reviewing design patterns from ErgoForum (since 2019) and ErgoHack submissions and converting them into reviewed, tested ErgoScript contracts. Four PRs were merged (Perpetual Token, Daily Withdrawal Limit, Stealth Address, Whitelisted Transfers) with additional work in progress on Trustless Hashrate Oracle, Bulletproof Range Proof Verification, Schnorr Signature Verification, and insurance contracts. The explicit goal is an open-source contract library compatible with an ErgoScript MCP for developer accessibility. A digital twin insurance ErgoScript application was also completed. The ErgoBook was announced for revival after the sub-blocks whitepaper publication.

### USE Stablecoin & Dexy Protocol
The USE stablecoin project — a Dexy-based crypto-backed algorithmic USD-pegged stablecoin — progressed significantly. A new USD Oracle Pool launched, Oracle Pool v2 began running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards, and USE parameters were being finalized toward a mid/late-November Community Liquidity Bootstrapping (CLB) event. The CLB mechanism plans to aggregate rsERG from multiple chains (including Cardano) as collateral via Rosen Bridge. USE is explicitly distinct from SigmaUSD in design and is positioned for greater trader attractiveness. The USE stablecoin public Telegram group was created at https://t.me/USE_stablecoin.

### Rosen Bridge Development & Cross-Chain Strategy
Rosen Bridge activity was substantial throughout the month: 125K rsERG transferred from Cardano to Ergo was clocked at fast execution, a Runes strategy for cross-chain assets was published, and the token map is expected to move on-chain to allow asset addition without infrastructure updates. A streamlined proof URI scheme to reduce copy-paste errors in bridge workflows was discussed. Armeanio stepped back from CEX listing involvement to focus on Rosen Bridge development. Community debate on Near Protocol's "intents" mechanism concluded that it rebrands 2012-era atomic swaps and still requires a backend marketplace/market-makers, whereas Rosen Bridge covers both cross-chain swaps and cross-chain liquidity pools natively. COMET cross-chain bridging via Rosen was also announced.

### New Financial Primitives: Mew Lending, ChainCash/Basis, GitCircles
Three new financial infrastructure projects advanced in parallel. The Mew peer-to-peer lending protocol passed a full smart contract audit with zero vulnerabilities: lenders specify asset/amount/block height/collateral; borrowers pay a fee and lock collateral; lenders claim collateral on default. Frontend refinement was underway at month-end. The Basis Protocol — a simplified ChainCash variant with off-chain cash backed by on-chain reserves — deployed its first reserve contract on mainnet, with a tracker server under testing and wallet development initiated. GitCircles, an open-source developer payment system, expanded GitHub contribution tracking to new communities and was described as one PR away from broader deployment. All three were framed by kushti as Ergo's leadership in reworking money issuance from top-down to bottom-up approaches.

### Lithos Protocol Testnet
Lithos Protocol announced a public testnet opening the week of November 2, featuring on-chain mining pools with transparent payouts, miner-controlled blocks, and non-interactive share proofs for scalability and efficiency. Kirat Singh (CheeseEnthusiast) presented Lithos at the Ergo Meetup 2025 in Colorado Springs (October 11) as a blockchain-agnostic GPU mining solution.

### Ergo Meetup 2025
The Ergo Meetup 2025 took place in Colorado Springs on October 11 (setup Friday, main event Saturday, optional Sunday activities). Speakers included Armeanio, mgPai/Luca, CheeseEnthusiast (Lithos Protocol), CannonQ, Marc the Shark (mining), and anon_br (Machina Finance). The event was livestreamed and recorded. Positive sentiment on upcoming development cycles was noted following the event.

### Infrastructure & GraphQL API Issues
Nautilus wallet's new requirements caused the main GraphQL servers to require updating, temporarily breaking Spectrum DEX and wallet functionality for some users. qx() provided a comprehensive list of functioning explorers, APIs, and GraphQL endpoints (P2P, Cornell, CodeUTXO, SigmaSpace) and confirmed that Nautilus GQL v0.5.5 was deployed with load balancers updated and an outdated API node fixed (circulation calculation bug) by end of month. Nautilus Ledger integration remained in flux — a GitHub update was available but reported non-functional in user testing; the developer was traveling, with no production ETA provided.

### PoW Ecosystem Politics & Community Governance
Kadena's bankruptcy prompted kushti to propose a roundtable with community-driven blockchains (Ergo, Nervos, Kaspa, Bitcoin Cash) vs. company-owned chains. Alephium's governance transition was also criticized — the team declined to join a Twitter Spaces discussion. The EF's legal status was clarified: registered as a DAO LLC in the Marshall Islands approximately two years ago, with @glasgowm authorized to sign US CEX listings. Armeanio confirmed Ergo as a fair-launched PoW coin with no single party controlling listing decisions. An Ergo X raiders group (https://t.me/ergraider) was established for coordinated social media awareness and FUD refutation.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2025-10-06): Can Ergo see sub-blocks implemented this year given years of discussion?
  **A** (@kushti): Sub-blocks implementation depends on community consensus and miner voting, not pre-agreement. Unlike developer-dictatorships (ZEC/ETH), Ergo miners must vote on protocol updates. No activation timeline exists without broader agreement. A DevNet is running on CPUs; interested parties should join https://t.me/ErgoDevelopers.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2025-10-20): NIPoPoW bootstrap sync time on Android?
  **A** (@Unknown, msg#578717): ~2 hours from scratch on Google Pixel 8 using NIPoPoW bootstrap + RocksDB, vs. 5 days for full sync. Enables personal nodes usable immediately with Ergo Wallet at 127.0.0.1:9053.

- **Q** (@T F, 2025-10-16): Does ChainCash work as follows — company locks ERG on-chain, issues off-chain notes to employees who circulate them, original issuer handles redemption for a small fee?
  **A** (@Glasgow): "Sounds about right." Reference: https://docs.ergoplatform.com/uses/chaincash/#practical-examples. Suggested using custom GPTs (AskTheCode, Chat with Code) for repository analysis.

- **Q** (@T F, 2025-10-16): In ChainCash, if notes can be redeemed against "any previous holder's reserves," do I become liable for backing a note I accepted and later spent?
  **A**: Unresolved — clarification needed on ChainCash holder liability in multi-holder scenarios.

- **Q** (@HQΣr, 2025-10-27): How to create Babel boxes for MewMart integration?
  **A** (@Glasgow): https://docs.ergoplatform.com/dev/protocol/tx/babel-impl/

- **Q** (@kushti, 2025-10-20): Why does Spectrum DEX LP creation require a new address / box consolidation?
  **A** (@Aco Šmrkas): Jank LP transaction creation code makes 2 chained transactions that often fail if multiple boxes exist. Never properly investigated; consolidating to a single address works around the issue.

- **Q** (@Grayman, 2025-10-23): Why not increase oracle security by having the gold oracle pool also report 50 and 200-day moving averages?
  **A** (Discussion): Proposed storing historical on-chain data to construct moving averages; attenuates damage from rogue/offline data sources. Weighted averages across LBMA/Comex/Shanghai could improve resilience. Gold oracle currently priced in USD (depends on USD oracle). No implementation commitment made.

- **Q** (@Patato, 2025-10-26): Is BIP39 passphrase support planned for Ergo Android and iOS wallets?
  **A** (@kushti): Feature exists in Ergo Wallet node but industry trend is rejecting BIP39 passphrases; considering hiding the field behind a checkbox. Satergo supports it; Minotaur does as well. Modularity in address generation needed across chains.

- **Q** (@rdot1477, 2025-10-31): Quantum threat to Ergo (re: Nic Carter post)?
  **A** (@kushti): Theoretically possible but practically distant — no quantum computer capable of breaking 128-bit discrete log is observable in the foreseeable future.

- **Q** (@Grayman, 2025-10-28): What's the mechanics behind a Dexy-based flatcoin using dual treasuries?
  **A** (@Grayman): Would require dual treasuries (risk-on: ERG/RWA; risk-off: SigmaUSD/stables) plus keeper bots for macro monitoring. Dexy Gold already fulfills the gold-peg use case.

- **Q** (@cnftharvey, 2025-10-31): Would a "zap-in" single-token liquidity provision feature work on Ergo?
  **A** (@cannon_q): A service could implement this; thin pools may cause issues with slippage.

- **Q** (@Jayedii, 2025-10-06): What does "trustless Bitcoin client on Ergo" mean?
  **A** (@kushti, implied): Building a trustless Bitcoin client on top of existing trustless relay implementation, enabled by 6.0 activation — demonstrated via PR #1085 hashrate oracle work.

- **Q** (@Quibbix, 2025-09-29): What is the difference between SigmaUSD and USE?
  **A** (@kushti/context): USE is Dexy-based, crypto-backed, and algorithmically USD-pegged — distinct in design and trader attractiveness from SigmaUSD, which continues to exist independently. Stablecoin pairs (USE/SigmaUSD) are considered useful for ecosystem depth.

- **Q** (@impulse, 2025-10-31): Are there flatcoin plans for Ergo?
  **A** (@Grayman): Dexy Gold fulfills the gold-peg use case. A flatcoin would require dual treasuries plus keeper bots; remains exploratory.

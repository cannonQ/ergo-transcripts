# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Developer Knowledge Base Summary
**Month:** July 2021

---

## Executive Summary

July 2021 was a milestone month for Ergo, marked by the platform's 2nd anniversary (Ergoversary), a wave of exchange listing announcements, and significant ecosystem development across wallets, DEXes, and core protocol research. The dominant themes were ecosystem expansion (exchange integrations, the Android wallet launch, ErgoDEX nearing release), ongoing community education around SigmaUSD/SigmaRSV mechanics, and substantive technical discussions on network scalability, NIPoPoWs, sidechains, and zero-knowledge proof capabilities.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Exchange Listings and Ecosystem Growth
On Ergo's 2nd anniversary (July 1st), three exchange partnerships were announced: Bitcoin.com Exchange, Changelly, and Changelly Pro. A fourth unnamed tier-1 (top-10 by volume) exchange remained under NDA throughout the month, with its listing expected the week of August 2nd. Community speculation centred on KuCoin. Bitcoin.com's listing was originally scheduled for July 29th but delayed to August 3rd due to internal technical issues on the exchange side. All four exchanges were in active integration/wallet testing phases throughout July, with no confirmed live dates by month end.

### ErgoDEX Development
ErgoDEX was the most anticipated development of the month. The team confirmed basic AMM contracts were successfully ported to Plutus (Cardano's Haskell-based smart contract language), with code published publicly on GitHub. The launch was repeatedly described as "days away" or "this month," but was held up by a Yoroi dApp connector fix. The DEX will feature both AMM and order book functionality, with cross-chain Cardano interoperability via NIPoPoWs and eUTXO compatibility — enabling direct token swaps without wrapping. No governance token was confirmed at launch, though one may be added later if needed for parameter changes. A Project Catalyst Fund 5 proposal for an ErgoDEX Plutus port was open for community voting during the month.

### Android Wallet Release
Ergo's first official mobile wallet (developed by Benjamin Schulte / MrStahlfelge, built on Ergo AppKit, and audited by the Ergo dev team) was released on the Google Play Store on July 30th. The wallet supports read-only functionality. Initial community questions about trust were addressed by confirming EF endorsement. A beta APK had been circulating for testing throughout the month, with testers receiving 0.5 ERG tips.

### SigmaUSD / SigmaRSV Mechanics and Incidents
The month saw extensive community education around the AgeUSD dual-token protocol. A whale redeeming approximately 2 million SigmaUSD caused the reserve ratio to spike above 1000%, temporarily re-enabling SigmaRSV redemptions after weeks of being locked. Separately, v1 SigmaRSV holders were unable to redeem due to an empty reserve caused by CoinEx's months-long delay in returning mistakenly deposited tokens. Armeanio manually facilitated exits by minting v1 SigmaUSD to raise the reserve ratio. CoinEx subsequently announced a policy of never supporting non-native assets. Multiple users also accidentally sent SigmaRSV to exchanges via Yoroi's "send all assets" button, with recovery possible via exchange support at a 20% fee.

### Ledger Hardware Wallet Integration
The Tesseract/Yehor team made progress but encountered significant delays: the week of July 12th was consumed almost entirely by fighting with the Ledger SDK, BOLOS compiler settings, and poor documentation — with no Ergo-specific code written yet. By late July, a working demo video was released showing Ergo running on a Ledger device, suggesting the timeline had moved from months to weeks. No public release date was confirmed.

### Mining Pool Decentralization
Nanopool approached and at times exceeded 50–60% of network hashrate, raising persistent 51% attack concerns throughout the month. The community actively pushed miners toward smaller pools including Woolypooly, Leafpool, 2miners, and the newly launched Coinfoundry pool. Kushti and Armeanio both publicly requested migration. By late July, Nanopool had dropped below 50% following an address change, though concentration remained a concern. Ergo was also added to the MoneroOcean pool API, with pool code implementation pending.

### NIPoPoWs, Sidechains, and Scalability
Kushti's blog post (July 1st) initiated a community discussion on network speed improvements. Key points: sub-blocks and microblocks are being evaluated, with existing implementations in draft PRs that need revival and community direction. Merge-mined sidechains are planned, where Ergo mainchain miners earn both ERG and sidechain tokens by delivering sidechain data — described as superior to Bitcoin's BIP-301 due to clearer security and trustless pegging via smart contracts. Kushti noted that Ergo's concrete application set (Oracle Pools, Spectrum DEX, SigmaUSD, Rosen Bridge) now provides a meaningful benchmark for optimization, unlike chains that implement scaling solutions before real usage exists. A testnet for parameter experimentation (block time, difficulty) was noted as available but requiring community leadership to run structured experiments.

### Storage Rent
Community discussion clarified the mechanics: 0.12 ERG per box every 4 years for inactive UTXOs, designed to return dormant coins to circulation and compensate miners post-emission. Debate continued about whether the fee structure should be fixed, scaling, or percentage-based, without resolution.

### Zero-Knowledge Proofs and Bulletproofs
Kushti provided a detailed explanation of bulletproofs as a trustless zero-knowledge proof technique (no trusted setup, unlike SNARKs) suitable for range proofs and generic statements. The key development goal is verifying bulletproof proofs within ErgoScript, which would enable hybrid on-chain/off-chain execution where sensitive data can be proven without disclosure — opening a "whole new world of possibilities" combining Ergo contracts with zero-knowledge statements such as Groth one-out-of-many proofs (used in Zcoin) and verifiable credentials.

### Community Token Activity
Multiple community tokens launched including Kushtis, Erdoge, Hopium, YAYERG, and UTXO tokens, with the Kushti tokens explicitly framed as educational tools for learning wallet and token mechanics. A dedicated Ergo Token Centre Telegram channel was created to separate this activity from the main channel.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@unknown, 2021-07-01): What are bulletproofs and can they be verified in ErgoScript?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge proof technique allowing specific statements to be proven without a trusted setup — unlike SNARKs. They can be used for range proofs (as in Monero) or generic statements, making them a trustless alternative to SNARKs. If bulletproof verification can be implemented in ErgoScript, it enables a variety of zero-knowledge protocols alongside Ergo contracts: hybrid execution where contracts are verified on-chain without revealing sensitive data. This also opens the door to techniques like Groth one-out-of-many proofs (used in early Zcoin) and verifiable credentials. It's a specific form of Sigma protocol, and the goal is combining these zero-knowledge statements with Ergo's existing contract capabilities.

- **Q** (@unknown, 2021-07-02): How do storage rents work, and what happens after max supply is reached if everyone moves ERG to avoid fees?
  **A** (@multiple community members): Storage rent is 0.12 ERG per UTXO box every 4 years — it applies per box, not per ERG held. It is designed to return lost/dormant coins to miners and clean up abandoned UTXOs. Active users can easily avoid it by consolidating UTXOs. If all wallets were active (an unlikely scenario), miners would still earn from transaction fees, which should grow with network usage. The fee structure debate (fixed vs. scaling vs. percentage-based) remains unresolved.

- **Q** (@unknown, 2021-07-02): How will merge-mined sidechains work for miners?
  **A** (@kushti): Miners earn both mainchain ERG and sidechain tokens. An emission contract on the mainchain rewards miners for delivering sidechain data. Trustless pegging is achievable via smart contracts — superior to Bitcoin's BIP-301, which has less clear security. Ergo's approach also supports trustless relays for updating sidechain data on the Ergo mainchain. This can enable CPU mining on sidechains while the mainchain remains GPU-mined. Sidechain construction details were described as coming "soon" to the Ergo forum.

- **Q** (@Jeronimo Backes, 2021-07-W27): Is there anything in Ergo's smart contracts that makes DeFi significantly more secure than other blockchains?
  **A** (@Ron): Ergo's eUTXO model and multi-stage contracts design means funds are locked in specific conditions rather than mutable global state — reducing attack surface. See: https://ergoplatform.org/en/blog/2021-04-16-multi-stage-contracts/ and https://ergoplatform.org/en/blog/2021-04-06-ergodex-model-amm-and-order-book-type-exchange/

- **Q** (@user, 2021-07-W28): Can Ergo Oracle Pools work natively with Cardano?
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin, @glasgowm): No — not currently. Cardano cannot read external UTXO data without consuming (burning) it. Ergo solved this with data inputs, which allow read-only access to a UTXO without spending it. Cardano lacks this feature post-Alonzo. A workaround exists via metadata slots (as used by nut.link) but is inefficient. Cardano may add read-only inputs in a future iteration, which would enable full native Ergo Oracle Pool integration.

- **Q** (@Rpalet, 2021-07-16): Why can't I redeem my v1 SigmaRSV for ERG?
  **A** (@Armeanio): The v1 reserve is nearly empty due to CoinEx holding SigmaRSV tokens for months before returning them — by which time the reserve had been depleted. To facilitate exits, I will manually mint v1 SigmaUSD to temporarily raise the reserve ratio above the 400% redemption threshold. PM for coordination.

- **Q** (@unknown, 2021-07-W29): How do you move Bitcoin to Ergo without wrapping it?
  **A** (@glasgowm): Atomic swaps are built into Ergo's core. NIPoPoWs enable cross-chain interaction between UTXO-based chains without wrapping tokens. See: https://ergoplatform.org/en/blog/2020_04_24_atomic_swaps/

- **Q** (@unknown, 2021-07-W27): How is Ergo tackling UTXO concurrency challenges for DEXes?
  **A** (@glasgowm): Ergo solved this using data inputs — allowing contracts to read shared state (e.g., an AMM pool box) without consuming it, enabling multiple transactions to reference the same UTXO in a single block. See also mirquir's proposed workaround: https://www.reddit.com/r/ergonauts/comments/o2vkat/the_utxodexissue_and_my_idea_of_a_solution/

- **Q** (@S, 2021-07-W26): Node not updating ERG balance after restoring wallet with mnemonic on new SSD?
  **A** (@glasgowm): Node not fully synchronised yet. Must wait for full sync to see accurate balance.

- **Q** (@Andromeda, 2021-07-W27): Will Ergo have non-custodial staking similar to Cardano ADA?
  **A** (@glasgowm): No direct equivalent is planned. Cardano staking has no downside risk because ADA never leaves your wallet and stakers function as the network's validators. Ergo's security model is different (PoW). Passive income options will come through liquidity provision on ErgoDEX, ErgoMixer tokenization, and similar mechanisms. See forum discussion: https://www.ergoforum.org/t/a-solution-for-staking/1057

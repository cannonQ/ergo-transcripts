# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Month:** August 2021

---

## Executive Summary

August 2021 was a landmark month for Ergo, defined by two transformative events: the KuCoin listing (the long-anticipated "tier 1 exchange" announcement) and the launch of the first eUTXO DEX beta (ErgoDEX, later Spectrum DEX). Alongside these milestones, the community worked through substantial technical education around eUTXO mechanics, SigmaUSD/SigmaRSV dynamics, and Ergo's long-term economic model — driven by a large influx of new users arriving via exchange exposure. Infrastructure development accelerated across wallets, bridges, and tooling, with Ledger integration, the Ergo paper wallet, and the Graviton cross-chain bridge all making meaningful progress.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### KuCoin Listing & Exchange Ecosystem
The KuCoin listing — with ERG/BTC and ERG/USDT pairs opening 2021-08-05 — was the dominant community event of early August, triggering significant price action (~36% in one day, reaching ~$9+). KuCoin simultaneously launched a $30,000 ERG prize pool campaign and enabled ERG/USDT grid trading bots. Community pressure via Armeanio successfully reduced KuCoin's initial 1 ERG withdrawal fee to 0.01 ERG. The period also saw the Bitcoin.com Exchange listing (2021-08-03) and confirmation of Changelly/Changelly Pro listings, though the latter was delayed to August 31 due to technical issues on Changelly's side. Gate.io (0.5 ERG) and Hotbit (1 ERG) withdrawal fees were noted as disproportionately high versus the protocol minimum. ERG appeared on TradingView for the first time following the KuCoin listing. Community consensus remained firmly against paying Binance or Coinbase listing fees, preferring organic growth.

### Spectrum DEX (ErgoDEX) Beta Launch
ErgoDEX beta went live on 2021-08-11 at beta.ergodex.io, becoming the first DEX on any eUTXO blockchain. The launch required Yoroi Nightly plus the Yoroi-Ergo dApp connector (Chrome/Brave only), with real ERG needed for transaction fees even in beta. Test tokens (WT_ERG, WT_ADA, Erdoge, Kushti) were distributed via faucet. Key technical constraints surfaced during testing: operations within a single pool are chained sequentially (though cross-pool operations are parallel), Yoroi could not handle chained transactions limiting users to one pending transaction at a time, and silent swap failures due to slippage required manual refunds from the transaction history. Ilya Oskin and the ErgoDEX team patched multiple issues during launch day. The AMM functionality was confirmed complete by month end, with an order book DEX targeted for approximately Q1 2022. ErgoDEX was also confirmed as funded via Cardano Catalyst Fund5 leftover funds (~$91,000).

### eUTXO Model & Ergo Technical Architecture
The influx of new users prompted extensive community-driven education on the eUTXO model: UTXO change addresses (not bugs), parallel vs. sequential transaction constraints, and how multi-recipient batching renders raw TPS metrics misleading. Glasgow summarised Ergo's scaling roadmap: L0 node optimisations (20-50× possible), L1 extension via Bitcoin NG-style microblocks and sharding, and L2 support for Lightning Network, Rainbow Network, Zero-Knowledge Contingent Payments, FairSwap/FastSwap. kushti noted that with a concrete set of deployed contracts now live (GARD protocols, Rosen Bridge, Oracle Pools, Spectrum DEX, AMM pool contracts, Trade House, lending contracts, SigmaUSD), meaningful throughput optimisation via sub-blocks is now tractable since the transaction profile is known. Sub-blocks vs. sidechains as the preferred path remained under discussion, with community drafts flagged for revival post-Ergoversary.

### SigmaUSD / SigmaRSV Mechanics
Repeated detailed explanations addressed the algorithmic stablecoin's mechanics for new users: SigmaUSD pegged to $1; SigmaRSV appreciates with ERG price and fee accumulation; reserve ratio must stay above 400% to redeem SigmaRSV and below 800% to mint it; 2.5% fees on minting/redemption flow back into the reserve. Community noted an active bearwhale strategy (mint SigmaUSD → dump ERG → redeem for more ERG if price drops), which had not succeeded since ERG was ~$2. The reserve ratio rose sharply above 2000% at one point (blocking new SigmaRSV minting) and dropped below 800% later in the month during bearwhale activity. Community members promoted minting SigmaUSD as a way to take profits without suppressing ERG price on exchanges. Armeanio discussed how SigmaUSD's utility would evolve with ErgoDEX — extending its "velocity" by enabling SigmaUSD to be used to buy assets on the DEX rather than purely for ERG long/short positioning.

### Wallet Ecosystem & Hardware Wallet Progress
The Android Ergo Wallet (MrStahlfelge) released a beta with token support — enabling Auction House and SigmaUSD interaction — with every line reviewed by morphic (core developer). The Ledger integration via Tesseract progressed substantially: the Attest Input method was confirmed working on device, extracting ERG and token amounts and signing transactions for both Nano S and X. Source code and API documentation were published for developer testing. A separate cold storage EIP for the Android wallet was opened for community review. anon_br published the first Ergo paper wallet in alpha at anon-br.github.io/ergo-paper-wallet. iOS wallet support remained unavailable with no timeline; Yoroi dApp connector remained on Nightly only with no Firefox support.

### Cross-Chain Infrastructure: Graviton, Rosen Bridge & Atomic Swaps
The Ergo–Gravity/Graviton partnership was announced, enabling cross-chain oracle aggregation and bridging without a separate token — fees paid in each chain's native token. SUSY Gateway (built on Gravity) would enable sending ERG/SigmaUSD to ETH addresses with tokens locked on source and issued on destination, and vice versa. Glasgow confirmed that trustless Schnorr-based atomic swaps between Ergo and Bitcoin would become possible after Bitcoin's Taproot fork in November 2021, enabling BTC in Ergo's Sigma protocol financial contracts without custodial wrapping — a more secure alternative to wrapped token bridges. Oracle Pool v2 was noted as in development to accommodate Cardano's eUTXO data-only input constraints.

### Mining Pool Centralisation
Nanopool held over 50% of Ergo's hashrate through early August, raising 51% attack concerns. Community members actively encouraged switching pools and Armeanio set a short-term goal of getting Nanopool below 40%. Hashrate was growing overall (6× increase in two months per an Ergo Foundation blog post) and pool distribution improved over the month, with Nanopool dropping to ~49% by mid-August. New pools listed during the period included beepool.com and K1 Pool.

### Storage Rent & Long-Term Economics
Storage rent received repeated explanation as a key differentiator: UTXOs inactive for 4+ years are charged ~0.14 ERG, with insufficient-balance boxes removed from the UTXO set and their coins returned to miners. Armeanio described this as "on-chain garbage collection" — recycling lost coins and dust back into circulating supply and reducing state bloat. Community consensus was that this, combined with transaction fees, provides sustainable long-term miner incentives after the ~6-year emission schedule completes.

### Ergo NFT Ecosystem
The Ergo Auction House saw growing activity: audio NFTs by Noah and Gazza (original guitar track), REROLABS rapping Biggie Smalls' "Juicy," a National Parks NFT series by Peppers CK (with 20% proceeds donated to national parks), a samurai art collection by the Spanish community, and ERGnomes' first Cardano NFT. A dedicated Ergo NFT Discord was established. On-chain token creation via ergoutils.org was actively used for test tokens including Erdoge (500,000 supply, created by Ron) and used in ErgoDEX beta testing.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Jake Nguyen, 2021-08-02): What are the use cases for ERG on the network?
  **A** (@Dmitry Usov): ERG is the main coin of the Ergo blockchain. Any transaction requires ERG as a fee. UTXOs cannot exist without ERG (analogous to ADA on Cardano). ErgoDEX will have two networks — fees on Ergo paid in ERG, fees on Cardano paid in ADA.

- **Q** (@Jake Nguyen, 2021-08-02): Is the 0.001 ERG transaction fee fixed permanently?
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge): The fee can be changed; 0.001 ERG is the minimum. Yoroi hardcodes 0.0011 ERG for unknown reasons; the Android wallet uses 0.001 ERG.

- **Q** (@Josh, 2021-08-03): What is the difference between an oracle and an oracle pool?
  **A** (@Hero Grinder): Oracle Pools use more than a single data point and provide hierarchy for the data feed, making them more reliable than single-oracle approaches. kushti previously worked with Sergey Nazarov on smartcontracts.com (which became Chainlink). Detail: https://ergonaut.space/en/Oracle-Pools

- **Q** (@TMR.ΣRG, 2021-08-03): With the SigmaUSD reserve ratio above 2000% (blocking SigmaRSV minting), can SigmaRSV be converted to SigmaUSD directly without routing through ERG?
  **A** (@Lorem): sigmausd.io is just a front end. Any dApp can be built on top of the AgeUSD protocol to facilitate that conversion. ErgoDEX may add SigmaRSV/SigmaUSD pairs. The direct swap would be a DEX function, not a core protocol function.

- **Q** (@smash, 2021-08-05): After sending ERG, a third unfamiliar address appeared with extra ERG going to it — is ERG missing?
  **A** (@Gazza): Standard eUTXO behaviour. When spending a partial amount, the wallet must spend the entire input UTXO and return change to a new change address controlled by your wallet. The "third address" is your own change address.

- **Q** (multiple, 2021-08-05): What is the benefit of eUTXO over account-based models?
  **A** (@Glasgow): UTXO is Bitcoin's original simpler design. Each UTXO transaction is self-contained and can be validated independently. Account-based models store more state making them easier to develop on, while UTXO is more efficient for scaling.

- **Q** (multiple, 2021-08-10): What is the current TPS of Ergo?
  **A** (@Glasgow): TPS doesn't mean much in Ergo's context — smart contracts allow thousands of transactions to be batched. A single transaction can have hundreds of recipients. Example: https://explorer.ergoplatform.com/en/transactions/c4e5276182543b17eae4e767d60965feceaf1fd1ba1f5bde76aae751b33a6bb8

- **Q** (@A, 2021-08-11): Are operations on ErgoDEX single-threaded?
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin): All operations within a single pool are chained sequentially. Operations across different pools are processed in parallel.

- **Q** (community, 2021-08-11): Is the single-transaction limitation a product of eUTXO?
  **A** (@Glasgow / @Dmitry Usov): Yes — eUTXO architecture. Yoroi cannot do chained transactions, which is why only one transaction can be sent at a time.

- **Q** (community, 2021-08-11): Why did my swap debit the sell-side asset but not credit the buy-side?
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin): Price slipped beyond the minimum output defined in your order due to high test token volatility. The order remains pending until price conditions are met. Refunds are always available for non-executed orders and can be initiated directly from the UI.

- **Q** (@Sambhav, 2021-08-14): Once all 97M ERG are in circulation, how will miners be incentivised?
  **A** (@Glasgow / @A): Miners will earn transaction fees. Additionally, Ergo's storage rent mechanism charges ~0.14 ERG every 4 years from inactive boxes, recycling lost coins back to miners. This provides long-term revenue beyond block subsidies.

- **Q** (@MrStahlfelge, 2021-08-23): How does token burning work in Ergo?
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge): Tokens in ingoing boxes of a transaction but not in outgoing boxes are burned. Tokens can also be burned by miners after four years if the box storing them cannot pay storage rent.

- **Q** (@Soberana, 2021-08-24): Could ERG be lost by holding in a wallet for more than 4 years?
  **A** (@Chris): Only a tiny amount (less than 1 ERG) is reclaimed when a box is untouched for 4 years. A single transaction resets the timer.

- **Q** (@koukarin, 2021-08-27): All ERG will be minted in ~8 years, leaving only transaction fees — is the protocol wholly dependent on mass adoption?
  **A** (community): Two mechanisms mitigate this: (1) storage rent — miners charge rent on UTXOs inactive for 4+ years, recycling lost/dust ERG back into supply; (2) if ERG appreciates, even small nominal fees become economically meaningful.

- **Q** (@J H, 2021-08-28): Can Ergo Oracle Pools compete with Chainlink?
  **A** (@CW): Anyone can deploy an oracle on Ergo now. Oracle Pool v2 is in development to address Cardano's eUTXO constraint of data-only inputs. The pool model aggregates multiple data points improving reliability without requiring a separate token for data access.

- **Q** (@Olli Tiainen, 2021-08-24): Is a Schnorr atomic swap different from wrapped token bridging?
  **A** (@glasgowm): Schnorr atomic swaps are more advanced and completely trustless, unlike custodial wrapping.

- **Q** (@GoodHabits.xml, 2021-08-26): Why do BSC and ETH share wallet addresses?
  **A** (community): BSC and ETH share the same address derivation scheme (secp256k1). Ergo uses a different scheme entirely. Sending ERG directly to an ETH address would result in permanent loss; a bridge (e.g., Graviton) is required.

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Month:** 2019-07

---

## Executive Summary

July 2019 was the foundational month for Ergo, dominated by the mainnet launch on July 1st and the immediate practical challenges that followed: mining setup complexity, wallet synchronisation issues, and early exchange infrastructure. By month's end, the EFYT-to-ERG gateway had opened for testing on the Waves DEX, marking the first major post-launch milestone and beginning the transition from the pre-launch token economy to the live network.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Mainnet Launch & Genesis Block Correction
Ergo mainnet launched on July 1st, but an incorrect initial difficulty parameter (`1200000000000` instead of `011765000000`) forced a five-hour postponement. After correction, the network mined 8 blocks immediately, confirming the chain was live. Initial hashrate reached ~173 GH/s before the first difficulty recalculation at block 2048 (thereafter every 1024 blocks). By July 3rd, total supply had reached ~300k ERG with ~54k ERG mined daily, and the foundation held ~30k ERG in treasury.

### GPU Mining & Autolykos
Mining at launch required NVIDIA GPUs with ≥3GB VRAM; CUDA support only, with no AMD miner available. Reported hashrates were 40–50 MH/s on RTX 2080Ti, ~40 MH/s on GTX 1080Ti, and ~31 MH/s on GTX 2070. AMD cards (RX 470/480/570/580) were discussed as theoretically viable at ~19–20 MH/s but had no official miner. Ergo's pool-resistant Autolykos design was a recurring point of tension: small miners were mathematically disadvantaged as network hashrate exceeded 1 TH/s, and no official mining pool was available. Community members investigated open-source pool implementations (ergoPool, Autolykos GPU miner on GitHub), though these required private key exposure and carried security risks.

### Wallet & Node Configuration
Throughout the month, the most consistent source of user friction was wallet and node setup. Key recurring issues included: configuring `apiKeyHash` correctly (must be the Blake2b256 hash of the secret, with the plaintext passed as HTTP header); the 720-block maturity delay before mining rewards are spendable; and a bug introduced in node version 3.0.2 where wallet balance height lagged behind block height because the optimised wallet scanner only processed blocks containing user transactions. Version 3.0.1 was recommended as a workaround. Memory allocation (`-Xmx4G` JVM flag) and peer configuration via `mainnet.conf` were also common setup hurdles.

### EFYT-to-ERG Gateway & Exchange Listings
A major theme across weeks 28 and 29 was the mechanics and timing of the EFYT (Waves) to ERG (mainnet) conversion. The tokens are separate with different supplies; the planned 1:1 swap via a Waves platform gateway was under testing throughout the month. By July 25th, the gateway went live for testing with limits of 0.1–50,000 ERG per transaction, and the first successful swap was executed on July 27th. Exchange infrastructure progressed from Tidex (live at launch, but with a 200-confirmation deposit requirement flagged as excessive) toward announced additions of SWFT, P2PB2B, and Waves DEX integration. An OTC Discord was established for peer-to-peer trading.

### Token Supply & Economics
Recurring confusion about ERG supply stemmed from discrepancies between CoinGecko (~2M), CMC (~5M), and the whitepaper figures, compounded by the existence of both EFYT and ERG as distinct tokens. The emission curve blog post was the canonical reference. Block rewards were confirmed at 75 ERG total per block (67.5 ERG to miner, 7.5 ERG to foundation share) for over two years before declining per the published emission schedule. Storage rent was referenced in the whitepaper but not yet practically relevant.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Max f, 2019-07-01): Getting 403 Forbidden on wallet init/unlock via Swagger despite what appears to be the correct `apiKeyHash`.
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin): `apiKeyHash` must be the Blake2b256 hash of your secret string. The `apiKey` HTTP header must contain the plaintext pre-image (your secret), not the hash itself. These are frequently confused.

- **Q** (@Max f, 2019-07-01): Miner returning "Incorrect points" error with 0 MH/s on all cards.
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin): Verify `minerPubKeyHex` is set correctly; check for leading spaces or a `0x` prefix in the hex representation, both of which will cause this error.

- **Q** (multiple users, 2019-07-01–07): Mined a block but balance not visible in Swagger; when can rewards be spent?
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin): A 720-block maturity delay is required before a mining reward becomes spendable. The `rewardAddress` (P2S format, beginning with `88`) can be looked up on the explorer to confirm the reward exists; restore your wallet with the mnemonic phrase to access it once mature.

- **Q** (@Rumato Estorsky, 2019-07-01): What is the formula for deducing hashrate from difficulty?
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin): `Hashrate = Difficulty / blockIntervalSeconds` — the relationship is linear.

- **Q** (multiple users, 2019-07-08–14): Why does node v3.0.2 show wallet `height` lower than the network's full block height, and why doesn't balance update after 720 confirmations?
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin, 2019-07-13): In v3.0.2, an optimisation was introduced causing the wallet to scan only blocks containing transactions related to user addresses. The `height` field therefore reflects the last block with a user transaction, not the chain tip. This can cause confirmed mining rewards to appear missing. Downgrade to v3.0.1 or reimport wallet on a clean install as a workaround pending a fix.

- **Q** (@M, 2019-07-22): Cannot find peers/connections; node not syncing.
  **A** (community): Peers are defined in `mainnet.conf` on GitHub. Use `reference.conf` as a template, override only your specific settings in a separate config file, and ensure the default mainnet port 9053 is reachable.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2019-07-22): How to initialise wallet and set API key on a fresh node?
  **A** (community): Hash your chosen secret using Blake2b256 (available via Swagger at `/swagger#/utils/hashBlake2b`), place the hash in config under `scorex.restApi.apiKeyHash`, restart the node with the config file, then call `/wallet/init` passing the plaintext secret as the `apiKey` HTTP header.

- **Q** (@Kookster, 2019-07-25): What is the maximum per-transaction limit on the gateway?
  **A** (@mentalist420): Maximum is 50,000 ERG per transaction (not the lower figure initially circulated).

- **Q** (@U S, 2019-07-22): Is CPU mining viable?
  **A** (community): No. CPU mining cannot compete with GPU hashrates and is not practical for finding blocks. Multiple GPUs are the minimum viable setup.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **AMD GPU Miner**: No AMD support at launch; community demand was clear but no development timeline was announced.
- **Mining Pools**: Ergo's pool-resistant Autolykos design disadvantages small miners. Community-built open-source pools exist but require private key sharing. No sanctioned pool solution or formal protocol change proposed.
- **Node v3.0.2 Wallet Sync Bug**: Root cause acknowledged by Ilya Oskin but fix not shipped during July. Users still advised to use v3.0.1.
- **EFYT Gateway Formal Launch**: Gateway was in testing at month end; official public announcement and full operational status not yet confirmed.
- **200-Confirmation Deposit Requirement on Tidex**: Flagged as excessive and likely a configuration error on Tidex's end; no resolution confirmed.
- **Block Time / Speed Parameter Discussion**: Community raised whether reducing block time from 2 minutes could improve small miner viability; no formal proposal or decision recorded.
- **Desktop Wallet**: No official standalone desktop wallet released; users directed to Magnum (web) or full node. Windows users particularly underserved.
- **Explorer Transaction Anomaly** (Alexey Kiselev, 2019-07-02): A transaction showing 290k ERG output against a ~288k ERG total supply was flagged for investigation; no resolution documented.
- **Bug Bounty Programme**: Asked about but no response recorded; programme status unknown.

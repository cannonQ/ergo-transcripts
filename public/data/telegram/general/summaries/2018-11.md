# Ergo General — Monthly Summary: November 2018

**Chat:** Ergo General
**Period:** 2018-11-01 to 2018-11-30
**Activity Level:** Low-to-moderate; primarily community Q&A, pre-mainnet announcements, and technical clarifications

---

## Executive Summary

November 2018 was dominated by pre-launch anticipation, with the team working toward a mainnet target of end-2018 or Q1 2019 and actively developing the Autolykos PoW algorithm for Testnet 2. Key community discussions centred on correcting misinformation about the emission curve, clarifying the novel solo-mining economics of Autolykos, and addressing contributor questions about the Scala codebase.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Autolykos PoW Algorithm
The new Autolykos proof-of-work algorithm was announced as under active construction (PR #548), designed to be GPU-friendly and ASIC-resistant. Traditional mining pools are being phased out by design, as Autolykos' privacy-preserving architecture would require miners to disclose private keys to pool operators. Alternative pool structures — trusted friend groups or deposit-backed pools — were proposed as viable substitutes. A GPU miner implementation was planned to begin after Testnet 2, largely with community assistance. Detailed specifications and an academic paper were deferred pending a November presentation.

### Mining Economics & Solo Miner Viability
Significant community concern arose over whether small miners would be squeezed out under the Autolykos model. Dmitry clarified that solo miners receive the same *average* reward as pool miners — simply at lower frequency. Using Ethereum as an analogy: 4 GPUs yields approximately one block per year; 57 GPUs yields approximately one block per month. The design intentionally favours miners capable of earning roughly one block per month or more, and the team viewed this as structurally superior to ASIC-dominated traditional pools.

### Emission Curve & Token Economics
Misinformation circulating on Bitcointalk claimed first-year emission would be 50 million ERG. The community corrected this: first-year emission is 19.7 million ERG, with a 10% project allocation (~1,971,000 ERG) funding development — analogous to Zcash's founder reward but via the publicly traded EFYT token. EFYT holders will swap 1:1 to native ERG post-mainnet. Emission is fixed for the first two years, then reduces every three months. A token burn of 3,029,000 tokens was recorded on-chain to align supply with the emission curve.

### Mainnet Launch Timeline
The team targeted end of 2018 or Q1 2019 for mainnet, with Testnet 2 stability as the primary readiness indicator. A "much anticipated news" announcement was flagged by @mentalist420 on 2018-11-09, described as getting "closer now." No hard launch date was committed to.

### Codebase, Scala, and Contributor Onboarding
Community members raised concerns about Scala as the implementation language, citing a higher barrier to entry versus Go or Rust. Defenders noted Scala's strong adoption in finance and data science. The core repositories — `ergoplatform/ergo`, `scorexfoundation/scorex`, `ScorexFoundation/sigmastate-interpreter`, and `input-output-hk/scrypto` — were shared, with issues tagged "help wanted" for contributors.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Rumato Estorsky, 2018-11-15): Does Autolykos require computation within a single location to prevent outsourcing? Would small miners with 4–8 GPUs be economically squeezed out?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Solo miners receive the same average reward as pool miners, just less frequently. With 4 GPUs expect roughly one block per year; with 57 GPUs, roughly one block per month. The design encourages independent miners at meaningful scale while structurally discouraging traditional pools. Deposit-backed pools or trusted friend groups remain possible alternatives.

- **Q** (@Rumato Estorsky, 2018-11-15): Will Autolykos work properly on GPU? Is miner software available, or only CPU mining via the node?
  **A** (@Dmitry): GPU miner implementation will begin after Testnet 2, primarily with community help.

- **Q** (@Noodles 💎, 2018-11-02): How does the emission curve work — isn't 10× first-year emission too inflationary?
  **A** (@mentalist420): First-year emission is 19.7 million ERG, not 50 million as misreported. This is comparable to Cardano and Zcash at equivalent stages. See: https://ergoplatform.org/news_emission_curve.html

- **Q** (@StakeWithJenni.com, 2018-11-15): How is the project funded?
  **A** (community): Via the EFYT token — similar to Zcash's private investor model but public. 10% of first-year mining supply (~1,971,000 tokens) will swap 1:1 to native ERG post-mainnet.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-11-early): What is Ergo's hashing algorithm?
  **A** (community): PoW code not yet finalised as Autolykos. GPU and ASIC mining planned; traditional pools to be phased out. Full specifications deferred to post-November presentation and academic paper.

- **Q** (@Waylay, 2018-11-late): Will Ergo use PoW, PoS, or DPoS?
  **A** (community): Proof of work (Autolykos).

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: November 2025

---

## Executive Summary

November 2025 was defined by three dominant themes: major cross-chain infrastructure milestones (Rosen Bridge's Bitcoin Runes+ deployment and the first stablecoin wrapped runes in Bitcoin history), significant ecosystem fundraising and community bootstrapping (USE Community Liquidity Bootstrap exceeding its 382k ERG goal, Bene V2 launch, and Unstoppable Hackathon sponsorship), and continued deep technical work on Ergo's core scaling roadmap (sub-blocks processing rework, Lithos Protocol testnet, and storage rent economics). Community governance and event format restructuring were also prominent, with ongoing debate around Ergo Foundation transition and developer engagement mechanisms.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Rosen Bridge Bitcoin Runes+ Integration
The month's most significant technical milestone was the deployment of SigmaUSD and Gluon Gold to Bitcoin as Runes+, creating the first algorithmic stablecoin as a Runes+ on Bitcoin Layer 1. Armeanio announced the historic achievement on November 14, with full deployment completed by November 25. The integration enables multi-chain asset backing with decentralized security and real-time auditability. Watcher operators were required to update to versions 6.0.0 (service) and 3.5.0 (app); collateral requirements changed to 50K RSN + 10 ERG per Bitcoin-Runes watcher. Cardano Ogmios watchers required the cbor output flag for raw-data storage compatibility. The Rosen team noted missing pieces for full Lightning Network interoperability with Runes, though the specific implementation roadmap remained unresolved. Armeanio proposed structured Rosen subdomains and Runes+ asset naming conventions (e.g., ROSEN•TECH = RSRSN) with supporting documentation.

### USE Stablecoin Community Liquidity Bootstrap (CLB)
The USE (Universal Stablecoin) Community Liquidity Bootstrap campaign launched on November 25 and exceeded its 382,000 ERG goal, closing at approximately 395k ERG. The event accepted direct ERG contributions and wrapped Cardano rsERG, with airdrop distribution scheduled for November 25. CLB tokens represent liquidity provider shares plus two years of trading fees. A community poll on memecoin inclusion in the bonus airdrop pool reached above 70% threshold but awaited final results. The USE launch was accompanied by a community townhall AMA held November 20 at 7pm UTC. This represented one of the most active community fundraising events of the year.

### Sub-blocks / Input-Ordering Blocks Research and Testnet
kushti progressed the sub-blocks (input-ordering blocks) implementation throughout the month, finishing the processing rework in W46, testing locally in W47, and launching a public devnet at 213.239.193.208:9058 in W47. The implementation is targeted at improving Ergo's throughput without the security trade-offs associated with sharding or dubious layer 2 approaches. Community discussion covered the trade-offs of faster block times: applications relying on block height (not timestamp) would be unaffected, but timestamp-based contracts require careful analysis. kushti invited passionate community contributors to join a dedicated testnet channel to tweak parameters (difficulty, block time), mine the testnet, and make scientific observations—no coding required. The "weak blocks" vs. "sub-blocks" vs. "soft blocks" naming debate continued without a final decision.

### Lithos Protocol Testnet
The Lithos Protocol v1.0-test client launched in W47 with a detailed GitHub README covering collateral UTXOs and deployment. Phase One testnet was completed by W48, with the project moving to the Fraud Proofs phase. Armeanio consistently emphasized testnet stability and open-source code quality over token distribution. No token has been announced; long-term tokenomics remain TBD. Community and qx() congratulated the Lithos team on the milestone.

### Privacy Architecture and Mixer Safety
Across the month, multiple discussions reinforced Ergo's privacy-as-apps approach: privacy protocols are implemented as dApps rather than hardcoded core features, avoiding hard forks when new cryptographic schemes are discovered. This covers bulletproofs, Sigma protocols, stealth addresses, ring signatures, and threshold signatures with real zero-knowledge proofs. The sentencing of the Samourai wallet developer (5 years) prompted community discussion about legal implications; qx() and Armeanio emphasized that Ergo Mixer is open-source and non-custodial (user-controlled), unlike Tornado Cash's custodial model, placing operational security responsibility on users. SafeW integrates the mixer locally. Armeanio proposed using stealth addresses for redemptions in off-chain cash schemes like Basis to enable fast, private cash with on-chain backing. A community member (Velvia) posted a bounty for a privacy-focused wallet implementing transaction mixing and stealth addresses.

### Bene V2 and Ecosystem Fundraising
Bene V2 launched as a generalized fundraising platform supporting custom tokens (SigmaUSD, GAU, ERG) for campaigns. Built as a Google Summer of Code project by Aditya Gupta, mentored by Josemi, kushti, and Luca, it powered both the USE CLB and the Unstoppable Hackathon/Spring of Code sponsorship campaigns. The hackathon sponsorship reached its 3,000 ERG minimum by November 30, enabling Ergo to participate as an official sponsor and attract developers through a GSoC-style model. Bruno Woltzenlogel Paleo announced plans for an independent Spring of Code 2026 to continue building Ergo projects.

### Kintsugi Campus Currency System
qx() deployed a blockchain-based recognition and procurement system at Cornell with 77 staff members using the BRICK token for campus supply store, print center, and peer-to-peer recognition. Wallets were seeded with automatic top-ups via Nautilus. Storage rent will reclaim unused tokens after approximately 3.5 years from inactive student wallets, serving as a practical demonstration of storage rent mechanics in an institutional setting. The on-chain transaction was publicly documented.

### New Ecosystem Projects
**Degens.World** (Arohbe) launched four demo dApps on Ergo: Artifact Arena (NFT generation), Ergo Labs (AI ErgoScript generation), MemeVSMeme (AI meme battles with voting), and Minted (HTML5 game NFT minting to IPFS). A Node Explorer and API Explorer followed in W48. Xergon (Decentralized Proof-of-Node-Work Network for AI Compute on Ergo) was announced in development. **MewFinance Lending** went live at lend.mewfinance.com with smart contracts audited by Aco and kushti, with ErgoPay support. **DuckPools V2** launched with smarter proxy contracts removing v1 slippage issues, upgraded dashboards, and Smart Reserves. **Gluon Gold** had a frontend bug fixed (GAU price was incorrectly dividing the kg oracle price by 1000; GAU = 1 gram of gold, GAUC provides ~1.94x leveraged exposure). **Mahadex** pivoted toward anti-censorship infrastructure using Handshake domains and web3 naming, evaluating Helium-like mesh networks and radio-based transmission.

### Storage Rent Economics and Post-AGI Use Cases
kushti elaborated on storage rent as a deflationary mechanism: collected rent is recycled to miners, not burned. Ergo's programmable reserve layer design avoids the tokenomics problems seen in DePIN projects. In a broader framing, kushti positioned Ergo as ideally suited for machine-to-machine commerce in post-AGI economies: storage rent solves blockchain bloat from 10B+ AI agents conducting micro-transactions 24/7, and Ergo serves as reserve asset for both human and machine peer-to-peer interactions.

### Ergo Foundation Transition
kushti stated the Ergo Foundation "will be dissolved soon," but Glasgow (Discord) clarified it will not be dissolved—rather, it will transition to community/DevDAO governance. The exact timeline and legal entity retention remained unresolved. This was tied to broader discussion about the foundation's role in early Ergo growth versus the current ecosystem's maturity and decentralization.

### Community Event Format Restructuring
The community reached consensus on a reformatted event schedule: monthly Ergo Live Community Discussion (first week of month), monthly YouTube AMA (third week), and weekly developer updates. qx() noted the community discussion format was receiving approximately 30% lower viewership than traditional AMA formats (~150–450 views vs. higher traditional ranges). The lower engagement was attributed to format saturation; alternating monthly cadence was proposed as a remedy.

### Infrastructure and Network Resilience
The month began with multiple explorer and API endpoint failures on November 3, traced to Hetzner service disruptions. The community identified workarounds via backup endpoints (Cornell, CodeUTXO, SigmaSpace). This renewed discussion of InfraDAO concepts and decentralized infrastructure incentive mechanisms. A Cardano 846-block reorg exceeding Rosen Bridge's confirmation thresholds required the Rosen team to shut down the Bitcoin-Cardano bridge path temporarily, highlighting PoS consensus risks versus PoW. Satergo's integrated node was highlighted as an accessibility solution for non-technical users.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Nikan, 2025-11-03): How much is Ergo's max TPS?
  **A** (@kushti): "For what?" — TPS depends on use case. Privacy protocols are heavy; simple transfers are lightweight; DeFi is mid-range. Sometimes optimal to have heavier protocols on L1 (ERG/tokenized reserves) with ultra-performant off-chain local payment systems on top.

- **Q** (@Ollie, 2025-11-03): Can Ergo be filtered as a privacy coin on CoinGecko?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo has privacy protocols (stealth addresses, ring signatures, threshold signatures with real zero-knowledge proofs) implemented as apps rather than hardcoded. Distancing measures were taken previously when privacy coins were delisted from CEXes. Crypto classification trends around privacy are "insane"; the community must maneuver between competing pressures.

- **Q** (@Nabz, 2025-11-06): How well does the Ergo Mixer work?
  **A** (@qx()): "Mixer is great, just depends on how many people are mixing at that time." It is non-custodial—the user controls mixing themselves, unlike Tornado Cash. Responsibility for operational security lies with the user (@TMR.ΣRG).

- **Q** (@T Mas, 2025-11-11): Does Ergo have private DAO functionality?
  **A** (@Armeanio): Multisig already discloses spenders minimally. Pointed to Ergo's first apps (mixer and zero-knowledge treasury from 2020) as privacy-focused roots. Paideia can handle whitelisted investor DAOs; no concrete integration timeline specified.

- **Q** (@Edko, 2025-11-11): Does Ergo support private transaction features like ZCash, Monero, or Dash?
  **A** (@cannon_q): Yes—mixer and stealth address examples are documented in the Ergo docs.

- **Q** (@Ich Binsnicht, 2025-11-18): What motivates people to run nodes besides direct payment? Are NFTs viable incentives?
  **A** (@kushti): Being your own bank is the motivation. (@Armeanio): Security is the incentive—it is the only way to trustlessly verify you own your assets. (@ArØhβΣ): Working on a tool that scores node uptime and active handshakes; needs further development.

- **Q** (@qx(), 2025-11-18): Why don't CEXes support UTXO chains?
  **A** (@kushti): CEXes periodically collect dust and are lazy with UTXO-based chains, which require more complex wallet handling compared to account-based systems.

- **Q** (@Jordan Mack, 2025-11-22): Does the Ergo node support archival functionality for querying state at specific block heights? What is the correct way to get state snapshots?
  **A** (@cannon_q): Yes. Archival nodes store the full UTXO set from genesis. Use `/blocks/at/{blockHeight}` endpoints. Enable extra indexing with `ergo.node.extraIndex = true` for advanced `/blockchain` path API endpoints via Swagger UI.

- **Q** (@Jordan Mack, 2025-11-22): Need to verify blockchain state snapshots for vote count and airdrop eligibility.
  **A** (@kushti): Extra indices should be sufficient. (@qx()): ergo.watch provides a fast single-address balance-at-height API. For multi-address queries, qx() created a Python script using GPHL with pagination support.

- **Q** (@DiscordBridge, 2025-11-20): How does Gluon Gold (GAU/GAUC) work? Is minting profitable given price discrepancies?
  **A** (@Joe / community): You always receive both GAU and GAUC when minting to maintain the ratio. GAU + GAUC ≈ 1,000 ERG input. GAUC can be converted to GAU with fees. A frontend bug was inflating GAU prices (incorrectly dividing the kg oracle price by 1,000); this was fixed by MDMTXO. GAU = 1 gram of gold; GAUC provides ~1.94x leveraged exposure. Both are redeemable at any time, unlike locked SigmaRSV.

- **Q** (@CW, 2025-11-30): Could dark liquidity pools be set up to minimise front-running on order books or AMMs?
  **A** (@CW): Flagged as potential Machina Finance feature. No concrete implementation plan confirmed beyond that exploration.

- **Ergo Foundation Legal Transition**: Conflicting statements between kushti ("EF will be dissolved soon") and Glasgow ("EF will not be dissolved, only transitioned"). Exact timeline, legal entity retention, and DevDAO governance structure remain TBD.

- **Sub-blocks Naming and Marketing**: No official decision on whether to call the feature "sub-blocks," "weak blocks," "soft blocks," or another marketing-friendly name. Community testnet channel exists but needs a leader to drive scientific observations.

- **Lithos Protocol Token and Tokenomics**: No token announced; Armeanio has emphasised testnet stability over distribution, but long-term tokenomics remain entirely unspecified.

- **Stablecoin / Flatcoin Protecting Purchasing Power**: kushti acknowledged impulse's question about an "unfreezable, immutable, decentralized stablecoin/flatcoin protecting purchasing power" and promised a detailed writeup; this has not yet appeared.

- **TVI (Treasury Value Index) Bank-Run Risk**: bob's critique of proportional redemption bleed from impulse's dual-pool flatcoin design was acknowledged but not resolved.

- **Prediction Market Protocols**: kushti mentioned two invented prediction market protocols, one partially implemented but on hold due to developer availability. No resumption timeline without Spring of Code funding.

- **Bitcoin Runes & Lightning Network Interoperability**: Armeanio noted missing pieces for Lightning integration with Runes; kushti asked for specifics but the implementation roadmap was not resolved.

- **USE Memecoin Airdrop Inclusion**: Community poll exceeded 70% threshold but final decision was pending as of November 28.

- **Spectrum DEX Status**: No official statement on whether Spectrum DEX remains operational; community directing users to alternatives (Machina Finance, MewFinance, DuckPools) without formal confirmation of status.

- **CometHODL Token Redemption**: mgPai appeared to shut down HODL Comet for maintenance with no confirmed reopening date.

- **Dark Liquidity / Front-Running Mitigation**: CW's question about dark liquidity pools for order book front-running remained exploratory with no implementation plan beyond a suggestion it could suit Machina Finance.

- **Fast Block Confirmation Trade-offs**: Full impact analysis of faster blocks on existing timestamp-based contracts and dApps relying on block height remains pending.

- **InfraDAO and Node Incentive Mechanisms**: Recurrent discussion with no formal proposal; Arohbe's node uptime scoring tool is in early development.

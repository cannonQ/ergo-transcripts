# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: April 2024

---

## Executive Summary

April 2024 was defined by three converging themes: major exchange and ecosystem expansion (MEXC listing progress culminating in official announcement, Rosen Bridge BTC testnet launch, Palmyra IDO), a deepening technical roadmap centred on Sigma Chains and scalability debates, and a vibrant grassroots ecosystem (Sigmanauts mining pool growth, ErgOne marketing initiative, meme token ecosystem). The month closed with ErgoHack VIII announced, a successful emission reduction completed, and the Ergo community rallying around the "future of Proof-of-Work" narrative articulated by kushti.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### MEXC Exchange Listing
The MEXC listing dominated community discussion throughout April. Integration complexity arose because MEXC became the first CEX to use FleetSDK, requiring significant adaptation to Ergo's eUTXO model rather than EVM-style accounting. Four to six Ergo developers collaborated daily with MEXC on API integration. Community rumours of a "first week of April" launch proved optimistic; the realistic window was mid-April to mid-May. ERG was detected on MEXC infrastructure by late April (W17), and the official listing was confirmed by 2024-04-28. The 60k ERG listing fee was mentioned in passing. Community sentiment was mixed: celebration tempered by questions about whether the listing would translate to price appreciation during ongoing emission inflation.

### Rosen Bridge Expansion
Rosen Bridge made significant forward progress. The BTC testnet launched during W14/W15, enabling cross-chain asset wrapping (rsBTC, rsERG, rsADA). The architecture supports direct chain-to-chain transfers with Ergo as facilitator rather than mandatory intermediary. Discussion covered token naming conventions for wrapped assets, UI mapping of token sources, and the constraint that rewrapping across multiple hops (e.g., rsBTC from Ergo to Cardano back to BTC) is handled cleanly by locking on the source chain. The Palmyra project used rsERG from Rosen Bridge for its IDO round. Bridge times of 60–90 minutes were flagged as a UX trade-off for security. A "port/bus" abstraction layer for all-chains support was in development with a 1–2 month timeline cited by kii's team.

### Sigma Chains Vision
kushti introduced Sigma Chains as a cornerstone of Ergo's long-term PoW strategy: parallel sidechains tailored to specific ASIC hardware ecosystems (e.g., Ethash ASICs), each supporting merged mining with Ergo and funnelling emissions back to ErgoTree development and a DevDAO. The concept positions Ergo as a hub for a multi-chain PoW future, absorbing orphaned mining hardware rather than competing with it. Sigma Chains are not a near-term release — kushti framed them as something to build through the next bear market for release on the next cycle. The design incorporates trustless pegging mechanisms analogous to BIP 301 and allows customisable block times and emission profiles per chain.

### Scalability & Block Speed Debate
A recurring thread across all four weeks concerned whether Ergo should pursue faster blocks, sub-blocks, sidechains, or layer 2 solutions. kushti noted that the current mature set of on-chain contracts (Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, AMM pool contracts, Trade House, lending contracts) provides a concrete application profile that makes optimisation tractable — unlike chains with empty parallel infrastructure. Technical constraints discussed included: timestamp/block-height dependencies in dApps, emission schedule sensitivity to block time, and security trade-offs of faster blocks in worst-case scenarios. Sub-blocks remained the most-requested near-term improvement but no ETA was confirmed. A testnet (v6.0 parameters) was made available for community-led experimentation; kushti invited interested participants to DM for Telegram access.

### Emission Reduction & Supply Mechanics
The block reward reduction from 30 ERG to 27 ERG completed successfully during W15. Projected annual inflation: ~8.54% (2024–2025), ~4.28% (2025–2026), ~1.22% (2026–2027), stabilising below 1% by 2028, reaching ~0.84% by 2041. Market cap remained roughly flat 2022–2024 despite supply near-doubling, which community members noted as a baseline for evaluating future price action. Storage rent (demurrage) mechanics were explained multiple times: ~0.14 ERG per UTXO lost to miners after ~4 years of inactivity; wallets include consolidation tooling to reset the timer; the parameter is adjustable via miner vote.

### Palmyra Economy & Chainlink Partnership
Dan Friedman's Palmyra project launched its PALM IDO on Cardano using rsERG from Rosen Bridge. Palmyra uses Chainlink oracles coupled with IoT devices (camera, weight sensor, motion sensor) for on-chain warehouse receipt issuance, enabling decentralised trade financing for commodity producers. Dan donated $50k USD worth of PALM tokens to the Sigmanauts DAO treasury. Community discussion addressed tribalism concerns — kushti and Armeanio both argued that companies choosing to use a subset of Ergo's ecosystem (e.g., Chainlink oracles initially, with plans to expand to native Ergo Oracle Pools) represent a net positive, not a betrayal. Oracle Pool v2 is running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards; plans to expand beyond EF and community operators were discussed.

### Sigmanauts Mining Pool & Community Infrastructure
The Sigmanauts mining pool reached the top 10 on MiningPoolStats (~68 GH/s by W16 end), listed on HiveOn with four port configurations. Random token distribution mechanics were added: DarkDoge every 420 blocks (donated by darkerdoge, 30 tokens per draw, 3-year supply), with Erdoge, GreasyCex, and rsHOSKY planned additions. kushti emphasised mining as a jurisdictionally neutral, anonymous method of ERG acquisition using only electricity. The reward distribution code was noted as reusable for any project wishing to airdrop tokens to pool participants. Pool concentration risk from dxPool (>44% hashrate in W14) remained an open concern.

### Meme Token Ecosystem
April saw significant activity in Ergo's meme token space. CruxFinance (a Spectrum DEX fork focused on meme tokens, previously referred to in earlier context) launched with Bober token achieving $39.5k total volume and 819 swaps by W16; a lottery was planned at 1,000 swaps. heisenbERG emerged as a mystery token with full supply and LP locked until 2026/2028, generating $2.6k+ trading volume. Community debate distinguished these projects — with LP locks, transparent mechanics, and verifiable contracts — from Solana-style rugpulls. COMET token from darkerdoge was distributed via pool rewards. CruxFinance was added to CoinGecko in W17.

### ErgoHack VIII & Developer Ecosystem
ErgoHack VIII was announced in W17 with the theme "Ergo as a Smart Layer," focused on Bitcoin–Ergo interoperability. Prize pool: $9k (1st), $5k (2nd), $4k (3rd). Registration opened. Separately, ergo-lib-go v0.26.0 was released (Go wrapper for sigma-rust C bindings) and ErgoScript/Sigma Lang 6.0 milestone work continued on GitHub. ErgoNames contracts were completed but review and implementation were delayed by the developer's illness; major push expected in summer.

### Zero-Knowledge Proofs & bulletproofs
kushti gave a detailed explanation during the weekly AMA of bulletproofs as a trustless (no trusted setup) zero-knowledge technique usable for range proofs and generic statements. Bulletproof verification can be implemented in ErgoScript, enabling hybrid contracts where sensitive data is proven without disclosure while on-chain conditions are still verified. This opens possibilities including verifiable credentials and Groth one-out-of-many proof constructions (as previously used in Zcoin). The discussion connected to broader Sigma protocol breakthroughs from T. Attema's PhD work.

### ErgOne Community Marketing Initiative
ErgOne launched as a decentralised, community-driven marketing protocol with a fixed 1M token supply, a Proof-of-Commitment reward system, and Twitter-first distribution expanding to YouTube. It operates independently of the Ergo Foundation, aiming to incentivise organic promotion of Ergo projects. kushti tipped 10,000 COMET to mgPai in recognition of ErgOne coverage.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Sky, 2024-04-03): Can you explain storage rent (demurrage) simply?
  **A** (@TMR.ΣRG): If coins are not moved for approximately 4 years, miners claim ~0.14 ERG per address as a fee. This incentivises UTXO consolidation, improves network efficiency, and allows lost coins to re-enter circulation as mining rewards. The parameter is adjustable via miner vote. Wallets include a built-in consolidation button to reset the timer automatically.

- **Q** (@ROB, 2024-04-03): Can you reset the storage rent timer by sending a transaction between wallets?
  **A** (@TMR.ΣRG): Yes. Any spending transaction resets the timer. Wallets have built-in consolidation tooling that does this automatically.

- **Q** (@J. P. Costa, 2024-04-11): If a Ledger wallet receives 20+ distinct assets in one transaction, are funds locked?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @Unknown): Funds are not locked — only signing is blocked. Restore the hardware wallet via "add new hardware wallet" in Nautilus (no seed input required for hardware wallet reconnection), or import the seed phrase into Ergo Mobile wallet. The Ledger app cannot sign transactions referencing more than 20 distinct tokens.

- **Q** (@Anthony M, 2024-04-13): I sent SigmaUSD but received no ERG; the transaction shows as successful on the explorer.
  **A** (@qx(), @HQΣr): Funds are stuck in a proxy address because the transaction had insufficient ERG to cover all fees. Use the SigmaUSD.io menu (upper right) → "Submit Refund" with the sending address and proxy address.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-04-13): Does Rosen Bridge allow direct BTC-to-Cardano wrapping, or must all traffic go through Ergo as intermediary?
  **A** (@CW): You can bridge BTC directly to chain X; Ergo facilitates transfers but is not always a mandatory intermediary. Token naming and display are handled via token ID UI mapping on the destination chain.

- **Q** (@hexa decimal, 2024-04-09): Could Ergo implement RGB++? ErgoScript seems well suited. Nervos is getting attention for this.
  **A** (@kushti): Expressed skepticism — "But is it really needed, or just hype?" — implying the functionality may already be achievable natively without adopting RGB++ specifically.

- **Q** (@Rj, 2024-04-22): Is Ergo susceptible to Bitcoin's timewarp vulnerability and Merkle tree attacks (as raised in the Great Consensus Cleanup revival)?
  **A** (@qx(), with context from kushti): Ergo's variable block timing makes certain timing attacks harder to execute. Specific vulnerability parity analysis was not fully provided but kushti's sharing of the discussion implied ongoing monitoring of Bitcoin consensus research for applicability.

- **Q** (@BTCMini, 2024-04-23): Is kushti's 6.0 Sigma update the same as the Reference Client?
  **A** (@Glasgow): No — these are separate. The Reference Client is the node software. The 6.0 milestone refers to Sigma Lang (ErgoScript) improvements tracked at the sigmastate-interpreter GitHub milestone.

- **Q** (@Bye H8er, 2024-04-24): Could developers build a yield aggregator across DuckPools, Spectrum DEX, and SigmaFi?
  **A** (@qx()): Lending (e.g., DuckPools) is the best approach for avoiding impermanent loss; yields from lending can offset IL in AMM positions. SigmaUSD holders are particularly well positioned via DuckPools lending. No aggregator project has been announced yet.

- **Q** (@cannon_q, 2024-04-27): Does storage rent only clear truly abandoned UTXOs, or can active wallets keep boxes indefinitely through regular consolidation?
  **A** (@dayumbbbb): Active consolidation (any spending transaction) keeps boxes indefinite. Only genuinely abandoned UTXOs are eventually collected by miners. The property is ephemeral only if unmaintained.

- **Q** (from recorded AMA, 2024-04-07/W14): What are bulletproofs?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a trustless zero-knowledge technique (no trusted setup required) enabling range proofs and generic statement proofs. They function as a specific form of Sigma protocol. Bulletproof verification can be implemented in ErgoScript, making it possible to verify proofs on-chain without disclosing sensitive data. This enables hybrid contracts combining on-chain execution with zero-knowledge statements — opening applications including verifiable credentials and Groth one-out-of-many proofs (as used in early Zcoin). An alternative to SNARKs without requiring a trusted setup ceremony.

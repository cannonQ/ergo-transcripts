# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General | **Month:** 2020-05

---

## Executive Summary

May 2020 was a foundational month for Ergo, marked by rapid ecosystem maturation across mining infrastructure, privacy tooling, and DeFi primitives. The community shifted deliberately away from airdrop-driven growth toward developer bounties and application-focused ecosystem building, while core development delivered multiple node releases, completed the DEX contracts and token mixer, and published the ZeroJoin research paper. A potential "Hardening" fork was proposed to batch several protocol improvements, signalling the team's intent to front-load complexity before the network matures.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Mining Infrastructure & Pool Development
Hash rate grew steadily throughout May, rising from approximately 3.3 TH/s at the start of the month to over 5.3 TH/s by week's end, reflecting genuine miner interest. Jason Davies developed and began beta testing the first public Ergo mining pool, using a PPS model with a 5% fee and a collateral-based contract design to overcome Autolykos's inherent pool-resistance. The pool remained in private testing through month-end, with a public launch anticipated imminently. RTX 2070 GPUs were achieving ~61 MH/s with the open-source miner, with optimized miners expected to reach ~100 MH/s. Mining remained somewhat centralised per explorer data, a concern noted but not yet resolved.

### Privacy: ErgoMix, ZeroJoin & Bulletproofs
The formal research paper "ZeroJoin: Combining ZeroCoin and CoinJoin" was published to IACR ePrint, receiving rapid positive attention from Charles Hoskinson. The token mixer was completed and testing finished, enabling stablecoin mixing as a significant new use case. A critical vulnerability in the mixing web application (previously reported by Jason Davies) was patched by anon92048. kushti also outlined the potential for bulletproofs to be verified within ErgoScript, enabling a wide variety of zero-knowledge protocols — including compact Sigma protocols and Groth one-out-of-many proofs — alongside Ergo contracts, allowing sensitive data to be proven without disclosure.

### DEX, Smart Contracts & DeFi Primitives
DEX contracts supporting multiple order matching were finalised by greenhat, with Ilya Oskin beginning work on the matcher service. Swap orders became visible in the explorer. The first MuSig (aggregated multisignature) transaction was executed on mainnet by Jason Davies, enabling cross-chain private swaps that appear as ordinary transfers on-chain — a significant milestone toward BTC ↔ USD private swaps once Schnorr support lands in Bitcoin. Jason Davies also began work on an ErgoScript IDE to allow visual inspection and experimentation with contract execution.

### "Hardening" Fork Proposal
kushti proposed batching several protocol improvements into a single "Hardening" fork: improving Autolykos memory-hardness for more robust ASIC resistance, introducing JIT costing for ErgoScript execution, reducing the storage rent collection period from 4 years to 2 years, and incorporating security fixes. The rationale was to avoid future forks by hardening the network economically in one pass. No firm date was set; community and miner consensus was still being gathered at month's end.

### Storage Rent Economics
storage rent activation (scheduled for July 1, 2023) was highlighted by kushti as a fundamental long-term economic shift: miners are incentivised to support applications that grow the UTXO set, creating sustainable rewards beyond block emission. The proposal to reduce the collection period from 4 to 2 years was debated; consensus emerged that 2 years should be a hard minimum, with the change to be delivered via hard fork rather than a miner-adjustable parameter.

### Ecosystem Partnerships & Exchanges
Three significant announcements were made at the May 13 community chat: a CoinsBit exchange listing with promotional campaign, a partnership with Cypra (a multi-currency SPV wallet featuring built-in ErgoMix, atomic swaps, and decentralised indexing), and Ergo's membership in the Blockchain Privacy, Security & Adoption Alliance (BPSAA). kushti indicated the next major exchange listing would be from a top-10 exchange. Integration with a CMC top-20 liquidity exchange was described as near completion. Exchange listings had generally been secured without paying listing fees, a point of principle for the Ergo Foundation.

### Community Growth & Quality
A large influx of low-quality participants via the CoinsBit airdrop prompted kushti to disable automatic member additions and declare an end to airdrop campaigns. The community pivoted firmly to rewarding node runners and developer bounties. Charles Hoskinson's public description of Ergo as "one of the most revolutionary cryptocurrencies ever built" provided strong external validation. Discord's Research and Development channels were identified as the highest-value discussion spaces.

### Node Releases & Scalability
Node versions 3.2.4, 3.2.5, and 3.2.6 were released during May, with 3.2.7 and 3.3.0 in the pipeline. Version 3.2.4 introduced token burning support and raised the transaction complexity limit to 250,000. The distributed signatures EIP was being finalised. kushti published a scalability plan to the Ergo Forum, noting that Ergo's flexible block parameters allow miners to increase throughput without protocol changes — already up ~50% since mainnet launch — with Layer 2 solutions (Lightning Network, Rainbow Network, state channels) available when on-chain load demands them.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Bullish Teddy, 2020-05-11): What is storage rent?
  **A** (@kushti): The most interesting economic changes begin on July 1, 2023 when storage rent activates. Miners are clearly incentivised long-term to support applications that increase the UTXO set size, creating sustainable rewards beyond emission. Most miners don't understand this yet.

- **Q** (community, 2020-05-11): Can you explain what bulletproofs are?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge proof technique allowing specific statements to be proven without a trusted setup, unlike SNARKs. Used in Monero for range proofs, they can also prove generic statements. The goal is to have verification done in ErgoScript, enabling a variety of zero-knowledge protocols — including compact Sigma protocols and Groth one-out-of-many proofs — alongside Ergo contracts, allowing sensitive data to be proven without disclosure on-chain.

- **Q** (community, 2020-05-13): What wallet should be used for secure ERG storage, particularly for institutional investors?
  **A** (@kushti): Magnum and CoinBarn store keys locally; Magnum offers a downloadable backup, CoinBarn requires a written mnemonic. The fullnode's encrypted keystore provides the highest security for offline or airgapped storage and has passed a security audit. For institutional-grade security, use a pre-2006 computer for transactions only (Intel backdoor concerns) with paper backups in a safe.

- **Q** (community, 2020-05-18): Are Ergo smart contracts safer because they are non-Turing-complete?
  **A** (@kushti): The ErgoScript language is not non-Turing-complete, but the blockchain's single-transaction execution is bounded. Ergo is theoretically Turing-complete across multiple transactions, similar to Bitcoin's approach but more expressive.

- **Q** (community, 2020-05-26): Is storage rent a parameter miners can vote on?
  **A** (@kushti, @Jason Davies): Miners cannot change the storage rent period through voting; they can only adjust the fee coefficient within defined limits. Changing the period from 4 to 2 years requires a hard fork, which is what is being proposed.

- **Q** (community, 2020-05-26): If the storage rent period changes via fork, can miners immediately collect rent from all old boxes?
  **A** (@kushti, @Jason Davies): Via a hard fork miners can technically do anything, but any soft-fork approach requires careful discussion of limitations. Contract designers should anticipate storage rent and design their contracts accordingly.

- **Q** (community, 2020-05-27): How does pooling work given Autolykos pool-resistance?
  **A** (@kushti): Autolykos requires miners to have access to their private key, meaning they could theoretically run off with a block reward. However, Ergo's contract capabilities allow pooling via collateral contracts — see https://blog.plutomonkey.com/2020/05/collateral-based-pool-for-ergo/ for the reference design.

- **Q** (community, 2020-05-28): Can developers determine the cost of deploying smart contracts in Ergo, unlike Ethereum?
  **A** (@kushti): The execution cost is defined by the protocol, but the associated fee is a matter of agreement between users and miners — it is not protocol-mandated as in Ethereum.

- **Q** (@Bullish Teddy, 2020-05-10): How many transactions per second can Ergo handle?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo is flexible — miners can increase throughput by raising the block size limit or the cumulative computational cost limit. Throughput has already increased approximately 50% since mainnet launch without any protocol changes.

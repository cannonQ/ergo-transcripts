# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: March 2025

---

## Executive Summary

March 2025 was defined by two parallel tracks: significant core protocol advancement (sub-blocks development, Ergo 6.0.0-RC3 testnet release, Nautilus wallet overhaul) and a community-wide debate about ecosystem growth strategy centred on the Charles Hoskinson/Dogecoin Sigma-chain proposal. Underlying both tracks was a persistent concern about mining profitability sustainability as block rewards continue declining, with the community exploring storage rent, DeFi tooling, and external partnerships as offsetting mechanisms.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Sub-blocks & Protocol Speed Improvements

Sub-blocks emerged as the month's primary technical development thread. By mid-March, kushti reported that out-of-order sub-block application was working, with fork handling and P2P messaging actively in development. Sub-blocks are confirmed to be created even with zero transactions in DevNet, giving solo miners roughly 120x more frequent chances to collect transaction fees and storage rent. The community acknowledged that any block time reduction has downstream effects on timestamp-dependent contracts, requiring careful parameter tuning on testnet before mainnet consideration. A community testnet channel exists for interested contributors; kushti invited anyone passionate about the parameter-tuning process to DM him, noting that no coding skill is required—just motivation to coordinate scientific observations. The broader "summer-ish" ETA for sub-blocks was acknowledged as an estimate only.

### Ergo 6.0 Release Candidate & Security Audit

Ergo protocol reference client 6.0.0-RC3 was released to testnet mid-month, buildable locally without issues and published as a Docker image. A community security audit raffle via Ergo raffle completed successfully. The GraphQL API was updated to version 0.5.3, fixing usage at gql.ergoplatform.com for Nautilus. The broader 6.0 upgrade encompasses sub-blocks, improved sidechain constructions, and performance improvements, with testnet participants invited to help validate parameter changes.

### Wallet Ecosystem Releases

March saw a notable cluster of wallet releases. Nautilus progressed from v1.0.0-beta.1 through v1.0.0 (Chrome Web Store) to v1.1.0 (Mozilla Add-on Store), the latter adding SigmaUSD dApp integration. Satergo v1.9.0 introduced Ledger hardware wallet support, Satergo Offline Vault for Android, a more secure wallet format, and update functionality, with v1.9.1 following shortly after. Keystone hardware wallet integration was reported 90% complete. The Ergo Node Android project was revived with one-click installation supporting stateless (~1 GB), NIPoPoW, and full node (~3 GB) modes.

### Dogecoin Sigma-chain & Ecosystem Growth Strategy

Charles Hoskinson's proposal to fork Dogecoin using Ergo's Sigma-chain technology dominated community discussion in early March and generated sustained debate through the month. The core question was whether this constitutes genuine collaboration or Ergo's technology being adopted without direct benefit to ERG holders. kushti characterised it as Ergo exposing millions of Dogecoin users to its technology via a trustless bridge, while cautioning against over-reliance on a single external actor. A secondary debate emerged around the Binance community voting mechanism for new listings, which multiple participants flagged as Ergo's best near-term CEX opportunity, though no coordinated campaign structure was announced. The ideological tension between "libertarian purist" and "pragmatist" camps on visibility vs. independence was a recurring undercurrent throughout the month.

### Mining Profitability & Emission Reduction

The upcoming step-down from 18 ERG to 3 ERG per block (within ~25 days as of W11) focused community attention on long-term miner incentives. Without price appreciation to roughly 4–5 USD, a roughly 6x decline in mining profitability is projected, with a 1.5 Th/s hashrate potentially enabling 51% attacks. The community explored storage rent economics, Lithos Protocol's role in abstracting pool operations, and DeFi revenue (lending, liquidity provision) as partial offsets. No concrete contingency plan was articulated if price targets are not met before the next halving cycle.

### ChainCash, Free Banking & P2P Monetary Primitives

kushti submitted the ChainCash paper and announced a collaborative "Ergo Book" initiative covering P2P money theory, grassroots finance, and programmable on-chain reserves. ChainCash was discussed extensively as a decentralised banking primitive enabling organisations and DAOs to issue their own stable notes backed by mixed reserves, with 2% redemption fees discouraging speculative redemption and on-chain reserve visibility providing trust signals. This was distinguished from simple token issuance by its ability to attach smart contract logic and its resistance to whale-induced volatility. Contributors including Armeanio and Grayman joined the Ergo Book effort.

### Rosen Bridge Activity & Runes Integration

Notable cross-chain activity included 0.5 BTC (~$39k USD) bridged Bitcoin → Ergo during W11. Armeanio published two Medium articles exploring Runes integration via Rosen Bridge and RuneScript for embedding metadata into Runes using Rosen's approach. Watcher economics were clarified (~25 RSN/week on Ergo chain, ~250 RSN/week on Bitcoin chain). The cross-chain arbitrage feature in MEW Bank was noted as partially implemented, with price feeds still under construction. Monero bridging via Edwards25519 multisig was flagged as an active R&D direction, with kushti seeking design review from Armeanio.

### MewFinance & SigmaUSD Ecosystem

MewFinance launched several live products during the month: the SigmaUSD bank UI at dev.mewfinance.com, MEW Bank (full AgeUSD protocol platform for minting/redeeming/trading SigmaUSD and SigmaRSV with ErgoPay integration) at bank.mewfinance.com, and a DuckPools loans dashboard. DuckPools testnet went live at test.duckpools.io. The SigmaUSD update box was refreshed for storage rent safety "for decades to come." A public testnet Dexy UI launched at test.ergfi.xyz.

### Palmyra & Real-World Asset Financing

The Palmyra/Chainlink partnership scope was detailed across multiple weeks: proof-of-reserve via Chainlink proxy functions in Winter Protocol, coupled with IoT devices (cameras, weight/motion sensors) for continuous commodity auditing, enabling on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralised trade financing. The model is positioned as a zero-sum complement to Ergo's native Oracle Pools rather than a replacement—plans include using token sale capital to build out native Ergo Oracle Pool v1 and v2 infrastructure (the latter already running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards). Palmyra's TGE was delayed again to April. The broader discussion touched on real estate debt financing using Merkle tree proofs of title digests and multisig "personal oracle" approaches for POC scenarios.

### Bulletproofs & Zero-Knowledge in ErgoScript

kushti elaborated on bulletproofs as compact Sigma protocol variants enabling range proofs and generic zero-knowledge statements without trusted setup. The key insight shared is that verifying bulletproof-style proofs directly in ErgoScript—alongside Groth one-out-of-many proofs—would enable hybrid contract patterns where sensitive data is proven without disclosure, opening verifiable credential use cases and a "whole new world of possibilities" for combining on-chain execution with zero-knowledge statements.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2025-03-09): How do you create a reduced transaction, and is that how Nautilus consumes it?
  **A** (@Aco, @HQΣr): Use `ReducedTransaction.from_unsigned_tx()` in ergolib on a normal unsigned TX. Nautilus uses normal TX (BigJSON format); reduced transactions are only for ErgoPay flows.

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2025-03-04): Is the projected hashrate drop a security problem? What is an acceptable minimum, and how does Ergo's GPU mining compare to ASIC chains?
  **A** (community): Current hashrate ~10 Th/s; a 6x emissions drop without price appreciation implies ~1.5 Th/s, sufficient for a 51% attack with ~500–600 Gh/s. Storage rent partially offsets lost revenue. GPU mining is more decentralised than ASIC chains; threshold depends on value at risk.

- **Q** (@Σddie Lin, 2025-03-04): Will the Sigma-chain use Ergo's coin or its own? What is the actual connection to Ergo?
  **A** (@Richi): Sigma-chain is a contractual layer of Ergo. Dogecoin could fork to become a Sigma-chain, retain its own coin, and get a trustless bridge to Ergo. Millions of users would encounter Ergo via the bridge, but no other mandatory connections exist.

- **Q** (community, 2025-03-10): Lightning swaps / Lightning Network implementation on Ergo — is this viable?
  **A** (@kushti): No full LN implementation; only an adaptor signature demo swap with Bitcoin Cash (Jason Davies). LN TVL is low even on Bitcoin. More interest in Ark, Cashu, and Fedimint — Ergo's capabilities enable better off-chain cash solutions on top of on-chain reserves.

- **Q** (@iVacuum, 2025-03-17): For a property development loan, how can off-chain proof that a charge has been registered on title trigger on-chain fund release?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo can post a short digest of all property titles; an on-chain contract uses the title plus a proof that it belongs to the current digest (Merkle tree approach). Alternatively, off-chain validators execute scripts against augmented context (titles), producing results that unlock on-chain vaults.
  **A** (@kii): For POC, point the contract to a multisig wallet acting as certifier. When a loan is approved, the certifier creates a box with a loan ID; the loan contract checks the certifier's wallet for a matching box and releases funds. This "personal oracle" approach is simpler than building full oracle infrastructure.

- **Q** (@J. P. Costa, 2025-03-17): What are average Rosen Watcher earnings, and do I need a fully synced Ergo node?
  **A** (@qx()): Average ~25 RSN/week on Ergo chain, ~250 RSN/week on Bitcoin chain. A fully synced node is required (indexing not required); ~20 GB unindexed — storage rent keeps the running state small. Startup cost is lower now; running early builds experience and channel support before demand increases.

- **Q** (@J. P. Costa, 2025-03-17): What is the Scorex API key in the node config?
  **A** (@qx()): It is the hash of your node API password — whatever you set is how you unlock the node interface at 127.0.0.1:9053. Not recommended to keep the default "hello."

- **Q** (@Harris, 2025-03-19): What stops users constantly redeeming ChainCash IOUs for collateral if the price appreciates, making it volatile like raw ERG?
  **A** (@qx()): ChainCash notes are certificates of value backed by a trusted entity or organisation (e.g., Sigmanauts). Redemption fees plus entity reputation (on-chain reserve visibility) and smart contract logic attachments differentiate it from raw token trading. Organisations issue their own money backed by treasury reserves rather than dumping tokens on a DEX, preventing whale-induced volatility and enabling stable organisation-issued money supply.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2025-03-26): Will sub-blocks be created even with no transactions in the mempool?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes — sub-blocks are created even in the current DevNet with zero transactions.

- **Q** (@Crypto Quokka, 2025-03-18): Recommended readings on the philosophy of decentralised finance before Bitcoin?
  **A** (@kushti): Papers: "Peer-to-peer money: Free currency over the Internet" (K. Saito); original Ripple whitepaper ("Money as IOUs in a Social Trust Network"); "Peer-to-Peer Economics for Post Catastrophic Recovery." Books: *The Ecology of Money* (Richard Douthwaite); *Rethinking Money* (B. Lietaer et al.).

- **Q** (community, 2025-03-25): Are there docs for running storage rent on a pool?
  **A** (@qx(), @Marc the Shark): No tutorial yet; code at https://github.com/K-Singh/SR-Service. Requires MiningCore config + node config + potentially custom node jar build. Help available from @dadreboi and @Marc the Shark.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

1. **Sub-block reward mechanics**: Exact mechanism for distributing transaction fees and storage rent between sub-block miners and mainchain miners remains unspecified. Community testnet needs participants to validate parameter changes before any mainnet proposal.

2. **Mining profitability contingency**: With the 18→3 ERG emission step approaching and no concrete price catalyst, what is the fallback if hashrate declines to attack-threshold levels? Storage rent and DeFi revenue offsets are speculative at current ecosystem scale.

3. **Dogecoin Sigma-chain execution model**: Will Dogecoin fork or run as a sidechain? Who funds development? What governance ensures Ergo's interests are protected? No confirmed roadmap as of month end.

4. **Binance listing campaign coordination**: Community flagged Binance's new voting mechanism as a high-priority opportunity but no formal campaign structure, lead, or timeline was established.

5. **ChainCash preprint publication**: Paper described as "more or less done" but no publication date given; Ergo Book structure and contributor list also still being defined.

6. **Palmyra TGE and oracle infrastructure**: TGE delayed to April (repeatedly); Oracle Pool v2 timeline for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards unconfirmed; native Ergo oracle build-out dependent on token sale capital.

7. **Monero bridging design**: Edwards25519 multisig overlay requirements under review by Armeanio; no design document or timeline published.

8. **Storage rent pool deployment documentation**: Full configuration walkthrough (Scorex, MiningCore, custom node jar) not yet published; currently requires direct support from pool developers.

9. **ErgoPay mobile wallet compatibility**: Persistent connection hang reported on iPhone with Minotaur wallet; root cause not diagnosed; QR slideshow standard proposal unresolved, no bounty committed.

10. **Community governance voting mechanism**: How to enable binding developer-priority votes without requiring miner veto or forking remains an open design problem raised by kushti with no proposed solution.

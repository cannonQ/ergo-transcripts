# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General | **Month:** 2017-11

---

## Executive Summary

November 2017 was Ergo's foundational public communications period, dominated by community education around the platform's core architecture, token economics, and imminent TestNet launch. The team — primarily kushti (Alexander Chepurnoy) and Dmitry Meshkov — fielded extensive questions about EFYT conversion, the relationship with Waves, and Ergo's technical differentiators. The month closed with a confirmed TestNet release date of December 4, 2017, marking Ergo's first major public milestone.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Platform Architecture & Scorex Framework
Ergo is built on the Scorex framework, shared with Waves, and will eventually launch as an independent blockchain. The platform's design emphasises lightweight validation — validators can operate on smartphones — while miners use Equihash proof-of-work. The relationship with Waves is described as synergistic: Ergo takes Waves NG "to the next level" while both projects share founding team history and evolve in parallel. Scalability claims of extreme throughput (building on Waves NG) were raised but the underlying technical architecture was not fully elaborated in community discussions.

### Authenticated Dynamic Dictionaries (AVL+ Trees)
A significant technical discussion covered Ergo's use of authenticated AVL+ trees for state management. Miners hold full data structures and publish proofs of correct modifications, while verifiers need only maintain a 32-byte digest. This design enables trustless verification with minimal storage overhead — a core differentiator from conventional blockchain state models. An academic paper on improved authenticated dynamic dictionaries was shared as supporting reading.

### Token Economics & EFYT Distribution
The community received detailed clarification on the token model: first-year supply is capped at a maximum of 50M ERG (subject to reduction), with total supply capped at approximately 100M. The team retains 10% of first-year emission. EFYT (Ergo First Year Token), currently trading on the Waves DEX, will convert 1:1 to mainnet ERG upon launch. Future airdrops were confirmed for holders of EFYT, WCT, and/or WAVES tokens, with precise criteria and dates to be announced closer to the event. The economic whitepaper remained in draft review throughout the month.

### TestNet Preparation & Roadmap
The December 4, 2017 TestNet release was the month's headline announcement. A "second drive" was planned for the following week with social media surprise announcements. The team acknowledged multiple technical variables still being resolved before public release. Mainnet launch and the formal EFYT→ERG migration path were confirmed as roadmap items but without specific dates.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@druidEast, 2017-W45): Can we mine Ergo with smartphones?
  **A** (@Crypto Rev): You will be able to *validate* (not mine) from a smartphone. Mining requires Equihash proof-of-work hardware.

- **Q** (@druidEast, 2017-W45): Will validators be rewarded for validating transactions?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Yes — validators earn transaction fees as rewards. This is one of the core economic solutions offered by the platform.

- **Q** (@Kaper024, 2017-W45): How is Ergo valued at 50M supply × $10 without inflation destroying the price?
  **A** (@Terri): First-year supply is capped at 50M ERG, total supply at approximately 100M — both figures subject to reduction. The inflation curve is not yet finalised; current price reflects early-stage market dynamics.

- **Q** (@pietrod, 2017-W45): What is the connection between EFYT and Ergo? Is Ergo still under construction?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): EFYT stands for "Ergo First Year Token." It is a Waves asset that will convert 1:1 to mainnet ERG upon launch. Full details are available on the token distribution page and in archived Q&A sessions.

- **Q** (@pietrod, 2017-W45): Has the economic paper mentioned in prior Q&A been released?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Not yet — the draft is still being edited.

- **Q** (@Scavangerr bogy, 2017-W45): How does Ergo differ from Waves, and will Ergo support Waves?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Ergo builds on Waves NG and takes it to the next level. Both share the Scorex framework and founding team history and evolve in synergy. Ergo will eventually operate as its own independent blockchain.

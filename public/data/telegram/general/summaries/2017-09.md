# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General
**Month:** 2017-09 (covering 2017-09-30 to 2017-11-01)

---

## Executive Summary

September 2017 marked the early pre-launch phase of the Ergo project, with the community focused on foundational questions around tokenomics, mining design, and project independence. The dominant themes were the imminent public testnet release, the novel storage rent mechanism for long-term miner sustainability, and clarifying Ergo's relationship with IOHK as a research collaborator rather than a parent organisation.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Ergo Independence & IOHK Relationship
A recurring point of confusion in the community was whether Ergo was an IOHK project, given kushti's listing on the IOHK team page. This was explicitly clarified: Ergo is a fully independent project that collaborates with IOHK only in a research capacity. The distinction was considered important for understanding the project's governance and direction.

### Testnet Launch
The public testnet was described as imminent throughout the period, though no exact date was committed to. Mining on the testnet was explicitly not yet enabled at the time of discussion, with the team promising a public announcement when that changed. The codebase health was evidenced by a shared link to Ergo's code coverage report.

### Tokenomics & Supply Model
The tokenomics model received significant discussion. First-year supply was capped at approximately 50M ERG (with some flexibility suggested), and total supply was not to exceed 100M ERG. The inflation curve was designed to issue coins quickly — within roughly five years — a deliberate design choice. Critically, the storage rent mechanism was introduced as the long-term solution for miner incentives: lost or unspent coins would be returned to circulation via storage rent, ensuring miners continue to receive rewards beyond the initial issuance period without requiring perpetual inflation.

### Proof of Work & Mining Algorithm
Ergo confirmed a proof of work consensus model using the Equihash algorithm. Dedicated mining hardware was acknowledged as an eventual requirement, though specifics were not yet finalised. Community members raised concerns about early mining concentration leading to centralisation, which prompted discussion of the long-term economic model — though the full economy model was explicitly flagged as still under construction.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2017-09-30): Can I mine the coin on the testnet?
  **A** (@Terri): Not yet — the team will make an announcement when mining becomes possible.

- **Q** (@Albert @ Genlayer, 2017-10-30): If first-year supply is ~50M ERG and the price is around $10, won't 10x inflation in year one crash the price?
  **A** (@Dmitry): The tokenomics model is designed so that even after fast initial issuance, the storage rent mechanism will return lost coins to circulation and provide sufficient long-term miner rewards to maintain decentralisation. The full economy model was still under construction at the time.

- **Q** (@Albert @ Genlayer, 2017-10-30): Won't concentrating mining rewards only in the first years lead to centralisation?
  **A** (@Dmitry): The long-term design goal is to ensure miner rewards remain sufficient after the initial issuance period to support a decentralised network. Storage rent is the key mechanism intended to achieve this.

- **Q** (@Andrey Kalinchev, 2017-11-01): When can I mine Ergo and with what algorithm?
  **A** (@Terri): Coming soon, using Equihash.

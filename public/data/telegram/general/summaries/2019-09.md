# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: September 2019

---

## Executive Summary

September 2019 was dominated by exchange integration activity, with ERG listing on P2PB2B, Bisq, and the questionable GJ.COM, alongside ongoing Tidex withdrawal issues. Community discussion centred on operational infrastructure — price oracle design, EFYT/ERG swap mechanics, and wallet support — rather than deep protocol development. The month saw the first developer tooling release (Ergo crowdfunding CLI v0.1.0) and early groundwork for dApp infrastructure via EIP-1.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Exchange Listings & Trading Volume
P2PB2B became the largest trading volume exchange for ERG during this period. GJ.COM listed ERG without coordinating with the Ergo team — completing integration in under 24 hours — raising community suspicion about legitimacy, though it was defended by community member Haiyan. Bisq v1.1.6 shipped with ERG/BTC support, providing a peer-to-peer option; no completed Bisq trades were reported by month end. Tidex withdrawal delays (up to 10 days in reported cases) were a recurring pain point, with the Ergo team escalating on behalf of affected users without confirmed resolution.

### EFYT/ERG Swap Mechanics
Community questions about the relationship between EFYT and ERG were addressed repeatedly across weeks. EFYT (Ergo First Year Token) was a pre-launch funding mechanism issued on Waves DEX, with a 1:1 swap ratio to ERG. Weekly swap walls continue distributing mined ERG against accumulated EFYT; the ratio occasionally dips below 1:1 on the peer-to-peer Waves market. Users were advised they can defer conversion indefinitely but should remain attentive to their positions. EFYT will remain operational as long as the Waves gateway continues to function.

### Price Oracle Design & Data Trustworthiness
Discussion arose around whether the Ergo team should run an independent ERG/BTC price ticker rather than relying on CoinMarketCap or CoinGecko data for ErgoScript contracts. CoinMarketCap attempts to exclude outliers to prevent fake volume from skewing prices, but this raises reliability concerns for on-chain contracts dependent on external price feeds. Oracle update frequency was speculated to be approximately 24 hours but was not confirmed. The broader question of trustworthy data sourcing for ErgoScript remained unresolved entering October.

### Developer Tooling & dApp Infrastructure
The Ergo crowdfunding CLI reached v0.1.0, with precompiled binaries for x86 Linux, ARM Linux, and Windows. EIP-1 was referenced as a forthcoming enhancement enabling dApps to build on Ergo without requiring browser extension embedding. The ErgoScript Playground (via Kiosk) was shared on the Ergo Forum. A P2S (pay-to-script) address concept — allowing exchanges to recover withdrawn funds within a 24-hour window before user control transfers — was discussed as mentioned in ErgoScript whitepapers but not yet prioritised for implementation.

### Wallet Support & Node Resource Usage
Magnum wallet supports ERG on web but not yet on Android (supporting only 7 coins at the time). A full node running v3.1.0 was reported allocating 13GB of RAM, flagged as potentially abnormal but without a definitive response from the core team. ERG transfer confirmation times were noted as exchange-dependent, with each platform setting its own confirmation threshold.

### Community Outreach
MHS_SAM presented Ergo at the Iran Blockchain Summit in late September. A smart contract presentation was created and shared internally; the community requested it be uploaded to YouTube for broader distribution, which remained unresolved at month end.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Jason Davies, 2019-09-13): How does CoinMarketCap calculate price, and what concerns exist for ErgoScript contracts relying on such data?
  **A** (@Jason Davies): CMC attempts to exclude outliers to prevent fake volume from influencing prices, but this raises reliability concerns if ErgoScript contracts depend on external price feeds. The community may need to run independent price calculations rather than trusting aggregator data.

- **Q** (community, 2019-09-16): Why do both EFYT and ERG exist? Is EFYT deprecated?
  **A** (@Kookster, @𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): EFYT was a pre-launch funding mechanism on Waves DEX with a 1:1 swap to ERG. It is not deprecated — EFYT will remain operational for Waves gateway access as long as the gateway continues to function.

- **Q** (@scalahub, 2019-09-27): Has anyone tested Bisq for ERG trading?
  **A** (@Jason Davies): ERG is live on Bisq but no completed trades had occurred yet. Integration does not require tight wallet coupling — users verify the ERG transfer on Ergo Explorer before releasing the BTC security deposit; disputes are also resolved via explorer verification.

- **Q** (community, 2019-09-27): Are there plans to implement P2S addresses allowing exchanges to recover withdrawn funds within a 24-hour window, with user control transferring after that?
  **A** (community): The concept is mentioned in ErgoScript whitepapers and is expected eventually, but had not been prioritised for implementation at this time.

- **Q** (community, W39): Why is ERG transfer speed very slow?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Normal behaviour — each exchange sets its own confirmation threshold before crediting a transfer, accounting for the variation in apparent speed.

# Ergo General — Monthly Summary: 2018-03

---

## Executive Summary

March 2018 was a foundational month for the Ergo community, dominated by clarification of token economics (supply, emission schedule, and EFYT conversion mechanics), updates to the TestNet, and early technical design discussions. Community engagement centred on understanding the dual-chain architecture, the non-outsourceability mining protocol, and ErgoScript's capabilities, with the core team providing regular transparency while deferring firm mainnet dates to Q2/Q3 2018.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EFYT Token Conversion & Supply Economics
The month saw sustained community confusion around circulating supply versus total supply, which the team worked to resolve. Total supply was definitively confirmed at 100M tokens over an approximately 8-year emission schedule, with first-year emission projected at approximately 20M tokens. EFYT converts at a 1:1 ratio to both chains — holders receive 1 ErgoMoney + 1 ErgoData per EFYT held. Of the original 5M EFYT, approximately 3M were burned, leaving roughly 1.97M circulating, with a float of approximately 600K at the time of discussion. Additional distributions were planned to IOHK employees and the general public via PoW mining — no ICO was confirmed multiple times throughout the month.

### Dual-Chain Architecture (MoneyChain & DataChain)
Ergo was planned to launch with two parallel chains: MoneyChain and DataChain, featuring identical underlying technology but different parameters. Community members asked repeatedly about the mechanics of the EFYT-to-ERG swap and about whether mining would reward one or both chains. These questions were addressed partially, though mining-specific semantics remained somewhat unclear. The two-chain design was positioned as a core differentiator but details on parameter divergence were not fully elaborated.

### Mining: Non-Outsourceability, ASIC Resistance, and GPU Mining
The non-outsourceability protocol — a mechanism designed to discourage centralised mining pool formation while keeping pools technically viable — was one of the most discussed technical topics. The protocol was still in development and gated by GitHub issue #218. Ergo's mining algorithm was confirmed as Equihash (GPU-mineable, based on a modified Zcash miner), with ASIC resistance achieved through periodic — though not monthly — algorithmic updates to render ASIC development uneconomical. Mobile device participation was scoped for light node validation (digest mode via ADProofs) with potential incentives, but mobile mining itself was not on the roadmap.

### ErgoScript Language Design
A language specification for ErgoScript was published during this period. Key features discussed included: Scala/TypeScript-influenced syntax, higher-order functions with first-class lambdas, call-by-value evaluation, static typing with local type inference, blocks as expressions, and type constructors (Tuple, Array, Option). Critically, ErgoScript's Sigma protocols enable zero-knowledge proofs without a trusted setup, positioning it as a privacy-capable smart contract language without the trusted ceremony risks associated with SNARKs.

### TestNet Progress & Mainnet Timeline
TestNet v0.2.3 was released on 2018-03-05 and TestNet v0.2.4 on 2018-04-05. Mainnet was initially rumoured for May 2018 but was clarified as targeting end of Q2 or mid-Q3 2018. Wallet functionality in early TestNet was minimal — no wallet.dat equivalent existed yet, and seed management was only available via the Swagger API. Mining software availability remained blocked on issue #218 resolution.

### Regulatory Positioning
Community members raised the question of how emerging US SEC/CFTC scrutiny of cryptocurrencies might affect Ergo or EFYT holders. The team's non-ICO distribution approach (airdrop to WCT holders, planned IOHK employee distribution, PoW mining for the rest) was noted as positioning Ergo favourably relative to projects that conducted public token sales.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-03-02): Will it be cheaper to buy ERG directly than to buy EFYT and convert, or will the price be the same?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): That's impossible to predict — token values at swap time are not determinable in advance.

- **Q** (@Natanael, 2018-03-28): Why does one source say total supply is 100M while another says 8M–20M?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Total supply is always 100M. The 8–20M figure referred to the first-year *emission rate*, which was undefined at the time of earlier discussions and has since been resolved in the GitHub pull request.

- **Q** (@pietrod, 2018-03-28): Can you explain the dual-chain conversion — how does EFYT convert to MoneyChain and DataChain?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): 1 EFYT converts to 1 ErgoMoney + 1 ErgoData. Both chains use identical technology with different parameters and identical first-year emission schedules.

- **Q** (@Anton Bogdanovich, 2018-03-30): For the non-outsourceability protocol, if a single person owns multiple mining rigs, would it be possible to share work between them assuming all are trusted?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): The protocol is still in development and testing. The concept aims to minimise the pool aspect while keeping trusted multi-rig setups technically viable.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-03-28): Will Ergo prevent ASIC dominance the way Monero does with frequent algorithm changes?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Ergo uses Equihash (GPU-mineable). ASIC development is discouraged via periodic — not monthly — algorithm updates combined with the non-outsourceability protocol. Frequent enough changes render ASIC ROI uneconomical without requiring monthly disruption.

- **Q** (@Weetgeennaam123, 2018-04-05): Can Ergo be mined with the same algorithm as Ethereum?
  **A** (@community): No. Ergo uses Equihash (a modified Zcash miner), not Ethash. Different GPU optimisation profiles apply.

- **Q** (@Сергей Иванов, 2018-04-06): Does the non-outsourceability protocol intentionally make pool formation inefficient?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Yes — the main goal is a mechanism that discourages pool participation. Still in development.

- **Q** (@community, 2018-04-06): Will mobile devices be rewarded for validating transactions via ADProofs?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Mobile reward incentives are expected but in SPV/digest mode (light node operation). Mobile mining itself is not available — participation is via light node validation only.

- **Exact mainnet launch date**: Roadmap states end of Q2 or mid-Q3 2018; precision pending technical milestone completion.

# Ergo General — June 2023 Monthly Summary

## Executive Summary

June 2023 was dominated by preparations for and execution of the Ergoversary (4th anniversary) Summit on July 1–3, with major protocol milestones including the Ergo 5.0.12 node release, the HODLcoin launch, and confirmation of storage rent activation at block 1,051,200 on July 20. Underlying technical conversations ranged from UTXO set snapshots and NIPoPoWs bootstrapping to layer 2 scalability options, zero-knowledge proofs in ErgoScript, and the mechanics of the forthcoming Dexy stablecoin protocol.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Storage Rent (Demurrage) Activation
Storage rent activation was confirmed for block 1,051,200 on July 20, 2023. Boxes older than 4 years with insufficient ERG (~0.14 ERG per 4-year cycle) become claimable by miners. Community analysis identified ~13k ERG across ~304 boxes eligible over the following 6 months, with the majority of mining reward boxes taking centuries to fully collect rent. A significant side discussion developed around terminology: @dayumbbbb advocated strongly for "demurrage" over "storage rent," arguing the former carries superior marketing framing—positioning Ergo's mechanism as equivalent to Monero's tail emission (both achieving ~0% inflation) and as a monetary policy feature rather than a tax. Concerns were raised about NFTs in SigmaFi loan boxes carrying insufficient ERG to cover future rent obligations.

### UTXO Snapshots, NIPoPoWs & Node Bootstrapping
Ergo node v5.0.12 shipped with optional UTXO set snapshot support (~1–1.5 GB additional storage per snapshot, taken every 52,224 blocks). kushti clarified the opt-in nature and that only two nodes initially held pre-height 1,044,480 snapshots. Version 5.0.13/5.0.14 will add NIPoPoWs bootstrapping support, enabling ~20-minute syncs under non-conservative settings. Storage rent's 4-year UTXO age cap means wallets and applications only need blocks from the past 4 years for full functionality, enabling a principled pruning strategy without breaking other nodes' access to historical data.

### Dexy Stablecoin Protocol & Oracle Pool v2
kushti presented the Dexy design at the Ergoversary Summit. Dexy is simpler than SigmaUSD's reserve-shares model and uses rate limiting to resist bear whale attacks, with gold backing via Oracle Pool v2. The GORT oracle reward token will receive buyback fees from Dexy transactions via an ERG/GORT LP. Mainnet launch was targeted for late July, pending completion of audits across three contracts: Dexy itself, Oracle Pool v2, and the GORT buyback contract. Oracle Pool v2 was on testnet throughout June with contract voting mechanism testing. A related clarification emerged: AgeUSD was Djed's original design name; SigmaUSD implements "Minimal Djed"; COTI's own "Djed" branding caused confusion, and Bruno is rebranding newer protocol iterations as "Gluon."

### Rosen Bridge & Cross-Chain Infrastructure
The Rosen Bridge public beta went live at `beta.rosen.tech` with Cardano integration active for testing. Development milestones included: generic chain type abstraction complete, CIP-14 dependency removed, Watcher APIs for Assets and Info merged with Observations and Events in code review. Multi-key guard solutions for DAO/team-based guard configurations were under R&D alongside a Catalyst Fund 10 proposal. Community consensus was that Rosen Bridge will be critical for US-accessible liquidity once KuCoin imposed mandatory KYC from July 15 (US users effectively excluded), with Rosen + Spectrum enabling rADA/ERG pairs as the primary trustless on-ramp alternative.

### HODLcoin Launch
HODLcoin launched on July 2 by Pulsarz, mgPai, and kushti as a game-theoretic degenerate token: a 3% redemption fee redistributes to remaining holders, hardcoded in the smart contract. It reached 61k+ ERG TVL within hours with ~48 unique holders. The launch generated substantive debate about zero-sum dynamics (last holders bear loss unless ERG price appreciation offsets the fee), Spectrum LP arbitrage opportunities, and whether SigmaFi bonds using HODLERG as collateral were viable.

### Layer 2 Scalability & Block Speed
kushti confirmed multiple L2 constructions are possible on Ergo: Hydra, state channels, CoinPool, Ark, plasma, rollups, and sidechains. Spectrum is developing a sidechain with a whitepaper forthcoming. A community-led 6.0 testnet was proposed to study parameter tweaking (block time, difficulty) scientifically, with kushti inviting volunteers. A key complication noted: faster block times affect contracts relying on block height rather than timestamps, requiring those contracts to be revised before any block speed change.

### Zero-Knowledge Proofs & ErgoScript
kushti discussed integrating bulletproof verification within ErgoScript, enabling range proofs, Groth one-out-of-many proofs (used in early Zcoin), verifiable credentials, and hybrid on-chain/off-chain contracts where sensitive data is verified via zero-knowledge statements without disclosure. This was framed as opening a "whole new world" of privacy-preserving smart contract possibilities, combining Sigma protocols with generic zero-knowledge proof systems.

### Paideia, Spectrum, SkyHarbor & Ecosystem dApps
Paideia MVP contracts were finalised and optimised, with the team focusing on frontend integration for beta launch. SkyHarbor migrated its API endpoint to `api.skyharbor.io` and added ErgoPay and SafeW support. Crux Finance announced its IDO on ErgoPad. DuckPools completed a security audit (contract changes required per audit findings) and was ready for private testing starting with the ERG pool. Spectrum Finance was preparing a Cardano DEX launch with order book–based trading.

### Mixer Privacy & Wallet Landscape
A detailed technical discussion clarified that Ergo Mixer security depends on **participant density**, not round count alone. Best practices include holding mixed funds in a dead wallet, splitting exits, avoiding amount-matching patterns, and recycling leftovers. Integration into Nautilus was flagged as the key adoption lever. Yoroi's Ergo support was confirmed discontinued; a "Yoroi Ergo Wallet Recover" tool was released. The Ledger Ergo app remains functional in developer mode (in use for 1+ year) but awaits an official audit. Nautilus, Satergo, Ergo Wallet, and Minotaur were the recommended alternatives.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2023-06-19): Can Ergo support layer 2 constructions like Lightning Network?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes. Multiple L2 options are possible: Hydra, state channels, CoinPool, Ark, plasma, rollups, and sidechains. Spectrum is developing an interesting sidechain with a whitepaper forthcoming.

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2023-06-13): Does Ergo have client diversity or does it depend on a single client?
  **A** (@Pulsarz, @Aberg): Currently a single full-node client in Scala. Sigma Rust is the eventual path to a Rust client but requires feature parity first. Multiple clients are hard to maintain during active protocol development—Ethereum's experience shows it cuts both ways (non-reference clients saved the chain once, but also introduced unique bugs).

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-06-14): Is the UTXO set snapshot mandatory on upgrade?
  **A** (@qx(), @Glasgow): No — opt-in via `application.conf`. Adds ~1–1.5 GB per snapshot.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2023-06-14): What happens if every node bootstraps from snapshots — wouldn't pre-snapshot transactions be lost?
  **A** (@qx()): Yes; pre-snapshot history and explorer functions would require at least some fully-synced nodes. Explorers are currently hardcoded to a small set of nodes.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2023-06-20): Is there a way to check which wallets will be hit by storage rent?
  **A** (@qx()): Use `/ep boxage ANYADDRESS` on the ErgoPort Telegram bot. A spreadsheet of addresses with insufficient ERG to cover rent is at: `https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1v4m-XAh6_uVKA1eTHm-elE0M-pRFikfPj8trQE8mars/`

- **Q** (@eπ, 2023-06-20): Could quantum computing threaten Ergo's Autolykos PoW?
  **A** (@kushti): PoW is not the primary quantum concern. Ergo's Autolykos k-sum problem remains exponential-complexity even against quantum algorithms; parameters are large enough to resist known quantum attacks.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2023-06-11): What is the real security difference between 30, 60, and 90 mixer rounds?
  **A** (@qx()): Security depends on **participant density**, not round count. More rounds with more concurrent users makes chain analysis harder. Best practice: hold mixed funds in a dead wallet, split exits, avoid amount-matching patterns, push leftovers back through mixer.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-06-25): What is AgeUSD — is it the same as SigmaUSD?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @kushti): AgeUSD was the original design name. SigmaUSD v1 and v2 both implement "Minimal Djed." COTI named their instantiation "Djed," causing confusion. Bruno is rebranding newer protocol iterations as "Gluon."

- **Q** (@Arthur, 2023-06-28): What happens to tokens in boxes older than 4 years with ERG below storage rent?
  **A** (@qx(), @kushti): The entire box — including all tokens — goes to miners. Minimum box creation is 0.0001 ERG but rent is ~0.14 ERG per 4-year cycle. NFTs with complex data (~4 KB) can require several ERG in rent.

- **Q** (@Arthur, 2023-06-28): Can 0-conf transactions be made safe for instant UX on Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): No single canonical "mempool" exists — each node's mempool differs. Achieving mempool consistency would require a separate consensus protocol that doesn't scale with node count. The block producer's mempool may differ from the merchant's node. Miner-signed transaction commitments or risk-buyer intermediary models are the viable alternatives.

- **Q** (community, 2023-06-23): Can you explain what bulletproofs are and how they relate to ErgoScript?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge technique allowing specific statements (e.g., range proofs) to be proven without revealing data. They can serve as trustless alternatives to SNARKs — no trusted setup required. The goal is to verify bulletproof proofs within ErgoScript, which would also enable Groth one-out-of-many proofs (used in early Zcoin) and open up hybrid contracts where sensitive data is verified on-chain via zero-knowledge statements without disclosure.

- **Q** (@HQΣr, 2023-06-28): Can anyone create storage rent collection transactions, or only miners?
  **A** (@kushti, @Glasgow): Anyone can create transactions spending expired boxes, but only miners choose which transactions to include in blocks. Miners also have a higher transaction size limit (~96 KB) for collecting many boxes at once.

- **Q** (@Wombo22, 2023-06-23): What is the significance of the Palmyra and Chainlink partnership?
  **A** (@Dan Friedman): Palmyra is using Chainlink's proof-of-reserve functions via a proxy in Winter Protocol to issue trustless on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralised trade financing. IoT devices (cameras, weight sensors, motion sensors) provide continuous proof of existence for physical commodities in bonded warehouses. This is not a zero-sum arrangement with Ergo Oracle Pools — the plan is to also build out native Ergo oracles using capital from the Palmyra token sale, as Ergo-native oracles are more secure. Currently only two Oracle Pools are running (one by the Ergo Foundation); v2 is running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Dexy Mainnet & Oracle Pool v2 Audit**: All three contracts (Dexy, Oracle Pool v2, GORT buyback) require audit completion before mainnet. No firm audit timeline was established; "late July" was aspirational.

- **Block Speed / 6.0 Testnet**: kushti proposed a community-led parameter-tweaking testnet but no volunteer sign-up list or formal group was confirmed. Contracts tied to block height (rather than timestamps) represent a non-trivial migration dependency.

- **NIPoPoWs Light Wallet Support**: Node bootstrapping via NIPoPoWs is shipping in v5.0.13, but full light wallet support using NIPoPoWs requires "significant additional work" — no roadmap or timeline.

- **Spectrum Sidechain Whitepaper**: Referenced multiple times as "forthcoming" or "soon" but not published during June.

- **Ledger Official App Release**: Ergo Ledger app functional in developer mode for 1+ year but official audit/release depends on Ledger prioritisation. No timeline.

- **US Exchange Access**: KuCoin KYC exclusion of US users from July 15 left the US on-ramp question unresolved pending Rosen Bridge mainnet launch.

- **Mixer Adoption & Nautilus Integration**: Flagged as the key lever for meaningful privacy on Ergo, but no commitment or timeline for Nautilus integration.

- **SigmaUSD Future vs. Dexy**: Concern that Dexy launch could draw SigmaRSV liquidity providers away, degrading SigmaUSD minting capacity. "SigGold" protocol improvements mentioned as conceptual mitigation but not designed.

- **CoinGecko/CMC API Listing for Spectrum**: CoinGecko unresponsive; CMC requested additional API work (completed) but still waiting. Native token price tracking remains unresolved.

- **HODLcoin Endgame Dynamics**: Whether last-holder losses are unavoidable without sufficient ERG price appreciation, and whether Spectrum LP arbitrage fully addresses the zero-sum concern, was unresolved.

- **Storage Rent Retroactivity Concern**: @dayumbbbb flagged that storage rent may be retroactively applied in a way that wasn't clearly communicated. Not addressed.

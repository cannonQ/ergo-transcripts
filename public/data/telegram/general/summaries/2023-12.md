# Ergo General Chat — December 2023 Monthly Summary

## Executive Summary

December 2023 was defined by the long-anticipated launch of Rosen Bridge (live December 30), marking Ergo's first production cross-chain bridge connecting the Ergo and Cardano ecosystems. Alongside this milestone, sub-blocks research and protocol performance improvements were actively discussed, and multiple ecosystem projects (SigmaO v2, ChainCash, Dexy Gold) progressed toward testnet. The month also saw significant community stress-testing around the Aneta BTC controversy and governance questions around Rosen's guard set expansion.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Rosen Bridge Launch and Watcher Infrastructure
Rosen Bridge moved from pre-launch preparation in W49 (35 Ergo watchers, 18 Cardano watchers) through active testing in W51 (live cross-chain transactions: 100 ERG → ADA, 1,899 ERG → Cardano, 4,000 rsADA → Ergo) to full UI launch on December 30. Initial token support: ERG, SigmaUSD, SigmaRSV, RSN, and ADA, with more tokens from both sides planned post-launch. The backend launched cleanly but immediately faced a throughput bottleneck: 77 watcher slots were filled on both sides, but many watchers held only 1–7 permits (each costing 3,000 RSN), which prevented the 60%+1 majority needed to confirm transactions. Watcher service v1.0.9 was released to fix commitment ordering and add a job for older unprocessed events. Armeanio announced that 100% of bridge transaction proceeds will be locked as liquidity on Ergo and Cardano DEXes, with the same mechanism planned for the ETH bridge. Governance concerns around the guard set were addressed publicly, with Armeanio committing to expanding the guard set transparently and considering recusal from Rosen fund management to avoid conflicts of interest.

### Sub-blocks and Protocol Performance Research
kushti confirmed that sub-blocks (microblocks) R&D is ongoing, with an EIP under consideration covering on-chain commitment requirements and velvet-fork (non-invasive) deployment. Glasgow noted sub-blocks were "being implemented now" with at least a two-week horizon from early December. The broader performance discussion touched on faster block confirmation (sub-blocks, microblocks, sidechains), on-chain parameter tweaking (difficulty, block time), and whether timestamp-tied vs. block-height-tied contracts would be affected by increased block speed. A testnet infrastructure exists for community-driven parameter experimentation; kushti issued an open call for a community lead to coordinate the testnet group. No implementation path was formally chosen by month end.

### Dexy Protocol, Dexy Gold, and ChainCash
kushti deployed new Dexy contracts with an updated fee structure matching Spectrum DEX and confirmed Dexy Gold (a stablecoin variant) was expected on testnet soon with GORT reward distribution. ChainCash (peer-to-peer monetary system) saw first note transactions completed on a development server; kushti planned contract finalisation and CCIP #0 completion. Both projects represent the next wave of Ergo-native DeFi primitives after Rosen Bridge stabilises.

### SigmaO v2 Contract Upgrade
haileypdll announced SigmaO v2 under testing with significant improvements: options can now be delivered to arbitrary contracts; exercised option outputs and payments can route to smart contracts; option duration capped at three years; a security defect allowing malicious issuers to create fake tokens was fixed. Users were advised to close long-running options before cutover to avoid disruption. Deployment was pending at month end.

### Aneta BTC Controversy and Ecosystem Governance
Aneta BTC announced withdrawal from the Ergo ecosystem after raising approximately 160,000 ERG (60,000 from community subpools, 100,000 from angel investors), delivering minimal code (~500 lines on GitHub) and abandoning a mobile wallet. Three options for remaining ERG were proposed; Option 2 (MEXC listing fund) appeared to be the community favourite, though many members remained sceptical given MEXC's reputation. The episode prompted broader discussion about governance accountability, fund transparency, and whether future ecosystem grants require stricter milestones.

### Rosen Bridge / SigmaFi / rsADA Composability
Community members highlighted emerging DeFi composability: rsADA (Cardano-wrapped ADA via Rosen Bridge) was integrated into Babel boxes in Nautilus, enabling strategies such as borrowing rsADA against ERG collateral on SigmaFi. SigmaFi's peer-to-peer 1:1 lending model (borrowing ERG against SigmaUSD/SigmaRSV collateral at 135–170% collateralisation) also saw active bond issuance, with some users taking 24-month terms at ~138% collateralisation, suggesting bearish ERG/USD sentiment through 2025.

### Ledger Connect-Kit Security Incident
A compromised NPM package in Ledger's connect-kit library allowed attackers to drain wallets from dApps using the library (patched in v1.1.8). Ergo was confirmed unaffected, as it uses only the `@ledgerhq/hw-transport@6.27.7` library. The incident reinforced the value of Ergo's dependency hygiene and Fleet SDK's clean provenance.

### ErgoSanta Community Event
ErgoSanta key minting launched December 20 with 0.2 ERG minimum entry and 20 keys per wallet per day. qx() donated 1.6M COMET tokens to the grand prize pool; the 10M COMET grand prize drew ~2,530 key submissions. Chest opening on December 26 revealed mempool congestion from zombie transactions blocking reward distribution — a useful stress test for Ergo's fee market and spam resilience. Revenue from the event was directed to ErgoCube and Ergo Explorer.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2023-12-21): Does Ergo's hashrate need to match Bitcoin's for equivalent security? How do you compare PoW chains?
  **A** (@digdug): Hashrate is algorithm-specific; direct cross-chain comparison is not valid. As Ergo hashrate grows, difficulty increases, making majority attacks proportionally harder. Ergo scientists could develop standardised comparison metrics for PoW chains and UTXO models. See the Ergo Manifesto for core security values.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2023-12-25): Where should I start learning ErgoScript from scratch?
  **A** (@qx()): Any programming foundation helps. For smart contracts, start with https://docs.ergoplatform.com/dev/scs/ergoscript/ and https://github.com/ergoplatform/ergoscript-by-example. For dApp development without writing contracts, Java, C#, and Python frameworks are available — see ErgPy (https://github.com/mgpai22/ergopy) and FleetSDK for C# (https://docs.ergoplatform.com/dev/stack/fleetsharp/).

- **Q** (@Scoobs, 2023-12-25): Can I use the same seed phrase across multiple wallets (Nautilus, Satergo) simultaneously?
  **A** (@Rj): Yes — the seed phrase represents the wallet, not the app. All devices using the same seed access the same wallet; nothing becomes obsolete. Transactions on one device are reflected on all others.

- **Q** (@Jon theRealJfox, W50): Why can I only send 57 ERG at once from Ergo Mobile?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Fragmented UTXO set from small miner payouts. Set your miner reward payout threshold higher to avoid future fragmentation. Current workaround: consolidate through multiple successive transactions.

- **Q** (@Unknown, W49): Is Ergo vulnerable to a Ledger connect-kit style supply-chain attack?
  **A** (@qx() / Fleet SDK discussion): No — Ergo uses only `@ledgerhq/hw-transport@6.27.7`, not the compromised connect-kit library. Fleet SDK's clean dependency provenance is a deliberate design choice.

- **Q** (@Unknown, W49): GitHub activity on ergo-core appears low — is development slowing?
  **A** (@qx(), @kushti): Active development is distributed across multiple repositories: ChainCash, Dexy, Oracle Pools, sidechains (ErgoHack), and sigma-state-interpreter. A substantial PR queue exists; ergo-core is not the sole indicator of protocol activity.

- **Q** (@Bye H8er, 2023-12-28): How does SigmaFi lending work? Can LP tokens be used as collateral?
  **A** (@qx()): No — tokens in LP pools cannot be used as collateral. Borrowers must provide SigmaUSD or SigmaRSV at 135–170% of the borrow amount. Interest rates are peer-negotiated; shorter-term loans may secure better rates depending on the lender.

- **Q** (@Unknown, W49): How does Ergo handle spam/DDoS attacks on the mempool?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo has a fee market — when blocks are busy, higher-fee transactions are prioritised; lower-fee transactions eventually flush out after 10–40 blocks. Node operators can raise minimum propagation fee rates to prevent spam from entering the mempool at all. A fee estimation API exists in the node but is not yet widely used by wallets. Spam protection has been in the reference client since v4.0.44, limiting transactions accepted from a single peer.

- **Q** (@Mar Bondicat, W49): Why does Karlsen have such high hashrate if it uses Blake3 like Ergo's ASIC-resistance claims?
  **A** (@kushti): Blake3 is ASIC-friendly. Karlsen's algorithm selection undermines its ASIC-resistance claims. Unit conversion also differs between algorithms — 1 TH on Karlsen is not equivalent to 1 TH on Ergo (Autolykos).

- **Q** (community, W52): Can miner transaction fees be changed without an EIP?
  **A** (@Pulsarz, @Glasgow): Yes — miners can vote to change transaction fee parameters via node config (simple majority required, no EIP needed). Tooling from ErgoHack exists but community awareness is low. See https://docs.ergoplatform.com/mining/governance/.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Rosen Bridge watcher permit scarcity**: 3,000 RSN per permit creates a throughput bottleneck; community consensus emerging to lower the minimum, but no formal proposal enacted. Should a minimum permit floor (10–20 permits per watcher) be enforced to prevent low-capacity watchers from blocking confirmations?

- **Sub-blocks implementation path**: Multiple approaches discussed (velvet fork, full EIP, parameter testnet) but no chosen path or community lead assigned. Testnet infrastructure ready — needs coordination.

- **Dexy Gold testnet**: Awaiting deployment; GORT reward mechanics and Oracle Pool v2 interaction need public documentation.

- **ChainCash CCIP #0**: Contract finalisation and first public spec pending; unclear timeline for testnet availability.

- **SigmaO v2 deployment date**: Contract improvements complete and tested; no confirmed cutover date announced.

- **Spectrum Finance / Splash cross-chain integration**: Community concern that Cardano and Ergo DEX instances will remain siloed rather than truly integrated; no clarification from Spectrum team.

- **Ergo Ledger hardware wallet**: Remains in developer mode with no production release ETA.

- **Aneta BTC remaining ERG allocation**: Option 2 (MEXC listing) appeared to lead in community sentiment but no governance vote concluded; legitimacy of MEXC as listing target disputed.

- **Guard set expansion roadmap**: Armeanio committed to transparency but no specific timeline or criteria for adding new Rosen guards published.

- **Ergo node fee estimation API**: Exists in the node but untested and unused by wallets — identified as a useful tool for DEX and auction bidding contexts, needs community testing.

- **Mining parameter governance awareness**: Tooling exists for miners to vote on fee parameters without an EIP, but low community awareness. No campaign to coordinate miner signalling planned.

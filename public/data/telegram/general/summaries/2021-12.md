# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: December 2021

---

## Executive Summary

December 2021 was a foundational month for the Ergo ecosystem, dominated by three converging themes: protocol-level governance (emission soft fork EIP-0027), ecosystem infrastructure maturation (wallet releases, bridges, DEX development), and external positioning (Kairon Labs market-making, Ergo Foundation incorporation, exchange listings). Despite bearish price action, developer and community activity accelerated significantly, with multiple new projects launching and long-standing technical initiatives reaching key milestones.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Emission Soft Fork (EIP-0027)
The most sustained community discussion of the month. The proposal extends ERG emission from ~2028 to 2057 by reducing block rewards more gradually, beginning around block 700,000. Multiple variants were debated (X=12, X=15, X=21 step-down schedules), with a community sentiment poll favouring the shorter extension option. Miners require 90% voting threshold to avoid a chain split. By end of December, community and pool sentiment had shifted broadly positive after forum discussion clarified the rationale. kushti confirmed development of the soft fork implementation was complete; vote timing remained TBD entering January. The first actual miner reward reduction (67.5 → 66 ERG/block) occurred at block 655,200 on January 2, 2022 — the first reduction to affect active miners directly (prior reductions came from foundation allocation).

### Wallet Infrastructure
Four parallel wallet tracks progressed simultaneously. MrStahlfelge released the iOS Ergo Wallet v1.0 via TestFlight (December 22); full App Store release blocked pending Ergo Foundation organisational Apple account. The Android wallet was fully operational. Satergo (desktop, embedded full node) released v0.0.2 on December 31. Nautilus was discussed as a potential "Ergo MetaMask" with built-in mixing. Ledger integration remained the most-requested feature: front-end binding complete, awaiting Ledger team approval, with no confirmed ETA. MrStahlfelge argued Android cold-wallet workflows (offline QR signing) offer equivalent security at lower cost. Yoroi Nightly remained problematic — slowness, transaction display bugs, and signing UI truncation (workaround: zoom out with Win+−) were persistent complaints throughout the month.

### Rosen Bridge & Susy Integration
The Solana↔Ergo bridge (Susy) was the primary near-term bridge milestone. By end of December, bridge code was complete and tested in localnet; Ergo-side oracle integration was nearly finished, with mainnet deployment expected within 7–10 days of January 2 when the Susy team returned. USDT was confirmed as the first supported asset, with other native Solana tokens to follow once wrapped on Ergo. The broader Rosen Bridge architecture (multi-chain) continued development alongside this. ErgoMixer was positioned as a key value-add for bridge users — the first non-custodial, non-interactive token mixer capable of handling any bridged native token.

### Spectrum DEX (formerly ErgoDex)
Spectrum DEX remained live on Ergo mainchain throughout the month. An extended community debate arose over the "ErgoDex" naming — kushti expressed preference against dApps using the "Ergo" prefix to avoid brand confusion; the community was divided on SEO value vs. clarity. The Cardano deployment was delayed to mid-January 2022, with the JavaScript SDK and backend consuming December development time. The dApp connector was deprecated in favour of Yoroi Nightly's integrated connector. Order book vs. AMM design tradeoffs were discussed extensively: order books provide full slippage control and depth transparency; AMMs introduce impermanent loss but simpler UX. Partial fill support and multiple upgrade keys (vs. single centralised upgrade key) were noted as key design features of the order book implementation.

### Kairon Labs, Exchange Listings & Ergo Foundation Incorporation
The Ergo Foundation engaged Kairon Labs as market maker, announced via Bloomberg and Yahoo Finance. Kairon performs liquidity arbitrage across exchanges (Gate, KuCoin, etc.) and provides strategic guidance on exchange readiness. The stated goal was building the liquidity/volume foundation required for a Tier-1 (Binance) listing. Dan Friedman explicitly clarified that listing on Binance "today" would be problematic; Kairon Labs prepares the infrastructure first. Ergo Foundation incorporation in Singapore was submitted to ACRA in early December; approval remained under review at month end. Active discussions with Indian exchanges (CoinSwitch Kuber, WazirX, CoinCDX) were confirmed; NDA prevented date disclosure.

### Palmyra / ZenGate & Chainlink Partnership
Dan Friedman (Palmyra/ZenGate) outlined a decentralised trade financing use case: on-chain warehouse receipts issued via Chainlink oracles coupled with IoT devices (weight sensors, motion sensors, cameras) to provide continuous proof of reserve for commodities deposited in bonded warehouses. This allows risk capital to be borrowed against receipts. Chainlink was chosen for network size and support breadth; the plan explicitly includes building out native Ergo Oracle Pools as a parallel oracle infrastructure, potentially funded from the Palmyra token sale. Armeanio noted the importance of avoiding tribalism — companies using only part of the Ergo ecosystem are still a net benefit. Oracle Pool v1 (with EF members) and Oracle Pool v2 (running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards) were noted as operational.

### Scaling, Throughput & Layer 2 Research
kushti confirmed that the computational cost limit per block had increased from 1M to ~7M since mainnet launch, and block size from 512kb to >1mb. A 30–50x TPS improvement was projected via non-sharding solutions: lighter clients, Bitcoin NG-style sub-block confirmation protocols, and stateless clients. Sharding via soft fork was described as theoretically possible but security research not finalised. Plasma rollups were identified as the preferred L2 research direction over sharding. The architectural framing settled on three layers: L0 (peer-to-peer network optimisation), L1 (extension framework: sharding, Hydra, macroblock protocols), L2 (off-chain settlement: Lightning Network, generic sidechains). Community proposals for microblocks and sidechains were noted as having draft implementations in open PRs awaiting revival and community leadership.

### SigmaUSD / Dexy Ecosystem
SigmaRSV locked at the 400% reserve ratio floor throughout the month. The 2% internal fee on minting/redeeming was discussed as potentially too high at low TVL volumes — at ~1M TVL, price dumps can severely drain RSV holders' returns. Richi proposed lowering minting fees to encourage TVL growth while raising redemption fees, but no governance vote was scheduled. The protocol's maintenance structure (7 key holders given keys for decentralisation, but lacking active incentives) was flagged as a governance gap. Dexy Protocol was expected to provide an additional stablecoin option; lending/borrowing infrastructure identified as key to expanding SigmaUSD utility.

### ErgoPad & Ecosystem Launchpad Activity
ErgoPad launched as Ergo's first IDO launchpad. The seed sale sold out in minutes; the next phase was announced for December 20. Multiple ecosystem projects were in development or announced: Gluon Gold, MewFinance, Machina Finance, Dexy Gold, Sigma Valley (metaverse NFT land DAO). ErgoLearn launched its flagship ErgoScript layman's class with 5 approved students; a January 6 cohort was opened. The ErgoHack III (Privacy & Security focus) was announced with applications open January 4–31, 2022.

### Zero-Knowledge Proofs & ErgoScript
Bulletproofs integration into ErgoScript was a recurring technical topic. Bulletproofs are a trustless alternative to SNARKs (no trusted setup) for zero-knowledge proofs — used in Monero for range proofs, and extensible to generic statements. Integration would allow ErgoScript to verify bulletproof proofs on-chain, enabling hybrid contracts where sensitive data is proven without disclosure. Applications discussed included verifiable credentials, non-interactive mixing of native tokens, and Groth one-out-of-many proofs (as used in early Zcoin). This was framed as opening a "whole new world of possibilities" combining on-chain contracts with zero-knowledge statements.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Hasnain, 2021-12-18): What does "computational cost limit per block" mean in simple terms?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Similar to gas limit per block in Ethereum — allows more transactions to be loaded into each block. kushti added: the limit has grown from 1M to ~7M since mainnet launch and is not a fixed formula — it scales as hardware and bandwidth improve.

- **Q** (@A, 2021-12-08): What does the v5.0 upgrade mean in non-technical terms?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Fee calculation changes from Ahead-of-Time (AOT) costing to Just-In-Time (JITC). Should provide a 5–6x processing boost and ease node syncing. Work has been ongoing since 2020.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-12-18): Is there a deterministic formula for transaction computational cost?
  **A** (@kushti): No concrete formula — depends on hardware miners have, network size, and other conditions. Unlike Bitcoin's strict byte limit, Ergo uses a flexible approach that scales over time.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-12-17): Can Ergo be affected by the same DDoS/spam attack that hit other chains?
  **A** (@kushti): Reference client v4.0.44+ includes spam protection (won't download excessive transactions from a single peer). Mempool filters allow node operators to raise minimum propagation fee rates, preventing spammers from even reaching the mempool. Fee market mechanisms — where miners prioritise higher-fee transactions — naturally flush out low-fee spam after 10–40 blocks. A fee estimation API exists in the node but is not yet widely used by applications.

- **Q** (@Ilya, 2021-12-20): Is there a way to have metadata transactions on Ergo, similar to Cardano?
  **A** (@kushti): Data storage is done at the UTXO (box) level, allowing individual ownership control. Up to 4kb of typed data can be stored in a box across its registers. Transactions can aggregate inputs from different owners.

- **Q** (@Dani, 2021-12-23): How does "chaining" of contracts work as mentioned in the Whitepaper?
  **A** (@Armeanio): Chaining is batching transactions into a single block, enabling complex contract execution sequences. ErgoDex's transaction bot already implements this pattern. This was Ergo's solution to the concurrency problem before the Cardano concurrency debate made it prominent.

- **Q** (@Ilya, 2021-12-05): Is Nimiq's light client implementation using NIPoPoWs like Ergo?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Nimiq uses macro/micro blocks + ZK-SNARKs for light clients, not NIPoPoWs. NIPoPoWs are PoW-specific; Cardano is PoS and cannot use them.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-12-18): How is ErgoMixer unique compared to Ethereum mixers?
  **A** (@Glasgow): ErgoMixer was the first non-custodial, non-interactive mixer. Ethereum's zk-SNARK-based mixers (e.g., Tornado Cash) require trusted setup ceremonies, are bug-prone, and create incentives for bad actors. ErgoMixer will support any bridged native token once cross-chain infrastructure is live.

- **Q** (@Grandify, 2021-12-07): Can Oracle Pools monetise varied user data points (shopping, location, social)?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Oracle Pool design requires each oracle to broadcast identical data — trust enforcement requires homogeneity. Broadcasting varied data is more appropriate for big data infrastructure than oracle price feeds.

- **Q** (@Adano, 2021-12-29): How many transactions per second can the Ergo chain process?
  **A** (@Armeanio): TPS is a vanity metric. eUTXO supports multiple outputs per transaction; transaction chaining adds complexity. Ergo is designed to support multiple L2 solutions. L1 is the settlement layer; maximum throughput is achieved across the multi-layer stack.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-12-28): What are the advantages of UTXO over account-based models?
  **A** (@Glasgow): UTXO enables heavy lifting off-chain validated on-chain (eliminating unpredictable fees). Benefits: privacy (one-time objects), scalability (parallel transaction processing), interoperability (off-chain/sidechain protocols), and transaction cost predictability (no gas auctions). Flash loans are impossible on the UTXO model, removing an entire class of Ethereum exploits.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2021-12-14): Can I be liquidated if SigmaRSV price falls?
  **A** (@Community): No liquidation mechanism exists. Worst case is inability to redeem if the reserve ratio depletes below 400%; SigmaRSV holders cannot be force-liquidated.

- **Q** (@T C, 2021-12-18): Should I buy SigmaRSV on Spectrum DEX — is that the same as minting?
  **A** (@Community): No. SigmaRSV collects fees from SigmaUSD minting/withdrawal. At low TVL (~1M), price dumps can severely drain RSV holders' fee returns. Secondary market purchase does not generate the same protocol fee revenue as minting directly.

- **Q** (@Sheen, 2021-12-21): What is NIPoPoWs short for?
  **A** (@Community): Non-Interactive Proofs of Proof-of-Work.

- **Q** (@PalaZ, 2021-12-23): Can ERG be burned?
  **A** (@Armeanio): A burn mechanism could be set up intentionally, but ERG is recycled via storage rent rather than permanently removed from circulation — this is by design.

- **Q** (@Ilya, 2021-12-28): I issued a token with quantity 100,000 and 1 decimal via ErgoUtils, but Yoroi shows 10,000. Bug or feature?
  **A** (@Community): Yoroi is correct. The "quantity" field in ErgoUtils represents the smallest unit. For 100,000 display tokens with 1 decimal, the input quantity must be 1,000,000 (100,000 × 10). The field labelling in ErgoUtils is misleading.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

1. **Emission soft fork miner vote mechanics**: How exactly do mining pools signal votes? What is the on-chain signalling mechanism? What happens if threshold is not reached? No formal procedure documented.

2. **Ledger integration ETA**: Front-end binding complete; awaiting Ledger team approval. No timeline. Community frustration high — most-requested missing feature.

3. **Susy bridge mainnet deployment**: Code and localnet testing complete as of January 2. Oracle integration and guardian coordination (3 Ergo-side, 3 Susy-side) still pending. Expected "7–10 days" but non-technical delays possible.

4. **SigmaUSD fee restructuring and protocol governance**: Who holds the 7 keys? What is their incentive to act? Richi's fee restructuring proposal (lower minting, higher redemption fees) has no formal governance path or vote timeline.

5. **Storage rent revenue sufficiency post-emission**: Glasgow noted ~40x UTXO set growth may be required by ~2057 to match current block reward value. Economic modelling and feasibility unresolved.

6. **V5.0 (JITC costing) deployment timeline**: Described as in development since 2020; no block height target or release date announced.

7. **Sub-block confirmation protocol**: Draft implementations exist in open PRs; community leadership needed to run testnets, tweak parameters, and produce scientific observations. kushti invited interested parties to DM.

8. **ErgoMixer UX improvements**: Community consensus on need for improved UI; Nautilus integration as partial solution. No assigned team or timeline.

9. **Yoroi long-term strategy**: Will the Ergo Foundation prioritise fixing Yoroi or building an independent browser wallet? No clear decision communicated.

10. **iOS App Store release**: Blocked on Ergo Foundation organisational Apple account; submission timeline unclear.

11. **Ergo Oracle Pool expansion**: Plan to build out native Ergo Oracle Pools (potentially funded via Palmyra token sale); only two pools currently operational (Oracle Pool v1 with EF members; Oracle Pool v2 for Dexy Gold). No timeline for expansion.

12. **L2 plasma/rollup research**: Identified as preferred L2 path over sharding; tutorials expected but no spec or prototype timeline given.

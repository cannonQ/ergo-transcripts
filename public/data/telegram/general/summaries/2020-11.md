# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: 2020-11

## Executive Summary

November 2020 was a month of significant infrastructure development for Ergo, marked by node releases, wallet migrations, and foundational dApp architecture advances. The dominant themes were expanding ecosystem tooling (node upgrades, ErgoMixer, headless dApps, Auction House), urgent user migration away from the shutting-down Magnum Wallet, and forward-looking technical discussions around Autolykos v2, stablecoin design, privacy schemes, and sidechain research.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Node Releases & P2P Improvements
Node 3.3.6 was released early in the month with major P2P layer improvements following extensive disconnection testing during bootstrapping and synced states. Node 3.3.7 entered development shortly after, adding distributed signature support. Upcoming node panel versions were confirmed to include quality-of-life improvements: "Send max ERG," multi-asset transfers, copy address, custom fee, improved number formatting, and a transaction status table.

### Autolykos v2 Hard Fork
A memory-hard PoW algorithm upgrade (Autolykos v2) was announced and detailed on the Ergo Forum. The primary motivation was restoring memory hardness to the PoW algorithm to improve ASIC resistance and broaden GPU/CPU mining participation. Hashrate was expected to drop by a factor of ~8x post-fork as the algorithm changed, with difficulty adjusting proportionally. The hard fork was explicitly framed as a decentralization and accessibility improvement, with the expectation that mining pools would become accessible to the general public. kushti also noted that pool-resistance would be switched off as part of the transition, a point that prompted discussion about miner anonymity and decentralization metrics.

### Magnum Wallet Sunset & Migration
Magnum Wallet announced closure on November 30, 2020, with only ~20 days' notice. Users were urged to migrate to Yoroi (browser extension) or ErgoMixer 3.1.0. A key complication: Magnum used derivation path `0'`, differing from Yoroi's scheme, meaning users could not simply import mnemonics — they needed to send ERG to a fresh Yoroi address instead. ErgoMixer 3.1.0 was timed to support Magnum addresses as covert addresses. The closure created a time-pressured migration wave with several users reporting transaction failures during bulk withdrawal.

### ErgoMixer & Privacy
ErgoMixer v3.1.0 was officially released, featuring the ability to import existing addresses (including from Magnum), convert them to covert addresses, performance refactoring, default covert address ring increased to 250 ERG, and a wallet persistence redesign. kushti clarified that Ergo is not a privacy coin by default, but offers optional non-interactive mixing using Sigma protocols. Research into more powerful privacy schemes (including Lelantus evaluation) was slated for 2021 without requiring forks.

### Stablecoin Development
A partnership with Emurgo and IOG (Input Output Global) was announced to build a stablecoin on Ergo, with full UI integration planned for Yoroi. The design uses only ERG and the stablecoin — no new governance token — with collateral enabling long/short positions on ERG. Supplying collateral keeps users long ERG while earning liquidity pool fees; minting the stablecoin is effectively shorting ERG. Exact specifications were not released and were deferred to late 2020/early 2021. Multiple requests for a research paper went unanswered during the month.

### Headless dApps Architecture
A blog post was published introducing headless dApps: an architectural approach decoupling backend dApp logic from frontends, enabling multiple independent frontends to profit from the same underlying protocol. This was framed as a key differentiator for Ergo's dApp ecosystem.

### Auction House, Trade House & Order Book DEXes
The Auction House was updated to support any wallet via the assembler service v2. qx() and others articulated the advantages of order book DEX designs (as used in Trade House and Spectrum DEX) over AMMs: full transparency of market depth, complete slippage control, peer-to-peer execution, partial order fills, and no impermanent loss. The contrast with AMM-based designs was a recurring discussion thread throughout the month.

### Oracle Pools, Cardano Interoperability & Gravity
An official blog post announced first steps toward interoperability with Cardano oracles, attracting significant community attention. Oracle Pools were contrasted with API3's first-party oracle model: Ergo's approach is permissionless and trustless by design. Gravity protocol integration was discussed for USDN/USDT gateways, with Waves, Binance Chain, and Tron identified as the most active partners. A Gravity gateway forum post was published. Community concern was raised about whether Cardano's API3 partnership would diminish Oracle Pool relevance; no clear strategic response was provided.

### Light Nodes & Decentralization
Full-node light clients were confirmed running successfully on Raspberry Pi and Rock Pi single-board computers, validating the vision of decentralized, low-cost node infrastructure. kushti also noted work toward smart syncing for even lighter full-node operation. Separately, concerns about mining centralization led to discussion of p2p network probing to assess true miner count; a Rust-based handshaking tool was noted as in development for nonce distribution analysis.

### NIPoPoWs & Sidechain Research
Research into improved sidechain constructions was noted, including BIP 301-style merge-mined approaches with Gravity working on gateways. Litecoin and Nimiq were mentioned as exploring NIPoPoWs. The potential for NIPoPoWs to significantly change cross-chain bridging capabilities was noted but not detailed.

### Exchange Listings Strategy
Community and core team discussed exchange priorities throughout the month. Huobi and KuCoin were identified as primary targets. Concerns were raised about Binance leverage products and FTX regulatory issues. The Coinbase listing form was highlighted as fee-free. Notably, community consensus leaned toward "too early" for a Binance-type listing, citing the Akro post-listing crash as a cautionary example. Huobi listing status remained unresolved. CoinGecko was consistently recommended over CoinMarketCap due to price inflation from wash trading on Citex (which had closed withdrawals/deposits).

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Reuben, 2020-11-02): What proof-of-work changes is Ergo considering?
  **A** (@kushti): Close memory optimizations in Autolykos and switch pool-resistance off; then a k-sum based Equihash variant requiring ~6 GB RAM for maximum efficiency.

- **Q** (@Tri Origin, 2020-11-17): If I hold ERG in a wallet for 4 years without spending, do I lose it due to storage rent?
  **A** (@kushti): No. Miners charge a small fee (~0.13 ERG per UTXO per 4-year period under current parameters). You retain your ERG; the fee is simply deducted from the UTXO.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-11-18): What is the expected hashrate impact of the Autolykos v2 hard fork?
  **A** (@Unknown/BigEvilSoloMinerToday): V1 and V2 hashrates are approximately equal in raw terms, but expect an effective hashrate drop of ~8x post-fork due to the algorithm change. Difficulty should be reduced proportionally.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-11-10): Will Ergo's stablecoin require a governance token like MKR?
  **A** (@mensrea): The design uses only ERG and the stablecoin; no new governance token is introduced.

- **Q** (@mensrea, 2020-11-10): Can tokens be used to pay transaction fees like ERG?
  **A** (@kushti): Theoretically yes, via fee emission boxes or by buying ERG from liquidity pools in the same transaction. Unlikely miners will accept arbitrary tokens in practice (90%+ have no market). The fee script is not fixed in the protocol.

- **Q** (@Spire Blockchain, 2020-11-23): How current is Oracle Pool data and what is the minimum refresh rate?
  **A** (@kushti): Datapoints refresh every 30 blocks with a ~2-minute average block time, enabling ~60-minute refresh intervals in current configurations.

- **Q** (@UpwindStrategy, 2020-11-27): What advantage does Turing-complete ErgoScript have over Bitcoin post-Taproot?
  **A** (@kushti): Contractual capabilities in Ergo are modest compared to Ethereum; Turing completeness was important in 2018 to demonstrate the expressiveness of the language. The key differentiator is eUTXO composability and Sigma protocols, not Turing completeness per se.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-11-27): Is Ergo a privacy coin?
  **A** (@kushti): No, but non-interactive mixing is available as a dApp (ErgoMixer) using Sigma protocols. More powerful privacy schemes are planned for 2021 without requiring forks.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-11-25): How can we verify Ergo's decentralization level given hidden miner identities?
  **A** (@kushti): Decentralization metrics can be obtained via p2p network probing and nonce distribution analysis. A handshaking tool in Rust is being developed; nonce analysis will be added. Existing analysis is published at ergoforum.org/t/ergo-nonce-distribution/243.

- **Q** (@Patryk, 2020-11-27): Restored Magnum wallet mnemonic in Yoroi but shows zero balance — what went wrong?
  **A** (@Dmitry Usov / @kushti): Derivation paths differ between Magnum (path `0'`) and Yoroi. Create a new Yoroi wallet and send ERG from Magnum to the new Yoroi address instead of importing the mnemonic.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-11-10): Why not use wrapped ERG instead of native tokens?
  **A** (@Jason Davies): Wrapping simplifies contracts that only handle arbitrary user-issued tokens. It is also useful to deploy a trustless 1:1 WERG ↔ ERG exchange contract for certain design patterns.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-11-18): Why is Ergo "no premine" if only technical people can mine it? Isn't this centralization?
  **A** (@kushti / community): The Autolykos v2 hard fork is specifically intended to address this by expanding competitive GPU mining to the general public. Additionally, high daily miner selloff (~50K ERG/day) improves distribution by preventing hoarding.

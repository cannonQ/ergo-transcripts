# Ergo General — Monthly Knowledge Base Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General | **Month:** 2022-11

---

## Executive Summary

November 2022 was one of Ergo's most technically productive months, marked by the soft launch of Rosen Bridge on Ergo and Cardano mainnets, the activation of Babel Fees enabling transaction fee payment in alternative tokens, and the release of Node 5.0 with JIT costing for improved throughput. These launches occurred against the backdrop of the FTX collapse, which reinforced community emphasis on self-custody and trustless infrastructure as core Ergo values.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Rosen Bridge Launch
The Rosen Bridge moved from development to soft launch across November. By W44, @HQΣr reported it was "very close to be live." The bridge deployed on both Ergo and Cardano mainnets in W45–W46 in private test mode, with the first cross-chain Ergo→Cardano transfer processed using test tokens. The framework uses multi-signature watchers monitoring both chains, creating commitments and payment transactions. A guard set of 30 was under testing by W47, with plans to include actors from outside the Ergo ecosystem for broader distribution. Public release was contingent on at least two weeks of private testing. The soft-launch UI was available at `soft.rosen.tech` but users were explicitly warned not to use real funds.

### Babel Fees
Babel Fees went live on mainnet in W45, allowing users to pay transaction fees using any token (e.g., SigmaUSD, or even the community "LOVE" token) instead of ERG, via smart contract pools. The first mainnet transaction paying fees in SigmaUSD was confirmed by @Capt. Nemo. TokenJay provided the liquidity provision UI. By W46, @MrStahlfelge was planning automatic Babel fee detection in the wallet app for users with insufficient ERG. The feature was celebrated as a breakthrough for onboarding and usability.

### Node 5.0 and JIT Costing
Node 5.0.2 was released in W44 and 5.0.3 in W46, with full soft-fork activation approximately 10 days out as of W47. The headline feature is hybrid costing: Ahead-Of-Time (AOT) estimation rejects over-expensive scripts before execution, while Just-In-Time (JIT) costing calculates costs during actual execution. This combination increases transactions per block, improves cost estimates, and raises overall blockchain throughput without changing block rewards. EIP-42 was merged as the official standard for JIT costing. EIP-27 (extended tail emission, pushing miner incentive timeline from ~8 to ~40 years) was also confirmed complete via a 90%+ supermajority mining vote.

### Stablecoins: SigmaUSD and Dexy
Community discussion clarified the distinction between SigmaUSD (collateralization-based) and Dexy (seigniorage-based). Dexy Gold was confirmed as the first Dexy reference implementation, with Oracle Pool v2 running to support it with GORT rewards. @Glasgow noted the goal is multiple coexisting stablecoins on Ergo. The Dexy whitepaper was published; kushti noted significant design differences from Fei Protocol despite surface-level comparisons. Community expects both to coexist, serving different use cases.

### Palmyra / Chainlink Partnership
@Dan Friedman outlined the Palmyra (formerly ZenGate) partnership with Chainlink during the W44 AMA. The use case is on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralized trade financing: producers deposit commodities into bonded warehouses, receive receipts issued on-chain via Chainlink oracles coupled with IoT devices (cameras, weight/motion sensors). Dan clarified Chainlink was chosen for network size and tooling, not exclusivity — Ergo oracles (Oracle Pool v1 with EF + community members, Oracle Pool v2) are planned to be expanded using token sale capital. Dan and @qx() both cautioned against tribalistic reactions to multi-chain tooling choices, noting Palmyra currently runs exclusively on Ergo.

### Speed Improvements and Layer 2 Discussions
kushti flagged multiple competing proposals for network throughput improvements — sub-blocks, microblocks, and sidechains — noting these drafts should be revived post-Ergoversary. The key insight shared: Ergo now has a concrete, representative set of on-chain contracts (Rosen Bridge, Oracle Pools, Spectrum DEX AMM pools, Trade House orders, lending protocols, SigmaUSD) that provide a realistic benchmark for optimization. kushti noted this is unusual and valuable — most blockchains propose scaling without real transaction profiles to optimize against. A testnet channel exists for parameter tweaking (block time, difficulty); kushti invited interested community members to DM for access. Separately, sub-block confirmation protocol literature ("Prism" paper) was discussed as relevant to near-instant point-of-sale confirmation.

### FTX Collapse and CEX Risk
The FTX implosion dominated broader crypto discourse throughout November and shaped Ergo community messaging. Community emphasis reinforced: do not hold funds on centralized exchanges. Self-custody wallets (Nautilus, Satergo, Terminus/Minotaur) were promoted. Rosen Bridge was positioned as a trustless cross-chain alternative to centralized exchange wraps. Phone-based cold wallet setups (factory-reset device + wallet app in airplane mode, QR code signing) were documented as accessible hardware wallet alternatives while Ledger integration remained pending.

### Sigmanauts Governance Launch
The Sigmanauts program formalized its governance structure in November. The first governance committee meeting was held 2022-11-06, covering: voting app demo, mission statement drafting, subcommittee structure (Governance meets Sundays 2pm UTC), treasury management, and formal roles documentation. A main Twitter account was established for transparency. The new weekly show "The Sigma Update" (hosts @qx() and @CW) launched in W47, covering Ergo and Sigmanauts updates for non-technical audiences.

### Mining and Difficulty
Discussion covered GPU optimization (3070 achieving ~170MH/s at 115W), FPGA landscape (~6 software implementations, ~20k FPGAs available but ERG not currently attractive), and the contrast between Ergo's sustainable emission schedule and Kaspa's aggressive front-loading (half mined in one year). The SNISP ErgoHack project introduced a decentralized mining pool paradigm using Succinct Non-Interactive Share Proofs. EIP-38 (mining voting via smart pools) remained unresolved; @qx() continued to argue hard fork is preferable to smart pool bifurcation.

### ErgoHack V Results and Community Projects
ErgoHack V results were announced in W47. New community projects launching this month included: DuckPools (lending protocol with ERG/SigRSV collateral), SigmaBonds (decentralized P2P bond protocol by @K-Singh, ~80% complete, awaiting Node 5.0), ergflip.com (first Ergo gambling dApp, centralized coinflip), Emporas.io (merchandise store accepting ERG/SigmaUSD), and CyberVerse (pixel-based open-world game with IDO on ErgoPad, adjusting tokenomics due to bear market). The Ergo Mining and Minting Mini Summit was scheduled for 2022-11-26.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Rj, 2022-11-26): Can you ELI5 Just-In-Time costing?
  **A** (@NoahErgo): AOT (Ahead-Of-Time) costing estimates script execution costs before running to reject over-expensive scripts. JIT (Just-In-Time) costing calculates costs during actual execution. The hybrid method in Node 5.0 uses both: AOT as a gate, JIT for accuracy. Result: more transactions per block, better cost estimates, improved chain performance overall.

- **Q** (@Şerafettin, 2022-11-05): Why can't individual miners vote Yes/No on protocol changes instead of following their pool's decision?
  **A** (@qx()): EIP-38 (smart pool voting) would split hash between Yes/No sub-pools, reducing each side's chance of finding blocks. A hard fork solution is preferable because it avoids this bifurcation problem. In practice, miners must understand the contracts to participate meaningfully; otherwise pool operators make effective decisions anyway.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2022-11-07): What are bulletproofs?
  **A** (@Flying Pig / community): A zero-knowledge proof technique allowing specific statements (e.g., range proofs) to be proven without revealing the underlying data. Used in Ergo for ZK proofs; functions as an alternative to SNARKs with no trusted setup required, making them trustless by design.

- **Q** (@TMR.ERG, 2022-11-26): What happens if a lending pool like DuckPools reaches 100% utilization — would lender funds be locked?
  **A** (@NoahErgo): This should be directed to @KrasaviceBlasen for a definitive answer on the protocol's mechanism for handling max utilization.

- **Q** (@qx(), 2022-11-26): Can Rosen Bridge be used as a mixer — send ERG→ADA→clean wallets→back to ERG in small amounts?
  **A** (@NoahErgo): Amounts and timestamps would make correlation obvious. Bridge transfers create on-chain receipts; inputs and outputs can be matched. Without massive delays and fragmentation it's not private. Better approach: use a mixer before bridging, then bridge to a chain without a native mixer.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-11-07): How are Babel Fee transactions created, and how does Nautilus identify and use them?
  **A** (@error): Full specification in EIP-0031: `github.com/ergoplatform/eips/blob/master/eip-0031.md`

- **Q** (@SJ, 2022-11-17): Do you have to transfer from a CEX to a hot wallet before sending to a cold wallet?
  **A** (@Kallo Shaman): No. The cold device generates the seed phrase and public address. You can send directly from the CEX to the cold wallet's public address. Only the cold device ever holds the seed; the hot device runs in read-only mode with the public address only.

- **Q** (@SJ, 2022-11-17): Does the phone-based cold wallet concept work for all blockchains?
  **A** (@Kallo Shaman): It works specifically for UTXO-based chains (Bitcoin, Ergo, Cardano). Account-model chains (Ethereum) differ. Ergo's specific advantage: you don't need to download blockchain state to sign offline.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-11-17): How do you consolidate UTXOs in simple terms?
  **A** (@qx()): Deposits to your address create separate boxes. If you have 1 ERG in one box and 2 ERG in another, spending 3 ERG in one transaction can fail or hit input limits. Sending your full balance back to yourself combines all boxes into one. This is UTXO model behaviour — you can't add to a box, you must spend boxes and create new ones. The transaction fee pays miners for this work.

- **Q** (@Abdulkrem, 2022-11-13): If a whale loses keys to 50,000 ERG, who receives the storage rent fees?
  **A** (community): Miners receive storage rent. At current fee rates, it would take approximately 1.6 million years to fully deplete 50,000 ERG through rent alone.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-11-20): Any literature on sub-block confirmation protocols for faster point-of-sale and DEX use?
  **A** (@Hans): "Prism: Deconstructing the Blockchain to Approach Physical Limits" — PoW can theoretically achieve near-instant confirmation and full network bandwidth throughput. Implementing Prism requires time and resources Ergo hasn't had available yet, but it's a known and viable path.

- **Q** (@Mark, 2022-11-26): Do you owe tax if you send ERG between your own wallets when the price has changed, even without converting to fiat?
  **A** (@NoahErgo): Yes, in the US/Canada each wallet-to-wallet transfer triggers a taxable event based on the price difference at the time of transfer. You realize gains at the moment of transfer. Losses can be realized later but deductions are capped per year. If you traded $1,000→$100,000→$0 without off-ramping, you can still owe tax on the realized gains made during that journey.

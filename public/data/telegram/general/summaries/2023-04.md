# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Period:** April 2023 (W14–W17)

---

## Executive Summary

April 2023 was a landmark month for the Ergo ecosystem, dominated by the public beta launch of Rosen Bridge and an explosive surge in DeFi activity centred on SigmaFi's P2P lending protocol and Spectrum Finance's yield farming rollout. Underlying these milestones was sustained technical discussion around protocol-level improvements — storage rent, emission mechanics, block speed optimisation, and zero-knowledge proof integration — reflecting a maturing developer community engaging seriously with both near-term shipping and long-term research.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Rosen Bridge: From Private Alpha to Public Beta

Rosen Bridge progressed from a private Sigmanauts-only test in W14 to a full public beta at `beta.rosen.tech` by W17. The initial beta supported ERG/COMET (Ergo) and ADA/HOSKY (Cardano), with bridge response times of approximately 1–2 hours Ergo → Cardano and faster in the reverse direction. Two sigma-rust issues were identified and patched during W15. The fee structure is dynamic (1% minimum, 2 ERG floor), configurable ahead of the full launch. Architecturally, Rosen Bridge supports direct chain-to-chain transfers (e.g., BTC to ADA) without routing through ERG as an intermediary — all security and verification logic runs on Ergo smart contracts. The RSN token is central to bridge economics: fees flow to the Rosen Fund, RSN emissions reward watchers and guards, and liquidity is added to pools for market depth. Guards are initially selected via bootstrap, with the existing guard set making later selections; watchers must stake RSN to receive permit tokens.

### SigmaFi P2P Lending: Rapid Ecosystem Adoption

SigmaFi emerged as the month's standout DeFi story, with TVL growing from approximately $20k in W15 to over $160k by W17 — a significant portion of that growth occurring within a single 24-hour period driven by 100% APR loan requests. The protocol is fully on-chain, headless, and oracle-free: borrowers post ERG collateral, lenders fund SigmaUSD loans, and settlement is purely time-based (no price-triggered liquidation). Lenders receive either repaid SigmaUSD plus interest or the ERG collateral at maturity. The absence of automated liquidation was flagged repeatedly by community members as a lender risk in volatile conditions; the team acknowledged this but made no protocol changes. Secondary market trading of loan positions as NFTs (effectively synthetic options) was proposed by community members but not yet implemented. The rapid growth exposed a structural constraint: with only ~617k–643k SigmaUSD in circulation and much of it locked on Spectrum, SigmaUSD supply became a bottleneck.

### SigmaUSD Supply Constraints and Protocol Enhancement Proposals

The SigmaUSD supply ceiling became a recurring concern through April, with minting locked and demand from SigmaFi borrowers causing a brief depeg to $1.03 on Spectrum in W17. Several proposals emerged to address this: Armeanio floated a Dual Mint mechanism allowing simultaneous SigmaUSD and SigmaRSV minting when the collateralisation ratio is below 400%, avoiding ratio slippage. Richi outlined a package of enhancements including RSV mint cashback instead of a fee, double burn capability, and zero UI fees for direct SigmaUSD interaction in dApps such as SigmaFi and DuckPools. SigmaGold was proposed as an experimental playground to test mechanics before a potential SigmaUSD v3 upgrade. No formal EIP or implementation timeline was committed during the month.

### Spectrum Finance Yield Farming Launch

Spectrum Finance completed public beta testing of its yield farming product in W16 and launched on Ergo mainnet in W17 (2023-04-28, 09:00 UTC). Early APY was approximately 30% on the ERG/SPF farm. The offchain execution layer uses Rust-based bots for yield farming transactions, distinct from the Scala-based AMM bots. The team also initiated a community raffle on Ergo Raffle to fund an Ergo Explorer UI redesign, targeting a 15k+ ERG fundraising goal. Cardano AMM and Spectrum Network were listed as subsequent milestones.

### Emission Mechanics, Storage Rent, and Fee Market

A FUD video questioning the transparency of Ergo's emission contract prompted a detailed public defence in W14. kushti and others clarified that emission is contract-enforced (not hardcoded), which prevents the overflow vulnerabilities seen in Bitcoin's implementation, and that any monetary policy change requires a >90% hashpower supermajority. Block rewards reduced by 3 ERG to 39 ERG at block height 979,200 in W15, per the scheduled emission curve. Storage rent — the mechanism by which miners can collect small amounts from UTXO boxes inactive for 4+ years — was explained multiple times across the month as a spam-prevention and UTXO-set-management tool; users were advised to periodically consolidate UTXOs to reset the timer. Discussion of whether the 0.001 ERG minimum fee should be dynamic (as in Monero) concluded that a fixed minimum better prevents spam under load, with layer 2 and sidechains serving micro-transaction use cases at extreme ERG price levels.

### Block Speed Optimisation and Network Research

kushti opened a sustained discussion across W16 and W17 on potential Ergo throughput improvements, including sub-blocks, sidechains, and layer 2 solutions. He noted that Ergo is now well-positioned to pursue these because a concrete set of deployed contracts (Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, AMM pools, Trade House, lending contracts, stablecoins) provides a realistic benchmark for optimisation — unlike chains that build scaling infrastructure before having meaningful transaction profiles. Key complications flagged included dApps tied to block height rather than timestamps, and emission contract dependencies on block timing. A community testnet for parameter experimentation (block time, difficulty level) was proposed; kushti invited interested parties to DM him for access, noting that no coding skill is required — scientific observation and coordination are sufficient to kickstart the effort.

### Bulletproofs and Zero-Knowledge Protocols

kushti gave a detailed explanation of bulletproofs as a trustless zero-knowledge technique requiring no trusted setup (unlike SNARKs). Compact Sigma protocol verification is possible within ErgoScript. The practical implication is that a variety of zero-knowledge protocols — including Groth one-out-of-many proofs (as used in early Zcoin) and verifiable credentials — could be combined with Ergo contracts to enable hybrid execution: sensitive data is verified in zero-knowledge on-chain without being disclosed, opening privacy-preserving contract patterns not easily replicated on account-model chains.

### ChainCash Protocol

kushti introduced ChainCash in W16 as a decentralised monetary system enabling creation of IOU notes backed by a basket of digital assets (ERG, SigmaUSD, Dexy Gold, wrapped tokens, etc.). Individual participants evaluate collateral quality and accept or reject tokens accordingly, making the system permissionless at the protocol level. Documentation and an explanatory video were shared; no mainnet deployment timeline was given.

### Ledger Live Integration

The Ergo Ledger app became discoverable in Ledger Live 2.55.0's catalog outside developer mode during W14. The integration allows address derivation using public extended keys while private keys remain on the hardware device. Importing hot-wallet seed phrases into Ledger is not supported. Occasional issues with transactions involving more than 20 tokens in developer mode were noted; no production ETA was given.

### Mining Difficulty Adjustment Debate

Post-merge epoch length changes and their intended permanence were debated across W15–W17. Ravencoin's Dark Gravity Wave (DGW) algorithm was proposed as an alternative to Ergo's current approach for smoother block-time variance, particularly during large miner entry and exit events. The network hashrate during the period was approximately 21–30 TH/s. No formal proposal was submitted; discussion remained at the exploratory stage.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@A P, 2023-04-06): Is the 2% SigmaUSD fee high? Where does it go?
  **A** (@kushti, @Glasgow): The 2% fee goes to the bank reserve (not individuals), as specified in the contract. This protects the bank against price fluctuations. Both directions cost 2%, so a round-trip costs approximately 4%. Spectrum DEX sometimes offers better rates depending on conditions.

- **Q** (@Aberg, 2023-04-09): Why is the minimum fee fixed at 0.001 ERG? At $1,000/ERG that would be $1 per transaction.
  **A** (@qx(), @kushti): At extreme ERG prices, layer 2 and sidechains handle micro-transactions. The protocol measures fees in ERG only (unit-agnostic). Dynamic fees during high load can backfire — a fixed minimum better prevents spam under congestion.

- **Q** (@Fred Clark Jr, 2023-04-06): How long until all ERG is in circulation?
  **A** (@kushti): See section 2.6 of the ErgoScript whitepaper for full emission contract details.

- **Q** (@M, 2023-04-12): Can Rosen Bridge transfer ETH from Ethereum to Polygon in wrapped form, secured by ERG?
  **A** (@dadreboi): Yes. Rosen Bridge will allow direct bridging between any two supported chains without stopping at Ergo. All verification and security logic runs on the Ergo side. The bridge functions even if front-end interfaces go down — contracts are fully on-chain.

- **Q** (@stenolog, 2023-04-15): What is storage rent?
  **A** (@dadreboi): After 4 years of UTXO inactivity, a small amount can be collected by miners to discourage blockchain bloat and keep the active UTXO set small. Prevent this by periodically sending all funds to yourself to reset the timer.

- **Q** (@abebeos, 2023-04-15): Why do I need 0.001 ERG in every UTXO box?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Each UTXO box requires a minimum ERG-per-byte amount (360 nanoERG/byte) to prevent spam and pay for blockchain storage. The exact minimum scales with box size based on its contents.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2023-04-16): How does SigmaFi loan repayment and liquidation work?
  **A** (@dadreboi): Repayment is a single lump sum before the block height deadline — no monthly payments. Interest is charged once. There is no price-based liquidation. If the loan is not repaid by the deadline, the lender liquidates the collateral from the lending dashboard. If the ERG price crashes below the loan value, the lender absorbs the loss on the collateral. The contract is fully on-chain with only time-based triggers.

- **Q** (@Anonrlx, 2023-04-19): What is the real use case for borrowing SigmaUSD if you are already posting the same value in ERG as collateral?
  **A** (@qx()): The primary use case is leveraged ERG exposure. Borrow SigmaUSD, buy more ERG immediately. When ERG appreciates, sell a portion to repay the SigmaUSD and keep the remaining ERG. A non-speculative use case: borrow long-term SigmaUSD for real expenses (e.g., a car purchase) while keeping ERG holdings intact, then repay from income after the term expires.

- **Q** (@gg, 2023-04-21): Are headless P2P loans like SigmaFi not possible on the account model? Is that a technical reality or a myth?
  **A** (@qx()): This is real — kushti has stated it directly. Truly on-chain, oracle-free, headless P2P lending as implemented in SigmaFi is not achievable in the account model. [Reference: https://youtu.be/M1u7EiketqM?t=1696]

- **Q** (@TMR.ΣRG, 2023-04-21): Does SigmaFi have any automatic liquidation if collateral value falls below a threshold?
  **A** (@qx()): No. SigmaFi is headless and relies on zero oracle feeds. Lenders receive either repaid SigmaUSD plus full interest or ERG collateral at the deadline, regardless of ERG price movement at the time.

- **Q** (@Marty ErgoPad, 2023-04-21): Can you recover a mnemonic seed phrase if you have full node access, an API key, and the wallet password?
  **A** (@Aberg): No. The node stores only the private key, not the original seed phrase. You can extract the private key for smart contract work, but the mnemonic cannot be reconstructed.

- **Q** (@A, 2023-04-29): On SigmaFi, can you repay a loan early or in monthly instalments?
  **A** (@HQΣr): You can repay at any time, but only in full. After the term ends, the lender must liquidate the bond from their dashboard to release the collateral. If you miss the block height deadline, repayment is no longer possible.

- **Q** (@gg, 2023-04-29): Rosen Bridge fee structure — why is it 2 ERG on a 3 ERG minimum transfer?
  **A** (@qx()): The fee is 1% dynamic (minimum 2 ERG) plus a network fee. On a 3 ERG transfer, 2 ERG covers fees and you receive the equivalent in ADA on Cardano. The economics improve significantly at higher transfer amounts.

- **Q** (@dayumbbbb, 2023-04-26): Does Ergo multisig use script-based verification (like Bitcoin's OP_CHECKMULTISIG) or Schnorr key aggregation (MuSig style)?
  **A**: Not conclusively answered in community chat; flagged as an open question for developer clarification.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **SigmaUSD supply bottleneck**: With only ~617k SigmaUSD in circulation and minting locked, how will demand be sustainably addressed? The Dual Mint and SigmaGold proposals are not yet formalised into EIPs or deployment plans.
- **Block speed optimisation**: The community testnet for sub-block and parameter experimentation needs a champion/organiser to move beyond discussion. kushti has provided the infrastructure; execution depends on volunteer leadership.
- **Ergo multisig implementation**: Whether Ergo uses script-based or Schnorr key-aggregation multisig was not definitively answered in community chat.
- **Mining difficulty adjustment**: Whether the post-merge epoch change was temporary or permanent remains unresolved; DGW proposal is at the discussion stage with no formal submission.
- **SigmaFi liquidation mechanism**: The absence of price-based liquidation continues to be flagged as a structural lender risk; no protocol change is planned.
- **Ledger Live production integration**: Developer mode is functional but the timeline for a stable, non-developer-mode Ergo Ledger app remains undefined.
- **Ergo Explorer redesign**: Conditional on the Spectrum Finance raffle reaching its 15k+ ERG fundraising goal; performance issues (slow search, high-tx-count addresses) remain in the interim.
- **CoinGecko native asset listing**: Community campaign underway but no confirmed response from CoinGecko.
- **ChainCash deployment**: Protocol design published and video released, but no mainnet deployment timeline given.
- **Secondary market for SigmaFi loan positions**: NFT-based bond trading proposed as a synthetic options primitive; acknowledged as technically feasible but not yet implemented.
- **Rosen Bridge full public launch**: Beta is live for ERG/ADA; timeline for additional chains, full public access beyond beta, and RSN token distribution remains TBD.

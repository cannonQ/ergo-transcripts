# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: January 2022

---

## Executive Summary

January 2022 was dominated by two converging themes: **protocol-level security debates** around the EIP-27 emission soft fork proposal, and **infrastructure friction** across wallets, exchanges, and network stability. A major P2P network disruption caused by the anetaBTC 5,000-output airdrop transaction exposed a 2016-era codebase vulnerability, resolved via hotfix v4.0.21.1, while simultaneously the community grappled with whether extending the emission schedule or relying on storage rent and transaction fees was the right long-term security model. Wallet UX — particularly Yoroi degradation and Nautilus's emergence — and repeated exchange maintenance issues (KuCoin, CoinEx) defined the operational experience for the month.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EIP-27 Emission Soft Fork

The dominant protocol debate of the month centred on EIP-27, a proposal to extend miner rewards beyond Ergo's initial emission schedule to maintain network security post-emission. The proposal requires ~90% miner approval via pool alignment, with voting beginning approximately 46 days after announcement. Community discussion was substantive and often technical: key objections included whether miners-only voting was appropriate (vs. token holder participation), whether it was premature to act given limited data on transaction fee and storage rent revenue, and whether the proposal violated Ergo's "social contract." Armeanio published multiple articles clarifying the proposal's intent, and kushti contributed technical feedback. A Twitter Spaces discussion on January 7 brought wider community participation. The emission milestone of 50% total supply mined (50,353,750 ERG of ~97.7M max) was reached during the month, adding urgency to the debate.

### Storage Rent Mechanics and Post-Emission Security Modeling

Closely related to EIP-27, the community conducted detailed analysis of whether storage rent alone could sustain miner incentives. Key data point: reaching 3 ERG/block from transaction fees alone would require approximately 490× current daily transaction volume (~5,000 tx/day to ~100,000+). The 4-year storage rent cliff (0.14 ERG per simple UTXO) was debated as potentially gameable by automated wallet transactions every 3.99 years. Alternative models discussed included exponential decay fees for dormant wallets, continuous storage fees, shortening the rent period, and pooling transaction fees into a re-emission contract. No consensus was reached, but kushti noted that transaction fee contracts are not part of the core protocol, leaving design space for future solutions. GetBlok contributed empirical framing with their Proof-of-Vote (POV) token framework to support miner governance participation.

### Network Disruption and Hotfix (January 19–20)

The most significant technical event of the month was a network stability incident initially suspected to be a DDoS attack. Investigation revealed the anetaBTC airdrop transaction with 5,000 outputs exhausted the P2P layer due to large block size, tracing to a vulnerability in the 2016 Scorex codebase. kushti identified and resolved the issue, releasing hotfix v4.0.21.1. Several major mining pools (Herominers, 2miners, Nanopool) experienced orphaned blocks and sync issues during the incident. The episode prompted renewed discussion about mempool spam protection, fee markets, and node-level filtering options (raising minimum propagation fee rates).

### Wallet Ecosystem: Yoroi Decline and Nautilus Emergence

Yoroi wallet experienced persistent and worsening issues throughout January — sync failures, unresponsiveness with large transactions (e.g., the 3,000-output NETA airdrop TX), slow dApp connector performance, and a derivation path inconsistency when restoring 12-word seeds across wallets. Nautilus wallet (browser extension) emerged as the community's preferred alternative, praised for speed, clean UI, and responsive development. MrStahlfelge confirmed the EF wallet had become a full-time role. Satergo was highlighted as the full-node desktop option. The mobile Ergo wallet (Android/iOS) advanced, with MrStahlfelge minting a testnet token using ErgoPay, though the iOS App Store release remained delayed by a DUNS number verification issue. Ledger hardware wallet integration was a recurring milestone: a critical bug was fixed by a third-party team, and Armeanio reported debugging complete around January 25, with planned rollout to Yoroi and Nautilus — but no firm release date was given.

### Cross-Chain Bridges and Spectrum DEX

The Solana bridge (via SuSy) was complete on Ergo's side, awaiting the Solana team's return from vacation. USDT was planned as the first asset. Subsequent EVM-chain bridges (BSC, ETH, Fantom, Matic, Harmony) were estimated at ~2 weeks apart once tooling was in place. Spectrum DEX (then referred to as ErgoDex) was live on Ergo mainnet, with Cardano testnet expected in a few weeks and mainnet in February 2022, contingent on Yoroi dApp connector v5.0. The cross-chain Rosen Bridge was planned for Q2 2022. Key differentiators of Spectrum's order book model over AMM-based DEXes were discussed: total transparency of depth and liquidity, full slippage control, partial order fills, and no single upgrade key risk.

### NIPoPoWs and Protocol Research

Active development on NIPoPoW proof verification was merged into Sigma Rust, enabling UTXO set snapshots for faster node synchronisation. The distinction between NIPoPoWs and FlyClient was clarified (both are technically NIPoPoWs with different trade-offs; Ergo's approach handles variable difficulty). kushti indicated plans to publish sidechain constructions shortly after the anniversary, covering merge-mined sidechains with trustless pegging via smart contracts — an improvement over Bitcoin's BIP 301. The possibility of token holder on-chain voting (for protocol changes) was acknowledged as technically complex but unresolved.

### New Tip Bot, GetBlok Governance, and Ecosystem Projects

The original @ergotip_bot was replaced by @ergotipperbot, supporting cross-platform tipping across Telegram, Discord, Reddit, and Twitter with native token support (ERG, SigmaUSD, SigmaRSV, COMET, Erdoge, and others). GetBlok completed a fundraising raffle (250+ ERG raised) and introduced GBGT (GetBlok Governance Token) — 1 GBGT per 1 ERG mined — as a proof-of-vote mechanism, offering a potential template for future PoW governance. ErgoLearn rebranded as DeCo (Decentralized Collaboration), expanding beyond education to broader eUTXO ecosystem support. Palmyra/Winter Protocol announced a Chainlink integration for proof-of-reserve on on-chain warehouse receipts, with plans to invest token sale capital into building out native Ergo Oracle Pools.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Ilya, 2022-01-21): Can two transactions signed by two different people be concatenated into a single TX?
  **A** (@kushti): UTXO is not much worse than account model for TPS in simple payments. For bulk payments, UTXO is more efficient as per-transaction overhead compresses linearly. UTXO is also more expressive — inputs can be from different users, and things that require smart contracts in account model are trivial in UTXO.

- **Q** (@Ilya, 2022-01-21): For a small country's economy with thousands of simple payments per second, how would instant confirmation work?
  **A** (@Marty ErgoPad): Use layer 2 — zk rollups, batch transactions. Satisfy hundreds or thousands of transactions per second, then submit once to the blockchain. Like a business cashing out at the end of the day.

- **Q** (@Nik, 2022-01-12): What is the difference between ErgoMixer and Tornado Cash?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @kushti): Tornado Cash only works with ETH and uses complex zk-SNARKs with historical bugs (including the Zcash emission vulnerability). ErgoMixer is trustless, uses simple DDH-based cryptography, supports all native Ergo tokens (including wrapped assets), and has no trusted setup.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2022-01-12): Does Ergo use FIFO transaction priority or fee-based like Ethereum?
  **A** (@kushti): Fee/byte sorting, plus transaction family averaging — chained transactions use average fee/byte. Not pure FIFO.

- **Q** (@A, 2022-01-20): Should storage rent be paid to nodes rather than miners, since nodes store the data?
  **A** (@kushti): Data storage and dust collection serve different purposes. Storing less data on-chain (via spending outputs) reduces fees and can have privacy benefits. The two functions are separable.

- **Q** (Community, 2022-W03): Why change the emission schedule now with limited data on fee/rent revenue?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @CW): Storage rent only begins to accrue from ~4 years post-genesis, so the window to gather data is constrained. To reach 3 ERG/block via transaction fees requires roughly 490× current daily volume — from ~5,000 tx/day to ~100,000 — which at 20× growth would still yield only ~0.14 ERG/block in fees.

- **Q** (@T F, 2022-01-24): Why would running a full node benefit the network?
  **A** (@TMR.ΣRG): Full nodes validate and relay transactions, increasing decentralisation. No direct rewards, but enables private/secure transaction submission, solo mining, Oracle Pool operation, and DEX bot running — all with potential monetary rewards.

- **Q** (@United4life, 2022-01-27): Will I lose ERG if my Yoroi wallet is dormant for 4 years?
  **A** (@A, @CW): Not all of it — approximately 0.14 ERG per inactive box is charged as storage rent after 4 years. Sending ERG to a new address in the same wallet resets the timer. Wallets will eventually automate this.

- **Q** (@JCFishing, 2022-W01): Any plans to integrate Ergo into Trust Wallet or other popular wallets?
  **A** (@Gazza): Popular wallets are account-model based; eUTXO coins won't work without wallet developers explicitly adding support. Ergo is more likely to gain support through Cardano wallets eventually. Four new Ergo wallets were in development as of January.

- **Q** (Multiple users, 2022-W04): Can a DDoS attack similar to what happened during the network incident affect Ergo again?
  **A** (@kushti): The January incident was not a DDoS but a large-block P2P exhaustion issue, now patched. For genuine spam attacks, Ergo has mempool filters: node operators can raise minimum propagation fee rates to prevent spam from entering the mempool at all. From node version 4.0.44 onward, limits exist on how many transactions a node will download from a single peer. Fee markets also self-correct — spammers' low-fee transactions flush out of the mempool after 10–40 blocks if not picked up.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **EIP-27 Vote Outcome**: Will miners achieve the ~90% consensus threshold? The 46-day voting window opened in mid-January; result will determine whether the emission schedule is extended. Competing short-term profitability vs. long-term security incentives remain unresolved.

- **Token Holder Voting Rights**: Can ERG holders participate meaningfully in protocol governance beyond miner-only voting? Technically acknowledged as complex; no concrete implementation proposed. Proof-of-vote mechanisms (e.g., GetBlok's GBGT model) offer partial templates but don't solve the full problem.

- **Ledger Integration Release Date**: Debugging completed ~January 25, but no firm timeline for Yoroi and Nautilus integration. Remains a prerequisite for institutional/enterprise users and a blocker for some tier-1 CEX listings.

- **Yoroi dApp Connector v5.0**: Required for Spectrum DEX Cardano integration. v4.9.2 current as of month end; v5.0 and Cardano mainnet launch targeted for February but unconfirmed.

- **Solana Bridge Completion**: Complete on Ergo's side; awaiting Solana team. EVM bridges (BSC, ETH, etc.) contingent on this completing first.

- **Post-Emission Security Model**: Storage rent gameable by automated wallet refreshes every 3.99 years; transaction fee volume 490× below what's needed for 3 ERG/block. No consensus on alternatives (continuous rent, exponential decay, fee pooling, sidechain revenue).

- **Explorer Centralisation**: Only the Ergo Foundation explorer node was reliably performant during the month. A replica was being added, but no decentralised solution proposed.

- **Exchange Maintenance Backlogs**: KuCoin and CoinEx had repeated ERG wallet maintenance issues throughout January; several users reported stuck deposits/withdrawals unresolved at month end. No structural fix in place.

- **Cross-Wallet Seed Derivation Path**: Restoring 12-word seeds across Ergo Mobile, Yoroi, and Nautilus produced different addresses in at least one reported case. MrStahlfelge was investigating; not resolved by month end.

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: September 2020

---

## Executive Summary

September 2020 was a pivotal month for Ergo's technical infrastructure, with major milestones across wallet ecosystem expansion, protocol upgrades, and DeFi primitives. The dominant themes were Oracle Pool architecture and deployment, exchange listing constraints, and the foundational DeFi tooling (ErgoMixer, ZK Treasury, DEX contracts, SigmaUSD stablecoin) taking shape. Community discussion was energetic but frequently tested by thin liquidity on existing exchanges and miner sell pressure.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Oracle Pools Architecture & Deployment
Oracle Pools emerged as a flagship Ergo primitive throughout September. The design uses multiple data sources aggregated through a stake-slashing mechanism to penalise inconsistent datapoints, with hash-with-salt submission during the collection window to prevent oracle copying. Two public pools were live: ERG/USD and ADA/USD (v1, operated by Ergo Foundation members and external parties). Oracle Pool v2 was running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards. The Emurgo Research whitepaper "Unlocking The Potential Of The UTXO Model" was released mid-month, crediting kushti and Chepurnoy, and providing academic grounding for Oracle Pool design on eUTXO systems. Discussion surfaced the possibility of using Chainlink's proof-of-reserve functions for Palmyra's trade-financing use case, while acknowledging plans to build native Ergo oracles as a longer-term priority.

### Wallet Ecosystem Expansion
Wallet availability was a recurring focus. Yoroi integration was the most anticipated milestone: initially expected "this month," it slipped to end-of-September for nightly support, with full release delayed to Q4 due to the Shelley hardfork and developer leave. Sebastien (Yoroi developer) confirmed fairly comprehensive Ergo support in Yoroi Nightly by 2020-09-19. The ErgoWallet desktop client (by gagarin55) was pre-announced on GitHub. Ledger signing code reached a Python implementation but the web demo remained in development, with no confirmed timeline for Ledger's own audit process. Magnum and CoinBarn remained available but with known UX issues. EIP-3 address compatibility support was added to node 3.3.4 to ensure wallet interoperability.

### Autolykos V2 & Protocol Upgrades
Autolykos V2 was activated on testnet during W39, with pool collateral reduced from 130 ERG to 65 ERG and updated AMD miner support. The self-amending protocol received significant discussion: miners vote incrementally to raise block size and computational cost limits without formal hard forks, distinguishing Ergo's governance from traditional DAO or committee models. Explorer backend 3.1.0 and 3.2.0 shipped during the month; the rich list was deployed with SQL optimisation planned. GitHub issue #1194 was opened to improve binary node distribution and reduce one-click install friction.

### DeFi Primitives: ZK Treasury, ErgoMixer, DEX & SigmaUSD
The ZK Treasury v0.1 shipped with executable jars and a demonstrated 3-of-5 multisig spending transaction on-chain. ErgoMixer was highlighted as the first non-interactive, non-custodial mixer in the space, with tokenomics and profit mechanics posted to the forum. DEX contracts (order-book model) were described as largely complete, with Rust developer integration and UI work remaining; a command-line demo existed for developers, with an AMM alternative also under consideration. SigmaUSD stablecoin was pushed from October to December 2020. DarkFund0, a ZK fund for privacy applications, was announced on the forum. NIPoPoWs bootstrapping was confirmed as a near-term roadmap item to enable fast wallet sync.

### Exchange Listing & Liquidity
The community grappled with thin liquidity on Hotbit, Tidex, and CoinEx throughout September, with spreads and sell walls making meaningful price discovery difficult. The Ergo Foundation DevFund (~2.5M ERG / ~$2M at then-current prices) was assessed as insufficient to cover Binance listing fees (>100 BTC) or even KuCoin (~$250–300k range). The KuCoin hack on 2020-09-26 (>$150M stolen) removed that exchange as a near-term option. Gate.io was raided during W37, adding further uncertainty. Community consensus acknowledged that major listings require organic demand and price appreciation rather than fee payment at current valuations.

### Storage Rent & UTXO Model Advantages
Storage rent was explained as Ergo's mechanism for preventing UTXO bloat and enabling on-chain garbage collection, creating profitability incentives for miners to reclaim dust UTXOs. P2S vs. P2SH contract deployment was discussed, with Ergo's P2S approach storing contracts directly in UTXOs vs. Cardano's hash-only model. Privacy advantages of the UTXO model over account-based systems were articulated, with formal prevention of information-leak inference scenarios cited as a key differentiator.

### Emission Schedule & Mining Economics
The emission schedule was clarified repeatedly: 75 ERG/block currently, dropping 3 ERG every 3 months starting July 2021, reaching zero around 2027, with a hard cap of 97.7M ERG. The Ergo Foundation receives 10% of block rewards for the first two years only. Mining profitability discussions surfaced concerns about large miners dumping 25–40k ERG/day, with network hashrate reaching 11.45 TH/s by end of W37. PoW-backed tokens as a concept—tokenising proof-of-work value for sybil-resistant identity—were demonstrated on-chain.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Crypto Rich, 2020-09-01): Can you explain Oracle Pools in simple non-techie terms?
  **A** (@PK / @Noah): Oracle Pools aggregate price feeds from multiple independent sources rather than a single centralised datapoint. A stake-slashing sanction mechanism validates data consistency across the pool. See: https://github.com/Emurgo/Emurgo-Research/blob/master/oracles/Oracle-Pools.md

- **Q** (@Gregg из Аризоны, 2020-09-15): Why submit a hash (with salt) of datapoints during the hash submission period in Oracle Pools?
  **A** (@scalahub): So other oracle operators cannot simply copy the datapoint and rebroadcast it — it's a copying-prevention measure enforced by the on-chain protocol.

- **Q** (@Gregg из Аризоны, 2020-09-15): Isn't the live epoch script in Oracle Pool UTXOs large? Won't that burden memory as it propagates?
  **A** (@Unknown): The datapoint-stage script is short; the live epoch script is the most complex but still compact. Scripts are destroyed when old UTXOs are spent and new ones created, so there is no cumulative memory burden. NIPoPoWs integration will eventually eliminate history-size concerns entirely.

- **Q** (@Cheng.alph, 2020-09-15): Miners increase block parameters without explicit proposals — how does that differ from traditional DAO voting?
  **A** (@kushti): That is how softforks activate in Ergo. For known parameters, simple miner voting achieves consensus without formal governance overhead. Miners have been incrementally raising computational cost limits and block size since mainnet launch.

- **Q** (@Ylly, 2020-09-01): How can you do DeFi with PoW and limited hashpower? What about 51% attacks?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo is secured against rented hashpower via Autolykos (non-outsourceable PoW), which favours professional mining operations with large farms but prevents rental-market 51% attacks. Ergo's self-amending protocol allows miners to adjust parameters like block size and computational cost daily without hard forks.

- **Q** (@Cheng.alph, 2020-09-21): Why is privacy harder to achieve on the account model?
  **A** (@kushti): Because inference scenarios around account-state leaks cannot be formally prevented; the UTXO model avoids this by design, making it possible to formalise information-leak prevention in privacy protocols.

- **Q** (@ニック ᴏʟᴇᴜᴍ, 2020-09-27): How does Ergo prevent 51% attacks?
  **A** (@Unknown): The softfork approach requires ~90% miner consensus for new features; old nodes skip new validation rules and continue operating. Miners can adjust parameters independently. Autolykos's non-outsourceable design also reduces the viability of rental attacks.

- **Q** (@Bag Holder, 2020-09-16): Is ERG required to manage and operate Oracle Pools?
  **A** (@Kookster / @Unknown): Yes — all transaction fees, oracle compensation, and stake-slashing collateral require ERG. No Oracle Pool operation is possible without ERG for on-chain settlement and storage rent.

- **Q** (@Mike [CANUK], 2020-09-23): Java node on Windows crashing with memory exception after 1.5 days at 2/3 sync.
  **A** (@Unknown): Use `java -jar -Xmx4G -Dlogback.stdout.level=INFO ergo-xxx.jar --mainnet -c settings.conf` — set heap size explicitly and adjust log level.

- **Q** (@mensrea, 2020-09-15): What is the connection between Ergo and Scorex?
  **A** (@scalahub): Scorex is a barebones blockchain framework originally created by kushti. Ergo uses it as a library but is a complete, independently designed blockchain — not a Scorex instance.

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: November 2024

---

## Executive Summary

November 2024 was a high-velocity month for Ergo development, marked by the release of Ergo node 6.0.0-RC1 with massive ErgoScript changes, the mainnet launch of Paideia DAO governance, and significant ecosystem expansion across Rosen Bridge, MewFinance, and hardware wallet integrations. Protocol-level discussions dominated — sub-blocks, EIP-50, SigmaUSD governance, and scalability roadmap — alongside growing momentum around a potential Kraken listing and a unified community marketing push.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Ergo 6.0 / Sigma 6.0 Development

The month's most significant technical milestone was the release of Ergo node **6.0.0-RC1** (testnet-only), with ErgoScript changes spanning ~16,000 lines of code documented in LangSpec.md via sigma-state-interpreter PR #930. kushti completed the final Sigma 6.0 interpreter PR early in the month, then published **EIP-50** (the 6.0 soft-fork proposal) and an EIP-5 writeup on ErgoScript contract templates toward month's end. The RC1 is testnet-only; mainnet database compatibility remains uncertain. A parallel **sub-blocks EIP** was identified as kushti's next priority after EIP-50 publication. Community testnet participation was solicited for parameter tweaking (block time, difficulty adjustments), with kushti inviting interested parties — even non-coders — to DM him for inclusion in a Telegram group to run testnets and record scientific observations.

### Sub-blocks & Scalability Roadmap

Sub-blocks emerged as the primary near-term scalability path under active discussion. kushti confirmed the node can already generate sub-blocks, with processing stubs and a votable parameter (number of sub-blocks per block) implemented. No firm ETA was given — "months not years." Broader scalability discussion covered multi-output transactions (15,000+ outputs per tx tested on mainnet), input ordering, layer 2 solutions, and merge-mined sidechains, with the consensus that concrete dApp patterns must be established before optimizing for throughput. Block height/timestamp dependencies were identified as a non-trivial complication for faster block times, since dApps relying on block height rather than timestamps would be affected. Community interest in a CPU/small GPU merge-mined sidechain was noted as a post-sub-blocks possibility.

### Rosen Bridge Multi-Chain Expansion

Rosen Bridge had a strong month: **BSC testing** was configured on Pandora, **Dogecoin** integration produced its first test transaction, and **RoseNet** p2p network testing completed successfully. Support was added for 20+ new tokens including MEW, CYPX, ErgOne, Paideia, QUACKS, and various cross-chain stablecoins. Watcher service v4.0.4 and UI v3.0.0 were released, with operators requested to update. The **Bitcoin Cash** and **TRON** integrations were confirmed as next roadmap targets. A large cross-chain transfer (460,376 rsERG, ~$641K) from Cardano to Ergo was observed, highlighting growing bridge utilisation. Community debated whether Rosen Bridge's dual-layer multisig/slashing model compares favourably to whitepaper-stage BitVM zk-bridge approaches, with Armeanio noting concerns about zk log(n) pairing performance on Bitcoin without pairing curves.

### Paideia DAO Mainnet Launch

Paideia governance quietly went live on mainnet at app.paideia.im during W46, with the legacy testnet DAOs remaining accessible at beta.paideia.im. Community members began casting votes. Luivatra noted Paideia returned to direct on-chain interaction (away from proxy approach) following improved mempool support. The broader **Sigmanauts DAO** structure was also formalised, with mining pool infrastructure established at sigmanauts.com and Mining Swaps Payments going live — seeded by ~19.5M COMET donated by qx() and Koutelier.

### MewFinance Ecosystem

MewFinance accelerated significantly: **MewFund Alpha** launched with initial meme tokens (Pumperino, Hodlerino) via fund.mewfinance.com; MewFund #1 completed and LP tokens were airdropped; a staking pool launched at CruxFinance. Campaign creation was integrated with a 10 ERG setup cost. The platform supports crowdfunding, Mint+LP, multi-asset LP, and ERG+asset LP creation on MewDex. A **UTXO consolidation tool** was published at tools.mewfinance.com/consolidate. Gridbot and hummingbot arbitrage integrations (across DEXes and CEXes) were announced as in development, with a December 15 estimate for hummingbot channel launch.

### Hardware Wallet Integrations

Both Ledger and Keystone integrations progressed. The **Ledger** app-ergo functional review passed; a security audit commenced, with no completion date given. Developer mode use confirmed safe until official release. **Keystone** integration by Pgr456 achieved public key derivation, communication layer, and TX parsing; the remaining blocker is sigma-rust compilation for the Keystone environment (dusek working on PR #781). A 3,000 ERG bounty is available via ErgoDevs. Keystone's airgapped QR-based signing was highlighted as an advantage over Ledger.

### SigmaUSD Governance & Reserve Ratio

The SigmaUSD reserve ratio exceeded 700% by late November, prompting kushti to recommend hedging via SigmaUSD lending on SigmaFi and DuckPools, and flagging SigRSV profit generation as an area for improvement. A significant governance debate emerged around whether to **burn the SigmaUSD bank keys** (held ~4 years without protocol updates) or retain them for future adjustments. A forum thread was opened; no decision was reached. The historical **Bearwhale frontrunning exploit** on the v1 contract was reviewed — Bearwhale ultimately lost ~170K ERG — confirming v2's slower oracle update mitigation remains effective.

### Community Marketing Consolidation

A community marketing call on November 15 (4pm UTC) successfully brought together multiple ecosystem teams. A unified strategy discussion covered Sigmanauts, MewFinance, Palmyra/Zengate, and ErgoPad successor initiatives. Anti-CBDC messaging was proposed as an emerging narrative — positioning Ergo as programmable money owned by individuals vs. government-controlled CBDC systems — with Elon Musk's publicly expressed interest in demurrage (via DOGE flat supply) noted as a potential mass-market angle. The **Ergo Book** project was announced: kushti compiling forum posts, articles, and papers into a single Markdown reference covering Ergo Manifesto, money programmability, CBDC critique, and sidechain/L2 constructions. Contributions, proofreading, and translations (French offered by Giufa) were solicited.

### Palmyra & Chainlink Oracle Integration

Dan (Palmyra/Zengate lead) detailed plans for **on-chain warehouse receipts** supporting decentralised trade financing via Chainlink's proof-of-reserve functions through Winter Protocol. IoT sensors (weight, motion, camera) provide continuous proof-of-existence for bonded warehouse commodities. Oracle Pools v1 (EF members + external) and v2 (Dexy Gold with GORT rewards) are operational. Plans include investing token sale proceeds in building out native Ergo oracles, with the current two active Oracle Pools noted as insufficient for the ecosystem's needs.

### CEX Listing Momentum

Community noted **Mark Greenberg** (former Kraken listings head) publicly expressing support for Ergo. qx() confirmed Ergo is "shortlisted" for Kraken but specific conditions and timeline were not disclosed. Multiple CEX listing agents (BingX, MEXC, Cexeslisting) reached out during the month; community consensus was that they had not done their homework and quoted inflated fees (~40K typical). Grassroots Twitter/X engagement campaigns were ongoing.

### ErgoPad Sunset

After three years, ErgoPad shut down. Staking contracts remain immutable on-chain; newer projects now use off-chain bots for retry logic. Community discussion explored alternatives including Paideia DAO-based crowdfunding and meme launchpads. MewFund was identified as the practical successor for token launches.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Mewbie, 2024-11-05): Who created ErgoMixer?
  **A** (@kushti): anon92048 developed v1 (1990s-style interface); anon2020* created the improved version with stealth addresses. Jason Davies identified vulnerabilities in initial contracts. Contributions documented in the Ergonaut NFT (token ID 000000002306564c7b03a78b76a2ce1d9d4e748ddf462cb73cc30b48e43e6e44).

- **Q** (@Steve F, 2024-11-13): Why does the Terminus wallet require a spending password to view the mnemonic phrase?
  **A** (@kushti, @Patato): Mnemonic is likely encrypted with the spending password. This protects against phone-unlock compromise — an attacker who unlocks the device cannot extract the seed without the spending password.

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2024-11-10): Redeeming SigRSV — transaction gets stuck or fails after an hour. Is the contract lacking ERG?
  **A** (@Rj, @kushti, @glasgowm, @Flying Pig): Multiple concurrent redeemers may cause conflicts; oracle price changes can block redemption. Try a higher miner fee or use Nautilus wallet. Use the refund button in the menu; disconnect and reconnect wallet as dApp connector. The sigmausd.io web interface has persistent TX assembly issues — the mobile wallet is a reliable alternative.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-11-21): Could storage rent UTXOs be auctioned, with proceeds going to miners if no bids?
  **A** (@Unknown): Would require a hard fork. Interesting as a guard rail mechanism for users around storage rent.

- **Q** (@LAKAY, 2024-11-23): If I hold ERG in a wallet for 3+ years without touching it, can miners take my ERG via storage rent?
  **A** (@cafebedouin.org and others): Only dormant boxes (untouched ~4 years) with insufficient ERG to cover demurrage are charged. Sending any TX or consolidating resets the clock. Holdings won't disappear unless a box sits dormant and falls below the storage rent threshold.

- **Q** (@Jayson, 2024-11-29): Is there any incentive for sub-blocks now? Are there rewards for miners?
  **A** (@cafebedouin): Miners earn TX fees and storage rent from input (sub-)blocks, plus a constant reward from ordering blocks. Post-emission reward mechanism (~28 years out) remains unspecified.

- **Q** (@Marc the Shark, 2024-11-26): What is the record for most recipients in a single transaction on Ergo?
  **A** (@qx()): Before adjustment, someone sent to 10,000–15,000 recipients at once on mainnet. Current practical limit is ~1,000 unique outputs; 1,500 achievable with a single token. Max transaction size is 98,304 bytes. Chained transactions bypass single-TX limits.

- **Q** (@Ergochampion, 2024-11-20): Best way to buy ERG in the US without exchange KYC?
  **A** (@cannon_q): CoinEx ($10K daily withdrawal limit), TradeOgre, Xeggex, or nonkyc.io. Alternatively, buy ADA on any CEX and swap for rsERG via Splash.trade.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-11-11): Why do dApps on Ergo frequently fail or run slowly?
  **A** (@qx(), @Luivatra): Contract design issues, not chain-level problems. ErgoPad staking used an older direct-interaction method where simultaneous unstakes collide. Newer contracts use off-chain bots for retry logic. Paideia returned to direct interaction with better mempool support. Unreliable dApps self-correct via market forces.

- **Q** (@Gad, 2024-11-17): Why does Kaspa retain input flags if Bitcoin Script is stripped?
  **A** (@kushti): Unclear from code inspection — likely future-proofing for programmability. Worth asking the Kaspa team directly. kushti confirmed prior knowledge of Yonatan (Kaspa founder) since 2016 and vouched for technical integrity.

- **Q** (@Biscuitz, 2024-11-16): Can I buy ERG in the USA with Coinbase wallet?
  **A** (@qx()): No direct Coinbase onramp. Best paths: (1) Buy ADA on Coinbase → swap on Splash DEX to rsERG; (2) Buy ADA → use simpleswap.io to swap to ERG. Also available on TradeOgre.

- **Q** (@gg, 2024-11-29): Could a ve(3,3) tokenomics model work on proof-of-work like Ergo?
  **A** (no formal response): ve(3,3) would allow hodlers to earn fees and gain voting power while locking ERG. Flagged as an open design discussion; no kushti response recorded.

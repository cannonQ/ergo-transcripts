# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: February 2019

---

## Executive Summary

February 2019 was dominated by pre-launch preparation as the Ergo community awaited the transition from TestNet 2.0 to MainNet, targeted for mid-March 2019. Core discussions centred on token economics (ERG supply, EFYT conversion mechanics, and founder allocations), mining infrastructure (CPU vs. GPU availability), and node/wallet setup guidance for early participants.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### MainNet Launch Countdown & TestNet 2.0
The team confirmed MainNet launch was "around the corner" following completion of TestNet 2.0, with a mid-March 2019 target. TestNet v1.9.1 was already available with CPU mining enabled. No formal bug bounty was announced for testnet participants, though significant bug discoveries were noted as potentially rewarded on a case-by-case basis.

### Token Supply & EFYT Conversion Economics
Considerable discussion clarified the ERG supply model: a hard cap of 100M ERG, with 75 ERG per block at a 2-minute block interval. The EFYT-to-ERG conversion ratio was confirmed at 1:1 (1 EFYT = 1 ERG(cash) + 1 ERG(data)). First-year supply was projected at approximately 19.7M ERG including the founder allocation of 10% of mined supply — with EFYT swap tokens included within that figure rather than added on top. The conversion was described as likely occurring gradually over time rather than as an immediate one-off swap.

### Mining Infrastructure
CPU mining was confirmed functional on testnet via a configuration flag in the node settings. GPU mining was confirmed to be in active development for post-MainNet release, with a commitment to support Linux mining software. The distinction between Proof of Work (hash-power-based) and Proof of Stake (master node/coin-holding-based) mechanisms was clarified for newer community members.

### Wallet & Node Setup
Early participants were directed to access wallet functionality via the node API, with configuration details available on the GitHub Wiki. EFYT trading was available via the Waves platform's built-in exchange in the interim period before MainNet.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (Anonymous, 2019-02-13): Is mining the same as master nodes? Can you mine with master nodes?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): No. Proof of Work mining is based on hash power; Proof of Stake (master nodes) is based on coin holdings — these are fundamentally different mechanisms.

- **Q** (Anonymous, 2019-02-13): Is ERG(cash) the same as the mining reward? How are they connected?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): ERG(cash) is the mining reward. ERG(data) comes from a separate chain. Both derive from EFYT conversion at a 1:1 ratio.

- **Q** (Anonymous, 2019-02-13): First-year supply — is it 19.7M ERG from mining plus 1.97M from the EFYT swap, totalling ~21.67M?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): No. The swap tokens are included within the 19.7M figure. Founders receive 10% of mined supply; the full EFYT swap is expected to occur gradually over time rather than immediately at launch.

- **Q** (@Nikolai, 2019-02-18): How long does a transaction take on the Ergo blockchain?
  **A** (Anonymous): Depends on miners and the solutions built on top — no fixed confirmation time was specified at this stage.

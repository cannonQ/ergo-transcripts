# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Month:** 2021-05

---

## Executive Summary

May 2021 was a pivotal month for Ergo, marked by rapid price appreciation, the resolution of the SigmaUSD "bearwhale" saga, and a series of significant ecosystem launches including ErgoDEX's landing page, the Gravity Network integration, and Ledger hardware wallet support. The community grappled with exchange listing delays behind NDAs, Yoroi infrastructure problems, and intense new-user inflows from the Cardano community, while the core team published a formal roadmap and several major technical blog posts.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### SigmaUSD Bearwhale Saga and Reserve Dynamics
A large actor spent much of May attempting to manipulate the SigmaUSD protocol by exploiting oracle price lag, minting SigmaRSV to short ERG. Following the v2 deployment, the bearwhale's exit resulted in losses of approximately 75k–170k ERG across separate incidents, with ~$500k in potential gains forfeited. The reserve ratio climbed to extreme levels (2089–2300%), well above the healthy 400–800% lock range. kushti and Armeanio repeatedly and urgently warned SigmaRSV holders to redeem profits throughout the month, noting that the inflated reserve attracted manipulation and was not sustainable. The episode was formally documented in an official blog post ("Bearwhale Saga") and highlighted both the resilience of the AgeUSD protocol under stress and the limitations of oracle price smoothing during rapid price moves.

### ErgoDEX Launch and Architecture
ErgoDEX's landing page (ergodex.io) launched on May 10, and an official Telegram channel was created. The DEX combines an AMM and an order book model, designed to operate across both Ergo (using ERG as the fee token) and Cardano (using ADA) once Cardano's Alonzo upgrade deployed. The AMM was targeted for public beta in the first half of June 2021, with the order book to follow. The team debated whether to implement a governance token, explicitly testing both approaches before deciding — no presale or ICO was planned regardless. Cross-chain ADA/ERG swaps were confirmed as dependent on Cardano smart contracts, with shared liquidity research ongoing. The DEX's eUTXO smart contract model was highlighted as keeping most logic off-chain, making it significantly cheaper than Ethereum-style on-chain execution.

### Exchange Listings and Business Development
Armeanio confirmed throughout the month that four CEX listings and two DEX listings were completed and awaiting technical wallet integration on the exchange side, under NDA. No names were disclosed, though community speculation centred on KuCoin. The Ergo Foundation's strategy of avoiding large listing fees (which would require a premine Ergo never had) was articulated clearly — organic growth and technical credibility over short-term price action. Gate.io reduced the ERG withdrawal fee from 2 ERG to 0.5 ERG in response to price appreciation. Hotbit remained problematic throughout May following a prior security incident, with withdrawals suspended. CoinEx experienced scaling issues. Community was consistently advised not to mine directly to exchange addresses.

### Ledger and Hardware Wallet Support
A major announcement confirmed the Tesseract firm had been hired to develop native Ledger Nano S and X support, with an estimated two-month timeline (targeting July 2021). A partnership with Opolo hardware wallet was also announced. In the interim, a workaround was documented: using Waves Exchange with a Ledger device to hold ERG with user-controlled private keys, though this involves wrapped tokens rather than native ERG. Full Yoroi + Ledger integration was separately noted as contingent on the Yoroi dApp connector reaching public release.

### Gravity Network Integration and Cross-Chain Interoperability
After months of development, cross-chain tests between Ergo and Gravity Network were completed, with Gravity-Ergo-Proxy contracts described as nearly ready. This integration would allow ERG to be wrapped and ported to Ethereum and BSC, and for Ergo to provide oracle services to chains connected via Gravity. Armeanio also referenced an Ergo.meta sidechain/L2 framework in development, described as potentially giving Ergo Polkadot-like multi-chain capabilities. The full bridge was estimated at approximately one month from completion as of mid-May.

### Yoroi Wallet Infrastructure Problems
From approximately May 26–30, Yoroi experienced significant syncing failures that prevented users from viewing balances or sending transactions, requiring 400+ confirmations for deposits to appear. The root cause was Emurgo's node infrastructure rather than a code defect, and was resolved by a restart. Users were directed to verify actual balances via Ergo Explorer during the outage. Separately, a bug was flagged where restoring an ERG wallet on Yoroi mobile created an ADA Shelley wallet instead, and the "Send All Assets" button in Yoroi was warned as dangerous — it sends all tokens including SigmaUSD and SigmaRSV indiscriminately.

### Mining Profitability and Autolykos v2
ERG was briefly the most profitable GPU-mineable coin during May's price spike, drawing significant new mining interest. The Autolykos v2 PoW algorithm's memory-hardness was highlighted as a key differentiator preventing ASICs while accepting mining pools (a deliberate design change from v1's pool-resistance, justified by the academic paper showing non-outsourceable PoW is impossible when smart contracts exist). Network hashrate crossed 2 PH/s, causing difficulty lag and temporary transaction delays as miners entered and exited with price volatility. The 4GB+ GPU requirement and Linux one-liner node setup were frequently shared for new miners.

### Ergo–Cardano Relationship and Ecosystem Positioning
A steady influx of Cardano community members throughout May prompted repeated clarifications: Ergo is an independent blockchain, not built on Cardano; the shared eUTXO model and IOHK/Emurgo collaboration make the projects complementary rather than competitive. kushti's background at IOHK and dcspark's formation (Robert Kornacki, Nico Arqueros, Sebastien Guillemot departing Emurgo) were both cited as evidence of deep technical alignment — Kornacki was paraphrased as saying ~80% of eUTXO work is interchangeable between the two chains. The dcspark team intended to build tooling benefiting both chains, establishing on Cardano first.

### Treasury Emission and Long-Term Funding
The 2.5-year treasury emission period was noted as approaching its end. Post-emission, miner incentives shift to storage rent, transaction fees, and community mechanisms including the ErgoFundMe dApp and DeFi application fees. The block reward structure (67.5 ERG to miners, remainder to foundation at the time) and the storage rent mechanism (small fee on UTXOs inactive for four years, recirculating lost coins) were frequently explained to new users as core to Ergo's long-term economic model.

### Dexy Gold Stablecoin Proposal
kushti published a new stablecoin design on the ErgoForum — "Dexy USD" — described as the simplest possible stablecoin design, soliciting community feedback. This was presented as a complement to or evolution from SigmaUSD/AgeUSD rather than a replacement.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@community, 2021-05-08): Why did Autolykos v2 enable mining pools after v1 was pool-resistant?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Autolykos v1 had non-outsourcability built in, but it became apparent that with smart contracts it is impossible to prevent pools — large players could exploit the loophole via collateralised smart contracts. The team disabled pool-resistance so the loophole was not available only to sophisticated actors. Ergo now focuses on memory-hardness to keep mining fair and prevent ASICs. Reference: "Bypassing Non-Outsourceable Proof-of-Work Schemes Using Collateralized Smart Contracts" — https://ia.cr/2020/044, presented by kushti at WTSC/Financial Cryptography 2020.

- **Q** (@community, 2021-05-11): What makes ErgoDEX smart contracts cheaper than Ethereum?
  **A** (@Armeanio): Ergo uses multi-stage contracts where most logic is off-chain but each stage is verified on-chain. Traditional Ethereum smart contracts execute all logic on-chain and charge gas for every operation. Reference video: https://youtu.be/g3FlM_WOwBU

- **Q** (@community, 2021-05-11): How does ErgoDEX operate across two networks?
  **A** (@Dmitry Usov): One DEX operates across two networks. On the Ergo network, ERG is the fee token. On the Cardano network, ADA is the fee token. When providing liquidity for an ADA/ERG pair, users specify which network they are on.

- **Q** (@community, 2021-05-11): Is there a governance token for ErgoDEX?
  **A** (@Glasgow / @Dmitry Usov): No governance token has been announced. ERG is the base fee token on Ergo. Test tokens observed in pool contracts were for testing only. The team is evaluating both approaches before deciding.

- **Q** (@Team Team, 2021-05-12): Can a PoS coin implement privacy functions like ErgoMixer?
  **A** (@kushti): Privacy technology is unrelated to consensus mechanism.

- **Q** (@community, 2021-05-12): Is Ergo vulnerable to a 51% attack?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Risk is lower on Ergo because there is no hash rental market — no off-ramps for renting hash. More pools will be added as the network grows. Full discussion: https://www.reddit.com/r/ergonauts/comments/mgpnb7/51_attack_possibilities

- **Q** (@community, 2021-05-14): What is the healthy reserve ratio range for SigmaUSD?
  **A** (@Glasgow): The contract locks between 400–800%. The current ratio far above that range is a result of the bootstrapping period; it is not a healthy or safe state for SigmaRSV holders.

- **Q** (@community, 2021-05-14): What are the disadvantages of Ergo being listed on Binance?
  **A** (@Glasgow / @Snowman Jason): Binance historically requests large sums of coins or cash annually for listings, which would give Binance 5–15% of supply and severely harm decentralisation. Ergo had no premine, so there are no coins to give.

- **Q** (@community, 2021-05-16): What will block rewards reduce to in July?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Block rewards reduce by 3.5 ERG per block every 3 months from July onward.

- **Q** (@community, 2021-05-21): How does Ergo's difficulty adjustment work? Why does difficulty stay high when hashrate drops?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Ergo uses a linear least-squares adjustment algorithm smoothed over 8–9 epochs (1 epoch = 1024 blocks). There is an intentional lag between hashrate changes and difficulty adjustment to prevent oscillation.

- **Q** (@Nick, 2021-05-26): How does the network collect storage rent without the address owner initiating a transaction?
  **A**: Not answered definitively in the chat during this period.

- **Q** (@Jon, 2021-05-28): Ergo has no gas like Ethereum — how does it handle network saturation?
  **A** (@c8): There is a simple transaction fee market on layer 1. Miners can adjust minimum fees. One Ergo transaction can send to 500 wallets simultaneously, and further scaling solutions would be implemented before saturation became critical.

- **Q** (@Jeronimo Backes, 2021-05-30): Why does SigmaUSD/SigmaRSV have a 2 ERG fee?
  **A** (@Glasgow / @Chris Ray): The fee prevents manipulation, grows the reserve, and incentivises SigmaRSV minting. It is paid into the reserve contract, not to Ergo or miners. For comparison, DAI fees are nearly double. The fee is contract-specific, not the general network transaction fee (which is 0.0001 ERG).

- **Q** (@community, 2021-05-30): Is Ergo planning to transition to PoS?
  **A** (@Ron): No. NIPoPoWs and other PoW-specific features are still being developed. Ergo intends to remain the most technologically advanced PoW chain.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Exchange listing identities and timelines**: Four CEX + two DEX listings confirmed complete but NDA-bound. Exchange-side wallet integration is the blocker. No names confirmed; KuCoin speculated but unverified.
- **ErgoDEX governance token decision**: Team explicitly undecided; testing both approaches. Distribution method (usage rewards only, no ICO) confirmed, but whether a token exists at all is TBD.
- **ErgoDEX cross-chain ADA/ERG swaps**: Dependent on Cardano Alonzo deployment (~August 2021 estimated). Shared liquidity research ongoing with no implementation timeline.
- **Ledger native integration**: Tesseract engaged, ~2-month timeline stated, no confirmed date. Yoroi dApp connector (required for full integration) not yet in public release.
- **Mobile wallet support**: Repeatedly described as "approximately 2 months away" with no firm date. Yoroi mobile creates ADA wallets instead of ERG wallets (known bug).
- **V1 SigmaRSV stuck funds**: Users with large V1 SigmaRSV positions face redemption limits (~16,383 RSV at a time) and may remain partially stuck depending on SigmaUSD redemption activity. No protocol-level fix proposed.
- **Storage rent collection mechanism**: How the network collects storage rent without a user-initiated transaction was asked but not answered technically during this period.
- **Dexy Gold stablecoin specification**: Published on ErgoForum for feedback; design details, oracle requirements, and launch timeline all TBD.
- **Merge-mined sidechain constructions**: kushti mentioned publishing sidechain constructions "soon" with no specific date or technical spec.
- **Nanopool hashrate inflation claims**: Raised but never formally acknowledged or refuted.
- **Hotbit withdrawal restoration**: Expected ~May 21 but unconfirmed; users advised to use CoinEx or Gate.io.

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: December 2022

---

## Executive Summary

December 2022 was a technically dense month for Ergo, dominated by three converging themes: the successful activation of Node 5.0 and the beginning of 6.0 roadmap planning, deep protocol discussions around storage rent mechanics and miner economics, and continued ecosystem development across Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, Dexy Gold, and Satergo. Despite a challenging bear market, developer activity remained high and community engagement was sustained by a series of structured AMAs, community chats, and weekly developer updates.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Node 5.0 Activation & Immediate Aftermath
Node v5.0 activated at block height 889,856 on approximately 2022-12-05, enabling 5.x costing that allows several times more transactions per block through optimized script validation. Initial observations of confirmed transactions appearing in the mempool UI were identified as a display issue rather than a network problem. kushti subsequently outlined a bootstrapping roadmap targeting UTXO set snapshots (verified without ~95% of blockchain) by February 2023, followed by NIPoPoWs bootstrapping aimed at 30–60 minute full-node sync on ordinary laptops. By the final week of the month, UTXO set snapshot downloading from multiple peers was demonstrated live at approximately 15 minutes versus the previous 5–6 hour full block processing time — a significant UX improvement for node operators.

### Node 6.0 Roadmap Planning
Following 5.0 activation, preliminary 6.0 discussions began in earnest. Core topics under review included sub-block confirmations (Bitcoin NG and Prism architectures), merge-mined sidechains, faster confirmation protocols, and layer 2 solutions. kushti emphasized that optimization decisions must be grounded in concrete application profiles — pointing to the existing ecosystem of GARD protocols, Rosen Bridge, Oracle Pools, Spectrum AMM pool contracts, and Trade House lending contracts as a meaningful benchmark. A recurring concern was that block time reduction requires careful handling of timestamp-dependent contracts and emission schedules. CW announced weekly community chats alternating between EIPs and "Road to 6.0" on Fridays at 14:00 UTC, starting mid-December. A testnet for parameter experimentation was flagged as available for interested miners and developers.

### Storage Rent Mechanics
Storage rent received sustained attention throughout the month, culminating in a dedicated community chat on 2022-12-13. The mechanism charges approximately 0.14 ERG per byte per 4-year cycle, with the first trigger date set for July 1, 2023 (4 years post-mainnet launch). Key clarifications: every unspent output is subject to rent regardless of incoming transfers; when a box cannot cover rent, all assets including NFTs are collectible by miners; at current rates, 1 ERG in a box takes approximately 32 years to be consumed. Discussion also confirmed that storage rent does not shrink historical blocks but reduces the active UTXO set, lowering node indexing load. Spam attacks were noted as potentially self-defeating for attackers since they create future storage rent income for miners. EIP-33/EIP-68 burning proposals were discussed as mitigations for NFT-based access keys at risk. The community identified a need for a global visualization tool for projected rent collection ahead of the July 2023 trigger — no such tool existed yet.

### Rosen Bridge Development
Rosen Bridge progressed from discussion to live demonstration during the month. The one-way locking/synthetic minting model was clarified: assets lock on the origin chain, wrapped synthetic tokens mint on the destination, with no liquidity pools required and 1:1 representation maintained. Both ADA→ERG and ERG→Cardano directions were confirmed live or near-live. Known issues included a Koios bug in reporting previous blocks and p2p connection bugs under active remediation. The bridge is intentionally slow to prioritize security over capital efficiency; tokenomics and whitepaper remained in development. A live ERG→Cardano (RsERG) cross-chain swap was demonstrated by kushti on 2022-12-22 with transaction details published. Guards' stake and exit-scam prevention mechanisms remained undocumented pending whitepaper completion.

### Spectrum DEX, Trade House & Order Book Design
The Spectrum Finance SPF token was announced for IDO on ErgoPad, with airdrop and blockchain distribution details published. Spectrum's AMM pool architecture was contrasted repeatedly with order book designs such as Trade House: order books provide full transparency of market depth and liquidity, complete slippage control, no impermanent loss, and support for partial order fills — all enforced on-chain with refund conditions and no central upgrade key. The possibility of sharing Spectrum AMM liquidity with Trade House order book pools was raised as a future integration point. Auction House v3 dark mode was previewed. Spectrum yield farming was described as nearly complete to attract deeper liquidity.

### Dexy Gold & SigmaUSD Economics
SigmaUSD circulating supply collapsed from approximately 7 million to under 600,000 during the month due to low ERG prices reducing collateralization incentives, with Reserve Ratio at 365% (redemptions exceeding minting). Dexy Gold was discussed as an anticipated alternative featuring improved oracle refresh mechanisms and dynamic fees; Armeanio noted its natural whale-attack prevention and composability properties, while acknowledging it requires battle testing. Djed on Cardano was compared as using signed exchange rate validity intervals and paced liquidity to reduce oracle attack vectors — a design difference from SigmaUSD's query-based approach. Oracle Pool v1 (run by EF members and community) and Oracle Pool v2 (running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards) were both active.

### Mining Economics, ASIC/FPGA Resistance & Miner Governance
The post-merge environment continued to weigh on ERG mining profitability. GetBlok mining pool shut down due to insufficient profitability at approximately 1% network hashrate, with the team remaining in the ecosystem and code open-sourced. Wolf9466 provided detailed technical explanations of GPU versus FPGA efficiency differences in Autolykos, noting that memory table adjustments periodically resist dedicated hardware. Community consensus held that ASICs are not currently economically viable for Ergo due to memory requirements and manufacturing lead times. A community chat on FPGA/ASIC resistance and Useful Proof of Work was scheduled for 2022-12-20. Transaction fee economics were debated: the base fee of 0.001 ERG is negligible relative to block rewards, and miner passivity on governance was flagged as a structural concern that becomes critical when block rewards fall below 5 ERG. Armeanio disclosed that the EF was exploring a potential partnership to experiment with useful proof of work, characterized as a "decentralized science experiment" rather than a core consensus upgrade.

### Ergo Foundation Treasury & Long-Term Sustainability
Glasgow disclosed that approximately 662,000 ERG was spent by the EF in 2022, with approximately 800,000 ERG remaining in the treasury — sufficient to complete the 6.0 roadmap. The largest historical expense category was CEX listing costs. The EF was described as designed to bootstrap the ecosystem and then step back; post-6.0, decentralized community governance and associations are expected to assume core development roles. A formal transparency report was announced as forthcoming. The treasury address is fully transparent on-chain.

### Palmyra / ZenGate & Chainlink Partnership
Dan Friedman and Armeanio outlined the Chainlink integration across multiple AMAs and community chats during December. Chainlink's proof-of-reserve functions are utilized via a proxy function in Winter Protocol to issue on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralized trade financing. IoT devices (cameras, weight sensors, motion sensors) provide continuous proof-of-existence for commodities deposited in bonded warehouses. This is not positioned as zero-sum with Ergo oracles; the plan includes building out native Ergo Oracle Pools using capital raised through the Palmyra token sale. The Palmyra IDO was announced on ErgoPad. Everything in the Palmyra ecosystem is currently running on Ergo.

### Wallet Ecosystem Updates
Satergo v1.5.0 was released on 2022-12-23 with a complete UI redesign, auto-restoration of derived addresses, full keyboard navigation, auto-update for nodes, and custom stylesheet support; v1.5.1 followed within 24 hours with critical fixes. Ledger Ergo app support remained in review, with Glasgow indicating it should be available via Ledger Manager within "the next couple of weeks" pending approval — sideloading had been possible since May 2022. Yoroi was confirmed as no longer actively maintained for Ergo; users were directed to Nautilus or Ergo Mobile Wallet. The Minotaur wallet team was credited with designing Ergo's first multisig wallet.

### ErgoMixer Privacy & Incentive Design
ErgoMixer usage was noted as low in the bear market. Proposals for incentivization included ERG-based rewards, LP-style reward sharing, and long-tail fee structures (early rounds expensive, later rounds free) to maintain large anonymity sets without revealing LP funding sources. SigmaJoin improvements under exploration included multiple boxes per mix, outsourceable mixing, and integration of mixing into dApps such as Dexy. No consensus on a final incentive mechanism was reached. SafeW was noted as having integrated mixer functionality.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Klaus, 2022-12-06): Are there security risks with UTXO set snapshot bootstrapping? Who generates snapshots?
  **A** (@kushti): Most full nodes provide snapshots; security is equivalent to full-node security. Risks are the same as downloading the full blockchain from multiple random peers.

- **Q** (@Pulsarz, 2022-12-08): If USDT collapses, does SigmaUSD fail since oracles use USDT pairs?
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge): Coingecko API averages across all trade pairs. (@Luivatra): Oracle reports ERG in USD not USDT; Coingecko should handle a depeg, but a centralized dependency exists. SigmaUSD remains at risk; Dexy Gold is independent of USDT.

- **Q** (@miwal, 2022-12-04): Is there any example where smart contracts are truly trustless? For it to be true there can be no developer private keys capable of updating the contract.
  **A** (@Glasgow): SigmaUSD v1 is fully immutable. SigmaUSD v2 has governance vote tokens for updates. DuckPools is also immutable on deployment. The update authorization key is an NFT held in the developer's crypto wallet.

- **Q** (@miwal, 2022-12-04): Does Paideia's goal allow a DAO voting mechanism for smart contract updates without a human in the loop?
  **A** (@Luivatra): When a vote passes, the change will be deployed on-chain. No human in the loop.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2022-12-15): Who has the exact date for the first storage rent trigger?
  **A** (@CW): July 1, 2023 — 4 years after mainnet launch.

- **Q** (@qx(), 2022-12-13): If I receive new inputs via transfers to my wallet, am I still at risk since old boxes haven't moved?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes. Every unspent output after 4 years can be charged by a miner under the storage rent subprotocol. Incoming transfers create new boxes; old boxes remain independently subject to rent.

- **Q** (@qx(), 2022-12-13): If a box has insufficient ERG, are all assets including NFTs taken at once?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes. When a box value cannot cover storage rent, all assets in that box are collectible.

- **Q** (@hoodieonwho, 2022-12-21): Mobile wallet says "use lower amount" when sending more than 20 ERG; slow confirmations.
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge): This is a UTXO consolidation issue. Small 0.2 ERG boxes require 100 inputs to reach 20 ERG, increasing transaction size and confirmation time. Use the box consolidation dApp to merge UTXOs. A higher transaction fee accelerates confirmation. This is expected network behavior, not a wallet bug.

- **Q** (@zhang zee, 2022-12-27): How does storage rent reduce blockchain size if transaction history remains?
  **A** (@qx()): Storage rent does not shrink historical blocks. It reduces the working set — the active UTXO index. Nodes only index spendable outputs; expired boxes are no longer part of the UTXO set. Fewer UTXOs means less RAM and indexing work for nodes.

- **Q** (@Rj, 2022-12-27): How do spam attacks on Ergo differ from those on Bitcoin or Litecoin?
  **A** (@kushti): Storage rent enables miners to extract rent from untouched UTXOs after 4 years. Spammers create UTXOs that eventually become profitable to mine, so spam actually benefits miners long-term — a fundamentally different economic dynamic from Bitcoin.

- **Q** (@Misfit Toy, 2022-12-28): Does Rosen Bridge require liquidity pools?
  **A** (@qx() & @Armeanio): No. One side locks the asset, the other side mints a synthetic. The asset never literally crosses chains. 1:1 wrapped representation is maintained. Community can optionally create LP pairs post-bridge for swaps.

- **Q** (@Arbion Halili, 2022-12-09): How do you prove knowledge of a DH tuple without revealing it?
  **A** (@kushti): This is exactly what ErgoMixer (zerojoin) checks. See proveDHTuple in https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/560.pdf. DH-based protocol specifics depend on use case.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-12-08): Why should regular users care about the mixer?
  **A** (@CW): P2P sends, NFT purchases, and DEX swaps all expose wallet balances on Ergo Explorer. The mixer breaks address linking. Example: gifting ERG to a nephew reveals the uncle's whale status on-chain without mixing. SafeW has integrated mixer functionality.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-12-07): How to earn ERG through holding without selling in future?
  **A** (community): Staking returns are a form of rent-seeking unless labor is provided. No passive return mechanism exists that doesn't involve some form of contributed value or trust assumption.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-12-15): How does an order book DEX differ from an AMM and reduce slippage concerns?
  **A** (@qx()): Order books provide total transparency of market depth and liquidity levels, full control over slippage, and no impermanent loss. Peer-to-peer matching allows partial fills. Trade House and Spectrum AMM serve different user needs and are complementary.

- **Q** (@Wolf9466, 2022-12-18): Why are FPGAs more efficient than GPUs for certain mining algorithms?
  **A** (@Wolf9466): GPUs use general-purpose instructions where an operation like XOR takes 4 cycles; FPGAs can implement the same in 1 cycle. FPGAs clock lower than GPUs but achieve better efficiency for hardware-friendly algorithms such as Keccak. The trade-off is that FPGAs are less flexible.

- **Q** (@TMR.ΣRG, 2022-12-27): Must the entire blockchain be stored by at least one node for the network to remain live?
  **A** (@kushti): The network stays alive if all nodes prune with security-preserving suffixes. Archiving early blocks is beneficial for transparency; Torrent-based distribution is a viable option for historical data.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **6.0 Sub-block & Microblock Parameterization**: No concrete implementation timeline or parameter proposals for block time reduction, microblocks, or sub-block confirmations. Impact on timestamp-dependent contracts and emission schedules unresolved. Community testnet available but needs a champion to organize experiments.

- **Mixer Incentive Design**: Long-tail fee structures, LP reward sharing, and ERG-based incentives all proposed but no final mechanism selected. Game-theoretic soundness of LP funding without privacy leaks remains an open problem.

- **Storage Rent Visualization Tool**: Community-requested global dApp showing projected rent collection events ahead of the July 2023 trigger does not yet exist.

- **NFT Access Key Protection from Storage Rent**: No standardized solution agreed upon — auction mechanisms, burning proposals (EIP-33/EIP-68), and wallet-level deadline warnings all discussed but not committed.

- **Miner Fee Governance**: No mechanism to incentivize profit-focused miners to organize around protocol health parameters (e.g., transaction fee increases). Flagged as increasingly urgent as block rewards decline toward tail emission.

- **Rosen Bridge Tokenomics & Whitepaper**: Guard stake economics, exit-scam prevention mechanisms, and full tokenomics remain unpublished. Bitcoin bridge considered not yet safe for mainnet.

- **Ledger Live Native Integration**: Custom tool available; Ledger Manager approval pending. Full Ledger Live integration timeline dependent on upstream approval process.

- **Oracle USDT Dependency**: All ERG price pairs currently USDT-denominated. Diversification to native Ergo Oracle Pools planned but not yet funded or built out.

- **EF Decentralization Transition**: Glasgow indicated that post-6.0 roadmap completion, community governance and associations are expected to take over core development. No concrete timeline or governance framework defined.

- **Useful Proof of Work**: EF exploring partnership for UPoW experimentation. Problem consistency, solution verification, and emission model if a problem is solved remain unresolved questions.

- **SigmaUSD Viability**: Circulating supply collapsed to under 600,000. Will conditions normalize, or will Dexy Gold effectively replace SigmaUSD as the primary Ergo stablecoin?

- **EIP-41 Stealth Address Status**: kushti noted needing to check status; no follow-up confirmed by month-end.

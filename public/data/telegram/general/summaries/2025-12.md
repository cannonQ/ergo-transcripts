# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: December 2025

---

## Executive Summary

December 2025 was a technically dense and philosophically rich month for Ergo, dominated by three converging themes: the near-completion of sub-blocks (Matrix Protocol) and faster block research as core scalability infrastructure, the live deployment and early performance of the USE algorithmic stablecoin, and sustained community work on cross-chain expansion via Rosen Bridge. Community governance tensions surfaced toward month-end, prompting structural discussions about leadership distribution and Sigmanauts moderation standards.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Sub-blocks (Matrix Protocol)
kushti spent the month progressing sub-blocks from ~95% core code complete (W49) to devnet deployment (W50) and active peer testing (W51–W52). The protocol targets mining pools and solo miners forming peer-to-peer sub-networks, with lock-in voting requiring 90%+ hashrate support before extension fields become mandatory. A whitepaper was promised "within days" by mid-month but remained in peer-review circulation by month-end, with public discussion planned for early January. Construction details were also committed to the ErgoForum. The community debated whether sub-blocks should be prioritised over layer 2 and sidechain optimisations; mx and Grayman considered them critical infrastructure for p2p cash; kushti noted parallels with Bitcoin Cash's TailStorm.

### USE Stablecoin Launch & Performance
USE contracts and offchain bots went live in W49, with UI deployment following shortly after. By W52, USE was demonstrating stronger peg stability than SigmaUSD despite holding 4× fewer reserves, with a growing reserve ratio trajectory. Minting is rate-limited to 1% of bank reserves per 12 hours (~2,000 ERG equivalent). MewFinance deployed a native USE/CLB liquidity pool on MewDex, and USE tracking went live on ErgoExplorer. Rosen Bridge minimum transfer fee for USE was reduced to $5.00 (0.1% above 5,000 USE). Community discussion identified USE and Dexy Gold as the same token with different naming conventions across frontends.

### Faster Block Times Research
A whitepaper for faster blocks was nearing completion by W52, with devnet testing in progress and peer invitations planned for the new year. Significant implementation concerns were raised: dApps relying on block height rather than timestamps would be affected by increased block speed; emission contracts and difficulty adjustment logic require careful parameter adjustment. kushti noted a dedicated testnet exists for parameter tweaking (difficulty level, block time) and invited community members with minimal coding knowledge to participate in testing and scientific observation. No consensus was reached on optimal block time targets.

### Rosen Bridge Expansion
Firo partnership was announced in W49 for decentralised cross-chain access to Ethereum, BNB Chain, and Cardano. By W52, three additional chains—Firo, HNS (Handshake), and CKB (Nervos)—had formally requested Rosen integration, with Kadena expressing interest for 2026. Armeanio framed Rosen as enabling p2p market access without external censorship risk, and the team emphasised that multichain usage should not be viewed tribally: companies using only partial aspects of the Ergo ecosystem still benefit the overall positioning.

### Palmyra & Chainlink Oracle Partnership
Dan Friedman detailed a plan for on-chain warehouse receipts supporting decentralised trade financing: producers deposit commodities into bonded warehouses, receive on-chain receipts issued via Chainlink proof-of-reserve oracles coupled with IoT devices (weight sensors, motion sensors, cameras) for continuous auditing. Chainlink was chosen for network size and support breadth, but the plan explicitly includes building out native Ergo oracles using capital from an ongoing token sale. The partnership was framed as non-exclusive, with Ergo oracles (Oracle Pool v1 and v2 running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards) earmarked for expansion.

### Stability Workshop & Academic Output
Seven peer-reviewed papers were accepted for the 1st Stability Workshop (15 December, LNM Institute, Jaipur, India), co-presented by kushti and Bruno Woltzenlogel Paleo among others. Papers covered: algorithmic stablecoin design (USE/Dexy), ChainCash elastic supply money creation, Gluon W stabilisation protocol, decentralised oracles (Orb), perpetual prediction pools (Fate Protocol), and reward distribution (RainDrop). kushti gave an opening speech at an associated hackathon. A separate Twitter Spaces on "algorithmic stablecoins: past, present and future" was proposed for 14 December at 3 PM UTC.

### Storage Rent, Token Economics & Demurrage
Data from sigmaspace.io showed tokens adding a significant percentage of value to storage rent profits for miners. Discussion on EIP-33 demurrage-based token burning found no strong consensus; miners opposed token burning. The broader monetary context shifted in W52 when Ergo's block reward reduction brought inflation below both ADA and USD inflation levels—Richi highlighted demurrage as the mechanism that will "truly shine" in this low-inflation environment. Bitcoin's UTXO bloat from Ordinals/Stamps was cited as a live case study validating Ergo's storage rent design.

### Community Governance & Sigmanauts
kushti proposed a "partial leadership" model with distributed leaders across five areas: core dev, tooling, research, community growth, and funding—characterising "funding & research" as currently in chaos. A Sigmanauts meeting on 28 December (11 attendees across 4–5 continents) addressed chat moderation standards, enforcement of community-voted guidelines, Finance review, Paideia infrastructure, and mining pool monitoring. Significant interpersonal conflict between kushti and Glasgow around moderation decisions led to kushti elevating a Sigmanauts account to admin. Future meetings shifted to 12:00 UTC to better include Asia timezones.

### MewFinance Ecosystem Development
MewDex launched native USE liquidity pool with deposit/redeem functionality; Ergopay support flagged as forthcoming. ErgoSanta 2025 airdrop event launched with a $1.2k pool tied to letter-writing participation and DEX trades on MewDex. CruxFinance acquisition by HQΣr was announced with plans to revive the platform. A web wallet integrating all Mew services was under active development, with Ronin cited as a UX reference for side-panel design over popup. The MewFinance Burn dApp (burn.mewfinance.com) went live with leaderboard rewards.

### Lithos Protocol v2.0 & Testnet
Lithos Protocol v2.0 was released with GPU mining optimisation and a testnet token airdrop for supporting miners. By W52, v2.2.0-test was available on GitHub, with pre-built binaries distributed via Telegram for users facing sbt build failures. Grayman raised ASIC-resistance concerns in the context of recent failures of ASIC-embracing chains (Kadena referenced).

### Infrastructure & Metadata Updates
ErgoForum was successfully rebuilt at its permanent hostname ergoforum.org (W51), resolving a period of redirect instability; a 302 strategy was adopted for future migrations. Nautilus Wallet v1.3.2 was released with updated logos for USE, CLB, and DORT tokens. Rosen Bridge Watcher updated to v6.0.2 and watcher-app to v3.7.0, adding USE and NIGHT token support. The testnet explorer was migrated to new servers. Yulius Kristianto updated Ergo's social links and explorer references across 13 major cryptocurrency information sites, and submitted Javanese translation PRs across four Ergo repositories. Ergo Explorer funding was flagged as having approximately 2 months of runway remaining.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Josemi, 2025-12-15): What was the final policy decision on EIP-33 regarding demurrage with tokens?
  **A** (@kushti): No strong consensus — miners opposed token burning in particular. Data from sigmaspace.io shows tokens adding significant value to storage rent profits but collection is early. Current policy remains unresolved.

- **Q** (@Josemi, 2025-12-15): Should early DeFi have standardised on wrapped ERG rather than native token support?
  **A** (@kushti): Both approaches have trade-offs. Using wERG everywhere requires an additional input step (converting ERG to wERG), making contracts more verbose, but provides unified handling. The native token choice was somewhat accidental — driven by early DeFi product decisions (ErgoDex and others) rather than deliberate architecture.

- **Q** (@Giufa OnliאΣ, 2025-12-15): Testnet node won't synchronise after 24 hours; "no peers available" error.
  **A** (@qx()): Use known peer list: `knownPeers = ["213.239.193.208:9023","176.9.15.237:9021","128.253.41.110:9020"]` with reemission activation height 100000001 and standard testnet voting parameters.

- **Q** (@Anonymous, 2025-12-15): Is Ergo quantum-resistant?
  **A** (@kushti): No. Ergo uses the same elliptic curve cryptography as Bitcoin and shares identical cryptographic assumptions.

- **Q** (@Anonymous, W52): At what point would Ergo consider quantum computing a practical threat rather than theoretical? What measurable indicators would trigger protocol changes?
  **A** (@kushti): "Most likely we are still decades away talking about 128-bit secure ECC. I don't know any developer in cryptocurrency space who is proficient enough in quantum computing to make predictions — specialists provide very different estimates."
  **A** (@Armeanio): Conservative approach recommended — wait for standardisations and widespread adoption. "No urgent need to pick a horse in the race for now, give it a few years."

- **Q** (@huskar, W52): How do I mint USE rather than swap it on DEX?
  **A** (@Richi): bank.mewfinance.com or crystalpool.cc (listed as DexyUSD).

- **Q** (@huskar, W52): Is there a minting limit for USE within a time period?
  **A** (@Richi): Yes — 1% of bank reserves per 12 hours, approximately 2,000 ERG worth at current reserves.

- **Q** (@huskar, W52): Is Dexy USD the same as USE?
  **A** (@Richi): Yes — same token; the name discrepancy is a frontend naming decision by the developer.

- **Q** (@Manolo, W52): Pre-built Lithos client zip for testnet v2.2.0 unavailable; GitHub releases only have source; sbt build fails on leveldbjni-all 1.18.3.
  **A** (@ArØhβΣ): Pre-built release at https://github.com/Lithos-Protocol/Lithos-Client/releases/tag/v2.2.0-test; contact Lithos Telegram for direct binary.

- **Q** (@LifeOfIFA, W52): Can you explain how USE keeps its peg better than SigmaUSD, which has "never lost its peg"?
  **A** (@Ant): "Algorithmic stablecoins when faced with extreme volatility have traditionally taken a while to absorb market pressure. USE design mechanics aid in absorbing said volatility quickly; potentially making algorithmic stablecoins scalable and decentralised."

- **Q** (@Ant, 2025-12-05): How are assets not mediums of exchange?
  **A** (@Grayman): Assets with utility (silver, cigarettes) distort monetary performance due to supply/demand dynamics in the denominator. Money functions as the lowest common denominator in a socio-political system; money and assets serve distinct roles in market context.

- **Q** (@fireman625 via DiscordBridge, W50): Has anyone created decentralised insurance on Ergo? Would work well with Oracle Pools.
  **A** (@bob): Posted ErgoForum design for optimistic oracle handling singular events; acknowledged Oracle Pools are insufficient for event-based decisions. Proposed design avoids additional tokens, uses SigmaUSD or USE as collateral, and seeks community builders: https://ergoforum.aap.cornell.edu/t/optimistic-oracle-on-ergo/5292

- **Q** (@cannon_q, W50): Can Dexy, SigmaUSD, or Gluon accept any asset as collateral, as DuckPools v2 now allows?
  **A** (@Bruno Woltzenlogel Paleo): Shared detailed article on multi-asset backing challenges: https://news.stability.nexus/the-challenges-and-problems-of-backing-by-a-basket-of-assets-8211ce8362ba

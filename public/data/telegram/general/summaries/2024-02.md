# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: 2024-02

---

## Executive Summary

February 2024 was dominated by three converging themes: community-funded CEX listing campaigns (Xeggex and MEXC), active development across multiple DeFi protocols (Dexy Gold, ChainCash, SigmaUSD), and foundational governance restructuring toward a decentralised DAO model. The month also saw significant technical discourse around stablecoin manipulation vectors, sub-blocks progress, and Rosen Bridge cross-chain expansion, alongside a successful 28,518 ERG community fundraise for the MEXC listing completed in under five days.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### CEX Listing Campaigns (Xeggex & MEXC)
The community ran two sequential crowdfunding campaigns for exchange listings. The Xeggex integration fee ($2,500) was raised within 24 hours via ergoraffle.com, with 2,200 ERG collected and listing confirmed by mid-February. The MEXC campaign was substantially larger — a 28,000 ERG (~$70k) target raised via Ergo raffle, completing in approximately five days with 567 contributors raising 28,518 ERG (101.85% of goal). Surplus fund allocation (buffer for future listings vs. contributor refund) remained unresolved at month's end. A separate Kraken fundraising group was established, though no formal raffle was launched. Discussion of market maker (MM) engagement ran in parallel: five MM proposals with differing fee structures (flat monthly vs. percentage cuts with call options) were reviewed, with community consensus leaning toward flat-fee structures for budget predictability. A parallel Hummingbot-based community MM strategy was proposed as a decentralised alternative.

### SigmaUSD Manipulation & Protocol Mechanics
A significant "BearWhale" event dominated the final week: a large actor minted approximately 540,000 SigmaUSD at a high Reserve Ratio (~800%), sold ERG to depress the price, then redeemed at profit. This prompted sustained discussion of mitigation strategies including per-oracle-epoch minting limits, time locks, and dividend mechanisms proposed by Bruno Woltzenlogel Paleo (protocol author). kushti confirmed that five multisig keys control SigmaUSD contract updates (held by kushti, Armeanio, and others), with plans to eventually burn keys. SigmaRSV was repeatedly characterised by kushti as a short-term speculation instrument only, not a HODL vehicle. Reserve Ratio dropped from ~800% to ~600% as community actors responded. No consensus on a specific mitigation approach was reached before month's end.

### Dexy Gold & Oracle Pool v2
Dexy Gold moved from bootstrapping to private UI testing across the month, with public testing expected imminently by late February. Gold Oracle Tokens (GOT) were auctioned at a 2,000 ERG opening bid, with 16 of 32 tokens distributed to early participants. Winners receive governance Ballot tokens and eligibility for GORT rewards (minimum 1 per hour, up to 32 oracle participants). The buyback contract structure was clarified: Dexy Gold swap fees feed a contract that purchases GORT from the DEX, creating upward price pressure. Per-day minting limits were mentioned as a design feature to prevent manipulation analogous to the SigmaUSD BearWhale attack. Oracle Pool v1 structure (EF members and outside participants) and v2 (running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards) were both active.

### Rosen Bridge & Cross-Chain Development
Rosen Bridge continued cross-chain expansion with Bitcoin chain implementation, EVM RPC extractor development, Bitcoin UTXO selection logic, and guard service WID hash fixes in progress. rsERG became usable as collateral on Liqwid Finance (Cardano), with the rsERG/ADA LP on Spectrum DEX yielding approximately 4–8% APR despite significant impermanent loss. Catalyst funding (Cardano treasury, not VC) was confirmed for the EVM bridge proposal, with a six-month post-funding delivery timeline. The Watcher cap of 77 was noted, with expansion expected at EVM bridge launch. Community members debated whether Rosen Bridge liquidity flows reduce Ergo mainchain TVL — correctly identified as not the case, since ERG remains locked on-chain until the wrapped token is returned.

### Governance & DAO Restructuring
kushti's departure from the Ergo Foundation was confirmed in late January, and February saw the formal proposal of an ErgoDevs R&D DAO for protocol research, development, and off-chain infrastructure maintenance. The Ergo Foundation's marketing team was restructured without a single dedicated lead; governance now involves EF, Sigmanauts, and the proposed core/ecosystem dev DAO. Sigmanauts weekly strategy included Paideia token distribution for contributor onboarding, treasury expansion, mobile app integrations (Terminus, Mosaik dApp framework), and market maker outreach. CW was announced as Chief Meme Officer, funded personally by Armeanio rather than the EF.

### Sub-Blocks, Scalability & Protocol Development
Sub-blocks remained a recurring community demand throughout February. kushti confirmed active development, including a sidechain prototype to incentivise miners and P2P message infrastructure for testnet. A community testnet for block parameter experimentation (difficulty, block time) was re-opened at ergo.rkdn.app. The relationship between block height-dependent contracts and faster block times was identified as a non-trivial migration concern. kushti noted that concrete optimisation is more tractable on Ergo than on empty chains, given the existing set of deployed contracts (Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, Trade House, lending contracts) providing a realistic performance baseline. Sub-blocks were deferred to a post-Ergoversary roundtable for formal roadmap discussion.

### ChainCash & Alternative Monetary Models
ChainCash white paper mechanics generated substantive discussion, particularly around non-backed note issuance incentivising meme coins and the enforceability of off-chain collateral redemption. kushti clarified that ChainCash is a P2P network of interacting subnetworks: users self-select risk exposure across fully-backed (Hawala-style), fractional-reserve, and community LETS models. Redemption reliability is enforced by user acceptance decisions and network reputation rather than on-chain guarantees. A separate LETS concept was highlighted as a potentially "disruptive" dApp for non-crypto-native mass adoption, with draft contracts available from a prior ErgoHack.

### Palmyra & Chainlink Integration
Dan Friedman's Chainlink partnership for the Palmyra project was discussed across multiple weeks. The integration uses Chainlink's proof-of-reserve functions via a proxy function in the Winter Protocol to issue on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralised trade financing. IoT devices (cameras, weight/motion sensors) provide continuous asset verification. The first batch of tokenised Ceylon cinnamon was delivered as a real-world asset milestone. Dan explicitly addressed community tribalism concerns, noting that Ergo being a "supplier of smart finance for all chains" via Rosen Bridge requires welcoming partial ecosystem adoption rather than demanding all-Ergo deployments.

### Wallet & Infrastructure Updates
Satergo v1.7.0 launched with light node support, chained transactions, stealth address sending, and improved design. Minotaur v2.0-rc1 was released as a testing release candidate. Ledger support porting to a new SDK was confirmed, with approximately two weeks estimated for completion (not delivered within February). The Mosaik framework in Ergo Mobile was discussed as a superior UX approach vs. browser-in-wallet. iOS Terminus App Store resubmission was underway. Nautilus wallet issues (malformed transaction errors, box size limits from LP/Farm token accumulation) were troubleshot in-chat. SigmaSpace, a custom Go-based indexer/explorer developed by a single developer over 1–2 years, launched at sigmaspace.io.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2024-01-29): Is a 50/50 ASIC/GPU algorithm like Flux viable for Ergo?
  **A** (@HQΣr): Multiple algorithms on the same chain create chaos — how do you allocate transactions per algorithm?

- **Q** (@Rj, 2024-01-30): Why not fixed 3-minute blocks instead of 2-minute average with 10+ minute spikes?
  **A** (@kushti): Block time distribution is statistically symmetric on both sides; the practical experience is almost identical to fixed block times.

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2024-02-05): Who secures Ergo — miners or node operators? Thought Bitcoin is node-secured.
  **A** (@Glasgow): Both PoW chains are secured by miners; nodes only enforce consensus rules. Miners vote on parameter changes; if not unanimous, a fork occurs. Economic activity ultimately determines which chain wins.

- **Q** (@Misfit Toy, 2024-02-04): What are GORT, Ballot tokens, and GOT? How many were sold?
  **A** (@qx()): GORT = contract-released rewards distributed to oracle runners; Ballot tokens = governance rights for oracle runners; GOT = the auctioned token you bid on (starting 2,000 ERG). Ballot token delivered with purchase; 1 GORT required in wallet to participate (can be donated). 16 of 32 tokens distributed initially.

- **Q** (@Misfit Toy, 2024-02-04): Is Dexy Gold price fixed at 2,000 ERG?
  **A** (@qx()): The bid started at 2,000 ERG. Once Dexy Gold is live, swap fees feed a buyback contract that purchases GORT from the DEX, creating upward price pressure.

- **Q** (@Jesper Agger, 2024-02-26): In ChainCash, doesn't allowing note issuance without collateral incentivise meme coins? How do off-chain assets get redeemed if bad actors refuse to pay?
  **A** (@kushti): ChainCash operates as a P2P network with interacting subnetworks — users choose their own risk level (fully backed, fractional reserve, or community LETS). Redemption is enforced by user acceptance decisions and reputation, not on-chain guarantees.

- **Q** (@MGpai, 2024-02-29): Can the SigmaUSD bank script be updated completely? Who holds update power?
  **A** (@kushti): Five multisig keys control updates, held by kushti, Armeanio, and others. Plan to burn keys eventually but not yet completed.

- **Q** (@MavΣrickBg, 2024-02-14): Nautilus wallet throwing "Malformed transaction: Every input of the transaction should be in UTXO" error on all transaction signing attempts.
  **A** (@qx()): Check which node server you're connected to. If using a Cyberverse or NFT-heavy wallet, LP and Farm tokens may share a UTXO causing box size limit violations — send them to yourself in a separate transaction to split the boxes first.

- **Q** (@JMASTERJ, 2024-02-25): How to acquire native ERG without bridging or KYC? Can MetaMask be used?
  **A** (@Affable Villain): Use Simpleswap to swap ETH or other assets for native ERG into an Ergo wallet (Nautilus, Ergo Mobile, or Terminus). MetaMask cannot hold ERG natively; a MetaMask Snap integration is in development. Once Rosen Bridge EVM support launches, wrapped ERG will be available on EVM chains.

- **Q** (@Patato, 2024-02-19): Can mining occur on a pruned full node?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes, if running in UTXO mode.

- **Q** (@OxCapitulation, 2024-02-18): Why not fully decentralise MM via scaled grid bots on DEX with fee incentives to LPs instead of hiring a third-party MM?
  **A** (@qx()): Limited by secure smart contract implementation capability at present. Phase one: gather Hummingbot metrics this week; refine proposal for decentralised alternative in parallel.

- **Q** (@Rj, 2024-02-10): Does bridging ERG to Cardano via Rosen Bridge decrease Ergo's TVL?
  **A** (@Rj, self-answered with community confirmation): ERG is locked on mainchain. To retrieve it you must send the wrapped token back from Cardano to Ergo, at which point the lock is released — TVL is not reduced.

- **Q** (@Velvia, 2024-02-29): Can bulletproof verification be implemented in ErgoScript?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes, this is being actively explored. Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge technique allowing specific statements to be proven without a trusted setup — a trustless alternative to SNARKs. Groth one-out-of-many and similar protocols could be verified inside Ergo contracts, enabling hybrid contracts where sensitive data is proven off-chain but verified on-chain without disclosure.

- **Q** (@vidicmu, 2024-02-10): What is the lowest-fee path to acquire ERG from ADA?
  **A** (community): ADA → rsERG via bridge → ERG via Spectrum DEX is currently best. For small orders (~1,000 rsADA), Spectrum plus bridge fees are already cheaper than Simpleswap; for 10,000 ADA swaps, the saving is approximately 300 ERG over Simpleswap.

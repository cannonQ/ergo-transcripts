# Ergo General — October 2021 Monthly Summary

---

## Executive Summary

October 2021 was a foundational month for Ergo, dominated by three converging themes: long-term economic sustainability (emission retargeting, storage rent), ecosystem infrastructure maturity (ErgoDEX launch, Ledger support, Ergo Pay, wallet development), and community growth following ErgoHack II. Debates around miner incentives post-emission and the post-ETH 2.0 hashrate migration landscape gave the month a strategic, forward-looking character, while practical tooling and dApp development moved rapidly toward mainnet readiness.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Emission Retargeting and Long-Term Miner Incentives

The most sustained technical and economic discussion of the month centred on Ergo's emission schedule. With the treasury contract set to end at block 655,200 and the original 8-year emission window raising questions about long-term miner incentivisation, kushti formally proposed a soft-fork at block 640,000 that would divert a portion of current block rewards (from the 67 ERG–24 ERG range) into a re-emission contract, extending the emission timeline without altering total supply. Community discussion examined storage rent (currently 0.14 ERG per UTXO box per 4 years) as a complementary long-term revenue stream for miners, with debate over whether current parameters are sufficient or need adjustment. No consensus was reached on final parameters; a dedicated community chat was announced but not yet held by month end. The interplay between emission prolonging and the anticipated post-ETH 2.0 hashrate influx gave the discussion added urgency.

### Post-ETH 2.0 Mining Landscape

Ergo's position as a primary destination for GPU miners displaced by Ethereum's transition to Proof of Stake was a recurring theme across all four weeks. Community members estimated approximately 393 TH/s from 30-series GPUs alone could migrate to Ergo, and the network's current capacity to absorb roughly 1% of ETH hashrate (scaling to ~750 TH/s ETH equivalent) was discussed. EIP-4345's confirmation of further difficulty bomb delays was noted as affecting timing. The importance of aligning any emission schedule changes with the ETH 2.0 transition was emphasised to ensure competitive miner rewards during the critical hashrate absorption window. Discussions of Intel GPU mining were discounted due to unknown performance and supply timelines.

### ErgoDEX: Launch, Governance Token, and Community Confusion

ErgoDEX dominated community discussion in weeks 41–42 after its beta went live at beta.ergodex.io and a governance token announcement caused significant price volatility and confusion. Multiple participants and moderators spent considerable effort clarifying that ErgoDEX is an independent organisation building on Ergo — analogous to Uniswap's relationship to Ethereum — and that ERG remains the transaction fee token on the DEX, not a governance token for ErgoDEX itself. ERG pairs with SigmaUSD and SigmaRSV became available on beta. UI v2 launch was delayed pending a stable Yoroi dApp Connector release, with full Ergo mainnet launch expected late October and Cardano deployment targeted for early November. The AMM pool contracts and order book design were discussed, with the Trade House order book model praised for full slippage control and transparent depth.

### ErgoScript, Smart Contract Education, and ErgoHack II

ErgoHack II concluded with results announced around October 20th. Projects included ErgoLend (uncollateralized trust-based micro-lending), Ergo Casino, a Graviton ETH bridge, Ergo Audio (on-chain music streaming), Ergo Subpooling, and approximately 20 solo projects. The ErgoLend team initiated weekly internal Thursday knowledge-transfer sessions covering boxes, registers, proxy contracts, and ErgoScript fundamentals, with plans to open these to the broader community. kushti confirmed the ergoscript-by-example repository as the canonical starting point for developers, emphasising that ErgoScript is a deterministic functional subset of Scala with no loops. A scalability blog post was published covering zk-rollups, NIPoPoWs, and Layer 2 options.

### Infrastructure: Ergo Pay, Ledger, Wallets, and Oracle Pools

Ergo Pay (EIP-37) was explained as enabling dApps to construct complete transactions for wallet signing via QR code or link, already implemented in the Android wallet and critical for ErgoDEX integration. Android wallet v1.4 shipped with cold wallet/air-gapped signing functionality. Ledger integration reached alpha (developer-only) with the Tesseract side complete and Yoroi integration in progress — no public ETA. iOS wallet development was underway as a port of the Android wallet, with ViaWallet as an interim option. A new ETH/USD oracle pool launched on Ergo, complementing the existing ERG/USD pool, and Luivatra published a setup guide for running oracle pool nodes. Oracle Pool v2 was noted as running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards.

### Sidechains, Velvet Forks, and Scaling

kushti explained the distinction between velvet soft forks (activated by any noticeable hashrate, used to improve NIPoPoWs) and normal soft forks (requiring majority). A just-in-time (JIT) costing soft fork was confirmed for approximately 5x faster script execution, simplifying the execution engine. Merge-mined sidechain constructions were described in detail: mainchain miners earn both ERG and sidechain tokens; emission contracts reward miners for delivering sidechain data; trustless pegging is achieved via smart contracts, representing an improvement over BIP 301. Ergo.meta (a unified L2/sidechain framework) was confirmed as in R&D with concrete development expected in 2022. The UTXO Alliance was noted as a potential source of a new cross-chain framework.

### SigmaUSD, Djed, and Stablecoin Mechanics

The relationship between SigmaUSD and Djed (COTI's KYC AgeUSD implementation for Cardano) was clarified: SigmaUSD is Ergo's non-KYC, ERG-backed implementation of the same AgeUSD protocol, with kushti's name on the Djed paper. SigmaRSV V1/V2 incompatibility continued to cause user confusion; old.sigmausd.io was explained as locked until V1 SigmaUSD holders withdraw. The 2% fee on both minting and redeeming was clarified as flowing to reserves, increasing SigmaRSV value. SigmaUSD availability cross-chain was noted as dependent on bridge readiness.

### Ergo Foundation Incorporation and Governance

Ergo Foundation non-profit incorporation in Singapore was confirmed as in progress, estimated to complete within a couple of weeks of late October. The legal entity was described as providing counterparty liability protection for business partners, assisting with exchange listings, and enabling formal agreements where a pure DAO structure is insufficient. The fair launch tokenomics were clarified: no initial token allocation or presale; the Foundation receives 4.5% of block rewards programmed into the emissions contract, representing a miner donation. Treasury ends at block 655,200, after which the Foundation relies on previously accumulated funds and dApp UI fees.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Aberg, 2021-W41): Why does Ergo have such a short emission schedule compared to Bitcoin? Concerned about miner incentives after emission ends.
  **A** (@Glasgow, @kushti): Storage rent (0.14 ERG per UTXO box per 4 years) provides a recurring revenue stream for miners indefinitely. Additionally, a soft-fork proposal is under discussion to divert a portion of current block rewards into a re-emission contract, extending the emission timeline while preserving total supply. Simply moving your ERG once every four years resets the storage rent timer; wallets will implement simple "renew" functions.

- **Q** (@unknown, 2021-W41): How does emission retargeting enforce miner participation? What prevents miners from not remitting to the re-emission contract?
  **A** (@kushti, 2021-W41): If a majority of miners adopt the updated rules, blocks with improper payments to the re-emission contract are rejected by the network. In practice, pools use the node's wallet, which handles this automatically.

- **Q** (@unknown, 2021-W40): How does the eUTXO model handle smart contract scalability differently from account-based chains?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @kushti): Only smart contract validation happens on-chain in Ergo's eUTXO model; other contract logic is executed off-chain and can be decentralised via bots. This architectural difference provides significant scalability advantages over account-based models where all logic executes on-chain.

- **Q** (@Tommy, 2021-W40): Is there any option to earn yield on ERG?
  **A** (@Gazza): No native staking — ERG is PoW. Liquidity provision on ErgoDEX beta earns fees. A staking solution is in development (ergoforum.org/t/a-solution-for-staking/1057). Future tokenisation of dApps will enable staking-like functionality.

- **Q** (@user, 2021-W43): How does ErgoScript relate to Scala?
  **A** (@kushti, @Gazza, 2021-10-28): ErgoScript is a super-primitive, deterministic functional subset of Scala with no loops. It is better to start directly with ErgoScript rather than learning full Scala, since ErgoScript-specific features must be learned regardless. Recursion is handled via fold operations; see arxiv.org/pdf/1806.10116.pdf for the formal treatment.

- **Q** (@unknown, 2021-W41): What is the TPS of Ergo?
  **A** (@CW, Glasgow): TPS is not a meaningful metric for eUTXO chains — thousands of transactions can be batched into a single block. Actual throughput depends on transaction size and complexity. Current block time is approximately 2 minutes. Scaling improvements including JIT costing, sub-blocks, and Layer 2 solutions are active development priorities.

- **Q** (@Steven, 2021-W40): How does merge mining work from a miner's perspective on Ergo sidechains?
  **A** (@kushti, 2021-10-09): Miners earn both ERG and sidechain tokens. An emission contract rewards mainchain miners for delivering sidechain data. Trustless pegging is achieved via smart contracts, similar to but more secure than BIP 301 because Ergo can natively recognise sidechain data. Sidechain constructions will be published to the forum soon.

- **Q** (@Mo A, 2021-W40): How is Ergo different from Cardano in terms of interoperability?
  **A** (@A, @CW): Both use the eUTXO model; core Ergo devs are former Cardano developers and both teams worked jointly on SigmaUSD. The Gravity network provides interoperability. The SuSy bridge is eUTXO-compatible; it awaits Cardano PAB/wallet backend. Planned liquidity pools (ERG:ADA, ERG:AGIX, etc.) will enable cross-chain swaps.

- **Q** (@Kevin, 2021-W43): What is the relationship between Djed and SigmaUSD?
  **A** (@kushti, @Glasgow, 2021-10-29): Djed is COTI's KYC implementation of the AgeUSD protocol for Cardano. SigmaUSD is Ergo's non-KYC, ERG-backed implementation of the same protocol. kushti's name appears on the Djed paper. SigmaUSD will be available cross-chain once bridges are complete.

- **Q** (@unknown, 2021-W43): Can a velvet fork improve NIPoPoWs without majority miner consensus?
  **A** (@kushti, 2021-10-30): Yes. Velvet soft forks are activated by any noticeable fraction of hashrate, making them lower-barrier than standard soft forks. NIPoPoWs are already in the protocol but can be improved via velvet fork as hashrate grows.

- **Q** (@BOTNIQ, 2021-W41): Why can't I interact with my SigmaRSV on ErgoDEX?
  **A** (@Glasgow): SigmaRSV purchased before May 2021 is the V1 contract, which is incompatible with the current V2 system. The old contract at old.sigmausd.io remains locked until a V1 SigmaUSD holder withdraws. New users should only use V2 at sigmausd.io.

- **Q** (@unknown, 2021-W40): Is Ergo vulnerable to the same DDoS/mempool spam attack seen on other chains?
  **A** (@kushti, @Glasgow): Ergo has a fee market; users can raise the minimum fee rate in their node config to prevent spam transactions from entering the mempool. From v4.0.44, the reference client limits transaction downloads from any single peer. Mempool spam transactions time out and are flushed if not picked up by miners. A fee estimation API exists in the node but is not yet widely used by wallets.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Emission retargeting parameters**: No final decision on specific block reward reduction amounts, activation block, or miner voting mechanism. Community chat announced but not yet held.
- **Storage rent parameter adjustment**: Whether 0.14 ERG per 4 years is sufficient for long-term miner incentivisation remains unresolved; kushti's earlier proposal of a 3-year threshold was previously rejected.
- **ErgoDEX Cardano launch**: Targeted early November but dependent on Yoroi dApp Connector stability; no hard date confirmed.
- **Ledger public release**: Alpha complete; Yoroi integration in progress but no ETA for public availability.
- **iOS wallet**: Android wallet port in progress; no fixed release date. ViaWallet as interim.
- **Ergo Foundation Singapore incorporation**: Expected within weeks of late October; not yet confirmed complete.
- **ErgoData sidechain**: EFYT holder distribution promised if technically feasible at launch, but sidechain design and data types remain undefined.
- **Ergo.meta / L2 framework**: R&D phase only; concrete implementation expected sometime in 2022, potentially influenced by UTXO Alliance collaboration.
- **Exchange listings**: Discussions confirmed under NDA; no timeline or names disclosed. Foundation incorporation cited as a potential prerequisite.
- **Ergo oracle integration with Cardano**: Dependent on Cardano adding data inputs support; no timeline given.
- **Emission prolonging vs. storage rent sufficiency**: Underlying modelling question — whether storage rent alone can sustain long-term miner rewards — remains unresolved and will drive November discussion.

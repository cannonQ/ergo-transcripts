# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: February 2025

---

## Executive Summary

February 2025 was dominated by two major technical milestones: the activation of the 6.0 soft-fork on public testnet (with sub-blocks as the headline feature) and the live deployment of Rosen Bridge's Binance Smart Chain integration. Alongside these, a wave of infrastructure releases — Ergo reference clients 5.0.25 and 5.1.2, SigmaSDK 5.0.15, Minotaur Wallet 2.4.0, and progress on Ledger hardware wallet support — signalled broad ecosystem maturity. Strategic discussions around eUTXO dApp developer experience, monetary innovation (demurrage, ChainCash, programmable reserve assets), and Ergo's positioning relative to Cardano partner chains and CEX listings rounded out a dense and productive month.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### 6.0 Soft-Fork & Sub-blocks

The 6.0 soft-fork was the dominant technical thread of the month. Voting began on public testnet in Week 6 (780,000 votes recorded), RC2 was released in Week 7, and by Week 9 the soft-fork had activated on testnet with protocol v4 blocks. Sub-blocks are the flagship feature: they enable faster block production by building a DAG of sub-blocks rather than storing only the best chain. Implementation requires P2P layer support, stratum proxy modifications, and DAG storage changes — placing the public testnet approximately 10–12 weeks out from Week 6. First API methods for sub-blocks were implemented during the month, though currently only suitable for local miner-based DevNets. A security audit fundraiser was launched via Ergo Raffle. Kushti identified technical risks: sub-blocks improve propagation but increase forking probability and adversarial attack surface at the sub-block level; block rewards must exceed sub-block rewards, and tighter script cost limits are likely needed.

- [@kushti, msg#544639, 2025-02-06]: Public testnet voting for 6.0 underway — 780,000 votes recorded.
- [@kushti, msg#546115, 2025-02-15]: 6.0 soft-fork activated on public testnet.

### Demurrage, Storage Rent & Monetary Innovation

A recurring theme throughout the month was Ergo's demurrage mechanism and its relationship to long-term miner security. @LouisLibre reframed demurrage not as a fee but as a use-it-or-lose-it incentive that redistributes profits to miners — holders who mine break even net-net; those who don't are diluted. The maximum demurrage increase via miner vote is capped at 2× the current default. An existing EIP draft (PR#93 by Huginn, dating to 2022) proposes even distribution of demurrage (e.g., 1 ERG/block via a separate emission contract) to avoid block-to-block variance, but remains unmerged. By Week 9, spontaneous Twitter adoption of demurrage terminology was noted, and kushti scheduled a March 13–14 Twitter Spaces on "Programmable reserve assets, and quest for better money." The emission reduction milestone was confirmed approximately 40 days away from early March. ChainCash was discussed as a transparent free banking alternative, with its paper and contract updates ongoing.

- [@kushti, msg#544751, 2025-02-06]: Demurrage cap confirmed — maximum 2× current default via miner vote.
- [@kushti, msg#548460, 2025-03-02]: Mar 13–14 Twitter Spaces on programmable reserve assets announced.

### Rosen Bridge Expansion (BSC & Bitcoin Runes)

Rosen Bridge's BSC integration progressed from final testing in Week 7 to live deployment by Week 9, with rsBNB/ERG trading pairs bootstrapped on MewDex via MewFund LP campaigns. Watcher v4.1.1 was made mandatory for all operators to capture rsBNB-related events and qualify for rewards. Separately, R&D on Bitcoin Runes integration with Rosen Bridge concluded (Week 6), with rate-limit concerns noted but development confirmed to proceed. A detailed article explored how Rosen Bridge could enrich Bitcoin Runes with metadata capabilities. Monero bridge R&D also underway. Kushti noted institutional investors in Dubai are reportedly separating PoW assets from other asset classes, adding strategic context to cross-chain expansion.

- [@Armeanio, msg#546888, 2025-02-20]: Watcher v4.1.1 update mandatory; v2.2.1 also released for Watcher UI.
- [@Rosen Bridge, msg#547434, 2025-02-25]: Rosen Bridge–BSC integration live; rsBNB/ERG pair trading on MewDex.

### Ledger & Keystone Hardware Wallet Support

Ledger integration made substantial progress: firmware nearly complete with minor fixes, Rust SDK and JavaScript Base SDK done and awaiting review, and fuzzing tests submitted for Ledger App Store approval. By Week 9, the Ergo Ledger app PR was merged to the main repository, expected to appear in the next Ledger release. The existing 20-token-per-transaction limit on Nano S was identified as a significant constraint; the new Ledger app version raises this to 100 tokens. Ledger Nano S deprecation was flagged as a gap. Keystone hardware wallet support was announced for March 2025 with discounted pricing for late adopters, providing an alternative signing path.

- [@kushti, msg#547905, 2025-02-27]: Ledger Ergo app PR #11 merged; expected in next Ledger release.
- [@HQΣr, msg#547780, 2025-02-26]: Keystone hardware wallet support shipping March 2025.

### eUTXO dApp Developer Experience

A sustained thread across Weeks 6–8 addressed the structural barriers to dApp proliferation on Ergo. @c8 outlined a cost reduction pathway: from ~$20–30k per dApp (current state) to $2–3k via reusable reference implementations, then to $200 via open source culture and copy-paste economics. The critical missing infrastructure identified was a standalone ErgoScript Language Server Protocol (LSP) with Debug Adapter Protocol (DAP): without "go to definition," utility libraries cannot be shared across projects and developers rediscover the same solutions in isolation. @kii emphasized writing extensive tests in Scala before moving to JavaScript for production ErgoScript development. The agentic wallet pattern — wallets as agents managing retry loops (e.g., "swap ERG → SigmaUSD within these parameters") — was proposed by @c8 as a UTXO-native design that enables private orders until execution and removes reliance on proxy contracts.

### ErgoScript Security & Verification

@c8 provided a clear articulation (Week 8) of why ErgoScript and the eUTXO model are structurally more secure than account-based smart contract languages: **(1) Immutability by default** — once a multisig address is created it cannot be modified; **(2) Radical simplicity** — any developer with a few weeks of training can decompile any ErgoTree contract back to readable source and verify behaviour. The practical security guarantee: even if a dApp website is compromised, users remain safe if they verify token proportionality directly in their wallet. This was framed as Ergo's answer to the Bybit-style multisig failures affecting other ecosystems.

### Market Making, CEX Strategy & Cardano Partner Chains

Debate continued throughout the month on CEX vs. DEX liquidity allocation. Centralised exchange market-making at approximately $5k/month (across MEXC, HTX, KuCoin) was compared against Rosen-native DEX liquidity. HTX was noted as practically useful for high-volume, KYC-free stacking (5 BTC/day limit). Kushti confirmed direct talks with Dave from Kraken regarding FAKU token airdrop and listing possibilities, though no commitment was made. Listing fees from a referenced Bybit pricing sheet were speculatively discussed at $1M–2M+. Separately, Charles Hoskinson publicly mentioned Ergo as a potential Cardano "partner chain" leveraging its superior consensus mechanism — the community discussed this could mean Rosen-based interoperability, dual merge-mined sidechains, shared reserve assets, or NIPoPoWs-based light client integration, but no formal specification exists.

### MewFinance, Open-Source Philosophy & Fee-Gated dApps

@HQΣr (developer of both MewFinance and ErgoExplorer) defended MewFinance's closed-source frontend and fee structure against community criticism, drawing a distinction between UI licensing and smart contract transparency. A philosophical debate emerged: closed UIs are arguably acceptable if open backup skeleton UIs (like ergfi.xyz) exist; closed contracts are a higher-order concern. @c8 raised the competitive risk of forks operating at 0% fees. The broader principle discussed: the Ergo ecosystem needs sufficient open-source tooling that any dApp's smart contracts remain independently verifiable and usable regardless of frontend availability.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@A P, 2025-02-03): Can you explain the 6.0 soft-fork like I'm five years old?
  **A** (@kushti): 6.0 enables voting on sub-blocks count; sub-blocks deploy *after* 6.0 activates. Sub-blocks DevNet already testable. See: https://www.ergoforum.org/t/6-0-soft-fork-proposal/5005

- **Q** (@kii, 2025-02-06): If demurrage increases 2×, can miners vote another 2× in future? Or is 2× the absolute maximum?
  **A** (@kushti): "Max stop limit is 2×."

- **Q** (@kii, 2025-02-06): Are sub-blocks a sidechain or layer 2? Can people build on sub-blocks?
  **A** (@kushti): Sub-blocks are neither a sidechain nor layer 2 — they are a block production optimisation. Applications continue to build on Ergo L1 regardless of sub-block deployment.

- **Q** (@Grayman, 2025-02-06): Does sub-blocks introduce network latency concerns?
  **A** (@kushti): Sub-blocks improve P2P propagation but increase forking probability and adversarial possibilities at the sub-block level. Block reward must exceed sub-block rewards. Tighter limits on sub-block sizes and script costs are likely needed. Verification dilemma and bribing attacks are identified risks.

- **Q** (anonymous, 2025-02-10): To learn ErgoScript, do I need to learn Scala first?
  **A** (@kushti): "No. ErgoScript is like 100× easier. And a different paradigm — you are composing a logical statement for a spending condition."

- **Q** (anonymous, 2025-02-10): If I'm not a coder, what language should I start with before ErgoScript?
  **A** (@c8): "All things considered, JavaScript. If you know you will never build a user interface and enjoy staying in a console, then Scala. You need basic knowledge of a language first; ErgoScript second."
  **A** (@kii): "JavaScript and ErgoScript is the way to go — it's more universal. Scala is very useful for writing extensive tests to find bugs in your contracts."

- **Q** (@kii, 2025-02-10): What is LSP, and why does ErgoScript need it?
  **A** (@Aberg): "A Language Server — provides syntax highlighting and completion suggestions."
  **A** (@c8): "The critical part is 'go to definition' — it enables utility libraries. Without that we will forever stay in one script file and rediscover the same solutions. LSP AND DAP (so you can debug)."

- **Q** (@Rj, 2025-02-22): Is multisig on Ergo more secure than approaches like Bybit's, and how can it be built more securely?
  **A** (@c8): Yes — two factors: **(1) Immutability by default** — once a multisig address is created it cannot be compromised by modification. **(2) Radical simplicity** — any hobby developer with a few weeks of training can decompile any ErgoTree contract back to readable source and verify behaviour. Even if a dApp website is hacked, you remain safe by verifying token proportionality directly in your wallet. ErgoScript multisigs are "the most fool-proof and secure way to manage large sums."

- **Q** (anonymous, 2025-02-22): Where can hobby developers begin learning to review ErgoScript contracts?
  **A** (@c8): Download the most recent Ergo JAR from GitHub and run a full indexed node. It's non-trivial — ask questions in the process and report issues; this directly improves onboarding documentation.

- **Q** (anonymous, 2025-02-22): How does the Ledger token limit affect UTXO consolidation?
  **A** (@Aco Šmrkas): Ledger is a signing tool only; Nautilus handles consolidation. The Nano S has a 20-token-per-transaction limit. Workaround: send 10 tokens + 0.001 ERG per box rather than all ERG at once. The new Ledger App version raises the limit to 100 tokens.

- **Q** (anonymous, 2025-02-27): Is there a risk of double-spend over short time horizons on Ergo?
  **A** (@c8): Value-dependent. Approximately 2 seconds average-case is acceptable for Ergo given RBF mechanics. A transaction unconfirmed for more than ~2 minutes remains replaceable via RBF; mempool timeout is approximately 8 minutes typical.

- **Q** (anonymous, 2025-02-28): Can the API key hash be changed in node config for security hardening?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes — set a new key hash in the node configuration file.
  **A** (@Patato): Change the API key hash in node config; that is sufficient.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Sub-blocks production timeline**: P2P layer support, stratum proxy modifications, and DAG storage implementation remain in progress; public testnet approximately 10–12 weeks from early February — now approximately 6–8 weeks out.
- **Sub-block script cost limits**: Whether tighter limits are needed and at what values — not yet formalised.
- **Demurrage even distribution (EIP PR#93)**: Huginn's draft from 2022 remains unmerged; implementation approach and governance path TBD.
- **ErgoScript LSP/DAP**: Critical tooling gap identified but no developer committed to implementation; no bounty posted.
- **CEX listing pathway**: Kraken talks initiated with no commitment; Binance remains a community goal with no official pathway; listing fee estimates ($1M–2M+) unconfirmed.
- **Rosen Bridge maturity threshold for DEX-only liquidity**: No consensus on when Rosen is "proven enough" to reduce CEX market-making spend.
- **MewFinance open-source debate**: Unresolved philosophical question on whether closed-source UIs are acceptable when contracts are open and backup interfaces exist.
- **Cardano "partner chain" definition**: Charles Hoskinson's mention remains informal; no technical specification for what Ergo–Cardano interoperability would entail.
- **Sidechain construction publication**: Kushti indicated merge-mined sidechain designs and trustless relay mechanics "soon to be published" on the forum — no date given.
- **Block time acceleration research**: No formal RFC exists; kushti seeking community contributors to run testnet experiments with modified parameters.
- **Monero bridge viability**: In R&D; no timeline or feasibility conclusion.
- **Bitcoin aggregated Schnorr deployment**: Technology not yet available on Bitcoin; timeline unknown for enabling trustless BTC unwrapping with large watcher sets.

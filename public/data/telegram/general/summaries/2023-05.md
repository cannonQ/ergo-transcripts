# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: May 2023

---

## Executive Summary

May 2023 was dominated by three converging themes: the imminent storage rent activation (~July 2023), significant protocol-level progress on Rosen Bridge and sidechain constructions, and sustained community debate about Ergo's economic design (emission reduction, demurrage, oracle infrastructure). Developer tooling matured across multiple fronts—Auction House V3 launched, DuckPools neared private testing, and Spectrum DEX completed CoinMarketCap/CoinGecko integration APIs—while foundational research into bulletproofs, NIPoPoWs, and block speed scaling generated substantive technical discussion throughout the month.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Storage Rent: Mechanics, Branding, and Monetary Policy

Storage rent activation dominated community education efforts across all four weeks. The activation block is 1,051,200 (~July 2023), after which boxes inactive for 4+ years become claimable by miners. Rent is charged by output size in bytes—not ERG value—meaning a box holding SigmaRSV without sufficient ERG coverage will be claimed. The standard consolidation fix is to send tokens to oneself, creating a fresh UTXO.

A deeper conceptual debate emerged in W20: @louissignet argued that "storage rent" undersells the feature as mere technical bookkeeping when it is in fact a *monetary policy mechanism*—a form of demurrage ensuring Ergo is the only chain guaranteeing truly fixed *circulating* supply (not just fixed emission). Suggested rebranding options included "Inactivity Fee," "Idle Fee," "Demurrage," and "Fixed Supply Commission," though no consensus was reached. @cafebedouin and @Benny noted that demurrage—the cost of holding currency—is historically accurate terminology. @kushti reinforced that storage rent solves UTXO set growth (referencing Gavin Andresen's 2015 "UTXO uh-oh" post) and enables genuine dust collection, unlike Bitcoin where BRC-20 inscriptions create indefinite bloat.

Community anxiety about UTXO expiration was widespread, requiring repeated clarification on tokenjay.app age tracking (which displayed incorrect ages for some wallets), box consolidation mechanics, and the fact that mining consistently to a wallet does not prevent rent without periodic consolidation.

### Emission Reduction and Mining Economics

@kushti confirmed the next emission reduction—from ~39 ERG to ~3 ERG per block—is expected around July 9, 2023. This triggered substantive discussion about network security. The consensus position from @kushti and @qx() was that with constant external demand, a 13x supply reduction should produce a ~13x price increase, maintaining similar hashrate economics. Additional miner revenue sources—storage rent, MEV, and potential FIMOs (Fair Initial Mining Offerings)—further support the transition. @kushti noted that in 2–3 years, the community complaint will likely reverse: emission will be seen as *too low* rather than too high.

@Mint LkV raised a concern about businesses holding capital during the current ~30% circulating supply inflation period. @kushti acknowledged the trajectory but pointed to the improving emission curve. The EIP-27 reemission merge was confirmed live in W18: miners now pay rewards to pay-2-reemission contracts, and the reemission merge script consolidates these boxes.

### Rosen Bridge Development

Rosen Bridge made consistent progress throughout May. The architecture uses a dual-layer verification model: Watchers monitor external chains and notify Guards on Ergo; Guards independently verify events before releasing funds. Only a multisig wallet exists on other chains, minimising the attack surface compared to full smart contract bridges. @qx() described this as "Fort Knox on Ergo + a guarded safe on the other chain."

By W21, Rollup integration was nearing completion and executable binaries for Mac/Windows/Linux were forthcoming. Cardano/Koios network testing was complete. @MHS_SAM confirmed the Watcher now includes a "redeem commitment" feature for commitments that fail to merge into an event trigger. Community questions about when ADA/ERG pools would appear on Spectrum DEX received the answer: still in testing phase. SNEK and BANK token bridge support was proposed for marketing purposes, with discussion moved to the dedicated Rosen Bridge Telegram channel.

### Sidechain Constructions

@kushti confirmed throughout May that sidechain constructions would be published soon, with a paper and code already prepared awaiting frontend development. Ergo sidechains will be merge-mined, allowing miners to earn both ERG and sidechain tokens simultaneously by delivering sidechain data to the Ergo mainchain. An emission contract rewards mainchain miners for this delivery. Trustless pegging will be handled via smart contracts—analogous to BIP-301 but with potentially clearer security guarantees. @kushti framed this as a significant opportunity for CPU/GPU miners to participate in multiple chains while maintaining Ergo mainchain security. This remained a "soon" commitment through month-end with no firm publication date.

### Block Speed Scaling and Performance Improvements

Discussion of potential block time reductions recurred across weeks. @qx() raised the concern that many dApps—particularly SigmaFi bonds and time-dependent contracts—assume a ~2-minute average block time, meaning premature maturation would occur if blocks speed up. @kushti identified existing drafts for sub-blocks and microblocks and proposed reviving community discussion post-Ergoversary, noting that the current concrete set of on-chain contracts (Rosen Bridge, Oracle Pools, Spectrum DEX AMM pools, Trade House, lending contracts, SigmaUSD) provides a good benchmark for optimisation that was previously unavailable. A testnet channel exists for parameter experimentation; @kushti invited interested community members to DM and join.

The key insight: it is now easier to consider layer 2 solutions and faster confirmation protocols because Ergo has a defined, real application set with similar design patterns—unlike chains that deployed sharding and parallel chains before having any meaningful load.

### Trade House Order Book Model

@qx() provided detailed explanation of Trade House's significance across W18–W19. Order book DEXes offer total transparency of market depth and liquidity levels, full user control over slippage, and enable arbitrage opportunities without impermanent loss. Partial order fills are supported through UTXO duplication: a sell order box is duplicated when partially filled, with the leftover in a new box using the same parameters. No single upgrade key (unlike Serum on Solana, which lost admin keys post-FTX). Multiple upgrade keys possible. @qx() contrasted this with AMM liquidity provision and encouraged community to "highly recommend" Trade House. Discussion of potential Spectrum liquidity sharing with Trade House orders was raised.

### Bulletproofs and Zero-Knowledge Proofs

@kushti discussed integrating bulletproof verification into ErgoScript contracts. Bulletproofs provide trustless zero-knowledge proofs without a trusted setup—an alternative to SNARKs. They enable range proofs (as used in Monero) and generic statement proofs. The goal is to have verification done in ErgoScript, enabling hybrid contracts that combine ZK statements with on-chain execution—allowing contracts to verify sensitive data without revealing it. @kushti noted this opens "a whole new world of possibilities" combining Ergo contracts with zero-knowledge statements. Groth one-out-of-many proofs (used in early Zcoin) were also mentioned as candidates.

### Babel Fees UX

@louissignet demonstrated successful Babel Fees onboarding—sending SigmaUSD to a fresh wallet and having the recipient transact without any ERG. However, a UX issue in Nautilus was identified: users must select the Babel fee token *before* adding assets to send, otherwise an ERG error appears. @anon_br clarified that the minimum ERG for box creation comes from the Babel swap itself, not the user's wallet. @Capt_Nemo noted a library is being built to simplify Babel Fees for dApps, but adoption remains limited.

### SigmaFi Non-Recourse Lending Model

Clarification was provided that SigmaFi implements P2P loans without automated price-triggered liquidations—only missed payments trigger action. @dayumbbbb proposed rebranding with "No Forced Liquidation" messaging, suggested front-page copy: "Crypto Loans on Your Terms: No Liquidations, No Worries." Bond terms discussed ranged from 12–20 months at 30–50% APR. @Glasgow confirmed SigmaBonds Loan Offers (lender-initiated bond offers) were ready for beta testing in W18. Auto-liquidation via off-chain bots is under development as an optional feature—not retroactive, not mandatory.

### Oracle Pool Infrastructure

@kushti explained Oracle Pool v2: allows oracle members to rotate by selling their NFT position, provides more flexible rewards, and both Dexy Gold and SigmaUSD Gold protocols will use buyback contracts that benefit oracle operators. @Dan mentioned potentially using capital from the Palmyra token sale to build out Ergo oracle infrastructure, noting only ~2 active oracle pools currently exist. The Palmyra/Chainlink integration uses Chainlink proof-of-reserve for on-chain warehouse receipts in Winter Protocol, with IoT devices (cameras, weight/motion sensors) providing continuous physical commodity verification—complementary rather than competitive to Ergo oracles.

### Ledger Hardware Wallet Controversy

Ledger's new "Recover" feature—which splits and exports seed phrases to third parties—generated significant community concern. Consensus was that this breaks the hardware wallet trust model. @FlyingPig committed to not updating Nano X firmware. Alternatives discussed: Trezor (awaiting Ergo's top-50 ranking), Tangem (integrating Ergo), and using Ergo Mobile as a cold wallet. No resolution on expanded hardware wallet support for Ergo.

### Privacy: Transparent L1 vs. Privacy-by-Default

Discussion in W21 coalesced around the view that transparent L1 with privacy on L2 is preferable to privacy-by-default (Monero) for institutional adoption. ETH/XMR atomic swaps unveiled at Monerotopia were noted as enabling XMR as a private L2. Monero's regulatory exposure prevents institutional capital access—a structural disadvantage. Ergo's approach was seen as better positioned for mainstream viability.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Crypto Chad, 2023-05-04): How is SigmaRSV price determined? What produces "1 ERG = 2162 SigmaRSV"?
  **A** (@Glasgow): `rcRate = equity / rcCirc` where `equity = baseReserve - liabilities`, and `liabilities = min(baseReserve, usdErgRate × scCirc)`. Default fallback rate applies if equity/circulation is zero. Formal spec in the AgeUSD Smart Contract Protocol.

- **Q** (@dayumbbbb, 2023-05-04): Potential threshold function bug in Minotaur `deriveNewMultiSigWalletAddress` setting minimum to 1-of-n.
  **A** (@MHS_SAM, confirmed by @kushti): Not an issue. Multisig wallets store `minSig` as a number in the database seed column, not as hex. @kushti inspected generated addresses and confirmed correct thresholds (e.g., 4-of-7). Funds are safe.

- **Q** (@Pulsarz, 2023-05-05): Would Lightning Network on Ergo work only for ERG transfers, not smart contracts or tokens?
  **A** (@kushti): Correct—Lightning on Ergo is for transfers only. Other applications use different constructions; Hydra is more suitable for smart contract use cases.

- **Q** (@Stasis, ~2023-05-10): What is the difference between "gas" and "transaction fee"?
  **A** (@qx()): Ethereum gas = tx complexity × price per unit, making total fee variable and potentially prohibitive. Ergo has a flat fee regardless of complexity—0.001 ERG for 1,000 addresses is the same as for 1 address.

- **Q** (@Allen, ~2023-05-11): What makes Rosen Bridge different from other smart contract bridges?
  **A** (@qx()): Rosen does the heavy lifting on Ergo using dual verification (Watchers + Guards). The other chain only holds a multisig wallet—minimal attack surface. Analogy: Fort Knox on Ergo, a guarded safe on the other chain.

- **Q** (@OxCapitulation, ~2023-05-12): Tokenjay shows 3.8-year-old boxes in a 1-year-old wallet. Does resending ERG reset the timer?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Tokenjay age display is incorrect for some wallets. Resending creates a new UTXO but does not reset the old one. Correct fix: send tokens to yourself to create a fresh UTXO consolidating everything.

- **Q** (@louissignet, ~2023-05-15): Can you send tokens using only Babel Fees with zero ERG in wallet?
  **A** (@anon_br, @Capt_Nemo): Yes—but Nautilus has a UX issue: select the Babel fee token *before* adding assets to send, or you receive an ERG error. The minimum ERG for box creation comes from the Babel swap itself, not the user's wallet.

- **Q** (@community, ~2023-05-18): Will hashrate remain stable after emission drops to ~3 ERG per block?
  **A** (@kushti, @qx()): With constant external demand, a 13x supply reduction should produce ~13x price increase, maintaining similar hashrate incentives. Additional revenue from storage rent, MEV, and FIMOs further supports miner economics.

- **Q** (@Aberg, ~2023-05-20): How do merge-mined sidechains work for miners?
  **A** (@kushti): Miners earn both ERG and sidechain tokens by delivering sidechain data to the Ergo mainchain. An emission contract on Ergo rewards mainchain miners for this. Trustless pegging is handled via smart contracts, analogous to BIP-301 but with clearer security guarantees.

- **Q** (@dayumbbbb, 2023-05-27): Why do mempool transactions sometimes disappear unexpectedly?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Transactions are only dropped if their inputs are spent in another transaction or a data input is consumed. The Ergo Explorer mempool view can show an inconsistent state, but nodes retain transactions per standard behavior until a conflict or expiry.

- **Q** (@community, ~2023-05-10): Can code minification reduce ErgoScript space on-chain?
  **A** (@Aberg): No—ErgoScript is already compiled into ErgoTree on-chain, not stored as source code. Minification is a web/JavaScript concept with no equivalent in this context.

- **Q** (@community, ~2023-05-05): What is Ergo's practical uniqueness vs. other L1s?
  **A** (@kushti): Tooling for adoption is largely ready—P2P lending (SigmaBonds/EXLE), lending pools (DuckPools), DAOs (Paideia), multisig (Minotaur), auctions (Auction House). Recently facilitated a real loan to a Kenyan farmers' cooperative via SigmaUSD + EXLE. Multiple L2 designs available. ChainCash represents an optimal balance between on-chain eUTXO logic and off-chain efficiency—a design space other chains have not explored.

- **Q** (@community, ~2023-05-10): How does spam protection work in Ergo given the flat fee model?
  **A** (@kushti): Reference client v4.0.44+ limits transaction downloads from a single peer, preventing mempool flooding. Node operators can raise the minimum propagation fee rate to prevent spam from even entering their mempool. Fee estimation API exists in the node but is not yet widely used by applications. In practice, if legitimate users raise fees above the spam floor, miners prioritise them and spam transactions eventually time out of the mempool.

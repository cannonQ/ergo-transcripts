## Executive Summary

June 2024 was dominated by three converging themes: major infrastructure milestones (Rosen Bridge Bitcoin support launch, Sigma 6.0 roadmap confirmation, Ledger app nearing completion), a highly productive ErgoHack VIII with nine strong submissions and community voting, and substantive ecosystem expansion through new DeFi primitives, cross-chain integrations, and the Ergoversary content release. The month closed with rsBTC/SPF lending pools going live on DuckPools and a large coordinated content release planned for July 1.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Sigma 6.0 Protocol Upgrade
Kushti confirmed the Sigma 6.0 roadmap: finalization July 5–15, testnet activation and review in August, mainnet voting proposal late August–early September, and activation November–December. The upgrade enables Lithos Protocol, trustless Bitcoin relays, and efficient rollups. Alongside this, community discussion explored throughput improvements including sub-blocks (microblocks), faster confirmation protocols, and sidechains — but no consensus was reached on implementation path. Kushti invited motivated community members to help coordinate parameter-tweaking experiments on testnet 6.0, noting that block time reductions would affect timestamp-dependent dApps and emission contracts.

### Rosen Bridge Bitcoin Integration
The Bitcoin-to-Ergo/Cardano bridge went live mid-June with UI support confirmed Friday June 14. All Rosen Watcher operators were required to update to version 3.0.0 (UI: 2.2.0) by June 18 UTC or risk losing permits. The expansion roadmap announced by the Rosen team includes an EVM adaptor, Bitcoin Runes support, Monero R&D, RosenFast service, Bridge Expansion Kit, Bridge SDK, Doge/BCH support, and Hummingbot integration. rsBTC pools launched on DuckPools by end of June with lending, borrowing, trading, and arbitrage functionality.

### ErgoHack VIII Results & Community Vote
ErgoHack VIII ("Ergo as Smart Layer") concluded with nine submissions exploring Bitcoin functionality augmentation and cross-chain tooling. Community voting on Paideia DAO distributed 18k SigmaUSD, 100k RSN, and 6,000+ ERG. Final results: 1st — Sigmanauts Mining BTC Swap; 2nd — Crystal Pool; 3rd (tied) — DuckPools & SigmaRand; 5th (tied) — Analog Ergo & Wallet Wonderland; 7th — Rosenport; 8th — Moria Finance; 9th — Huangians. Video presentations were released throughout the week following the hackathon.

### Crystal Pool & Order Book DEX Design
C8's Crystal Pool emerged as a standout ErgoHack project: a self-custodial exchange with a centralized batcher enabling near-CEX-like UX while maintaining on-chain settlement. The design allows composable liquidity sharing with Spectrum AMM pools and Trade House order books. Broader discussion throughout the month emphasized the advantages of order book DEXes (Trade House model) over AMMs: full slippage control, transparent depth, partial order fills, and avoidance of impermanent loss. The composability of these primitives was highlighted as a key Ergo DeFi differentiator.

### Palmyra (Zengate) & Chainlink Partnership
Zengate announced a Chainlink integration for the Winter Protocol, using Chainlink's proof-of-reserve oracle feeds for on-chain warehouse receipts supporting decentralised trade financing. IoT devices (cameras, weight/motion sensors) coupled with Chainlink provide continuous commodity auditing. Armeanio and other community members engaged in meta-discussion about multi-chain partnerships, arguing that tribalistic "Ergo-only" reactions undermine ecosystem growth — noting that Palmyra is and will remain primarily Ergo-native while pragmatically leveraging best-available tooling. Plans exist to use token sale proceeds to build out Ergo Oracle Pools infrastructure.

### Ergoversary & Community Content (July 1)
The month closed with a major coordinated Ergoversary content release planned for July 1, including ecosystem updates from Rosen Bridge, Trade House, Palmyra, Paideia, and Lithos Protocol; research from Dionysis Zindros and Lukas Aumayr on fork choice rules and light clients; Gluon Gold and Djed Alliance content from Bruno W; Nervos guest content on RGB++; the first P2Podcast episode; and kushti presenting on Sigma Trees and "The Next 5 Years of Ergo."

### Spam/DDoS Resistance & Fee Markets
Following Cardano's mempool spam attack, discussion examined Ergo's defenses. Ergo's fee market, mempool filters, p2p layer protections (since v4.0.42), and fee estimation API provide layered protection. Node operators can raise minimum propagation fee rates to block spammers from the mempool entirely. Transactions that are not picked up eventually flush from the mempool. Kushti noted the fee estimation API exists but is underutilized by wallets and dApps.

### Ledger Integration
Glasgow confirmed the Ledger app for Ergo has completed requested audit changes and is awaiting final approval before exiting dev mode, after approximately three years of development. The pull request was visible on GitHub.

### Demurrage Automation & Fee Markets
A proposal explored pre-signed consolidation transactions and secondary markets for "future fee" tokens to automate demurrage avoidance without requiring active wallet management. The model involves wallet provider and mining pool cooperation with on-chain proof of performance. Incentive structure discussed included pools selling "future option" tokens at a premium and profiting from fee collection; viability depends on on-chain honoring mechanisms.

### Gluon Gold SDK Raffle
The Ergo Raffle for Gluon Gold SDK development was successfully funded at 100.32% (4,645 ERG raised from 36 contributors). A separate 200 ERG raffle for rsBTC bonus rewards on the Sigmanauts mining pool was also fully funded by six contributors.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@HelixEvo, 2024-06-03): Node receiving high volume of `/utxo/byid/` requests from external domain — malicious activity?
  **A** (@kushti): Likely a dApp that discovered the node's public API; common occurrence, not a problem.

- **Q** (@Pgr456, 2024-06-06): Pre-signed consolidation TXs for demurrage automation — feasibility and incentive structure?
  **A** (@dayumbbbb): Wallet recreates pre-signed TXs after each spend and caches with provider. Pool sells "future option" token at premium; profits from collecting fees today vs. waiting 4 years. User hedges against future fee inflation. Risk: wallet provider downtime, pool exits business. Countervailing incentive: all non-honoring is recorded on-chain, making future tokens worthless.

- **Q** (@BTCMini, 2024-06-07): How to explain Rosen Bridge's superiority over WBTC or Portal?
  **A** (@Glasgow): WBTC is custodial; Portal is an atomic swap, not a bridge.
  **A** (@kushti): Rosen Bridge uses double-layered multisig. No known trustless ERG↔BTC transfer method exists; MPC aggregation is theoretically possible but provides marginal practical advantage.

- **Q** (@Daskre, 2024-06-11): Why do blocks from a specific miner show repeated identical timestamps?
  **A** (@kushti): Likely server time desynchronization. If the first miner used a future timestamp, subsequent miners add milliseconds to match; mining pools typically use NTP.

- **Q** (@Luivatra & @kushti, 2024-06-18): Can Crystal Pool orders share liquidity with Spectrum LPs? How do context extension reads work in ErgoScript?
  **A** (@kushti): ChainCash uses context extension reads for FSM-like processing (action IDs encode output paths). Sigma 6.0 adds the ability to read context extensions of another input. Grid trading orders are made compatible with Spectrum LPs via contract design for composability.

- **Q** (@HQΣr, 2024-06-23): Can a CIP-68-like updateable token metadata system be implemented on Ergo — mint once, update registers without changing token ID?
  **A** (@kushti): Possible, but requires off-chain scanner modifications. "You can have a box committing to a database and then set update rules in its contract." An EIP proposal would be useful for standardization.

- **Q** (@Rj & @Pulsarz, 2024-06-18): Is Rosenport like the Lightning Network for cross-chain transfers? Would it need pre-bridged liquidity?
  **A** (@Pulsarz): No pre-bridging required. Rosenport is a batching contract (bus) that collects transactions up to a liquidity limit, then processes them in bulk. Slower than direct bridging but cheaper for small amounts. Users can exit before the batch departs; edge-case risk of fund lock if batch breaks in mempool.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-06-25): How does Ergo handle DDoS beyond hashrate increases?
  **A** (@kushti): Fee market prevents spam economically. Mempool filters allow node operators to raise minimum propagation fees. P2P layer protections since v4.0.42 prevent a single peer from flooding its neighbours. A fee estimation API is available but underutilised. Replace-by-fee is available by default, which is more flexible than Bitcoin's implementation.

- **Q** (@Alex, 2024-06-16): In one sentence, what is Ergo's vision? Short-term target? Why conservative marketing?
  **A** (@qx()): Vision = "smart money (eUTXO) built with true resiliency (PoW) creating unstoppable financial tooling for anybody." Short-term goal = create a real system and store of value. Target users = those suffering inflation or corruption, not speculators. Marketing strategy focuses on tools and base-layer adoption via projects and teams, not influencer hype; adoption success = price appreciation.

- **Q** (@Adrián, 2024-06-26): Cannot access ERG wallet with seed phrase from Ergo Wallet app.
  **A** (@MavΣrickBg, @Mewbie): Check each word against the BIP-39 wordlist; a single character typo causes total wallet mismatch. Adrián recovered 540 ERG after identifying the typo.

- **Q** (@Saphuza, 2024-06-03): What is an oracle box? Where is the ID located?
  **A** (@qx()): An oracle box is a UTXO holding collateral and/or tokens. The ID is the box ID, inspectable via the Ergo Explorer token interface.

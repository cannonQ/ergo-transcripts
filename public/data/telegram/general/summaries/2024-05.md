# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General
**Month:** 2024-05 (Weeks 18–22)

---

## Executive Summary

May 2024 was defined by three converging themes: the completion of foundational Bitcoin interoperability primitives (trustless relay, Rosen Bridge BTC keygen), continued progress toward Sigma 6.0 activation, and growing ecosystem maturity signalled by ErgoHack VIII, MEXC listing, and exchange listing applications to Binance, Kraken, and Coinbase. Community discourse deepened around Ergo's monetary narrative ("Forever Money," demurrage-as-options-contracts) while exposing ongoing tensions around privacy wallet UX, VC accessibility, and early miner wealth distribution.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Sigma 6.0 Development
kushti confirmed Sigma 6.0 as the singular development priority for 2024, targeting November activation per Lithos team request. The update delivers (de)serialization support for all types (benefiting rollups, sidechains, and Lithos), nbits encoding for trustless hashrate derivatives, and groundwork for post-6.0 compressed Sigma protocols and Bulletproofs verification in ErgoScript. The last major non-trivial implementation piece — mod Q operations and big integer semantics — began in mid-May. Sub-blocks, ChainCash persistence, and p2p networking polish were all formally deferred to post-6.0. Contributors were actively solicited across Rust, Go, and TypeScript.

### Trustless Bitcoin Relay & Rosen Bridge BTC Support
The month's most significant technical milestone: kushti completed and tested a trustless Bitcoin relay contract in ErgoScript, including transaction inclusion Merkle proof validation ([Draft PR #962](https://github.com/ScorexFoundation/sigmastate-interpreter/pull/962)). This enables trustless verification of Bitcoin PoW and transaction inclusion directly on Ergo — a core primitive for cross-chain contracts. Concurrently, Rosen Bridge completed successful keygen ceremony tests for the Bitcoin bridge and initiated the Guard keygen ceremony, with mainnet BTC bridge launch described as imminent by end of the month. Rosen Bridge also submitted Catalyst Fund 11 Milestone 3 covering multi-sig mechanisms (ECDSA/TSS) for Ethereum/EVM bridges.

### ErgoHack VIII: "Ergo as a Smart Layer"
ErgoHack VIII launched 2024-05-24 with a prize pool of approximately $40k: $9k/$5k/$4k top prizes, 500 ERG from kushti's raffle (8,462 ERG total raised by Sigmanauts), 100k RSN, and 1,000 CKB from Nervos CKB for a dedicated UTXO-based lending protocol task. The Bitcoin interoperability theme attracted 12+ projects including Lightning Swaps, CrystalPool (self-custodial exchange), OptionPools, Moria Finance (rsBTC tooling), Ephemeral Token Messaging, and a Reputation System using Runes. The Nervos CKB collaboration generated community debate over cross-chain resource allocation vs. ecosystem dilution.

### Demurrage, Storage Rent & "Forever Money" Narrative
An extended philosophical thread ran across all five weeks reframing Ergo's monetary properties. @dayumbbbb articulated demurrage not as a fee or tax but as an **options contract** — miners may execute against UTXOs inactive for 4+ years, but are not obligated to. This framing was proposed as a distinctive marketing angle: "Blockchains, Infinitely Scalable," with demurrage preventing both memory explosion (via storage rent) and supply implosion. By W21, community consensus coalesced around positioning Ergo as "Forever Money" — fixed 100M supply combined with a shrinking UTXO set over time, contrasting with Bitcoin's approaching-zero tail emission and Ethereum's unbounded supply. No formal Foundation directive adopted this framing during the period.

### MEXC Listing & Exchange Outreach
ERG/USDT launched on MEXC (ranked 11th on CoinMarketCap, 10M+ users, 170+ countries) on 2024-04-29, with the Sigmanauts organising an Ergo Raffle raising over 28,000 ERG and the Ergo Foundation providing market-making funds. By W21, the Ergo Foundation had submitted formal listing applications to Binance (under their new merit-based framework for small/medium-cap projects), Kraken, and Coinbase. No timelines or acceptance indicators were provided. Community also flagged suspicious 2% spreads and potentially fake liquidity on Gate.io; no formal investigation or exchange response was documented.

### Rosen Bridge & Palmyra Ecosystem
Rosen Bridge security audit passed with official report pending (W19). The Bitcoin Runes compatibility feature entered design phase with potential to bring Runes tokens onto Ergo. Palmyra (ZenGate's project) confirmed that the PALM token will launch as a Cardano native asset (bridged to Ergo via Rosen Bridge) in mid-2025 due to compliance requirements, with a 2-year vesting period. Armeanio detailed Chainlink integration for proof of reserve and on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralised trade financing — IoT sensors (weight, motion, camera) verify commodity reserves in bonded warehouses, with receipts issued on-chain. Plans to build out native Ergo Oracle Pools using token sale proceeds were announced.

### Privacy Wallet UX Gap
A recurring thread across W20–W21 identified a structural gap: no wallet adequately combines fine-grained spending/change address controls with dApp connector support, meaning mixer privacy gains are destroyed by downstream wallet behaviour. Nautilus was cited as privacy-destroying by default; SAFEW attempts control but has bugs and limited dApp support; Minotaur supports all three required features (choose sender addresses, choose change address, multi-address sends) in desktop mode. No resolution or consolidation path was agreed.

### Dexy Protocol & Gluon Gold Progress
Dexy LP contracts were confirmed for imminent deployment in W19, pending update key holders before bank contracts could follow. Oracle Pool v2 for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards was referenced but exact mechanics and launch timing remained unclear. Gluon Gold's testnet identified one smart contract bug (PR #10, fixed) and began multi-sig treasury development (PR #9); mainnet ETA remained TBD through end of month.

### Early Miner Wealth Distribution Debate
A contentious discussion in W22 questioned whether approximately 12M ERG mined in 2019–2020 via superior private mining software should be subject to a hard fork and redistributed for ecosystem benefit (e.g., Binance listing fees). Counter-arguments emphasised: fair market competition, developer failure to provide optimal public miner, storage rent already capturing idle value, and the precedent risk of breaking the social contract. No consensus was reached; kushti recommended a community vote mechanism; the broader issue was flagged but not formalised into a governance proposal.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@dayumbbbb, 2024-05-05): What is demurrage, really? Is it a fee or a tax?
  **A** (@dayumbbbb): Demurrage is an **options contract**, not a fee or tax. Miners *may* execute against a UTXO if it has been inactive for more than 4 years, but are not obligated to. Every UTXO is inherently an options contract with miner strike eligibility after 4-year inactivity. Creation height can also be manually set to a past value to bring forward eligibility.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-05-13): If demurrage parameters are adjustable by miners, couldn't a 51% attack set the interval to zero seconds and instantly seize all coins?
  **A** (@Pulsarz): Network voting requires 90% consensus, not 51%. (@dayumbbbb): Parameters have hard upper and lower bounds — miners would theoretically push to limits via game theory, but the limits exist precisely to prevent this scenario.

- **Q** (@Rj, 2024-05-06): ELI5 Sigma 6.0?
  **A** (@kushti): (De)serialization of all types, nbits encoding, and support for trustless ZK verification including Bulletproofs and compressed Sigma protocols. See EIP-100 PR for full details.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-05-18): Can you explain what Bulletproofs are?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge proof technique allowing specific statements (e.g., range proofs) to be proven without a trusted setup — making them trustless alternatives to SNARKs. They are a specific form of Sigma protocol and can enable verification in ErgoScript, opening the possibility of hybrid contracts where sensitive data is verified on-chain without disclosure.

- **Q** (@Pulsarz, 2024-05-06): Is Dexy on hold pending Sigma 6.0?
  **A** (@kushti): No. LP contracts deploy this week; bank contracts follow once update key holders are available.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-05-19): Can wallets support: (1) choose sender addresses, (2) choose change address, (3) multi-address sends?
  **A** (@Unknown): SAFEW attempts this but has bugs and poor dApp connector support. Minotaur supports all three in desktop mode. Terminus mobile supports change address control but not multiple sender addresses.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-05-18): What are the implications of reducing block time for contracts based on block height?
  **A** (@kushti): Contracts tied to block height rather than timestamp would be affected — emission schedules and time-locked contracts could break. Block time was chosen with security considerations in mind; worst-case scenario design is core to Ergo's ethos. Any change requires careful community study.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-05-27): Is trustless relay the same as double merge-mined sidechains?
  **A** (@Glasgow): No. Trustless relay uses a smart contract to store Bitcoin headers and verify Bitcoin transactions — no mining involvement. Double merge mining has the sidechain act as a Bitcoin sidechain with commitments to both Bitcoin and sidechain UTXO sets written on-chain, enabling richer applications on Bitcoin.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-05-27): Will trustless relay let us sign transactions onto the Bitcoin chain?
  **A** (@Glasgow): You need a private key to sign Bitcoin transactions. Trustless relay bridges information which you can then use in smart contracts on each side — it is not a signing mechanism.

- **Q** (@Şerafettin Bayrakdar, 2024-05-06): Can a single rig achieve 17 GH/s for Ergo mining? Are there ASICs?
  **A** (@kushti, prior context): Autolykos2 is ASIC-resistant as a side effect, not by explicit design goal. ASICs can be designed for any algorithm but with less advantage. No confirmed commercial Ergo ASICs exist.

- **Q** (@Ich Binsnicht, 2024-05-06): Windows aarch64 vs x64 — which download?
  **A** (@qx()): aarch64 = ARM chip (M1/M2/M3 Mac running Windows VM) — nearly impossible on native Windows. Use x64 on standard Windows laptops and desktops.

- **Q** (@Aco Šmrkas, 2024-05-24): Is it possible to mint multiple tokens in a single transaction?
  **A** (@Pulsarz): No. See [GitHub issue #2013](https://github.com/ergoplatform/ergo/issues/2013) for ongoing discussion of pros and cons.

- **Q** (@Son Nguyen, 2024-05-23): Does the Ergo Watch figure of ~197k represent all addresses holding ERG? Are DEX addresses included?
  **A** (@HQΣr): Total addresses ~197k including dust (<0.01 ERG). Approximately 26k unique DEX user addresses; estimated ~26k actual individual ERG owners.

- **Q** (@Alien, 2024-05-28): Does running a public node expose physical location?
  **A** (Context): IP address is visible to peers; VPN use recommended. IP is not stored on-chain.

- **Q** (@Alien, 2024-05-28): Correct syntax for pointing a mobile wallet to a local Android Termux node?
  **A** (@qx()): Use only `http://127.0.0.1:9053/` — do not append `/panel` or additional path segments.

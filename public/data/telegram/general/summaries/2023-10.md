# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: October 2023

---

## Executive Summary

October 2023 was one of the most consequential months in Ergo's recent history, marked by three major ecosystem developments running in parallel: the Rosen Bridge RSN token launch and bridge infrastructure activation, the ErgoHack VII hackathon producing nine distinct projects, and a significant Twitter/X account crisis that ultimately resolved with Sigmanauts consolidating the community presence under @ergo_platform. Underlying these events was sustained technical debate around block speed improvements (weak blocks / sub-blocks), storage rent framing, and the long-term architecture of Ergo's scaling roadmap.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Rosen Bridge Launch & RSN Token

The RSN token launched with an initial RSN/ERG liquidity pair live on Spectrum DEX. The bridge infrastructure (watchers and guards) was phased in over 1–2 weeks before full activation, with the system described as not having an on/off switch. Watcher requirements were clarified as 1,000 ERG + $1,000 worth of RSN each. The Rosen Bridge supports multiple tokens bidirectionally — not ERG-only — and Ergo Foundation and Sigmanauts recommended users bridge to native chains for long-term holding rather than holding wrapped assets on CEXes. EVM bridge (Ethereum) was soft-estimated for year-end 2023 with an explicit disclaimer that this was not a hard commitment; UTXO chains (Bitcoin) were estimated to be faster to integrate. Guards are shared across chains; watchers need only monitor an additional chain's transactions, making multi-chain expansion lightweight.

### ErgoHack VII — "Future of Finance"

ErgoHack VII ran October 13–15 with nine teams registered and a prize pool of 9,000 SigmaUSD (1st place), 5,000 (2nd), and 4,000 (3rd), with TabbyPOS sponsoring additional prizes. Notable projects included: Satergo integrating NIPoPoWs for faster secure full-node wallets; SideChainProto for trustless sidechains; Lilium (NFT ticketing); Atomic Swap (cross-chain); a Metamask Snap plugin for Ergo; Hodlbox (gamified ERG locking with price-target unlocks); a Reputation System; Poor Man's Wallet (affordable hardware wallet); and Auction Coin (auction-based token emission). The hackathon served as the public launch vehicle for Auction Coin's dynamic auction mechanism.

### Auction Coin Protocol

Auction Coin (AC) launched during ErgoHack VII as an autonomous emission machine: token price starts at 2× LP price and decays to −5% of LP over a 3-day auction window, with 10 auctions every 4 days releasing 1,000 AC each. ERG from auctions is recycled via a buyback contract back into the LP. kushti acknowledged genuine theoretical uncertainty about whether AC would pump, stabilize, or dump, citing thin initial liquidity (~5k AC). All auctions worth >2,000 ERG sold in the first batch; a second batch commenced approximately 1 day after the first concluded. The Auction House itself operates with no slippage, making it distinct from AMM-based DEX mechanics.

### Twitter/X Crisis and Sigmanauts Account Consolidation

The @ergo_platform Twitter account was deleted after Armeanio ran analytics showing 43–92.5k bots among followers (80–90% of the 500k total). Following a community vote organized by qx() via a Twitter Space on Sunday October 8 at 4 PM UTC, the Sigmanauts account (@Sigmanauts) was renamed to @ergo_platform, preserving 600k+ followers. The old account was temporarily parked as @Ergo_Platform_X. Community debate covered governance, decentralization messaging, and professional marketing strategy.

### Block Speed Improvements, Weak Blocks, and Naming

Sustained discussion ran across all four weeks on whether and how to improve Ergo's ~2-minute block time. kushti confirmed that the expected block delay is effectively immutable due to dependencies in existing smart contracts (emission contracts, dApp timestamp logic) and that raw block time reduction would break deployed applications. The feasible path is **weak blocks** at 1/128th difficulty (~1 per second), enabling ~20-second weak confirmations after majority hashrate commitment, with sidechains providing incentivization for weak block reporting. kushti placed this firmly in R&D phase with no ETA; community estimates of "at least a year" circulated. A naming debate produced strong advocacy for "Fast/Strong Blocks" (from @dayumbbbb), "Flash Blocks," "Lite Blocks," and "Neutrino Blocks," with no official decision reached. kushti announced a testnet is available for community-driven parameter research and invited motivated members to lead testing.

### Storage Rent, Demurrage, and Terminology

Storage rent (0.13–0.14 ERG per UTXO every 4 years) was a recurring education topic throughout October. @dayumbbbb proposed "opt-in demurrage" or "inactivity demurrage" as normie-friendly framings and identified "Supply Security" as superior conceptual framing — emphasizing that demurrage fixes both mining security (via tail emission) and supply security (fixed 97M ERG cap forever), contrasting with chains having inflationary or deflationary "insecure" supply. Historical references to Silvio Gesell's Freiwirtschaft and the 1930s Wörgl demurrage experiment were cited. Armeanio clarified that the storage rent rate is adjustable within caps but the 4-year cycle speed is fixed. An ERC-20 demurrage token implementation on Ethereum (nolash/erc20-demurrage-token) was flagged as a potential parallel development to watch.

### GORT Token & Oracle Pool v2

The first Dexy Gold oracle pool participation token (GORT) reached the Auction House in early October, with all ERG from the auction going into a GORT/ERG LP. Oracle Pool v2 runs Dexy Gold with GORT rewards over an emission contract lasting approximately 90 years. A GORT call option contract (85 GORT for 5 ERG) was made available on SigmaO for 30-day price speculation. Oracle Pool v1 includes Ergo Foundation members plus external participants.

### Palmyra (formerly ZenGate) & Chainlink Partnership

Dan detailed Palmyra's use of on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralized trade financing: commodity producers deposit goods into bonded warehouses → receipt issued on-chain via Chainlink oracle + IoT devices (weight sensors, motion sensors, cameras) for continuous proof of reserve → receipt serves as collateral for borrowing. The Chainlink integration uses a proof of reserve function via a proxy function in Winter Protocol. Dan explicitly framed this as not zero-sum with Ergo-native oracles, stating plans to invest capital from the token sale into building native Ergo Oracle Pools (v1/v2) and tooling specifically for Palmyra's trade-finance use case. All current Palmyra infrastructure runs on Ergo.

### SigmaUSD Admin Key Risk & Protocol Governance

Community members raised concerns about centralized admin keys in SigmaUSD (held by Armeanio and others), noting that economic incentives to sell key access grow with TVL. Armeanio acknowledged the collusion risk is present in all systems — mining pools, PoS validators, treasuries — and committed publicly to burning SigmaUSD admin keys, with transparency as the interim safeguard. No ETA for key burning or a "SigmaUSD v2" was provided.

### ErgoMixer 4.4.0 & Stealth Addresses

ErgoMixer 4.4.0 released mid-October with stealth address support: receivers create stealth addresses (not directly payable), senders generate stealth payment addresses per transaction, enabling privacy-preserving fund receipt without address reuse. The release also improved ChainScanner and box rescan logic. A separate anon2020 experiment tested proportional bleed-off of funds across multiple mixer stages for enhanced privacy.

### Exchange Access & Regional Liquidity

CoinEx resumed ERG withdrawals after a node migration. Huobi's ERG withdrawals were suspended because storage rent broke their withdrawal system (subsequently resolved). KuCoin, Gate.io, and CoinEx remained unavailable in SE Asia and some North American regions due to KYC blocks and withdrawal restrictions in Malaysia, Singapore, and other countries — identified as a significant adoption blocker. Non-KYC alternatives (StealthEX, SimpleSwap, TradeOgre) were recommended. An inquiry from Efficient Frontier (Ben) regarding a Loan & Offer market-making service was escalated to EF leadership, contingent on a legal opinion that ERG is not a security.

### Spectrum Finance & Ecosystem DeFi

Spectrum Finance donated 30 million SPF tokens (3% of supply, ~$1.6M value) to the Ergo Foundation. qx() provided a detailed comparison of Trade House (order book DEX) over AMM-based Spectrum, highlighting: total depth/liquidity transparency, full slippage control, partial order fills, peer-to-peer execution without impermanent loss, and grid-order automation. Spectrum was confirmed to have no admin keys — value locked in immutable smart contracts with proxy contracts only adjustable for fees. SkyHarbor website rebuild was announced and deployed during W42.

### Autolykos2 & Mining Economics

kushti confirmed Autolykos2 will increase RAM requirements over time (modeled after Ethash) to protect against ASIC dominance and progressively phase out older 4GB GPUs. Block difficulty recalculates every 128 blocks; short-term hashrate swings (40% variance observed) cause block time variance of 85–144 seconds. Large pools exploit low-difficulty windows profitably. The combination of weak blocks and Autolykos2 RAM scaling was identified as the primary long-term mining stability mechanism.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Mahdyar, 2023-10-17): How is a cofactor >1 problematic in elliptic curves?
  **A** (@kushti): Cofactor >1 enables small-subgroup attacks (cf. Monero's 2017 bug) unless every Sigma protocol application implements explicit defenses. The only clean solution is cofactor-1 curves, but they lack Montgomery ladder support, creating performance trade-offs on large keys. This is why secp256k1 (cofactor 1) was originally chosen for Ergo.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2023-10-07): Why do block times fluctuate 85–144 seconds with 40% hashrate swings?
  **A** (@Flying Pig / @kushti): Large mining pools exploit low-difficulty windows (difficulty only recalculates every 128 blocks) by mining through low-difficulty cycles then migrating to other coins, capturing 20–30% extra profit. A hardfork fix would not pass miner vote due to pool incentives. Weak blocks are the intended long-term solution.

- **Q** (@Marty ErgoPad, 2023-10-10): What is the rough plan for block time improvement?
  **A** (@kushti, via ergoforum.org/t/a-scalability-plan-for-ergo/226/5): Weak blocks at 1/128th difficulty (~1 per second), cut-through transaction ID propagation, ~20-second weak confirmation after majority hashrate commitment, sidechain incentivization for weak block reporting. Raw block time reduction is not viable — expected block delay cannot change without breaking emission contracts and timestamp-dependent dApps.

- **Q** (@Marty ErgoPad / general, 2023-10-10): Can the 2-minute block time be reduced directly?
  **A** (@kushti): No. The expected block delay is baked into emission contracts and existing dApp logic. Ethereum reduced to 17s by paying uncle blocks but introduced fairness compromises (see https://eprint.iacr.org/2022/1020.pdf). Weak blocks are the only viable path for Ergo.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-10-28): What is the watcher collateral requirement for Rosen Bridge?
  **A** (@Glasgow): 1,000 ERG plus $1,000 worth of RSN (each independently, not combined). Requirements will likely be set to specific amounts after price discovery; dynamic adjustment plan unclear.

- **Q** (@Lyndon, 2023-10-28): How long after Rosen Bridge launch to add additional chain bridges?
  **A** (@CW): UTXO chains (BTC) fast to add. EVM soft estimate is year-end 2023 but explicitly not a hard commitment. The framework is composable with Ergo-centric smart contracts — no per-chain smart contract deployment needed, just configuring watchers/guards, testing, and organizing liquidity. Same guards serve all chains; watchers need only monitor additional chain transactions.

- **Q** (@Buccoooo, 2023-10-26): Can USDT swapped on Spectrum Finance be sent to Kraken/Binance?
  **A** (@Glasgow): No. USDT on Ergo is a native token, not ERC-20. Cannot send directly to CEXes — would require bridging via Rosen Bridge or using a cross-chain swap service.

- **Q** (@Ilya Oskin, 2023-10-22): Why do Cardano dApps blame eUTXO for performance issues with many tokens, when Spectrum has no such issues?
  **A** (@Ilya Oskin): Most likely poor coin selection and inefficient UTXO consumption by application developers creating unnecessary outputs. This is not an eUTXO architectural flaw — Spectrum's smooth operation demonstrates the model works correctly when implemented properly.

- **Q** (@HostFat, 2023-10-10): How much of a user's balance can storage rent claim?
  **A** (community): ~0.13–0.14 ERG per UTXO every 4 years. If ERG balance is insufficient, any tokens in the box can cover the fee. Wallets prevent accidental drainage through UTXO consolidation. Consolidating UTXOs semi-frequently is best practice.

- **Q** (@W || Mafia, 2023-10-01): What if two people scan the same QR code for the Ergo Raffle?
  **A** (@qx()): Structure it as a payment request transaction sending 0.0001 ERG to a smart contract that returns prizes and tracks claims on-chain. "Already claimed" feedback requires wallet integration (e.g., Minotaur) to check smart contract state without submitting a transaction.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-10-22): Is Spectrum immutable — can admin keys change contracts?
  **A** (community): Spectrum has no admin keys; value is locked in immutable smart contracts. Proxy contracts can be updated for fee routing, but users can always interact directly with underlying smart contracts or build alternative UIs. Same architecture applies to DuckPools, SigmaFi, ErgoPad, and Paideia.

- **Q** (@M B, 2023-10-07): How can I get passive income from my ERG?
  **A** (@Flying Pig): Provide liquidity on Spectrum DEX, lend on SigmaFi, lend on DuckPools, or stake on ErgoPad. All carry some risk; SigmaFi bonds were offering ~50% APY on SigmaUSD with 150% collateralization at time of discussion.

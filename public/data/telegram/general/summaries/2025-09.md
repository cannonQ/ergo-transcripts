# Ergo General — Monthly Summary: September 2025

---

## Executive Summary

September 2025 was defined by three converging themes: ecosystem resilience under exchange adversity (HTX delisting, TradeOgre seizure), accelerating cross-chain infrastructure via Rosen Bridge (Runes mainnet, Nervos integration planning, ENDtheCEX movement), and a deepening technical roadmap centred on sub-blocks, USE stablecoin, and ChainCash free banking. The month also saw Ergo gain significant external validation when Charles Hoskinson publicly described it as the "spiritual successor to Bitcoin" amid the HTX controversy.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Sub-blocks and Network Throughput
kushti confirmed active work on 6.0 activation and sub-block development across W36–W38. The core challenge is that many contracts and dApps rely on block height rather than timestamp, meaning faster blocks would require careful audit of emission contracts and time-sensitive logic. kushti called for community volunteers to run testnets, vary difficulty and block time parameters, and produce scientific observations for comparison — emphasising no coding skill is required to lead this effort. A dedicated testnet parameter group was flagged as the coordination point; interested parties were directed to DM kushti. No concrete timeline was given for whitepaper publication, pending thorough devnet p2p protocol testing.

### Rosen Bridge Expansion
Rosen Bridge saw its most active month yet. Armeanio confirmed the **first working extension of the Runes protocol on mainnet** (W39), with watchers and guards already running. The bridge now supports BTC, Cardano, Ethereum, BSC, Dogecoin, and Runes, with Nervos (CKB) integration targeting October and Ink/Base pending. Armeanio also sent Rosen Bridge contribution standards to Komodo and BPSAA projects. The guard set is governed via a Paideia DAO open to public voting. HQ3rr launched a Rosen agent programme offering fee-sharing to teams onboarding projects onto supported chains. A recurring concern was the minimum fee (~$10) making small retail swaps uneconomical compared to SimpleSwap — flagged as an architectural friction point rather than a bug.

### HTX Delisting and Exchange Adversity
HTX Global delisted ERG alongside AZERO, KMD, and FIRO, with Armeanio gathering evidence of a $100K bribery demand to avoid delisting. Multiple affected projects (Firo, Komodo, Vemp) reported similar treatment. Armeanio filed a formal notice to HTX/Huobi and lodged a complaint with the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS), with findings published on GitHub. Separately, Canadian law enforcement seized ~163,564 ERG from TradeOgre. These events catalysed the **ENDtheCEX** initiative led by The Legend Captain (LooTz), proposing built-in DEX functionality in wallets and shared order books across Ergo, Komodo, and Firo.

### USE (Dexy USD) Stablecoin
A new oracle pool and USD pricing mechanism was prioritised over updating the legacy SigmaUSD pool. A dedicated Telegram group was established at https://t.me/USE_stablecoin. Richi and Armeanio discussed v3 concentrated liquidity, 0.05% fees on USE/USDC pairs via Aerodrome, and Danogo Finance (Cardano leverage trading) as a target integration. cannon_q confirmed USE launching "soon" by end of W39. The key design principle: stablecoins require fixed low fees; other token pairs can have variable LP-set fees.

### ChainCash, Silver Tokenization, and Free Banking
Grayman pursued SilverCents — junk silver tokenized as fungible collateral for ChainCash notes. The architecture uses vendor networks as distributed reserves: vendors accept physical junk silver or ChainCash they underwrite; customers verify on-chain ERG collateral. Batch redemption and LETS-based fallback for full outages (1–3 days) were discussed. EC Riegel's trading circles concept (discovered by kushti in 2024) was identified as a foundational parallel. A dedicated group at https://t.me/chaincashtalks was active throughout the month.

### Ergo's Strategic Positioning
kushti published a comprehensive strategic analysis (W37) positioning Ergo as an **active global neutral reserve asset** — not merely a store of value — enabling derivatives and smart reserves across three niches: (1) AI/P2P network economies, (2) community currencies with global reserve tooling, and (3) programmable money for digital free banking. This framing gained external traction when Charles Hoskinson endorsed Ergo as the "spiritual successor to Bitcoin" following the HTX controversy.

### Mining Economics and Autolykos
Network hashrate declined from ~10 TH in spring to ~4 TH in September, coinciding with post-emission-reduction dynamics. kushti confirmed this aligns with historical patterns and that the Autolykos memory table readjusts every ~30 days. Ergo was described as "the only GPU coin left" following Kaspa's move to ASIC dominance. Merge mining was flagged as an upcoming miner profitability boost. The next emission reduction was 12 days away as of W39.

### EPMW Open-Source Hardware Wallet
kushti introduced the EPMW (Ergo Paper Wallet Module) — an open-source hardware wallet written in C with a 3D-printed case. It uses multisource entropy (user-tappable buttons, clock phase shifts, thermal noise), supports Schnorr signatures, and parses transactions. Token support was not yet implemented. kushti argued this approach is superior to Ledger given Ledger's data breach history, closed-source firmware, and update controversies.

### Community Infrastructure and Governance
- The **Lykos DAO** platform reached 70% website completion; tokenomic configuration and seed round voting planned for October.
- **SigmaLok** (life insurance trust) UI was nearly complete by W39, with CryptoxInfant outlining a whole-life/term-life insurance mechanism backed by ERG collateral.
- **Nautilus Wallet** integrated scam token hiding via a community-maintained list synced with ErgoExplorer; Javanese language support added via PR #316.
- Community Chat replaced the traditional Weekly AMA, running Thursdays at 13:00 UTC on Zoom and YouTube Live.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Man ish, 2025-09-02): Braid says ASIC machines merge mine ERG and BTC, but ERG is ASIC-resistant. How does this work?
  **A** (@kushti): Bitcoin miners mine Bitcoin merge-mined blocks; Ergo miners mine Ergo merge-mined blocks. There are no Braid ASICs — the two miner populations remain separate.

- **Q** (@Koshiro Tanaka, 2025-09-03): Is the EPMW hardware wallet genuinely more secure than Ledger?
  **A** (@kushti): Ledger leaks customer data, its hardware is not open-sourced, and firmware update scandals have eroded trust. EPMW uses open entropy sources — user taps, clock phase shifts, thermal noise. Original Bitcoin adopters favour custom unbranded solutions for exactly this reason.

- **Q** (@blake starky, 2025-09-05): Who validates silver reserves in ChainCash? Has third-party trust been eliminated?
  **A** (@Grayman): Vendors act as distributed bankers, accepting physical junk silver or ChainCash they themselves underwrite. Customers can verify on-chain that the vendor holds ERG or other collateral in the ChainCash reserve. Redemption occurs via purchase or token exchange, not store credit alone.

- **Q** (@Alx, 2025-09-10): What are the chances of SigmaUSD de-pegging like UST/Terra?
  **A** (@Glasgow): SigmaUSD is over-collateralised (4$+ backing per SigmaUSD). The peg-breaking point is explicit and visible on-chain. There is no bank-run incentive analogous to UST, and SigmaRSV supply is not dependent on Ergo ecosystem growth in the way Luna was on Terra.

- **Q** (@TMR.ΣRG, 2025-09-11): How do off-chain transaction solutions work during partial or full network outages? How is double-spending prevented and reconciliation handled post-recovery?
  **A** (@kushti): For partial outages, parties with Internet connectivity broadcast NIPoPoWs, new headers, and transactions with proofs. For full temporary outages (1–3 days), use off-chain cash equivalents such as Basis or credit-based LETS; redeem on-chain when connectivity is restored.

- **Q** (@Jessie Jane, 2025-09-11): When are sub-blocks coming?
  **A** (@kushti): The devnet p2p protocol needs thorough testing (a few days), after which the current writings will be extended into a whitepaper for community discussion and design decisions.

- **Q** (@HQΣr, 2025-09-16): Can sub-blocks lower Rosen Bridge transaction costs to enable micro-swaps directly in ERG?
  **A** (@kushti): A detailed response will be posted on the Ergo Forum during the developer chat. Sub-blocks are expected to improve throughput and fee economics generally, making micro-swap use cases more viable.

- **Q** (@cannon_q / @Shai Deshe, 2025-09-17–18): How does the federated Rosen Bridge guard set work and how are new guards added?
  **A** (@Armeanio): The guard set is built, run, and watched by community members. It is currently permissioned during the bootstrap phase — analogous to proof of work in governance: whoever sacrifices time and energy earns participation. New guards are voted in via the Paideia DAO.

- **Q** (@DiscordBridge/@V__C, 2025-09-24): What is Ergo's unique value proposition for cross-chain activity like Rosen Bridge DOGE swaps?
  **A** (@Armeanio): Every cross-chain transaction is verified on Ergo. Miners are paid now; the long-term benefit is captured via storage rent. Ergo benefits both actively (transaction fees) and passively (storage rent on all Rosen state) from all bridge activity regardless of which chain the user is bridging to.

- **Q** (@DiscordBridge/@Jayedii, 2025-09-26): Why is Ergo's maximum supply ~97.5M — an odd number rather than a round figure?
  **A** (@TMR.ΣRG): The starting block reward and emission schedule were determined first; total supply is the arithmetic result, not a design target. Starting at 75 ERG/block for two years, then decreasing by 3 ERG per quarter until reaching zero, the series sums to ~97.5M ERG.

- **Q** (@Ioannis, 2025-09-22): Was a longer emission schedule possible? Why celebrate the reduction given declining hashrate and volume over four years?
  **A** (@kushti): The community voted for the shortest option; longer schedules would have lower emission right now to preserve a larger tail budget. Other re-emission options would cut even deeper at this point. Memory table size also readjusts every ~30 days, which over time excludes GPUs with less than 8 GB VRAM, making large commodity farms less economical and favouring committed miners.

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General
**Month:** 2018-04
**Weeks covered:** W15, W17

---

## Executive Summary

April 2018 was an early community-building period for Ergo, dominated by onboarding discussions, foundational technical questions, and tokenomics clarification ahead of mainnet launch. Key themes included Ergo's positioning as a technically superior proof of work blockchain, the mechanics of the EFYT-to-mainnet token swap, and early ErgoScript capability questions from developers exploring the platform.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Ergo's Proof of Work Design & Positioning
Community members frequently asked how Ergo compared to Bitcoin and Ethereum. Ergo was consistently described as a Bitcoin improvement focusing on simplicity (hash functions and Sigma protocols only), bounded script verification time, light full nodes with full-node security guarantees, soft-forkability, and a redesigned fee mechanism. There were no masternodes; the chain is GPU-mineable via Autolykos. Mobile mining was discussed but deemed practically difficult given hardware competition, with "light nodes" clarified as being about efficient blockchain access rather than competitive PoW mining.

### EFYT Token Swap & Emission Model
Significant discussion centred on the planned swap from EFYT (pre-mainnet Waves token) to mainnet ERG. The swap was confirmed as non-automatic, requiring manual action during an announced window. The indicated ratio was 1 EFYT → 1 Data token + 1 Money token. The team's 10% mining allocation was clarified as running for the entire mining period (not a fixed two-year window as in Zcash), with rewards decreasing over time. Total first-year supply was confirmed at approximately 19,710,000 ERG, with a total supply cap of ~97.7M ERG.

### ErgoScript Smart Contract Capabilities
Early developer interest in ErgoScript surfaced, with requests for documentation and questions about contract state persistence. The language specification was shared from the sigmastate-interpreter repository. A key limitation identified at this stage was that OOP-style persistent contract memory (analogous to Ethereum's storage model) was not yet implemented. P2SH was confirmed as possible within the Sigma protocols framework.

### Early Investor Distribution & Community Concerns
Questions were raised about large EFYT wallet holders (100K+) who appeared to have received direct allocations from the team wallet rather than accumulating via open trading. Community members raised concerns about potential price manipulation. The response from community representatives was that these were angel backers and early investors receiving normal allocations, comparable to ICO distributions, and that post-mainnet PoW mining should stabilise markets.

### Tauchain Comparison
Brief discussion compared Ergo's development maturity to the competing Tauchain project, which uses a non-Turing-complete language. Ergo's codebase was noted as significantly more mature at this stage (2000+ commits across 12+ repositories versus 71 commits in Tauchain's single repository).

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-04-09): Is there any documentation on ErgoScript smart contract capabilities?
  **A** (@Alex Slesarenko): Language specification available at https://github.com/ScorexFoundation/sigmastate-interpreter/blob/master/docs/LangSpec.md

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-04-09): Can contracts save data in their own memory, OOP-style, similar to Ethereum?
  **A** (@Alex Slesarenko): Not yet.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-04-28): Does the team receive 10% of each block reward for the whole mining period, or only for two years like Zcash?
  **A** (@Unknown): The team receives 10% throughout the entire mining period; rewards decrease over time. It is not a fixed two-year window.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-04-24): Can P2SH (pay-to-script-hash) be implemented in Ergo?
  **A** (@Unknown): P2SH is possible in the Sigma protocols language.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-04-24): How do light nodes relate to mobile mining feasibility?
  **A** (@Unknown): Light nodes are about efficient blockchain access via novel methods, not competitive mobile PoW mining. Mobile mining is theoretically possible but practically difficult due to hardware competition.

- **Q** (@Ilya Cherevkov, 2018-04-22): Regarding the hackathon project — how does the "private information at entrance" and "anonymous employee messages" use case work?
  **A** (@Unknown): The project used ErgoScript/Sigma protocols to open gates while preserving privacy — nobody can determine who opened the gate, and this property holds post-mainnet by design.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Contract State Persistence**: ErgoScript does not yet support persistent OOP-style contract memory. Whether and when this will be added remains open.
- **EFYT Swap Window**: The exact timing and mechanics of the mainnet swap window had not been formally announced; community was awaiting details.
- **Large EFYT Holder Identities**: The identity and intentions of 100K+ EFYT wallet holders remained unconfirmed; no formal transparency disclosure was committed to.
- **Mobile Mining Roadmap**: No concrete timeline or technical plan for practical mobile mining support was provided.
- **Fee Estimation API**: Noted in ecosystem context as existing but untested and unused by applications — not yet formally on the community's radar this month.

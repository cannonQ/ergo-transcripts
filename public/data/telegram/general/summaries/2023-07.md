# Ergo General — Monthly Developer Knowledge Base Summary
**Month:** July 2023

---

## Executive Summary

July 2023 was a landmark month for Ergo's core infrastructure, defined by three major milestones: the white-hat hodlERG exploit and subsequent security hardening, the activation of storage rent at block 1,051,200, and the release of node v5.0.13 with complete NIPoPoW bootstrapping support. Running beneath all of this was steady progress toward the Rosen Bridge public launch and the Dexy Gold/Oracle Pool v2 ecosystem, positioning Ergo's DeFi layer for a significant expansion in August.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### hodlERG Exploit, Postmortem, and v2 Development

A critical vulnerability in the hodlERG smart contract was discovered and white-hat exploited by community member Krasavice Blasen, who drained ~90k ERG to prevent malicious theft. All users were refunded; kushti personally compensated the affected LP provider. The bug was traced to a commit that corrected `reserveDelta` but inadvertently removed a validation check that appeared tautological. kushti published a detailed postmortem, committed the first negative tests, and confirmed that multiple additional audits and thorough code review would be required before hodlERG v2 relaunch. Bruno Paleo confirmed v2 relaunch details would follow shortly via the hodlCoinGame Telegram. Community interest remained high, with requests for fee-tier variants (including a "Dan Edition" at 69.420%).

### Storage Rent Activation

Storage rent activated at block 1,051,200 on July 20, 2023, completing a long-anticipated protocol milestone. Miners can now charge approximately 0.14 ERG per four years on unspent outputs, reducing state bloat and providing an additional revenue stream. Partial spending of affected boxes requires miners to include transactions directly via the `/mining/candidateWithTxs` endpoint, bypassing mempool validation due to the `output.creationHeight == currentHeight` condition. Full spending of boxes under 0.14 ERG has relaxed conditions and can proceed through the mempool. A Spectrum bug caused some boxes to have `creationHeight = 0`, meaning recently-mined boxes were immediately subject to rent; `@error` announced refunds for all tokens claimed from those affected boxes. Notably, no mining pools had yet implemented storage rent collection by end of month despite the revenue potential.

### NIPoPoWs and Node v5.0.13

The release of node v5.0.13 on July 29 delivered the final piece of NIPoPoW implementation — a breakthrough for Ergo's accessibility. A NIPoPoW proof of only ~180KB replaces ~400MB of headers, reducing verification time from ~90 minutes to under 500ms. Combined with UTXO snapshot bootstrapping, full sync times dropped to 7–11 minutes on gigabit connections and under 10 minutes on a Raspberry Pi 4, compared to 12+ hours previously. Kushti clarified the security model: UTXO snapshots have formal verification backing (eprint.iacr.org/2018/129.pdf), and a stateless client mode storing only ~32 bytes of state hash is now feasible. Full-block-scanning applications (Oracle Pool v2, Rosen watchers) still require complete block history; combining UTXO-set-only and full-block scans remains an open design question.

### Rosen Bridge Progress

Rosen Bridge infrastructure moved steadily toward public launch throughout July. MHS_SAM reported the backend refactor was nearly complete with new TSS integration, and Watcher and Guard apps were described as days from release by mid-month. The architecture was clarified: Watchers (off-chain bots run by anyone) monitor cross-chain transactions and must lock ERG and RSN as collateral to receive permits, mitigating Sybil attacks; Guards verify watcher consensus and manage settlement. Reward distribution is 30% to guards, 70% to watchers. Spectrum Finance and Palmyra were announced as launch accelerators. A Cardano Catalyst proposal for 850k ADA to promote Rosen Bridge adoption within the Cardano ecosystem was submitted by Dan Friedman.

### Oracle Pool v2, Dexy Gold, and Gluon

Oracle Pool v2 underwent final testing, with only the Dexy buyback box withdrawal of reward tokens remaining. The major architectural changes include: a single pool box design, GORT (Gold Oracle Reward Token) replacing ERG rewards, a buyback contract funded by 0.2% of Dexy/Gluon minting fees, and all operations manageable via CLI with Prometheus/Grafana metrics. The target is 25 oracle operators. Dexy Gold public testnet was targeted for late July, with the main contract ready and the fee contract estimated at 2–3 more weeks. Bruno Paleo explained that Gluon improves on the AgeUSD/SigmaUSD design by removing hard reserve-ratio discontinuities (no min/max limits), replacing them with progressively higher fees for large transactions analogous to AMM slippage — eliminating scenarios where reservecoin holders cannot exit. Gluon may target Silver for its first deployment to avoid oracle fragmentation.

### Palmyra (formerly ZenGate) and Chainlink Partnership

Dan Friedman explained Palmyra's use of Chainlink's proof-of-reserve and proxy functions through Winter Protocol to issue on-chain warehouse receipts for physical commodities stored in bonded warehouses. IoT devices (weight sensors, cameras, motion sensors) provide continuous proof-of-existence, enabling decentralized trade financing. Chainlink was chosen for network size and support breadth; the plan includes eventually building out Ergo's native Oracle Pools as part of Palmyra's ecosystem investment, potentially using token sale capital. Armeanio noted that Ergo's role as a multi-chain smart-finance provider means community members should avoid tribalistic reactions when ecosystem projects use non-Ergo tools for specific functions — everything in Palmyra currently runs on Ergo.

### Block Time, Scaling, and Governance Discussions

Community discussion revisited Ergo's 2-minute block time. Kushti confirmed that changing block time would break monetary policy, NIPoPoW proofs, emission contracts, and numerous security assumptions, making it infeasible without extensive review. The preferred path is improving P2P block propagation and exploring sub-block or microblock confirmation protocols (drafts exist) after NIPoPoW bootstrapping is fully deployed. The concrete application set now on Ergo — GARD's protocols, Rosen Bridge, Oracle Pools, Spectrum, Trade House — makes optimization more tractable than for empty chains pursuing throughput abstractions. A testnet channel exists for interested community members to run parameter-tweaking experiments; kushti invited anyone passionate about this to DM him for access.

### SigmaFi and P2P Bond Activity

SigmaFi saw notable activity with large bond requests at 38% APR over 18–24 month terms, backed by ERG collateral. The platform was highlighted as a trustless, KYC-free, jurisdiction-agnostic alternative to traditional banking yield. Kushti confirmed having reviewed several contract versions, though not as a security guarantee.

### Exchange Access and Marketing

KuCoin and CoinEx closing US operations on July 15 prompted community discussion of alternatives: TradeOgre (no KYC), SimpleSwap, StealthEX, ChangeNOW. BitMart confirmed it does not accept US citizens despite ERG being listed. Community consensus favored organic marketing over paid influencer campaigns, with Rosen Bridge and DEX solutions as the long-term answer to exchange access for US users. A Sigmanauts treasury funding raffle reached ~450 ERG for community-driven marketing initiatives. An infographic competition was launched with 200 ERG prize pool (later supplemented by 1.5M COMET from kushti and other community contributions) across five themes: Privacy, Ergo DeFi dApps, NIPoPoWs, Rosen Bridge, and Babel Fees.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Cheese, 2023-07-20): How does storage rent work for miners and pools technically?
  **A** (@kushti): Partial spending requires `output.creationHeight == currentHeight`, which mempool rejects — miners must use `/mining/candidateWithTxs` to include rent collection transactions directly in blocks. Full spending of boxes under 0.14 ERG has relaxed conditions and can go through the mempool normally.

- **Q** (@Hobithole, 2023-07-14): Can you make block times faster on L1 Ergo without compromising safety?
  **A** (@kushti): Not safely with current mempool transaction processing. 15-second blocks like early Ethereum led to well-documented 2016 attacks. Better path: improve P2P block propagation and explore sub-block confirmation protocols after NIPoPoW bootstrapping is complete.

- **Q** (@Hobithole, 2023-07-14): Would changing block time require recalculating security assumptions?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes — it would break monetary policy, NIPoPoW proofs, emission contracts, and many other things. Reviewing all impacts is extremely difficult.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2023-07-11): If everyone ran a bootstrapped node, would historic transactions be lost?
  **A** (@kushti): Theoretically yes, but in practice projects like Paideia and CruxFinance need history and will run full history nodes for indexing. The assumption that all nodes switch to snapshots is unrealistic.

- **Q** (@Luivatra, 2023-07-11): Why does Paideia need full block history?
  **A** (@Luivatra): To be able to rebuild AVL trees.

- **Q** (@Armeanio, 2023-07-14): What Oracle Pool v2 upgrades are you most excited about?
  **A** (@Dennis Zadorozhnyi): Single pool box design and reward tokens replacing ERG. Plus simplified operation — all commands through CLI parameters, Prometheus/Grafana metrics added. Running an oracle is no more technically challenging than running a node.

- **Q** (@Armeanio, 2023-07-14): What advantages does Gluon have over AgeUSD?
  **A** (@Bruno Paleo): Removes the min/max reserve ratio discontinuities that cause UX problems in SigmaUSD — situations where reservecoin holders cannot sell. Gluon is designed to eliminate all discontinuities, using progressively higher fees for large transactions instead of hard cutoffs.

- **Q** (@R, 2023-07-14): Will Dexy have unlimited liquidity unlike SigmaUSD?
  **A** (@kushti): Dexy will be strict but allow continuous small minting. Large whale mints are impossible by design — the Neutrino and other crashes demonstrate why whale mints at the top are catastrophic.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-07-29): Can you use NIPoPoW bootstrap without UTXO bootstrap for full-block sync?
  **A** (@kushti): No — NIPoPoW only accelerates header downloading. For full-block sync you must download all headers anyway, so NIPoPoW gains are minimal. For additional indices (extraIndex), you must scan all blocks and cannot use UTXO bootstrap at all.

- **Q** (@Pulsarz, 2023-07-29): Can Oracle Pool v2 and Rosen guards run in NIPoPoW + UTXO snapshot mode?
  **A** (@Pulsarz, confirmed by kushti): No — both require full block scanning for security. NIPoPoW + UTXO snapshot modes are primarily for light clients and wallets.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-07-29): What is a stateless client in Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): A client with full-node security that does not store the UTXO set. It downloads and verifies proofs of UTXO set transformation correctness, storing only ~32 bytes of state hash. Cannot mine or broadcast others' transactions, but fully supports wallets and custom scans.

- **Q** (@Aberg, 2023-07-21): Why can't smart contracts be interacted with directly on Ergo Explorer like Etherscan?
  **A** (@Pulsarz): In eUTXO, most logic is off-chain; on-chain scripts only validate. In EVM, all code lives in the contract with triggerable functions. ErgoTree can be decompiled but you'd still need a transaction builder and off-chain logic. Some contracts like SigmaUSD could be implemented in an explorer UI, but it defeats the architectural purpose.

- **Q** (Community, 2023-07-20): Could a DDoS attack like Kaspa experienced happen to Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo has had spam protection since v4.0.44 with limits on transaction downloads per peer. The fee market handles congestion — users adjust fees via slider in wallets like Nautilus. Low-fee transactions eventually flush from the mempool if not picked up. Node operators can also raise minimum propagation fee rates to prevent spam from even entering their mempool.

- **Q** (@Hassen, 2023-07-11): Do Ergo native tokens have contract addresses like ERC-20 tokens?
  **A** (@NoahErgo): No. In an account model like Ethereum, tokens exist at contract addresses. In the eUTXO model, tokens are discrete pieces that move directly wallet-to-wallet — there is no contract address concept for native tokens.

- **Q** (@Richi, 2023-07-14): Should Gluon have asymmetric fees — lower for reservecoin vs stablecoin minting?
  **A** (@Bruno Paleo): Considered having four different fee parameters for each operation direction. Will think more carefully about fine-grained fee structure based on this suggestion; cashback mechanisms were avoided due to gaming concerns.

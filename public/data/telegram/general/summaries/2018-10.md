# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General
**Month:** 2018-10

---

## Executive Summary

October 2018 was dominated by pre-launch positioning discussions centred on the MainNet timeline (pushed to Q1 2019), privacy design choices, and the Autolykos mining algorithm. Community conversation reflected an early-stage project clarifying its roadmap, decentralisation philosophy, and developer contribution pathways ahead of the TestNet v2 milestone in November 2018.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### MainNet Timeline & TestNet v2
The MainNet launch was formally acknowledged as delayed from end-2018 to a more realistic Q1 2019 target, with Version 1.7 recently shipped and Version 2.0 in progress. TestNet v2 was scheduled for November 2018 as the critical pre-MainNet milestone, described as closely resembling the final MainNet environment. Exchange listings were explicitly deferred until after MainNet launch, with no specific exchanges discussed.

### Privacy Design
Privacy in Ergo was confirmed as optional (opt-in) rather than mandatory by default, analogous to Zcash rather than Monero. The implementation was scheduled to follow completion of the Sigma-language layer, with more complex privacy schemes described as possible in later phases. Whether privacy defaults might eventually become mandatory post-launch was left open.

### Autolykos Mining & Hash Rental
The final Autolykos mining algorithm implementation was confirmed as coming soon in TestNet v2, after which high-performance CPU and GPU miners were expected to follow. Community members asked about mining without dedicated hardware; hash rental services such as NiceHash were confirmed as viable options once MainNet launched, with integration details to be discussed based on user preferences.

### Decentralisation Philosophy & Governance
Ergo's approach to preventing centralisation was discussed across multiple dimensions: the treasury system for development funding, ASIC- and pool-resistant Autolykos for miner decentralisation, and light nodes for service decentralisation. A notable commitment was made to community governance handoff within one year of MainNet launch, contrasting with other chains (Ethereum, Cardano, Waves) that pushed hardforks post-release. The mechanism for this handoff was not specified.

### Developer Tooling & Contributions
Smart contract testing, examples, and tutorials were identified as the most impactful pre-MainNet developer contributions. A question about tooling for recursive ErgoScript calls and inter-contract composition at scale—specifically whether a standard library or import model was planned—was raised but not answered by the core team. Vasily Chepurnoy was noted as preparing a formal protocol presentation, though the academic paper was not yet finished.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-10): Will Ergo have ring signatures like Monero?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): One goal is to improve efficiency over Monero. Monero-like privacy will be included after Sigma-language implementation, with more complex schemes possible in later phases.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-10): Will privacy be mandatory like Monero or opt-in like Zcash?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Privacy will be optional, as confirmed by developers.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-10): What prevents centralisation in Ergo?
  **A** (@Unknown / community): Ergo's treasury system prevents governance and development centralisation; ASIC- and pool-resistant Autolykos prevents miner/pool centralisation; light nodes prevent service centralisation.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-10): Can I mine using NiceHash or a hash rental service without strong dedicated hardware?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Yes — rent hash at any service and point it to the chain at MainNet launch.
  **A** (@Dmitry): Final Autolykos implementation coming soon in TestNet v2; NiceHash or similar integration can be discussed once user hardware preferences are known.

- **Q** (@Kaichao | Keyring, 2018-10): Is there a plan to switch to PoS after a few years?
  **A** (@Kim): No PoS transition is documented or planned as of this date.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-10): What can developers contribute before MainNet?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Smart contract testing, examples, and tutorials are the most helpful contributions. DM for discussion on additional tooling needs.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2018-10): What tooling is planned for recursive ErgoScript calls and inter-contract composition at scale? Is a standard library model under consideration?
  **A** (unresolved): No direct answer provided by the core team in this period.

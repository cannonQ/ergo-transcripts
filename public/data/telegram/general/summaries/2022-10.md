# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: October 2022

---

## Executive Summary

October 2022 was a technically productive month for Ergo, marked by two major protocol milestones: the successful activation of EIP-37 (faster difficulty adjustment) and the release of Node 5.0.0 with hybrid JIT/AOT costing. These were accompanied by active ecosystem development across Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, Paideia, and DuckPools, while community debate over the @eth_classic Twitter account rebrand surfaced deeper questions about governance transparency and community input into Foundation decisions.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EIP-37 Hard Fork & Difficulty Adjustment
EIP-37 activated successfully on 2022-10-03, reducing difficulty epoch length from 1,024 blocks to 128 and capping per-epoch adjustment at 50%. Initial network difficulty dropped sharply from ~16 PH to ~4.41 PH as the algorithm recalibrated, with block times stabilising around the intended 120 seconds after some volatility. The 128-block minimum was confirmed as the security/efficiency tradeoff floor. The fork opened discussion on miner behaviour during epoch transitions — large miners can exploit epoch-based changes to hop on and off, but community consensus held that ASIC resistance keeps the system decentralised enough that protocol-level penalties would harm honest participants more than they'd deter hoppers.

### Node 5.0.0 Release & Roadmap to 6.0
Node 5.0.0 shipped during W43 with hybrid costing (AOT + JIT), faster script execution, and improved block space utilisation — expected to deliver an effective 5–10x throughput increase after on-chain activation via miner voting. Kushti confirmed block size and cost limits are governed by miner voting, with current limits already higher than at launch. Post-5.0, the roadmap turns to UTXO set bootstrapping and NIPoPoWs. Early discussion of Node 6.0 features began — candidates include unsigned 256-bit BigInts, Merkle trees for extension values, and L2-oriented opcodes — but no decisions were finalised and community input was invited.

### Rosen Bridge Progress
Rosen Bridge repositories were made public on GitHub early in the month, and a soft launch began with watchers and guards being deployed. Version 3.1 shipped with bug fixes; users were advised to update to Watcher UI 2.2.1 via `docker compose pull`. A manual TX was submitted to refund users whose permits were slashed due to an accidental high-fee transaction (a missed signature delayed it). Despite community requests for a firm launch date throughout the month, the team consistently described status as "work in progress" with no ETA — partly due to a team member illness during W42.

### Twitter Account Controversy & Governance
Charles Hoskinson transferred the @eth_classic Twitter handle (670k followers) to the Ergo Foundation, who rebranded it @ergoplatformorg. This sparked significant community debate about ethics, process, and governance. Glasgow confirmed Foundation officers discussed and approved it as a marketing opportunity, but community opinion was divided (~2/3 supportive, ~1/3 opposed). The account was briefly suspended (a bot sweep, per Bob Summerville) and restored. The episode surfaced a broader unresolved concern: there is no formal mechanism for community input on non-technical Foundation decisions, and multiple users called for improved transparency and voting infrastructure.

### Spectrum DEX & Liquidity Mining
Spectrum Labs shared the liquidity mining (LM) protocol specification and contracts for community review, with yield farming described as forthcoming. A trading rewards airdrop was announced based on historical usage, with tokenomics promised "next week" from 2022-10-22 and airdrop distribution estimated a few weeks after that. The AHT/$ERG pair launched on Spectrum during W43. Community interest was high but details on distribution timing and token sale structure remained incomplete through month end.

### Paideia DAO Demo Launch
Paideia's web2 demo launched on 2022-10-21 with a Twitter Spaces community event. Teams could request manual DAO configuration to handle launch-day traffic. The demo represented a significant ecosystem milestone, though public DAO creation functionality was not yet open — timeline for that was unspecified. The release of EIP-37-related docs and Paideia's growing presence reinforced Ergo's governance tooling narrative.

### DuckPools Collateralized Lending
DuckPools entered beta at duckpools.io, offering collateralised lending that allows miners to earn yield without selling ERG. This was distinguished from uncollateralised lending (Exle) and noted as a meaningful boost to ecosystem TVL and miner holding incentives. Community reception was positive.

### Ledger Hardware Wallet Integration
Ledger integration was a recurring topic throughout the month with no resolution. The core obstacle is the severe RAM constraint on Nano S (<1 KB available), which makes it difficult to display all TX outputs as required by Ledger's security audit. Nano S+ and X are unaffected but the standard Nano S represents a significant portion of the user base. Work passed between internal teams and third-party developers (Tesseract, anon_br), with a rough estimate of "a couple of months" given mid-October but no hard commitment made.

### KuCoin ERG Suspension
KuCoin suspended ERG deposits and withdrawals for approximately 1.5 weeks due to UTXO dust accumulation (60,000+ UTXOs) created by miners sending fractional payments without consolidation. Withdrawals resumed on 2022-10-21. The episode illustrated how improper UTXO management at scale creates downstream exchange problems, and reinforced the value of wallet-level UTXO consolidation tools (Nautilus has built-in consolidation; normal blockchain usage consolidates naturally over time).

### Stablecoin Protocol Clarification
Community discussion clarified the relationship between AgeUSD (the protocol), SigmaUSD (Ergo's implementation, comprising SigmaUSD the stablecoin and SigmaRSV the reserve token), and Djed (a rebranded implementation of the same protocol, developed in partnership with Emurgo/IOHK for Cardano). SigmaUSD maintains 400–800% collateralisation. The Dexy Protocol was distinguished as a separate alternative using DEX incentives rather than collateral for stability.

### ChainCash Proposal
Kushti published the ChainCash proposal on the Ergo forum — a spender-signed currency system on Ergo enabling new payment patterns and privacy features. The proposal generated interest but limited technical follow-up discussion within the general channel during the month.

### AVL Tree Security
Following the $600M BSC bridge hack traced to an AVL tree implementation vulnerability, kushti confirmed Ergo's AVL+ tree uses domain prefixing to prevent extension attacks. Critically, verification is performed at protocol level via ErgoScript rather than within contracts, making it more secure and independently testable — a structural advantage over the BSC implementation.

### Block Time & Speed Improvements
Discussion of potential block time reductions or sub-block architectures was consistently deferred to post-Ergoversary. The key technical constraint is that many contracts and dApps rely on block height rather than timestamps, so faster blocks have non-trivial ripple effects. A testnet environment exists for parameter tweaking but requires a community volunteer to lead coordination — kushti invited interested parties to DM for Telegram group access.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Wombo22, 2022-10-03): Can you explain what bulletproofs are?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge proof technique allowing specific statements — for example, range proofs as used in Monero — to be proven without revealing underlying data. They can also prove generic statements as a trustless alternative to SNARKs (no trusted setup required). The goal is to have bulletproof verification done in ErgoScript, which would enable a wide variety of zero-knowledge protocols alongside Ergo contracts, including hybrid contracts that verify proofs on-chain while keeping sensitive data undisclosed.

- **Q** (@Steven, 2022-10-03): How does a merge-mined sidechain work from a mining perspective?
  **A** (@kushti): Sidechain constructions will be published on the Ergo forum. A merge-mined sidechain delivers sidechain data with trustless pegging via smart contracts. An emission contract can reward mainchain miners for delivering sidechain data. Unlike BIP 301 (Bitcoin's approach), Ergo uses a trustless relay to update sidechain data on the Ergo mainchain with proof-of-work verification — providing clearer security guarantees.

- **Q** (@Luke Graysmith, 2022-10-03): Can the same DDoS attack that hit another chain happen on Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Many spam attack vectors against the p2p layer and mempool have been fixed quietly in recent months. Since version 4.0.44, nodes limit transactions downloaded from a single peer. Fee markets handle spam organically — as legitimate users raise fees, low-fee spam transactions fall behind and eventually flush from the mempool. Node operators can also raise minimum propagation fee rates to prevent low-fee transactions from entering their mempool at all. A fee estimation API exists in the node (though not yet widely used by wallets or dApps) to help users set appropriate fees for next-block inclusion.

- **Q** (@community, 2022-10-03): What happens to spammer transactions in the mempool if they're never picked up?
  **A** (@kushti): Transactions time out and are dropped from the mempool after a period if miners don't include them. In a fee-market scenario, transactions paying below the prevailing rate are progressively deprioritised. Once the spam event subsides and block space is available, lower-fee transactions may eventually be included — or they simply expire.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2022-10-24): Who is responsible for the Ledger integration delay — Ledger or the Ergo side?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Both. The parties are at different points in the coordination cycle and are mutually pending on each other's progress.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-10-24): Can a smart contract automatically burn sent funds, or does ERG have to go somewhere?
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge): ERG cannot technically be burned on Ergo — storage rent means any "burned" ERG would eventually be claimed. Alternatives are to redirect it to transaction fees or back to the originating address. Any contract locking ERG must allow someone to take it eventually.

- **Q** (@Odiseus Me, 2022-10-29): With box consolidation tools becoming standard, will miners lose storage rent incentives?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Storage rent is primarily a chain-bloat and dust-prevention mechanism, not a primary miner income source. Any storage rent collected is a bonus. Both transaction fees and storage rent create miner incentives, but the system is not designed to depend on dust accumulation for miner revenue.

- **Q** (@Hassen, 2022-10-20): What is the relationship between AgeUSD, SigmaUSD, and Djed?
  **A** (@community): AgeUSD is the protocol/design. SigmaUSD is Ergo's implementation (comprising SigmaUSD the stablecoin and SigmaRSV the reserve token, over-collateralised at 400–800%). Djed is a rebranded implementation of the same protocol, developed by Emurgo and IOHK for Cardano. The Dexy Protocol is a distinct alternative that uses DEX incentives rather than collateral for stability.

- **Q** (@Hassen, 2022-10-20): How can an algorithmic stablecoin work if you can't mint the base currency (ERG)?
  **A** (@community): You don't mint ERG — you use ERG as collateral to mint either SigmaUSD (the stablecoin) or SigmaRSV (the reserve token). Minting SigmaRSV is blocked above 800% collateral; minting SigmaUSD is blocked below 400% — these bounds prevent bank-run scenarios.

- **Q** (@Hassen, 2022-10-29): What does UTXO mean by "one-time objects" and "formalising privacy leaks"?
  **A** (@Armeanio): In an account-based model (like Ethereum), a transaction touching an account exposes its full state to anyone observing the chain. In the UTXO model, each box is a one-time object — spent or unspent — and a transaction only touches the UTXOs it directly involves. This limits information leakage to the participants in that specific transaction rather than exposing a global account state.

- **Q** (@ArØhβΣ, 2022-10-14): What is the non-interactive share proof (SNISP) ErgoHack project?
  **A** (@ArØhβΣ): An algorithm to verify individual miner work on-chain. Enables SmartPool hosting and SNISP collateralisation for decentralised mining with verifiable shared block reward distribution.

- **Q** (@gg, 2022-10-29): Is SQL useful for Ergo blockchain development?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Yes — the blockchain itself is slow for queries, so SQL databases cache chain data. Ergo Explorer uses PostgreSQL for indexing. Backend developers working on Ergo ecosystem tooling regularly use SQL.

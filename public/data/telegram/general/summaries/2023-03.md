# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: March 2023

---

## Executive Summary

March 2023 was one of the most active months in Ergo's development history, dominated by three interconnected themes: the imminent launch of the Rosen Bridge cross-chain infrastructure, significant progress on next-generation stablecoin and oracle infrastructure (Dexy, Oracle Pools v2), and sustained community-led discussion around scalability, privacy, and ecosystem onboarding. The month culminated in the Ergo Summit: Cypherpunk Finance showcase and the Rosen Bridge entering public beta, marking a major step toward Ergo's cross-chain future.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Rosen Bridge: From Stress Testing to Public Beta

The Rosen Bridge was the single most discussed topic across all four weeks. In early March, the bridge was completing malicious-action stress testing (forks, DoS simulations, data source malfunctions). By mid-March, a private testing group was formed with Sigmanauts as the first cohort, with plans to expand to public testing via ErgoFaucet one week later. By the final week of March, the bridge had entered formal public beta on both Ergo and Cardano networks with soft-launch test tokens.

The bridge uses a two-layer architecture: Watchers monitor and report events on source chains, while Guards verify and sign multi-sig transactions to release funds on target chains. The initial guard set is small (~10 guards), with additional guards to be onboarded as the bridge expands to new chains (BTC, BSC, ETH planned). Transfer times are deliberately slow (80+ minutes for Cardano↔Ergo) to prioritise security over speed. The beta fee was set at 10%, expected to decrease for mainnet. Full public launch was estimated at approximately one month from the end of March.

### Oracle Pools v2 & Dexy Stablecoin

kushti released Oracle Pools v2.0.0-beta6 in W10, introducing a fundamental redesign: oracle operators are rewarded in custom protocol tokens rather than ERG, eliminating dust issues. The reward mechanism creates a symbiotic feedback loop — stablecoin protocols (Dexy, ChainCash) split protocol fees to buy reward tokens from liquidity pools, directly incentivising oracle operators. This design is documented in EIP pull request #41.

Dexy development progressed steadily throughout the month: backend and UI in active development, Spectrum LP integration planned, testnet deployment largely complete by end of March. kushti updated simulations and payout contracts in W13, with a whitepaper update and explainer video planned. SigGold was identified as a likely first production deployment of the full Oracle Pools v2 + Dexy stack for live testing before a potential SigmaUSD upgrade. The Dexy protocol is asset-agnostic — additional collateral types (beyond gold) require only a new LP and oracle feed.

### Ergo Summit: Cypherpunk Finance

The Ergo Summit ran 2023-03-18 to 2023-03-19 as a 14-video showcase of ErgoHack VI projects. Community voting (653 votes from 272 members) selected Ergo Tizen (gold), Lithos (silver), and Explorer UI improvements (bronze) for community fund distribution. Notable featured projects included: Lilium (NFT minting/distribution infrastructure), Analog Ergo (cross-chain atomic swap marketplace), Sigma Subscriptions (on-chain subscription framework), SigmaO (options trading via EIP-4 tokens), Lithos Protocol (collateralised mining pool contracts), and SingleTxSwap (Discord-native token swapping).

### eUTXO Model, ErgoScript & Developer Education

A recurring theme throughout March was the conceptual gap between Ethereum-trained developers and Ergo's eUTXO model. Key clarifications repeatedly surfaced: ErgoScript is a guard script (evaluates to true/false at spending time), not a deployed contract in the EVM sense. All possible ErgoScript programs already "exist"; a box with a script becomes active only when a UTXO is sent to that address. Understanding the eUTXO model is a prerequisite for writing ErgoScript effectively — jumping straight to language syntax without this foundation is the most common mistake. Max box size is 4 KB. ChatGPT-generated ErgoScript was demonstrated to contain multiple hallucinated function names (e.g., `createProof`, `TxUtil.createTx`) that do not exist; Appkit (JVM SDK) handles transaction construction separately from ErgoScript spending conditions.

### Storage Rent & NFT Loss Risk

Storage rent edge cases generated sustained discussion, particularly the risk of valuable NFTs being permanently lost to miners if a box contains insufficient ERG after ~4 years of inactivity (full depletion takes ~32 years for 1 ERG). Community consensus held that: storage rent applies to ERG only (tokens can be preserved if the box is spent before ERG is depleted); the timer resets on every transaction; wallets should warn users at creation time rather than relying on post-hoc education. No protocol-level solution was finalised, but proposals included smart contract vaults with auto-renewal and wallet-side minimum ERG enforcement.

### Scalability & Block Speed Discussion

The question of whether to reduce Ergo's 2-minute block time was debated across multiple weeks. Consensus leaned strongly against L1 parameter changes: applications dependent on block height/timestamp create compatibility constraints, and worst-case security considerations informed the original design. Armeanio explicitly stated "scaling on L1 is a bad idea long term." Community discussion coalesced around sub-blocks, sidechains, and layer 2 solutions as the preferred path, with a dedicated testnet channel available for those wanting to empirically study parameter tweaking. kushti noted that the concrete set of existing production contracts (Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, GARD protocols) now provides a realistic benchmark for optimisation that was unavailable earlier.

### Wallet Ecosystem Developments

Several wallet developments landed in March: Ledger support for Ergo became available (via Nautilus) following a firmware update, though the app remains in developer mode rather than standard Ledger Live. A 20 different token ID per transaction limit on Ledger was clarified (not 20 tokens total). Satergo announced an upcoming release adding Windows installer, ErgoPay support, Ledger support, DeFi transaction labelling, and all fiat currencies via CoinGecko. Minotaur wallet improved multisig secret data persistence. Paper wallet QR code restoration (mnemonic string format) was committed to for implementation by Anon_BR and MrStahlfelge. Mobile box consolidation on Android was confirmed working on every transaction.

### Privacy: ErgoMixer & Sigma Protocols

ErgoMixer was explained as a peer-to-peer exchange contract using Sigma protocols — not a centralised service like Tornado Cash and therefore not subject to the same killswitch risk. Ring signatures allow proving transaction knowledge without revealing which UTXO is yours from a decoy set; no trusted setup required (unlike SNARKs). Stealth addresses were flagged as coming soon for private dApp interactions. Discrete box sizes (10/50/100/250 ERG) and configurable mixing rounds (30/60/90/180) were documented.

### Palmyra (formerly ZenGate) & Chainlink Integration

Dan Friedman explained the Palmyra use case: on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralised trade financing, using Chainlink proof-of-reserve functions via a proxy combined with IoT sensors (camera, weight/motion) to provide continuous proof of commodity deposits in bonded warehouses. The integration was framed as complementary rather than competing with native Ergo Oracle Pools — plans include investing token sale proceeds to build out Ergo Oracle Pool v2 infrastructure. The broader point was made that Ergo should welcome companies using any subset of its ecosystem rather than demanding exclusive commitment.

### SigmaFi Growth

SigmaFi TVL crossed $10K in W11 and $13.3K by W12, growing at over $1K/day. Uses babel fees infrastructure. Yield farming for Spectrum Finance completed stress testing with positive results and was expected to go live before end of March.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@TMR.ΣRG, 2023-03-01): How has performance improved since version 5.0?
  **A** (@Luivatra): TPS is not the right metric; the biggest gain in 5.0 was optimisation for complex transactions. Current load is ~6k transactions/day; an entire day's transactions could fit in a single block if max cost were increased.

- **Q** (@Pulsarz, 2023-03-01): How is ErgoScript actually deployed on-chain?
  **A** (@Pulsarz / @Luivatra): ErgoScript is compiled to ErgoTree, then to an address. There is no deployment step — all possible scripts already "exist." A box becomes active only when a UTXO is sent to that script address. ErgoScript defines spending conditions; Appkit (JVM SDK) constructs and submits transactions.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2023-03-01): What is the data limit for a box?
  **A** (community): 4 KB maximum box size.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-03-07): How will Oracle Pools v2 reward tokens be valued, and what happens if Dexy doesn't generate enough fees?
  **A** (@kushti): Dexy splits protocol fees — part to bank reserves, part to buy reward tokens from LP. This creates a self-reinforcing loop: stablecoin adoption → fee generation → token buyback → oracle incentivisation. If fees are insufficient, LP token price drops, reducing protocol-based oracle incentives; manual sponsorship (as in v1) and revenue from other dApps using the same pool provide fallback support.

- **Q** (@eπ, 2023-03-10): Are browser extension wallets as safe as desktop app wallets?
  **A** (@Aberg): No. Browser extensions face risks from browser bugs allowing inter-extension data access, centralised distribution control (bans, malicious updates), loss of software control (delisting), and shared browser-managed data storage. Desktop apps provide explicit wallet file control. Mobile apps (Android/iOS) provide stronger process isolation than desktop; desktop is comparable to mobile only under Qubes OS.

- **Q** (@Hassen, 2023-03-16): Is IP address linked to Ergo transactions?
  **A** (@qx()): No; Ergo is a public ledger but provides stronger privacy via ErgoMixer and Sigma protocols. Ring signatures allow proving transaction knowledge without revealing which output is yours from a decoy set.

- **Q** (@A P, 2023-03-16): Should I consolidate DCA'd boxes?
  **A** (@qx()): Yes. Advantages: dApps may refuse highly fragmented inputs; storage rent is charged per box after 4 years of inactivity. Consolidation is straightforward — send all assets to yourself from the same wallet; no CEX required. Mobile wallets consolidate automatically on every transaction.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2023-03-16): Storage rent risk for NFTs in low-ERG boxes — what happens?
  **A** (@Flying Pig / community): If a box contains tokens but ERG is depleted by storage rent, the tokens go to miners. With 1 ERG, full depletion takes ~32 years. Storage rent timer resets on every transaction.

- **Q** (@PalaZ, 2023-03-22): What is a SigmaProp?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Understand eUTXO before looking at ErgoScript — jumping straight to language syntax without the foundational model is the most common mistake new developers make.

- **Q** (@Mahdyar, 2023-03-22): Does storage rent mean 1 ERG in my wallet will be less after 4 years?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Only if it remains unmoved; the timer resets each time you make a transaction.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2023-03-29): How accurate is ChatGPT-generated ErgoScript?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Multiple hallucinated functions — `createProof`, `getTransaction`, `TxUtil.createTx`, `serialize`, `encode58` do not exist in ErgoScript; these are Appkit (Scala) functions. ErgoScript defines spending conditions only; use Appkit to construct and sign transactions for submission.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2023-03-29): What determines which Watchers are involved in a Rosen Bridge event?
  **A** (@MHS_SAM): Each chain has its own set of Watchers. Running the Watcher code and obtaining reporting permits by locking Rosen tokens is sufficient to join. Reporting is two-step: commit to an event, then reveal when enough commitments are present.

- **Q** (@Siavash, 2023-03-29): What is the difference between Rosen Bridge and Spectrum DEX?
  **A** (@qx()): Spectrum is a DEX for Ergo native assets. Rosen Bridge connects multiple chains (currently Cardano) and wraps tokens from external chains; a direct Cardano-to-Ergo swap sidechain is a longer-term goal.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-03-29): Is ERG supply truly capped?
  **A** (community): Yes; hard cap is written in code. No additional ERG can ever be minted, comparable to Bitcoin's 21M cap.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-03-07): How flexible is Dexy for assets beyond gold?
  **A** (@kushti): The protocol is asset-agnostic. Adding a new collateral type requires only a new LP and oracle feed.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Ledger official release timeline**: Ergo app remains in developer mode only; no confirmed date for standard Ledger Live integration. Ergo Foundation has stated it will not pay to accelerate Ledger's review process.
- **SigmaUSD upgrade to Oracle Pools v2**: Depends on Dexy/SigGold production testing completion and finalisation of Armeanio's RoLR SIPS proposal. No timeline committed.
- **Storage rent NFT protection at protocol level**: Community recognises the risk of valuable tokens being lost to miners in low-ERG boxes but no consensus on whether to solve this at protocol level, wallet level, or via smart contract vaults.
- **Rosen Bridge tokenomics finalisation**: Whether Rosen will issue its own token, guard/watcher reward structure, and long-term fee model still being determined.
- **Rosen Bridge guard decentralisation**: Initial era uses a small, known guard set (some slots reserved for team); process for expanding and decentralising guard selection not yet formally specified.
- **Ergo Explorer search function fix**: Confirmed broken and assigned to Dmitry Usov; no ETA provided.
- **Block time reduction research**: Active testnet channel exists but no volunteer has stepped forward to lead the empirical study; kushti has invited anyone passionate about the topic to DM him.
- **ChainCash and Dexy backend open-sourcing**: Both confirmed as forthcoming but without specific dates ("soon™").
- **Trust Wallet integration**: Ergo not yet in CoinGecko top 100 by market cap; ineligible for Trust Wallet integration without meeting that threshold. No roadmap to address.
- **Spectrum Finance CMC/CoinGecko listing**: Gazza confirmed ongoing attempts since SPF token launch with no response from aggregators.

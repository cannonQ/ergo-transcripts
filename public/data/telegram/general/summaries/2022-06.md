# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: June 2022

---

## Executive Summary

June 2022 was dominated by three major threads: the successful activation of EIP-27 (emission soft fork at block 777,217 on June 21), growing ecosystem development highlighted by ErgoHack VI and Ergoversary (Ergo's 3rd mainnet anniversary on July 1), and persistent community focus on a long-anticipated tier-1 CEX listing that remained unannounced throughout the month due to NDA constraints. Infrastructure themes — wallet reliability, mining onboarding, oracle development, and Layer 2 scaling philosophy — ran consistently across all four weeks.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EIP-27 Emission Soft Fork
EIP-27 activated at block 777,217 on approximately June 21, reducing block rewards from 63 ERG to 48 ERG via a one-time 12 ERG reduction routed to the re-emission contract, with the standard 3 ERG per epoch reduction schedule resuming thereafter. Over 92% of miners' hashpower voted in support. Two new tokens were injected into the emission contract: a singleton token for tracking and a re-emission token sent to mining reward boxes. Pool operators were required to update configurations to reflect the new reward structure, and some pools displayed stale reward data in the immediate aftermath. Block reward emission is now projected to complete around 2044, with no further one-time reductions planned. [@kushti, msg#286727–286728, 2022-07-02; @Flying Pig, msg#286753, 2022-07-02]

### Tier-1 Exchange Listing
The most persistent community concern throughout June was a tier-1 CEX listing described as having missed an end-of-May deadline. [@Armeanio, msg#279491, 2022-06-02] confirmed the integration was completed on Ergo's side but remained blocked by the exchange's NDA, making public announcement impossible. Speculation centered on Huobi, Kraken, and OKX, but no confirmation emerged across the entire month. KuCoin separately reopened ERG deposits and withdrawals on mainnet during the final week. [@RETRO, msg#285356, 2022-06-27]

### Ergoversary & ErgoHack VI
Ergo's 3rd mainnet anniversary (Ergoversary) was celebrated on July 1 with a video marathon launching at 10am UTC. [@Andy L, msg#286277, 2022-07-01] ErgoHack VI project excitement ran through the early weeks, with notable submissions including ErgoSphere (off-chain bots/mixer/node packaged UI), dApp Step, and Ergo Names (ENS-like naming service). ErgoHack Fest voting closed June 27 with top-5 proposals receiving 40 ERG each. The community raised feedback about formalizing judging criteria (5–8 weighted scoring points), with Armeanio acknowledging the need but no formal timeline given. [@Armeanio, msg#286430, 2022-07-01]

### Wallet Ecosystem: Yoroi Recovery, Nautilus, Satergo, Ledger
Yoroi remained unreliable throughout June. EMURGO and the Ergo Foundation issued a joint statement confirming new management undertaking "massive refactoring back to front" to resolve scalability and memory issues. [@Armeanio, msg#279569–572, 2022-06-03] Community consistently redirected users to Nautilus (browser extension) and Satergo. Satergo v0.0.3 launched mid-month with 50% reduced program size, transaction history, password-required sends, and price charts. [@Aberg, msg#284310, 2022-06-22] Ledger support remained in side-load-only status for Nano S; Nano X support was described as "coming soon" with no ETA. iOS mobile wallet was fully developed but blocked by the Ergo Foundation bank account resolution required for TestFlight release.

### Mining: GPU Profitability, FPGA, and Subpooling
Post-Ethereum PoS merge discussions brought significant GPU miner interest to Ergo throughout June. Autolykos was consistently described as one of the lowest-heat mining algorithms, with NBMiner, SRBMiner, and Team-Red Miner as recommended software. Wolf9466 shared FPGA development progress, achieving ~640 MH/s across multiple XCVU35P FPGAs at 234W, while cautioning that FPGA mining requires significant expertise. GetBlok.io surpassed 1 TH/s hashrate and launched the anetaBTC subpool, enabling miners to earn wrapped BTC alongside ERG; GuapSwap's multi-token swap capability (funded by a 10K SigmaUSD Good Whale Grant) complemented this mechanism. The eTOSI airdrop from anetaBTC successfully distributed to ~20,000 addresses split across multiple transactions, functioning as a positive network stress test. [@Glasgow, msg#282946, 2022-06-17]

### Palmyra / Chainlink Partnership & Oracle Development
Dan Friedman detailed Palmyra's use of Chainlink proof-of-reserve functions through Winter Protocol to issue on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralized trade financing. IoT devices — cameras, weight sensors, and motion sensors depending on commodity — provide continuous proof-of-existence for bonded warehouse reserves. The architecture is explicitly described as not a zero-sum game with Ergo-native oracles: Oracle Pool v1 is operated by Ergo Foundation members and external contributors; Oracle Pool v2 runs for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards. Plans were announced to invest token sale capital in building native Ergo Oracle infrastructure as a longer-term complement to Chainlink. [@Dan Friedman, context across W22–W25]

### Layer 2, Sidechains & Speed Improvements
kushti consistently described the Layer 2 and speed improvement discussions as being in draft/sketch stage, with community drafts covering microblocks, sub-blocks, and faster confirmation protocols. The key insight articulated was that concrete optimization targets require a known application set — the current contract ecosystem (GARD protocols, Oracle Pools, Spectrum AMM pool contracts, Trade House, lending contracts) now provides such targets. Sidechain constructions leveraging NIPoPoWs for trustless relay and merge-mined data delivery were described as being published to the forum, with a BIP 301-equivalent framework for trustless pegging. A testnet environment for parameter tweaking (difficulty, block time) was noted as available but requiring a motivated champion to run. [@kushti, multiple messages across W22–W26]

### Stablecoins: SigmaUSD Health & Dexy Design
SigmaUSD reserve ratio reached approximately 400% by late June, indicating strong collateral backing. The Dexy stablecoin paper draft became available in the SigmaUSD channel; Dexy uses an oracle-and-arbitrage mechanism and can be pegged to any commodity with a price feed (including a jocular reference to a "Costco hotdog peg" as stable reference). [@Armeanio, msg#285624, 2022-06-28] The key architectural distinction: SigmaUSD is over-collateralized algorithmic; Dexy is arbitrage-based using a DEX mechanism. Oracle Pool v2 runs the Dexy Gold instance with GORT rewards.

### Ergo's Economic Design Philosophy
Recurring discussions across the month reinforced Ergo's core design principles: fair launch (no premine, no VC, 4% treasury from mined blocks), permanent PoW for decentralization and GPU accessibility, storage rent (demurrage) recycling dormant coins to miners as an alternative to fixed-supply cap mechanics, and legal review confirming ERG is not classified as a security. The eUTXO Alliance was acknowledged as comprising only Cardano and Ergo as true eUTXO chains, with Nervos (cell model) and Alephium (stateful UTXO) as related but distinct approaches. [@Flying Pig, msg#286469, 2022-07-01; @CW, msg#286477, 2022-07-01]

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@PalaZ, msg#278789, 2022-06-01): Can ERG be burned through an ErgoScript contract sending to a burn address to avoid storage rent permanently?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @MrStahlfelge, msg#278809–278837): ERG cannot be truly burned. Moving ERG to a false/uncontrolled contract is closest to burning but storage rent will eventually recycle those coins back to miners. Storage rent is charged per box, not per address. To preserve all funds, send everything to yourself every 4 years; doing so creates a fresh box safe for another 4 years.

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, msg#279217, 2022-06-02): Opening the same wallet seed on three phones results in zero balance on non-last-used devices.
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge, msg#279223): Nautilus tends to move all funds to newly created addresses. Go to the addresses tab in the mobile app and manually derive addresses to access funds across devices.

- **Q** (@Ntuthuko1983, msg#281534–281535, 2022-06-13): What is the difference between transaction fees and gas fees, and how does Ergo sustain miners after all ERG is mined?
  **A** (@Glasgow, msg#281537–281539): Gas fees are unpredictable and charged even on failed transactions; eUTXO transaction fees are fixed (typically 0.001 ERG). Post-emission, miner income will be supplemented by storage rent, subpooling fees, and sidechain fees.

- **Q** (@zhang zee, msg#282659, 2022-06-17): How does ergo-assembler work — two transactions or one?
  **A** (@Luivatra, msg#282674): Two transactions: the dApp generates a proxy contract, the user sends funds to the proxy, then the assembler uses the proxy box as input to generate a follow-up transaction with specific register data. Mostly superseded by dApp connectors now.

- **Q** (@Unknown, msg#283285, 2022-06-19): Can a smart contract automatically burn a token or NFT after a validity period expires (e.g., a hotel access pass)?
  **A** (@Luivatra, msg#283287; @Unknown, msg#283295): A contract cannot burn an NFT held in someone else's wallet. However, a contract can sit in escrow and burn if not redeemed by a deadline, or the NFT can be invalidated (becoming a stub) while the access logic rejects it.

- **Q** (@co met, msg#286468, 2022-07-01): What eUTXO-based blockchain projects exist besides Cardano and Ergo?
  **A** (@Flying Pig, msg#286469; @CW, msg#286477): Only Cardano and Ergo use the explicit eUTXO model. Nervos uses a "cell model"; Alephium uses "stateful UTXO." The UTXO Alliance was formed for collaborative research across these approaches.

- **Q** (@Giufa, msg#280963, 2022-06-08): What FPGA models enable Ergo mining? Can consumer FPGAs be used?
  **A** (@Wolf9466, msg#280967–280978): A single XCVU35P FPGA achieves 200+ MH/s; a Xilinx E300 with three FPGAs achieves 600+ MH/s at ~234W total. Small gaming FPGAs are not equivalent. FPGA miner development requires significant expertise — not beginner-friendly.

- **Q** (@M J, msg#287048, 2022-07-03): Can I stake ERG?
  **A** (@CW, msg#287049): Ergo is PoW — no native staking. Options include providing liquidity on Spectrum DEX, staking ErgoPad tokens, or exploring yield options at ergonaut.space/Guides/yield (watch for impermanent loss).

- **Q** (@Ahmad, msg#285263, 2022-06-27): Can personal wallets "go down" like Mt. Gox?
  **A** (@Unknown, msg#285266): Non-custodial wallets are access methods to the blockchain, not custodians of funds. For a wallet to "go down," the entire Ergo blockchain would need to go down. The wallet is analogous to a bank card; funds live on-chain, not in the wallet application.

- **Q** (@Aberg, msg#284371, 2022-06-22): Can wallets without mnemonic password support restore wallets created with a mnemonic password?
  **A** (@Aberg, msg#284371–284380): No. Wallets without mnemonic password support can only restore wallets created with an empty mnemonic password. The mnemonic password is treated as a separate field, not appended as an extra word, and compatibility across implementations is unclear.

# Ergo General — Monthly Summary: February 2026

---

## Executive Summary

February 2026 was dominated by stablecoin mechanics analysis (SigmaUSD, Dexy, USE), wallet UX improvements for non-technical users, and infrastructure health monitoring. Demurrage processing was observed functioning as intended at scale, while sub-blocks remained in testnet without a mainnet ETA. Community coordination around cross-chain outreach (Cardano, Rosen Bridge) and developer tooling (ErgoAuth, paper wallets, transcripts archive) was notably active.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Demurrage Processing & Chain Maintenance
Over 38,000 ERG was claimed during demurrage processing of old boxes, with 17.7M ERG rebuilt and approximately 0.2% demurrage charge overall. The event was treated as a practical demonstration of Ergo's storage rent mechanism functioning as a chain bloat mitigation tool at scale. No significant technical issues were reported.

### Stablecoin Dynamics: SigmaUSD, Dexy, and USE
SigmaUSD depegged upward to approximately $1.07–$1.08 during a downmarket period due to the AgeUSD reserve ratio mechanics: when the reserve ratio falls below 400%, new minting is blocked, preventing arbitrage from restoring the peg. Grayman identified shallow liquidity (fewer than 1,000 wallets holding 10,000+ ERG) as the underlying structural constraint across Ergo's stablecoin ecosystem. Dexy was noted as more market-responsive in peg maintenance, while USE's multi-chain capabilities offer a different design trade-off. The asymmetric behavior of SigmaUSD on major price upswings remains an open empirical question.

### Wallet UX for Non-Technical Users
Grayman proposed an encrypted key export model for mobile wallets: private key stays on-device, with a passphrase-encrypted backup exportable via email, enabling recovery on a new device without seed phrase management. The proposal generated substantive debate around email-based risk versus SIM-jacking risk, with no fatal flaw identified. Existing partial implementations were catalogued: Satergo (passphrase support), Minotaur (BIP39 passphrase), Zelcore. Patato55 independently shipped a BIP39 passphrase-enabled fork of the Ergo paper wallet, verified against Satergo, with an obfuscation section that physically warps the mnemonic when folded to resist casual viewing or photography.

### ErgoAuth Implementation (EIP-0028)
bmfink resolved a Terminus iOS wallet authentication failure ("reply URL not on host" error) that did not affect Nautilus desktop. A full Wallet-Authentication Guide was published covering ClawdBot/OpenClaw Gateway implementation using ErgoAuth with HTTPS session management, providing a reusable reference for dApp developers integrating mobile wallet authentication.

### Infrastructure Monitoring & Outages
A notable multi-system outage on 2026-02-08 affected Spectrum Finance, the ErgoDEX API, Crux Finance, Cyberverse, and the Sigmanauts Mining Calculator simultaneously. In response, Arohbe published ergostatus.degens.world, a real-time API/infrastructure status dashboard with Telegram bot integration, filling a gap in ecosystem observability tooling.

### Sub-blocks Development
Sub-blocks remained in testnet with recent changes and redeployments reported. No mainnet readiness timeline was provided.

### Rosen Bridge & Cardano Outreach
A Cardano Over Coffee Twitter Space was held on 2026-02-03 with Armeanio discussing Rosen Bridge integration proposals for Catalyst Fund 15. Audio was normalized and published via the Sigmanauts Podcast. The event represented continued cross-community outreach around Rosen Bridge as shared infrastructure.

### Transcripts Archive
CannonQ's ergo-transcripts platform added 26 additional meetings, bringing the indexed total to 76 calls and 51+ hours of content, with 500+ meetings still to be processed.

### Reddit AMA on ErgoBook
A Reddit AMA was scheduled and coordinated for February 5th at 17:00 UTC, involving kushti, Joe Armeanio, Grayman, qx(), and others. Promotion coordination spanned Twitter and Reddit.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@saturn_return, 2026-02-XX): What price do miners consider profitable for ERG given cost vs. reward?
  **A** (@T_Mas): Profitability depends heavily on energy costs by region (Germany is expensive; other regions cheaper). Home miners often mine at a loss using rigs as heaters; commercial miners require positive margin to cover operating costs. See hashrate.no for profit calculations.

- **Q** (@kushti, 2026-02-02): What are the main differences between Ergo Raffle v1 and Bene?
  **A** (@Josemi): Raffle uses a lottery incentive model (one winner). Bene uses a Proof of Funding Token (proportional to contribution). Bene supports ERG or a single alternative token per campaign; Ergo Raffle v1 is ERG-only. Bene is fully on-chain with no server; campaigns include on-chain reviews.

- **Q** (@kushti, 2026-02-02): Can a Bene campaign accept ERG + USE + SigmaUSD simultaneously?
  **A** (@Josemi): No — each campaign must select a single asset. A hackathon requiring multiple contribution types would need three separate campaigns. A UI update is planned to improve this workflow.

- **Q** (@bmfink, 2026-02-07): Terminus wallet returns "reply URL not on host" ErgoAuth error; Nautilus desktop works correctly with the same server. How to resolve?
  **A** (@bmfink): Resolved through debugging mobile-specific authentication flow. Published a full Wallet-Authentication Guide with working implementation patterns for ErgoAuth + HTTPS session management. https://ipfs.io/ipfs/bafkreian2uemscp4kdozgyj2pah2stek2nf6t2abvnupiwq675cwe7px4a

- **Q** (@T_Mas, 2026-02-08): Why did SigmaUSD depeg upward to $1.07–$1.08 during a downmarket?
  **A** (@Richi): By design — SigmaUSD cannot be minted when the reserve ratio falls below 400%. If the market price is above $1.00, arbitrage cannot restore the peg from above. Existing SigmaUSD holders benefit. Dexy maintains its peg more robustly in this scenario; USE is more responsive due to its multi-chain design.

- **Q** (@Ozzle, 2026-02-07): Why include the actual seed phrase in the paper wallet obfuscation section?
  **A** (@Patato): Intentional design — the obfuscation section specifically warps the real mnemonic text such that when the wallet is folded along the dashed lines, the overlapping text creates visual confusion, resisting casual viewing or photography. The distortion is not random; it is geometrically designed around the actual seed words.

- **Q** (@Grayman, 2026-02-05): Is a mobile wallet design viable where the private key stays on-device, with an encrypted seed backup exportable via passphrase to email for recovery on a new device?
  **A** (@Grayman et al.): No fatal flaw identified. Risk trade-off: email compromise vs. SIM-jacking (both are real attack vectors). Satergo, Minotaur (BIP39 passphrase), and Zelcore offer partial implementations of this pattern. The gap is a unified, non-technical UX that abstracts seed phrase management entirely.

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: February 2022

---

## Executive Summary

February 2022 was dominated by three converging themes: the EIP-27 emission soft fork moving toward activation with strong miner support, rapid wallet ecosystem maturation (Nautilus, SafeW, Satergo, Ledger integration), and foundational scaling research culminating in the Ergo Summit AMA. Ecosystem infrastructure — Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, ErgoMixer, ErgoPad staking — all advanced meaningfully, establishing February as one of the most productive development months to that point.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EIP-27 Emission Soft Fork
The proposed reduction of block rewards (66 ERG → 54 ERG) to extend long-term mining sustainability was the month's defining governance event. Community and miner sentiment was strongly positive throughout: GetBlok's Proof-of-Vote system reached ~82–90% approval by mid-month, and by week's end all major mining pools had signaled support, with on-chain adoption reaching ~93%. The vote threshold required is >90% miner support. Activation timing was confirmed as longer than initially expected due to testnet testing requirements, but no blocking technical issues emerged. The forum discussion at ergoforum.org/t/emission-soft-fork-proposal/2996 served as the primary reference. A minority community member proposed an "ERG2" successor coin as an alternative; this was rejected by core contributors as more problematic than the original proposal.

### Wallet Ecosystem Maturation
February saw the Ergo wallet landscape consolidate and improve substantially. Nautilus moved through private beta with its dApp connector, ErgoPay was released on Android open beta enabling mobile dApp interaction, and ErgoMixer was integrated into SafeW. Ledger support was implemented across Nautilus, SafeW, and Satergo simultaneously via a coordinated ErgoHack effort — only a signing bug fix remained outstanding at month's end. Yoroi continued to suffer memory leaks and sync failures, accelerating community migration to newer wallets. The Minotaur multi-platform wallet also advanced, with MHS_SAM shifting focus from urgency there to Rosen Bridge work given the improved wallet landscape.

### Rosen Bridge Progress
The Rosen Bridge (Susy) watcher UI was reported as nearly complete in early February, with UI integration and final testing estimated at 10–15 days to live launch. The bridge operates one-way (Solana→Ergo) by current mining agreement, though the underlying protocol supports two-way transfers. An Ergo–Cardano bridge was discussed as architecturally simpler due to eUTXO model compatibility on both sides, but remained in R&D with a Q2 2022 target explicitly flagged as "not straightforward." Watcher operators were advised to update to version 2.2.1 and run `docker compose pull` / `docker compose up -d`.

### Scaling Research & Speed Improvements
kushti provided substantive technical context on Ergo's scaling roadmap across multiple discussions. The v5.0 soft fork was confirmed to deliver 4–6x throughput improvement via a faster script engine and more accurate cost prediction, with theoretical throughput of ~45 tx/sec extensible to 5–10x via parallelization. A detailed performance report was published on GitHub. For faster finality without changing block time, sub-block confirmation protocols (Bitcoin-NG, Flux) were identified as the preferred path — block time itself cannot be adjusted without a very hard fork that would compromise NIPoPoW security. Draft proposals for sub-blocks, sidechains, and layer 2 solutions exist but no implementation path was formally chosen; a community-led testnet for parameter experimentation was proposed, with kushti inviting interested volunteers via DM.

### NIPoPoWs & Sidechain Infrastructure
kushti reported that NIPoPoW proofs and verification were stabilized, with P2P messaging and UTXO set snapshot database support ready. Download and reconstruction logic remained the outstanding phase. BIP-301-style merge-mined sidechain constructions were described as nearly finalized for forum publication, enabling mainchain miners to earn both ERG and sidechain tokens simultaneously via trustless relay mechanisms in smart contracts.

### Spectrum DEX & Trade House
Spectrum DEX passed $2M TVL during February. The order book model (Trade House) was discussed at length as offering superior transparency, slippage control, and partial fill support compared to AMM pools, which carry impermanent loss risk. Partial order fills work by duplicating the seller's box with residual amounts and matching parameters. Multiple upgrade keys (vs. a single central contract) were highlighted as a decentralization feature. Sharing of liquidity between Spectrum AMM pools and Trade House order books was identified as a future possibility.

### Ergo Summit & Community Governance
The Ergo Summit AMA on February 18 (13:00 UTC) featured kushti, Dan Friedman, and Armeanio. Technical presentations included Dionysis Zindros on soft forks and Johnny Wu on Handshake DNS. The AMA covered scaling, bridge status, oracle infrastructure, and the Palmyra (ZenGate) ecosystem — including a Chainlink partnership for on-chain warehouse receipts and decentralized trade financing using IoT-coupled proof-of-reserve oracles. Dan Friedman outlined intent to invest token-sale capital in building out native Ergo Oracle Pools beyond the two currently running.

### SigmaUSD / SigmaRSV Dynamics
Extended community discussion clarified the SigmaRSV investment thesis: SigmaRSV performs well in sideways/choppy markets but loses value during sustained downtrends as SigUSD holders redeem. It was deemed unsuitable for long-term DCA due to race conditions at redemption. SigmaUSD legacy users were reminded to migrate from old.sigmausd.io. Dexy Protocol was confirmed as undergoing design fixes with documentation to follow; kushti compared it favorably to Djed for exchange-rate-tied stablecoin use cases.

### ErgoPad & Paideia
ErgoPad staking went live on February 27, becoming the first staking option in the Ergo ecosystem. Staking provides tiered IDO allocations and token rewards. The Paideia DAO framework was introduced via a Medium post and whitepaper, establishing on-chain governance infrastructure for Ergo-based projects.

### Node Sync & Spam Protection
Multiple users encountered node sync stalls around blocks 400k–570k, particularly on Manjaro and Ubuntu Linux. Recommended mitigations: reduce `maxConnections` to 10, lower `-Xmx` to 3GB, use Java 11. The reference client from v4.0.44 onward implements per-peer transaction download limits as spam mitigation. Fee estimation API exists in the node but is untested and not used by applications — identified as a community contribution opportunity. Minimum propagation fee rate is configurable by node operators as a mempool filter.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-02-01): What is Ergo's scaling roadmap? Can Hydra handle smart contracts?
  **A** (@Glasgow): v5.0 soft fork delivers 4–6x throughput. No clear L2 winner yet; active research in Plasma, Hydra, others. Hydra suitable for some contracts (e.g., poker games); heavy dApps (DEX, lending, staking) need dedicated L2 protocols. v5.0 targets ~45 tx/sec; parallelization can extend to 5–10x. Full report on GitHub.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-02-01): Is "parallelization of transaction verification" equivalent to sharding?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Sharding is one type of transaction parallelization, but not all parallelization is sharding.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-02-15): Why is Ergo's 2-minute block time not adjustable as a miner-voted parameter?
  **A** (@kushti): Changing block time would break NIPoPoW security and require a very hard fork replacing NIPoPoWs with Flyclient or abandoning logspace SPV clients entirely. Sub-block confirmation protocols (Bitcoin-NG, Flux) are the planned alternative for faster finality without altering block time.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-02-15): Do sub-block confirmation protocols improve throughput as much as sharding, or mainly reduce finalization time?
  **A** (@kushti): Sub-block protocols optimize client bandwidth utilization, making bandwidth the primary bottleneck. Sharding spreads transactions across chains but requires cross-shard proof sizes and security guarantees. Commit chains may be a better alternative to sharding.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-02-09): Is the Rosen Bridge one-way or two-way? What is the timeline?
  **A** (@MHS_SAM): Two-way technically, but one-way (Solana→Ergo) by current mining agreement. One-way launches first; two-way can be enabled post-launch. Ergo–Cardano bridge is architecturally simpler due to eUTXO compatibility on both sides; Solana required additional conversion logic for the account model.

- **Q** (@Igor, 2022-02-11): Where should tokens be sent to properly burn them?
  **A** (@Igor, community): Do not send to a "burn address" — storage rent will recover them in ~4 years. Correct method: create a transaction with an input box containing the tokens but no output box. This feature is not yet implemented in wallets.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-02-23): Does Ergo support Babel fees (paying transaction fees in custom tokens)?
  **A** (@kushti): ErgoMixer already implements this — custom fee tokens are swapped to ERG via contract at execution time. This predates the IOHK Babel fees paper; Ergo's prior art is documented in a blog post.

- **Q** (@André, 2022-02-24): Full node sync stuck with empty mempool on co-located nodes after changing `chain{}` config.
  **A** (@kushti): `chain{}` settings (e.g., `minerRewardDelay`) are consensus-critical; mismatches between nodes break sync. Mining pools handle reward maturation via pre-funding from their own reserves rather than config modification — similar to Bitcoin's 100-block coinbase lock.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-02-25): What are bulletproofs and how do they relate to ErgoScript?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge technique allowing specific statements (e.g., range proofs) to be proven without a trusted setup — they are a form of Sigma protocol. The goal is to verify bulletproof statements within ErgoScript, which would enable a variety of zero-knowledge protocols (including Groth one-out-of-many proofs) to be used with Ergo contracts. This enables hybrid contracts where sensitive data is proven zero-knowledge on-chain without disclosure, opening significant new possibilities for privacy-preserving dApps.

- **Q** (@Anton, 2022-02-17): Full node sync stalls at blocks 400k–570k on Linux, consuming all CPU.
  **A** (@Glasgow): Reduce `maxConnections` to 10, lower `-Xmx` to 3GB, use Java 11. Issues observed on Manjaro and Ubuntu; database corruption from unexpected shutdown produces "Got GetReaders request in state" error. Consider using a pre-synced database on an alternative OS.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-02-27): How does Ergo handle spam attacks on the mempool? Can the same DDoS that hit another chain happen here?
  **A** (@kushti): From v4.0.44, the reference client limits transactions downloaded from a single peer. Node operators can raise minimum propagation fee rates to prevent low-fee spam from entering their mempool. Fee market dynamics naturally flush out spammers — miners prioritize higher-fee transactions; spam transactions time out and are dropped from the mempool after sufficient blocks without pickup. A fee estimation API exists in the node but is currently untested and unused by applications.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-02-11): What is SigmaRSV's investment thesis vs. SigmaUSD?
  **A** (community consensus): SigmaRSV performs well in sideways/choppy markets but loses value during sustained downtrends as SigmaUSD holders redeem. Not suitable for long-term DCA due to race conditions at redemption. Better used for short-term scalping or as a hedge during volatile sideways periods.

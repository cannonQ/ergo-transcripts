# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: 2017-12

---

## Executive Summary

December 2017 was defined by the launch of Ergo's public testnet and a community-wide debugging initiative, with multiple node releases (v0.1.0 through v0.1.3, with v0.1.4 pending) shipped across the month. Alongside technical progress, the team actively worked through foundational economic questions — particularly the EFYT-to-ERG swap mechanism and dual-chain token model — that would shape Ergo's mainnet launch structure. The month closed with a growing core team (expanded to 7 members) and a clearer, if still approximate, roadmap targeting mainnet readiness by Q1–Q2 2018.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Testnet Debugging Campaign
A community-wide debugging round launched in early December, with bounty rewards for bug reports submitted via GitHub. Participation required running a full node with a unique node name; Docker deployment was the recommended method, with a standard config published. Multiple releases followed in quick succession: v0.1.0 on 2017-12-05, v0.1.2 and v0.1.3 by week two, and v0.1.4 announced as pending after New Year holidays. A testnet explorer became available at `http://ergo.dsalab.de/`. Community members encountered syncing issues, Docker IP configuration problems, and port-forwarding challenges throughout, with the team and community helpers (notably @hawky) providing iterative guidance.

### EFYT Token Economics & Swap Mechanism
The EFYT token (tradeable on Waves DEX for BTC or WAVES) was confirmed as converting 1:1 to mainnet ERG upon launch, though by late December the team acknowledged this was not finalised. Community concern about a 10x supply disparity prompted discussion of alternatives: a 1:1.x multiplier, an ATH-based swap rate, or a dual-chain emission model (MoneyChain + DataChain) where each EFYT converts to one ERG on each chain. The exact mechanism remained unresolved at month's end, with a clearer picture expected by end of Q1 2018.

### Dual-Chain Model (MoneyChain / DataChain)
A significant architectural proposal formalised mid-month: a dual-chain structure with MoneyChain and DataChain operating in coordination via sidechaining. Under this model, 50 billion tokens would be emitted across both chains in the first year, with EFYT holders receiving tokens on both. The proposal was framed as a way to reward early adopters fairly while enabling distinct use cases for monetary and data functions. Specific incentive design and emission parameters remained under active consideration.

### Mining Timeline & Token Supply
Total ERG supply confirmed at 100 million, with a maximum of 50 million issuable in the first year through mining; the remaining 50 million would enter circulation over time. GPU mining was confirmed as available from mainnet launch; open mining was targeted for early Q2 2018 using the Equihash algorithm. Mobile device mining — enabled by the IADD (Improved Authenticated Dynamic Dictionaries) light-node architecture — was planned as a post-launch feature.

### Waves Collaboration & Ergo's Independent Identity
The relationship between Ergo and Waves was clarified repeatedly throughout the month: the two are independent projects sharing common founders and history from the NXT era, collaborating on specific technical areas (notably authenticated dictionary implementation and NG protocol testing) while maintaining separate roadmaps. Waves provides the DEX infrastructure for EFYT trading. The rationale for Ergo's independence — a focused team with a specific vision, built in Scala/Scorex — was articulated in response to questions about why Ergo was not incorporated into Cardano.

### Light Nodes, IADD, and Storage Architecture
The IADD protocol was highlighted as a core differentiator enabling light node operation on mobile devices — reducing hardware barriers to full participation and decentralisation. The Aspen protocol was referenced as the mechanism by which light nodes access relevant blockchain subsets. Ergo's approach was distinguished from general-purpose storage platforms (e.g., Storj): the focus is on nodes accessing pertinent on-chain data efficiently, not arbitrary data storage.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2017-12-05): Can Ergo testnet run on Windows?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Yes, Windows support confirmed.

- **Q** (@Ali Kara, 2017-12-05): Will feature development (authenticated dictionaries, smart contracts, NG protocol, mining) occur with the Waves team or independently?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Some features — particularly authenticated dictionaries — are being implemented collaboratively; the Waves team may adopt Ergo's solutions. Ergo is also drawing on Waves' NG protocol expertise. Both teams face common technical challenges.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2017-12-05): How will 50 billion tokens be distributed in the first year?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): EFYT is purchasable on Waves DEX and converts to mainnet ERG with potential holder discounts. Under the dual-chain model, each EFYT yields 1 ErgoMoney + 1 ErgoData; sidechaining enables MoneyChain/DataChain coordination. Emission parameters still to be defined.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2017-12-05): Can Ergo be mined?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): GPU mining available immediately at mainnet launch; mobile mining planned post-launch via IADD protocol.

- **Q** (@Amir Boziev, 2017-12-19): Which hashing algorithm is used?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Equihash.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2017-12-19): What is the maximum supply?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): 100 million total; up to 50 million issued during the first year through mining.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2017-12-19): Is EFYT a fork of Waves or a token?
  **A** (@Terri): It is a custom application token on the Waves platform, not a fork. It will be backed 1:1 by real ERG upon mainnet launch.

- **Q** (@PHBA | VietStaking Validator, 2017-12-28): Running testnet node with 0 peers — what's wrong?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Check Docker IP configuration and public IP accessibility. Try `http://<yourPublicIP>:9051/swagger`. Note that testnet nodes may have been shut down during the holiday period; the explorer was also offline.

- **Q** (@PHBA | VietStaking Validator, 2017-12-28): What is the difference between `scorex-bindAddress` and `network-bindAddress` in the config?
  **A** (@hawky): The REST API uses one for endpoint calls; the network binding handles blockchain sync protocol. Network binding is more critical for syncing.

- **Q** (@PHBA | VietStaking Validator, 2017-12-28): Should `network.bindAddress` be set to public IP, and is port forwarding required?
  **A** (@hawky): Yes to both — implement port forwarding from `<public IP>:9001` to `<private IP>:9001`.

- **Q** (@creeea, 2017-12-28): What makes Ergo unique?
  **A** (@PHBA | VietStaking Validator): The ability to run full light nodes on mobile devices.

- **Q** (@PHBA | VietStaking Validator, 2017-12-28): Why create Ergo separately from Cardano rather than incorporate it?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Ergo was founded in Scala/Scorex; having an independent vision and a focused team ensures better execution toward a specific goal.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2017-12-28): Do I need to own WAVES to pay exchange fees on Waves DEX?
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ): Yes — 0.003 WAVES required per buy/sell order.

- **EFYT-to-ERG swap rate**: The exact conversion mechanism (1:1, 1:1.x, dual-chain emission, or snapshot-based) remains undecided; a clearer picture is expected by end of Q1 2018.
- **Mainnet launch date**: No firm date; contingent on node stability and debugging progress. Best estimate: timeline clearer by end of January 2018.
- **Dual-chain incentive design**: Specific incentive structures for MoneyChain/DataChain coordination and emission parameters remain to be defined.
- **IADD implementation challenges**: @Dmitry acknowledged drawbacks and complexity; resolution approach pending further testnet results.
- **Mobile mining mechanics**: The precise method for enabling mining on mobile devices via IADD was not detailed.
- **Additional airdrop plans**: Whether further airdrops to EFYT holders will occur, and eligibility criteria, left as an option under consideration.

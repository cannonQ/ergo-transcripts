# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: September 2024

---

## Executive Summary

September 2024 was a milestone month for Ergo's infrastructure maturity: storage rent collection went live on mainnet via the Sigmanauts Mining DAO pool, the Ergo 6.0.0-alpha1 devnet released with major language upgrades, and Rosen Bridge Ethereum integration entered final testing. Across all four weeks, the dominant themes were scaling research (sub-blocks vs. layer 2 tradeoffs), ecosystem tooling launches (Trade House, Gluon Gold, MewFinance, ErgoNames), and community-led growth initiatives spanning mining governance, localization, and merchandise.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Storage Rent — Live on Mainnet
Storage rent collection activated on mainnet during W36–W37 and became the most discussed infrastructure milestone of the month. The Sigmanauts Mining DAO pool, built by qx(), Cheese Enthusiast, and Marc the Shark, won its first block and successfully extracted rent from 2 boxes, with change boxes returned and 4-year timers reset. A storage rent dashboard launched at sigmaspace.io tracking upcoming obligations across boxes. Technical discussion clarified that non-miners can fully spend smaller storage rent UTXOs but struggle with larger ones where only the fee is removed, due to candidate block regeneration requirements addressed in 5.0.24. kushti noted that as emissions decline, the protocol will increasingly enforce miner participation in rent collection, and the Sigmanauts pool offers the clearest path to maximising this revenue.

### Ergo Node Releases — 5.0.23 → 5.1.0 → 6.0
Version 5.0.23 was finalized and put under review during W37; 5.1.0 was slated to follow immediately after. The major development was the release of Ergo 6.0.0-alpha1 for devnets during W38, introducing unsigned big integers, higher-order functions/lambdas, new numeric and collection methods, and header serialization improvements. A 3-month on-chain voting period for 6.0 mainnet activation was confirmed. RocksDB as an alternative database backend to LevelDB (PR #2115) was evaluated, partly prompted by node corruption issues encountered when copying LevelDB databases across operating systems. An indexed node capability was noted as underutilized by the broader crypto ecosystem.

### Sub-blocks Research & Scalability
Sub-blocks were the dominant scalability topic throughout the month. kushti indicated an initial 5.x-based devnet for throughput testing could release within weeks and called for community load testing volunteers and stratum proxy modification help. The design is inspired by the Prism blockchain (which claimed 70k TPS in datacenter conditions but has not shipped a real network). kushti emphasized the importance of formal security proofs (GKL-15 or Prism-style) and the principle of non-breaking, research-driven development. Key concerns raised included: whether dApps tied to block height or timestamp would require adjustment if block times decrease; orphan sub-block risk at global scale with many distributed Lithos mining nodes (kushti clarified average sub-block count per block remains constant regardless of miner count); and whether to pursue sub-blocks, microblocks, sidechains, or layer 2 solutions. A community testnet channel was created for parameter experimentation, but no committed volunteers were named by month end.

### Rosen Bridge — Ethereum Integration
Rosen Bridge Ethereum integration progressed through W36–W39. Armeanio confirmed Ethereum deployment was nearing completion, with UI tested and under final review in W38. A semi-public testing phase requiring 30+ volunteers launched in W39, with watcher-service versions prior to 3.0.0 deprecated. Users can wrap rsnBTC and rsnADA on DuckPools via SigmaFi. Additional planned integrations include Bitcoin Runes support (design published in W36), native token listings, and potential Doge support. Rosen DAO pool runs for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards; v2 planning is underway. kushti also published a Medium post on Rosen Bridge origins during W38.

### Gluon Gold SDK — Community Fundraising
The Gluon Gold SDK raffle, launched before W36, successfully reached 100% funding at 6,896 ERG with 45 contributors during W36. Gluons were distributed as Contribution Accounting Tokens (CATs) to benefactors proportional to their contributions. The community collectively funded the final 54 ERG to complete the second half of SDK development. The EF's gluons were clarified to stem from prior SigmaUSD development benefaction, not a new grant, and The Stable Order received a separate EF marketing grant without gluon exchange.

### Sigmanauts Mining DAO — Governance & Operations
The Sigmanauts Mining DAO pool launched on mainnet during W36 with Cheese Enthusiast coding, qx() handling pool operations, and Marc the Shark on UI/server infrastructure. Pool governance runs through Paideia DAO: miners receive voting tokens, fees accumulate in a DAO treasury, and on-chain votes govern distribution. A first block was won and storage rent successfully collected during W37. Beta testing of a miner reward swap feature (minting/burning miner ID tokens) was live at dev.ergominers.com by W39. MiningCore storage rent was also confirmed live on mainnet.

### Trade House, Order Book DEXes & DeFi Tooling
qx() was a consistent advocate for Trade House (order book DEX at ergoauctions.org/trade) throughout the month, contrasting it with AMM designs: order books offer total transparency of depth and liquidity, full slippage control, peer-to-peer matching without impermanent loss, and support for partial fills. Crystal Pools was noted as supporting both models. Gluon Gold (ERG-backed stablecoin at gluon.gold) went into production. MewFinance v1 whitepaper published during W38; MewFinance Marketplace launched a "Phygital" section for NFTs tied to physical merchandise. The Dexy Protocol completed its first external audit by 4Eyes Consulting during W39, with findings being addressed.

### Lithos Protocol & Decentralized Mining
Lithos Protocol development progressed alongside 6.0 devnet work. Cheese Enthusiast published a forum post on Decentralized Mining and Non-Interactive Share Proofs (NISPs), researching different NISP constructions and MEV support using the 6.0 devnet. The protocol moves block candidate generation responsibility from the pool to miners, requiring protocol-level safeguards against censorship or manipulation (e.g., storage rent prioritization). The interaction between Lithos, sub-blocks, and storage rent collection was noted as requiring careful design.

### Community Growth — Localization & Merchandise
Yulius Kristianto completed a full Indonesian-language Ergo wiki translation, published an Ergo platform overview on Kompasiana, and during W38–W39 added Javanese language support to Ergo Explorer (PR #198 merged) and Satergo. The Sigmanauts MMGA Hat #001 was sold and shipped via MewFinance Marketplace's token-based redemption system. Giufa proposed electing Ergo representatives per linguistic territory (India, China, France, Italy, Spain, Turkey, Russia, Japan) with KOL/influencer partnerships, though this lacked a concrete implementation plan or EF endorsement by month end.

### ErgoHack IX Announced
ErgoHack IX was announced for October 25–31, 2024, hosted on Discord with the theme "Advancing UI/UX Design & Mining Solutions on Ergo." Prizes TBC. kushti proposed stealth address-based anonymous voting as a community idea. Community brainstorming in dev channels was active throughout W39.

### Peer-to-Peer Finance & Alternative Monetary Systems
kushti articulated a broader vision for Ergo as infrastructure for peer-to-peer economies: ROSCAs, unstealable donations, free banking digital currencies (FBDCs), trustless derivatives, and ChainCash as practical tooling. He referenced Stokvel (South African rotating credit systems) and Gresham's law implications for non-inflationary currencies. A related educational series on blockchain foundations (hash functions, PoW, ADS, Sigma protocols, UTXO, ErgoScript, storage rent) was proposed to start in November.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2024-09-02): Can miners add transactions to unfinished block candidates as time passes — is there "lost progress" from generating a new candidate?
  **A** (@Cheese Enthusiast): Yes, miners can always generate new block candidates for the same height including new/additional transactions. Each hash with a new nonce is a separate solution attempt; there is no lost progress from generating a new candidate.

- **Q** (@Patato, 2024-09-03): Node stuck syncing; copied LevelDB database from PC to mining rig and got "Corruption: CURRENT file does not end with newline" error.
  **A** (@kushti): LevelDB binary formats differ across operating systems — cross-OS copies are problematic. Try the RocksDB-based node (PR #2115) or generate the database from scratch on the target device.

- **Q** (@Cheese Enthusiast, 2024-09-10): Why can non-miners fully spend smaller storage rent UTXOs but not claim larger ones where only the fee is removed?
  **A** (@kushti): The former case has no propagation issue (creation height set to expected block height); the latter is hard for non-miners without candidate block regeneration, which is coming in 5.0.24. HEIGHT is set to last block + 1 during mempool check in recent versions, but without candidate regeneration it remains difficult to include in the next block.

- **Q** (@Cheese Enthusiast, 2024-09-10): Will miners rationally exclude low-fee rent transactions, wasting the opportunity?
  **A** (@kushti): Over time, as emissions decline, the protocol itself will enforce miners to care. Currently, miners should join the Sigmanauts pool to collect maximum storage rent.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2024-09-10): What happens to locked LP tokens after 4 years if not traded?
  **A** (@qx() & @Gazza): Could be addressed by requiring ERG deposited in the box when locking that exceeds storage rent × years locked. Contracts cannot create UTXOs that skip storage rent; every UTXO is affected. The locking feature is designed for months to a year, not 4+ years.

- **Q** (@Patato, 2024-09-16): What is the NIPoPoWs superblocks target difficulty — how many normal blocks until a superblock on average?
  **A** (@kushti): Superblocks exist at multiple levels. Level 1 superblocks occur approximately 2x less often than normal blocks on average. Each successive level is 2x less frequent — not ×3. Difficulty targets maintain this schedule. See nipopows.com for full details.

- **Q** (@Patato, 2024-09-17): With Lithos mining nodes spread globally, won't sub-block propagation cause many orphans at scale?
  **A** (@kushti): The number of sub-blocks per block on average stays the same regardless of hashrate or miner count. Orphaning risk does not scale with decentralization in the way described.

- **Q** (@qx(), general context, 2024-09-18): What about dApps that rely on block height or timestamp if block times decrease?
  **A** (@kushti): Some dApps (including emission contracts) are tied to block height and timestamp. Changing block time would affect these and requires careful analysis of worst-case scenarios and security implications — not all can be instantly optimized.

- **Q** (@HQΣr, 2024-09-26): What is the current state of stealth address creation — do you need Mixer?
  **A** (@kushti): Currently yes; the main bottleneck is not address creation but scanning, which requires testing every stealth payment encountered at roughly 1ms per test — this is what prevents support in non-desktop wallets. Minotaur support does not yet exist. For scenarios like voting, scanning can be fast because only voting group transactions need to be scanned.

- **Q** (@ThatOtherFella, 2024-09-27): How similar are Ergo's sub-blocks to Prism? Did Bitcoin ever implement this?
  **A** (@kushti): Sub-blocks are inspired by Prism. Prism claims 70k TPS in a datacenter testbed but no real network has launched. Security proofs (GKL-15 or Prism-style) are essential. Ergo's design priorities: flat P2P, non-breaking research-driven development, security before throughput claims.

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2024-09-20): Error: "transaction exceeds input boxes limit" when transferring ERG.
  **A** (@HQΣr): Consolidate your boxes using tools.ergexplorer.com.

- **Q** (@qx(), 2024-09-06): Can miners include zero-fee transactions in blocks?
  **A** (@Pulsarz): Yes — miners can include any transactions respecting consensus rules, including zero-fee TXs. MEV mechanisms (bribing miners for priority inclusion) work similarly on PoW chains; if you mine your own blocks, you control which transactions are included.

- **Q** (@kushti, 2024-09-20): Why charge listing fees — can't exchanges simply profit from trading fees?
  **A** (@Killian Mexc): This is standard industry practice. [@kushti countered that Ergo listed free on Bitmart and Bitrue with healthy order books, questioning the necessity of paid listings.]

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Sub-blocks mainnet path**: The design (input blocks + ordering blocks, Prism-inspired) is clear at research level, but the initial sub-blocks devnet is still "a few weeks away." The tradeoff analysis between sub-blocks, microblocks, sidechains, and layer 2 solutions has not been formally published. A forum post was committed to but not yet delivered.

- **6.0 mainnet activation**: 6.0.0-alpha1 shipped for devnets; 6.0.0-alpha2 targeted for early October. The 3-month on-chain voting period has been confirmed, but the forum post with voting details and activation specifics was still TBD at month end.

- **Rosen Bridge Ethereum go-live**: Armeanio said "less than two weeks" from 2024-09-17; final testing with 30+ volunteers was initiated in W39. Exact go-live date not confirmed.

- **Dexy audit remediation**: First 4Eyes audit results received; specific findings and remediation timeline not yet published.

- **ErgoNames launch**: Contracts nearly complete and testnet minting confirmed; mainnet deployment timeline not stated.

- **Stealth address mobile support**: 1ms-per-test scanning bottleneck remains unresolved; no concrete fix or timeline from any wallet team.

- **Faster block times feasibility**: Community testnet channel created; impact on block-height-tied dApps and emission contracts identified as a concern but not scoped. No committed testnet coordinator named.

- **EF sustainability / runway**: kushti flagged ~4-month runway at current burn rate (W36); debate on EF investment model vs. pure infrastructure support unresolved.

- **Paideia dual-token complexity**: B Paideia (testnet) and mainnet Paideia to run in parallel for one year; no elegant migration path identified.

- **Regional KOL/influencer programme**: Giufa's proposal lacks implementation plan or EF endorsement.

- **Fee estimation API adoption**: API exists but is untested and unused by wallets; no wallet team committed to integrating it.

- **SigmaO revival**: Floated as a 2025 ErgoHack idea; no lead developer or funding confirmed.

- **RocksDB Docker image**: Requested; publication path not confirmed.

- **Sigmanauts pool miner reward swap**: Beta live at dev.ergominers.com; broader rollout timeline unclear.

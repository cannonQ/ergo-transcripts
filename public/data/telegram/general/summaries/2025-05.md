# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Month:** 2025-05 | **Chat:** Ergo General

---

## Executive Summary

May 2025 was a landmark "delivery month" for Ergo, marked by the live deployment of sub-blocks on DevNet, the full deployment of Dexy Gold mainnet contracts, and the launch of the 6.0 soft-fork voting process. Alongside core protocol progress, the ecosystem saw a wave of new tooling—AI-assisted dApp development, on-chain decentralised chat (Pulse), and the Rosen Bridge Expansion Kit—while ErgoHack X concluded with AI-themed submissions and active community participation.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Sub-blocks Development & Deployment
Sub-blocks moved from in-progress to live DevNet across the month. kushti finalized p2p protocol changes, merged the latest 6.0 code into the sub-blocks branch, and deployed a public CPU mining seed node (`213.239.193.208:9058`). Sub-blocks enable faster confirmations for low-security-requirement transactions while normal blocks retain full security guarantees for high-value transfers. Key technical clarifications: timestamp-based contracts can only be spent in ordering blocks (not sub-blocks), making them immune to sub-block timing issues; contracts relying on block height rather than timestamps require careful redesign or proxy wrappers for sub-block compatibility. Mainnet deployment requires 6.0 lock-in via miner voting, an informal security review, testnet polish, and broad miner consensus—no fixed date was given.

### Sigma 6.0 Soft Fork & Protocol Voting
kushti published a formal voting proposal for miners on the Ergo Forum and began coordinating with pool operators on a suitable voting height. The 6.0 update encompasses sub-blocks protocol support, input/ordering blocks EIP, and Sigmachains scaffolding. A final testnet resync was underway by end of month. The process is miner-consensus-driven with no hard deadline imposed by the development team.

### Dexy Gold Mainnet Deployment
All Dexy Gold mainnet contracts were deployed by kushti in W21, with 1,000 ERG bootstrapped into the liquidity pool and 100 ERG seeded into the bank. The fee structure is 0.1% service fee and 0.3% pool fee. A private UI entered testing by end of month (W22); public release pending test completion. Oracle Pool v1 (EF members and external operators) and Oracle Pool v2 (with GORT rewards) are both active for Dexy Gold price feeds. An open question remained on how the DexyBank rebalances the liquidity pool when the gold price changes and what attack vectors this creates.

### Rosen Bridge: Rosenet Upgrade & Expansion Kit
Armeanio published Rosen Treasury Proposals 1–4 across W18–W19, signalling the move out of the "Rosenlite" era with the Rosenet upgrade. In W22, the Rosen Bridge Expansion Kit (RCS-003) was released, providing a formal framework for integrating new blockchains into Rosen Bridge with a proposed fee distribution model to reward external integration teams. Progress confirmed on Bitcoin, Dogecoin, and Firo implementations. A 5,000 ERG bounty was announced for Rosen Bridge integration with Handshake Name System (HNS). rsERG became a borrowable asset on Lenfi.

### Pulse: On-Chain Decentralised Chat
HQΣr built and shipped Pulse (`ergochats.vercel.app`) across W20–W22—an on-chain chat system storing messages in UTXO boxes at 0.0004 ERG per message. Features developed during the month include encrypted messaging, stealth address support, direct ERG/token tipping, an upvote/downvote reputation system, an AI assistant integration, and a livestream tipping widget (with character-based ERG values). Privacy limitations were acknowledged: covert addresses provide one-time receives but on-chain amounts remain public for large payments without additional mixing. HQΣr noted that current encryption needs hardening. Long-term roadmap includes IPFS image upload, translation, and private encrypted rooms using public-key cryptography.

### AI-Assisted Development & ErgoHack X
ErgoHack X (AI theme, running through June 1) drove significant community tooling activity. AI-assisted development using Lovable, Grok, and Cursor with Ergo documentation enabled rapid prototyping—TMR.ΣRG built a wallet-connected dApp in approximately half a day with near-zero manual code. Marc the Shark published an AI Project Starter Kit for ErgoHack. By W22, approximately 70% of hackathon submissions were AI-generated; judging was on utility and merit rather than novelty. TMR.ΣRG's "Honeypot Kickstart" AI game raffle reached 100% funding (200 ERG from 8 contributors).

### Palmyra, Winter Protocol & Chainlink Integration
Dan Friedman detailed Palmyra's use of Chainlink's proof-of-reserve and proxy functions to issue on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralised trade financing, coupled with IoT devices (cameras, weight/motion sensors) for continuous commodity auditing. Chainlink was chosen for network size and support breadth; multi-oracle support including native Ergo Oracle Pools is planned. A portion of token sale capital is earmarked to build out Ergo's native oracle infrastructure. The core argument was made that using non-native oracles is not tribalism but pragmatic tooling selection for specific use cases.

### Community Currency, Off-Chain Payments & "Plan B" Infrastructure
kushti framed Ergo as emergency financial infrastructure for failed states and proposed tutorials on bootstrapping financial systems using Ergo tools. The Basis on-chain reserve proposal was circulated for supporting off-chain IOU and community currency protocols. Offline peer-to-peer payment mechanisms were technically scoped: time-locked UTXO contracts with DAG resolution by timestamp and slashed ERG for double-spend punishment, requiring no special hardware. A Git Circles framework for creating community currencies around open-source projects was posted to the Ergo Forum.

### Mobile App Distribution & Developer Tooling
A critical friction point identified early in the month: lack of DUNS numbers and Apple signature requirements block anonymous developers from app store distribution. A Marshall Islands DAO legal structure (~$5k setup) was proposed as a path to enable anonymous developer app signing, with the alternative being use of the Ergo Foundation UK LTD entity. No final decision was reached. @qx() granted @Aco PostgreSQL access to the full ErgoExplorer database, enabling new on-chain analytics capabilities. Satergo v1.9.2 shipped with an Offline Vault bug fix; Keystone wallet merged latest Ergo changes with full Nautilus integration planned.

### Storage Rent, UTXO History & Node Pruning
Clarifications were made on storage rent mechanics: nodes only need the last ~4 years of history (1,051,200 blocks) to trace active UTXOs; full history is required only for tracing miner UTXO origins. Sub-blocks are pruned after a few ordering blocks, with NIPoPoWs and UTXO set snapshots providing higher-order pruning. Empty boxes (e.g., Pulse chat messages with minimal ERG) may be exempt from storage rent decay if they carry no significant value.

### Token Economics & Value Leakage
Extended debate across W18–W19 on whether projects require tokens. The emerging consensus: tokens can bootstrap liquidity but create value-leak problems when service fees are structured to enrich token holders at the expense of LPs and users. Referenced successful no-token or well-structured models: Dexy Gold (0.1% service / 0.3% pool, no value-leaking token), SigmaUSD, Spectrum DEX contracts. RSN's tail supply model was cited as well-executed but not easily replicable.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Ioannis, 2025-05-12): What are the biggest improvements from sub-blocks — more TPS or faster confirmation?
  **A** (@kushti): Double spending is not possible in follow-up sub-blocks. For confirmations it works the same as with blocks — you wait for enough hash to confirm your transaction.

- **Q** (@Ioannis, 2025-05-12): Doesn't sub-blocks reduce security? What happens if a double-spend is attempted after a sub-block confirmation?
  **A** (@LouisLibre): Sub-blocks are still mined and hashed at a lower difficulty target (thus faster), so security is not zero. Different use cases benefit differently: exchanges may require 1–2 normal blocks; a coffee shop may require 1–2 sub-blocks.

- **Q** (@Pulsarz, 2025-05-13): What is the best way to check contracts — block height or timestamp?
  **A** (@kushti): Inputs with timestamp-based contracts cannot be spent in sub-blocks, only in ordering blocks.

- **Q** (@cannon_q, 2025-05-13): Will smart contracts need to dictate acceptable confirmation levels, or can users choose at signing time?
  **A** (@HQΣr): A smart contract would require a rewrite or a proxy to support sub-block confirmation logic.

- **Q** (@c8, 2025-05-07): Is the current plan to keep blockspace minimal and scale on layer 2?
  **A** (@kushti): In many cases you don't need global consensus except for settlement events — community currencies, social media currencies, bonus points, carbon credits. ERG and Ergo token reserves can be useful for such systems with no need for expensive global consensus over fully replicated data.

- **Q** (anon_br, 2025-05-28): Does sub-block data need to be stored after the ordering block is found?
  **A** (@kushti): Pruned after a few blocks. Current full blocks act as a pruning mechanism for sub-blocks; higher-order pruning via NIPoPoWs and UTXO set snapshots is also available.

- **Q** (@HQΣr, 2025-05-28): Will sub-blocks provide new explorer endpoints or just node-level APIs?
  **A** (@kushti): Explorers (Ergo Explorer, SigmaSpace, etc.) need to be updated in parallel to make sub-block endpoints available for developer testing.

- **Q** (@Grayman, 2025-05-21): So all Pulse messages are in boxes with minimal ERG — will they all be erased in 4 years via storage rent?
  **A** (@HQΣr): Not actually — boxes are essentially empty so they may not be subject to storage rent decay.

- **Q** (@Patato, 2025-05-28): Is it true that only 4 years of blockchain history is needed to operate a node?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes — all UTXOs created in the last 4 years. Tracing back to miner UTXOs requires full history; for most use cases the 4-year window is sufficient. Older blocks can live on archival nodes or DHT.

- **Q** (@c8, 2025-05-30): How do we assign monetary value to PR contributions without relying on lines of code?
  **A** (@kushti): Create recognisable value through code review (as in existing open-source projects). Alternative: rolling basic income — if N PRs merged into master within M months, receive regularly minted tokens.

- **Q** (@TMR.ΞRG, 2025-05-07): Is there an API endpoint callable when sub-blocks are voted as true?
  **A** (@kushti): There will be no voting — consensus-wise the network remains the same; it just uses different messaging. Sub-block-specific API endpoints will require explorer updates.

- **Q** (@Grayman, 2025-05-21): Can covert addresses hide transaction flows if amounts are large and later consolidated?
  **A** (@Patato & @TMR.ΣRG): Covert addresses provide one-time receive addresses but amounts and on-chain flows remain public; large payments remain traceable without additional mixing steps through the Ergo Mixer.

- **Q** (Team Team, 2025-05-09): Can ERG be staked in a Ledger wallet?
  **A** (@Patato): Ergo is pure proof of work — there is no staking. ERG held in a Ledger hardware wallet is fully supported with a production-ready integration.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

1. **Sub-blocks mainnet timeline**: 6.0 miner voting height not yet finalised; security review and testnet polish outstanding. No fixed date.
2. **Dexy Gold public UI release**: Private UI in testing at month end; public launch date not specified. Bank rebalancing attack surface when gold price changes remains unaddressed.
3. **Sub-block API and explorer coverage**: Ergo Explorer, SigmaSpace, and other infrastructure need updates before sub-block endpoints are developer-accessible. Coordination timeline unclear.
4. **Mobile app store distribution**: Marshall Islands DAO vs. Ergo Foundation UK LTD vs. personal LLC — cost verification and legal path not finalised. Major friction for anonymous developers.
5. **Pulse encryption hardening**: HQΣr acknowledged current encryption is insufficient; cryptographic audit and hardening approach not yet determined.
6. **Mixer/privacy integration in Pulse**: Regulatory concerns raised; no concrete path forward for adding Ergomixer functionality to Pulse.
7. **Multisig user-facing solution**: Identified as a major DAO tooling gap on Ergo (and across all chains); no solution in progress.
8. **Private sidechain technology choice**: DarkFi, Midnight, and Aleo mentioned as candidates for a private sidechain option; no decision or timeline established.
9. **Rosen Bridge HNS integration**: 5,000 ERG bounty announced; no team assigned.
10. **Tokenisation compliance path**: No standard regulatory framework for properly tokenising utility projects in the post-2025 US regulatory environment; most projects still rely on "no expected returns" disclaimers.
11. **Kaspa integration into Rosen Bridge**: rsKAS utility discussion ongoing; no formal decision.
12. **DuckPools collateral term flexibility**: Whether collateral lockup periods can be extended beyond 3-week windows remains unresolved.

# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: November 2023

---

## Executive Summary

November 2023 was one of Ergo's most eventful months, dominated by three intersecting themes: the CoinMarketCap data-integrity crisis and its partial resolution through community-coordinated public pressure; the imminent (but perpetually "1-2 weeks away") launch of Rosen Bridge and the RSN token's successful debut on Spectrum DEX; and deepening technical discussion around sub-blocks, demurrage economics, and long-term monetary policy. The month also saw Paideia DAO enter public beta, Ergo protocol v5.0.16 ship, and significant community debate over Palmyra's multi-chain strategy and CEX listing economics.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### CoinMarketCap Data-Integrity Crisis ("Ergogate")
The month opened with a confrontation after CMC demanded $5,000 to correct circulating supply data that had been wrong for over two years — specifically, CMC had merged EFYT (a 2017 pre-launch Waves token) with mainnet ERG into a single price history, falsely implying Ergo peaked in 2017. After coordinated Twitter pressure from the community, CMC fixed the circulating supply but retaliated with a libelous warning banner on Ergo's listing page. Glasgow negotiated privately; CMC's developer ultimately apologized and clarified the $5K was for an advertorial service, not a data correction. The chart separation and spiteful messaging remained partially unresolved through month-end, though the CMC description was successfully updated to reference demurrage. Multiple other projects (Radix, Nano, Kaspa, Alephium) reported identical pay-to-play experiences, lending credibility to the community's characterisation of systemic extortion. The episode generated significant media coverage and reinforced community appetite for decentralised price-data alternatives (floated as a potential ErgoHack VIII project).

### Rosen Bridge Launch & RSN Token
The RSN token launched on Spectrum DEX in early November and achieved approximately $1M TVL within 48 hours — the strongest token debut in recent Ergo ecosystem history. The bridge itself was described as "days or weeks away" throughout the entire month, becoming a running community joke by week four. Technically, Rosen operates as a cross-chain oracle protocol: Watchers submit reports on-chain, locking RSN and ERG as collateral (~$1K each), and are rewarded for settled transfers and slashed for fraud. Initial chain support covers Cardano and Bitcoin; EVM chain integration (enabling wrapped stablecoins on Ergo) is planned but likely 2025. A Watcher UI v2.2.1 was released; a manual TX was sent to GARD's DAO to refund slashed permits from a prior failed high-fee transaction. Armeanio confirmed Guard server setup was ongoing but declined to commit to a hard launch date.

### Sub-blocks (S-blocks) Design & Scaling
Sub-blocks emerged as the central technical upgrade under active development. After community debate over naming ("weak blocks," "swift blocks," "microblocks"), kushti settled on **sub-blocks** as the canonical term and updated the codebase accordingly. The design: sub-blocks are 1/128th of main block size (~1.27 MB currently, miner-votable), with miners rewarded for sub-block creation via an ErgoScript-programmable reward mechanism that requires no supply expansion. Expected outcome is ~10 sub-blocks between main blocks, yielding ~20-second perceived confirmation times. Papers estimate up to 100x throughput improvement; kushti conservatively estimated 15–20x under real-world conditions. A 6.0 testnet is being configured; community members with interest in tweaking parameters were invited to DM kushti. Key complication: some contracts rely on block height rather than timestamps, meaning block time reductions require careful audit and migration planning.

### Demurrage as Monetary Policy Narrative
@dayumbbbb drove sustained, highly engaged discussion positioning Ergo's demurrage mechanism as its primary differentiator: unlike Bitcoin (supply trending toward zero via key loss) or Ethereum (inflationary), Ergo enforces a genuinely fixed circulating supply by redistributing abandoned UTXOs after a 4-year epoch. The fixed storage rent is currently ~0.14 ERG per UTXO per 4 years; at a 100 ERG box this implies ~2,857 years to full reclamation. Community debate explored whether percentage-based fees (1–3% annually) or exponential scaling factors would be preferable at high ERG valuations, but any change requires a hard fork and governance vote. The interactive demurrage simulator at deadit.github.io/paizo/ was widely shared. The CMC description update successfully included demurrage language as a community win. @dayumbbbb also proposed calling 10⁻⁸ ERG a "SIGMA" (paralleling Bitcoin's "satoshi") to improve UX accessibility — the suggestion gained organic traction with tips increasingly denominated in SIGMAs.

### Palmyra Multi-Chain Strategy & Chainlink Partnership
Dan Friedman and Luca D'Angelo clarified Palmyra's architecture repeatedly through November in response to community concern about "abandoning Ergo." The Winter Protocol (tokenizing real-world commodities via on-chain warehouse receipts) is developed entirely on Ergo and would not perform well on Cardano due to transaction costs. The PALM token is launching on Cardano (mid-2024) and will be bridged to Ergo via Rosen Bridge — a compliance-driven decision, not a strategic pivot. The Chainlink partnership provides proof-of-reserve functions via a proxy in Winter Protocol: IoT devices (cameras, weight/motion sensors) in bonded warehouses feed continuous existence proofs on-chain, enabling decentralised trade financing. Dan noted this is not zero-sum with Ergo oracles; plans exist to use token sale proceeds to build out native Ergo Oracle Pools. Community pushback centred on tribalism vs. pragmatism, with qx() and Armeanio both noting that companies using parts of the Ergo ecosystem is a net positive, not a betrayal.

### Paideia DAO Public Beta
Paideia launched its public beta on November 20th after being rebuilt from scratch using the Plasma library. bPaideia tokens were airdropped to stakers; high trading volume was reported on Spectrum DEX with 14–21% price movement in the launch window. Community members were directed to Discord for bug reports via support tickets. Testing initially focused on a single DAO (Ergcube) before broader rollout.

### CEX Listings & Market-Making
Ben Villalobos (Efficient Frontier) pitched a loan-and-option deal where the EF would borrow ERG, provide CEX liquidity, and grant price-premium options — initially based on market cap without understanding the EF's ~$2M treasury. Community feedback was direct but constructive; Ben acknowledged the poor pre-research publicly and committed to a revised proposal. Dan Friedman confirmed ongoing talks with two US/Canadian exchanges for spot and derivatives products; the MEXC deal collapsed over non-negotiable pricing demands. T1 exchange listings (Kraken, Binance) require $120K–$500K+ fees plus sustained 200K–500K daily volume minimums — purely a money-and-volume game, not amenable to community letters. xeggex was mentioned at ~$2K; Glasgow was pursuing a free-listing negotiation path.

### Mining Decentralisation & Security
F2Pool's blacklisting of OFAC-flagged Bitcoin addresses prompted discussion of censorship risk in PoW mining. Ergo's Autolykos (GPU-native, ASIC-resistant) was highlighted as structurally resistant to this class of centralisation, though community noted Autolykos is not FPGA-resistant (FPGAs with HBM perform ~2x best Nvidia cards but aren't cost-effective). At current hashrate lows, an external 51% attacker would require ~90,000 RTX 3070Ti-equivalent GPUs — a significant barrier. Pool concentration (2miners holding substantial hash share) was flagged but framed as a spectrum rather than a binary; selfish mining remains the primary theoretical risk, with little financial incentive given chain size.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Jayson, 2023-11-07): How are sub-block miners rewarded if the emission schedule is fixed? Doesn't paying ERG expand supply?
  **A** (@Richi / kushti AMA): kushti designed an elegant reward mechanism using ErgoScript programmability that requires no supply expansion. Full details in AMA video at https://www.youtube.com/live/yuy5uWMtcOg at ~30:00–40:00.

- **Q** (@Pulsarz, 2023-11-10): Do all sub-block transactions get included in normal blocks, or only proofs?
  **A** (@Pulsarz/community): Only proofs of sub-block transactions are included in normal blocks, so effective TPS does increase. Full implementation details remain TBD.

- **Q** (@lexymon, 2023-11-06): What are the security trade-offs of sub-blocks vs. full blocks?
  **A** (community): Sub-blocks are suited to lower-security applications (e.g., gaming, micropayments); full blocks remain required for high-value operations. Wallet UX can surface a security-vs-speed preference toggle for end users.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2023-11-07): Why did my transaction take 10 minutes to confirm when blocks are ~2 minutes?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @Flying Pig): Block times are probabilistic, not deterministic — they depend on mining luck, not a clock. Explorer timestamps reflect when mining started, not completion. Sub-blocks improve UX granularity but do not accelerate full-block confirmation for contracts that require it.

- **Q** (@Mar Bondicat, 2023-11-15): If a 100 ERG box is abandoned, how long until demurrage exhausts it?
  **A** (@dayumbbbb): At current flat-fee rates (~0.14 ERG per 4-year epoch), approximately 2,857 years — but the important property is *modelability*: unlike Bitcoin's lost-coin problem, Ergo's liquid supply is calculable with certainty. Future governance could introduce percentage-based or exponential fee curves (requiring a hard fork) to accelerate reclamation.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2023-11-15): Can dApps chain unconfirmed transactions in the mempool atomically within a single block?
  **A** (@qx()): Yes, with indexed nodes. Mempool transactions can be treated as confirmed inputs and entire chains of transactions built on top atomically — if one fails, the whole chain fails. Most dApps currently underutilise this capability.

- **Q** (@Cr Gu, 2023-11-15): A transaction was sent to an address with spaces stripped by email forwarding — is the ERG lost?
  **A** (@qx(), @kushti): Ergo is robust at bouncing malformed addresses. The transaction pays a mining fee to the wrong address but fails at the protocol level; TradeOgre should mark it correctly and refund via their support channel.

- **Q** (@Mar Bondicat, 2023-11-27): Can a box contain zero ERG on Ergo?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @Luivatra): No. Every box requires a minimum value (360 nanoerg × box size in bytes). The fee box is separate and optional but must also meet this minimum.

- **Q** (@Mar Bondicat, 2023-11-27): How is the minimum required transaction fee calculated?
  **A** (@Pulsarz): The minimum box value formula (360 nanoerg × box size) is a dust-prevention mechanism entirely separate from the fee mechanism. The fee has its own box; the network only verifies that the ERG in that box meets the minimum required fee. Full docs: https://docs.ergoplatform.com/dev/protocol/tx/min-fee/

- **Q** (@Nash, 2023-12-01): As the network grows, does a 51% attack become easier or harder?
  **A** (@MavΣrickBg): Harder. At current all-time-low hashrate, an external attacker would need approximately 90,000 RTX 3070Ti-equivalent GPUs. A larger, more valuable network raises the cost of attack proportionally.

- **Q** (@Me, 2023-11-25): Is FPGA mining viable for Autolykos?
  **A** (@Mar Bondicat): FPGAs are technically possible and perform ~2x best Nvidia cards, but are not cost-effective at current ERG prices. Autolykos is ASIC-*resistant* (ASICs perform comparably to GPUs, making dedicated hardware uneconomic to manufacture), not ASIC-impossible. FPGAs with HBM memory remain viable as reprogrammable soft circuits.

- **Q** (@Me, 2023-11-27): Are normal users able to claim storage rent from expired boxes, or is that restricted to miners?
  **A** (implied by qx() / community): Non-mining users cannot directly claim demurrage boxes; miners and smart contracts control recollection. Precise mechanics of user-accessible reclamation remain an open design question.

- **Q** (@eπ, 2023-11-19): Can the 4-year storage rent epoch frequency be changed by miners without a hard fork?
  **A** (@Glasgow): No. The 4-year frequency is fixed at the protocol level.

- **Q** (@Johnny, 2023-11-19): Can Ergo confirm copyright or authorship of written works on-chain?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Yes — via the SigmaStamp project (https://github.com/sigmastamp), which enables verifiable on-chain timestamps and ownership proofs.

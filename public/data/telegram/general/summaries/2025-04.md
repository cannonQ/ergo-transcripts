# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: April 2025

---

## Executive Summary

April 2025 was a landmark month for Ergo development, dominated by three converging themes: the successful completion of the EIP-27 emission reduction and the progressive maturation of the v6.0.0 protocol release candidate, significant infrastructure milestones across hardware wallet integration (Ledger, Keystone) and cross-chain tooling (Rosen Bridge NFT bridge, rsERG on Cardano), and active community planning around ErgoHACK X, the Colorado Springs Ergo Meetup, and ecosystem roadmap discussions. Sub-blocks implementation progressed from early P2P design to near-testnet-ready status, and the broader conversation around Ergo's speed improvement strategies intensified with concrete proposals under review.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EIP-27 Emission Reduction & Block Rewards
The month opened with a landmark event: kushti announced the successful completion of the EIP-27 emission reduction on 2025-04-06. The emission contract now distributes 30 ERG plus 12M reemission tokens per block, with miners netting 18 ERG per block after redemption. This reinforced Ergo's unique architecture where ERG is emitted via a smart contract rather than as a protocol-level coinbase reward. DXPool was noted as holding approximately 15% of network hashrate. The Sigmanauts mining pool distributed 150 ERG in demurrage rewards for the prior week.

### Ergo v6.0.0 Release Candidate Progression
The reference client advanced through RC4 (released 2025-04-06) to RC5 (released 2025-04-18) and toward a finalized 6.0.0 codebase by late April (>16,000 lines of changes). The soft-fork nature of the release adds complexity to the audit and voting process. New capabilities unlocked by 6.0 include Bitcoin trustless relays, Lithos Protocol support, bulletproof verification, log-size ring signatures, and compact Sigma protocols. The mainnet voting start height remained TBD pending final audit completion. Node 5.0.26 was simultaneously under review for a parallel release track.

### Sub-blocks Implementation
Sub-blocks moved from a design and P2P networking phase to near-testnet-ready implementation across April. kushti completed support for disallowing transactions from reading header candidate fields (timestamp, miner pubkey, votes) in Sigma, reworked block candidate generation, and completed initial transaction validation tests. The full implementation roadmap includes AVL+ tree diffs, in-memory persistence, a new transaction fee script, and efficiency improvements before mainnet. A public devnet or testnet deployment was projected for end of the final week of April (7–10 days from 2025-04-25). Community volunteers were sought to host devnet nodes on public IPs. Sub-blocks were consistently cited as the preferred solution to the rsERG-on-Cardano outperforming native ERG problem, attributed to Ergo's 2-minute block time being unsuitable for rapid DEX trading.

### Ledger & Keystone Hardware Wallet Integration
Two major hardware wallet milestones were reached. The Ergo Ledger app (v0.0.7) exited developer mode and received official audit clearance, making it accessible to all Ledger Live users without enabling developer mode. Key constraints: 100 token limit per transaction on newer devices; Ledger Nano S support fully sunset, meaning the Ergo app will not be available on that device. Users must continue using Nautilus browser extension for Ledger signing — the Ledger Live app itself does not manage Ergo assets. Separately, Keystone firmware progressed with 2 of 3 Ergo integration PRs merged for the cypherpunk firmware edition (alongside Bitcoin, Zcash, and Monero).

### Cross-Chain Infrastructure: Rosen Bridge & rsERG
MewFinance launched a seamless 2-way NFT bridge between Cardano and Ergo at bridge.mewfinance.com, with DiscoSolaris as the initial partner collection. The rsERG Cardano pathway was confirmed by qx() as the primary route toward a Kraken listing, requiring growth from approximately 660 holders to an estimated 6,600+ (benchmarked against Wrapped Flux on EVM at 10,000 holders). Kraken was reported as still working on Cardano infrastructure updates with no concrete listing timeline. Native ERG integration on Kraken was assessed as not currently an option. The Watcher service was updated to version 4.1.3 with support for PALM and SOCKZ tokens, with urgent requests for operators to update before a UI upgrade.

### Ecosystem Roadmap, DeFi Protocols & DexyUSD
A community roadmap chat on 2025-04-25 covered core development, new dApps, AI integration, interfaces, and tooling for adoption. DexyUSD liquidity pool updates were flagged as critical for increasing ERG demand and TVL, described as likely "a few months" away with pool update challenges acknowledged. Discussion compared Dexy Protocol's 1:1 reserve design against SigmaUSD's 4:1/3:1 approach, with questions around delayed oracle data impact deferred for future research. The Palmyra/Chainlink partnership was detailed by Dan Friedman: proof-of-reserve integration via Chainlink oracles coupled with IoT devices (cameras, weight/motion sensors) to issue on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralized trade financing, with capital from the Palmyra token sale earmarked to build out Ergo Oracle Pool infrastructure.

### ErgoHACK X & Community Events
ErgoHACK X (10th anniversary edition) was announced for May 25 – June 1, 2025, with a $9,000 ERG prize pool and an AI-on-Ergo theme. Registration opened at ergohack.io. The Colorado Springs Ergo Meetup 2025 was announced with GitHub-based fork/PR registration, structured across Friday evening through Sunday morning. The Colchise educational project proposed by Chris Ray — a structured course using open-source Ergo code with tokenized incentives — was well-received but left details deferred. A token minting dApp with AI generation was announced by Arohbe, seeking beta testers and considering ErgoHACK submission.

### Speed Improvements Strategy
Across all four weeks, the community revisited potential Ergo speed improvements: sub-blocks, sidechains, faster block times, and layer 2 solutions. kushti consistently noted that the current moment is well-suited for optimization because a concrete set of applications (Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge, Spectrum DEX, Trade House, lending contracts, SigmaUSD) provides a real transaction profile to optimize against, unlike early-stage chains with empty parallel chains. The challenge of block-height-dependent contracts (vs. timestamp-based) affecting downstream dApps was flagged. kushti called for community volunteers passionate about the topic to coordinate testnet parameter experiments, with a dedicated Telegram group available.

### Oracle Pool Architecture Improvements
Oracle Core v2.0.3 was released. Post-wallet-dependency removal, Oracle Pool infrastructure can now run multiple instances against the same node, enabling potential multi-wallet support and expanded data point reporting. Oracle Pool v1 (with Ergo Foundation members and external participants) and Oracle Pool v2 (running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards) were both active. The Palmyra plan to build out Ergo Oracle infrastructure using token sale proceeds was framed as a net positive for the ecosystem.

### Handshake DNS Integration
The Ergo ecosystem secured .ergo, .erg, .mew, and .palmyra top-level domains on Handshake (a PoW-secured decentralized DNS). This was positioned as a disintermediation tool paralleling Ergo's role in money, with concrete roadmap details described as "coming ASAP."

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2025-04-06): What is the emission contract transaction seen on-chain?
  **A** (@kushti): This is EIP-27 emission — miners receive 30 ERG plus 12M reemission tokens per block; only 18 ERG can be redeemed initially. See https://docs.ergoplatform.com/mining/standards/eip27/ and ErgoScript documentation Section 2.6 for emission contract details.

- **Q** (@J. P. Costa, 2025-04-06): Full node stuck at 99.84% sync on Ubuntu 5.0.25 with extra index enabled. What is blocking completion?
  **A** (@kushti): Run `cat ergo.log | grep WARN` and `cat ergo.log | grep ERR` to diagnose. Share logs via pastebin, GitHub issue, or DM.

- **Q** (@Major Popsicle, 2025-04-03): Did Ergo get a contract with terms from Kraken?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Ergo was shortlisted late last year but Kraken switched teams. No contract proposal exists. CEXes deliberately obscure listing requirements. Dan Friedman is an advisor involved in preventing bad deals, not a formal role holder.

- **Q** (@Major Popsicle, 2025-04-03): Where do we stand on rsERG Cardano holder count for Kraken listing?
  **A** (@qx()): Currently 660 holders on Cardano. Estimated ~6,600 holders needed, benchmarked against Wrapped Flux on EVM at 10,000 holders.

- **Q** (@LIFΣ OF IF₳, 2025-04-10): Can I run a full node by installing Satergo and selecting full node option for paired Rosen Watcher operation?
  **A** (@HQΣr): Yes, Satergo's full node option works for this purpose.

- **Q** (@cannon_q, 2025-04-12): With current Oracle setup, can only one data point be reported per oracle type? Is vector reporting possible?
  **A** (@Pgr456): Currently one data point per oracle type; vector approach feasibility unclear.

- **Q** (@Giufa OnliאΣ, 2025-04-12): Would a SigmaEUR stablecoin be viable for MewMart online store use cases?
  **A** (@Richi [MANA]): Each oracle typically requires a dedicated coin — historical pattern: GORT, DORT, SORT. Multiple mapping approach could allow same infrastructure with different wallet/pool configurations.

- **Q** (@flying_pig, 2025-04-22): What is the token limit now for Ledger?
  **A** (@anon_br): 100 tokens per transaction on newer devices. Nano S maxes at 20 but is no longer supported.

- **Q** (@dihe, 2025-04-22): Can't find Ergo when trying to receive ERG on Ledger Live.
  **A** (@pulsarz): Ergo is not on Ledger Live for asset management. Must use Nautilus browser extension wallet. The Ergo app on Ledger is for hardware signing only.

- **Q** (@richi, 2025-04-23): Is listing rsERG on Kraken vs. native integration a business decision?
  **A** (@kushti): Mostly decided by Kraken; native integration not currently an option they will discuss. (@cannon_q): Fireblocks or similar custody integration needed for many CEXes and ramps; Ergo lacks this.

- **Q** (@richi, 2025-04-23): Should Ergo pursue Midnight network framework integration? Does it require consensus changes?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Cardano is reaching out about the framework; shouldn't require consensus changes per current understanding. Ergo is more compatible out-of-the-box than Cardano. Also pushing for native integration via Lagon contact.

- **Q** (@anon_br, 2025-04-24): Is it possible to do multisig without commitment sharing on Ergo, like Bitcoin-style?
  **A** (@kushti): Can do token-based voting or custom sig validation including Bitcoin-style. Native threshold signature preserves ZK (outside the signing ring, who signed is unknown); requires off-chain communication.

- **Q** (@anon_br, 2025-04-24): Will multisig be supported by Nautilus?
  **A** (@anon_br): Yes, planned. First at dApp connector level, then full support with communication protocol (similar to Minotaur).

- **Q** (@tmr.σrg, 2025-04-24): Sidechains overview — how different from layer 2, what is possible on Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Sidechains vs. layer 2 is use-case dependent. Reference: https://x.com/chepurnoy/status/1915836974411243805

- **Q** (@K_Chan, 2025-04-25): Where is the sub-blocks introduction? Does it reward ERG?
  **A** (@cafe_ergo): https://docs.ergoplatform.com/uses/sidechains/subblocks/

- **Q** (@Wombo22, AMA 2025-04-17): What is the significance of the Palmyra and Chainlink partnership?
  **A** (@Dan Friedman): Chainlink proof-of-reserve via proxy functions in Winter Protocol enables on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralized trade financing. IoT devices (cameras, weight/motion sensors) coupled with on-chain oracle issue receipts when producers deposit commodities in bonded warehouses. Plan includes utilizing multiple oracles including Ergo oracles; capital from token sale to build out Ergo Oracle Pool infrastructure. Not a zero-sum game — multiple oracle providers can coexist.

- **Q** (AMA, 2025-04-17): Can you explain what bulletproofs are?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge technique allowing specific statements to be proven in zero-knowledge — used in Monero for range proofs and applicable to generic statements as a trustless alternative to SNARKs (no trusted setup required). A specific form of Sigma protocol. If bulletproof verification can be done in ErgoScript, similar proofs like Groth one-out-of-many could be used — enabling hybrid contracts where sensitive data is verified on-chain without disclosure. This opens a whole new world of zero-knowledge possibilities combined with Ergo contracts.

- **Q** (AMA, 2025-04-17): Can the same DDoS/spam attack that hit another chain happen to Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo has a fee market different from chains without one. Spam can fill mempools but fee market self-corrects — users raising fees get prioritized, spammers' transactions eventually flush out after 10–40 blocks. Reference client 4.0.44+ includes limits on transactions downloaded from single peers. Node operators can raise minimum propagation fee rates to prevent spam from entering mempools. A fee estimation API exists in the node but is not yet widely used by applications.

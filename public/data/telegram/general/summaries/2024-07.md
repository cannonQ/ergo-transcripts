# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: July 2024

---

## Executive Summary

July 2024 was dominated by Ergoversary celebrations marking Ergo's 5th anniversary, accompanied by a wave of technical research disclosures covering sub-blocks, SigmaChains, faster confirmations, and merge-mined sidechains. Alongside core protocol development, the ecosystem saw significant dApp activity with ErgoHack VIII results, TradeHouse launch, Gluon Gold progress, and Palmyra/Winter Protocol's real-world asset integrations using Chainlink oracles. Community-level concerns about mining pool centralisation and a push for grassroots marketing rounded out a highly active month.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Ergoversary & Protocol Research Roadmap
The 5th Ergoversary (July 1) served as a natural forcing function for kushti to publish and discuss a broad research agenda. This included Parallel PoW, Prism, and sub-block specifications for faster confirmations; improved fork choice rules adaptable to variable difficulty; non-breaking sidechain and Bitcoin-NG constructions; and a "UTXO Summit" proposal to showcase concrete UTXO dApp design patterns rather than theoretical throughput. Dionysis Zindros presented PoEM (Better Proof of Work Fork Choice Rule), which kushti noted is velvet-forkable and includable in new node releases without breaking old clients. A video series and Twitter Spaces on the future of PoW accompanied the week.

### Sub-blocks & Sigma 6.0
kushti outlined a sub-blocks proposal in which smaller transaction batches are released faster within the existing 2-minute block time, sharing block height with the parent block. Unlike reducing block time, sub-blocks improve mempool chaining UX and transaction settlement speed without the security trade-offs of faster blocks. Sigma 6.0 was reported at ~95% completion with miner pubkey-in-scripts as the last major outstanding issue; devnet targeting late August. Sub-block development will begin in earnest after Sigma 6.0 is finalised, with miners voting on sub-block pulsation frequency.

### SigmaChains & Merge-Mined Sidechains
kushti published SigmaChains Pt. 1 (chain commitments) on the Ergo forum, describing a framework for merge-mined sidechains with Bitcoin and other chains, standalone PoW blockchains, and private chains. The design draws on BIP 301-style trustless relay, Bitcoin UTXO mapping to Ergo UTXOs via Blink light clients, and isomorphic binding with Bitcoin. Proposed minimum block time for public p2p SigmaChain networks is 10–15 seconds. The biggest open design question — merge-mined vs. standalone PoW — remained unresolved. Lithos Protocol confirmed mainnet launch targeting November–December.

### Storage Rent, Demurrage & Monetary Design
Extended community discussion across July clarified that storage rent is not merely dust mitigation but a mechanism for predictable long-term security budgeting that enables safe ledger pruning (all UTXOs created within a 4-year window). A demurrage Twitter Spaces (July 26, moderated by qx() with kushti, Armeanio, and LouisLibre) explored demurrage as complementary to storage rent for fixed-supply monetary design. Chaum's Better-Than-Money paper and ChainCash were compared as privacy-focused monetary experiments; LETS on top of ChainCash was discussed as a mutual credit clearing use case targeting BRICS/Africa markets and CFA zone monetary replacement.

### Oracle Pools, Palmyra/Winter Protocol & Dexy Gold
Dan Friedman detailed Palmyra's use of Chainlink oracles through Winter Protocol for proof-of-reserve on-chain warehouse receipts, with IoT devices (camera, weight, and motion sensors) providing continuous commodity monitoring for decentralised trade financing. Oracle Pool v2 is live for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards. Plans exist to invest token sale capital in building out native Ergo oracles alongside Chainlink, avoiding single-vendor lock-in. DuckPools v2 issued a new QUACKS-2.0 token (900M emission) for borrow functionality. Gluon Gold mainnet remained weeks away per Bruno Woltzenlogel Paleo, with a bug bounty programme launched and a Catalyst funding vote requested.

### TradeHouse & DEX Ecosystem
TradeHouse (formerly associated with Auction House) launched as a peer-to-peer order book DEX supporting partial order fills, full slippage control, and no impermanent loss — a direct contrast to AMM pools. qx() provided an extended explanation of order book mechanics and the advantages of full trade transparency and depth visibility. CruxFinance was clarified as a lighter Spectrum DEX variant sharing the same off-chain bot infrastructure with different asset lists and UI optimisations. Crystal Pool (ErgoHack VIII winner) is seeking an Elixir + Rust backend developer to replace its JavaScript backend. Discussions about shared Spectrum liquidity pools across DEXes were raised but not resolved.

### Mining Pool Centralisation & Autolykos
dxpool approached 50%+ hashrate concentration, prompting kushti and the community to urgently discourage mining there, directing miners to Sigmanauts pool and SkyHarbor. Armeanio discussed hardware manufacturer centralisation risks on other chains, with Autolykos v3 flagged as a potential defence if ASICs emerge. Autolykos 2 was clarified as k-sum problem-based (not generalised birthday problem like Equihash), and GPU farm profitability was discussed in the context of a potential 400-GPU 3090 farm restart. Lithos Protocol was cited as a long-term solution to GPU mining centralisation.

### Sigmanauts DAO & Community Infrastructure
The Sigmanauts Mining DAO was formally created with ~12–13 tokens distributed and beta testing imminent. Revenue roadmap published by qx(): mining pool (live), SigmaLock (soon), ephemeral messaging (very soon), Auction House NFT merch store (very soon), SkyHarbor UI takeover (longer term). ChainCash top-up reserve transaction builder was completed and merged (PR #45). The ErgoSocials Telegram group was created for coordinated community outreach after debate about hiring external marketers was rejected in favour of grassroots dApp-leveraging.

### p2pFi, Bulletproofs & Zero-Knowledge
kushti introduced the term "p2pFi" (also "self-sovereign DeFi") to distinguish UTXO-based peer-to-peer finance from EVM DeFi, which requires central contracts, admin keys, and governance overhead. Bulletproofs were explained as zero-knowledge range proof and generic statement primitives requiring no trusted setup, implementable in ErgoScript via compact Sigma protocols; Groth one-out-of-many proofs and hybrid contracts (on-chain verification with off-chain sensitive data) were discussed as practical applications.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Pulsarz, 2024-07-02): Does the node API account for boxes being spent in the mempool — can you daisy-chain transactions?
  **A** (@Pulsarz): "It doesn't account for boxes being spent in mempool. So can't daisy chain by default atm." References GitHub issue #2093 and PR #2131 as pending solutions.

- **Q** (@TMR.ΣRG, 2024-07-08): What happens to boxes older than 4 years if unclaimed and the ledger is pruned — does supply get eliminated?
  **A** (@qx()): Collecting storage rent is the pruning mechanism itself. Boxes older than 4 years currently exist; Sigmanauts pool will collect rent once code is integrated. After that, only boxes reaching the 4-year mark will have rent collected — supply is not eliminated but re-enters circulation.

- **Q** (@Phillip Scheindlinger, 2024-07-11): What is the largest block size Ergo can scale to?
  **A** (@Pulsarz): Current practical maximum is ~8MB. No hard limit exists (1GB mentioned in testing app); miners can vote to increase governance parameters.

- **Q** (@HQΣr, 2024-07-11): Is peer-to-peer encrypted messaging possible on-chain with data in registers?
  **A** (@kushti): Possible, yes. AES is quantum-secure (ref: eprint.iacr.org/2019/272), as is other symmetric encryption. Standard Diffie-Hellman key derivation works for shared secrets but is not quantum-secure — a quantum adversary could recover the shared key.

- **Q** (@Ilya Oskin, 2024-07-07): How can preconfirmations guarantee inclusion of a TX within a predefined time range? What if two validators receive payment for conflicting tx preconfirmations?
  **A** (@kushti): The conflict scenario — paying two validators for preconfirmations of conflicting transactions — is an unresolved design problem in Ethereum-style intent/preconfirmation architectures.

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2024-07-19): Getting "Minimal ERG value not met" error on SigmaUSD refund page despite having 6.7 ERG. How to resolve?
  **A** (@Luivatra): There is not enough ERG in the proxy address. Send 0.01 ERG to the proxy address to cover the transaction fee.

- **Q** (@The Horticulturalist, 2024-07-20): What would the sub-block experience look like for users? Can funds received in a 10-second sub-block be used immediately?
  **A** (@kushti): Blocks are still released at the same speed; sub-blocks share block height with the parent block. Sub-blocks enable faster transaction arrangement and preparation within a block, which is then sent as a complete set. Applications decide whether to act on weak (sub-block) confirmation.

- **Q** (@Misfit Toy, 2024-07-22): Will sub-blocks make Ergo faster than Solana?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Sub-blocks provide weak confirmation and spread p2p load over the 2-minute block time rather than creating a peak at block discovery. They do not change block production speed but improve UX for transaction chaining; applications determine whether to use weak confirmation.

- **Q** (@NΣL!0, 2024-07-22): Why has transaction volume on Ergo suddenly spiked?
  **A** (@kushti): New producers active on-chain. (@Glasgow): Likely an ETH/ERG pair bug on GateIO contributed to the spike.

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2024-07-23): When Ergo or Gluon Gold is bridged to Cardano, where is the TVL counted?
  **A** (@kushti): The assets are locked on Ergo in the Rosen Bridge; the TVL increase is recorded on the destination chain's (Cardano's) DeFi products.

- **Q** (@qx(), 2024-07-24): Did George208's fork of hodlERG follow the friendly-fork rules?
  **A** (@Bruno Woltzenlogel Paleo): No. The friendly-fork rule requires a small fee to the original creators (the hodlERG repo). George208's project did not comply. See: https://github.com/PhoenixErgo/phoenix-hodlcoin-contracts

- **Q** (implicit, 2024-07-09): How does Sigma Rust performance compare to the Scala interpreter for ErgoScript execution?
  **A** (@kushti): Rust can provide better performance if libsecp256k1 is properly utilised, but Sigma Rust is still significantly slower than the Scala interpreter. "A lot of work ahead to match the interpreter even."

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Sub-block miner adoption mechanism**: Exact voting mechanism for sub-block pulsation frequency and handling of old miners rejecting sub-blocks not yet specified.
- **Miner pubkey-in-scripts**: Identified as the last major issue blocking the 6.0 devnet; whether minimal or substantial rework is needed remains TBD.
- **SigmaChains first implementation partner**: Framework requires a partner chain for the first launch; no named partner or MOU confirmed.
- **Merge-mined vs. standalone PoW sidechains**: Described by kushti as "the biggest design question" with no resolution.
- **Quantum-secure on-chain messaging**: Diffie-Hellman key derivation acknowledged but quantum vulnerability unresolved; no alternative specified.
- **Oracle Pool v2 and native Ergo oracle build-out**: Dependent on Palmyra token sale capital allocation; no concrete timeline or resource plan.
- **Preconfirmation/intent conflict scenarios**: Validator double-payment for conflicting transactions flagged as an unresolved design problem in intent architectures.
- **Friendly-fork enforcement**: Bruno raised the hodlERG plagiarism issue but no governance mechanism or enforcement policy has been established.
- **Keystone hardware wallet integration**: Community interest confirmed; integration stalled with a 9-month communication gap and no clear path forward.
- **Manifesto 2.0**: Draft exists but community composition process, approval method, and publication timeline not confirmed.
- **Crystal Pool backend rewrite**: Elixir + Rust developer sought to replace JavaScript backend; no confirmed hire or timeline.
- **Sigma 6.0 final release date**: Code ~95% complete and under review; no confirmed ETA.

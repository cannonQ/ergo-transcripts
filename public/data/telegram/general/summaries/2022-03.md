# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: 2022-03

---

## Executive Summary

March 2022 was dominated by the EIP-27 soft fork activation process, progressing from testnet deployment to imminent mainnet activation, alongside rapid ecosystem maturation across wallets, bridges, and DeFi primitives. Secondary themes included network resilience challenges (large airdrop transactions, node fork issues, mempool spam), accelerating wallet migration from Yoroi to Nautilus, and growing momentum around cross-chain infrastructure with Rosen Bridge and SuSy Bridge advancing in parallel.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EIP-27 Soft Fork (Emission Extension)

EIP-27 achieved ~90% miner consensus across all major pools (GetBlok, Nanopool, WoolyPooly, and others) and progressed from testnet activation to near-mainnet readiness. The soft fork activates at block height 724,000, reducing the block reward from ~67 ERG to ~51 ERG per block while routing a portion into a re-emission contract that extends miner rewards until approximately 2045. The activation mechanism uses no block header voting — instead, a version flag inbuilt to the node release triggers the fork. The first full emission reduction epoch occurred on 2022-04-03 (75 ERG → 63 ERG), with the subsequent 63 → 51 ERG reduction following ~9 hours later. kushti confirmed total re-emission of 14,337,600 ERG sufficient for ~27.28 years of additional block rewards. A 5.0 soft fork was also announced but without scope or timeline.

### Wallet Ecosystem: Nautilus Ascendant, Yoroi Declining

The month marked a decisive community shift away from Yoroi (characterised as slow, broken, and largely abandoned) toward Nautilus as the primary recommended wallet. Users were consistently advised to restore their existing seed phrase in Nautilus rather than transferring funds, as all Ergo wallets share compatible seed derivation. Minotaur Wallet launched on desktop with cold wallet functionality, dApp integration (SigmaUSD, token issuance), and cold signing support. SAFEW (Firefox extension) offered advanced features including token minting and burning via its transaction builder. Ledger integration remained delayed due to developer relocations from Ukraine, though code was described as substantially complete pending a bug fix from the Ledger team. Aneta Wallet v1 entered public testing with Ergo and ADA support.

### Cross-Chain Bridges: Rosen Bridge & SuSy Bridge

Two distinct bridge initiatives advanced simultaneously. The Rosen Bridge (Ergo-centric design by MHS) uses native multisig with dual guardian sets — one for settling cross-chain transfers, one for block-height-based security checks — representing a fundamentally different architecture from wrapped-token models. The Wormhole hack on Solana (which occurred this month) was cited as validating Rosen Bridge's security-first design. By month end, Cardano watcher implementation was nearly complete, tokenomics nearly finalised, and proof-of-concept libraries for Cardano, Bitcoin, and Ethereum had been successfully tested. Separately, the SuSy bridge (initially bringing centralised stablecoins USDT/USDC to Ergo) was operating on testnet, with kushti scheduling a meeting with the SuSy team. Both bridges were described as essential infrastructure for broader ecosystem growth.

### DeFi Primitives: Stablecoins, Lending, and Oracle Pools

Extended community discussion addressed the need for multiple stablecoin designs beyond SigmaUSD, including Dexy Protocol (Dexy USD), Yield Protocol-based stables, spender-signed currencies, and LETS. The SigmaUSD reserve ratio dropped below the healthy 400% threshold during the month, locking new minting and prompting discussion of dynamic fees and external incentives to encourage SigRSV minting. Dexy USD development continued with the team focusing on whale-attack protection mechanisms before publishing an EIP. Oracle Pools v1 (including Ergo Foundation members and external contributors) and v2 (running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards) both active; plans announced to build out native Ergo oracle infrastructure further, potentially funded through Palmyra's token sale capital. ErgoLend progressed with single-lender ERG contracts nearly complete, SigmaUSD lending version in progress, and a Kenyan microfinance pilot program in planning.

### Network Resilience: Spam, Airdrop Congestion, and Node Issues

Two notable network stress events occurred. First, the MiGoreng token airdrop (~15,000 outputs per transaction) clogged the mempool and caused KuCoin withdrawal delays; the transaction was eventually included in block #711,908 (5,983 kB) with assistance from WoolyPooly. Second, on 2022-03-28, the network stalled at block #716,475 for over an hour due to a multi-output airdrop transaction. These events prompted kushti to propose miners vote on increasing transaction output cost from 100 to 1,000–1,500 units per output (vs. 2,000 per input currently), and node version 4.0.44+ was confirmed to include limits on downloading large transaction volumes from single peers. A separate fork issue involving nodes getting stuck with mismatched block hashes was tracked in GitHub issue #1657.

### Palmyra / Winter Protocol & Chainlink Oracle Partnership

Dan Friedman detailed Palmyra's integration of Chainlink's proof-of-reserve functionality for decentralised trade financing. On-chain warehouse receipts are generated when commodity producers deposit goods into bonded warehouses, with continuous verification via IoT devices (cameras, weight sensors, motion sensors) coupled with Chainlink oracles. The approach was described as complementary rather than competitive with Ergo's native oracles, with Dan explicitly stating plans to allocate capital from Palmyra's token sale to build out the Ergo Oracle Pool network (currently only two instances). All Palmyra infrastructure is running exclusively on Ergo.

### Scaling, Sub-Blocks, and Merge-Mined Sidechains

kushti outlined the rationale for timing scaling discussions now: with a concrete set of DeFi contracts live (GARD protocols, Rosen Bridge, Oracle Pools, Spectrum DEX, AMM pools, Trade House, lending contracts), it becomes possible to identify real optimisation targets rather than speculative sharding. Proposals discussed included sub-blocks, faster block times, layer 2 options, and merge-mined sidechains. Hard constraints were identified: some contracts rely on block height rather than timestamps, and emission schedules are tied to block height — making faster blocks non-trivial. Ergo-specific sidechain constructions (improving on BIP 301) were described as forthcoming publications on the forum, using trustless pegging via smart contracts and emission contracts rewarding mainchain miners for delivering sidechain data.

### ErgoHack IV

ErgoHack IV was confirmed with a decentralisation focus. Priority tracks included full node wallets, P2P infrastructure utilisation, multiple dApp and Oracle Pool instances, and distributed Ergo Explorer backends. The single Ergo Explorer instance was identified as the most centralised component of the ecosystem and a key target for hackathon projects.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2022-03-07): If running a full node, do I need to download the entire ~17 GB blockchain?
  **A** (@CW): Yes, that is what a full node requires. NIPoPoWs will enable light clients and light mining once integrated, reducing sync requirements substantially.

- **Q** (@qx(), 2022-03-09): Will EIP-27 soft fork activation be reflected in block header voting?
  **A** (@kushti): No block header voting mechanism is used — simpler to release a node version with the activation height inbuilt as a flag.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2022-03-09): Does higher hashrate (more miners) increase network TPS?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Block time averages 2 minutes and is controlled by consensus rules, not hashrate. TPS scaling happens at the application/off-chain layer and is not affected by hashrate.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-03-14): Can Python developers build on Ergo?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Yes — AppKit + JPype + Stubgenj now supports Python. A Python wrapper is in development at the ergopad-api repo to hide the underlying Java. Vesting contracts for Paideia presale have already been demonstrated this way.

- **Q** (@Steph K, 2022-03-15): Are there any undercollateralised lending attempts in the Ergo ecosystem?
  **A** (@Chris Ray): ErgoLend contracts are ready; the single-lender ERG solution is nearly complete; SigmaUSD lending is next. The microfinance market is projected to reach $304B by 2026 — much of that will be undercollateralised. Real-world integration with Kenyan cooperatives using legal accountability is being explored.

- **Q** (@Richi, 2022-03-22): Is it possible to put limitations at the protocol level to avoid ultra-large transactions?
  **A** (@kushti): Node-level limits are more flexible than protocol-level changes. Very large output transactions create load during bootstrapping, increase orphan rates, and cause light client problems. The better solution is for miners to vote on increasing tx output cost from 100 to 1,000–1,500 units per output — outputs are currently heavily undervalued relative to inputs (2,000 units each).

- **Q** (@Hash, 2022-03-25): Don't you think a 2-minute block interval is too slow for DeFi? What is the plan to improve?
  **A** (@CW): The 2-minute block time is a conservative, secure improvement over Bitcoin. It can be improved with sub-blocks and faster transactions on layer 2s and sidechains. Ergo layer 1 is designed with many scalability options and is not intended as a fast processor of simple transactions.

- **Q** (@Hash, 2022-03-25): What secures the network once the 8-year emission schedule ends?
  **A** (@CW): EIP-27 extends block rewards to 2045 via the re-emission contract. Storage rent provides additional miner revenue (miners can claim tokens or wrapped assets from boxes with insufficient ERG). Projects can also distribute tokens through miners for fair launches.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2022-03-19): How does Ergo transaction prioritisation work without gas? Will fees explode at adoption scale?
  **A** (@Armeanio): Priority is based on fee per byte (cost per block space) — miners optimise for space, so users can voluntarily increase fees for priority. Computational cost is known pre-execution in eUTXO (unlike Ethereum). Block size is adjustable by miner vote. At Ethereum-scale adoption, layer 2 solutions are expected to absorb load before on-chain fees become prohibitive. Much computation in eUTXO is also handled off-chain.

- **Q** (@Ian Lee, 2022-03-19): What will the block reward be immediately after EIP-27 mainnet activation?
  **A** (@Armeanio, @CW): 54 ERG per block, with a further 3 ERG reduction on a 3-monthly schedule thereafter.

- **Q** (@Thomas Eh, 2022-03-18): What is Ergo's relationship to Cardano?
  **A** (@Glasgow): kushti was Scorex team lead at IOHK, left to build a PoW blockchain with the eUTXO model. Ergo partnered with Emurgo on AgeUSD (the basis for minimal Djed) and is now part of the UTXO Alliance alongside Cardano, Nervos, and others. Both blockchains share research ethos, decentralisation values, eUTXO architecture origin, and peer-reviewed published specifications — with slightly different approaches to consensus and governance.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-03-28): How does one compete with bots on SigmaUSD redemption?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Bots are using 0.1 ERG fees. Trying 0.5 ERG did not always succeed. Spectrum DEX pools often work better as an alternative. Minotaur wallet can bypass assembler-related issues.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-03-28): How do I burn unwanted spam tokens received via airdrop?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Use the token burn utility at https://tokenminter.ergo.ga/burn.html. Wallet-level blocking is not yet universally implemented.

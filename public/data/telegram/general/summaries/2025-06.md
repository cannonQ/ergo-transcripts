# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: June 2025

---

## Executive Summary

June 2025 was defined by two major milestones: the release of Ergo protocol versions 6.0.0 and 6.1.0, bringing sub-blocks voting and RocksDB performance improvements to mainnet, and the successful community-funded Banxa/Onramper fiat on-ramp integration raffle. Infrastructure decentralization accelerated with the launch of InfraDAO, storage rent collection activated across multiple mining pools, and the Dexy protocol moved toward mainnet with active gold minting and SigmaUSD v2 nearing launch.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Node 6.0.0 & 6.1.0 Releases

The most significant technical event of the month was the release of Ergo protocol reference client 6.0.0 on 2025-06-23, with miner voting for sub-blocks activation set to begin at block height 1,561,601. Sub-blocks (also called "input blocks") are lower-difficulty, high-frequency block candidates that improve throughput without sacrificing security; kushti published a WIP specification and invited community review. Version 6.1.0 followed on 2025-06-26, adding RocksDB as a backend optimization for improved node performance. The release cycle was preceded by v6.0.0-RC6 in week W23, which had been undergoing community testing. kushti also noted that 6.0 opens the door to a community-led testnet experiment for faster block times, and invited anyone passionate about the research to DM and join a dedicated group.

### Storage Rent Collection Activation

Storage rent (demurrage) became a live economic topic as HeroMiners and the Sigmanauts Mining Pool both activated automated collection during the month, with one block showing 17.314 ERG in rent payouts. The mechanism charges a maximum of ~0.14 ERG per box after four years of UTXO inactivity, calculated by box size in bytes. Debate emerged about whether third-party bots collecting rent without redistributing to miners was ethical; kushti's position was that permissionless systems allow anyone to collect, but community social pressure is a valid response. qx() committed to documenting how pools can claim and fairly distribute storage rent to miners. Users were educated that Nautilus wallet's "optimize" function consolidates boxes and resets the four-year demurrage timer at a cost of ~0.0011 ERG.

### Banxa/Onramper Fiat On-Ramp Integration

The community-funded raffle for Banxa and Onramper integration reached 100% of its 21,820 ERG (~$19.6k USD) goal by 2025-06-15 with 158 contributors. HQΣr deployed a matching incentive of 500 ERG in the final 24 hours to push it over the line. This integration enables direct USD and credit card on/off-ramps for retail users and dApp integration. Banxa's implementation timeline was stated as "a few weeks" after funding closed.

### InfraDAO & Infrastructure Decentralization

qx() announced InfraDAO at the end of the month to decentralize the hosting of Ergo nodes, explorers, APIs, and GraphQL endpoints. Load-balanced community-run endpoints went live (explorer-p2p, api-p2p, graphql-p2p, node-p2p), all running 6.1.0 RocksDB nodes. The DAO targets a ~$800/year budget for Cloudflare costs, with membership open to the community. This represents a meaningful step toward removing single points of failure from Ergo's public infrastructure.

### Dexy Protocol & SigmaUSD v2

DexyGold minting began incrementally during W24, with kushti arbitraging between the bank contract and liquidity pools to grow reserves faster than LP size. A DexyGold / SigmaUSD liquidity pool was announced as the next step, alongside plans to launch GluonUSD and DexyUSD. Separately, the SigmaUSD v2 pool contract and off-chain bot were completed by W24; UI bug fixes were the only remaining blocker before official launch. Crystal Pools launched a testnet interface for Dexy Gold swaps at test.crystalpool.cc. Community members noted that DexyGold testers are different from anticipated DexyUSD users, meaning protocol behavior at scale remains an open question.

### ErgoHack X: AI on the Ergo Blockchain

The tenth ErgoHack (2025-05-25 to 2025-06-01) delivered nine projects across AI integration, privacy, and infrastructure: SigmaQuest (AI puzzle game), RustChain (proof-of-antiquity), CodeUTXO (natural language blockchain interaction and new simple explorer), Pattern (multi-agent explorer), Game of Prompts, Pulse (decentralized on-chain chat), Cortex (GPU miner with ML tuning), SCypher (on-chain seed phrase encryption), and ErgoMempool Visualizer. Project showcase videos were published across W25–W26, with community voting underway at month's end. The $9k ERG prize pool was distributed to judged winners.

### Rosen Bridge Developments

Three new tokens were issued for Rosen Protocol v2 Dogecoin bridge infrastructure (watcher token, cleanup job NFT, collateral identifier). Armeanio published Rosen Treasury Proposal 5, proposing a 5% incentive allocation to encourage bridge contribution and adoption, with no KYC requirement. The Rosen team was also in contact with the Kaspa community for potential integration. Documentation for the RCS integration, SDK, Watcher, and Guard Service was published on DeepWiki. A community member exploring Rosen Bridge integration for Handshake (HNS) was offered investigation into bounty opportunities by Armeanio.

### Sigma Chains, Sidechains & Layer Architecture

An ongoing conceptual thread across the month distinguished sigma chains, sidechains, and layer 2 solutions. Armeanio clarified that sigma chains port the Sigma computational layer across new consensus/Sybil-resistance layers (merge mining philosophically interesting here), whereas sidechains share the computational layer with separate consensus, and layer 2 uses separate consensus and computational layers requiring bridges. The Lithos Protocol for decentralized mining pools was flagged as a near-term milestone, with testnet launch announced. kushti published a sub-blocks draft specification on GitHub and noted that Ergo's concrete existing contract set (Rosen, Oracle Pools, Spectrum, SigmaUSD, Trade House) makes on-chain optimization more tractable than chains that scale empty infrastructure.

### Palmyra & Chainlink Partnership

Armeanio detailed Palmyra's use of Chainlink proof-of-reserve combined with Winter Protocol for on-chain warehouse receipts enabling decentralized trade financing. IoT devices (cameras, weight and motion sensors) provide continuous proof-of-existence for commodity collateral stored in bonded warehouses; an oracle issues on-chain receipts. Chainlink was chosen for its network size and support, but plans include building out native Ergo oracles using capital from Palmyra's token sale. The discussion generated community pushback on using non-Ergo tools, to which Armeanio responded that Ergo being a supplier of smart finance for all chains requires welcoming partial adoption rather than tribal rejection.

### Privacy, Mixing & Stealth Addresses

ErgoMixer usage patterns were discussed in depth. kushti explained the mixer works best as a long-running wallet with incomplete mixes over time — periodic withdrawal and remix cycles, batch operations — creating complex on-chain analysis rather than a single anonymization session. The low historical user count was acknowledged as a weakness for anonymity set size, but historical incomplete mixes add to the set retroactively. Stealth addresses were discussed as a complementary tool, though business privacy use cases need better UX. Pulse encrypted messaging was proposed as an alternative channel for coordinating sensitive operations.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Shai Deshe, 2025-06-02): What does Ergo use NIPoPoWs for?
  **A** (@Pulsarz, @TMR.ΣRG): Light clients, UTXO set snapshots, and lite node sync. Android devices can sync a full Ergo node in minutes; examples include Samsung phones and other consumer hardware.

- **Q** (@Shai Deshe, 2025-06-02): Autolykos prevents pooling — is that a form of miner governance?
  **A** (@TMR.ΣRG, @mrtn): Miners can vote with hashpower. Autolykos v1 had an outsourceable puzzle (deactivated in v2). Lithos Protocol re-introduces decentralized pooling principles in a blockchain-agnostic way.

- **Q** (@Shai Deshe, 2025-06-02): By Sigma protocols do you mean three-round zero-knowledge proofs?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo contracts are based on mixed zero-knowledge Sigma protocols with predicates over blockchain context. ErgoScript Whitepaper II (Leo Reyzin) describes the design in detail.

- **Q** (@TMR.ΣRG, 2025-06-02): What is the difference between a sidechain, a sigma chain, and a layer 2?
  **A** (@Armeanio): A sigma chain ports the Sigma computational layer across a new consensus or Sybil-resistance layer — merge mining is philosophically interesting here. A sidechain shares the computational layer but uses separate consensus. A layer 2 uses both separate consensus and separate computational layers, requiring bridges.

- **Q** (@K Chan, 2025-06-06): A UTXO has insufficient ERG to cover storage rent but holds valuable tokens — what happens?
  **A** (@cannon_q): This has been a known design characteristic since genesis. Consolidate your wallet every four years to avoid the situation. Paying a small fee to store value on a peer-to-peer ledger is not unreasonable.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2025-06-17): ErgoMixer has historically had only two or three users — doesn't that make deanonymization trivial by process of elimination?
  **A** (@kushti): The mixer works best with incomplete mixes maintained over time, with periodic withdrawal and remix cycles. Unlike a single-session mixer, Ergo's mixer acts as a wallet with historical UTXOs of different ages, creating complex chain analysis. Historical incomplete mixes from prior years remain in the contract, adding to the anonymity set retroactively.

- **Q** (@Turkish Delight, 2025-06-17): I have a three-year-old box in Nautilus — what does "optimize" do, and will it protect me from storage rent?
  **A** (@cannon_q): The optimize function consolidates all boxes by sending them to yourself in a single transaction, merging them into one new box and resetting the four-year demurrage timer. It costs approximately 0.0011 ERG.

- **Q** (@Alx, 2025-06-19): Block reward drops from 15 to 3 ERG in roughly ten months. How does Ergo handle this without a hardfork?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo's emission is an on-chain contract, not hardcoded. Miners can vote to increase storage rent collection up to 2× the current value without a hardfork. No hardforks have been planned since 2022. Radiant's failure was due to ASIC takeover, a separate issue.

- **Q** (@Marc the Shark, 2025-06-11): Reviewing an AI-generated ErgoScript emission contract — what are the key issues?
  **A** (@HQΣr): Fix governance to use proper signature verification instead of proposition hash comparison; explicitly validate ERG and token balances; add a token balance check to ensure the state output has `SELF.tokens` minus the emission rate; define governance constraints; verify transaction inputs properly.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2025-06-23): Are storage rent rewards exclusive to mining pools?
  **A** (@Pgr456): There is no formal exclusivity. Historically miners had priority because they can include their own transactions above others. With newer node versions anyone can collect, but miners retain a structural advantage through transaction ordering.

- **Q** (@Dmitriy B., 2025-06-25): Why does Ergo feel fast some days and slow on others during dApp testing?
  **A** (@qx(), @kushti): Block time averages two minutes but has natural variance. Use an indexed node for better dApp performance. If your application needs predictability, consider chaining transactions in the mempool.

- **Q** (@Shai Deshe, 2025-06-29): Which protocol concept is more important to study — sigma chains or Autolykos?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo currently follows Bitcoin-style PoW with ASIC-resistant Autolykos. Sub-blocks (input blocks) are a work in progress to improve throughput; the draft spec is on GitHub and community collaboration on the specification is welcome.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

1. **Sub-blocks specification finalization**: kushti invited community review and collaboration on the input blocks specification; consensus process, testnet parameters, and activation timeline are all TBD.

2. **SigmaUSD v2 official launch**: Contract and off-chain bot complete; only UI bugs remain. Exact launch date not confirmed at month's end.

3. **DexyGold to DexyUSD scaling**: Community noted that DexyGold testers differ significantly from anticipated DexyUSD users; protocol behavior and LP dynamics at scale are unknown.

4. **Fiat on-ramp implementation timeline**: Banxa/Onramper integration funded; Banxa stated "a few weeks" for implementation, but no hard date confirmed.

5. **Storage rent bot ethics and pool competition**: Debate on whether permissionless bot collection is fair or harmful remains unresolved. Documentation by qx() for pool-side collection is in progress; competitive reshaping of pool economics ongoing.

6. **Faster block time testnet**: kushti offered to invite community members to run a 6.0 testnet with tweaked block time parameters, but needs a community leader to organize it. No volunteer confirmed.

7. **ErgoMixer accessibility vs. privacy trade-off**: Whether to wrap the mixer in a hosted web UI (Tornado Cash-style) or keep it self-hosted only; privacy implications of each approach unresolved.

8. **Keystone hardware wallet native ERG support**: Integration being pursued but not confirmed; Twitter reference indicated it was not yet natively supported as of W23.

9. **Paideia UX issues**: Network errors on comment submission and root cause (node, wallet state, or application) remain undiagnosed.

10. **ErgoPay compatibility with Spectrum DEX**: Works on MewFinance but fails on Spectrum DEX; root cause not determined.

11. **Rosen Bridge Handshake (HNS) integration**: Armeanio offered to investigate bounty opportunities; no formal commitment made.

12. **InfraDAO static IP requirements**: Whether node operators need static IPs to participate in InfraDAO was unclear at announcement.

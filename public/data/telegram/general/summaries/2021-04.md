# Ergo General — Monthly Developer Knowledge Base Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General | **Month:** 2021-04

---

## Executive Summary

April 2021 was a defining month for Ergo's ecosystem maturity, dominated by three converging themes: the launch and stress-testing of SigmaUSD v2 through a sustained "bearwhale" attack, the progressive rollout of developer tooling (Yoroi dApp connector, ErgoMixer v3.3.0, ErgoScript by Example), and intensifying community discussion around Ergo's strategic relationship with Cardano and its own independent value proposition. Exchange infrastructure friction (Hotbit cyberattack, CoinEx dust issues, BitMart price isolation) created headwinds, but major media coverage via Cointelegraph and a ~80,000-attendee Chinese AMA significantly raised Ergo's profile.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### SigmaUSD v2 Launch, Reserve Mechanics & the Bearwhale Attack

SigmaUSD v2 went live at sigmausd.io on 2021-03-29 with a bootstrapping period (upper reserve ratio limit of 800% unenforced) running until block 460,000. The core mechanic — reserve ratio as a safeguard against dilution rather than a direct price lever — was repeatedly clarified throughout the month, with the 400%–800% corridor governing SigRSV mint/redeem eligibility.

By Week 14–15, a large holder had taken a substantial short position, minting approximately 900,000 SigUSD at an average of $2.58 ERG and dumping to suppress price. The community tracked the position in real time; by mid-Week 15 the whale was down approximately 57,221 ERG as ERG price pushed past $3.80, putting the short at serious risk. Reserve ratio spent much of this period below 400%, preventing SigRSV redemptions. The episode served as an unplanned stress test that demonstrated the protocol's resilience, though it highlighted edge-case UX gaps around reserve depletion behavior.

SigRSV mechanics remained a persistent source of community confusion. The ERG:SigRSV exchange ratio is path-dependent (affected by cumulative fees, mints, and redemptions), not directly readable from the reserve ratio alone. Multiple community members (@Klaus, @Glasgow, @Gazza) attempted simplified explanations but consistently concluded the formula required reading protocol documentation. A solution for PoW-chain "staking" — liquidity provision to ErgoMixer — was proposed by kushti on the forum late in the month.

### Yoroi dApp Connector & ErgoDEX Progress

The Yoroi dApp connector (EIP-12, MetaMask-equivalent) entered beta testing in Week 15, with Chrome and Firefox test builds released. This was widely celebrated as a critical infrastructure unlock: it enables single-transaction interactions with smart contracts (e.g., SigmaUSD auctions previously requiring two manual transactions). By Week 16, anon_real was integrating it into the Ergo Auction House and the ErgoDEX team was integrating it for DEX interactions.

ErgoDEX core contracts were described as "essentially complete" by late April, with UI development underway. The architecture combines AMM pools and an order book with shared liquidity — a design noted as unique in the space. Port to Cardano planned for Alonzo launch; a Project Catalyst Fund5 proposal was submitted for the Plutus port. No separate ErgoDEX token was confirmed as necessary for operation; a possible governance token for contract update council was floated but not finalized. The community repeatedly pushed the Catalyst proposal for votes.

### ErgoMixer v3.3.0

The mixer was updated with SigmaUSD/SigRSV mixing and direct mint capability, making it the first non-custodial, non-interactive mixer supporting programmable tokens on an eUTXO chain. Known limitations included Safari browser incompatibility and the requirement for the node to be online during active mixing (though covert addresses accumulate funds offline). A one-liner install was published by @Glasgow. The mixer's zero UI fee for SigUSD/SigRSV was highlighted as a technical differentiator.

### Ergo–Cardano Relationship Clarification

The most recurring strategic discussion of the month concerned Ergo's role relative to Cardano. The dominant framing that emerged: Ergo is a *testbed* for eUTXO concepts (stablecoins, DEXes, mixers, oracle pools) that can later be ported to Cardano — not a competitor. AgeUSD on Cardano will use a separate ADA-denominated reserve. The concern raised (@Marek M, @Klaus) was that high DeFi yields on Cardano could drain staking participation, reducing security — a concern that doesn't apply to Ergo's PoW model.

At the same time, @Armeanio and others pushed back on over-reliance on the Cardano narrative, arguing Ergo's DEX and stablecoin have independent value: cross-chain swaps and Rosen Bridge-style interoperability mean Cardano is not a prerequisite for Ergo's success. The Ergo Manifesto (published 2021-04-26) articulated the cypherpunk, fair-launch, anti-CBDC values underpinning the project's independent identity.

### Oracle Pools Architecture

Ergo oracle pools were compared extensively to Chainlink throughout the month, particularly after a dedicated blog post (2021-04-27). Key differentiators: hierarchical trust scores via multi-stage contracts rather than token staking; no Link-drain problem; native to UTXO model. The ADA/USD oracle pool was confirmed as deprecated post-testing; the ERG/USD pool remains active. Chainlink was chosen by Palmyra (ZenGate) for its network size and breadth of support, but the team stated this is not zero-sum — Ergo oracle pools remain on the roadmap for Palmyra use cases, with capital from the token sale earmarked to build out the network.

### Exchange Infrastructure Issues

Three exchange crises ran in parallel across the month:
- **CoinEx**: ERG withdrawals suspended ~7 days due to wallet overload from mining pool dust deposits (sub-0.01 ERG hourly from Autolykos v2 pool payouts). Resolved by merge-collecting inputs.
- **Hotbit**: Extended maintenance (2021-04-08), followed by a cyberattack announcement requiring 7–14 days for full recovery. All funds confirmed safe; recommended workaround was Gate.io or CoinEx.
- **BitMart**: Disabled deposits/withdrawals caused an isolated market showing artificially inflated ERG prices ($17–70). @Armeanio reached out to BitMart contacts.

Withdrawal fee comparison consistently cited: CoinEx ~0.1 ERG, Hotbit ~1 ERG, Gate.io ~2 ERG. Exchange listing discussions (KuCoin rumored, Coinbase speculated) remained NDA-covered throughout.

### Mining & Emission

GPU mining profitability discussion was active throughout, with WhatToMine showing ERG in top 5 by mid-month. Single-GPU viability confirmed: GTX 1070 yields ~4–5 ERG/week at 55–58 MH/s, ~80W power draw. Ergo's smooth emission curve (no halvings, ~6 more years of primary emission from 2021) and storage rent mechanism for long-term security were explained repeatedly. Autolykos v2's removal of pool outsourceability was noted as the root cause of the dust deposit problem at exchanges.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Klaus, 2021-03-31): At what reserve ratio should SigRSV be purchased for optimal entry?
  **A** (@Klaus, detailed self-analysis): When RR is high (e.g., near 800%), the ERG:SigRSV ratio is closer to 1:1, meaning less leverage. After cooling-off at block 460,000 when RR drops below 800%, ratios improve significantly (v1 was ~1:930–960 vs ~1:847 at launch). Better to wait for RR <800% before entering a SigRSV long.

- **Q** (multiple users, 2021-W13–W14): How is SigRSV redemption value calculated?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @Gazza, @Dan): SigRSV value is path-dependent — affected by cumulative protocol fees, mints, and redemptions over the contract's history. The ERG:SigRSV ratio changes over time; if you minted at 1 ERG = 800 SigRSV and the ratio is now 1 ERG = 750 SigRSV, you receive more ERG per SigRSV on redemption. Formula is in the AgeUSD GitHub; not calculable in real-time from reserve ratio alone. If reserve ratio drops below 400%, SigRSV redemption is blocked entirely — it becomes a perpetual call option on future reserves.

- **Q** (@ivysauceda, 2021-W13): Transaction left wallet, no history in SigmaUSD dashboard. How to recover?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Use the refund endpoint: `https://assm.sigmausd.io/return/YOUR_ADDRESS/LONG_CONTRACT_ADDRESS`. Find the contract address by clicking the TX ID in Yoroi. Returns a refund TX ID; funds return in minutes. Root cause was a node bug related to frequent oracle refresh (being fixed).

- **Q** (@JayDee12Thr33, 2021-W13): Does Ergo support multisig?
  **A** (community): Yes. See kushti's 2020 confirmation: https://twitter.com/chepurnoy/status/1230236923295346688

- **Q** (@LBZ_TRADING, 2021-04-17): What is Ergo used for, other than testing projects for ADA?
  **A** (@Gazza): A DEX with blockchain interoperability is the holy grail — SigmaUSD is needed for that DEX, and Cardano is not a prerequisite for Ergo's success. The framing should be inverted: it's not what Cardano can do for Ergo, it's what Ergo can do for Cardano.

- **Q** (@Joey_Z, 2021-04-15): Can someone summarise the bearwhale situation?
  **A** (@Glasgow): A large holder minted ~500k–900k SigUSD (shorting ERG), then attempted price manipulation. By mid-week the position was down ~57,000+ ERG as ERG price exceeded $3.80. The reserve ratio staying below 400% blocked SigRSV redemptions, keeping the short under pressure.

- **Q** (@Team Team, 2021-W17): How do oracle pools incentivize good behaviour without token staking?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Multi-stage contracts create hierarchical trust scores. The incentive is the oracle service fee. Bad oracles receive low trust scores and earn no fees — no upfront staking capital required. Contrast with Chainlink where node operators must stake LINK.

- **Q** (@Ys, 2021-W16): What does ErgoScript allow that Cardano cannot do?
  **A** (@Marek M, @Klaus): ErgoScript's sigma protocols enable non-interactive, non-custodial mixing of tokens (not just base asset), hybrid on-chain/off-chain ZK contracts, and oracle pools with trust-score hierarchies — all without trusted setups. A concern specific to Cardano's PoS: high DeFi yields could drain staking participation, reducing network security. This attack vector doesn't apply to Ergo's PoW model.

- **Q** (@user, 2021-W16): Can ErgoScript support randomised NFT package opening?
  **A** (@c8): Yes, for randomised package mechanics a script would be required; for standard art NFTs, minting is straightforward without scripting.

- **Q** (@MHS_SAM, 2021-W14): Must the ErgoMixer node stay running during an active mix?
  **A** (community): No. Covert addresses accumulate funds offline; mixing and withdrawal can be triggered at any time. The mix is not time-locked to an active session.

- **Q** (@$yuji, 2021-W13): How will Ergo stay relevant as Ethereum transitions to PoS?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @Chris Ray): Ergo's PoW is a deliberate design choice, not a legacy constraint — GPU-friendly, fair launch, NIPoPoW-enabled, energy-efficient via Autolykos. The "PoW = bad" narrative should be resisted. Ergo is committed to adopting the best available technology, not stagnating.

- **Q** (@WhiskyWizzy, 2021-W15): Is mining a single GTX 1070 worth it?
  **A** (@ponte): Yes — 4–5 ERG/week at 55–58 MH/s, under 80W. Profitable relative to electricity cost, and contributes to network security.

- **Q** (@btt, 2021-W17): Why would Cardano need multiple oracle solutions?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Cardano's design philosophy is to support multiple oracle providers and let developers choose based on quality, trust scores, and use-case fit. Competition between providers drives quality. Ergo oracle pools, Chainlink, Wolfram, and Charli3 can coexist.

- **ErgoDEX governance token**: No confirmed tokenomics for a governance/update-council token; "not needed for operation" is established, but governance mechanism for contract upgrades unresolved.

- **Cardano Africa / AgeUSD on Cardano specifics**: Community asked about Ergo involvement at Cardano Africa event (April 29). AgeUSD Cardano deployment details — separate ADA reserve, oracle integration, cross-chain functionality — not yet specified.
- **Scalability roadmap**: Sub-blocks, microblocks, sidechains, and layer 2 discussed as medium-term options. kushti stated post-Ergoversary community discussion planned; no concrete implementation timeline.
- **Mobile Yoroi for ERG**: "Mobile bindings" in progress; Emurgo possibly funding; no release date.

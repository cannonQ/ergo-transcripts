# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: January 2026

---

## Executive Summary

January 2026 was a month of broad ecosystem maturation across Ergo, with significant parallel progress on protocol infrastructure (sub-blocks/Matrix devnet, node v6.0.x releases), bridge expansion (Rosen Bridge gaining Nervos, Firo, and Catalyst funding for BASE/INK integrations), and community tooling (VoltPay v2, Lithos Protocol v3.0, Ergo Knowledge Base, Ergo Book). A recurring theme was the tension between rapid dApp development — including several fully decentralized IPFS-hosted applications — and the need for better developer documentation, decentralized mining infrastructure, and long-term protocol scaling decisions. The month closed with active Matrix devnet testing and growing community investment in knowledge infrastructure and stablecoin adoption.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Sub-blocks (Matrix) & Protocol Scaling

The sub-blocks whitepaper (internally called "Matrix" or "input blocks") was the most sustained technical discussion of the month. kushti published the draft paper to GitHub in early January and relaunched devnet testing with two peers by month's end. The specification aims to reduce confirmation times to approximately 2 seconds and combines sub-blocks with non-democratic leadership and Autolykos v2 to position Ergo as competitive p2p cash infrastructure. Community discussion explored whether sub-blocks, layer 2 solutions, or sidechains represent the best scaling path — noting that Ergo's current concrete contract ecosystem (GARD protocols, Rosen Bridge, Oracle Pools, Spectrum DEX, Trade House, lending contracts) now provides a meaningful benchmark for optimization. No mainnet activation date was set; kushti noted a community roundtable on speed improvements was forthcoming and invited anyone passionate about the topic to help run testnet parameter experiments.

### Rosen Bridge Expansion

Rosen Bridge saw substantial expansion activity across the month. Catalyst F15 proposals for BASE integration (Coinbase) and INK integration (Kraken), both involving Ergo native tokens, passed review and opened for voting. The Nervos DAO vote for CKB integration passed mid-month. Firo was also announced as an incoming integration. Armeanio celebrated the first external PR from the Rosen Bridge Expansion Kit being merged, marking a milestone in bridge extensibility. kushti introduced a multi-address reward distribution system enabling external developers from partner chains to earn from fee volume, opening new infrastructure monetization paths. The Rosen Bridge was also featured on the MoneroTalk podcast.

### USE Stablecoin & Dexy Gold

USE stablecoin matured considerably during January. It launched concentrated liquidity pools on Binance Smart Chain with USDT pairs, was verified across Taptools, GeckoTerminal, CMC DEX Terminals, and listed on DefiLlama (recategorized as algorithmic). VoltPay completed its first ADA→USE swap. Oracle Core v2.0.5 and v2.0.6 were released to fix CoinGecko API integration issues that had affected oracle pools, including the Dexy Gold pool running on Oracle Pool v2 with GORT rewards. Richi confirmed the GOLDmg ticker for Dexy Gold and raised the issue of aligning measurement units with international standards (troy ounces vs. milligrams) for broader adoption. Community discussion produced a proposal for redundant smart contracts displaying both units, with USE positioned as the critical adoption foundation before Dexy Gold launch.

### Community dApps & Decentralized Applications

qx() released three fully decentralized, IPFS-hosted applications in early January: **SIGHT** (anonymous reporting with PoW spam prevention), **SAY** (p2p Twitter alternative with on-chain follow lists and ErgoPay integration), and **Ephemeral Messenger v1.1** (self-destructing on-chain messages with passphrase encryption and ERG burn rewards for burners). MewPump launched as a meme token platform with its first token deployed. Josemi launched Game of Prompts, a bot competition platform on Ergo. The Degens.World ecosystem deployed LocalDex (AMM, limit orders, arbitrage scanner), LithosPanel (one-click node/miner deployment), and began work on a redesigned Ergo Mobile Wallet UI with dApp browser and swap features.

### Lithos Protocol & Mining Infrastructure

Lithos Protocol v3.0 testnet was released in mid-January, and LithosPanel went live for streamlined node, Lithos client, and miner deployment. Mining centralization concerns emerged late in the month when 2miners was identified as holding approximately 56% of network hashrate with Herominers at 23%. Community calls for pool diversification were made, though no concrete campaign was launched. Autolykos's memory-hard and frequency-hopping requirements were discussed as sufficient protection against ASIC development in the near term, with an upgrade path available if needed. GPU performance discussions (AMD MI60 achieving up to 353 MH/s with SRBminer) and Lithos Protocol's suitability for single-GPU home miners were addressed.

### Knowledge Infrastructure: Ergo Book & Ergo Knowledge Base

Two major documentation initiatives launched simultaneously in the final week. cannon_q deployed the **Ergo Knowledge Base** (ergo-transcripts.vercel.app), a searchable archive of 76+ ecosystem videos, AMAs, and technical discussions with executive summaries, speaker identification, and live YouTube timestamps. kushti initiated the **Ergo Book** — a comprehensive mdbook on Ergo's monetary philosophy and technical foundations covering alternative monetary stacks, protocol design, Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge, SigmaUSD, Dexy, and practical scenarios. Both projects reflect a recognized gap: developer and community documentation has been scattered across multiple repositories, with no integrated, step-by-step tutorial pathway. Community discussion proposed a separate aggregation repo for developer tutorials covering on-chain, off-chain, multi-stage smart contracts, and scanner integration.

### Node Infrastructure & Explorer Issues

Node v6.0.2 was released to fix an explorer parsing failure at block 1705794, where an output containing an unparseable script caused strict parsers to fail — a known trade-off of Ergo's soft-fork flexibility allowing non-Ergo protocol support in UTXOs. Users with extra indices enabled were directed to upgrade. Node v6.0.3 was queued with multiple PRs. The Ergo dark mode PR was submitted, and Ergo wallet-app 2.5 release candidate was prepared. GraphQL endpoint issues prompted users to use the p2p fallback (graphql-p2p.ergoplatform.com) or Sigmaspace.

### Keystone Hardware Wallet Integration Stalemate

The Nautilus-Keystone integration bounty was completed by Pgr456 but blocked due to security concerns: Keystone's dependencies would add 160+ transient NPM packages from numerous authors to Nautilus, representing a significant supply chain attack surface. The team had previously reduced Nautilus's dependency count from ~120 to ~90. Work continues on extracting a minimal SDK; no timeline was provided. The same blocker affects FleetSDK.

### Palmyra & Chainlink Partnership

Dan Friedman detailed Palmyra's use of Chainlink for proof-of-reserve and on-chain warehouse receipts enabling decentralized trade financing. IoT devices (cameras, weight/motion sensors) would provide continuous commodity monitoring, with receipts issued on-chain via oracle. Dan noted this is not a zero-sum choice — multiple oracle providers including native Ergo oracles are planned, with capital from the Palmyra token sale earmarked for building out native Ergo oracle infrastructure. The use of Chainlink was driven by network size and support capabilities for this specific use case, while the overall Palmyra ecosystem runs exclusively on Ergo.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Cheese Enthusiast / qx(), 2026-01-02): If fees are paid through the TRUE contract, is the miner expected to reject transactions which attempt to spend the fee UTXO of another previous transaction?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes — miners should prioritize their own fee UTXOs logically.

- **Q** (@T Mas, 2026-W01): What countermeasures exist if Autolykos is cracked (e.g., ASIC development analogous to Monero's situation)?
  **A** (@ArØhβΣ): Many built-in features prevent most ASIC development. Memory cost makes custom hardware economically unviable; FPGAs are more plausible but require ongoing bitstream maintenance and good chips at reasonable cost. A long-term algorithm upgrade path exists if needed.

- **Q** (@fiqihalam32, 2026-W01): Is Lithos Protocol suitable for mining with a single gaming GPU rather than a dedicated rig?
  **A** (@GabGno): Yes — the card can mine without relying on a centralized pool operation while remaining as profitable as pool mining.

- **Q** (@Josemi, 2026-01-10): How can a token appear with its full emission amount across two separate output boxes simultaneously?
  **A** (@Luca D'Angelo): Token uniqueness is enforced by token ID, not amount distribution. Emission amounts can be spread across multiple UTXOs without violating uniqueness.

- **Q** (@DiscordBridge/ErgoMinnow, 2026-01-16): Token market cap for Comet appears incorrect — showing 12k using liquid supply rather than total supply.
  **A** (@Pluno AI via DiscordBridge): Confirmed — ErgoExplorer may be using liquid supply only. Flagged to Aco for review. Best current option is `emission × price`; CruxFinance circulating supply data could be integrated.

- **Q** (@Ollie, 2026-01-14): What is the status of the Keystone hardware wallet integration with Nautilus?
  **A** (@Pgr456; @HQΣr): Integration is technically complete but blocked — Keystone's dependencies would add 160+ transient NPM packages to Nautilus representing major supply chain attack vectors. Team is attempting minimal SDK extraction. Same blocker affects FleetSDK.

- **Q** (@qx(), 2026-01-22): How many addresses can Nautilus handle in a single airdrop transaction?
  **A** (@qx()): Successfully tested 1,000 token recipients in a single TX (~2 seconds to build, ~2 seconds to send). The app warns after 20 recipients but allows proceeding at user's own risk.

- **Q** (@kushti, 2026-01-23): Why did explorer backend parsing fail at block 1705794?
  **A** (@kushti): The output contained an unparseable script. Ergo's consensus protocol intentionally allows this for soft-fork flexibility and non-Ergo protocol support in UTXOs, but strict parsers failed. A similar issue occurred in 2021–22 and was fixed. Resolved in v6.0.2.

- **Q** (@wolfgrimm, 2026-01-29): AMD MI60 GPUs only achieving ~14 MH/s on teamredminer for Autolykos — expected performance?
  **A** (@Grayman): MI60 cards have ~1TB/s memory bandwidth and should perform equivalently to Radeon VII (~180 MH/s). Recommended SRBminer for better optimization. User later achieved ~353 MH/s with SRBminer.

- **Q** (@Bart Bartimeus, 2026-W05): Is there a way to get yield on ERG for accumulation?
  **A** (@HQΣr): No direct ERG yield by design. Alternatives include yield farms on MewDex (mostly off-native assets), DuckPools, P2P bonds on SigmaFi, and liquidity provision on Spectrum DEX. (@kushti): All yield carries risk; the absence of native ERG yield is intentional.

- **Q** (@T Mas, 2026-01-13): Are NIPoPoWs relevant for LoRaWAN and radio-based transactions in internet outage scenarios (e.g., Iran-style shutdowns)?
  **A** (@kushti): Confirmed relevance. Guides on maintaining economic activity using Ergo tech under partial or full internet outage are needed and would be valuable.

- **Q** (@n1ur0, 2026-01-29): Should the Ergo Book include a practical developer section with setup, on-chain, and off-chain examples?
  **A** (@cannon_q): Docs exist at docs.ergoplatform.com but lack seamless connection. Suggestion: a separate repo aggregating developer information with step-by-step tutorials, multi-stage smart contract examples, scanner integration, and real dApp case studies.

## Open Questions Entering February 2026

1. **Matrix/Sub-blocks Mainnet Timeline**: Devnet relaunched but no activation date or network upgrade vote scheduled. Path selection (sub-blocks vs. layer 2 vs. sidechain) remains open; community roundtable promised but not yet held.

2. **Keystone SDK Minimal Extraction**: Work in progress to reduce NPM supply chain attack surface below current ~90 packages; no target count or timeline communicated. Blocking both Nautilus and FleetSDK hardware wallet support.

3. **Token Market Cap Methodology**: ErgoExplorer confirmed to be using liquid supply rather than total or circulating supply for market cap calculations. No correction timeline provided; Aco flagged for review.

4. **Mining Hashrate Concentration**: 2miners at ~56%, Herominers at ~23%. No formal decentralization campaign launched despite community concern. Lithos Protocol's decentralized pooling model is the structural solution but adoption pace unclear.

5. **Dev Documentation Integration**: Both the Ergo Book and Ergo Knowledge Base are early-stage. No owner or timeline assigned for an integrated developer tutorial repository covering on-chain, off-chain, multi-stage smart contracts, and scanner integration.

6. **USE Deployment Spec Accuracy**: Decimal configuration mismatch (spec lists 6, actual is 3) was flagged in Week 2 but resolution status was not confirmed in subsequent weeks.

7. **iOS App Store Strategy for Ergo Mobile Wallet**: Updated UI/UX in development (Degens.World); distribution approach (App Store, F-Droid, direct APK) and security review process TBD.

8. **Native Ergo Oracle Build Timeline**: Dan committed capital from Palmyra token sale toward building native Ergo oracles; no start date or milestone targets provided. Currently only two Oracle Pools active (one EF-run, one for Dexy Gold).

9. **Hackathon Format in LLM Era**: kushti flagged concern about 100% AI-generated submissions in external hackathons. No decision made on whether Ergo should modify its own hackathon format or criteria.

10. **Speed Improvements Governance**: Sub-blocks testnet parameter experimentation requires a community volunteer to lead — no one formally stepped up despite an open invite from kushti by month's end.

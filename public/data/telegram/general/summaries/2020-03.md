# Ergo General — Monthly Developer Knowledge Base Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General | **Month:** 2020-03

---

## Executive Summary

March 2020 was an early-stage community-building month for Ergo, dominated by onboarding activity, airdrop campaign management, and articulation of core use cases to a growing audience. Technical discussion remained largely conceptual rather than implementation-focused, with recurring themes around mining economics, exchange listings, and the distinctive features of Ergo's eUTXO model — particularly storage rent, Sigma protocols, and privacy tooling.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Ergo's Value Proposition & Use Cases
Across all four weeks, community members and team representatives consistently articulated five core use cases: (1) digital gold via proof-of-work scarcity; (2) privacy-preserving applications, including a non-interactive, non-custodial mixer under development; (3) financial contracts using complex Sigma protocol-based signature schemes; (4) alternative financial systems such as targeted loans and local exchange trading (LETS); and (5) money for hard times — light client support and digital gold properties for regions with failing institutions or limited internet. The positioning of Ergo as "Smart Money" rather than a purely speculative asset was repeated throughout the month as a framing device for newcomers.

### Storage Rent
@scalahub (msg#24519, 2020-03-12) highlighted storage rent as a critically underemphasized differentiating feature, noting it requires ongoing payment to maintain unspent coins on-chain. The mechanism was also referenced in the context of post-emission economics: unlike Bitcoin, which depends entirely on users after block rewards end, Ergo's storage rent provides a sustainable miner incentive independent of transaction volume. No detailed technical implementation discussion occurred in public chat; interested parties were directed to documentation.

### Sigma Protocols
A "Building Ergo" series article on Sigma protocols was published and shared during W12. The protocol was described as the foundation for Ergo's flexible cryptographic contracts, enabling complex multi-signature schemes and privacy-preserving applications. The month ended with discussion of how Sigma protocols could be combined with zero-knowledge constructions, though detailed technical elaboration remained limited in the general channel.

### Airdrop Campaign (Magnum)
The Magnum airdrop campaign was a dominant operational topic throughout March. Rewards were set at 0.7 ERG per participant with 0.05 ERG per referral. Community sentiment was mildly negative regarding reward size (perceived as undervalued at ~$0.12–$0.18 USD per ERG at the time). The distribution deadline was confirmed as 27 April 2020. Questions about wallet compatibility (Waves wallets, exchange wallets) were frequent but often inadequately answered in the general channel.

### Mining Economics & Market Dynamics
Mining profitability questions appeared every week, particularly around mid-range GPU performance (GTX 1080). The presence of industrial miners from mainnet launch was noted as having pushed out smaller speculators and contributed to early price suppression. @Vadim V (msg#21964–21967) argued that institutional buyers accumulating $300k+/month and a Binance listing were prerequisites for sustainable price support given the daily mining supply (~50 ERG/block) entering the market. Hashrate was reported as continuing to grow throughout the month.

### Exchange Listings & Liquidity
Ergo was confirmed live on ProBit, Tidex, HotBit, and Waves.exchange, with an USDC pair available on OKEx by W13. Community members repeatedly requested Binance and Bittrex listings; the marketing team (@bez) confirmed ongoing negotiations under NDA. Charles Hoskinson's public mention of kushti (Alexander Chepurnoy) and Scorex in a TwoBitIdiot interview (W10) was noted as meaningful third-party validation.

### Privacy Tooling & Regulatory Risk
W13 surfaced substantive discussion around the non-custodial mixer in development and whether regulatory risk was material. The community position, articulated by multiple participants, was that Ergo's design cannot be stopped by authorities — the *existence* of such possibilities is structurally embedded in the protocol. Legal context varied by jurisdiction (Poland, Russia referenced); no consensus on mitigation strategies was reached.

### Community Infrastructure
Localization channels were formally established during W11 across Indonesian, Hindi/Bengali, Chinese, Russian, and Persian communities, alongside @ErgoSocials for coordinated Twitter/Reddit marketing and @ergo_mining for technical mining support. @bez led a community-driven marketing initiative targeting social media promotion.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, msg#21017, 2020-03-07): What is Ergo's value proposition vs. Bitcoin and Ethereum?
  **A** (@Unknown, msg#21018): "Ergo Platform is a multi-asset proof-of-work cryptocurrency that combines powerful financial contracts with the extended UTXO model, making it Smart Money. It has unique flexible crypto-contracts based on Sigma protocols, storage rent, stateless and ultra-efficient SPV client support, and a focus on long-term survivability."

- **Q** (@Martin, msg#25215, 2020-03-24): How does Ergo work when max supply is reached? Won't the chain collapse or become vulnerable without mining rewards?
  **A** (@Unknown, msg#25229): Blockchains are sooner abandoned by developers than they collapse — forks or delistings occur instead. Ergo's economy supports miners via storage rent with or without high transaction volume; Bitcoin depends entirely on users after emission ends.

- **Q** (@Willy, msg#25239, 2020-03-24): Aren't you afraid of law enforcement attention after creating a crypto mixer?
  **A** (@Unknown, msg#25243): The mixer and Ergo use cases cannot be stopped from existing. Ergo's design itself is not a problem to authorities — the *possibility* of Ergo's design is their problem.

- **Q** (@Evgeny, msg#24974, 2020-03-16): How many coins per day with 1 GH/s?
  **A** (@Unknown, msg#24975): Directed to @ergo_mining channel for mining profitability calculations.

- **Q** (@Unknown, msg#25373, 2020-03-27): How to generate an address using Swagger's `/utils/ergoTreeToAddress/{ergoTreeHex}`? How is ErgoTree generated?
  **A** (@Kookster, msg#25375): Discord is a better resource for this technical question.

- **Q** (@scalahub, msg#24519, 2020-03-12): [On storage rent as a feature] Is storage rent being sufficiently emphasised in marketing?
  **A** (implicit, community): Storage rent was identified as critically underemphasised; highlighted as a key differentiator from Bitcoin and Ethereum with no long-term miner incentive mechanism comparable to Ergo's.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Wallet compatibility for airdrop**: Whether Waves wallets or exchange wallets can receive airdrop distributions remained inconsistently answered; native ERG wallet requirement not universally communicated.
- **Hardware wallet support (Ledger/Trezor)**: Status of integration with Ledger Nano S/X and Trezor not addressed in public chat.
- **Mining ROI specifics**: No concrete daily ERG earnings figures published for common GPU models (e.g., GTX 1080); community consistently redirected to Discord/mining channel.
- **Major exchange listings**: Binance and Bittrex listing timelines unknown; NDA prevents disclosure; community frustration visible but unresolved.
- **Mixer UI and privacy tooling**: Non-custodial mixer confirmed in development but no timeline, testnet, or implementation details shared publicly.
- **Full node wallet with privacy features**: Community requests for a convenient, all-platform full node wallet with anonymous transactions acknowledged but unscheduled.
- **DEX and DeFi tooling**: Interest in a full-featured DEX and stablecoin/DeFi layer expressed repeatedly; no roadmap commitments made.
- **ERGO vs. ERGO2 address confusion in Magnum wallet**: User-reported issue with dual address display in Magnum wallet unresolved by month end.
- **Waves.exchange withdrawal delays**: Reports of 560+ confirmation delays and 24+ hour withdrawal windows escalated but resolution status unclear.

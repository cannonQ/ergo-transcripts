# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: 2023-02

---

## Executive Summary

February 2023 was defined by three converging threads: Rosen Bridge moving from internal stress testing toward public testnet launch, ErgoHack VI (Cypherpunk Finance) running across the full month, and an active ecosystem build-out across DeFi primitives including Spectrum DEX yield farming, DuckPools lending, Machina Finance, and SigmaFi. Alongside these, the community engaged in substantive technical debates around speed improvements, merge-mined sidechains, bulletproofs/zero-knowledge proofs in ErgoScript, and the long-term security implications of Ergo's mining decentralization.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Rosen Bridge — From Stress Testing to Imminent Public Testnet
Rosen Bridge dominated infrastructure conversation throughout February. Early in the month, a memory leak in Sigma Rust was fixed via PRs from @greenhat, and private testing began. By week two, concurrent transaction stress tests (9+ parallel transfers) completed successfully; race conditions and bottlenecks were resolved, and the team switched from Koios to Ogmios for improved reliability. The first draft of tokenomics was finalized by late February. A Watcher UI and Guards frontend were in early development, and GitHub/GitLab branches were kept in sync for transparency. Public testnet launch was described as imminent pending a final stress test run, with ErgoFaucet updated to distribute test tokens.

### ErgoHack VI — Cypherpunk Finance
ErgoHack VI launched on 2023-02-08 with an 18k SigmaUSD and 300k ErgoPad prize pool, running through February 22. Fifteen or more project submissions were recorded by mid-month, focused on cypherpunk philosophy and crypto first principles. Community fundraising included a matching donation offer from @qx() (50 ERG if raffle hit +50 ERG within one hour). Notable submitted projects included a mobile full-node implementation and web3 UX challenge work. The hackathon served as a visible rallying point for developer activity and community engagement throughout the month.

### DeFi Ecosystem Expansion
Multiple DeFi protocols either launched or reached significant milestones in February. DuckPools completed its ErgoPad IDO staker round (closed February 9–10) and entered public whitelisting. SigmaFi launched its peer-to-peer collateralized lending platform in public beta, with multi-asset collateral support and date-based liquidation; a future v2 with automated liquidation conditions based on custom parameters was announced. Machina Finance announced an order book-based DEX as a complement to AMM systems, enabling price transparency and arbitrage. Spectrum DEX began private yield farming testing in week four, with Round 2 launched immediately after. Oracle Pool v2 went into beta supporting Dexy Gold with GORT rewards, and the Dexy Protocol's arbitrage-based peg mechanism was extensively explained across the community.

### Speed Improvements and Protocol Optimization
A sustained discussion ran across all four weeks on potential Ergo throughput improvements. @kushti outlined several approaches: sub-blocks, sidechains, and faster confirmation protocols, noting that the existence of a concrete, well-understood contract set (Oracle Pools, Rosen Bridge, Spectrum, SigmaUSD, Trade House) now makes optimization tractable in a way it wasn't on empty chains. A 6.0 testnet was confirmed available for parameter tweaking, with @kushti inviting interested community members to DM for access. The discussion surfaced important dependencies: contracts relying on block height rather than timestamp would be affected by increased block speed, and emission contracts would tail off faster. No consensus on which approach to pursue was reached, leaving this as a key open question entering March.

### Merge-Mined Sidechains
@kushti outlined BIP-301-style improvements for trustless relay and sidechain data delivery, with mainchain miners rewarded for delivering sidechain data. The approach was described as more secure than Bitcoin's BIP-301 due to Ergo-specific constructions enabling improved trustless pegging via smart contracts. The potential for CPU/GPU miners to earn additional sidechain tokens while contributing mainchain block rewards was highlighted as a particularly compelling incentive structure. Sidechain construction documents were noted as forthcoming on the Ergo forum.

### Bulletproofs and Zero-Knowledge Proofs
@kushti explained that bulletproofs (compact Sigma protocols with no trusted setup) can be verified in ErgoScript, enabling range proofs and generic zero-knowledge statements without a trusted setup. This was contrasted with SNARKs, which require trusted setups. The ability to combine ErgoScript contracts with zero-knowledge statements was described as opening a "whole new world" of privacy-preserving contract possibilities, including verifiable credentials and sensitive-data hiding. @kushti noted that Groth one-out-of-many proofs (used in early Zcoin) are also expressible in this framework.

### Wallet Ecosystem Shifts
Yoroi's end-of-February sunset for Ergo support was a major practical concern, with community members guided to migrate to Nautilus, SafeW, Satergo, or Ergo Mobile Wallet. Minotaur Wallet released versions 1.1.2 and 1.1.3 during the month, fixing multisig bugs and updating BIP39 packages (requiring database deletion and wallet restoration for older versions). Ledger ERG support remained in security audit with no confirmed release date. EIP-0028 (ergoauth) was highlighted as enabling user verification via signed message without an on-chain transaction.

### Mining Decentralization and MAV
Community member @0077 raised a pointed concern: with approximately 183k P2PK wallets but only ~36 Autolykos v2 miners, the Minimum Attack Vector is roughly 19, representing a meaningful centralization risk via custodial pool dependency. A non-custodial pool-of-pools had previously shut down, worsening the picture. No on-chain expert provided a formal assessment. Profit-hopping miners moving to Conflux was also noted as creating additional sell pressure on ERG during the month.

### Order Book DEXes — Trade House and Auction House
@qx() provided an extended explanation of why order book DEXes are valuable: full price transparency, depth visibility, complete slippage control, and support for partial order fills. Trade House was highlighted as the Ergo implementation, with partial fills creating refund boxes containing leftover assets at the same parameters. The peer-to-peer nature means no single upgrade key controls the system. Sharing Spectrum liquidity pools with Trade House orders was raised as a future possibility.

### Palmyra / Chainlink Partnership
Dan from Palmyra explained their use of Chainlink's proof-of-reserve and oracle network for on-chain warehouse receipts supporting decentralized trade financing. IoT devices (weight sensors, cameras, motion sensors) would provide continuous proof-of-existence for commodities deposited in bonded warehouses, with receipts issued on-chain. The partnership was framed as not zero-sum with Ergo oracles; Dan indicated plans to use raised capital from the token sale to build out native Ergo Oracle Pools further. @Armeanio added that companies may use one or several aspects of the Ergo ecosystem without being all-in, and tribalistic reactions to partial adoption are counterproductive.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@eπ, 2023-02-01): How does Dexy work in simple terms?
  **A** (@qx()): Mint Dexy when oracle price > LP price; sell to the LP. Reverse: swap ERG for Dexy when LP < oracle, sell back when it catches up. No reserve token (unlike SigRSV). Arbitrage keeps the peg; no trusted setup required.

- **Q** (@Gabriel, 2023-02-01): Do SigRSV holders receive fees from SigUSD ↔ ERG conversions?
  **A** (@qx()): No; fees are added to the ERG reserve. SigRSV value depends entirely on reserve ratio changes — only profitable if you exit before others while the ratio has improved. SigRSV is effectively a claim on the SigUSD pile; more SigUSD minted = more SigRSV worth, but only if you exit before the ratio reverses.

- **Q** (@HelixEvo, 2023-02-01): What is the difference between DuckPools and SigmaFi?
  **A** (@qx()): DuckPools = pooled lending (all lenders deposit into a shared pool); SigmaFi = peer-to-peer (specific loan proposals matched between parties). DuckPools uses ratio-based liquidation; SigmaFi uses date-based liquidation. SigmaFi supports any token including NFTs as collateral; DuckPools requires ERG collateral.

- **Q** (@TMR.ΣRG, 2023-02-01): Why borrow stables against crypto instead of just swapping?
  **A** (@TMR.ΣRG, self-answered): Borrowing is not a taxable event; it keeps your ERG exposure while accessing USD liquidity. Leverage strategy: borrow stables, buy more ERG, profit on upside — while risking liquidation on downside.

- **Q** (@Luivatra, 2023-02-09): How do you verify a user without requiring an on-chain transaction?
  **A** (@Luivatra, self-answered via EIP): EIP-0028 (ergoauth) enables user verification via signed message without any on-chain transaction.

- **Q** (@Mh Mh, 2023-02-10): How will ERG fees work after all coins are mined post-2045?
  **A** (@Benny, @ThatOtherFella): Storage rent replaces block rewards as the long-term fee mechanism; miners collect rent from dormant boxes rather than relying solely on transaction fees.

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2023-02-10): If Ergo had Bitcoin's hashrate, would energy use be 1/3 or 1/10 of BTC?
  **A** (@Armeanio): Not an apples-to-apples comparison. GPU vs. ASIC efficiency differences make this question undefined — different mining algorithms produce fundamentally different hardware/power curves.

- **Q** (@eπ, 2023-02-18): Has any transaction been censored on Ethereum? Do high fees indicate network value?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Yes — MEV Watch documents extensive Ethereum transaction censoring. (@TMR.ΣRG): Supply and demand dictate fee price; more supply and tech improvements will reduce fees over time.

- **Q** (@gg, 2023-02-22): Are NIPoPoWs already enabled or a future feature?
  **A** (@HQΣr): NIPoPoWs are not currently enabled on mainnet. Mobile nodes are runnable now via Satergo without NIPoPoWs. Android/Termux setup is possible with a terminal emulator.

- **Q** (@Siavash, 2023-02-25): Is it dangerous to keep coins in Yoroi given the upcoming sunset?
  **A** (@qx()): If you have your seed phrase, restore to Nautilus, Ergo Wallet, or SafeW and verify the restore before removing the Yoroi plugin. If you are not 100% certain of your seed phrase, do not rush the migration.

- **Q** (@0077, 2023-02-19): With 183k P2PK wallets but only ~36 Autolykos v2 miners, does a MAV of ~19 create a meaningful attack vector?
  **A** (unresolved): Acknowledged as an important concern; no on-chain expert provided a formal assessment during the month.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2023-02-25): What is the practical difference between an order book DEX and an AMM?
  **A** (@qx()): Order books allow manual price setting, full transparency of depth and liquidity levels, and complete slippage control. AMMs use mathematical curves to adjust price dynamically based on pool composition. Order books enable explicit arbitrage when prices diverge between systems. Partial order fills in Trade House create refund boxes with leftover assets at the same parameters, maintaining the user's original execution conditions.

- **Q** (Community, AMA): Can you explain what bulletproofs are and how they apply to ErgoScript?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a form of compact Sigma protocol allowing zero-knowledge proofs of specific statements (e.g., range proofs) with no trusted setup — a trustless alternative to SNARKs. It is possible to verify bulletproofs within ErgoScript, meaning a whole range of zero-knowledge protocols (including Groth one-out-of-many, used in early Zcoin) can be combined with Ergo contracts. This enables privacy-preserving contracts where sensitive data is proven correct without being disclosed on-chain — a significant expansion of what hybrid contracts can do.

- **Q** (Community, AMA): How do merge-mined sidechains work on Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Mainchain miners are rewarded (via emission contracts) for delivering sidechain data to the mainchain. This is similar in concept to BIP-301 but with Ergo-specific constructions enabling more secure trustless pegging via smart contracts. Miners can CPU/GPU mine sidechain blocks and earn sidechain tokens while simultaneously contributing to mainchain block rewards — a compelling dual-incentive structure.

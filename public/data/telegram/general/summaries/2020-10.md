# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: October 2020

---

## Executive Summary

October 2020 was a landmark month for Ergo, marked by simultaneous progress across exchange listings, wallet infrastructure, dApp launches, and cross-chain partnerships. The Gate.io listing (reaching 44+ million community votes), Yoroi browser extension launch, ErgoMixer v3.0.0, and the debut of the Ergo Auction House collectively established Ergo's first functional dApp ecosystem. Underlying all of this was sustained technical development: node releases (v3.3.4 and v3.3.5), stablecoin research, Oracle Pool v2 deployment, and early-stage sidechain and zero-knowledge proof explorations.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Gate.io Listing & Exchange Strategy
The month's single most impactful community event was the Gate.io listing, achieved through a voting campaign that reached 44+ million votes against a 10 million target. ERG/USDT, ERG/BTC, and ERG/ETH pairs went live on October 10th. The Ergo Foundation sponsored a 100k ERG pool (50k for airdrop/rewards at $0.40, remainder for market making) but did not pay listing fees — Gate.io approached the EF after sustained community pressure. Gate.io was ranked #4 on CoinGecko and #8 on BTI, making it Ergo's highest-quality listing to date. A secondary free listing on BTCNEXT (ERG/BTC) was announced October 20th. Community discussion flagged Citex as a scam exchange with manipulated volume, leading to its removal from ergoplatform.org. Binance, Kraken, KuCoin, and Huobi were all discussed as targets, but listing fees and absence of market makers remained blockers. CoinGecko was established as the preferred data source over CoinMarketCap due to accurate circulating supply figures.

### Yoroi Wallet Integration & Desktop Wallet Launch
Yoroi browser extension support for ERG launched October 8th following successful nightly testing, representing the first mainstream wallet supporting Ergo tokens. The integration confirmed BIP44 derivation compatibility with node v3.3.4+ (path: m/44'/429'/0'/0/0), resolving longstanding confusion around wallet interoperability. CoinBarn and ErgoWallet (launched October 23rd at ergowallet.io) are also BIP44-compatible, while Magnum wallet uses a non-standard derivation path and cannot be migrated. Mobile Yoroi and Ledger support remained in progress throughout the month. ErgoWallet's Windows build had input field bugs under investigation; macOS binaries arrived via Google Drive by October 24th. Toward month-end, work began on a TX Assembler Service and EIP-23 (Yoroi dApp connector standard) to enable MetaMask-style dApp interaction without requiring users to run a full node.

### Ergo Auction House (First dApp)
The Ergo Auction House launched October 16th, built by anon_real as the first production implementation of auction contracts designed on the Ergo Forum in October 2019. It introduced a 2% fee model tied to NFT business tokens, where the coordinator retains the right to sell the business via NFT. The first auction — a vanity token ID collectible — opened at 555 ERG and closed above 1,027 ERG by late October. The platform initially required a running full node; Yoroi integration was planned within months. A self-hosted GitHub version was recommended over the HTTP-hosted version for security. Auto-extending auction duration was added as a feature update during the month.

### Node Releases: v3.3.4 and v3.3.5
Node v3.3.4 shipped October 5th with EIP-3 support, an updated panel, macOS crash fixes, memory leak improvements, and p2p efficiency gains. Node v3.3.5 followed October 23rd (PR #1225) with distributed signatures API, mempool improvements (double-spend filtering, fee/byte sorting, tuned mempool pool retention), and transaction handling fixes that resolved persistent issues at CoinEx and Magnum. The fee estimation API was noted as present but untested by applications. kushti invited community members to test it and flagged it as an area needing attention. Minimum propagation fee rate configuration was highlighted as a node operator tool for spam mitigation.

### ErgoMixer v3.0.0
ErgoMixer v3.0.0 shipped around October 6th with covert addresses, Tor support, multiple node request distribution, backup/restore, data pruning, UI/UX improvements, backward compatibility, and a security bug fix (described as never exploited). Over 110,000 ERG had been mixed cumulatively across v2 and v3 by this point. Community discussed renaming the project to "Erebus" (Greek god of darkness/shadow) or "Harpocrates" (god of silence/secrets) to better distinguish it as a dark pool rather than a traditional one-shot mixer. kushti clarified ErgoMixer's legal status: as a non-custodial, non-coordinated local UI where users interact directly with the blockchain protocol, FinCEN's ruling on mixers (targeting custodial/coordinated services) does not apply.

### Stablecoin Development
The month clarified the stablecoin design: an ERG-collateralized algorithmic stablecoin based on a lesser-known research paper with novel additions to fix protocol holes. It is described as simpler than MakerDAO and less vulnerable to blockchain congestion. The dependency chain for release runs: Ergo Connector → Sigma Rust library → Oracle connection → Yoroi integration → stablecoin UI release. Emurgo/Yoroi was confirmed as the integration partner. Minting via ERG collateral and a Yoroi-embedded UI were planned. kushti mentioned seeking "partners/whales" to seed initial reserves but did not name them. ERG holders discussed whether a stablecoin airdrop or reward token was planned; details were deferred.

### Oracle Pools
Oracle Pool v1 remained operational with Ergo Foundation members and external participants. Oracle Pool v2 was running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards. kushti discussed plans to allocate capital from the Palmyra token sale to build out native Ergo oracles, noting the current ecosystem had only two Oracle Pool instances (one run by the EF). The Chainlink partnership (for Palmyra's decentralized trade financing with on-chain warehouse receipts and IoT-based proof of reserve) was announced as a complementary — not competing — choice, with Chainlink selected for network size and support breadth rather than exclusivity.

### Waves/Gravity Partnership
The first non-Cardano interoperability partnership was announced in the final week of October: Ergo integrating with Waves' Gravity network for cross-chain DeFi and decentralized oracles. Gravity functions as a multi-chain gateway framework enabling cross-chain messaging and value transfer. The announcement was framed as the beginning of a broader interoperability strategy beyond the Cardano relationship.

### DEX Development
DEX development was active throughout the month but UI/UX remained the primary blocker. kushti identified the UI as "the biggest problem" and noted that a UTXO model makes Uniswap-style AMM replication difficult due to the challenge of sharing global price/reserve state between UTXOs. An order-book-based DEX was considered more natural for Ergo's eUTXO architecture, though AMM and hybrid models were not ruled out. The Ergo Auction House was positioned as a proof-of-concept for the same contract architecture that the DEX will build on. A dedicated DEX team was formed after the Auction House launch. The Trade House concept (order-book DEX) was discussed favourably, with partial order fills, peer-to-peer trading, and multiple upgrade keys highlighted as advantages over single-admin contract models.

### Hard Fork Planning
A minimal hard fork was confirmed throughout October, containing: (1) a PoW memory-hardness fix to restore ASIC resistance and home mining viability, (2) security fixes, and (3) soft-fork support for contractual layer evolution. No token swaps or new issuance were planned. Full node operators must update; the change would be transparent to end-users. kushti described this as potentially "the second and maybe last chance to mine Ergo at home." Technical write-up and video were assigned but not completed during the month; kushti planned to prepare materials after returning from New York.

### Sidechain & Merge Mining Research
kushti discussed theoretical sidechain constructions improving on Bitcoin's BIP 301. The proposed model uses trustless relay mechanisms and mainchain emission contracts to reward mainchain miners for delivering sidechain data, enabling merge-mined sidechains where miners earn both ERG and sidechain tokens. Ergo-specific constructions were described as potentially offering cleaner security guarantees than BIP 301's "less clear security." No committed timeline or testnet was announced; publication of sidechain construction details on the Ergo Forum was flagged as forthcoming.

### Zero-Knowledge Proof Integration
kushti explored bulletproof verification within ErgoScript as a trustless alternative to SNARKs (no trusted setup required). Bulletproofs were described as a specific form of Sigma protocol enabling range proofs (as used in Monero) and, importantly, generic zero-knowledge statements. Verification done in ErgoScript would open the door to verifiable credentials, hybrid on-chain/off-chain contracts, and complex zero-knowledge applications where sensitive data is proven without disclosure. The Groth one-out-of-many proof (used in early Zcoin) was cited as a comparable approach already used in some projects. Research was ongoing with no completion date stated.

### Spam & Mempool Dynamics
The mempool fee market, spam resistance, and DDoS comparisons to Bitcoin were discussed in detail. Node v4.0.44+ has limits preventing nodes from downloading too many transactions from a single peer. Node operators can raise minimum propagation fee rates to block spam from reaching their mempool. Fee estimation API exists in the node but was noted as untested. Transactions with fees below the prevailing market rate are eventually flushed from the mempool after sufficient block confirmations pass without pickup. kushti noted that during Bitcoin's block size war, similar spam was resolved naturally by fee markets within weeks.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-10-01): Getting "ERROR: Some of expected fields not present in /block/candidate" on GPU miner setup.
  **A** (@kushti): Related to Autolykos GPU miner issue #66; use commit 7b0d424 before the latest PR with a specific git reset command.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-10-03): Why does CoinBarn→Yoroi work but Yoroi→CoinBarn fail for wallet restoration?
  **A** (@Sebastien): CoinBarn is a single-address wallet; Yoroi is an HD wallet generating multiple addresses per BIP44. Once Yoroi generates multiple addresses, CoinBarn cannot recover funds from addresses beyond the first.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-10-09): What happens to spammers' transactions in the mempool if they're not picked up?
  **A** (@kushti): Miners sort by fee per transaction byte. Low-fee transactions remain in the mempool until eventually picked up when blocks are not full, or until they time out. Node operators can raise minimum propagation fee rates to prevent spam from reaching the mempool at all. A fee estimation API exists in the node but is currently untested by applications.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-10-09): Can the same DDoS attack that happened to Bitcoin happen to Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo does not yet have a mature fee market like Bitcoin. Users can raise fees slightly above the spammer to get prioritised. Node v4.0.44+ limits transactions downloaded from a single peer. Mempool filters are available to node operators. During spam events, simply increasing the transaction fee slider in Nautilus (or other wallets) above the spam floor is sufficient to get into the next block.

- **Q** (@Samoan Kipto, 2020-W41): Why would developers use Ergo for oracles rather than building natively on Cardano with Goguen?
  **A** (community): Goguen (smart contracts on Cardano) was not yet implemented — pipeline target was Q1 2021. Oracle Pools are an off-chain solution validated by the eUTXO model; Cardano would need to rebuild the same stack. Ergo had approximately a one-year advantage in production-ready UTXO-based smart contracts.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-W41): Can you explain what bulletproofs are?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge technique allowing specific statements to be proven without revealing underlying data. Monero uses them for range proofs. They can also prove generic statements, serving as a trustless alternative to SNARKs — with no trusted setup required. In Ergo's context, they are essentially a specific form of Sigma protocol. If bulletproof verification can be done in ErgoScript, this opens a wide variety of zero-knowledge protocols alongside Ergo contracts: verifiable credentials, hybrid contracts where sensitive data is proven without disclosure, and much more.

- **Q** (@mensrea, 2020-10-15): Why can't Ergo replicate Uniswap-style AMM?
  **A** (@kushti): The eUTXO model makes it difficult to share global state (current price and reserve levels) across UTXOs simultaneously. Workarounds exist but introduce UX trade-offs. Ergo can pioneer novel UTXO-native DEX designs that achieve equivalent goals without forcing the account-model paradigm onto an incompatible architecture.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-10-15): What stablecoin category is Ergo building?
  **A** (@kushti): An ERG-collateralized algorithmic stablecoin, based on a lesser-known research paper with novel improvements to fix protocol holes. Simpler than MakerDAO and less vulnerable to blockchain congestion. Details to be released by the stablecoin team.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-W42): Can the Auction House serve as a trustless OTC venue for miners selling large ERG blocks?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes, once the stablecoin is delivered it becomes very interesting as a trustless OTC mechanism. If the stablecoin ships before the DEX, this use case is immediately viable.

- **Q** (@Rodrigo, 2020-W43): What makes Ergo's eUTXO model unique versus Cardano, Polkadot, and similar chains?
  **A** (community): The combination of eUTXO with non-interactive zero-knowledge proofs via Sigma Protocols, data inputs in the eUTXO model, and built-in atomic exchange for custom tokens is not replicated anywhere else. Replicating it would require cloning the entire Ergo stack. Many future projects may adopt eUTXO alone, some may add Sigma Protocols, but the full combination remains rare.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2020-W43): Can I restore a Magnum wallet mnemonic in ErgoWallet or Yoroi?
  **A** (@kushti): No. Magnum uses a non-standard BIP derivation path. ErgoWallet, Yoroi, CoinBarn (for single-address), and node v3.3.4+ all use BIP44 and are mutually compatible. Create a new mnemonic in a BIP44-compatible wallet and transfer funds.

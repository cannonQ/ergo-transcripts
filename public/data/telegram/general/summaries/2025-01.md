# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: January 2025

---

## Executive Summary

January 2025 was defined by two converging themes: the first block reward emission reduction (from 21 ERG to 18 ERG) marking a pivotal monetary policy milestone, and an intensive burst of core protocol development across sub-blocks, Ergo 6.0, and hardware wallet integrations. The month concluded with kushti publishing a strategic forum post framing Ergo as the "endgame for crypto" — a PoW commodity layer — while the ecosystem simultaneously matured through Dexy contract audits, Ledger integration passing audit, and the MewFinance trading platform going live.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Block Reward Emission Reduction & Monetary Policy
The first scheduled emission reduction of 2025 — from 21 ERG to 18 ERG at block 1,432,800 — occurred on approximately January 4th and became a community celebration event. CannonQ published the full 2025 reduction schedule (18→15→12→9→6 ERG across five reductions), and the community organized a Twitter Spaces watch party with WalrusDAO. Alongside the emission milestone, storage rent (demurrage) gained renewed attention as the mechanism that sustains miner revenue as emissions decline. A community townhall was announced for February 4th specifically on "Monetary aspects of the Ergo protocol: algorithmic monetary policies, demurrage, custom emission contracts." kushti argued that "demurrage" better captures the economic concept than "storage rent," noting the terminology traces back to Ethereum community discussions from 2015. The Sigmanauts Mining Pool distributed a PHP meme token (1B supply, 80% to LP, 15% to mining pool bonus) recovered entirely via demurrage — described as the first-ever demurrage-recovered meme token — relaunching it as a demonstration of the mechanism's real-world utility.

### Sub-blocks EIP & Protocol Performance
Sub-blocks emerged as the dominant technical theme of the month. kushti published a first EIP draft (inspired by Prism, parallel PoW, and Tailstorm designs), added the best sub-block ID to the node's `/info` endpoint, and completed a rough local miner-based devnet implementation by month's end. The performance case was made concretely by c8: current SigmaUSD minting failures occur within a ~4-minute window due to mempool competition; sub-blocks reduce reliable confirmation to ~2 seconds — a cited 17,900% UX improvement. Sub-blocks were repeatedly identified as foundational infrastructure blocking other improvements: CoinGecko listing fixes, mempool subscription APIs, and cooperative (non-PvP) mempool behavior all depend on their deployment. Volunteers were sought to spin up testnets and conduct "scientific observations" comparing block time and difficulty configurations. A related debate emerged around contracts relying on `HEIGHT` vs. timestamp — faster block times affect emission contracts and storage rent clock calculations, requiring careful parameter analysis before mainnet deployment.

### Ergo 6.0 Soft-Fork
The 6.0 soft-fork progressed from audit to near-testnet-ready status across the month. All audit-identified issues were fixed, a community raffle was launched to raise ERG for audit costs, and by month's end kushti confirmed "hopefully 6.0 RC for public testnet will be released today" (February 1st). The Sigma Rust implementation and extra indexes work (to simplify oracle and Rosen Watcher operations) were identified as parallel workstreams. Public testnet activation was described as taking ~4 days once the release candidate shipped. The soft-fork was framed as enabling the performance and contract improvements that underpin the 2025 roadmap.

### Hardware Wallet Integrations (Keystone & Ledger)
Two hardware wallet integrations advanced significantly. The Keystone integration reached the point of transaction parsing and signing with QR-based display; the first Keystone SDK Rust PR was submitted and awaited Keystone team review, with Web SDK, Web USB SDK, and Nautilus/Satergo UI integration remaining. Ledger Live integration passed its security audit with one low-level issue fixed; the auditor was reviewing changes before issuing the final report. Both integrations were described as contingent on available reviewer bandwidth at the respective hardware wallet teams. Keystone was noted as "mostly done on firmware"; completion timeline was characterized as "another 3 months" worst-case, acceleratable via MewFinance purchase funding.

### Rosen Bridge Growth & Cross-Chain Strategy
Rosen Bridge surpassed Gate.io in ERG holdings (4,331,552 ERG vs. 4,324,641 ERG) — a milestone demonstrating significant liquidity concentration in bridge infrastructure. Binance Smart Chain integration entered final testing; Doge-related changes completed final review; refactored packages for Ergo multisig and transaction agreement were integrated. kushti outlined a long-term vision: Rosen as a trustless relay enabling fiat→Monero flows — a user selects an amount, pays fiat via an escrow mechanism (Bisq-like), and receives native XMR via Rosen unwrapping. The "rs" prefix convention was clarified: rsERG sent to the Ergo chain unwraps to native ERG; rsADA sent to Cardano unwraps to native ADA. A community proposal ("ErgRX") for a trusted Rosen-based cross-chain swap service was floated by Chris Ray but remained in discussion phase.

### Dexy Protocol & Oracle Pools
The Dexy contract audit completed with the full report published to Proton Drive. Oracle Pool v2 was confirmed operational (running for Dexy Gold with GORT reward distribution); Oracle Pool v1 continues under EF member management. Dan/Armeanio described Palmyra's Chainlink integration for proof-of-reserve and on-chain warehouse receipts for decentralized trade financing, while clarifying this was not exclusionary of Ergo's native oracles — capital from Palmyra's token sale was earmarked to build out Ergo Oracle Pool infrastructure. Silver datafeed support was added to the oracle-core PR. A "v2 in two weeks" timeline was mentioned for the Oracle Pool v2 public channel, pending external partner integration.

### MewFinance Ecosystem Maturation
MewFinance expanded significantly across the month: a buy/sell offers interface, MewBoxes (smart contract asset organization), MewMart escrow capabilities, and a live trading platform at trading.mewfinance.com launched. The Asset Trade Volume Challenge went live with FAKU token testing and a real-time leaderboard. A strategic development fund presale progressed to 87%+ of its 1,000 ERG target. c8's ergfi.xyz (direct SigmaUSD interaction bypassing proxy contracts) went live, offering 1:1 values with no proxy markup, and work on a Dexy UI was announced. The community debated proxy contract vs. smart wallet architecture — c8 explained proxy contracts serve "dumb" mobile wallets, while smart wallets (capable of multisig and direct contract interaction) will eventually eliminate the need.

### 2025 Strategic Vision
kushti's January 2025 roadmap encompassed: most performant real P2P network (sub-blocks, Tailstorm), multi-stablecoin ecosystem (Gluon/Dexy/Djed in gold/lithium/silver/USD variants), secure cross-chain bridges (Rosen), p2p DEX for fiat/BTC (Bisq-like), decentralized PoW mining (Lithos), off-chain stablecoin payment layers, and p2p digital banking. A forum post titled "Ergo as the Endgame for Crypto" framed Ergo as a "Proof-of-Work commodity layer" and positioned the DeFi protocol suite as "1000x bigger than oracle equivalents." Index funds (anon-real, kushti) and prediction pools (Destiny Protocol) were announced as new ecosystem additions. A recurring tension emerged between building technical foundations vs. narrative construction — kushti argued narratives are needed; Armeanio and qx() emphasized that quality and resilience generate organic themes.

### AI & Developer Tooling
Glasgow trained and deployed an LLM on ErgoScript available at ergoplatform.com (trained on developer support and ErgoScript chats). Marc the Shark published an ErgoScript data collection repository for LLM fine-tuning. kushti noted that AI-assisted code generation is "extremely beneficial to Ergo" given the density of open-sourced financial product solutions. The Ergo Bounties board (github.com/ErgoDevs/Ergo-Bounties) launched with 100 open bounties totaling 27,512.25 ERG (largest category: Scala at 23,891.90 ERG). Fleet SDK v0.8.2 was released. Minotaur v2.4.0 shipped with token detail display and animated QR codes.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Edvin, 2025-01-06): SigmaRSV redeemed but no ERG received after 30 minutes; no transaction visible.
  **A** (@Glasgow, @cafebedouin.org): Likely stuck in proxy address. Use tokenjay.app or select "refund" via sigmausd.io, then retry with dApp connector or ErgoPayment.

- **Q** (@Alex, 2025-01-08): Why "storage rent" instead of "chain fee" — feels like the wallet doesn't belong to you?
  **A** (@CannonQ, @kushti): Consolidating once per 4 years avoids the fee entirely — sustainable for a public ledger. The term originated in Ethereum community discussions since 2015 for UTXO/state growth management. "Demurrage" may better capture the economic concept.

- **Q** (AMA context, 2025-01-09): What are bulletproofs and how do they relate to ErgoScript?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are compact Sigma protocols for zero-knowledge proofs without a trusted setup, enabling range proofs and custom statements. The goal is verification done in ErgoScript — enabling hybrid contracts where sensitive data is proven without disclosure. Groth one-out-of-many proofs (used in early Zcoin) follow similar patterns. This opens a variety of zero-knowledge protocols usable alongside Ergo contracts.

- **Q** (@Alex, 2025-01-16): What does "direct interaction" mean in the context of SigmaUSD?
  **A** (@c8): Direct interaction executes the Algo Stable Contract directly — you send ERG and receive SigmaUSD back without a proxy middleman. Values shown are 1:1 with what you receive. Proxy contracts exist to serve "dumb" mobile wallets that cannot parse smart contracts; smart wallets will eventually eliminate the need.

- **Q** (@Chris Ray, 2025-01-13): Is there a smart contract for timed payment at a specific block height?
  **A** (@Patato): Yes — sample ErgoScript using `HEIGHT >= unlockHeight` check with recipient `proveDlog` provided.

- **Q** (@Alex, 2025-01-25, ELI5 sub-blocks): How do sub-blocks work?
  **A** (@c8): Today transactions wait ~2 minutes on average before block confirmation. Replace-By-Fee allows fee increases or cancellations, causing SigmaUSD mint failures when users compete for the same box. Sub-blocks reduce average reliable confirmation to ~2 seconds — a 17,900% UX improvement over the current 4+ minute failure window.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2025-01-27): Do dApps need to be adapted to use sub-blocks?
  **A** (@Flying Pig, self-resolved): Only dApps with box competition need adaptation. "If you're sending a tx where there's no competition for a box, the current mempool works fine."

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2025-01-28): Is there a solution for Bitcoin UTXO consolidation comparable to Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): With storage rent in Ergo, miners can collect dust; normal users cannot. This creates a clean asymmetric dust-collection mechanism absent in Bitcoin.

- **Q** (@RETRO, 2025-01-29): Unable to transfer 48 ERG — receiving "too many boxes" error.
  **A** (@CannonQ): Click "dApps" inside the mobile wallet → "Box Consolidation" → select the main wallet. Cost is 0.001 ERG. Alternatively, try sending in two parts.

- **Q** (@Man ish, 2025-01-27): After sub-blocks, will token swaps from a DEX be received immediately?
  **A** (@c8): Yes, in the average on-chain case. For off-chain settlement of larger amounts, a few block confirmations are still advisable.

- **Q** (@Archer, 2025-01-30): When bridging with rsERG, do you then need to swap rsERG for ERG?
  **A** (@Affable Villain): "rs" is the wrapped version. Sending to the original chain unwraps it — send rsERG to Ergo to receive native ERG; send rsADA to Cardano to receive native ADA.

- **Q** (@Flying Pig, 2025-01-25): Why should dApps provide open-source or locally runnable UIs?
  **A** (@Flying Pig, self-stated): If a main website (e.g., DuckPools) goes down, borrowers cannot repay loans and face forced liquidation. An open UI — even unglamorous — lets users interact with contracts locally during emergencies. Spectrum and ergfi.xyz provide good precedents.

## Open Questions Entering Next Month

- **Sub-blocks testnet parameters**: Which block time and difficulty configurations are safe? Contracts dependent on `HEIGHT` vs. timestamp need auditing before any block time reduction. Volunteers sought to run testnets and publish scientific observations.
- **Block time reduction decision**: No formal decision on whether to reduce block time alongside sub-blocks deployment. Security implications, emission contract effects, and storage rent clock interactions remain unresolved.
- **Dexy Protocol mainnet launch date**: Contracts audited and UI underway, but no specific mainnet deployment date confirmed.
- **Keystone integration completion**: Keystone team reviewing PRs with limited bandwidth; Web SDK, Web USB SDK, and Nautilus UI integration still pending. Worst-case estimate: 3 more months.
- **Native Ergo Oracle Pool funding and expansion**: Palmyra token-sale capital earmarked for Oracle Pool build-out, but amount and timeline unspecified. Currently only 2 active oracle pool instances.
- **Palmyra/ErgoDevs 2025 alignment**: kushti described ecosystem coordination (ErgoDevs, Palmyra, anon-real, Oracle Pools) as "messy" with no resolution pathway stated.
- **Narrative vs. foundations debate**: Ongoing tension between proactive narrative building (kushti) and organic resilience (Armeanio, qx()) with no community consensus.
- **US exchange access**: KuCoin and Gate.io both blocking US users; Kraken application resubmitted but outcome pending. Xeggex available but low liquidity.
- **DApp UI accessibility standard**: Community feedback on need for local/offline fallback UIs acknowledged by developers but no formal standard or adoption policy proposed.
- **LCX listing terms**: Fee structure (listing vs. market making vs. marketing) and impact on adoption remain unclear.
- **Mobile "super dApp" for TradFi integration**: Alex's framing of the need for a Revolut-equivalent acknowledged by c8 with legal/process challenges cited as unresolved blockers.
- **Solana bridge via Rosen**: Technically feasible, confirmed possible by community, but no active development or partnership announced.

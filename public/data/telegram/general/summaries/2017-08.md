# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Month:** August 2017
**Chat:** Ergo General

---

## Executive Summary

August 2017 was the founding month of the Ergo General Telegram community, with the project in its pre-testnet phase and core team activity focused on establishing technical and legal foundations. Dominant themes were the upcoming September testnet launch, Ergo's academic research presence at international conferences, and community onboarding around basic project positioning. Activity was light but substantive, with kushti and Dmitry directly answering technical questions from early community members.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Testnet Development & Timeline
Four developers were actively working toward a September 2017 testnet release, confirmed as the team's highest priority. The economy model was still under construction and explicitly deferred from the first testnet release. No mainnet date was discussed.

### Ergo Foundation & Legal Structure
The team was actively working on a legal framework for the Ergo Foundation, with Dmitry traveling to a Swiss assembly to discuss implementation. This represented early groundwork for the project's organisational structure.

### Academic Research & Conference Presentations
The team was presenting Ergo research at two conferences: a difficulty recalculation algorithm at a blockchain workshop in Oslo, and the economy model at the CESC conference in Berkeley. A small Ergo Meetup was floated as a possibility if community members attended either event.

### Technical Architecture & Positioning
Key clarifications were made throughout the month: Ergo uses Scorex as a library (not a fork), all ERG must be mined with no ICO planned, and ring signatures are implemented as a native language feature — making them cheaper and more space-efficient than Monero's scheme, without requiring special transaction construction. Blockchain bloat was discussed in the context of Rollerchain vs. proof-of-stake approaches, with the conclusion that PoS cannot solve the bloat problem because full transaction history is required to recover current balances.

### Community & Scam Alert
A BitcoinTalk thread impersonating Ergo was identified as a scam. The team designated Twitter as the sole official channel for announcements. The community was small but engaged, with discussion also ongoing in the Waves Slack `#ergo` channel. A live Q&A with kushti and the core team was conducted in Ergo Slack on 2017-09-21, with a transcript published publicly.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Noah, 2017-08-17): How does Ergo relate to Scorex — is it considered an offshoot?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Ergo uses Scorex as a library.

- **Q** (@Noah, 2017-08-17): How do Ergo and Rollerchain compare with PoS for addressing blockchain bloat and security?
  **A** (@Dmitry): Rollerchain is not compatible with PoS. The blockchain bloat problem is not solvable in PoS because you need all transactions to recover current balances.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2017-08-26): Ring signatures are described as "free" — does that mean Monero-level confidentiality without blockchain bloat?
  **A** (@Dmitry): "Free" means ring signatures are included in the transactional language by design — no special transaction construction needed. Monero uses inefficient schemes; Ergo's ring signatures take less space.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2017-08-17): Is Nimiq a competitor to Ergo, given it offers browser-based mining with a betanet already live?
  **A** (@Dmitry): The lack of technical documentation for Nimiq makes it hard to assess whether their promises are feasible.

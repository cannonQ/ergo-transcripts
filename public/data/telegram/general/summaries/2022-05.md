# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary
**Chat:** Ergo General | **Month:** 2022-05 | **Weeks Covered:** W18–W21

---

## Executive Summary

May 2022 was dominated by two major governance and protocol milestones: the lock-in of EIP-27's emission reduction schedule and the activation of Ledger hardware wallet support in Nautilus. The Terra Luna/UST collapse triggered extensive community discussion contrasting SigmaUSD's overcollateralized design against failed algorithmic stablecoin models, reinforcing Ergo's DeFi credibility during a turbulent market period. Development momentum continued with ErgoHack Fest (the largest cohort yet at 18 teams), Palmyra's public debut, and multiple DeFi primitives advancing toward production.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### EIP-27 Emission Schedule Activation
EIP-27 was locked in and will activate at block #777,217–777,272 (approximately June 21, 2022), reducing block rewards by 12–20 ERG per block while extending Ergo's emission schedule by ~17.38 years. The remainder of the supply will be transferred to a transparent on-chain reemission smart contract rather than being destroyed. This soft fork is processed entirely through miner governance (~90% hashrate support required), and the community framed it as a governance success story — particularly notable given it was executed during a bear market. Community clarified that the ~42M ERG held in the reemission contract will not count as circulating supply.

### SigmaUSD vs. UST — Stablecoin Design Debate
The Terra Luna/UST collapse in mid-May triggered the month's most sustained technical discussion. Community members systematically contrasted SigmaUSD's overcollateralized design (400–800% reserve ratio, fully transparent on-chain reserves, forced minting limits) against UST's opaque seigniorage-shares model. SigmaUSD maintained ~250% reserve ratio during the crash, and redemption mechanics — including the refund button for failed transactions — were widely explained to newcomers. kushti also outlined a new analytical solution for the Dexy Protocol linking bank reserves directly to liquidity pools with transparent reserve ratios, without requiring a separate reserve instrument like SigRSV. The naming lineage was clarified: AgeUSD is the base protocol; SigmaUSD is the minimal Djed implementation in ErgoScript; Djed and Extended-Djed are the broader IOG specifications.

### ErgoHack Fest — DeFi Marathon
ErgoHack Fest was announced as Ergo's largest hackathon yet, with 18 participating teams and co-sponsorship from ErgoPad (the first external co-sponsor). Target areas included Djed protocol implementations, Oracle Pools v2, Dexy, YieldDAO, stealth address mixers, LETS, p2p tooling, and developer libraries. A recurring theme was the gap between ErgoScript contract knowledge and dApp frontend integration: wallet connector libraries, React components for Nautilus/SafeW integration, and transaction builder tutorials were all identified as high-priority missing tooling. Frontend completeness (UI + backend) was explicitly weighted equally to smart contract work in judging criteria.

### Ledger Hardware Wallet Integration
Ledger Nano S support was finalized and made side-loadable via Nautilus wallet on Linux/macOS by end of May. A p2s bug was fixed during beta testing. Ledger Nano X support was submitted for Ledger's app store approval, expected within a few weeks. This represented a significant UX milestone for the ecosystem, closing a major hardware wallet gap.

### Palmyra / ZenGate Public Debut
Dan Friedman publicly presented the Palmyra initiative for decentralized commodity trade financing, targeting Sri Lanka and Eastern Africa. The system uses Chainlink oracles coupled with IoT devices (weight sensors, cameras, motion sensors depending on commodity) to issue on-chain warehouse receipts as collateral for trade financing. Oracle Pool v2 is already running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards. Dan clarified the Chainlink choice was not exclusionary — the plan includes investing capital raised through token sales to build out native Ergo oracles with improved security and tooling. The community was reminded not to be tribalistic about multi-chain tool choices: Palmyra is currently Ergo-exclusive but designed to utilize whichever infrastructure best fits each use case.

### Protocol v5.0 and Performance Improvements
kushti outlined upcoming v5.0 features: AOT/JIT costing for aggressive complexity estimation in multi-branch contracts, UTXO set snapshot bootstrapping, and — once those are stable — potential complexity limit increases. Transaction sorting by ERG/complexity ratio was discussed as a mitigation for contract-heavy transactions (e.g., ErgoPad staking) crowding out regular transactions. Broader speed improvements (microblocks, sidechains, layer 2) were deferred pending concrete application profiles, with kushti noting that empty parallel chains are wasteful and optimization is easier with a known transaction mix.

### Merge-Mined Sidechains
kushti discussed Ergo-specific sidechain constructions enabling trustless pegging via smart contracts, with emission contracts rewarding mainchain miners for delivering sidechain data — drawing on BIP 301 research but with improvements possible in the Ergo context. Work was still being published to the forum at month's end. Security properties were noted as clearer than Bitcoin's BIP 301 approach. GPU miner migration from Ethereum's anticipated PoS merge (expected August 2022) was discussed as a potential tailwind, though no concrete marketing strategy was finalized.

### NIPoPoWs and Zero-Knowledge Proofs
kushti proposed bulletproofs verification within ErgoScript as a trustless alternative to SNARKs — no trusted setup required. Applications include range proofs (as in Monero) and generic zero-knowledge statements. This enables hybrid contracts where execution happens on-chain but sensitive data is kept off-chain, verified only via proof. Groth one-out-of-many proofs (used in early Zcoin) and Sigma protocols were identified as compatible. Verifiable credentials were mentioned as a near-term application area.

### Wallet Ecosystem — Yoroi Deprecation and Fragmentation
Yoroi wallet was effectively abandoned for Ergo by Emurgo during this period, with widespread sync failures and no official EOL statement. Community consensus coalesced around Nautilus (browser extension), SafeW, Ergo Mobile Wallet (core team audited), and Minotaur as alternatives. A practical UX issue emerged for users with fragmented UTXO sets (250+ boxes): cold wallet signing fails with "cost exceeds limit 1,000,000" above ~12 input boxes, requiring consolidation first. MrStahlfelge committed to adding pre-signing error warnings and exploring automatic consolidation.

### RealFi Positioning and Marketing Gap
A recurring theme across the month was Ergo's "RealFi" positioning — DeFi designed for real-world financial inclusion rather than speculation — as an underexplored competitive differentiator. The ErgoLend (Exle) project received its first real loan proposal (from Kenya) during W21. LETS (Local Exchange Trading Systems) was discussed both as an ErgoHack topic and as a community experiment in Discord. Community acknowledged tension between the grassroots ethos (no aggressive shilling) and the visibility needed for mainstream developer and user adoption.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@K Chan, 2022-05-16): What is the TPS for Ergo?
  **A** (@Pulsarz): TPS is a misleading metric for UTXO-based systems — a single Ergo transaction can output to thousands of addresses simultaneously, so raw TPS numbers don't capture actual throughput capacity.

- **Q** (@HelixEvo, 2022-05-02): How does storage rent processing work? Does a miner identify old boxes, open them, take a fee, and shift to a new box?
  **A** (community): Miners can process old boxes after ~4 years and collect storage rent fees (~0.001 ERG minimum), either spending the box or allowing it to be reclaimed.

- **Q** (@Man ish, 2022-05-12): Can SigmaUSD face a Luna/UST-style collapse?
  **A** (@Ile): No. SigmaUSD maintains fully transparent on-chain collateral at 400–800% reserve ratio. If reserves fall to 80%, 1 SigmaUSD = $0.80 — but that visibility incentivizes early redemption, restoring the reserve before collapse. UST had no such transparency or enforcement mechanism.

- **Q** (@miwal, 2022-05-14): Does SigmaUSD use seigniorage like UST/Luna?
  **A** (@Glasgow): No. SigmaUSD does not use seigniorage. The protocol lineage is: AgeUSD → SigmaUSD → SigmaUSD v2 → Djed & Extended-Djed. SigmaUSD is overcollateralized crypto-backed, not algorithmic seigniorage.

- **Q** (@LPTakerK, 2022-05-20): I tried to redeem SigRSV for ERG but the transaction didn't go through — can I get my SigRSV back?
  **A** (@TMR.ΣRG): You cannot redeem SigRSV while the reserve ratio is below 400%. Use the refund button on the SigmaUSD interface for failed transactions.

- **Q** (@Marty, 2022-05-12): What scaling plans exist to prevent network slowdown from heavy contract usage like ErgoPad?
  **A** (@kushti): Protocol v5.0 will include AOT/JIT costing for accurate complexity estimation, UTXO set snapshot bootstrapping, and then complexity limit increases become feasible. Transaction sorting by ERG/complexity ratio also helps prevent contract-heavy transactions from blocking regular ones.

- **Q** (@Aatrox, 2022-05-23): Cold wallet transaction fails with "cost of transaction 1,003,959 exceeds limit 1,000,000" when sending 1,600+ ERG requiring 41 QR codes. Why?
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge): The problem is UTXO fragmentation (250+ input boxes), not the ERG amount. Each input box increases transaction size and complexity. Maximum ~12 boxes per cold wallet transaction. Solution: consolidate to a hot wallet first, or split into smaller sends. A pre-signing error warning will be added.

- **Q** (@Fabulaowski, 2022-05-27): Cardano uses 24-word seed phrases, Ergo uses 15 — wouldn't more words be more secure?
  **A** (@MrStahlfelge): 15 words = 160 bits entropy, which is sufficient. The mobile app supports up to 100 words if desired. However, manually chosen words have *less* entropy than program-generated seeds because humans are poor random generators — extra words only help if they're truly random.

- **Q** (@Koutelier, 2022-05-05): How do I integrate Nautilus wallet connection into a dApp website? Are there tutorials?
  **A** (@Koutelier, @Chris Ray): No formal library or tutorial exists yet. Workaround is to use ErgoPad's frontend as a reference implementation. This gap was flagged as a high-priority ErgoHack submission target.

- **Q** (community, 2022-05-05): Can bulletproofs be verified in ErgoScript, and what would that enable?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes — bulletproofs are a form of zero-knowledge proof (specifically a Sigma protocol variant) that require no trusted setup, making them trustless alternatives to SNARKs. Verification in ErgoScript would enable hybrid contracts: on-chain execution with sensitive data kept off-chain, verified only via range proofs or generic zero-knowledge statements. Groth one-out-of-many proofs and verifiable credentials are near-term applications.

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2022-05-27): Can Ergo function as a Bitcoin sidechain?
  **A** (@Glasgow, @kushti): Possible but "yet to be explored." NIPoPoWs compatibility, Lightning Network integration, and ANETA are relevant existing work. No active development team assigned to a Bitcoin sidechain.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2022-05-05): How does Ergo's spam/DDoS protection work? Can a low-fee transaction flood clog the network like it did to another chain?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo has several layers of protection: (1) fee market — miners sort by fee/complexity ratio, crowding out low-fee spam naturally; (2) mempool filters — node operators can raise minimum propagation fee rates to prevent spam from entering their mempool; (3) reference client limits since v4.0.44 — nodes won't download too many transactions from a single peer. Fee estimation API exists in the node but is not yet used by wallets/applications.

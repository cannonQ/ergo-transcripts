# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: 2024-12

## Executive Summary

December 2024 was defined by two major technical milestones: the progression of the Ergo 6.0 soft-fork proposal (including the renaming of sub-blocks to "input blocks" and active devnet work) and continued Rosen Bridge expansion across BSC, Dogecoin, Solana, and Sui. Alongside core protocol development, the ecosystem saw significant DeFi activity — DuckPools Phase 2, MewFinance Christmas campaigns, Gluon stablecoin audit fundraising, and the Palmyra/Chainlink proof-of-reserve announcement — while demurrage and storage rent emerged as recurring philosophical and technical themes across all four weeks.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Ergo 6.0 Soft-Fork & Input Blocks (Sub-blocks)

The 6.0 proposal was published in early December with a short forum post and full specification in EIP-50. Throughout the month, kushti moved from proposal to active implementation: renaming "sub-blocks" to "input blocks" in code, completing initial linking logic, and beginning transaction packing and UTXO set commitment updates. The expected activation timeline was communicated as "after Monday" on 2024-12-20, with a public testnet RC release planned after devnet parameter review. Community discussion covered implications for timestamp-dependent contracts, faster block confirmation, and enabling mobile/lightweight nodes. A dedicated testnet channel was opened for community members to participate in parameter tweaking without requiring coding skills.

### Rosen Bridge Expansion & Cross-Chain Momentum

Rosen Bridge activity dominated ecosystem news throughout December. BSC integration entered Pandora testing with improved MetaMask support; Dogecoin was manually tested and pending merge; Solana and Sui onboarding discussions were underway via Cardano Catalyst proposals. Mid-month the bridge reached $207k in Cardano→Ergo transfers, and a single 24-hour window saw 54 transfers totalling ~$340k USD. Armeanio announced Rosen Bridge CKB (Nervos) integration on the Nervos forum. Community debate addressed the `rs` prefix naming convention (e.g., rsADA, rsERG) — justified by different trust assumptions post-bridging and prevention of user error sending wrapped tokens to native wallets. Watcher economics were detailed: November APY ~15%, early December ~25%, requiring 30k RSN + 800 ERG baseline with 3k RSN per permit.

### Storage Rent, Demurrage & Monetary Philosophy

Storage rent was a recurring thread across all four weeks, evolving from a technical discussion into a broader monetary philosophy debate. LouisLibre led extended comparisons between demurrage (constant circulation incentive), gold (declining yield), and Bitcoin (fixed supply trending to zero). Marc the Shark highlighted a live mainnet example of storage rent recirculating dormant ERG from long-inactive addresses. kushti emphasized that demurrage stability over centuries outperforms Bitcoin's fixed supply model, and noted that the BlocX fork critically omitted demurrage when copying Autolykos 2. The Ergo Book initiative saw LouisLibre express interest in contributing a dedicated demurrage chapter, with kushti planning to open contributions post-Ergoversary.

### DeFi Ecosystem Growth: DuckPools, MewFinance & Gluon

DuckPools Phase 2 IDO raised ~$31.5k in 22 hours at a 16% market discount, supporting SigmaUSD, SigmaRSV, ERG, and native ADA contributions. DuckPools v2 enables multiple assets as loan collateral via SmartReserves smart contracts, with BTC lending available via Rosen Bridge-wrapped assets. MewFinance expanded significantly: ErgoSanta dApp launched and sold out in under 5 minutes; MewMart (formerly MewStore) listed Mac Pro M1 for 570 ERG; MewFUN lottery launched with 24-hour rounds and perpetual jackpot; a Christmas token sale of 2M MEW at 0.01 ERG/MEW supported LP strengthening. The Gluon stablecoin protocol launched a security audit fundraising campaign via the Bene platform (ErgoHack 9 second-place winner), with kushti confirming GluonUSD would be an additional stablecoin alongside DexyUSD.

### Palmyra / Chainlink Proof-of-Reserve Integration

Dan Friedman detailed the Palmyra (formerly ZenGate) integration with Chainlink oracles for on-chain warehouse receipts enabling decentralized trade financing. Producers depositing commodities in bonded warehouses receive on-chain receipts issued via Chainlink's proof-of-reserve function, coupled with IoT devices (cameras, weight sensors, motion sensors) providing continuous verification. Armeanio defended the Chainlink choice as non-zero-sum with native Ergo Oracle Pools, with plans to use token sale capital to expand Oracle Pool v2 (currently running for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards). The community was reminded that using non-Ergo tools does not preclude Ergo-native development — the entire Palmyra stack runs on Ergo.

### Sigma 6.0 Merged Mining & Sidechain Constructions

kushti outlined improved sidechain constructions post-6.0 with trustless pegging via smart contracts and emission contracts rewarding mainchain miners for delivering sidechain data — a BIP 301-equivalent approach but with stronger security properties. Discussion addressed how Ergo-specific constructions enable trustless relay updates of sidechain data on the Ergo mainchain, contrasted with Bitcoin's less secure BIP 301. Community questions explored whether GPU/CPU miners could mine sidechains simultaneously, and whether the BlocX fork (Autolykos 2 rewrite in C++) could partner as a sidechain rather than a competing fork.

### Hardware Wallet & Tooling Progress

The Keystone hardware wallet integration advanced via a multi-party effort: dusek (Sigma Rust), Pgr456 (Keystone firmware), and anon_br (Nautilus wallet integration), originating from a DevDAO community bounty matched by the Ergo Foundation. By late December, the Keystone SDK Rust draft PR was open with SDK work mostly complete; firmware PRs remained pending updates. Fleet SDK 0.8.0 was released. Ledger integration passed functional testing and moved to security audit. Nautilus v0.15.0 was released. Spectrum SDK update finalizing Dexy AMM contract merge was under review.

### Order Book DEX Architecture (Trade House)

qx() provided an extended explanation of Trade House's order book model versus AMM (Spectrum DEX) design: order books offer complete depth transparency, full slippage control, partial order fills, and peer-to-peer execution with no single upgrade key vulnerability. AMMs remain preferable for liquidity providers. Partial fills work by duplicating the seller's box with leftover parameters. A potential aggregator combining both models was floated. Trade House AMM Access upgrade raffle reached 55.76% funding (2,509 ERG of 4,500 ERG goal) with community discussion of sharing Spectrum liquidity pools with Trade House orders.

### Quantum Computing Threat Assessment

Google's Willow quantum computer announcement prompted community concern in W50. kushti clarified that Ergo's Autolykos 2 PoW consensus is quantum-secure (unlike PoS systems), but digital signature schemes (secp256k1 Schnorr) face long-term risk. NIST post-quantum signature candidates were reviewed; all present size/speed tradeoffs making them suboptimal for blockchains. A post-quantum sidechain exodus path was floated as contingency. The consensus: no imminent technical threat, but perception risk and migration planning warrant attention.

### ErgoHack 9 Results & Community Events

ErgoHack 9 winners were announced: 1st place Satergo Offline Vault, 2nd place Stable Benefaction Platform (Bene), 3rd place Last Byte Bar, with special recognition for Minotaur Wallet. Videos were published to the Ergo YouTube channel. The Sigmanauts mining pool distributed its first-ever storage rent ERG airdrop (37 ERG, weekly going forward), with Marc the Shark seeking partnerships with Cardano SPOs interested in merge-mined sidechain mining. The $24,000 EF mining ecosystem grant was confirmed active over 12 months.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Matt, 2024-12-03): Is it possible to Merklize the RAM elements for Autolykos like Ethash?
  **A** (@kushti): Merklization not needed. PoW verification depends only on block height, not blockchain context. The verifier computes elements from the table without holding it; only the generator needs the table (or accepts exponential slowdown). Reference: [Autolykos PoW spec](https://www.docdroid.net/mcoitvK/ergopow-pdf)

- **Q** (@Matt, 2024-12-03): Doesn't the verifier need table data to compute elements?
  **A** (@kushti): No — the verifier only computes elements on demand. Efficient PoW generation requires the full table; without it, generation becomes exponentially slower scaled to parameter k.

- **Q** (@Alex, 2024-12-05): Why do bridged tokens need an `rs` prefix instead of seamless cross-chain movement?
  **A** (@Luivatra, @kushti): Different trust assumptions after bridging justify different names. Wrapped assets carry greater counterparty risk than native assets; distinct naming prevents user errors (e.g., sending rsADA to a Cardano wallet). This follows industry convention (wETH on Ethereum) and is transparent, not deceptive.

- **Q** (@Patato, 2024-12-06): Can 23KB base64 data be stored in a single UTXO box? Funds appear stuck in contract.
  **A** (@Pulsarz): Individual boxes are limited to 4KB. Multiple output boxes in a single transaction can be combined to exceed this. For larger data storage, spreading across multiple boxes is required.

- **Q** (@cafebedouin.org, 2024-12-08): Does directly spending AMM contract boxes interfere with batcher execution? Does 6.0/input blocks address this?
  **A** (@Flying Pig): Not a problem.

- **Q** (@cafebedouin.org, 2024-12-09): Is there a generic ErgoPay server that doesn't require each project to host its own backend?
  **A** (@Luivatra): An "ErgoPay server" is an unsigned transaction server (HTTP + Redis/DB backend). Unsigned transactions are often too large for QR codes, so the QR code points to a URL serving the actual unsigned transaction. Each project typically needs its own backend for custom transaction logic.

- **Q** (@Nabz, 2024-12-09): Can ErgoMixer be integrated into a wallet app?
  **A** (@Glasgow): ErgoMixer is integrated into SafeW but requires local execution. SigmaJoin is the recommended solution for non-local mixing: https://docs.ergoplatform.com/eco/sigmajoin/

- **Q** (multiple, 2024-12-09): Does Minotaur Wallet support chained transactions?
  **A** (@HQΣr, @Luivatra): Minotaur does not fully support chained transactions and fails frequently when attempted. Using an indexed node preference improves reliability. Mobile wallets should prefer indexed nodes only; Ergo Explorer has a dodgy mempool that causes chained transaction failures.

- **Q** (multiple, 2024-12-10): What is the quantum computing threat to Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo's Autolykos 2 PoW consensus is quantum-secure. Digital signature schemes (secp256k1 Schnorr) face long-term theoretical risk, but NIST post-quantum candidates all present size/speed tradeoffs making them currently suboptimal for blockchains. No imminent threat; a post-quantum sidechain exodus path exists as contingency if needed.

- **Q** (multiple, 2024-12-11): How does DuckPools v2 multi-asset collateral work?
  **A** (@kushti): DuckPools v2 enables multiple assets as loan collateral through the SmartReserves smart contract layer. Full viability requires a DEX upgrade enabling multi-asset single-step trades; parallel development is planned but timeline undefined.

- **Q** (@HelixEvo, 2024-12-15): Best way to lend BTC currently?
  **A** (@cannon_q): duckpools.io — deposit BTC (wrapped via Rosen Bridge at app.rosen.tech) and earn yield waiting for borrowers.

- **Q** (@Richi, 2024-12-18): After 6.0, a trustless Ergo↔Cardano bridge should be easy. Why prioritize BTC bridges?
  **A** (implicit, kushti context): BTC bridges have greater marketing visibility. Cardano sidechain is viable post-6.0 but lower priority than core infrastructure delivery.

- **Q** (@Σddie Lin, 2024-12-21): Do input blocks (sub-blocks) enable easy mobile nodes?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes — input blocks enable lighter clients and mobile node participation via faster confirmation mechanisms without requiring full chain state.

- **Q** (@cafebedouin.org, 2024-12-23): If rsERG is sent to a Cardano wallet and the passphrase is lost, is there a recovery mechanism for the underlying ERG in the Rosen Bridge contract?
  **A**: Not answered in available logs; flagged as unresolved.

- **Q** (multiple, 2024-12-27): What is the fee structure for MewFund RSN/SigUSD pool contribution?
  **A** (@HQΣr): 1% fee on contributions; 95% of LP tokens returned to contributors proportionally; 5% retained by MewFund for growth and operational support.

- **Q** (from AMA, 2024-12-W51): Can you explain what bulletproofs are and their relevance to Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulletproofs are a zero-knowledge proof technique enabling specific statements (e.g., range proofs) to be proven without trusted setup — making them trustless alternatives to SNARKs. They are a specific form of Sigma protocol and can be verified within ErgoScript, meaning a variety of zero-knowledge protocols (including Groth one-out-of-many, used in early Zcoin) can be combined with Ergo contracts. This enables hybrid contracts where execution occurs on-chain while sensitive input data is verified without disclosure — opening a new design space for privacy-preserving Ergo applications.

- **Q** (from AMA, 2024-12-W51): Can you describe DDoS/spam attack resilience on Ergo? Can the mempool be wiped by node operators in emergencies?
  **A** (@kushti): Ergo has layered spam resistance. Node operators can raise minimum propagation fee rates to prevent spam transactions from entering their mempool. From node version 4.0.44, limits exist on how many transactions a single peer can push. Fee estimation APIs exist (though underused by applications). In a fee market scenario, spammers paying below the median fee will eventually be flushed — their transactions time out and are dropped from the mempool after not being picked up for enough blocks. During Bitcoin's block size wars, similar spam filled mempools for weeks but the fee market resolved it automatically.

- **Q** (from AMA, 2024-12-W51): From a mining perspective, what do Ergo miners earn from merge-mined sidechains?
  **A** (@kushti): In Ergo's improved sidechain construction, emission contracts on the mainchain reward mainchain miners specifically for delivering sidechain data. This is better than Bitcoin's BIP 301 approach. Miners earn both ERG block rewards and sidechain tokens for the same work, with trustless pegging via smart contracts rather than a weaker multisig model.

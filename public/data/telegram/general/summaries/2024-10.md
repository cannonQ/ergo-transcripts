# Ergo General Chat — Monthly Summary: 2024-10

---

## Executive Summary

October 2024 was a pivotal month for Ergo, marked by three converging themes: major infrastructure milestones (Rosen Bridge Ethereum live, Ergo 6.0-alpha2 released, Ledger Stax/Flex integration completed), ecosystem expansion through cross-chain liquidity (rsERG on Uniswap, CoinGecko, and Cardano pools), and deep strategic discussions about Ergo's post-6.0 development direction. Community-driven initiatives — ErgoHack IX, MewFinance presales, RoseNet testing, and Palmyra partnership announcements — reinforced Ergo's positioning as a VC-free, real-world-utility blockchain while raising unresolved questions about layer 2 vs. Sigmachains prioritisation.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Ergo 6.0 Protocol Development & Sub-blocks

The month opened with kushti completing the final PRs for Sigma 6.0, culminating in the release of Ergo protocol client 6.0.0-alpha2 (2024-10-09) with all planned Sigma 6.0.0 features, followed by reference client 5.0.23 (2024-10-10) and 5.0.24. Sub-blocks generated their first test run via the inbuilt miner early in the month, with kushti confirming the design: sub-blocks will be introduced via velvet fork, then locked in via soft fork after 6.0 activation. Community voting on 6.0 was proposed for December. A soft-fork mechanism for new parameters (including adjustable sub-blocks per block via miner voting) was confirmed with existing clients able to skip validation of new parameters. Sub-block naming conventions remained debated throughout the month — proposals included "weak blocks," "input blocks," "contract blocks," and "ordering blocks" — with no final decision reached. kushti noted that a security proof is still pending, and the broader community discussion about block time impacts (timestamp-based contracts, emission schedules) established that speeding up the chain is non-trivial and requires careful consideration of dApps relying on block height rather than timestamps.

### Rosen Bridge Ethereum Integration & Cross-Chain Expansion

The Rosen Bridge Ethereum integration went live on 2024-10-19, representing the month's most significant ecosystem milestone. Watcher operators updated to version 4.0.0 (with Ethereum health checks and decimal drop refactor) and UI version 2.4.1. ADA-to-ETH bridging followed within days. RSERGsuccessfully appeared on CoinGecko and was verified on Uniswap, with rsERG/ETH liquidity pools growing on both Uniswap and MewFinance. The full multi-chain roadmap was confirmed publicly: Ergo, Cardano, Bitcoin, and Ethereum live; BSC, Solana, Sui, Base, Dogecoin, Flux, Kaspa, all EVM chains, and all L2s in progress. RoseNet — the new p2p communication layer for Rosen guards — entered final testing, seeking global decentralized node operators (8GB+ RAM, 8+ cores, stable internet) for a ~24-hour test to improve bridging speed and prevent stuck events. Discussions about bridged stablecoin risk (USDC/USDT freeze functions) led to community consensus favouring decentralised alternatives (FRAX, LUSD, CRVUSD) for bridge integrations.

### Ledger & Hardware Wallet Support

A 4,100 ERG bounty (2K from ErgoDevs DAO + EF match + 100 ERG from Russian community) was posted for Ledger Stax and Flex UI support. A developer started 2024-10-07; by mid-month the integration was reported complete. The ErgoDevs DAO fundraising raffle for the third-party audit was fully funded (210 ERG from 3 contributors). By month's end, kushti confirmed Ledger code would be sent for functional testing (~2 weeks), with third-party audit to follow before Ledger Live availability. Separately, a ~8k ERG bounty was posted for Keystone hardware wallet integration, with address generation reported as working. Ledger dropped Nano S support (device EOL), and the community discussed whether Keystone or Tangem should be prioritised next.

### MewFinance Platform Expansion

MewFinance had a significant month: a presale sold out in under 3 hours (41 participants) with a second presale following; Ergo Essentials was rebranded as MewTools with IPFS minting and EIP standard compliance; MewDex swap UI launched with notably faster performance than Spectrum DEX (partly attributed to Aco's code cleanup); Mew Kitties NFT collection presale went live with tier-based benefits (Tier 6 granting free trading and 5% profit sharing from MewMart + DEX ERG fees); MEW token was integrated into the tipperbot ecosystem; and the Bober DAO proposal (YF: ErgExplorer partnership) passed with 54.6% participation. The Cup of Sugar peer-to-peer store launched on MewFinance, selling raw honey for ERG, SigmaUSD, and wrapped Cardano assets — a concrete demonstration of Ergo's real-world commerce vision. MewFinance also announced a Hummingbot partnership for automated market-making on Ergo DEXes.

### Palmyra / ZenGate Strategy & Chainlink Partnership

Dan Friedman's Palmyra project announced a Chainlink partnership for decentralised trade financing using proof-of-reserve functions via Winter Protocol. The design uses IoT devices (cameras, weight sensors, motion sensors) coupled with Chainlink oracles to provide continuous proof of commodity existence in bonded warehouses, issuing on-chain warehouse receipts as collateral for DeFi lending. The plan includes building out native Ergo Oracle Pools in addition to Chainlink, with capital from the token sale earmarked for Ergo oracle development. Community discussion emphasised avoiding tribal reactions to multi-chain tool choices — Ergo currently runs the full Palmyra stack, and other tools are supplements, not replacements. Dan's presence in Argentina and multi-chain partnership strategy generated both enthusiasm and pointed questions about Ergo-exclusivity commitments.

### Post-6.0 Strategic Direction

kushti raised the strategic question of post-6.0 priorities explicitly toward month's end: Sigmachains (application-specific merged-mined sidechains), layer 2 solutions with mass-market stablecoin wallets, or other directions. Armeanio proposed a first sidechain focused on small commodity GPUs with merged mining rewards for Ergo miners, overseen by Paideia and Rosen. The broader sidechain vision was framed around real-world utility (credentials, privacy, commodity tokens) rather than crypto-native metrics. A community call was proposed to resolve the direction, with kushti noting "no sidechain work will proceed without real-world utility justification." The fee market, spam protection, and meme coin application suitability (requiring layer 2) were discussed as related constraints.

### Storage Rent, Governance & Fee Market

DX Pool (>51% hashrate) began voting to increase the cost per output from 252 toward the historically recommended 1000–1500, following proper governance procedures. This reduces cheap output spam and affects airdrop transaction costs. The fee estimation API was confirmed to exist in the node but is untested in production and underused by applications — community noted this should be integrated Bitcoin-wallet-style. Reference client v4.0.44+ has per-peer transaction limits as spam protection; node operators can raise minimum propagation fees. The autumn 2020 spam event (mixers creating load, exchanges raising fees from 0.001 to 0.1 ERG) was cited as precedent for how Ergo's fee market handles stress.

### ErgoHack IX

ErgoHack IX (theme: UI/UX and Mining, Oct 25–30) received nine project submissions: Community Liquidity Bootstrap, Stable Benefaction Platform, Last Byte Bar (NFT/token generation on block discovery), OnErgo (gamified engagement platform), Satergo Offline Vault, Minotaur multisig improvements, OneStopShop onboarding guide, Cup of Sugar peer-to-peer local economy tracking, and a 3D UTXO Explorer. The deadline was extended to November 1st midnight UTC.

### Lithos Protocol & Commodity Stablecoins

Armeanio funded a silver support oracle fork for Lithos Protocol, advancing a broader vision for Gluon-based commodity-backed tokens. A lithium-backed algorithmic stablecoin was announced — redeemable 1:1 when physical mining capacity is available. Lithos NISP contract prototypes were noted in the weekly ecosystem update. Collection minting standards on Lithos at 0.4 ERG were confirmed fair using AVL Trees with logarithmic pricing.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2024-10-01): If Palmyra brings high transaction volume, will everyone pay more in fees?
  **A** (@kushti): Block limits can be safely raised from current levels. Mixing transactions are 8x heavier than simple payments, so TPS depends on transaction mix. Raspberry Pis run light clients only; multithreading on modern CPUs can drastically improve parallel validation.

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2024-10-01): How will Ergo handle increased Palmyra NFT minting load?
  **A** (@kushti): Bulk NFT minting using authenticated trees was already proposed. The main limiting factor is heavy cryptographic operations, not size. Long contracts execute faster than simple payments.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-10-07): Isn't it illegal to create your own local currency?
  **A** (@kushti): Depends on jurisdiction. Japan has 800+ local currencies and is very relaxed. Most jurisdictions require legal workarounds.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-10-09): How long until sub-blocks enable faster wallet-to-wallet transfers (currently 10–20 minutes)?
  **A** (@kushti): Sub-blocks will be introduced via velvet fork, then locked in via soft fork post-6.0. Security proof still needed. Timeframe unspecified.

- **Q** (@Man ish, 2024-10-18): Will dApps be able to choose between normal block confirmation and sub-block confirmation per transaction?
  **A** (@Luivatra): All p2p transactions go through sub-blocks by default. dApps using chained transactions can assume instant finality because the chain breaks if the start falls away.

- **Q** (@Marc the Shark, 2024-10-17): Are block times faster on testnet by chance?
  **A** (@qx()): Testnet is set to 30-second block times. Explorer at testnet.ergoplatform.com.

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2024-10-20): What is the best market interest rate to borrow SigmaUSD with rsBTC as collateral?
  **A** (@cannon_q): It's trial and error — see what the market will bear. Alternatively, swap BTC to ERG, then borrow SigmaUSD from DuckPools.io at 6.8% currently.

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2024-10-20): How does DuckPools liquidation work? The 30% penalty is confusing.
  **A** (@cannon_q): If collateral value drops below the 135% threshold, liquidation triggers. The 30% fee is the liquidation penalty. Keep collateral at 140%+ if the threshold is 130%.

- **Q** (@Patato, 2024-10-13): Can quantum computers be defeated by using invented non-dictionary words in a 24-word seed phrase?
  **A** (@kushti): No. Quantum computers break secrets derived from seeds efficiently by solving the discrete logarithm problem — there is no need to iterate over the seed space at all.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-10-28): Why is ERG priced at 3.3 SigmaUSD instead of the expected rate?
  **A** (@Luivatra): SigmaUSD v1 bank is outdated. Use v2 for current rates.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-10-29): How will rsERG be listed on centralised exchanges?
  **A** (@kushti): It only makes sense where ERC-20 tokens can be listed free or cheaper than native tokens. ERG must be bridged to the target chain first, then supplied to the CEX.

- **Q** (@cannon_q, 2024-10-31): Why aren't mempool operators worried about spam attacks?
  **A** (@kushti): Reference client v4.0.44+ limits transactions accepted per peer, preventing single-peer spam vectors. Node operators can also raise the minimum propagation fee to filter low-fee spam before mempool acceptance.

- **Q** (@Richi, 2024-11-02): Can snek.fun-style rapid meme coin apps run on Ergo's 2-minute block time?
  **A** (@kushti): No — 2-minute blocks are unsuitable for that transaction volume. Requires either Sigmachains or a layer 2 solution. High-frequency traffic burdens miners with bandwidth and CPU costs without proportional rewards.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-10-29): Is there a fee estimation API for wallets?
  **A** (@kushti): The node has a fee estimation API showing cost to enter the next block(s), but it is untested in production and underutilised by applications. It should be integrated the way Bitcoin wallets do it.

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-10-14): Is there risk in minting all rsERG supply on Ethereum?
  **A** (@HQΣr): The cold wallet requires more than 5 signatures to validate any transaction — making hijacking extremely difficult.

- **Q** (@Jack Σ, 2024-10-01): Can sub-blocks be used as fast oracle feeds for live events?
  **A** (@kushti): Interesting idea — asked if the intent was "fast Oracle Pools."

- **Q** (@Unknown, 2024-10-28): What happens if ERG is sent to an invalid address?
  **A** (@qx()): Nautilus validates addresses in real time and prevents bad sends. Unlike Bitcoin or Ethereum, ERG sent to invalid addresses is recycled via storage rent (demurrage), ensuring a true fixed supply.

- **Q** (@Shiv Chauhan, 2024-10-22): Can Zephyr Protocol's PoW-based stablecoin reward model work on Ergo?
  **A** (@kushti): Not yet on mainnet Ergo. Could work in a sidechain context. Alternatives include locking sidechain block rewards in LP and rewarding miners with vested LP tokens. Connecting protocol core with dApp logic is not ideal for a generic DeFi platform.

## Ergo General — Monthly Summary: 2025-07

---

## Executive Summary

July 2025 was defined by three converging themes: protocol maturation through the Ergo 6.0 soft fork vote and sub-blocks development, ecosystem expansion via new financial primitives (DexyGold beta, MewFinance tooling, VoltPay, Banxa onramp), and a renewed focus on infrastructure decentralisation through InfraDAO. The Ergoversary 6th anniversary served as a catalyst for content, community momentum, and governance activity, including five pivotal Rosen Bridge treasury proposals and the conclusion of ErgoHack X.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Ergo 6.0 Soft Fork

Voting for the 6.0 soft fork was initiated on 2025-07-06 and rapidly achieved 92.97% miner support, entering the activation phase. The upgrade includes BigInt support, improved Merkle tree handling, faster proof verification, cross-input inspection, new developer APIs, governance parameterisation, and a reduction in difficulty retarget from 1,024 to 128 blocks. The voting window spans 32 epochs (~45 days), followed by a 32-epoch update period, with mainnet activation expected around the October 2025 Ergo Meetup in Colorado. The 90% support threshold must be sustained within the full 32 × 1,024 block window rather than held per-epoch. Sub-blocks and improved protocol fundamentals are contingent on this upgrade completing.

### Sub-Blocks Development

Sub-blocks (input blocks) development progressed substantially through July. A DevNet was made available with reduced effective block times (estimated ~2-minute blocks with sub-blocks resolving in seconds), and testnet injection of supporting mining nodes was planned for July, with August targeted for polish and formal security research. kushti clarified that transactions in input blocks referenced by an ordering block are always confirmed — there is no risk of dropping regular p2pk transactions. Miners earn transaction fees from input blocks, with block emission rewards reserved for standard ordering blocks. Merge-mined sidechain rewards provide supplemental income. Full ecosystem adoption, including dApp adaptation and parameter tweaking, is expected to take 3–6 months after mainnet deployment.

### Block Reward Emission Reduction

The block reward dropped from 15 ERG to 12 ERG on 2025-07-07, reducing daily new supply to 8,640 ERG (down from 10,800). Rewards will continue falling by 3 ERG every three months until reaching 3 ERG in April 2026. This milestone was highlighted during the Ergoversary week as evidence of Ergo's predictable, fair emission schedule. Ergo.watch emission tracking briefly went offline and required a node restart.

### DexyGold Beta Launch

DexyGold Beta went live on mainnet on 2025-07-15 as a gold-pegged stablecoin backed by Bank and Liquidity Pool contracts and an upgraded Gold v2 oracle. Minting is available on mainnet with trading on Crystal Pools; additional stablecoins (DexyUSD, etc.) are planned. Community discussion noted that gold-peg assets like DexyGold and Gluon may warrant lower interest rates than SigmaUSD due to the inflation-hedge properties of gold. Oracle Pool v2 is running specifically for Dexy Gold with GORT rewards.

### Rosen Bridge Governance & Cross-Chain Expansion

Five pivotal Rosen Bridge treasury proposals were released by Armeanio covering RSN liquidity, RSN utility (Hodl Game+), Cardano SPO rewards, ecosystem expansion and tooling, and developer incentives. Community governance feedback was solicited across the month. Armeanio indicated Rosen Bridge had achieved cryptographic integration with multiple major chains. The Rosen SDK was noted as in progress but deprioritised; no timeline was given. rsERG/ADA liquidity depth on Cardano DEXes was flagged as insufficient for whale participation. rsDOGE went live on 2025-07-13 following the Watcher v5.0.0 and UI v3.4.0 mandatory update.

### MewFinance Ecosystem

MewFinance expanded rapidly through July, launching `fund.mewfinance.com`, `tools.mewfinance.com`, `bank.mewfinance.com` (SigmaUSD trade offers), `payment.mewfinance.com`, and MewLock (`lock.mewfinance.com`) with a 3% withdrawal fee. An audio player for Cyberverse soundtracks and a VoltPay widget embedding system with iframe-based integration and 300+ coin support were also shipped. The ecosystem was praised for its UI clarity relative to other Ergo dApps.

### VoltPay & Banxa Fiat Integration

VoltPay (`voltpay.store`) launched as a non-custodial multi-chain swap tool supporting ~300 trading routes (XMR→ERG, ADA→ERG, ETH→ERG, etc.) using background CEX liquidity via Swapter and Exolix. Community testing reported sub-2% total fees. Banxa fiat-to-ERG onramp went live at `checkout.banxa.com` (KYC required) and was integrated directly into Nautilus wallet with a "Buy ERG" button. Banxa readiness had been contingent on resolving MEXC's UTXO dust accumulation issue, which was caused by ~15,000 UTXOs in an unmaintained cold wallet. MEXC withdrawals reopened on 2025-07-25 after kushti offered technical assistance.

### InfraDAO

qx() launched InfraDAO with the goal of creating globally distributed, p2p load-balanced Ergo node and explorer infrastructure independent of Ergo Foundation funding. Requirements for membership include ≥50 Mbps upload, ≤100ms ping, a public static IPv4, and SSH/terminal access. Load-balancing endpoints went live: `explorer-p2p.ergoplatform.com`, `node-p2p.ergoplatform.com`, `graphql-p2p.ergoplatform.com`, and `api-p2p.ergoplatform.com`. A Sigmanauts DAO proposal for 50,000 Paideia allocation to formalise the DAO was submitted, with a Paideia DAO token and dashboard planned. The initiative is positioned as a move away from Cloudflare dependency.

### Merge-Mined Sidechains & Lithos Protocol

kushti discussed BitVM2/3-powered trustless peg designs combining double merge-mined Bitcoin and Ergo sidechain construction, referencing BIP 301 as a relevant framework for improved sidechaining security over Bitcoin's model. Miners can mine both Ergo and a sidechain simultaneously with only sidechain block validation overhead. Emission contracts reward mainchain miners for delivering sidechain data, enabling trustless pegging. Lithos Protocol made concrete progress: storage rent contract testing completed, NISP Fraud Proof contracts tested, and difficulty fraud-prevention contract development underway. A whitepaper draft was circulated with an ERG holder airdrop proposed. An initial research phase for Lithos BTC implementation on Braid also commenced.

### Privacy: DarkERG & Bulletproofs

kushti proposed bulletproof-based range proof verification in ErgoScript as the foundation for a DarkERG privacy system featuring virtual UTXOs, hidden amounts, stealth addresses, and trustless 1:1 ERG swaps. A bounty was issued on GitHub (issue #1032 in sigmastate-interpreter). kushti additionally noted that a 2025 IACR paper enables many zero-knowledge proofs efficiently within Ergo's framework, including BitVM-like techniques, with compressed Sigma protocols predating Bulletproofs already implemented. Curve trees and Full Chain Membership Proofs (FCMPs) from Monero research were discussed as further privacy primitives relevant to Ergo contracts.

### Global Transfer Policies (GTPs)

kushti confirmed feasibility of introducing GTPs to Ergo via soft-fork — enabling programmable blacklists, whitelists, tax enforcement, and reserve thresholds at the application level without introducing first-class protocol changes. GTPs operate on UTXOs rooted in an issuer's UTXO and do not require contract-level enforcement. Whether cross-address rejection of blacklisted tokens (contingent settlement) is supported remains exploratory.

### Ergoversary & ErgoHack X

The Ergoversary content series ran across the first week of July, celebrating Ergo's 6th anniversary with video presentations from Lithos Protocol, Palmyra, Bene, SigmaFi, Gluon, BlitzTCG, Rosen Bridge, Proof of Merch, and others. ErgoHack X concluded with Game of Prompts (1st), CodeUTXO (2nd), and Cortex (3rd); Pulse, Pattern, and RustChain were community favourites. A community Zoom roundtable replaced the standard AMA format on 2025-07-24.

### GPU Mining & Hashrate

Current mining profitability is marginal, with 3070 GPUs near break-even at 11¢ electricity. Hardware depreciation outpaces daily earnings for most miners. Hashrate shifts were attributed to hardware mix changes (loss of 4GB cards post-Ethereum Merge) and Autolykos v2 parameter adjustments every three months, making cross-year hashrate comparisons unreliable. Nicehash hashrate availability dropped significantly (~0.26 TH available) due to mandatory KYC implementation deterring miners. kushti compared current GPU mining conditions to the 2013–2014 CPU era, noting 100× more capital availability now.

### Storage Rent

Extended discussion covered storage rent collection competition among bots, with Storage_Hunter proposing a capped RBF step-size rule (≤0.0001 ERG per replacement) to prevent fee wars destroying rent value. The counter-argument noted that mining pools (especially Herominers) can now claim rent directly on-chain, making individual bot competition less relevant once pools implement it fully. Community members were reminded that UTXOs older than four years incur ~0.09 ERG storage rent deductions every two weeks, and consolidation via MewFinance tools or Nautilus wallet refreshes the timer. Boxes under 0.14 ERG holding tokens are consumed entirely.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@LouisLibre, W27): Do sub-block transactions risk non-confirmation, or just longer wait times?
  **A** (@kushti): Transactions depending on block data (e.g., the Ergo emission contract using miner pubkey) must go into ordering blocks only. Regular transactions included in an input block and referenced by an ordering block chain are always included — no risk of dropping.

- **Q** (@LouisLibre, W27): Will regular p2pk transactions in sub-blocks always reach ordering blocks?
  **A** (@kushti): Once a transaction is included in an input block and an ordering block references that input block, the transaction is always included. Input blocks function identically to regular blocks in this respect.

- **Q** (@Ergochampion, W28): Does merge-mining sidechains cause additional GPU wear, or do miners reuse Ergo's PoW security at no extra cost?
  **A** (@kushti): Marginal cost only — sidechain block validation overhead. No additional computational burden beyond verification.

- **Q** (@Richi, W28): If support falls below 90% for 2–3 epochs within the 32-epoch voting window, does it impact activation?
  **A** (@kushti): No. 90% support is required within the full 32 × 1,024 block window total, not as a per-epoch floor.

- **Q** (@Man ish, W29): How many epochs until EIP-50 activation?
  **A** (@mx via DiscordBridge): First 32 epochs is the voting window; activation period is another 32 epochs. Protocol live expected around the October Ergo Meetup. Block size and computational cost changes are dynamic per epoch; difficulty retarget reduced from 1,024 to 128 blocks.

- **Q** (@dfdgrfr, W28): Can in-browser nodes be implemented in Ergo?
  **A** (@AwesomeQA/Pluno): Yes. Ergo's UTXO set authentication via Merkle tree structure enables lightweight full nodes in-browser.

- **Q** (@Krim, W31): How will sub-blocks reward miners? Will there be a new token for sub-block mining?
  **A** (@cannon_q): No additional rewards for sub-blocks (input blocks). Miners earn transaction fees from input blocks; block reward emission only on standard ordering blocks. Merge-mining sidechains provides additional income.

- **Q** (@Man ish, W31): Can a sidechain miner mine BTC, Ergo, and a sidechain coin simultaneously?
  **A** (@kushti): Yes — different options exist, including direct stablecoin mining without Babel fees. After Braid, a Monero + Ergo merge-mining sidechain is feasible.

- **Q** (@Richi, W31): Can Global Transfer Policies enforce address blacklists/whitelists and auto-burn unwanted tokens?
  **A** (@kushti): GTPs are generic — programmable logic for blacklists, whitelists, tax enforcement, and reserve thresholds on tokens or UTXOs rooted in your UTXO. They operate at the application level, not the contract level; no first-class protocol citizens are introduced.

- **Q** (@kenan5593, W30): Is my ERG being burned from my wallet? (~0.09 ERG every two weeks).
  **A** (@HQΣr, @D7🐳): Storage rent — UTXOs older than four years need consolidation via MewFinance tools or mobile wallet. Once reclaimed by the network, ERG cannot be recovered. Boxes under 0.14 ERG holding tokens are consumed entirely. Consolidate remaining UTXOs to refresh the timer.

- **Q** (@kushti, W30): Can Ergo do zero-knowledge proofs efficiently?
  **A** (@kushti): Sigma-protocol verification is supported via low-level group operations; compressed Sigma protocols predate Bulletproofs and are already implemented. A 2025 IACR paper shows many ZK proofs are possible with efficiency, including BitVM-like techniques with better failure-path handling.

- **Q** (@Ioannis, W28): Why does Ergo have less hashrate than pre-Ethereum Merge if PoW is valuable?
  **A** (@kushti): Hardware mix changed (no 4GB cards post-Merge). Autolykos v2 parameters readjust every three months. Hard to compare hashrate across years due to shifting PoW function and hardware assumptions.

- **Q** (@A, W30): How does VoltPay interchain swap work? Where does liquidity come from?
  **A** (@HQΣr): Swaps execute via background CEX activity using Swapter and Exolix; a Monero tunnel and other services handle routing across ~300 trading routes.

- **Q** (@DiscordBridge/Kahnloc, W31): Is GUAC just like SigmaRSV?
  **A** (@Luca D'Angelo): Essentially yes — both tokenise bank equity. GUAC can be minted freely with no restrictions on GUAC levels; users receive both GAU and GAUC on deposit and can transmute GAU to GAUC via the "Transmute from Gold" option.

- **Q** (@Matt, W27): Doesn't Ergo's GPU mining create a 51% attack vulnerability?
  **A** (@Glasgow): Never formally raised as a concern. No rational incentive at current scale; cost, logistics, liquidity, and defensive responses eliminate economic motivation. If truly cheap, attackers would already exploit it.

- **Sub-blocks mainnet rollout sequencing**: Exact timeline for testnet parameter tweaking, ecosystem dApp adaptation, and community testing group coordination remains TBD. Volunteers sought for parameter testing group.

- **DarkERG bulletproof bounty**: Whether bulletproof-based range proof verification in ErgoScript is achievable remains unconfirmed pending bounty resolution (GitHub issue #1032).

- **Global Transfer Policies scope**: Whether GTPs will support cross-address rejection of blacklisted tokens (contingent settlement) remains exploratory; no implementation timeline.

- **Lithos testnet launch**: Conditional on off-chain code completion; no specific date provided.

- **Merge-mined sidechain whitepaper**: Formal publication on Ergo Forum of canonical sidechain construction designs deferred post-Ergoversary; community feedback consolidation status unreported.

- **Sidechain fee denomination**: Whether sidechain transaction fees will be ERG-only, sidechain-coin-only, or hybrid remains unconfirmed.

- **Curve trees / FCMPs in Ergo**: kushti noted "still need to figure out how FCMPs are derived from them" — no implementation timeline.

- **Native Ergo Oracle network expansion**: Armeanio/Palmyra plan to build out additional Ergo-native Oracle Pools beyond the two currently running; no budget or timeline announced.

- **GitHub multisig release signing**: Decision on multisig for release signing deferred to 2025; review of GitHub trust model planned but unscheduled.

- **Minotaur ErgoPay compatibility**: Root cause of "not connected" state and ErgoPay support gap under investigation; fix in progress (GitHub issue #28).

- **Marketing and UX funding allocation**: Whether future dApp fundraising will enforce 15–20% allocation for marketing and UX, or continue the grassroots development model, remains a policy question without resolution.

- **Storage rent bot fee wars**: If mining pools fully implement on-chain rent collection, coordination mechanism to prevent pool undercutting remains an open design question.

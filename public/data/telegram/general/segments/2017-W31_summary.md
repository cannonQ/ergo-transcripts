# Ergo General Chat — 2017-W31 Summary

## Key Topics Discussed

- **Ergo Platform Status & Release Timeline**: Early-stage project discussion with no mainnet release. Testnet expected in September 2017. Economy model still under construction and deferred from first testnet release.

- **Ergo vs. Scorex**: Clarification that Ergo uses Scorex as a library rather than being a direct fork or offshoot.

- **Blockchain Scalability & Storage**: Discussion of how Ergo and Rollerchain compare to Proof-of-Stake models for addressing blockchain bloat. Acknowledged that storage and balance recovery require full transaction history in PoW systems.

- **Competitive Landscape**: Comparison with Nimiq (browser-based mining) and general market positioning. Noted lack of technical documentation made competitive assessment difficult.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Noah, msg#69): How does Ergo relate to Scorex? Is Ergo considered an offshoot of Scorex?  
  **A** (@Dmitry, msg#71): Ergo uses Scorex as a library.

- **Q** (@Noah, msg#74): In terms of addressing blockchain bloat, how do Ergo & Rollerchain compare with a PoS model, especially for security?  
  **A** (@Dmitry, msg#79): Rollerchain is not compatible with PoS. Blockchain bloat problem is not solvable in PoS because you need all transactions to recover current balances.

- **Q** (@Unknown, msg#77-78): Is Nimiq a competitor to Ergo? (Notes Nimiq offers browser-based mining on any device with betanet live)  
  **A** (@Dmitry, msg#80): Lack of technical documentation for Nimiq makes it hard to assess whether their promises are feasible.

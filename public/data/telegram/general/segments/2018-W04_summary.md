# Ergo General Chat — 2018-W04 Summary

## Key Topics Discussed

- **Website & Brand Design**: Community discussed potential redesign of Ergo's website before mainnet launch, with suggestions to adopt a more polished, modular UX similar to Cardano's branding approach while maintaining a distinction from generic crypto sites.

- **EFYT Token Storage & Security**: Multiple inquiries about safe custody of EFYT tokens on Waves platform, with clarification that Waves Lite Client (desktop wallet) is secure if machine security is maintained, unlike centralized exchange risks. Paper wallet generation was also mentioned as an option.

- **Token Supply & Economics**: Uncertainty about EFYT supply distribution raised concerns. Community discussed estimates: ~400K circulating at time of discussion, potential expansion to 1M, with first-year supply potentially 10x circulation amount and 50% of total supply, but no confirmed figures available.

- **Sigma Protocols & Cryptography**: Early enthusiasm about Sigma protocol paper release, with acknowledgment that the technical content would require multiple readings to understand fully.

- **Ergo vs. Waves Positioning**: Clarification that while both platforms offer smart contract functionality, they are not direct competitors—each solves different problems within the broader blockchain ecosystem.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Unknown, msg#1703): Is Ergo a competitor to Waves since both introduce non-Turing smart contracts?  
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ, msg#1721): Both are pieces of the same puzzle. While functionally comparable on smart contracts, each platform offers distinct solutions and neither is a direct competitor—blockchain is complex enough to need complementary approaches.

- **Q** (@Unknown, msg#1712): Where can EFYT be held? Is there a cold wallet option?  
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖎𝖘𝖎𝖙կշօ, msg#1715): Tokens can be held on waveswallet.io. Cold storage support expected by February. Desktop Waves Lite Client also functions as a secure wallet if machine security is maintained.

- **Q** (@Unknown, msg#1768): Is the Waves Lite Client safe for token storage, or is it like EtherDelta which was DNS-hacked?  
  **A** (@Waylay, msg#1773): Waves is decentralized infrastructure (like MEW for Ethereum), not a centralized exchange. Centralized exchanges get hacked, but decentralized peer-to-peer clients do not face the same attack surface.

- **Q** (@Unknown, msg#1799): What is the definitive supply distribution for EFYT in the first couple of years? Are more airdrops planned?  
  **A** (@𝖒𝖊𝖓𝖙𝖆𝖑𝖎𝖘𝖙կշօ, msg#1805): Questions compiled for upcoming Q&A session (date TBD).

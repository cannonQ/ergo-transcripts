# Ergo General Chat — 2020-W13 Summary

## Key Topics Discussed

- **Mixer and Privacy**: Non-interactive, non-custodial mixer in development as a key Ergo use case. Discussion of legal/regulatory concerns around privacy tooling and whether law enforcement could effectively block such applications.

- **Exchange Listings**: USDC trading pair available on OKEx. Discussions of efforts to secure major exchange listings (Binance mentioned); community being informed new listings are under NDA.

- **Ergo Use Cases**: Five primary use cases articulated: (1) digital gold via scarce native tokens, (2) privacy-preserving applications, (3) financial contracts and complex signature schemes, (4) alternative financial systems (targeted loans, local exchange trading), (5) money for unstable regions with limited internet.

- **Post-Emission Economics**: Discussion of how Ergo sustains security and miner incentives after max supply is reached; storage rent mechanism referenced as key to long-term viability.

- **Mining and Market Positioning**: Industrial miners present from launch; high competition pushed away speculators. Hashrate continuing to grow. Production cost discussions around price floor.

- **Community Growth**: Marketing efforts underway across Reddit, Twitter, Telegram, BTT. National community groups established (Russian, Indonesian, Chinese, Persian, Indian/Bangladesh). Content creation and article outreach planned as speculation factor needed.

- **UI and Developer Tooling**: Emphasis on need for full-fledged DEX and mixer UI accessibility. Contributors delivering on core tech but UIs for all use cases acknowledged as long-term projects.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Willy, msg#25212): How do Russian authorities treat cryptocurrencies?
  **A** (@Unknown, msg#25219): Poland-based respondent notes tax forms already exist for crypto; authorities recognize banning creates black markets. Russia likely similar in accepting crypto as fact.

- **Q** (@Willy, msg#25239): Aren't you afraid of law enforcement attention after creating a crypto mixer?
  **A** (@Unknown, msg#25243): Mixer and Ergo use cases cannot be stopped from existing; Ergo design itself is not a problem to authorities—the *possibility* of Ergo's design is their problem.

- **Q** (@Martin, msg#25215): How does Ergo work when max supply is reached? Won't the chain collapse or become vulnerable without mining rewards?
  **A** (@Unknown, msg#25229): Blockchains are sooner abandoned by developers than they collapse; forks or delistings occur instead. Ergo's economy supports miners with or without users; Bitcoin depends entirely on users after emission ends.

## Unresolved Questions

- **Speculation Factor**: Community acknowledged that adding a "speculation factor" to Ergo is difficult but necessary for growth; no concrete strategies articulated beyond marketing content.
- **Full Node Wallet UI**: Multiple respondents requested convenient full node wallet with anonymous transactions for all platforms; acknowledged as a long-term project with no timeline given.
- **DeFi and Stablecoins**: Interest expressed in DeFi features and algorithmic fiat coins (stablecoins), but no roadmap commitments made.
- **Law Enforcement Risk**: Ongoing debate about whether regulatory risk to mixer/privacy features is real; no consensus reached on mitigation strategies beyond asserting Ergo's technical resilience.

# Ergo Developer Chat — Monthly Summary
## February 2022

---

## Executive Summary

February 2022 was a technically focused month for Ergo developers, dominated by wallet infrastructure improvements, explorer backend performance bottlenecks, and the activation of EIP-27 on testnet. Ongoing AppKit API design discussions and library gaps (particularly around sigma-rust vs. ergo-wallet feature parity) highlighted maturing developer tooling challenges, while Satergo continued rapid iteration as a primary JVM-based wallet implementation.

---

## Key Topics This Month

### Explorer Backend Sync Performance
A critical infrastructure issue emerged early in the month: the Ergo Explorer backend was taking approximately two weeks to synchronise on virtual infrastructure, causing significant downstream problems for services like GetBlok that depend on uninterrupted payout processing. kushti ordered a more powerful dedicated server expected to reduce sync time to 3–4 days. GetBlok flagged that even 3–4 days was unacceptable for their use case and began exploring alternative approaches with abchris. A scan-based short-term mitigation was mentioned but not confirmed as implemented.

### Satergo Wallet Development
Aberg made substantial progress on Satergo throughout February, reducing application size from approximately 100 MB to 50 MB, adding password confirmation on transaction signing, and progressing NFT viewing and transaction history features. The 0.0.3 release was delayed pending resolution of a wallet security/derivation issue. Aberg also investigated the ergo-wallet library's lack of an `ExtendedPublicKey`-to-address conversion method, finding that sigma-rust exposes `ExtPubKey.to_address` but the ergo-wallet library does not, highlighting a feature parity gap between the two libraries.

### EIP-27 Testnet Activation
kushti announced EIP-27 activation on testnet at block `ce3e9e73638a6c1880a2516d76791f71b8f52696f445f87f2eb737247306f203`. Follow-up work was identified across multiple workstreams: mining pool accounting updates, Ergo Explorer integration for the new emission schedule, and broader testing. No mainnet activation timeline was discussed within the month.

### Ergo AppKit API Design
Ongoing review of AppKit PR #134 generated discussion between Aberg and MrStahlfelge around several design decisions: whether Scala objects should appear in the public API layer vs. only in implementation, naming conventions (`Asset` vs. `Eip4Token`), and method naming patterns (`to` vs. `as`, consistent with JDK conventions such as `File::toPath`). Resolution was pending review by aslesarenko and morphic's preferences on wrapper exposure.

### Node Wallet Derivation History
kushti flagged the node wallet derivation scheme as already being "a nightmare" due to divergent implementations across versions — pre-Yoroi wallets used a different derivation scheme, and Coinbarn and Yoroi had further variations. No remediation or refactoring timeline was proposed, but the complexity was formally acknowledged as a known technical debt item.

### HD Wallet xpub Key Encoding
Aberg raised a concern that hex encoding for extended public keys lacks visible distinction from Bitcoin xpub keys and wastes space relative to base58. The discussion reached consensus that the encoding decision was already finalised and too late to change without breaking compatibility.

### WebAssembly Dependency in Ergo dApps
Aberg raised the developer experience concern that Ergo dApp websites unnecessarily require WebAssembly without providing asm.js fallbacks, blocking integration into embedded browser environments within wallets. No resolution was reached and no maintainer commitment to address it was recorded.

### Privacy and Cryptography
kushti shared forum posts on mixing protocol design and Schnorr signature verification within ErgoScript contracts, indicating active research interest in privacy primitives. Luivatra emphasised understanding the eUTXO model as a prerequisite for dApp development, directing contributors to the developer documentation.

## Technical Q&A Worth Preserving

- **Q** (@Aberg, 2022-02-06): Why do Ergo dApp websites require WebAssembly without providing asm.js fallbacks, blocking embedded browser integration?
  **A** (no direct response): Flagged as an unresolved developer experience gap with no maintainer commitment recorded.

- **Q** (@Aberg, 2022-02-xx): How can one convert `org.ergoplatform.wallet.secrets.ExtendedPublicKey` to an address using the ergo-wallet library?
  **A** (@Aberg, self-resolved): The method `ExtPubKey.to_address` exists in sigma-rust but is absent from ergo-wallet. Aberg resolved their specific case through direct investigation; the library gap was not formally addressed.

- **Q** (@AllCloudy | GetBlok.io, 2022-02-08): Can anything be done short-term to alleviate explorer backend sync delays affecting GetBlok payouts?
  **A** (@kushti, 2022-02-08): A scan-based approach is available as a short-term mitigation; a more powerful server has been ordered for a 3–4 day sync long-term. GetBlok noted 3–4 days is still insufficient for uninterrupted payouts and began exploring alternatives with abchris.

- **Q** (@Aberg, 2022-02-xx): Is AppKit API naming better as `Asset` or `Eip4Token`, and should `to` or `as` prefixes be used for conversion methods?
  **A** (@Aberg / aslesarenko reference, 2022-02-23): `to` is preferred over `as` per JDK conventions (e.g., `File::toPath`); `Asset` was suggested as a cleaner public API name than `Eip4Token`. Final resolution pending PR #134 review.

- **Q** (@Aberg, 2022-02-xx): Does slow sync represent a meaningful decentralisation bottleneck for Ergo?
  **A** (@Aberg, self-assessed, 2022-02-xx): Ergo syncs in approximately 3 hours from scratch, which is faster than Monero (a full day), and is not considered a practical bottleneck.

- **Q** (@Luivatra, 2022-02-xx): What is the best starting point for Ergo dApp development?
  **A** (@Luivatra): Understanding the eUTXO model is essential as it fundamentally influences dApp architecture. See https://docs.ergoplatform.com/dev/
